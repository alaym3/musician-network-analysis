'''Ingrid Elizabeth Andress''' (born September 21, 1991) is an American [[country music]] singer from Colorado. Her debut single "More Hearts Than Mine" peaked at number 30 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot 100]] chart dated May 1, 2019. In March 2020, she released her debut studio album, ''[[Lady Like]]''.

==Life and career==
Andress was born in [[Southfield, Michigan]]<ref name=Birthplace/> and grew up in [[Highlands Ranch, Colorado]]. As a child, she learned to play piano and drums while also participating in high school choir competitions at [[Rock Canyon High School]]. Andress attended [[Berklee College of Music]] and majored in songwriting and performance.<ref name=bio />

She joined an [[a cappella]] group called Pitch Slapped, which performed on the [[NBC]] singing competition ''[[The Sing-Off]]''. Another one of her groups, Delilah, also performed on the show and placed sixth. After leaving the show, Andress finished her degree and underwent a mentorship with pop songwriter [[Kara DioGuardi]]. She then moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] where she began writing songs for artists such as [[Sam Hunt]], [[Alicia Keys]], and [[Charli XCX]].<ref name=bio>{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ingrid-andress-mn0002596016/biography | title=Ingrid Andress biography | publisher=AllMusic | access-date=October 20, 2019 | author=Matt Collar}}</ref>

Andress signed with [[Warner Records|Warner Nashville]] in mid 2018.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://musicrow.com/2018/07/ingrid-andress-signs-with-warner-music-nashville-atlantic-records/ | title=Ingrid Andress Signs With Warner Music Nashville, Atlantic Records | work=MusicRow | date=July 27, 2018 | access-date=October 20, 2019 | author=Lorie Hollabaugh}}</ref> In 2019, she debuted with the single "[[More Hearts Than Mine]]".<ref>{{cite web | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/ingrid-andress-more-hearts-than-mine-interview/ | title=Ingrid Andress Parents' Perfect Reaction to More Hearts Than Mine | work=Taste of Country | date=August 26, 2019 | access-date=October 20, 2019 | author=Billy Dukes}}</ref> The song has charted on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts. Andress cites [[Faith Hill]] and [[The Chicks]] as her musical inspirations, and notes that she always wants to write her own songs unless she finds an exceptional song written by someone else.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://theboot.com/ingrid-andress-music-sound/ | title=Ingrid Andress: 'I Just Feel Like Keeping It Open-Minded' | publisher=The Boot | date=July 23, 2019 | access-date=October 20, 2019 | author=Carena Liptak}}</ref> On March 27, 2020, she released her debut studio album ''Lady Like'' through Warner Music Nashville.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/9343148/ingrid-andress-lady-like-album-breakdown|title=Ingrid Andress' 'Lady Like' Album: Stories Behind the Songs|date=2020-03-26|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref> Andress reached No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Emerging Artists chart in April 2020 due to the chart arrival of ''Lady Like'' and the rise of her single "More Hearts Than Mine", which reached the top five of the American and Canadian country charts.<ref name="leading">{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9353831/ingrid-andress-tops-emerging-artists-chart |title=Leading 'Lady': Ingrid Andress Hits No. 1 on Emerging Artists Chart |date=2020-04-08 |website=billboard.com |access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref> The deluxe edition of ''Lady Like'' was released on October 2, 2020.<ref name="all access lady like">{{cite web |title=Future Releases for Country Radio Stations |url=https://www.allaccess.com/country/future-releases |website=All Access |access-date=September 25, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200922020516/https://www.allaccess.com/country/future-releases |archive-date=September 22, 2020}}</ref>
