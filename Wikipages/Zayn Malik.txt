'''Zain Javadd Malik''' ({{IPAc-en|'|m|æ|l|ɪ|k}}; born 12 January 1993), [[Mononymous person|known mononymously]] as '''Zayn''', is a British singer. Born and raised in [[Bradford]], Malik auditioned as a solo contestant for the British music competition ''[[The X Factor (UK TV series)|The X Factor]]'' in 2010. After being eliminated as a solo performer, Malik was brought back into the competition, along with four other contestants, to form the boy band [[One Direction]], which went on to become one of the [[Boy band#Best-selling boy bands|best-selling boy bands of all time]]. Malik left the group in March 2015 and subsequently signed a solo recording contract with [[RCA Records]].

Adopting a more [[alternative R&B]] music style with his debut studio album, ''[[Mind of Mine]]'' (2016), and its lead single, "[[Pillowtalk (song)|Pillowtalk]]", Malik became the first British male artist to debut at number one [[List of artists who have achieved simultaneous UK and US number-one hits|in both the UK and US]], with his debut single and debut album. His collaborative singles, "[[I Don't Wanna Live Forever]]" with [[Taylor Swift]] and "[[Dusk Till Dawn (Zayn song)|Dusk Till Dawn]]" featuring [[Sia (musician)|Sia]], were met with international success. Malik released his second studio album, ''[[Icarus Falls]]'', in 2018. His third album, ''[[Nobody Is Listening]]'', was released in January 2021.

Malik is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Zayn Malik|several accolades]], including an [[American Music Award]] and a [[MTV Video Music Award]]. He is also the only artist to have won the [[Billboard Music Award for New Artist of the Year]] twice, receiving it once as a member of One Direction in 2013 and then again in 2017 as a solo artist.
