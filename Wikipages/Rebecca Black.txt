'''Rebecca Renee Black''' (born June 21, 1997<ref>{{cite tweet|user=MsRebeccaBlack|author=Rebecca Black|number=50024241491492864|date=March 22, 2011|title=@RBeccaBlackFan June 21(:}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=MsRebeccaBlack|author=Rebecca Black|number=877729387851857920|date=June 21, 2017|title=Thank you for all of the birthday love. So far 20 feels real NICE üåüüåüüåü}}</ref>) is an American singer and [[YouTuber]]. She gained extensive media coverage when the music video for her 2011 single "[[Friday (Rebecca Black song)|Friday]]" went [[viral video|viral]] on YouTube and other [[social media]] sites. "Friday" was derided by many music critics and viewers, who dubbed it "the worst song ever".<ref>{{cite news |publisher=BBC|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/12784330 |last=Whitworth |first=Dan |title='Worst song ever' gets 29m views after going viral |date=March 21, 2011 |access-date=April 2, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/chatter/2011/03/rebecca-black-teen-singer-admits-she-cried-when-her-hit-song-was-deemed-worst-song-ever.html?cid=6a00d8341c630a53ef014e5ff8c4bc970c |last=Pierce |first=Tony |title=Rebecca Black, teen singer, admits she cried when her hit song was deemed 'Worst Song Ever' |date=March 18, 2011 |access-date=April 2, 2011 |work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2011/03/22/3170156.htm |title='Worst song ever' tops 30 million views |date=March 22, 2011 |access-date=April 2, 2011 |publisher=[[ABC News]] Australia |author=AFP}}</ref> Black went on to release other songs including her [[Dave Days]] collaboration "[[Saturday (Rebecca Black and Dave Days song)|Saturday]]". She currently uploads videos on her YouTube channel about various topics.<ref>{{cite web|author=rebecca |url=https://www.youtube.com/rebecca |title=Rebecca |publisher=YouTube |access-date=May 27, 2016}}</ref>

At [[VidCon]] 2015, Black revealed that she had been working on an album which she hoped to release by the end of 2015. According to her, the album would not be anything like her previous songs. She had been working on the album since releasing her song ''Friday''; however, but it took her a while to be free from ARK Music Factory and find producers with whom she was comfortable.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Holub |first=Christian |url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/07/23/rebecca-black |title=Rebecca Black teases new album at VidCon |magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |date=July 23, 2015 |access-date=May 27, 2016}}</ref> However, no album or new original songs were released. Towards the end of 2015, Black performed a cover of "[[Wild (Troye Sivan song)|Wild]]" by [[Troye Sivan]].<ref>{{YouTube|8DRG2BMqOIM}}</ref> Black created a web video in January 2016 on [[Go90]] called "Life After Friday". In the show, Black graduates from high school, tells her parents she is not going to college and follows her adventures after moving to Los Angeles.<ref name="go90.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.go90.com/profiles/series_5f776e31bfee454e88c0b9a80df1045d|title=go90|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817004414/https://www.go90.com/profiles/series_5f776e31bfee454e88c0b9a80df1045d|archive-date=August 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvguide.com/tvshows/rebecca-black-life-after-friday/episode-1-season-1/life-after-friday/892141/|title=Rebecca Black Life After Friday|work=[[TV Guide]]|access-date=July 4, 2016}}</ref>

===2016‚Äìpresent: ''RE / BL'' and ''Rebecca Black Was Here''===
On August 26, 2016, Black released a new single titled "The Great Divide", her first release of new material in almost three years.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/rebecca-black-the-great-divide|title=Rebecca Black's Comeback Single "The Great Divide" Is Here|magazine=Teen Vogue|access-date=August 26, 2016}}</ref> This single was the last of her songs to make the charts, which was achieved at No. 23 under the "[[Dance Club Songs|US Dance]]" category. Black came out with two more singles in the following year, which included "Foolish" on April 21, 2017, and "Heart Full of Scars" on September 1, 2017. She then released an EP on September 15, 2017, titled ''RE / BL'', which included these two songs as well as "The Great Divide". In June 2018, she appeared on [[BBC Radio 5 Live|BBC Radio 5]] talking about [[cyberbullying]], the adverse effects online attacks had on her and how cruel comments "stick with you forever".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/av/44397473/rebecca-black-cruel-comments-stick-with-you-for-ever|title=Cruel comments stick with you forever|work=BBC News}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-44397472|title=Online abuse troubled Black for years|first=Dan|last=Maudsley|work=BBC News|date=June 7, 2018}}</ref> Black released the final single from ''RE / BL'', titled "Satellite", on June 4, 2018.

On June 7, 2018, Black appeared as a contestant in [[The Four: Battle for Stardom (season 2)|season 2]] of the reality television musical competition series ''[[The Four: Battle for Stardom]]'', broadcast on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]]. The series is hosted by [[Fergie (singer)|Fergie]] and judged by [[Sean Combs]], [[Meghan Trainor]] and [[DJ Khaled]]. Black sang "[[Bye Bye Bye]]" from [[NSYNC]] for her audition, and received the go-ahead for the next round with "three blue rings" from all three judges. In the next round, she picked contestant James Graham from the boy band [[Stereo Kicks]] as her opponent. Graham performed "[[A Song for You]]", followed by Black singing [[Ednaswap]]'s "[[Torn (Ednaswap song)|Torn]]". The audience voted on the two performances, preferring to save Graham, with Black being eliminated from the show.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/music/a21237138/rebecca-black-friday-the-four-video-performance/|title=It's Friday, Friday, REBECCA BLACK Performed *NSYNC On TV Last Night|last=Rees|first=Alex|date=June 8, 2018|website=Cosmopolitan}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8459994/rebecca-black-performed-an-nsync-song-on-the-four|title=Rebecca Black Performed an *NSYNC Song on 'The Four': Watch|magazine=Billboard|date=June 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hollywoodlife.com/2018/06/07/the-four-season-2-premiere-episode-1-recap/|title='The Four' Premiere Recap: Rebecca Black Proves She's Grown Since 'Friday' With Epic Challenge|first=Courteney|last=Larocca|date=June 8, 2018}}</ref> After her appearance, she tweeted: "I am just blown away reading your comments and seeing so much support online,‚Äù she wrote. ‚ÄúSending all of my love right back to you. Thank you for hearing me, for seeing me. This is just the beginning. Also I don't think I'm gonna sleep at all tonight I'm way too excited."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/music/rebecca-black-friday-four-audition/|title=See Rebecca Black's Surprise Audition on The Four 7 Years After Releasing Her Viral Hit 'Friday'|website=PEOPLE.com}}</ref>

On National French Fry Day 2018,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://money.com/money/5335914/free-french-fries-deals-national-french-fry-day-2018/|title=All the Places You Can Get Free French Fries Today for National French Fry Day 2018|website=Money}}</ref> YouTube personality [[Miranda Sings]], in collaboration with [[Dunkin' Donuts]], released an adapted version of "Friday" titled "Donut Fry Day featuring Rebecca Black", as part of the Donut Fries marketing strategy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.dunkindonuts.com/blog/miranda-sings-and-rebecca-black-come-together-to-celebrate-fry-day-at-dunkin|title=Miranda Sings and Rebecca Black Come Together To Celebrate "Fry-Day" at Dunkin'|website=Dunkin'}}</ref> Black continued to release singles throughout 2019 and 2020 with the singles "Anyway", "Do You?", "Sweetheart" and "Self Sabotage".{{citation needed|date=July 2020}} In September 2020, Black featured on the [[Dorian Electra]] track "Edgelord".

On January 29, 2021, Black released the song "Girlfriend". Shortly after, on February 10, 2021, she released a [[hyperpop]] remix of "Friday" produced by [[Dylan Brady]] of [[100 Gecs]] and featuring [[3OH!3]], [[Big Freedia]] and [[Dorian Electra]] to commemorate the song's tenth anniversary. After releasing the singles "Personal" and "Worth It for the Feeling", Black announced her second EP, ''Rebecca Black Was Here'', which was released on June 16, 2021.
