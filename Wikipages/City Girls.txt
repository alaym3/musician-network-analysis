'''City Girls''' is an American [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] duo consisting of '''Yung Miami''' (pronounced "Young Miami", born Caresha Romeka Brownlee in February 1994)<ref>{{cite newspaper|url= https://www.miaminewtimes.com/news/pregnant-city-girls-rapper-yung-miami-shot-leaving-miami-dade-recording-studio-11236434|title= Someone Tried to Shoot City Girls Rapper Yung Miami|author= Iannelli, Jerry|date= August 6, 2019|newspaper=[[Miami New Times]]}}</ref> and '''JT''' (born Jatavia Shakara Johnson<ref name="all music">{{Cite news |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/city-girls-mn0003706669/biography |title=City Girls Biography |last=Kellman |first=Andy |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=November 16, 2018}}</ref><ref name="rolling-stone">{{Cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/city-girls-separated-by-prison-want-to-be-icons-702646/ |title=City Girls, Separated by Prison, Want to be Icons |last=Spanos |first=Brittany |date=July 26, 2018 |work=Rolling Stone |access-date=November 16, 2018}}</ref> in December 1992).<ref>[https://www.ancestry.com/discoveryui-content/view/160559058:62209?tid=&pid=&queryId=4d1b199bd767a6f491be8dc51cb42b7e&_phsrc=aow1368552&_phstart=successSource Jatavia Shakara Johnson] in U.S., Index to Public Records, 1994-2019. " Birth Date: Dec 1992".</ref> The duo originate from [[Miami]], [[Florida]], and garnered attention after making an uncredited guest appearance on "[[In My Feelings]]", the 2018 chart-topping single by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref name="rolling-stone"/> The duo signed to [[Quality Control Music]], and later released their debut mixtape, ''[[Period (mixtape)|Period]]'' (2018); as well as the duo later released two studio albums: ''[[Girl Code (album)|Girl Code]]'' (2018), of which spawned the [[RIAA certification|Platinum-certified]] singles "[[Twerk (City Girls song)|Twerk]]" (featuring [[Cardi B]]) and "[[Act Up (song)|Act Up]]", and, ''[[City on Lock]]'' (2020).

==Career==
The group's name derives from the pair being from [[Opa-locka, Florida]] and [[Liberty City (Miami)]], described by [[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] magazine as "two of the roughest neighborhoods in Miami, Florida".<ref name="complex-set">{{Cite news|last1 = Roland|first1=Dria|date=July 23, 2019|title=Yung Miami of City Girls Says JT's Incarceration Is 'A Minor Setback for a Major Comeback'|language=en|website=Complex|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/07/in-my-feelings-drake-city-girls-free-jt|access-date=April 30, 2019}}</ref>

=== 2017: Early beginnings ===
The duo recorded their debut studio track, "Fuck Dat Nigga", which was a diss track towards their ex-boyfriends for not giving them money when they asked.<ref name="all music" /> Yung Miami promoted it through social media and by paying DJs to play it in clubs. Soon the track, which features a prominent sample of fellow Florida rapper [[Khia]]'s "[[My Neck, My Back (Lick It)]]", racked up hundreds of thousands of plays.<ref name="in-my-feelings-fader">{{cite news|last1=Darville|first1=Jordan|date=August 1, 2018|title=Yung Miami gets in her feelings about City Girls|work=The Fader|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/01/yung-miami-in-my-feelings-city-girls-interview|access-date=September 7, 2019}}</ref> The official music video for the song came out in January 2018 and featured an appearance from rapper [[Trina]]. Later that year, the track was included in [[Quality Control Music|Quality Control]]'s compilation album, ''[[Quality Control: Control the Streets Volume 1|Control the Streets, Volume 1]].''<ref name="all music" />

=== 2018â€“2019: ''Period'', ''Girl Code'', and breakthrough ===
Shortly after the release of "Fuck Dat Nigga", JT was arrested and charged with [[Cybercrime countermeasures#Identity Theft and Aggravated Identity Theft|aggravated identity theft]], and was sentenced to 24 months in federal prison. The judge eventually agreed to push back her surrender date.<ref name="the-cut">{{Cite web|last=Zeichner|first=Naomi|date=September 2, 2018|title=City Girls Are More Like You Than You Think|url=https://www.thecut.com/2018/09/city-girls-are-more-like-you-than-you-think.html|access-date=May 7, 2019|website=The Cut|language=en}}</ref> JT began her prison term in July 2018 and was set to be released on March 1, 2020.<ref name="billboard-jail">{{Cite news|last1=Saponara|first1=Michael|date=July 2, 2018|title=City Girls Member JT Begins Serving Jail Sentence for Fraud Charges|website=Billboard|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8463594/city-girls-jt-begins-jail-sentence-fraud|access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref> While JT was incarcerated, Yung Miami continued to promote the group's music, by saying: "When she was in jail, I was going to strip clubs and I would pay a DJ 20 dollars to play the song. It started taking off."<ref name="in-my-feelings-fader" />

==Personal lives==
Both JT and Yung Miami grew up in Opalocka. JT has said "my mother was an addict" and that "drugs ruined my child hood".<ref>{{Cite web|title=City Girls' JT Denies Cocaine Allegations: "I Hate Drugs I Don't Even Smoke Weed"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/city-girls-jt-denies-cocaine-allegations-i-hate-drugs-i-dont-even-smoke-weed-news.99570.html|access-date=2021-03-25|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en}}</ref> At 17, they were performing in strip clubs, night clubs and block parties.<ref name="rolling-stone"/> Yung Miami said that she loved [[trap music]] from a young age, and told ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', "My little boyfriend used to take me to school every day, so I grew up listening to a lot of trap music."<ref name="rolling-stone"/> Before rapping, Yung Miami was an [[Instagram]] influencer who promoted her own fashion line.<ref name="vulture">{{cite news |last1=Lockett |first1=Dee |title=City Girls Are Our Greatest Scammers |url=https://www.vulture.com/2018/11/city-girls-on-scamming-drake-and-jt-going-to-prison.html |access-date=April 25, 2019 |work=Vulture |date=November 29, 2018}}</ref>

=== Legal issues and controversies ===

==== 2018â€“2020 JT incarceration ====
Shortly after the release of their 2017 debut single, "Fuck Dat Nigga", JT was arrested and convicted of aggravated [[identity theft]] on [[Credit card fraud|fraudulent credit card charges]], and was sentenced to 24 months in federal prison. Following a pushback of her surrender date, JT turned herself in to the authorities on June 29, 2018, and began serving her sentence while being held at [[Federal Correctional Institution, Tallahassee|FCI Tallahassee]] in July 2018.<ref name="billboard-jail" /> She was slated to be released March 21, 2020.<ref name="billboard-jail" /> As she awaited her release, JT was transferred from FCI Tallahassee to a [[halfway house]] in [[Atlanta]] on October 8.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=October 9, 2019|title=City Girls' JT Released to Halfway House|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2019/10/09/city-girls-jt-released-to-halfway-house/|access-date=October 10, 2019|website=[[Rap-Up]]}}</ref> During her stay in the halfway house, she was able to leave the house during the day to work and visit family and friends.<ref name=":0" /> To celebrate her release, JT released a track titled "JT First Day Out".<ref name=":1" /> On the song, she shouts out Yung Miami, rapping,<blockquote>"I been a real bitch way before the fed case / Yung Miami held me down, that's a bitch ace / And if a bitch try her, it's a cold case".<ref name=":1" /> </blockquote>A few celebrities showed to be displeased with the incarceration of the artist: [[Trina]] wore a T-shirt that had "Free JT" written on it, and Drake posted on [[Instagram]] "Free my shorty".<ref name="billboard-jail" /> JT was officially released from federal custody on March 7, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|last=Centeno|first=Tony M.|date=March 9, 2020|title=City Girls' JT released from federal custody, no longer in halfway house|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/03/city-girls-jt-released-federal-custody-halfway-house/|access-date=March 9, 2020|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]}}</ref>

==== Yung Miami homophobic remarks ====
In 2013, Yung Miami stated that she would not want one of her sons to be gay and would beat him if she found out that he was.<ref name="Daw">{{cite web|last1=Daw|first1=Stephen|date=November 13, 2018|title=City Girls Rapper Yung Miami Doubles Down on Homophobic Comment|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8484814/city-girls-rapper-yung-miami-breakfast-club-interview-homophobic-comments|access-date=November 19, 2018|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> In August 2018, Yung Miami was among several rappers who faced criticism after the circulation of [[Twitter#Tweets|tweet]]s she had written in the past that contained [[homophobia|homophobic]] remarks.<ref name="fader-tweets">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/29/yung-miami-doja-cat-share-apologies-homophobic-tweets-resurface|title=Yung Miami of City Girls, Doja Cat, and More Apologize in Homophobic Tweet Uproar|last1=Darville |first1=Jordan|date=August 29, 2018|work=[[The Fader]]|access-date=February 11, 2019}}</ref> Following the resurfacing of her statements, Yung Miami publicly issued a formal apology through an [[Instagram]] post.<ref name="Daw" />

Though she apologized, Yung Miami found herself embroiled in controversy once more on November 13 when she doubled down on her homophobic statements in an appearance on [[Power 105.1]]'s radio show ''[[The Breakfast Club (radio show)|The Breakfast Club]]''.<ref name="Daw" /> During the interview, she was questioned by radio host [[Charlamagne tha God]] in regard to her controversial tweet that claimed what she would do if she found out that her son was gay. Yung Miami replied that her previous tweet had nothing to do with the LGBTQ community and was specifically about her son. She said, "I was just talking about my son. I just said that if I saw anything gay in my son, that I would beat him".<ref name="Daw" /> The rapper attempted to provide clarity for what she had actually meant. She elaborated, "But that's just like when your mama be like, 'If you break my table I'm gonna beat the shit out of you.' That don't mean she's gonna beat the shit out of you, she's just saying it."<ref name="Orcutt">{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/11/yung-miami-comments-son-gay/|title=Yung Miami Faces Backlash for Saying Doesn't Want Son to Be Gay|last1=Orcutt |first1=KC|date=November 14, 2018|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|access-date=November 15, 2018}}</ref> While she stood by her comment that as a mother she does not want a gay son, Yung Miami insisted that she does not harbor any resentment towards gay people. She explained that she spends much time around many gay people, including her cousin and hairstylist.<ref name="Orcutt" />

Commentators throughout social media immediately decried the rapper, saying that her very line of reasoning was homophobic and the comments she made on ''The Breakfast Club'' were hateful and [[Anti-LGBT rhetoric|anti-LGBTQ]].<ref name="Daw" /> It was also emphasized that such [[corporal punishment]] is still in practice among some parents who condemn their children's sexual orientation.<ref name="Williams">{{cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/hiphop/city-girls-young-miami-breakfast-club-interview-homophobic-comments/3/|title=City Girls' Yung Miami Is Under Fire For Making Homophobic Comments On 'The Breakfast Club'|last1=Williams |first1=Aaron|date=November 13, 2018|work=[[Uproxx]]|access-date=November 15, 2018}}</ref>
