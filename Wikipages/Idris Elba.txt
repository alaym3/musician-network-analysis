He has been nominated four times for a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Miniseries or Television Film]], winning one, and has been nominated five times for a [[Primetime Emmy Award]].<ref>{{cite journal|first=Brian |last=Lowry |url=https://variety.com/2014/tv/news/emmy-nominations-2014-list-emmys-nominees-1201260236/ |title=Emmy Nominations 2014 — Full List: 66th Primetime Emmys Nominees |journal=Variety |date=10 July 2014 |access-date=28 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="Luther">{{cite news |title=Wire actor Elba joins BBC drama |work=BBC News |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/8237924.stm |access-date=7 September 2009 |date=4 September 2009}}</ref>

Elba appeared in [[Ridley Scott]]'s ''[[American Gangster (film)|American Gangster]]'' (2007) and ''[[Prometheus (2012 film)|Prometheus]]'' (2012). Elba portrayed [[Heimdall (comics)|Heimdall]] in ''[[Thor (film)|Thor]]'' (2011) and its sequels ''[[Thor: The Dark World]]'' (2013) and ''[[Thor: Ragnarok]]'' (2017), as well as ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]]'' (2015) and ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]]'' (2018). He also starred in ''[[Pacific Rim (film)|Pacific Rim]]'' (2013), ''[[Beasts of No Nation (film)|Beasts of No Nation]]'' (2015), for which he received [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Motion Picture|Golden Globe]] and [[BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role|BAFTA]] nominations for Best Supporting Actor, and ''[[Molly's Game]]'' (2017). In 2016, he voiced Chief Bogo in ''[[Zootopia]]'', [[Shere Khan]] in the live action/CGI adaptation of ''[[The Jungle Book (2016 film)|The Jungle Book]]'', Fluke in ''[[Finding Dory]],'' and played the role of Krall in ''[[Star Trek Beyond]]''. He made his directorial debut in 2018 with [[Yardie (film)|an adaptation]] of the 1992 novel ''[[Yardie (novel)|Yardie]]'' by [[Victor Headley]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://collider.com/idris-elba-yardie-director/ |title=Idris Elba Set to Make Directorial Debut With ''Yardie'' |last=Cabin |first=Chris |website=[[Collider (website)|Collider]] |date=5 July 2016 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6imkfzpT4?url=http://collider.com/idris-elba-yardie-director/ |archive-date=5 July 2016 |url-status=live |access-date=5 July 2016 }}</ref> In 2021, he portrayed [[Bloodsport (character)|Bloodsport]] in [[The Suicide Squad (film)|''The Suicide Squad'']], set in the [[DC Extended Universe]].

Aside from acting, Elba performs as a DJ under the moniker '''DJ Big Driis''' (or '''Big Driis the Londoner''') and as an [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] musician.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hiphopwired.com/2011/09/13/idris-elba-driis-releases-new-music-video-secret-garden-video/ |title=Idris Elba (Driis) Releases New Music Video 'Secret Garden' |date=13 September 2011 |publisher=hiphopwired.com |first=Danielle |last=Canada}}</ref> In 2016, he was named in the [[Time 100|''Time'' 100]] list of the Most Influential People in the World.<ref>{{cite news |author=David Simon|url=http://time.com/4299764/idris-elba-2016-time-100//|title=Idris Elba: TIME 100|work=Time|access-date=27 April 2016}}</ref> As of May 2019, his films have grossed over {{US$|9.8&nbsp;billion|long=no}} at the global box office, including over {{US$|3.6&nbsp;billion|long=no}} in North America,<ref>{{cite web |title=Idris Elba Movie Box Office Results |url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/people/chart/?view=Actor&id=idriselba.htm |website=[[Box Office Mojo]] |access-date=6 May 2019}}</ref> where he is one of the top 20 highest-grossing actors.<ref>{{cite web |title=People Index {{ndash}} Actors: Total Gross |url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/people/?view=Actor&sort=sumgross&p=.htm |website=[[Box Office Mojo]] |access-date=6 May 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Idrissa Akuna Elba was born on 6 September 1972<ref name="guardian"/> in the [[London Borough of Hackney]],<ref name=Biography.com>{{cite web|url=https://www.biography.com/people/idris-elba-21429871| title= Idris Elba:Singer, Actor (1972–)|publisher=[[Biography.com]] ([[FYI (TV network)|FYI]] / [[A&E Networks]])| access-date=2 August 2017|quote=Idrissa Akuna Elba was born on September 6, 1972, in the Hackney section of East London, England. An only child of Sierra Leonean descent, Elba eventually attended the National Youth Music Theatre's training programs.}}</ref> to Winston, a [[Sierra Leonean]] man who worked at the [[Ford Dagenham]] plant, and Eve, a Ghanaian woman.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/film/beasts-of-no-nation/cary-fukunaga-interview/|title=Idris Elba meets his Waterloo – in Ghana|date=3 October 2015|work=The Telegraph|access-date=23 November 2018|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> Elba's parents were married in Sierra Leone and later moved to London.<ref name="observer_2010">{{cite web |url= https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2010/jan/24/idris-elba-interview-the-wire |title=Idris Elba: life after 'Stringer' Bell |first=Miranda |last=Sawyer|author-link=Miranda Sawyer |work=[[The Observer]] |date=24 January 2010 |access-date=28 January 2015}}</ref> Elba was raised in Hackney and [[East Ham]],<ref name="Midas Touch">{{cite news|first=Stuart |last=Jeffries |date=9 May 2009|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2009/may/05/television-the-wire-idris-elba-obsessed |title=The Midas Touch |work=The Guardian |access-date=29 July 2009|location=London}}</ref> and shortened his first name to "Idris" at school in [[Canning Town]], where he first became involved in acting. He credits ''[[The Stage]]'' with giving him his first big break, having seen an advertisement for a play in it; he auditioned and met his first agent while performing in the role.<ref name="baftaguru_2013">{{cite web |url= http://guru.bafta.org/idris-elba-big-questions |title=Idris Elba: Big Questions |work=[[BAFTA]] Guru |date=19 June 2013 |access-date=17 August 2015}}</ref> In 1986, he began helping an uncle with his wedding DJ business; within a year, he had started his own DJ company with some of his friends.<ref name="guardian"/> 

Elba left school in 1988 and won a place in the [[National Youth Music Theatre]] after a £1,500 [[The Prince's Trust|Prince's Trust]] grant.<ref>[https://www.princeofwales.gov.uk/news-and-diary/princes-trust-celebrate-success "Prince's Trust Celebrate Success"]. Prince of Wales.gov.uk. Retrieved 2 December 2017</ref> To support himself between roles in his early career, he worked in odd jobs including tyre-fitting, [[cold-calling]], and night shifts at Ford Dagenham.<ref name="times">{{cite news|first=Chris |last=Ayres |date=23 August 2008 | url = http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/film/article4572201.ece | title=Life as a RocknRolla: meet the crafty Cockney Idris Elba |work=The Times |access-date=11 April 2009|location=London}}</ref> He was working in nightclubs under the DJ nickname "Big Driis" during his adolescence, but began auditioning for television roles in his early twenties.<ref name="guardian"/>

==Career==
===Television===
Elba's first acting role was in ''[[Crimewatch]]'' murder reconstructions and in 1994 he appeared in a BBC children's drama called ''The Boot Street Band''. In 1995, he landed his first significant role on a series called ''[[Bramwell (TV series)|Bramwell]]'', a medical drama set in 1890s England. He played a central character in an episode of Season 1, an African petty thief named Charlie Carter, who lost his wife to childbirth and had to figure out how to support his newborn daughter. His first named role arrived earlier in 1995, when he was cast as a [[gigolo]] on the "Sex" episode of ''[[Absolutely Fabulous]]''. Many supporting roles on British television followed, including series such as ''[[The Bill]]'' and ''[[The Ruth Rendell Mysteries]]''. He joined the cast of the soap opera ''[[Family Affairs]]''<ref name="guardian">{{cite news|first=Esther |last=Addley |date=20 March 2008 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2008/mar/21/television |title=He often has fewer lines than anyone else but you still feel as though he has the bigger part, because he is luminous |work=The Guardian |access-date=23 August 2013| archive-date= 1 December 2016| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20161201214845/https://www.theguardian.com/media/2008/mar/21/television| url-status=live}}</ref> and went on to appear on the television serial ''[[Ultraviolet (TV serial)|Ultraviolet]]'' and later on ''[[Dangerfield (TV series)|Dangerfield]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Idris Elba – Biography|url=https://movies.yahoo.com/person/idris-elba/biography.html |publisher=[[Yahoo! Movies]]|access-date=17 July 2013}}</ref> He decided to move to New York City soon after.<ref name="guardian"/> He returned to England occasionally for a television role, such as a part in one of the ''[[Inspector Lynley Mysteries]]''. In 2001, Elba played [[Achilles]] in a stage production of ''[[Troilus and Cressida]]'' in New York City.<ref name="guardian"/>

After a supporting turn on a 2001 episode of ''[[Law & Order]]'', Elba landed a starring role on the 2002 HBO drama series ''The Wire''. From 2002 to 2004, Elba portrayed Russell "Stringer" Bell in the series, perhaps his best-known role in the United States. In 2005, he portrayed [[Captain (armed forces)|Captain]] Augustin Muganza in ''[[Sometimes in April]]'', an HBO film about the [[Rwandan genocide]]. Elba appeared on the 2007 [[BET]] special ''Black Men: The Truth''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Idris Elba |url=http://uk.askmen.com/celebs/men/entertainment/idris-elba/ |work=askmen.com |access-date=17 July 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130807231102/http://uk.askmen.com/celebs/men/entertainment/idris-elba/ |archive-date=7 August 2013 }}</ref> He appeared as Charlie Gotso on ''[[The No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency (TV series)|The No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency]]'', filmed in [[Botswana]].<ref name="variety">{{cite news|title='Detective Agency' finds big auds |work=Variety |url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117982869.html?categoryid=14&cs=1 |access-date=11 April 2009 |first=Archie |last=Thomas |date=25 March 2008}}</ref> The series premiered on 23 March 2008, Easter Sunday, on BBC One, receiving a high 6.3&nbsp;million viewers and 27% of the audience share.<ref>{{cite news |title=Minghella's last film watched by 6.3m |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2008/mar/25/tvratings.television |access-date=11 April 2009 |location=London |first=John |last=Plunkett |date=25 March 2008}}</ref>

In January 2009, it was reported by ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' that Elba would portray [[List of The Office (U.S. TV series) characters#Charles Miner|Charles Miner]], a new rival to Dunder Mifflin regional manager Michael Scott ([[Steve Carell]]) for [[NBC]]'s ''[[The Office (U.S. TV series)|The Office]]''.<ref name="variety"/> Elba appeared in a six-episode story arc later in the 2009 season as well as the season finale.<ref name="variety"/>
In September 2009, he signed a deal to star as the lead role on the six-part BBC television series ''[[Luther (TV series)|Luther]]'', which aired in May 2010.<ref name="Luther"/> He appeared on [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime's]] ''[[The Big C (TV series)|The Big C]]'' in 2010.<ref name="Deadline">{{cite web |title=Idris Elba Is New Alex Cross in Relaunched James Patterson Film Franchise |website=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |date=18 August 2010 |url=https://www.deadline.com/2010/08/idris-elba-is-new-alex-cross-in-relaunched-james-patterson-film-franchise |access-date=19 August 2010}}</ref> At the [[69th Golden Globe Awards]] telecast on 15 January 2012, Elba won the Award for Best Actor in a Series, Mini-Series, or Motion Picture Made for Television for his role on the BBC crime thriller series ''Luther''.<ref name="Luther"/>

In April 2018, it was announced that Elba was cast as Charlie in the [[Netflix]] comedy series, ''[[Turn Up Charlie]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Otterson|first1=Joe|title=Idris Elba to Star in Netflix Comedy Series 'Turn Up Charlie'|url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/idris-elba-netflix-comedy-series-turn-up-charlie-1202756227/|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|access-date=21 February 2019|date=18 April 2018}}</ref> It premiered on 15 March 2019 and was cancelled after one season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2020/04/idris-elba-turn-up-charlie-canceled-netflix-one-season-1202918925/|title=Idris Elba's 'Turn Up Charlie' Canceled By Netflix After One Season|website=Deadline Hollywood|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva|date=27 April 2020|access-date=27 April 2020}}</ref> He also created and starred in the semi-autobiographical comedy ''[[In the Long Run]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last= Sturges |first= Fiona |date= 24 March 2018 |title= In the Long Run: Idris Elba's joyful portrait of community in 1980s Hackney |url= https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2018/mar/24/idris-elba-in-the-long-run |newspaper= [[The Guardian]] |access-date= 4 April 2019 }}</ref>

He appeared in ''[[28 Weeks Later]]'' (2007) and ''[[This Christmas (film)|This Christmas]]'' (2007), which brought in nearly $50&nbsp;million at the box office in 2007.<ref name="moviesonline">{{cite web|title=Idris Elba and Columbus Short Interview, This Christmas |publisher=Moviesonline.ca |url=http://www.moviesonline.ca/movienews_13494.html |access-date=11 April 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150114063242/http://www.moviesonline.ca/movienews_13494.html |archive-date=14 January 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=This Christmas|publisher=Thenumbers.com|url=https://www.the-numbers.com/movies/2007/TCHRS.php|access-date=11 April 2009}}</ref> In 2008, he starred in the horror film ''[[Prom Night (2008 film)|Prom Night]]'' and the [[Guy Ritchie]] London gangster film ''[[RocknRolla]]''.<ref name="moviesonline"/> In 2009, he starred in the horror film ''[[The Unborn (2009 film)|The Unborn]]'' and in ''[[Obsessed (2009 film)|Obsessed]]'', a thriller that had him cast opposite [[Beyoncé]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Idris Elba And Beyonce To Play Married Couple In 'Obsessed'| publisher =Blackvoices.com|url=http://www.blackvoices.com/blogs/2008/03/06/idris-elba-and-beyonce-to-play-married-couple-in-obsessed/|access-date=11 April 2009}}</ref> The latter was a box office success, taking $29&nbsp;million in its opening weekend.<ref>{{cite web|title=Weekend Report: Moviegoers Fixate on 'Obsessed'|publisher=Box Office Mojo|url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/news/?id=2581&p=.htm|access-date=26 April 2009}}</ref>

He played [[Heimdall (comics)|Heimdall]] in [[Kenneth Branagh]]'s film ''Thor'' (2011) (based on the [[Marvel Comics]] [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|superhero of the same name]]).<ref name="elba">{{cite news|url=http://www.heatvisionblog.com/2009/11/idris-elba-joins-marvel-studios-thor-natalie-portman-kenneth-branagh.html|title=Idris Elba joins Marvel Studios' 'Thor'|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=20 November 2009|access-date=20 November 2009}}</ref>

In June 2012, Elba portrayed Captain Janek in Ridley Scott's ''Prometheus''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ifc.com/2012/09/prometheus-video-idris-elba|title=Prometheus video teases more from Idris Elba's Captain Janek|publisher=ifc.com|date=September 2012|access-date=13 April 2016}}</ref> He joined the cast of the film ''Pacific Rim'' (2013) in the role of Stacker Pentecost.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/archives/its_official_idris_elba_will_star_in_guillermo_del_toros_pacific_rim_bla|title=It's Official! Idris Elba Will Star in Guillermo del Toro's "Pacific Rim" (Black Superhero Next?)|publisher=Blogs.indiewire.com|access-date=28 December 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120710105832/http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/archives/its_official_idris_elba_will_star_in_guillermo_del_toros_pacific_rim_bla|archive-date=10 July 2012}}</ref> He reprised his role as Heimdall in ''[[Thor: The Dark World]]'' in 2013. In January 2014, he confirmed that he would be starring in a film adaptation of ''Luther''.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/tv/tv-news/mandelas-idris-elba-confirms-luther-2984600 |title=Mandela actor Idris Elba confirms Luther film |work=The Mirror |date=3 January 2014}}</ref> In 2014, he starred in ''[[No Good Deed (2014 film)|No Good Deed]]'' as a vengeful psychopathic serial killer.

In 2015, Elba appeared as Heimdall in the superhero blockbuster ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]]'', directed by [[Joss Whedon]]. Elba also starred alongside [[Abraham Attah]] in the film ''[[Beasts of No Nation (film)|Beasts of No Nation]]'' which premiered in select theatres and on [[Netflix]]. He earned a [[Golden Globe Award]] nomination for Best Performance by an Actor in a Supporting Role in any Motion Picture as well as a [[BAFTA Award]] nomination for Supporting Actor in the Film category. In 2016, he had several voice roles: the cape buffalo chief of police, Chief Bogo, in Disney's ''Zootopia'', alongside [[Ginnifer Goodwin]] and [[Jason Bateman]]; villainous tiger [[Shere Khan]] in ''[[The Jungle Book (2016 film)|The Jungle Book]]'' (2016), a live-action adaptation of [[The Jungle Book (1967 film)|the animated 1967 film of the same name]], directed by [[Jon Favreau]]; and sea lion Fluke in [[Pixar]]'s ''[[Finding Dory]]'', alongside [[Ellen DeGeneres]] and [[Albert Brooks]], both reprising their roles from ''[[Finding Nemo]]'' (2003).
