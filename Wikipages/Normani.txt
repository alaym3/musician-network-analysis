'''Normani Kordei Hamilton''' ({{IPAc-en|n|O:|r|'|m|a:|n|i}} {{respell|nor|MAH|nee}}; born May 31, 1996)<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7386173/fifth-harmony-sing-happy-birthday-normani-hamilton-facebook-live |title=Watch Fifth Harmony Sing Happy Birthday to Normani Hamilton |date=May 31, 2016 |work=Billboard |access-date=June 13, 2018 |archive-date=June 14, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180614081340/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7386173/fifth-harmony-sing-happy-birthday-normani-hamilton-facebook-live |url-status=live }}</ref> is an American singer and dancer. <!-- lead intro is for occupations the subject is notable for per reliable sources. Do not add "songwriter", "model" or other occupations without citing secondary reliable sources showing she is NOTABLE for them--> She auditioned as a solo act for the American television series ''[[The X Factor (American TV series)|The X Factor]]'' in 2012, after which she became a member of the girl group [[Fifth Harmony]]. While in Fifth Harmony, Normani competed in ''[[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 24)|Dancing with the Stars]]'' (2017) and released her first song outside of the group "[[Love Lies (song)|Love Lies]]" (2018), a duet with [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]], which peaked in the top ten in the US and was certified 5x platinum by [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]. 

Following Fifth Harmony's indefinite hiatus, Normani released a two-song [[extended play|EP]] with [[Calvin Harris]], ''[[Normani x Calvin Harris]]'' (2018). That same year, she released the single "[[Waves (Normani song)|Waves]]" featuring [[6lack]]. Her duet with [[Sam Smith]], "[[Dancing with a Stranger]]" (2019), reached the top ten in the UK and US. In August 2019, Normani released her first solo single, "[[Motivation (Normani song)|Motivation]]". The following year, Normani released "[[Diamonds (Megan Thee Stallion and Normani song)|Diamonds]]" with [[Megan Thee Stallion]] for ''[[Birds of Prey: The Album]]''. In July 2021, Normani released the lead single from her upcoming untitled debut solo album, "[[Wild Side (Normani song)|Wild Side]]", featuring [[Cardi B]], which reached the top 20 in the US.

==Career==

===2012–2018: ''The X Factor'' and Fifth Harmony ===
{{further|Fifth Harmony}}
[[File:Normani Kordei performing in 2013 (cropped).jpeg|thumbnail|left|150px|Normani performing at an [[iHeartRadio]] event in 2013 as a member of [[Fifth Harmony]]]]

Normani auditioned for the second season of ''[[The X Factor USA]]'' in 2012 with "[[Chain of Fools]]" by [[Aretha Franklin]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.teen.com/2015/05/21/music/fifth-harmony-x-factor-audition-videos |title=The Truth Behind Fifth Harmony's Auditions on The X Factor |work=teen.com |first=Kaitlin |last=Cubria |date=May 24, 2015 |access-date=February 28, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170511055317/http://www.teen.com/2015/05/21/music/fifth-harmony-x-factor-audition-videos/ |archive-date=May 11, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She advanced to the next round after getting approval from all four of the judges. It was during the second round of boot camp that led to her elimination.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2016/06/fifth-harmony |title=Fifth Harmony |work=Wonderland Magazine |first=Dean |last=Kissick |date=June 5, 2016 |access-date=February 28, 2017 |archive-date=December 28, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161228153441/http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2016/06/fifth-harmony/ |url-status=live }}</ref> That marked the end of Normani's ''X Factor'' participation as a solo artist. However, she was brought back to the show and put into a group with four other girls, [[Ally Brooke]], [[Lauren Jauregui]], [[Dinah Jane]], and [[Camila Cabello]], who would later become [[Fifth Harmony]].<ref name="auto">{{cite news |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2911257/from-x-factor-to-727-relive-fifth-harmonys-biggest-moments |title=From X Factor To 7/27, Relive Fifth Harmony's Biggest Moments |publisher=MTV |first=Madeline |last=Roth |date=July 27, 2016 |access-date=February 28, 2017 |archive-date=March 30, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190330101506/http://www.mtv.com/news/2911257/from-x-factor-to-727-relive-fifth-harmonys-biggest-moments/ |url-status=live }}</ref> At the end of the show's season, the group managed to make it to the finale and finished in third place.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/people/2012/12/20/x-factor-crowns-season-2-winner/1783261 |title='X Factor' crowns Season 2 winner |work=[[USA Today]] |first=Ann |last=Oldenburg |date=December 20, 2012 |access-date=February 28, 2017 |archive-date=June 13, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180613043451/https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/people/2012/12/20/x-factor-crowns-season-2-winner/1783261/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In October 2013, Fifth Harmony released their first EP, ''[[Better Together (EP)|Better Together]]''. The group released their debut album, ''[[Reflection (Fifth Harmony album)|Reflection]]'', in 2015, and their second album, ''[[7/27]]'', in 2016. The latter two albums spawned the hit singles "[[Worth It (Fifth Harmony song)|Worth It]]" and "[[Work from Home (song)|Work from Home]]", respectively.<ref name="auto"/> After Cabello's departure from the group in December 2016, Fifth Harmony released their [[Fifth Harmony (album)|self-titled album]] as a quartet in August 2017.<ref>{{cite news
