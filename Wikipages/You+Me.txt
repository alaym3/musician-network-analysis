'''You+Me''' is a Canadian-American [[folk music]] duo consisting of singers and songwriters [[Dallas Green (musician)|Dallas Green]], better known as City and Colour, and [[Pink (singer)|Alecia Moore]], better known as Pink.<ref name="ym-digitalspy-sep2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a595380/pink-teams-up-with-dallas-green-unveils-first-track-from-youme-project.html|title=Pink teams up with Dallas Green, unveils first track from You+Me project|work=[[Digital Spy]]|date=September 8, 2014|access-date=September 13, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141014002943/http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a595380/pink-teams-up-with-dallas-green-unveils-first-track-from-youme-project.html|archive-date=October 14, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="ym-chartcomp-sep2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/pink-forms-duo-youme-debuts-new-song-3105/|title=Pink forms duo You+Me, debuts new song|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|date=September 8, 2014|access-date=September 13, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141015073946/http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/pink-forms-duo-youme-debuts-new-song-3105/|archive-date=October 15, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="ym-musict-sep2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/9798/20140908/pink-dallas-green-city-and-colour-you-plus-me-new-album-rose-ave-release-date.htm|title=P!nk & Dallas Green of City and Colour Form Folk Duo You+Me, New Album 'rose ave.' Due in October|work=Music Times|date=September 8, 2014|access-date=September 13, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006090218/http://www.musictimes.com/articles/9798/20140908/pink-dallas-green-city-and-colour-you-plus-me-new-album-rose-ave-release-date.htm|archive-date=October 6, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> Their debut album, entitled ''[[Rose Ave.]]'', was released on October 14, 2014, by [[RCA Records]]. The first promotional single from the album, "You and Me", premiered with a lyric video on their [[Vevo]] channel on September 8, 2014.<ref name="ym-rca-sep2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.rcarecords.com/news/alecia-moore-pnk-dallas-green-city-and-colour-team-new-album-rose-ave|title=Alecia Moore (P!nk) & Dallas Green (City and Colour) team up for new album 'rose ave.'|publisher=RCA Records|date=September 8, 2014|access-date=September 13, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140908201841/http://www.rcarecords.com/news/alecia-moore-pnk-dallas-green-city-and-colour-team-new-album-rose-ave|archive-date=September 8, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

==History==
===Background===
Moore and Green met through mutual friends several years prior to their collaboration in 2014.<ref name="ym-ew-oct2014">{{cite web|url=http://music-mix.ew.com/2014/10/16/pink-dallas-green-rose-avenue/|title=Pink and Dallas Green talk their new collaboration, 'You + Me'|publisher=Entertainment Weekly|date=October 16, 2014|access-date=October 21, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141019235350/http://music-mix.ew.com/2014/10/16/pink-dallas-green-rose-avenue/|archive-date=October 19, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> The first time they sang together Green had asked Moore to sing with him at one of his shows.<ref name="ym-npr-oct2014">{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2014/10/11/354821424/a-pop-star-and-an-indie-folk-singer-intersect-at-rose-ave|title=A Pop Star And An Indie Folk Singer Intersect At 'Rose Ave.'|publisher=NPR.org|date=October 11, 2014|access-date=October 21, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141011152044/http://www.npr.org/2014/10/11/354821424/a-pop-star-and-an-indie-folk-singer-intersect-at-rose-ave|archive-date=October 11, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> They remained friends and Green opened several shows for Moore in the U.K.<ref name="ym-vulture">{{cite web|url=http://www.vulture.com/2014/10/pink-dallas-green-alecia-moore-you-me.html|title=Alecia Moore and Dallas Green on Their Non-Pink Musical Project and the Horrors of Pineapple Beer|publisher=Vulture|date=October 16, 2014|access-date=October 21, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141020003356/http://www.vulture.com/2014/10/pink-dallas-green-alecia-moore-you-me.html|archive-date=October 20, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> Moore had considered singing acoustic ballads, and as friends the two had considered collaborating for several years. In March 2014, their schedules allowed them to get together and compose original songs.<ref name="ym-time-oct2014">{{cite web|url=https://time.com/3486776/you-and-me-alecia-moore-pink-dallas-green/ |title='It Was Meant to Happen': Alecia Moore and Dallas Green Team Up for You+Me |publisher=Time magazine |date=October 13, 2014 |access-date=October 13, 2014 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6TJ7I9NL2?url=http://time.com/3486776/you-and-me-alecia-moore-pink-dallas-green/ |archive-date=October 14, 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> They had written musical nuggets in advance, and their debut album ''[[Rose Ave. (album)|Rose Ave.]]'' took shape within a week.<ref name="ym-vulture" /><ref name="ym-time-oct2014" /> The artists attributed their quick progress to the freedom from usual production pressures and the joy of making music together.<ref name="ym-time-oct2014" /><ref name="ym-ap-oct2014">{{cite news|url=http://bigstory.ap.org/article/e8ca00fc91da452299354bd9eb40445e/not-pink-alecia-moores-new-music-youme|title=Not Pink: Alecia Moore's new music is with You+Me |agency=Associated Press|date=October 14, 2014|access-date=November 6, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141107034242/http://bigstory.ap.org/article/e8ca00fc91da452299354bd9eb40445e/not-pink-alecia-moores-new-music-youme|archive-date=November 7, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> The name of the duo, You+Me, is based on the two friends wanting to sing together.<ref name="ym-time-oct2014" />

Green recalls how the collaboration came about: "Alecia and I had talked about (working together) for a long time — not about making a record, but the idea of singing together and trying to write a song. (...) When we had the time to do it, that really lit a fire of creativity because it was something different. The idea of collaborating with a female voice and a different writer was what I needed, I guess, to create a bunch of new songs."<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.680news.com/2016/06/07/qa-city-and-colours-dallas-green-on-the-hips-influence-working-with-pink/|title=Q&A: City and Colour's Dallas Green on the Hip's influence, working with Pink|last=Friend|first=David|date=June 7, 2016|work=680 News|via=The Canadian Press|access-date=June 11, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160608124857/http://www.680news.com/2016/06/07/qa-city-and-colours-dallas-green-on-the-hips-influence-working-with-pink/|archive-date=June 8, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

===''Rose Ave.''===
The duo's debut album ''[[Rose Ave.]]'' was released on October 14, 2014. The work was initiated and produced by Moore and Green. They had considered releasing the music independently; however, in order to reach a large audience and for legal concerns the album was released through Moore's label [[RCA Records]].<ref name="ym-guardian-oct28">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/oct/28/pink-alecia-moore-country-duo-you-and-me|title=Pink on You+Me: why I've ditched the acrobatics and leotards to go country|work=The Guardian|date=October 28, 2014|access-date=November 1, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141031103942/http://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/oct/28/pink-alecia-moore-country-duo-you-and-me|archive-date=October 31, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="ym-show2-oct2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2014/10/14/you-me-the-trouble-with-side-projects|title=Pink, You + Me, and the Trouble with Side Projects|publisher=laweekly.com|date=October 14, 2014|access-date=November 1, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141016004845/http://www.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2014/10/14/you-me-the-trouble-with-side-projects|archive-date=October 16, 2014|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The album charted at number one in Canada and at number four in the United States, the home countries of the artists.<ref name="ym-bb-canada">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2014-11-01/canadian-albums|title=Canadian Albums - November 1, 2014|publisher=Billboard Magazine|access-date=November 1, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141031011730/http://www.billboard.com/charts/2014-11-01/canadian-albums|archive-date=October 31, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="ym-bb-us">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2014-11-01/billboard-200|title=Billboard 200 - November 1, 2014|publisher=Billboard Magazine|access-date=November 1, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141027051109/http://www.billboard.com/charts/2014-11-01/billboard-200|archive-date=October 27, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Possible Second Album ===
In an interview from June 2021, Green stated that he had recently written "almost a whole new record" for You+Me.<ref>{{Citation|title=Notes To Spare (Episode 1) - City and Colour|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6FKmX0RPH8A|language=en|access-date=2021-06-16}}</ref>
