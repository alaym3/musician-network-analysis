{{Nihongo|'''Rina Sawayama'''|„É™„Éä„Éª„Çµ„ÉØ„É§„Éû||born 16 August 1990}} is a [[Japanese people in the United Kingdom|Japanese-British]]<ref name=":7"/> singer-songwriter, model, and actress based in [[London]]. In 2017, she [[Self-publishing|self-released]] her debut extended play, ''[[Rina (EP)|Rina]]''. After signing to [[Dirty Hit]] in 2020, she released her debut studio album, ''[[Sawayama]]'', to widespread critical acclaim.

==Early life==
Sawayama was born on 16 August 1990<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/rinasawayama|title=Rina Sawayama's Twitter profile|website=Twitter.com|access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref> in [[Niigata Prefecture|Niigata]]<!-- Do not change link to "Niigata (city)" without a reliable source. The current cited source, https://vibesofsilence.com/introducing-rina-sawayama, just says "Niigata", which seems to imply the prefecture rather than the city (although it probably does not refer to the Niigata area Mitsuke City specifically), but even were this not the case it would still be ambiguous. We know that she was born somewhere in Niigata Prefecture, so that is what we should say. -->, Japan.<ref name="VoS">{{cite web |url=https://vibesofsilence.com/introducing-rina-sawayama |title=Introducing: Rina Sawayama |work=Vibes of Silence |date=4 November 2017 |access-date=18 November 2017 |archive-date=19 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180619035731/https://vibesofsilence.com/introducing-rina-sawayama/ |url-status=live}}</ref> She lived there until the age of five, when her family decided to move to London, where she was raised and currently resides.<ref name="Vogue">{{cite news |url=http://www.vogue.com/article/rina-sawayama-model-singer-japanese-beauty-standards-orange-hair |title=Meet the Tangerine-Haired Model and Singer Who's Taking On Asian Beauty Standards |last=Kim |first=Monica |date=17 October 2016 |magazine=Vogue |access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> She holds an [[indefinite leave to remain]] (ILR) visa in the UK.<ref name=":7"/>

While studying politics, psychology and [[sociology]] at [[Magdalene College, Cambridge]],<ref name="Pixelated People">{{cite web |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/pixelated-people-rina-sawayama-interviewed |title=Pixelated People: Rina Sawayama Interviewed|date=8 February 2018 |access-date=19 April 2020 |archive-date=9 May 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200509174702/https://www.clashmusic.com/features/pixelated-people-rina-sawayama-interviewed |url-status=live}}</ref> Sawayama decided to pursue music and modelling. During her time at university, she performed in a [[hip hop music|hip-hop]] group called Lazy Lion with Theo Ellis from the [[indie rock]] band [[Wolf Alice]].<ref name="VoS"/> She graduated from the university with a degree in [[political science]]. In May 2020, Sawayama said that she was doing an online course at the [[University of Oxford]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=20 May 2020 |title=Welcome to Rina's world: Rina Sawayama on Charli XCX, Cambridge and her pop icons |url=https://www.thelovemagazine.co.uk/article/welcome-to-rinas-world-rina-sawayama-on-charli-xcx-cambridge-and-her-pop-icons |access-date=19 August 2020 |website=LOVE |archive-date=21 August 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200821041623/https://www.thelovemagazine.co.uk/article/welcome-to-rinas-world-rina-sawayama-on-charli-xcx-cambridge-and-her-pop-icons |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
===2013‚Äì2017: Early career===
Sawayama began her solo career in February 2013, with the single "Sleeping in Waking", produced by Justin "Hoost" Tailor. A 7-inch vinyl of the single, featuring a new song titled "Who?" as the B-side, was released in April 2013 through UK label Make Mine.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/makemine/posts/388898544542143|title=Make Mine - Posts|website=Facebook.com|date=4 April 2021|access-date=3 June 2021|quote=Rina Sawayama joins us for her debut 7"}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://makemine.bandcamp.com/album/sleeping-in-waking|title=Sleeping in Waking - Make Mine|website=Makemine.bandcamp.com|access-date=3 June 2021}}</ref> Later that year, she released the single "Terror" on Halloween under the stage name of "Riina".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://miojoindie.com.br/riina-terror/|title=RIINA: "Terror"|website=Miojo Indie|date=1 November 2013|access-date=3 June 2021|last=Facchi|first=Cleber|language=Portuguese}}</ref> Sawayama would later reveal in a 2021 ''Billboard'' interview that the reason she used only her first name is because her last name was "an inconvenience".<ref name="Billboard2021">{{cite web|title=Rina Sawayama Is Turning Pop Inside Out: Pride 2021 Cover|last=Kuga|first=Mitchell|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/9580420/rina-sawayama-billboard-cover-story-interview-2021/|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=2 June 2021|access-date=3 June 2021}}</ref>

In June 2015, she released a music video, directed by [[Arvida Bystr√∂m]], to her track "Tunnel Vision". In 2016, she released the single "Where U Are", with an accompanying music video co-directed by Alessandra Kurr.<ref name=":1">{{cite web |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/where-u-are-single/1489409855 |title=Where U Are ‚Äì Single by Rina Sawayama on Apple Music |work=iTunes |access-date=29 January 2021}}</ref> The single explored human interaction with digital media, with Sawayama explaining: "Online, you can present your best edited self [and] your overheating phone substitutes human warmth. Weirdest of all ‚Äì you're together, but also very alone."<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.c-heads.com/2016/02/01/rina-sawayama-streams-smooth-new-track-where-u-are/ |title=Rina Sawayama streams smooth new track "Where U Are" ‚Äì C-Heads Magazine |date=1 February 2016 |work=C-Heads Magazine |access-date=13 July 2018 |language=en |archive-date=13 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713171551/http://www.c-heads.com/2016/02/01/rina-sawayama-streams-smooth-new-track-where-u-are/ |url-status=live}}</ref> The song has been described as "nostalgic greatness‚Ä¶ ultra-sweet and shimmering pop",<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/rina-sawayama-s-where-u-are-is-ultra-sweet-145 |title=Rina Sawayama's 'Where U Are' is ultra-sweet |last=http://thefourohfive.com |work=The 405 |access-date=13 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190527044412/https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/rina-sawayama-s-where-u-are-is-ultra-sweet-145 |archive-date=27 May 2019 |url-status=dead}}</ref> and "90s R&B pop perfection".<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_au/article/6azqgj/rina-sawayama-where-u-are-premiere-57a206f40007d1df16fc2f2b |title=Fall for the Smooth Curves of Rina Sawayama's "Where U Are" |date=28 January 2016 |work=Noisey |access-date=13 July 2018 |archive-date=13 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713165511/https://noisey.vice.com/en_au/article/6azqgj/rina-sawayama-where-u-are-premiere-57a206f40007d1df16fc2f2b |url-status=live}}</ref>

===Collaborations===
In 2016, Sawayama collaborated with [[Taiwanese people|Taiwanese]] visual artist, [[John Yuyi]], on a visual series critiquing Asian, including Japanese, beauty standards. Subsequently, she was profiled in ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]''. In the interview, Sawayama described the genesis of the idea and collaboration: "For a lot of women in Japan, these are the expectations people put on them, from [[anime]] culture, [[kawaii]] culture‚Ä¶ that can really put women at a disadvantage, objectifying and infantilising them."<ref name="Vogue" /> Sawayama has worked with [[Nicola Formichetti]] for [[MAC Cosmetics|MAC]] x Nicopanda. Formichetti also directed the music video for Sawayama's track "Ordinary Superstar". The genesis of the partnership was outlined in ''[[i-D]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/a3aj9j/read-rina-sawayama-and-nicola-formichettis-texts-to-each-other|title=read rina sawayama and nicola formichetti's texts to each other|date=5 June 2018|work=I-d|access-date=13 July 2018|language=en-uk|archive-date=13 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713165701/https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/a3aj9j/read-rina-sawayama-and-nicola-formichettis-texts-to-each-other|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In August 2018, Sawayama [[coming out|came out]] during an interview with [[Vice (magazine)|''Broadly'']], declaring: "I've always written songs about girls. I don't think I've ever mentioned a guy in my songs, and that's why I wanted to talk about it." She identified as both [[bisexuality|bisexual]] and [[pansexuality|pansexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://broadly.vice.com/en_us/article/zmkgyx/rina-sawayama-comes-out-pansexual-interview|title=Singer Rina Sawayama Is Ready to Rep Her Pansexuality|last=Tsjeng|first=Zing|website=Broadly|access-date=17 August 2018|archive-date=14 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180814170214/https://broadly.vice.com/en_us/article/zmkgyx/rina-sawayama-comes-out-pansexual-interview|url-status=live}}</ref>
