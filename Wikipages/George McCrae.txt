'''George Warren McCrae, Jr.''' (born October 19, 1944)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/87814-George-McCrae |title=George McCrae Discography |publisher=Discogs.com |date=October 19, 1944 |access-date=August 27, 2015}}</ref><ref name="AMG"/> is an American [[soul music|soul]] and [[disco]] [[singing|singer]], most famous for his 1974 hit "[[Rock Your Baby]]".

==Biography and career==
McCrae was the second of nine children, born in [[West Palm Beach, Florida|West Palm Beach]], [[Florida]].<ref name="AMG"/> He formed his own singing group, the Jivin' Jets, before joining the [[United States Navy]] in 1963.<ref name="GM"/> He married [[Gwen McCrae]] (nÃ©e Mosley) in 1963.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/gwen-mccrae-mn0000651549/biography|title=Gwen McCrae {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2019-07-13}}</ref> Four years later, he re-formed the group, with his wife Gwen joining the line-up, but soon afterwards they decided to work as a duo, recording for [[Henry Stone]]'s Alston record label. Gwen then won a solo contract, with George acting as her manager as well as doing some singing on sessions and in clubs in [[Palm Beach, Florida|Palm Beach]].<ref name=larkin>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_NNmFiUnSmUC&q=%22george+mccrae%22&pg=PA1265 |title=The Encyclopedia of Popular Music - Colin Larkin |date=May 27, 2011 |isbn=9780857125958 |access-date=August 27, 2015|last1=Larkin |first1=Colin }}</ref><ref name=benarde>{{cite web|url=http://www.georgemccrae.com/?p=295 |title=Longing for... - George McCrae - World wide ambassador of Love |publisher=George McCrae |access-date=August 27, 2015}}</ref>

He was about to return to college to study law enforcement, when [[Richard Finch (musician)|Richard Finch]] and [[Harry Wayne Casey]] of [[KC and the Sunshine Band]] invited him to sing the lyrics for a song that they had recorded for the band, but could not reach the high notes that were required for the song. The original intention was that Gwen, his wife, should record it, but she was late for the session and George recorded alone.<ref name=larkin/><ref name=benarde/> It suited his high-pitched voice to the extent that the song, "Rock Your Baby", became one of the first hits of the disco era in 1974, selling an estimated eleven million copies worldwide,<ref name="The Book of Golden Discs">{{cite book

The song was so successful that ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' [[magazine]] voted it the No. 1 song of the year in 1974.<ref name="GM">[http://www.georgemccrae.com/biography.html ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081227052514/http://www.georgemccrae.com/biography.html |date=December 27, 2008 }}</ref> McCrae received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for Best Male R&B Vocalist the following year.

Two further [[single (music)|single]] releases, "I Can't Leave You Alone" and "It's Been So Long" also reached the [[UK Singles Chart]] [[Top 40|Top 10]].<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/George-McCrae-Rock-Your-Baby/release/570981 |title=George McCrae - Rock Your Baby (Vinyl, LP, Album) |publisher=Discogs.com |date=January 5, 2015 |access-date=August 27, 2015}}</ref> He recorded several further albums for TK, including ''George McCrae'' (1975) and ''Diamond Touch'' (1976),<ref name=disco>[http://www.discomuseum.net/BioGeorgeMcCrae.html]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140606205420/http://www.discomuseum.net/BioGeorgeMcCrae.html |date=June 6, 2014 }}</ref> and also continued to record with, and manage, his wife until their divorce in 1976.<ref name="benarde"/> They had two daughters together, Sophia and Leah.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadjam.com/bio/leahmccrae|title=LEAH MCCRAE Bio {{!}} Broadjam.com|website=Broadjam|language=en|access-date=2019-07-13}}</ref><ref name="Arena">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qrLuCwAAQBAJ&q=george+mccrae+shaka&pg=PA162|title=Legends of Disco: Forty Stars Discuss Their Careers|last=Arena|first=James|date=2016-04-06|publisher=McFarland|isbn=9781476624570|language=en}}</ref>

While he continued to record albums including ''We Did It!'' and his second self-titled album ''George McCrae'' (both 1978), his commercial popularity slipped as the decade progressed.<ref name="AMG">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p4866|pure_url=yes}} |title=Biography by Bill Dahl |publisher=Allmusic.com |access-date=February 18, 2009}}</ref> He married a second time, moved to Canada, and entered a period of semi-retirement, leaving TK at the end of the 1970s.<ref name="benarde" /> With his second wife, he had another daughter, Jennifer McCray.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jennifermccray.com/|title=www.jennifermccray.com|website=www.jennifermccray.com|access-date=2019-07-13}}</ref>

He returned with the album ''One Step Closer to Love'' in 1984, the title track from which entered the charts in the United Kingdom, Canada and the Netherlands. In 1988, he had a daughter, Marcella, with his then-girlfriend, Rosanna Molignini.<ref>{{Citation|title=George McCrae|date=2018-12-23|url=https://nl.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=George_McCrae&oldid=52845997|work=Wikipedia|language=nl|access-date=2019-07-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bzk5p1xo1LW/|title=Marcella MCcrae MuSiC ğŸ¶ on Instagram: "No words needed DADDY â¤ï¸ @mccraegeorge ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ Biggie , @snoopdogg nd also @50cent sampled Ur Musik .... Ur such a great Artistâ€¦"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2019-07-13}}</ref> He moved to the village [[Munstergeleen]] in the [[Netherlands]] and remarried again, to [[Dutch people|Dutch]] model Yvonne Bergsma, in 1989.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mariezhuikov.wordpress.com/tag/george-mccrae/|title=George McCrae {{!}} Marie's Meanderings|language=en|access-date=2019-07-13}}</ref> They have a son, Shaka.<ref name="Arena"/>

His later albums found some success in Europe, and he continued to perform regularly there.  By the 2000s, he shared his time between homes in Florida, [[Aruba]], and the Netherlands. In 2016 George McCrae released a new concept album called LOVE that was produced by the Dutch producer / composer Roger Heijster. The album was recorded without sequencing using only vintage instruments. Leah and Sophia McCrae, George's two daughters from his first marriage to Gwen McCrae, provide the backing vocals for this album. The album was album of the week in Germany and the single "Sexy Woman" was #1 in Mallorca. George keeps on performing live and in December 2017 George performed at the BBC Hootenanny show with Jools Holland.<ref name="benarde" /><ref name="disco" />
