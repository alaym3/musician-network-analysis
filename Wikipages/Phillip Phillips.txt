'''Phillip LaDon Phillips Jr.''' (born September 20, 1990)<ref name=birthdate>{{cite web| url=http://www.eonline.com/news/709915/american-idol-winner-phillip-phillips-is-married | title='American Idol' Winner Phillip Phillips Is Married!|first=Natalie|last=Finn|publisher= [[E!]] |date= October 24, 2015|access-date=October 24, 2015}} {{cite web| url=https://instagram.com/p/73HaJXR1Tn/|title=25 never looked so good....|publisher=Hannah Blackwell Instagram account|date= September 20, 2015|access-date=October 25, 2015}} Note: An inaccurate birthdate (Sept. 29, 1990) and birthplace (Leesburg, Georgia) appear in {{cite web| url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/phillip-phillips-mn0002944976/biography|title=Phillip Phillips Biography|first= Matt|last=Collar|publisher=[[AllMusic.com]]|access-date=October 25, 2015}}</ref> is an American musician, singer-songwriter who rose to fame after winning the [[American Idol (season 11)|eleventh season]] of ''[[American Idol]]'' on May 23, 2012. His coronation song, "[[Home (Phillip Phillips song)|Home]]", became the best-selling song in ''American Idol'' history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtvm.com/2020/05/04/national-star-phillip-phillips-will-stage-concert-phoebe/|title=National star Phillip Phillips stages a concert for Phoebe|website=WTVM|access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> His debut album ''[[The World from the Side of the Moon]]'' was released on November 19, 2012 and was on the Billboard Top 200 album chart for 61 weeks.<ref name="mjsbigblog.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mjsbigblog.com/more-billboard-chart-date-06-01-19.htm|title = More Billboard - Chart Date 06/01/19|date = May 31, 2019}}</ref>  It has been certified Platinum by the RIAA.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Phillip+Phillips&ti=The+World+from+the+Side+of+the+Moon&format=Album&type=#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum}}</ref> The album included the single "Gone, Gone, Gone."  He followed up with ''Behind the Light,'' in 2014 with the lead single "Raging Fire." His third album, ''Collateral,'' was released in 2018 and included the songs "Into the Wild" and "Magnetic." Phillips toured with John Mayer, Gavin DeGraw, the Goo Goo Dolls and has shared the stage with Bruce Springsteen.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://patch.com/connecticut/ridgefield/calendar/event/20210807/1093966/phillip-phillips|title = Local Event: Phillip Phillips|date = July 13, 2021}}</ref>

In the United States, Phillips has sold 7.5 million digital singles and has sold 2.7 million albums including album streaming equivalent units.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/tv/2014/01/14/tv-singing-shows-stars-story/4478095/|title=TV singing shows fail to create stars}}</ref>  He has over 580 million streams on Spotify<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/6p5JxpTc7USNnBnLzctyd4|title=Phillip Phillips}}</ref> and 1.2 billion streams on Pandora.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nextbigsound.com/profile/337036|title = Next Big Sound}}</ref>

Phillips started playing music, particularly the guitar,<ref name="Bibliography">{{cite web|title=Bibliography|url=http://www.phillipphillips.com/biography|publisher=Interscope Records|access-date=12 March 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140227090656/http://www.phillipphillips.com/biography|archive-date=February 27, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref> when he was 14. He was mentored by his long-time friend and brother-in-law, Benjamin Neil, whom he credits for piquing his interest in music.<ref name="albanyuncovered">{{cite web |url=http://www.albanyuncovered.com/phillip-phillips/ |title=Phillip Phillips has hit the local the music scene like a rocket! |publisher=Albany uncovered |date=September 13, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121008235254/http://www.albanyuncovered.com/phillip-phillips/ |archive-date=October 8, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> In 2009, he formed the Phillip Phillips Band with Neil and another brother-in-law, Todd Urick, and performed in various local venues and events.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thealbanyjournal.com/2011/04/albany-panther-announce-tailgate/ |title=Albany Panther Announce Tailgate |publisher=The Albany Journal |date=April 29, 2011 |access-date=June 10, 2012 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130204045602/http://thealbanyjournal.com/2011/04/albany-panther-announce-tailgate/ |archive-date=February 4, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He won a local singing competition, "Albany Star," in 2010.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mysouthwestga.com/news/story.aspx?id=620780 |title=Albany Star holds round one of competition Saturday |publisher=Fox31online |first=Ashley |last=Knight |date=May 21, 2011 |access-date=June 10, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140312225237/http://www.mysouthwestga.com/news/story.aspx?id=620780 |archive-date=March 12, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Phillips' favorite singer is [[Jonny Lang]]. Other favorites include [[John Butler (musician)|John Butler]], [[Dave Matthews]], and [[Damien Rice]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/video/phillip-phillips-american-idol-297243 |title=Phillip Phillips 'American Idol' |first= Portia|last= Medina |publisher=The Hollywood Reporter |date=May 23, 2012 |access-date=May 26, 2012 }}</ref> He also enjoyed [[Mumford & Sons]] and [[Tool (band)|Tool]].<ref name="albanyuncovered"/> He grew up listening mainly to '60s and '70s music such as [[The Jimi Hendrix Experience|Jimi Hendrix]] and [[Led Zeppelin]];<ref name="Bibliography"/> "Then as I got older I found Damien Rice and Dave Matthews and John Butler, which is what really set it off for me in terms of finding my voice as a musician."<ref name="Bibliography"/>  He has described his music as "jazz and rock alternative sound."<ref name="THR">{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/idol-worship/american-idol-phillip-phillips-home-single-329202 |title ='American Idol' Champ Phillip Phillips on 'Home': 'I Told Them It's Not My Single' (Video) |first1=Michele Amabile|last1=Angermiller|first2=Didi |last2=Benami |work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=May 22, 2012 |access-date=May 26, 2012 }}</ref>

Prior to trying out for ''American Idol'', Phillips also auditioned on the second season of ''[[America's Got Talent]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.accesshollywood.com/idol-finalists-jessica-sanchez-and-phillip-phillips-dish-on-their-americas-got-talent-past_article_65162 |title=Idol Finalists Jessica Sanchez & Phillip Phillips Dish On Their America's Got Talent Past |first=Erin |last=O'Sullivan |work=[[Access Hollywood]] |date=May 23, 2012 }}</ref>

His performance style on the show has been compared to [[Dave Matthews]], and he covered one of his songs, "[[The Stone (Dave Matthews Band song)|The Stone]]," in the competition.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://davematthewsblog.com/phillip-phillips-dave-matthews-mannerisms-on-american-idol/ |title=Phillip Phillips Dave Matthews Mannerisms on American Idol |publisher=Dave Matthews blog }}</ref> When asked about Phillips' imitation of his style, Dave Matthews said: "More power to him, I don't mind," and added "He should kick my ass, [then] maybe I can retire and he can take over my band."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vulture.com/2012/05/dave-matthews-phillip-phillips-american-idol.html |title=Dave Matthews: Idol's Phillip Phillips Should  |first=Meg |last=Miller |publisher=Vulture |date=May 2, 2012 }}</ref> Mentor Stevie Nicks said Phillips would have been good enough to join Fleetwood Mac back in 1975, after his performance of Jonny Lang's "Still Rainin," for which he received a standing ovation from the judges.

For his Top 3 performance, Phillips sang Bob Seger's "We've Got Tonight." For Top 4, he did a cover of Damien Rice's "Volcano" - which has been heralded as one of the best American Idol performances of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elle.com/culture/movies-tv/videos/a33095/the-10-best-american-idol-moments/|title=The 10 Best Moments in 'American Idol' History|date=April 7, 2016|website=Elle.com|access-date=December 23, 2017}}</ref> As the sole contestant who was never in jeopardy of elimination in any week of the competition, Phillips became the winner on the finale against [[Jessica Sanchez]] after a record-breaking 132 million votes were cast.<ref name="beautymania1">{{cite web|url=http://www.beautymania.biz/2012/05/entertainment-paradise-american-idol_23.html |title=Entertainment Paradise:American Idol season 11 Winner is Phillip Phillips! |publisher=Beautymania.biz |date=May 23, 2012 |access-date=May 23, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/movies/news/phillip-phillips-wins-american-idol-20120523 |title=Phillip Phillips Wins 'American Idol' |publisher=Rolling Stone |date=May 23, 2012 |access-date=May 23, 2012}}</ref> His coronation song, "[[Home (Phillip Phillips song)|Home]]," was released after his performance, and had the biggest digital sales week for any ''Idol'' winner's coronation song.<ref name="cauldfied">{{cite web|title=Phillip Phillips Makes 'Idol' History as 'Home' Sells 278,000 Downloads |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/485449/phillip-phillips-storms-hot-100s-top-10-gotye-has-years-longest-running-no-1 |last=Caulfield |first=Keith |publisher=Billboard |date=May 30, 2012 |access-date=May 30, 2012}}</ref><ref name="billboard_cover">{{cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1481573/phillip-phillips-the-billboard-cover-story|title= 'Phillip Phillips' The Billboard Cover Story |first=Phil |last=Gallo |publisher=Billboard|date= January 7, 2013 |access-date=January 8, 2013 }}</ref>

Phillips has suffered from a congenital kidney condition.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1686861/phillip-phillips-kidney-surgery-american-idol.jhtml |title=Phillip Phillips Has Kidney Surgery |first=Gil|last= Kaufman |publisher=MTV|date=June 7, 2012}}</ref>  Soon after ''American Idol'' finished, he had the [[kidney stone]]s removed as well as reconstructive surgery done on his kidney.<ref>{{cite news |title=Phillip Phillips Reveals Details of Year-Long Kidney Crisis |url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20607359,00.html |first=Marisa |last=Laudadio |date= July 1, 2012 |work=People|access-date= July 3, 2012 }}</ref>

On December 26, 2014, Phillips announced his engagement to girlfriend Hannah Blackwell.<ref>{{cite news |title='American Idol' Winner Phillip Phillips Gets Engaged |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/idol-worship/american-idol-winner-phillip-phillips-760402 |first=Michele Amabile |last=Angermiller |date= December 26, 2014 |publisher=The Hollywood Reporter }}</ref>  The two were married at the Resora Plantation in [[Albany, Georgia]] on October 24, 2015.<ref>{{cite news |title=Phillip Phillips Weds Longtime Girlfriend Hannah Blackwell |url=http://www.people.com/article/Phillip-Phillips-Weds-Hannah-Blackwell |first=Melody |last=Chiu |date= October 24, 2015 |work=People }}</ref>

On July 3, 2019, Phillips and Blackwell announced that they were expecting their first child, a son, in the fall.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/entertainment/music/phillip-phillips-announces-that-he-will-become-a-father/article/553323 |title=Phillip Phillips announces that he will become a father |website=Digitaljournal.com |date=2019-07-03 |access-date=2020-04-08}}</ref> Patch Shepherd Phillips was born November 10, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://people.com/parents/phillip-phillips-welcomes-son/ |title= Phillip Phillips and Wife Hannah Welcome First Child, a Son |work=People |date=November 14, 2019 }}</ref>

In addition to his singing career he has supported organizations including the Sarah Foundation and the Gibson Flood Relief Campaign.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cameron|first1=Andrew|title=Mr|url=http://www.gibson.com/News-Lifestyle/Features/en-us/gibson-foundation-0512.aspx|website=Gibson.com|access-date=2016-01-09}}</ref> as well being an ambassador for Brave Beginnings.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bravebeginnings.org/platinum-recording-artist-phillip-phillips-named-brave-beginnings-ambassador-featured-in-nationwide-psa/|title=Platinum Recording Artist Phillip Phillips Named Brave Beginnings Ambassador; Featured in Nationwide PSA - Brave Beginnings|date=October 7, 2016|website=Bravebeginnings.org|access-date=December 23, 2017}}</ref>  He has also supported DoSomething, National Park Foundation, NCIRE - the Veterans Health Research Institute, Save The Music Foundation, and Habitat for Humanity.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.looktothestars.org/celebrity/phillip-phillips|title=Phillip Phillips: Charity Work & Causes|website=Looktothestars.org|access-date=December 23, 2017}}</ref>
