'''Arizona Zervas''' (born April 19, 1995) is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is widely known for his song "[[Roxanne (Arizona Zervas song)|Roxanne]]", which reached number four on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in 2019, after being prominently featured in [[Spotify]] playlists.<ref name="bb">[https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/record-labels/8543764/roxanne-arizona-zervas-columbia-records-deal 'Roxanne' Singer Arizona Zervas Signs With Columbia Records]. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', November 15, 2019.</ref> The success of the single led him to tour nationwide in 2019, and in November of that same year he partnered with [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="bb" /> "Roxanne" also topped the charts in New Zealand, and reached the top 10 in the UK, Australia, and several other nations.

==Career==
[[File:ArizonaZervasLogo.jpg|thumb|Arizona Zervas' logo]]
Zervas released his first song in the eleventh grade.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://arizonazervas.com/bio|title=Arizona Zervas â€” Bio|website=Arizona Zervas|access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref> His debut single "Don't Hit My Line" was released in 2016.<ref name="Independent">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8543673/roxanne-arizona-zervas-spotify-unsigned-independent|title=The First Artist in Years to Top Spotify Without Label Support|last=Cirisano|first=Tatiana|date=November 14, 2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref> This was followed up by the release of more than 30 singles - with nearly every song totaling 1 million streams on [[Spotify]]. A debut three-song [[Extended play|EP]] ''Living Facts'' was released in 2018.<ref name="Independent" />

Zervas released his first charting song "[[Roxanne (Arizona Zervas song)|Roxanne]]" on October 10, 2019. It quickly became a [[Viral video|viral]] hit.<ref name="Independent" /> The song reached number 1 on Spotify's United States Top 50 chart on November 8, becoming the first track by an unsigned, fully independent artist to top the chart.<ref name="Independent" /> The song subsequently debuted at number 34 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8543356/arizona-zervas-roxanne-five-burning-questions|title=Five Burning Questions: Billboard Staffers Discuss Arizona Zervas' Viral Smash 'Roxanne'|website=Billboard|access-date=December 16, 2019}}</ref> as well as debuting at number 24 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and reached number 1 in New Zealand, becoming his first number one on any national chart. The song sparked a bidding war among labels, and on November 15, 2019, it was reported that Zervas had signed with [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="bb" /> This marks Columbia's second major signing of a viral star in 2019 following the label's deal with [[Lil Nas X]], thanks to his breakout hit "[[Old Town Road]]".<ref name="bb" />

According to ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine, Zervas has performed more than 50 shows across the United States, including at New York's [[Webster Hall]].<ref name="Independent" />

On May 8, 2020, Zervas released his single "24". It is the follow-up to his hit single "Roxanne".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/2vkLpvnUXzuuo2kRjpgJvP?si=7rqG-v4UR-C6vCe0AH4WcQ|title=24 - Arizona Zervas|website=[[Spotify]]|access-date=May 11, 2020}}</ref> He then released his single "RIP" on September 25, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=RIP - Arizona Zervas|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/3bhKNe0TUHPXXYK9PtSA1Y?si=RY2M3JaQRkyRCK6p_n9l2g|access-date=January 21, 2021|website=[[Spotify]]}}</ref>
