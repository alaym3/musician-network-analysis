'''The Band Perry''' is a music group composed of siblings Kimberly Perry (lead vocals, guitar), Reid Perry (bass guitar, [[background vocals]]), and Neil Perry ([[mandolin]], [[bouzouki]], background vocals). They signed to [[BMLG Records|Republic Nashville]] in August 2009 and released their [[The Band Perry (album)|self-titled]] debut album on October 12, 2010. From this album, "[[If I Die Young]]" reached number one on the U.S. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Adult Contemporary (chart)|Hot Adult Contemporary Tracks]] charts and has been certified 6x multi-platinum.<ref name="charthistory">{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/382918/band-perry/chart |title=The Band Perry Chart History |website=Billboard |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=THE+BAND+PERRY |title=The Band Perry |date=29 January 2015 |website=[[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref> Their second album, ''[[Pioneer (The Band Perry album)|Pioneer]]'', was released April 2, 2013. It produced additional number one singles in "[[Better Dig Two]]" and "[[Done (The Band Perry song)|Done]]", plus the top 10 hits "[[Don't Let Me Be Lonely]]" and "[[Chainsaw (The Band Perry song)|Chainsaw]]". The band transitioned to pop in 2017 and began to release music independently in 2018.

==Career==
===2005–09: Formation and early career===
[[File:The Band Perry 2013.jpg|200px|thumb|right|The Band Perry in 2013.]]
Kimberly Perry sang in her own band as a teenager with her brothers Neil and Reid working as roadies.<ref name=sauro>{{Cite web |url=http://www.recordnet.com/article/20150806/ENTERTAINMENTLIFE/150809807 |title=Family ties keep The Band Perry on road to success |last=Sauro |first=Tony |date=6 August 2015 |publisher=Recordnet.com |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref> At ages 8 and 10, the brothers began performing as an opening act, the Mobile Music Machines, for Kimberly.<ref name=sauro /> Kimberly released an EP entitled "Carry On" with a song of the same name inspiring two songs on "Pioneer". Once they had united to form as "the Band Perry", they joined a New Faces of Country tour in 2005.<ref name="allmusic">{{Cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p2075800/biography|pure_url=yes}} |title=The Band Perry biography |last=Leggett |first=Steve |publisher=Allmusic |access-date=July 19, 2010}}</ref><ref name=sauro /><ref>{{cite news|last=Cox|first=Joanie|title=The Band Perry's sibling harmony|url=http://www.sun-sentinel.com/citylink/sfl-the-band-perrys-sibling-harmon-01262011,0,2592823.story|access-date=2012-08-25|newspaper=[[South Florida Sun-Sentinel]]|date=2011-01-25}}</ref>

In 2008, they were discovered by [[Garth Brooks]]' manager Bob Doyle, who helped them make recordings that were sent to [[Scott Borchetta]], head of the newly established [[Republic Nashville]] label. They signed to Republic Nashville in August 2009 and released their debut single "[[Hip to My Heart]]".<ref name="sauro" /><ref name="musicrow">{{Cite web |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2009/08/republic-nashville-signs-the-band-perry/ |title=Republic Nashville Signs The Band Perry |last=Ross |first=David M. |date=August 17, 2009 |website=Music Row |access-date=July 19, 2010}}</ref> All three members wrote it with [[Brett Beavers]]. The song peaked at No. 20 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country]] chart.<ref name="charthistory" /> A self-titled [[extended play]] followed in April.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1634640/the-band-perry-releasing-digital-ep-on-april-6.jhtml|title=The Band Perry Releasing Digital EP on April 6|date=March 24, 2010|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]|access-date=July 19, 2010}}</ref>

===2010–14: ''The Band Perry'' and ''Pioneer''===
After "Hip to My Heart" fell from the charts, the Band Perry released their second single, "[[If I Die Young]]", written by Kimberly. "If I Die Young" and "Hip to My Heart" are on the band's self-titled debut album, released on October 12, 2010. The album was produced by [[Paul Worley]] and [[Nathan Chapman (record producer)|Nathan Chapman]].<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="musicrow"/> "If I Die Young" reached No. 1 on the country music chart, and No. 14 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="charthistory" /> The album's third single, "[[You Lie (The Band Perry song)|You Lie]]", was written by Brian Henningsen, Aaron Henningsen, and Clara Henningsen. It debuted in December 2010 and hit No. 2 in 2011. After it, "[[All Your Life]]" reached number 1 in February 2012, and "[[Postcard from Paris]]" reached number 6. The album sold 1.6 million copies.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7368134/the-band-perry-signs-interscope-records |title=The Band Perry Signs New Label Deal: Source |last=Newman |first=Melinda |date=12 May 2016 |website=Billboard |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref>

In mid-2012, the band began working with producer [[Rick Rubin]] on their second album, ''[[Pioneer (The Band Perry album)|Pioneer]]'',<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/482917/band-perry-writing-like-crazy-for-rick-rubin-helmed-2nd-album |title=Band Perry 'Writing Like Crazy' for Rick Rubin-Helmed 2nd Album |last=Graff |first=Gary |date=19 June 2012 |publisher=Billboard |access-date=17 May 2013}}</ref> which was released on April 2, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/review/1555833/the-band-perry-pioneer-track-by-track-review |title=The Band Perry, 'Pioneer': Track-by-Track Review |last=Dauphin |first=Chuck |date=2 April 2013 |publisher=Billboard |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://theboot.com/the-band-perry-pioneer-album-preview/ |title=The Band Perry, 'Pioneer' Album Previewed at Nashville Listening Party |last=Betts |first=Stephen L. |date=26 March 2013 |website=The Boot |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref> The album's first single, "[[Better Dig Two]]", was released to country radio on October 30, 2012. The album's second single, "[[Done (The Band Perry song)|Done]]", charted in March 2013 ahead of its release date, reaching number 8 on the [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country]] chart.<ref name="charthistory" /> It reached number 1 on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in August 2013.<ref name="charthistory" /> ''Pioneer'' ended up being produced by [[Dann Huff]] and became the group's first number one on the [[Top Country Albums]] chart in addition to reaching the number two spot on the all-genre [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="charthistory" /> The album's third single, "[[Don't Let Me Be Lonely]]", was released to country radio on August 26, 2013. It reached number 2 on the Country Airplay chart in February 2014.<ref name="charthistory" /> The album's fourth single, "[[Chainsaw (The Band Perry song)|Chainsaw]]", which was originally recorded by the group [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]], was released to country radio on March 3, 2014. The song peaked at number 10 on Country Airplay.<ref name="charthistory" /> Later that year, they recorded a cover of the [[Glen Campbell]] hit, "[[Gentle on My Mind (song)|Gentle on My Mind]]", for the documentary about the singer titled ''[[Glen Campbell: I'll Be Me]]'', which was released as a standalone single. The band performed in the pre-game show of [[Super Bowl XLVIII]], on Sunday, February 2, 2014.

===2015–2017: Label change, ''My Bad Imagination'' and transition to pop===
On August 14, 2015, the band released their new single called "[[Live Forever (The Band Perry song)|Live Forever]]", the first single off their upcoming third studio album. One of the musicians they collaborated with on "Live Forever" is pop producer [[RedOne]] ([[Lady Gaga]], [[Enrique Iglesias]]). The music video for it premiered on August 15, 2015 on CMT. On March 1, 2016, the Band Perry parted ways with their record label, [[Republic Nashville]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6897188/the-band-perry-part-ways-with-big-machine |title=The Band Perry Part Ways With Big Machine |last1=Stark |first1=Phyllis |last2=Aswad |first2=Jem |date=March 1, 2016 |website=Billboard}}</ref> [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] announced on May 12, 2016, that the band had signed with [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="Billboard Interscope"/> According to the magazine, the Band Perry is transitioning to [[pop music]] but will continue releasing select singles to the country format through sister label [[Universal Music Group|UMG Nashville]].<ref name="ToC Interscope">{{Cite news |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/the-band-perry-interscope-records-pop/ |title=The Band Perry 'Transitioning to Pop' With New Interscope Deal |last=Whitaker |first=Sterling |date=12 May 2016 |access-date=13 May 2016 |publisher=[[Townsquare Media]] |work=Taste of Country}}</ref> Their third studio album, tentatively titled ''Heart + Beat'', was to have included material recorded while the trio was signed to Republic Nashville (including "Live Forever") as well as new songs.<ref name="ToC Interscope"/> The band later shot down rumors that they were "going pop". The Band Perry stated that the main reason they signed with Interscope was so that their music could be distributed to other formats of radio, besides country.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/the-band-perry-not-going-pop/ |title=The Band Perry Are Not 'Going Pop' |last=Dukes |first=Billy |date=1 August 2016 |website=Taste of Country |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref> In July 2016, Neil, Reid, and Kimberly appeared on ''[[Celebrity Family Feud]]'', where Kimberly scored 196 points in the Fast Money round, which host Steve Harvey said might be the record.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/the-band-perry-kimberly-perry-impresses-celebrity-family-feud/|title=The Band Perry's Kimberly Perry's Amazing Family Feud Round|last=Reuter|first=Annie|website=Taste of Country|language=en|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref> The Band Perry released their next single, "Comeback Kid", to country radio on August 1, 2016, through [[Mercury Nashville]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cantor|first1=Brian|title=The Band Perry's New Single "Comeback Kid" Impacts Country Radio On August 1|url=http://headlineplanet.com/home/2016/07/25/the-band-perrys-new-single-comeback-kid-impacts-country-radio-on-august-1/|access-date=26 July 2016|work=Headline Planet|publisher=Cantortainment|date=July 25, 2016}}</ref>

On February 2, 2017, the band announced the title of their third album, ''My Bad Imagination''. Unlike their previous two albums, this one would be pop. They said, "Creating it has been the most exciting thing we've ever done."<ref name="Pop Album">{{Cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1776548/the-band-perry-confirm-first-pop-album/ |title=The Band Perry Confirm First Pop Album |last=Tingle |first=Lauren |date=February 3, 2017 |website=CMT |publisher=Viacom}}</ref> Another pop single, "Stay in the Dark", followed in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/band-perry-plan-pop-album-share-new-song-stay-in-the-dark-116839/|title=Band Perry Plan Pop Album, Share New Song 'Stay in the Dark'|last1=McKenna|first1=Brittney|date=2017-02-03|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref> The album was subsequently confirmed a year later to have been scrapped.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/band-perry-country-pop-interview-new-music-758788/|title=The Band Perry on Reinvention and the Electronic Pop of New EP 'Coordinates'|last1=Bernstein|first1=Jonathan|date=2018-11-20|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-19}}</ref>

===2018–present: Label change and ''Coordinates''===
In mid-2018, the band left Interscope Records/Mercury Nashville to release music independently. On September 21, the band released a five-song collection called ''Coordinates'' via ARTRAT, their newly formed creative house. The band wrote and produced all 5 songs themselves while [[Rick Rubin]] oversaw the project as executive producer.<ref name="Coordinates">{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8476226/the-band-perry-coordinates-tour |title=The Band Perry on 'Coordinates' EP and Live Shows: 'We Just Want to Be Our Most True Selves' |last=Graff |first=Gary |date=September 21, 2018 |website=Billboard}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
The Band Perry started their November tour in September by putting on a concert to benefit Heifer International and their fight to end global hunger.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/band-perry-team-up-with-heifer-international-to-fight-hunger-20130915#ixzz3oZmzkMCD |title=Band Perry Team Up With Heifer International to Fight Hunger |last=Baltin |first=Steve |date=September 15, 2013 |newspaper=Rolling Stone |access-date=February 11, 2017}}</ref>

They gained positive publicity in 2014 after offering to cover the burial expenses for the funerals of nine people (a mother and eight of her children) killed in an accidental house fire in the [[Greenville, Kentucky]], area in [[Muhlenberg County, Kentucky]]. The band also paid the costs of family members' hotel expenses while the father and the surviving daughter were being treated for burns at [[Vanderbilt University Medical Center]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. They have also volunteered their time to support Alzheimer's research.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cnn.com/2014/02/01/showbiz/kentucky-band-perry-funerals/index.html?hpt=hp_t2 |title=The Band Perry to pay for burial of mom, 8 children killed in fire |last=Stapleton |first=AnneClaire |date=2 February 2014 |website=CNN |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref>
