'''Lauren Michelle Jauregui Morgado''' ({{IPAc-en|Àà|h|a ä|r|…õ|…°|i|}}; born June 27, 1996) is an American<!--citizenship, not ethnicity--> singer and songwriter.<!--Do not add non-music occupation like "activist" until this is established by multiple reliable sources as integral to artist's notability. Discuss in talk page.--> A former member of the girl group [[Fifth Harmony]], Jauregui began experimenting with different sounds and exploring solo songwriting, collaborating on songs with [[Marian Hill]], [[Steve Aoki]] and [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]]. She began working on solo music in May 2018 and released her debut solo song "[[Expectations (Lauren Jauregui song)|Expectations]]" in October 2018 under [[Columbia Records]]. In January 2019, she released the song "[[More Than That (Lauren Jauregui song)|More Than That]]". Jauregui contributed "Invisible Chains" to [[Birds of Prey: The Album|the soundtrack]] of the film ''[[Birds of Prey (2020 film)|Birds of Prey]]'' (2020), and released the [[Tainy]]-produced Latin urban song "Lento" in March 2020. In April 2020, she released the song "50ft". Jauregui's debut solo [[Extended play|EP]], ''Prelude'', was released on November 5, 2021.

== Career ==

=== 2012‚Äì2018: ''The X Factor'' and Fifth Harmony ===
{{See also|Fifth Harmony}}
[[File:Lauren Jauregui X Factor 2012 (1).jpg|thumbnail|150px|right|Jauregui on ''[[The X Factor U.S.|The X Factor]]'' in 2012, as a member of [[Fifth Harmony]].]]
In 2012, at the age of 15, Jauregui auditioned for the second season of ''[[The X Factor U.S.]]''.<ref name="Gutowitz">{{cite web|url=https://nylon.com/articles/lauren-jauregui-nylon-september-2018-cover|title=Higher and Higher. Lauren Jauregui Is Flying Solo|first=Jill|last=Gutowitz|date=September 4, 2018|access-date=January 17, 2019|work=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]]}}</ref><ref name="Reiter">{{Cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2012/sep/28/entertainment/la-et-st-the-x-factor-recap-20120928|title='The X Factor' recap: Season 2 auditions end ... strangely|last=Reiter|first=Amy|date=September 28, 2012|work=Los Angeles Times|access-date=July 8, 2017|issn=0458-3035}}</ref> After four rounds of preliminary auditions, she was called back at the age of 16 for the televised audition in front of the judges.<ref name="Vulkan">{{Cite web|url=http://www.vulkanmagazine.com/lauren-jauregui-by-jason-barbagelot|title="Lauren Jauregui" by Jason Barbagelott|work=Vulkan Magazine|date=May 3, 2017|access-date=December 23, 2018|first1=Alexander|last1=Jamall|first2=Rebecca|last2=Besnos|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170506011806/https://vulkanmagazine.com/lauren-jauregui-by-jason-barbagelott/|archive-date=May 6, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Girl Cult">{{cite podcast|url=https://open.spotify.com/episode/51JrEVBYp0LcCzzF3ya1b7|title=Episode 18 ‚Äì Lauren Jauregui ‚Äì Girl Cult Week|website=Girl Cult|date=November 27, 2018|publisher=Galore Magazine}}</ref> Jauregui performed "[[If I Ain't Got You]]" by [[Alicia Keys]] for her audition. Judge [[Antonio Reid]] described Jauregui's voice as "husky, round, mature" and her audition as "perfect". She advanced to the next round after getting approval from all four of the judges.<ref name="Reiter"/> During the second round of bootcamp, she was put up against [[Country music|country]] group Sister C with the song "[[These Arms of Mine (Otis Redding song)|These Arms of Mine]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/blogs/reality-rocks/x-factor-boot-camp-pt-2-voice-042116153.html|title='X Factor' Boot Camp, Pt. 2: This Is 'The Voice'!|work=[[Yahoo! Entertainment]]|date=October 5, 2012|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite episode|title=Bootcamp|series=[[The X Factor (U.S. season 2)|The X Factor]]|network=[[Fox Broadcasting Company|FOX]]|date=2012|season=2}}</ref> After being eliminated as a solo artist in the competition, Jauregui was brought back and put into a group alongside [[Ally Brooke]], [[Normani]], [[Dinah Jane]], and [[Camila Cabello]], which would later become [[Fifth Harmony]]. The group made it to the finale and finished third in the competition.<ref name="relive">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2911257/from-x-factor-to-727-relive-fifth-harmonys-biggest-moments |title=From X Factor To 7/27, Relive Fifth Harmony's Biggest Moments|publisher=[[MTV]]|date=July 27, 2016|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref>

In January 2013, Fifth Harmony was signed to [[Simon Cowell]]'s label [[Syco Music]] and [[L.A. Reid]]'s [[Epic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/x-factors-fifth-harmony-signs-413118|title='X Factor's' Fifth Harmony Signs With Syco Music, Epic Records (Exclusive)|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date=January 17, 2013|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref> Jauregui dropped out of school to work with Fifth Harmony, and later received her high school diploma through homeschooling.<ref name="Girl Cult"/><ref name="Sortal"/> The group released their debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[Better Together (EP)|Better Together]]'' in October 2013. Their debut album ''[[Reflection (Fifth Harmony album)|Reflection]]'' was released in January 2015. Jauregui did not participate in promotion for the release of ''Reflection'' as her grandmother died the day of the album's release and her bandmates recommended she head to Florida.<ref name="Martins"/> The album's third single "[[Worth It (Fifth Harmony song)|Worth It]]" peaked at number 12 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], their highest peak yet on the chart.<ref name="Martins"/><ref name="relive"/> The group also contributed the song "[[I'm in Love with a Monster]]" to the soundtrack of the animated film ''[[Hotel Transylvania 2]]'' (2015). In December 2015, Fifth Harmony was honored as "Group of the Year" at the [[Billboard Women in Music|''Billboard'' Women in Music]] ceremony.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/women-in-music/6805592/Fifth-harmony-women-in-music-2015-destinys-child-sister-sledge-group-of-the-year|title= Fifth Harmony Receives Group of the Year Honor at Billboard Women in Music 2015|work=Billboard|date=December 11, 2015|access-date=February 3, 2019}}</ref>

Fifth Harmony's second album ''[[7/27]]'' was released in May 2016. "[[Work from Home (song)|Work From Home]]", the album's lead single, peaked at number four on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, and reached the top 10 in several international charts.<ref name="Martins"/><ref name="relive"/> "Work From Home" became the first song from a girl group to reach the top 10 of the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 in eight years.<ref name="Martins">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/cover-story/7356880/fifth-harmony-billboard-cover-story-fame-challenges-new-album-the-x-factor|title=Billboard Cover: Fifth Harmony on Surviving Pop-Star Fame and 'Finally Having a Damn Voice'|work=Billboard|date=May 5, 2016|access-date=February 2, 2019|first=Chris|last=Martins}}</ref> The group's third album, ''[[Fifth Harmony (album)|Fifth Harmony]]'', their first as a foursome after Cabello's departure in December 2016, was released in August 2017.<ref name="Kennedy">{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-fifth-harmony-20170817-htmlstory.html|title=After a rocky year, Fifth Harmony is in control ‚Äî and tighter than ever|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=August 17, 2017|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref> The album marked the first time the group was able to co-write and have agency over the creative direction of an album.<ref name="Kennedy"/> In March 2018, the group announced their decision to take an indefinite hiatus to grow as individual artists and pursue solo ambitions.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8252203/fifth-harmony-hiatus-pursue-solo-careers |title=Fifth Harmony Announce Hiatus to Pursue Solo Careers |last=Kaufman |first=Gil |date=March 19, 2018 |work=Billboard |access-date=March 19, 2018}}</ref>
