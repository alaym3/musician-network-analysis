'''The Washboard Union''' is a Canadian [[country music]] group from [[Vancouver, British Columbia]] led by principal members David Roberts, Aaron Grain (aka Brett Ellis) and Chris Duncombe.<ref name="cmt">{{cite web|title=The Washboard Union Biography|url=http://www.cmt.ca/artist/washboard-union|website=[[CMT (Canadian TV channel)|CMT]]|access-date=February 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Abdellatif|first1=Nouran|title=Washboard Union performing for first time in Leduc|url=http://www.leducrep.com/2016/01/18/washboard-union-performing-for-first-time-in-leduc|access-date=February 19, 2016|work=Leduc Rep|date=January 18, 2016}}</ref>

==Biography==
Aaron Grain and Chris Duncombe met in [[Kelowna]], British Columbia at age 15 when Duncombe's father began dating Grain's mother.<ref name="cmt" /> Duncombe and Grain eventually moved to Vancouver, where they met David Roberts, who was living in a Point Grey mansion.<ref name="cmt" /> The three began writing and singing together.<ref name="cmt" /> They originally performed under the name Run GMC.<ref>{{cite news|title=Kelowna: Nothing wishy-washy about The Washboard Union|url=http://www.kelownacapnews.com/entertainment/159610005.html|access-date=February 19, 2016|work=Kelowna Capital News|date=June 19, 2012}}</ref> Their debut album, ''The Washboard Union'', was released in 2012 and produced by [[Garth Richardson]] and [[Bob Ezrin]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Varty|first1=Alexander|title=Washboard Union explores a new road|url=https://www.straight.com/music/411911/washboard-union-explores-new-road|access-date=February 19, 2016|work=[[The Georgia Straight]]|date=August 20, 2013}}</ref>

In January 2015, The Washboard Union recorded an [[extended play]], ''In My Bones'', at RCA Studio A in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] with producer [[Trey Bruce]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Washboard Union|url=http://music.cbc.ca/#!/artists/The-Washboard-Union|website=[[CBC Music]]|access-date=February 19, 2016}}</ref> It was released by Slaight Music/[[Warner Music Canada]] on July 10, 2015.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Hudson|first1=Alex|title=The Washboard Union Make Major Label Debut with New EP|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/washboard_union_make_major_label_debut_with_new_ep|access-date=February 19, 2016|work=[[Exclaim!]]|date=May 22, 2015}}</ref> The first single, "Some Day", went top 40, and the second and third singles, "Maybe It's the Moonshine" & "Shot of Glory", reached the top 10 and top 5 respectively on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canada Country]] chart.<ref name="Canada Country" /> In October 2015, The Washboard Union won the British Columbia Country Music Association Award for Roots Canadiana of the Year.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Marchand|first1=Francois|title=Dallas Smith ropes in the wins at 2015 B.C. Country Music Awards|url=http://blogs.vancouversun.com/2015/10/27/dallas-smith-ropes-in-the-wins-at-2015-b-c-country-music-awards/|access-date=February 19, 2016|work=[[The Vancouver Sun]]|date=October 27, 2015}}</ref>

The Washboard Union's next album, ''What We're Made Of'', was produced by Matt Rovey (Zac Brown Band, Dean Brody) in Nashville and Jeff "Diesel" Dalziel in Toronto and Vancouver over the winter of 2017/18.  The album's first single, “Shine”, was the band's third Top Ten Country Radio hit which they performed live on the Canadian Country Music Awards and walked away with a CCMA Award for 2017 Roots Artist of the Year.

In 2018 the group performed in Liverpool, Nova Scotia at the Queens Place Emera Centre.<ref>[https://www.thechronicleherald.ca/living/the-best-nova-scotia-concerts-of-2018-270134/ "The best Nova Scotia concerts of 2018"]. ''Chronicle Herald'', Stephen Cooke, Dec 20, 2018</ref>

On November 22, 2019, The Washboard Union released "Country Thunder"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=IST7pMNjC2s|title=The Washboard Union - Country Thunder - Official Music Video|website=[[YouTube]]|accessdate=August 6, 2021}}</ref> as the leadoff single to their third studio album, ''Everbound'', which was released on April 24, 2020. The album's second single was "Dock Rock," followed by "If She Only Knew"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusicexpress.ca/the-washboard-union-reveal-new-single-if-she-only-knew/|title=The Washboard Union Reveal New Single 'If She Only Knew'|website=The Music Express|accessdate=August 6, 2021}}</ref> and "Never Run Outta Road."
