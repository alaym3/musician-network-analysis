'''Adam Richard Wiles''' (born 17 January 1984), known professionally as '''Calvin Harris''', is a Scottish DJ, record producer, singer, and songwriter. After focusing on self-sung projects, in 2012, Harris released his third album ''[[18 Months]]'', which involved various vocalists in its single records and gained international recognition. He has released five studio albums and at one point ran his own record label, [[Fly Eye Records]], which he founded in 2010 (defunct in 2016).

His debut studio album, ''[[I Created Disco]]'', was released in June 2007. Its singles "[[Acceptable in the 80s]]" and "[[The Girls (song)|The Girls]]" both reached the top 10 in the UK. In 2009, he released his second studio album, ''[[Ready for the Weekend (album)|Ready for the Weekend]]'', which debuted at number one on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and whose lead single, "[[I'm Not Alone (song)|I'm Not Alone]]", became his first song to top the [[UK Singles Chart]]. In 2012, Harris rose to international prominence with the release of his third studio album, ''[[18 Months]]'', which topped the UK Albums charts and became his first album to chart on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], peaking at number 19. All eight of the album's singles reached the top 10 in the UK, breaking the record for the most top 10 songs from one studio album on the UK Singles Chart with eight entries, surpassing [[Michael Jackson]]'s record. In 2014, he released his fourth studio album, ''[[Motion (Calvin Harris album)|Motion]]'', which debuted at number two in the UK and number five in the US. In 2017, he released his fifth studio album, ''[[Funk Wav Bounces Vol. 1]]'', which reached the top 2 in the UK and the US and became his third consecutive number one album on the US [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] chart.

In October 2014, he became the first artist to place three songs simultaneously on the top 10 of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'s}} [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6304284/calvin-harris-sets-dance-chart-records|title=Calvin Harris|work=Billboard|access-date=14 July 2015}}</ref> He also became the first UK solo artist to reach more than one billion streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name="spotify">{{cite web |url=http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/calvin-harris-s-billion-streams-on-spotify-are-worth-7-million/059527 |title=Calvin Harris's billion streams on Spotify are worth $7 million |work=Music Week |date=8 September 2015 |access-date=9 September 2015}}</ref> Harris has received 18 [[Brit Awards|Brit Award]] nominations–winning [[Brit Award for British Producer of the Year|British Producer of the Year]] and British Single of the Year for "[[One Kiss (song)|One Kiss]]" with [[Dua Lipa]], both in 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-02-20|title=Calvin Harris and the 1975 lead winners at 2019 Brit awards|url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/feb/20/brit-awards-winners-calvin-harris-the-1975-jorja-smith|access-date=2021-04-09|website=The Guardian}}</ref> as well as five Grammy nominations, including a win for [[Grammy Award for Best Music Video|Best Music Video]] in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-23|title=Calvin Harris|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/calvin-harris/3679|access-date=2021-04-09|website=GRAMMY.com}}</ref> In 2013 he also received the [[Ivor Novello Award]] for Songwriter of the Year from the [[British Academy of Songwriters, Composers, and Authors]], and was named the Top Dance/Electronic Artist at the [[2015 Billboard Music Awards|2015 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]]. He appeared on [[Debrett's]] 2017 list of the most influential people in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite news |title=Debrett's 500 List: Music |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/0/debretts-500-list-music/ |work=The Telegraph |date=21 January 2017}}</ref> He topped ''[[Forbes]]''{{'}} list of the world's highest-paid DJs for six consecutive years from 2013 to 2018.

== Early life and beginnings ==
Adam Richard Wiles was born on 17 January 1984 in [[Dumfries]],<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/calvin-harris-mn0000945951/biography |title=Calvin Harris Biography |first=Stewart |last=Mason |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=12 October 2015}}</ref> to [[English people|English]] parents, David Wiles, a biochemist, and Pamela Wiles ({{née}} Noon), a housewife, who married in their native [[Oxford]] before moving to Dumfries.<ref name="Wight"/> He has an elder sister, Sophie, and an elder brother, Edward.<ref name="Wight">Douglas Wight (2015). "Calvin Harris: The $100 Million DJ". Chapter 1: Tall Oaks from a little Acorns Grow. p. 1. Black & White Publishing</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/calvin-harris-how-the-nondancing-foulmouthed-antisocial-scot-became-the-caledonian-justin-timberlake-1764506.html |title=Calvin Harris: How the non-dancing, foul-mouthed, anti-social Scot became the 'Caledonian Justin Timberlake' |work=The Independent |access-date=14 July 2015 |location=London |date=2 August 2009}}</ref> Wiles attended Calside Primary School then [[Dumfries High School]], and, after leaving school, he stocked shelves in supermarkets and worked in a local fish processing factory in order to buy DJ gear.<ref name="Herald">{{cite news |title=From bedroom to Billboard |url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/news/13040026.From_bedroom_to_Billboard/ |work=The Herald |location=Glasgow |date=29 July 2015}}</ref>

He was first attracted to electronic music in his teens and began recording bedroom demos in 1999.<ref>{{cite web |last=Mason |first=Stewart |url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=calvin-harris-mn0000945951|pure_url=yes}} |title=Calvin Harris |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=26 October 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100923150541/http://www.cmc.dk/ |archive-date=23 September 2010 }}</ref> When Harris was 18, he released two songs, "Da Bongos" and "Brighter Days". Both were released as 12-inch club singles and CD-EPs by the label Prima Facie in early 2002 under the name "Stouffer".<ref name="BBBio">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/275856/calvin-harris/biography |title=Calvin Harris |work=Billboard |access-date=14 July 2015 |archive-date=30 June 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150630112248/http://www.billboard.com/artist/275856/calvin-harris/biography |url-status=dead }}</ref> With these singles to his credit, Harris moved from Scotland to London, hoping to learn from the local music scene.<ref name="Herald"/><ref name="BBBio"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Calvin Harris: I worked in a fish factory |url=http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/entertainment/news/calvin-harris-i-worked-in-fish-factory-28879231.html |work=Belfast Telegraph |date=29 July 2015}}</ref> Only one of his songs, "Let Me Know" with vocalist [[Ayah Marar]] on the Unabombers' 2004 live-mix CD Electric Soul, Vol. 2, was released during his time in London.<ref name="BBBio"/> With the lack of job opportunities and money, he returned home to Dumfries and began posting homemade solo recordings to his [[Myspace]] page.<ref name="BBBio"/><ref>[http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-breaks/blog/calvin-harris-fish-factory-to-fame "Calvin Harris: fish factory to fame"]. MTV. Retrieved 31 July 2015</ref> His popularity on the social media website prompted Mark Gillespie, a talent booker for the dance-festival firm [[Global Gathering]] who had recently founded his own management firm,<ref name="360roc">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1097917/calvin-harris-and-rihannas-power-players-whats-next-from-three-six-zeroroc |title=Calvin Harris and Rihanna's Power Players: What's Next From Three Six Zero/Roc Nation's EDM-Pop Partnership |work=Billboard |access-date=11 October 2015 |archive-date=3 September 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150903003027/http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1097917/calvin-harris-and-rihannas-power-players-whats-next-from-three-six-zeroroc |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="ADint">{{cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/entertainment-news/article/interview-calvin-harris/4665762 |title=Interview: Calvin Harris |publisher=artistdirect |access-date=11 October 2015}}</ref> to make Harris the company's first signee.<ref name="360roc"/> Regarding his choice of his stage name, Harris stated that, "My first single was more of a soul track, and I thought Calvin Harris sounded a bit more racially ambiguous. I thought people might not know if I was black or not. After that, I was stuck with it."<ref>{{cite news |title=You Won't Believe What Made Calvin Harris Choose His Stage Name |url=https://www.capitalxtra.com/artists/calvin-harris/real-name-adam-wiles/ |work=Capital Xtra |date=16 April 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==
{{see also|List of awards and nominations received by Calvin Harris}}
=== 2006–2008: Career beginnings and ''I Created Disco'' ===
{{Main|I Created Disco}}

Harris signed contracts with Three Six Zero Group (management), [[EMI]] (publishing), and [[Sony BMG]] (recording) in 2006 after he had been discovered on the social networking website Myspace.<ref>{{cite web |last=Ronan |first=Liam |url=http://www.gigwise.com/features/29792/acceptable-in-the-noughties---calvin-harris |title=Acceptable in the Noughties – Calvin Harris |publisher=Gigwise. Giant Digital |date=26 March 2007 |access-date=12 June 2007}}</ref> Later in 2006, Harris produced a remix of [[All Saints (group)|All Saints]]' single "[[Rock Steady (All Saints song)|Rock Steady]]".<ref>[https://www.allmusic.com/song/rock-steady-calvin-harris-remix-mt0029695700 "Rock Steady (Calvin Harris Remix)]". AllMusic. Retrieved 28 July 2015</ref>

Harris's debut album, ''[[I Created Disco]]'', was released in June 2007. He started working on the album in 2006 after he moved back from living in [[London]] to his hometown of [[Dumfries]].<ref name="ICD">{{cite web |url=http://www.musicradar.com/news/tech/interview-calvin-harris-on-software-hardware-and-hit-making-530744/3 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140517193859/http://www.musicradar.com/news/tech/interview-calvin-harris-on-software-hardware-and-hit-making-530744/3 |url-status=dead |archive-date=17 May 2014 |title=Interview: Calvin Harris on software, hardware and hit-making |publisher=MusicRadar. [[Future plc|Future]] |date=20 February 2012 |access-date=30 July 2014 }}</ref> All 14 tracks were written, produced, and performed solely by Harris, and all recording and producing for the album took place on an [[Amiga]] computer.<ref name="ICD"/> To promote ''I Created Disco'', Harris embarked on a tour of the UK, supporting [[Faithless]] and [[Groove Armada]].<ref name="kyliecolabboration">{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/scotland/south_of_scotland/6377017.stm |title=Web wonder Calvin wins over Kylie |work=BBC News |date=19 February 2007 |access-date=12 March 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.calvinharris.tv/story?newsID=41 |title=Calvin to support Groove Armada on UK Tour |publisher=calvinharris.tv |date=19 April 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090113172351/http://www.calvinharris.tv/story?newsID=41 |archive-date=13 January 2009 |access-date=12 June 2007}}</ref> ''I Created Disco'' was certified gold by the [[British Phonographic Industry]] (BPI).<ref name="BPI">{{cite certification|region=United Kingdom|artist=Calvin Harris|accessdate=24 August 2015}}</ref> It reached number eight on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and number 19 on the US [[Dance/Electronic Albums|Top Electronic Albums]].<ref name="UK-charts"/><ref>{{cite web |url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=calvin harris|chart=Dance/Electronic Albums C}} |title=Calvin Harris – Chart history: Dance/Electronic Albums |work=Billboard |access-date=30 July 2014}}</ref>
[[File:Calvin Harris 001.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Harris performing at the [[Eurockéennes]] (2008)]]
The album contained uptempo [[electroclash]] songs that were influenced by music from the 1980s. The song, "[[Vegas (Calvin Harris song)|Vegas]]", was issued on limited edition vinyl. The first wide-release single from the album was released in March 2007. "[[Acceptable in the 80s]]", a tribute to the style and culture of the decade, peaked at number ten on the [[UK Singles Chart]], remaining on the chart for 15 weeks.<ref name="UK-charts">{{cite web |url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/2141/calvin-harris/ |title=Calvin Harris |publisher=Official Charts Company |access-date=21 February 2015}}</ref> "[[The Girls (Calvin Harris song)|The Girls]]", the album's second single, peaked at number three on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. The third and final release from the album, "[[Merrymaking at My Place]]", peaked at number 43 on the chart.<ref name="UK-charts"/>

The same year, Harris caught the attention of Australian pop singer [[Kylie Minogue]] after his recordings had been passed on to her by another record producer.<ref name="kyliecolabboration"/> This led to him co-writing and producing two songs, "Heart Beat Rock" and "[[In My Arms (Kylie Minogue song)|In My Arms]]", on her 2007 album ''[[X (Kylie Minogue album)|X]]'', the latter becoming a top 10 single in the UK. Harris said that working with Minogue was "surreal, but fun" although he admitted to ''Mixmag'' in 2007 to "needing a few drinks before meeting her".{{cn|date=November 2019}} Harris also contributed the song "[[Off & On]]" to [[Róisín Murphy]]'s album ''[[Overpowered]]'', but it was cut from the album. Harris would later give the song to [[Sophie Ellis-Bextor]] to record for her 2011 album ''[[Make a Scene]]''. Harris also produced a remix of the second single "4th of July (Fireworks)" from [[Kelis]]' album ''[[Flesh Tone]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/4th-of-july-the-remixes-ep/id378505669 |title=4th of July (The Remixes) – EP by Kelis |publisher=iTunes Store (US) |access-date=26 October 2014}}</ref> He additionally reworked singles by [[Jamiroquai]], [[Groove Armada]], [[All Saints (group)|All Saints]], and [[CSS (band)|CSS]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/calvin-harris-mn0000945951/biography |title=Calvin Harris {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic |website=AllMusic |access-date=23 April 2018}}</ref>

In 2008, Harris collaborated with rapper [[Dizzee Rascal]] on his single "[[Dance wiv Me]]", producing the track and singing the hook. The single topped the [[UK Singles Chart]] for four weeks and was certified platinum by the BPI, selling 600,000 copies.<ref name="BPI"/> It was shortlisted for the 2008 [[Popjustice £20 Music Prize]]. In 2009, it received a [[Brit Award]] nomination for British Single and an [[Ivor Novello Award]] nomination for Best Contemporary Song.<ref>{{cite news |title=Full list of awards and nominees for 2009 Brit Awards |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/full-list-of-awards-and-nominees-for-2009-brit-awards-1452048.html |work=The Independent |date=29 July 2015 |location=London}}</ref> On 18 October 2008, Harris was featured on [[BBC Radio 1]]'s ''[[Essential Mix]]'' on a two-hour set.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00dwktx |title=BBC Radio 1's Essential Mix, 18/10/2008 |publisher=BBC Radio 1 |date=18 October 2008 |access-date=12 September 2012}}</ref>

=== 2008–2010: ''Ready for the Weekend'' ===
{{Main|Ready for the Weekend (album)}}
[[File:CalvinharrisDJ.jpg|thumb|left|Harris performing at Xbox Reverb (2009)]]

Harris's second album, ''[[Ready for the Weekend (album)|Ready for the Weekend]]'', was released in August 2009 and entered at number one on the [[UK Albums Chart]], being certified gold by the BPI within two months of release.<ref name="BPI"/><ref name="UK-charts"/><ref name="ReadyWe">{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/news/calvin-harris/45305 |title=Calvin Harris announces new album details |work=NME |date=12 June 2009}}</ref> Eleven of the fourteen tracks on the album were sung, produced, and written solely by Harris.<ref name="ReadyWe"/> "[[I'm Not Alone (song)|I'm Not Alone]]", released as the album's lead single in April 2009, debuted at number one on the UK Singles Chart. The follow-up single, "[[Ready for the Weekend (song)|Ready for the Weekend]]", reached number three.<ref name="UK-charts"/> The third single, "[[Flashback (Calvin Harris song)|Flashback]]", featuring Jordanian singer [[Ayah Marar]] reached number eighteen in the UK.<ref name="UK-charts"/> During promotion of the album, Harris hosted a series of videos on YouTube titled Jam TV, in which musicians such as [[Florence Welch]], [[Goldie]], and [[Katy Perry]] tried to open pots of jam.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.getyourearsout.com/times/2009/07/its-jam-tv-from-calvin-harrisgenius/ |title=It's JAM TV from Calvin Harris...genius. |publisher=Get Your Ears Out |date=16 July 2009 |access-date=7 November 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425141834/http://www.getyourearsout.com/times/2009/07/its-jam-tv-from-calvin-harrisgenius/ |archive-date=25 April 2012  }}</ref> At the [[2010 Brit Awards]], Harris received a nomination for Best British Male.<ref>[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/celebritynews/7020625/Brit-Awards-nominations-2010-full-list.html "2010 Brit Awards nominations in full"]. The Telegraph. Retrieved 28 July 2015</ref>

On 8 February 2010, "[[You Used to Hold Me (Calvin Harris song)|You Used to Hold Me]]" was released as the fourth and final single from the album. It peaked at number 27 on the UK Singles Chart.<ref name="UK-charts"/> The song marked the last time Harris regularly sang on his records, opting to focus more on music production while having guest singers provide the vocals for him.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a290067/calvin-harris-ive-quit-singing.html | title=Calvin Harris: I've quit singing | work=Digital Spy | access-date=11 October 2015}}</ref> The same year, shortly after his "Ready for the Weekend tour" wrapped up, Harris parted ways with members of his band in which he served as lead vocalist and made the decision to stop doing live shows.<ref name="BBlead">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/495316/calvin-harris-is-done-singing-lead-despite-solo-hits |title=Calvin Harris Is Done Singing Lead, Despite Solo Hits |work=Billboard |access-date=11 October 2015}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', Harris said that, "I thought I'd exhausted every avenue [on the two albums] and it takes a long time to make me sound good, which is why I stopped singing live as well. I'd like to think of someone who's better-looking, a better singer, better dancer to be the frontperson for the song."<ref name="BBlead"/> He also released several remixes, including remixes of [[Shakira]]'s "[[She Wolf (Shakira song)|She Wolf]]", Katy Perry's "[[Waking Up in Vegas]]", [[Mr Hudson]]'s "[[Supernova (Mr Hudson song)|Supernova]]" (featuring [[Kanye West]]), and [[Mika (singer)|Mika]]'s "[[We Are Golden]]".<ref name="BBlead"/>

Harris also produced Dizzee Rascal's next single titled "[[Holiday (Dizzee Rascal song)|Holiday]]", which topped the [[UK Singles Chart]] in August 2009.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/calvin-harris-how-the-nondancing-foulmouthed-antisocial-scot-became-the-caledonian-justin-timberlake-1764506.html |title=Calvin Harris: How the non-dancing, foul-mouthed, anti-social Scot became the 'Caledonian Justin Timberlake' |work=The Independent |publisher=Independent Print Limited |date=2 August 2009 |access-date=2 August 2009 |location=London}}</ref> Harris made a guest appearance as vocalist on [[Tiësto]]'s song "Century" on the Dutch producer's album, ''[[Kaleidoscope (Tiësto album)|Kaleidoscope]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last=Dombal |first=Ryan |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/36120-sigur-ross-jonsi-bloc-partys-kele-okereke-on-new-tiesto-album/ |title=Sigur Rós's Jónsi, Bloc Party's Kele Okereke on New Tiësto Album |work=Pitchfork Media |date=4 August 2009 |access-date=8 August 2009}}</ref> Harris also produced and mixed [[The Ting Tings]]' single ''[[Hands (The Ting Tings song)|Hands]]'' which was released on 18 August 2010.<ref name = "hands">{{cite web |last=Copsey |first=Robert |url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a363022/the-ting-tings-scrapped-album-was-s-euro-pop.html |title=The Ting Tings: 'Scrapped album was s*** Euro-pop' |work=Digital Spy |date=31 January 2012 |access-date=18 February 2012}}</ref> The song was originally set to become the first single of the duo's second studio album, but they cancelled the album plans and the song was included as a bonus track on the deluxe edition of ''[[Sounds from Nowheresville]]''.<ref name = "hands"/> On 14 November 2009, Harris invaded the stage of the [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] talent competition ''[[The X Factor (UK)|The X Factor]]'' during a live performance by Irish contestants [[Jedward|John and Edward Grimes]], holding a [[pineapple]] on his head. He later apologised on Twitter.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/calvin-sorry-for-jedward-stunt-1943856.html |title=Calvin sorry for Jedward stunt |work=Irish Independent |date=14 November 2009 |access-date=15 November 2009}}</ref>

Harris claimed that Chris Brown's "[[Yeah 3x]]", released in October 2010, plagiarised his 2009 single "I'm Not Alone".<ref name="BBC">{{cite web|last=Izundu|first=Chi Chi |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/11443681|title=Calvin Harris says Chris Brown 'stole' his song|work=[[Newsbeat]]|date=4 October 2010|access-date=12 February 2011}}</ref> After considering similarities between the two songs, Harris was subsequently added to the songwriting credits on the single and the ''[[F.A.M.E. (Chris Brown album)|F.A.M.E.]]'' album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/14648839|title=Calvin Harris and Chris Brown make up after song row|work=Newsbeat|date=25 August 2011|access-date=30 August 2011}}</ref> Harris was also featured on Kylie Minogue's eleventh studio album, ''[[Aphrodite (album)|Aphrodite]]'', collaborating the track "Too Much".<ref>{{cite web|title=Kylie Minogue, "Aphrodite"|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/review/1069108/kylie-minogue-aphrodite|work=Billboard|access-date=16 September 2013|date=25 June 2010}}</ref>

=== 2011–2013: ''18 Months'' and international prominence ===
{{Main|18 Months}}

In 2011, Harris toured as a support act for the European leg of Barbadian singer [[Rihanna]]'s [[Loud Tour]]. Rihanna was quoted as saying, "Calvin is the perfect fit for the Loud Tour. == Other ventures ==

The situation in Myanmar has been deemed "beyond critical" by Amnesty International.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2017/11/30/calvin-harris-donates-20000-dj-snakes-charity-campaign-benefiting-rohingyas-totaling-almost-1-5-million/ |title=CALVIN HARRIS DONATES OMNIA BOOKING FEE TO FAMILY MEMBERS & VICTIMS OF LAS VEGAS SHOOTING|publisher=MTV |date=30 November 2017 |access-date=17 May 2018 }}</ref> In July 2020, Harris donated £20,000 for the Save Our Sub campaign to benefit Glasgow's Sub Club which was at risk of permanent closure after temporarily closing doors on March due to the [[coronavirus pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.youredm.com/2020/07/09/calvin-harris-sub-club-20k-donation/|title=Calvin Harris Donates £20K to Glasgow's Save Our Sub Club|publisher=Your EDM|date=9 July 2020 |access-date=26 November 2020 }}</ref>

== Income ==
Calvin Harris is one of the highest paid disk jockeys/producers. ''[[Forbes]]'' began reporting on Harris's earnings in 2013, calculating that he earned $46 million between May 2012 and May 2013, for his music, tour and his residency in Las Vegas which placed him as the highest-paid DJ of the year.<ref>{{cite news|last=Greenburg|first=Zack O'Malley|author-link=Zack O'Malley Greenburg|date=14 August 2013|title=Electronic Cash Kings 2013: The World's Highest-Paid DJs|work=Forbes|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2013/08/14/electronic-cash-kings-2013-the-worlds-highest-paid-djs/|access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> In 2014, Harris came in at number one on the list again for the second consecutive year with a total of $66 million in annual earnings.<ref>{{cite news|last=Greenburg|first=Zack O'Malley|date=19 August 2014|title=The World's Highest-Paid DJs: Electronic Cash Kings 2014|work=Forbes|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2014/08/19/the-worlds-highest-paid-djs-electronic-cash-kings-2014/|access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> In 2015, Harris topped the highest-paid DJs list for a third consecutive year, having earned $66 million over the previous 12 months.<ref>{{cite news|last=Greenburg|first=Zack O'Malley|date=24 August 2015|title=The World's Highest-Paid DJs: Electronic Cash Kings 2015|work=Forbes|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2015/08/24/the-worlds-highest-paid-djs-electronic-cash-kings-2015/|url-status=live|access-date=24 August 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150826065623/http://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2015/08/24/the-worlds-highest-paid-djs-electronic-cash-kings-2015/|archive-date=26 August 2015}}</ref> On ''[[The Sunday Times (UK)|The Sunday Times]]'' [[Sunday Times Rich List|Rich List]] published in April 2015, Harris was ranked the 30th richest British millionaire in music, with a personal fortune of £70 million ($105 million).<ref>{{cite news|date=29 July 2015|title=Paul McCartney tops musicians' rich list. Again|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/apr/23/paul-mccartney-tops-musicians-rich-list-again}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|date=29 July 2015|title=Calvin Harris is the Richest DJ, but Not the Richest Musician|publisher=ESA Records|url=http://www.esarecords.co.uk/calvin-harris-is-the-richest-dj-but-not-the-richest-musician/|url-status=dead|access-date=29 July 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171019221416/http://www.esarecords.co.uk/calvin-harris-is-the-richest-dj-but-not-the-richest-musician/|archive-date=19 October 2017}}</ref> In 2018, he made $48.5 million.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-06-12|title=Here's how fast Calvin Harris and Tiësto earn the average salary|url=https://djmag.com/content/here%E2%80%99s-how-fast-calvin-harris-and-ti%C3%ABsto-earn-average-salary|access-date=2020-12-02|website=DJMag.com}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Harris dated [[Rita Ora]] from 2013 to June 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Duff|first1=Anna|title=Hot new couple?! They started dating after Wolf appeared in the music video for his song "[[Blame (Calvin Harris song)|Blame]]".<ref>{{cite web |title=Calvin Harris Reunites With Ex-Girlfriend Aarika Wolf for 4th of July Celebration|url=https://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/calvin-harris-reunites-with-ex-gf-aarika-wolf-on-4th-of-july/|publisher=Us Magazine |date=7 July 2018}}</ref>

Harris stopped drinking alcohol in 2008, stating, "I wasn't an alcoholic or anything like that, but it was clearly affecting what I do."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/8205489.stm|title=Harris gave up drink 'for fans'|date=17 August 2009|access-date=4 October 2020|website=News.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> He is a supporter of his hometown football team [[Queen of the South F.C.|Queen of the South.]] Harris considered writing a song to celebrate the team reaching the [[2008 Scottish Cup Final]], but the idea was scrapped due to the time constraints recording his 2009 album ''Ready for the Weekend''.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Wight|first=Douglas|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/908081567|title=Calvin Harris : the 100 million DJ|date=2015|publisher=Black & White Publishing|isbn=978-1-84502-981-4|location=New York, NY|oclc=908081567}}</ref> Queen of the South would go on to lose the match 2-3 to [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]].
