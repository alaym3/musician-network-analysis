'''Pentatonix''' (abbreviated '''PTX''') is an<!--do not add "Grammy Award-winning," that is undue weight--> American [[a cappella]] group from [[Arlington, Texas]], consisting of [[vocalists]] [[Scott Hoying]] ([[baritone]]), [[Mitch Grassi]] ([[tenor]]), [[Kirstin Maldonado]] ([[alto]]), [[Kevin Olusola]] ([[vocal percussion]] and vocals), and Matt Sallee ([[Bass (voice type)|bass]]). Characterized by their [[Pop music|pop]]-style [[arrangement]]s with vocal harmonies, [[scat singing|basslines]], [[riff]]ing, [[vocal percussion|percussion]], and [[beatboxing]], they produce [[cover version]]s of modern pop works or [[Christmas music|Christmas songs]], sometimes in the form of [[List of musical medleys|medleys]], along with original material. Pentatonix formed in 2011 and subsequently won the [[The Sing-Off (season 3)|third season]] of [[NBC]]'s ''[[The Sing-Off]],'' receiving $200,000 and a recording contract with [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Hyman |first=Vicki |title=Pentatonix scores 'The Sing-Off' title |url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/tv/index.ssf/2011/11/pentatonix_scores_the_sing-off.html |work=The Star-Ledger |access-date=August 24, 2012 |date=November 29, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Nededog |first=Jethro |title='The Sing-Off's' Pentatonix on Going From Newly Formed Group to the Show's Winners |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/nbc-the-sing-off-winners-pentatonix-interview-266936 |work=The Hollywood Reporter |access-date=August 24, 2012 |date=November 29, 2011}}</ref> When Sony's [[Epic Records]] dropped the group after ''The Sing-Off'', the group formed its YouTube channel, distributing its music through [[Madison Gate Records]], a label owned by Sony Pictures.<ref name="NewYorkTimes">{{cite news |title=A Tiny Label Is Behind an<!--not a mistake--> a Cappella Group's Success|work=NewYorkTimes |url= https://www.nytimes.com/2013/11/18/business/media/behind-a-cappella-groups-success-a-tiny-label.html|access-date=November 17, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Interview: Entertainment.ie speaks to US a cappella group Pentatonix |work=Entertainment.ie |url= http://entertainment.ie/music/news/Interview-Entertainmentie-speaks-to-US-a-cappella-group-Pentatonix/317164.htm|access-date=November 27, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Pentatonix Tears Up the Charts">{{cite news |title=Pentatonix Tears Up the Charts|work=Wallstreet Journal|date=December 11, 2014|url= https://www.wsj.com/articles/pentatonix-tears-up-the-charts-1418322119|access-date=December 15, 2014|last1=Karp|first1=Hannah}}</ref> Their YouTube channel currently has over 19 million subscribers and 5 billion views. The group's video tribute to [[Daft Punk]] had received more than 355 million views as of November 20, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=5 November 2013|title=[Official Video] Daft Punk - Pentatonix - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3MteSlpxCpo| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211030/3MteSlpxCpo| archive-date=2021-10-30|url-status=live|access-date=20 February 2021|website=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref name="NewsOK.com">{{cite web |title=Wednesday Video Spotlight: Pentatonix has a hit with holiday album ''That's Christmas To Me'', performing during Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade |work=NewsOK.com |url= http://newsok.com/wednesday-video-spotlight-pentatonix-has-a-hit-with-holiday-album-thats-christmas-to-me-performing-during-macys-thanksgiving-day-parade/article/5370614 |access-date=November 27, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Interview: Entertainment.ie speaks to US a cappella group Pentatonix |work=Entertainment.ie |url= http://entertainment.ie/music/news/Interview-Entertainmentie-speaks-to-US-a-cappella-group-Pentatonix/317164.htm|access-date=November 26, 2014}}</ref>

Their debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[PTX, Volume 1]]'' was released in 2012, followed by their holiday release ''[[PTXmas]]'' the same year, with Pentatonix's third release, ''[[PTX, Vol. II]]'', debuting at number 1 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Independent Albums]] chart and number 10 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] in 2013.<ref name="NewYorkTimes" /> In May 2014, Pentatonix signed with [[RCA Records]], a "flagship" label of [[Sony Music Entertainment]],<ref name="HollyRep">{{cite web |title=Pentatonix Signed to RCA Records |url= http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/pentatonix-signed-rca-records-703811 |work=The Hollywood Reporter |date= May 13, 2014 |access-date=May 13, 2014}}</ref> while in the same year, the group released their fourth EP, ''[[PTX, Vol. III]]'', and two full-length studio albums; ''[[PTX, Vols. 1 & 2]]'', a compilation album released in Japan, Korea and Australia, and their second holiday release, ''[[That's Christmas to Me]]'', with the album certified gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), platinum on December 24, 2014, and double platinum on February 11, 2016, becoming the highest-charting holiday album by a group since 1962, and the fourth-best-selling album in the United States in 2014.<ref name="That's Christmas to Me">{{cite certification|region=United States|type=album|title=That's Christmas to Me|artist=Pentatonix|access-date=December 10, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Yahoo Music">{{cite web |title=Pentatonix Has the Highest-Charting Christmas Album by a Group Since 1962|work=Yahoo Music|url= https://www.yahoo.com/music/pentatonix-has-the-highest-charting-christmas-104256396166.html|access-date=December 3, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6415138/penatonix-thats-christmas-1-million-sales |title=Pentatonix's 'That's Christmas To Me' Surpasses 1 Million in Sales |last=Caulfield |first=Keith |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=December 24, 2014 |access-date=December 24, 2014}}</ref> The following year, Pentatonix released [[Pentatonix (album)|their eponymous album]], their first consisting mostly of original material, which debuted atop the US [[Billboard 200]] chart for the first time in their career, followed by a third Christmas album, ''[[A Pentatonix Christmas]]'', in 2016, and a new EP, ''[[PTX, Vol. IV â€“ Classics]]'', the year after.

Pentatonix have won three [[Grammy Award]]s: they were the first a cappella act to win [[Grammy Award for Best Arrangement, Instrumental or A Cappella|Best Arrangement, Instrumental or A Cappella]], doing so in [[57th Annual Grammy Awards|2015]] and [[58th Annual Grammy Awards|2016]],<ref name="Grammy" /> and [[Grammy Award for Best Country Duo/Group Performance|Best Country Duo/Group Performance]] in [[59th Annual Grammy Awards|2017]].<ref name="Grammy3" />

== Career ==
=== Background ===
Pentatonix began with [[Kirstin Maldonado]] (born May 16, 1992), [[Mitch Grassi]] (born July 24, 1992), and [[Scott Hoying]] (born September 17, 1991) who grew up together and were schoolmates at [[Martin High School (Arlington, Texas)|Martin High School]] in [[Arlington, Texas]].<ref name="NBCbio">{{cite web |title=Biography of Pentatonix |url=http://www.nbc.com/sing-off/contestants/pentatonix/bio/ |publisher=nbc.com |access-date=June 29, 2012}}</ref> For a local radio show competition to meet the cast of ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'', they arranged and submitted a trio version of "[[Telephone (song)|Telephone]]," the hit song by [[Lady Gaga]] and featuring [[BeyoncÃ©]]. Despite losing the competition, their singing sparked attention at their school, and they began performing. Their version of "Telephone" subsequently gained attention on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Malkoff |first=Amy |title=An Exclusive Interview With Pentatonix's Scott Hoying |url=http://www.casa.org/content/exclusive-interview-pentatonixs-scott-hoying |work=casa.org |access-date=August 25, 2012 |date=November 27, 2011}}</ref>

[[File:Avi Kaplan Barcelona 15.jpg|upright=0.8|thumb|Avi Kaplan's [[basso profondo]] vocal range was a prominent element of Pentatonix's sound from the group's founding through his departure in 2017.]]

Hoying and Maldonado both graduated from [[Martin High School (Arlington, Texas)|Martin High School]] in 2010, Grassi in 2011. Hoying went off to the [[University of Southern California]] (USC) to pursue a degree in popular music performance, while Maldonado pursued a degree in musical theatre at the [[University of Oklahoma]].<ref name="DFW">{{cite web |last=Walker |first=Patrick M. |title=As part of Pentatonix, Arlington trio shines on NBC's 'The Sing-Off' |work=dfw.com |date=November 8, 2011 |url=http://www.dfw.com/2011/11/07/535039/as-part-of-pentatonix-arlington.html#storylink=misearch |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref> While at USC, Scott Hoying joined {{Not a typo|an |a cappella}} group called [[SoCal VoCals]]. He found out about ''The Sing-Off'' from another member of the group, Ben Bram (also their arranger, producer, and sound engineer), and was encouraged to audition for the show.<ref name="DFW" /> He persuaded Maldonado and Grassi to join him, but Bram suggested having a bass and beatboxer as well to support the group.<ref>{{cite web |last=Hodges |first=John |title=A Brief Interview with Scott Hoying of Pentatonix |work=casa.org |url=http://www.casa.org/content/brief-interview-scott-hoying-pentatonix |access-date=August 25, 2012 |date=September 25, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=The Sing-Off Season 3 â€“ Official Casting Site |date=September 7, 2011 |url=http://www.singoffcasting.com/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110907030116/http://www.singoffcasting.com/ |archive-date=September 7, 2011 |access-date=November 7, 2013}}</ref> Through a mutual friend, Hoying met [[Avi Kaplan]] (born April 17, 1989), a highly recognized vocal bass in the a cappella community. Then, the trio found [[Kevin Olusola]] (born October 5, 1988) on YouTube, where one of his videos in which he was simultaneously beatboxing and playing the cello (called "celloboxing") had gone viral. Olusola was born in [[Owensboro, Kentucky]], and graduated pre-med from [[Yale University]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qOiej7q9nHE |title=Kevin Olusola: Cello and Beatboxing |via=YouTube |date=January 15, 2009 |access-date=November 7, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Russ |first=Stephen |title=Faith, Innovation, and Cello Beat Boxing â€“ An Interview with Kevin Olusola of Pentatonix |work=Beliefnet.com |url= http://www.beliefnet.com/Entertainment/Music/Faith-Innovation-and-Cello-Beat-Boxing-An-Interview-with-Kevin-Olusola-of-Pentatonix.aspx?p=1 |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref>

The group met the day before the auditions for the third season of ''[[The Sing-Off]]'' began.<ref name="DFW" /><ref>{{cite web |last=Bentley |first=Jean |title='The Sing Off' winners Pentatonix on YouTube, Nick Lachey and recording an album |work=zap2it.com |date=November 29, 2011 |url= http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/11/the-sing-off-winners-pentatonix-on-youtube-nick-lachey-and-recording-an-album.html |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref> The group successfully auditioned for the show and eventually went on to win the title for 2011 (season three).<ref>{{cite web |last=Gallo |first=Phil |title='The Sing-Off' Finale: Pentatonix Takes The Trophy |work=Billboard |date=November 29, 2011 |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/464941/the-sing-off-finale-pentatonix-takes-the-trophy |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref>

Pentatonix, as suggested by Scott Hoying, is named after the [[pentatonic scale]], a musical [[scale (music)|scale]] or [[musical mode|mode]] with five [[Musical note|notes]] per [[octave]], representing the five members of the group.<ref>{{cite web |last=O'Neal |first=Cathy |title=Pentatonix on The Sing-Off |work=TheaterJones.com |url=http://www.theaterjones.com/stagewhispers/20111011124311/2011-10-11/Pentatonix-on-The-Sing-Off |access-date=August 24, 2012 |date=October 11, 2011}}</ref> They replaced the last letter with an "x" to make it more appealing.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite web |last=Trust |first=Gary |title=Weekly Chart Notes: Whitney Houston, Pentatonix, Andy Griffith |work=Billboard |date=July 6, 2012 |url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/chartbeat/482047/weekly-chart-notes-whitney-houston-pentatonix-andy-griffith |access-date=August 24, 2012}}</ref> The quintet derives its influences from [[Pop music|pop]], [[dubstep]], [[Electronic music|electro]], [[reggae]], [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], and classical music.<ref name="NBCbio" />

Scott Hoying and Kirstie Maldonado dropped out of college in hopes of winning ''The Sing-Off'', while Mitch Grassi skipped his high school graduation to audition for the show.<ref>{{cite web |last=MacKenzie |first=Carina Adly |title='The Sing-Off' winner: Pentatonix tunes out Dartmouth Aires and Urban Method |work=zap2it.com |date=November 28, 2011 |url=http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/11/the-sing-off-winner-pentatonix-tunes-out-dartmouth-aires-and-urban-method.html |access-date=August 25, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120905072543/http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/11/the-sing-off-winner-pentatonix-tunes-out-dartmouth-aires-and-urban-method.html |archive-date=September 5, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> After they won, all of the members relocated to Los Angeles in order to pursue a career as recording artists.<ref>{{cite news |last=Smith |first=Grady |title='Sing-Off' champs Pentatonix talk competition, their future album, and (of course) Nick Lachey's puns |work=Entertainment Weekly |publisher=Inside TV |date=December 2, 2011 |url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2011/12/02/the-sing-off-pentatonix-interview/ |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Still |first=Jennifer |title='The Sing-Off' Pentatonix interview: 'We want mainstream success' |work=Digital Spy |date=November 30, 2011 |url= http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/ustv/s184/the-sing-off/interviews/a353572/the-sing-off-pentatonix-interview-we-want-mainstream-success.html |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref> The main goal of the group was to become the first mainstream a cappella group in recent times.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Sing Off Season 3: Conference Call With Pentatonix |url= http://www.realitywanted.com/newsitem/4889-the-sing-off-season-3-conference-call-with-pentatonix |work=Reality Wanted |access-date=August 25, 2012 |date=December 2, 2011}}</ref>

In January 2012, having signed with Sony Pictures-owned label [[Madison Gate Records]],<ref name="PRN">{{cite news |title=THE SING-OFF Season 3 Winners Pentatonix Release Debut EP "PTX VOLUME 1" Tuesday, June 26th |url= http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/the-sing-off-season-3-winners-pentatonix-release-debut-ep-ptx-volume-1-tuesday-june-26th-160396195.html |agency=PR Newswire |access-date=June 29, 2012}}</ref> the group began working on their first album with producer Ben Bram. During that six-month period of picking covers and writing originals, Pentatonix released covers of both popular and classic songs on YouTube. In interviews, the members mention that it was a way to stay relevant to their audience that enjoyed their work on "The Sing-Off", in addition to gaining new fans.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pentatonix Perform At The Recording Academy |publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences |date=October 1, 2012 |url=http://www.grammy.com/news/pentatonix-perform-at-the-recording-academy |access-date=October 7, 2012}}</ref> Almost all of their covers, including "[[Somebody That I Used to Know]]" by [[Gotye]] featuring [[Kimbra]], "[[Gangnam Style]]" by [[PSY]] and "[[We Are Young]]" by [[Fun (band)|Fun]], went viral on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pentatonix 'We Are Young' A Cappella Cover Goes Viral |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/marielle-wakim/pentatonix-a-cappella_b_1677454.html |work=HuffPost |access-date=August 25, 2012 |date=May 9, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Wakim |first=Marielle |title=Pentatonix Are Making a Cappella Cool Again (or Maybe for the First Time) |work=HuffPost |date=July 16, 2012 |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/marielle-wakim/pentatonix-a-cappella_b_1677454.html |access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref>

On June 26, 2020, the group released another EP, ''At Home'', containing five new covers, plus a 13-song medley, a total of 6 tracks.<ref name="FVM2020-06-27">{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Pentatonix releases "At Home" |url=https://www.frontview-magazine.be/en/news/pentatonix-releases-at-home |publisher=FrontView Magazine |location=[[Belgium]] |date=June 27, 2020 |access-date=July 25, 2020}}</ref><ref name="BB2020-07-22">{{cite news |last=Aniftos |first=Rania |date=July 22, 2020 |title=Pentatonix Unveils Ethereal Video for The Cranberries' 'Dreams' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9422618/pentatonix-cranberries-dreams-video |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 25, 2020 }}</ref> The EP contains Pentatonix covering [[Billie Eilish]]'s "[[When the Party's Over (song)|When the Party's Over]]", [[Dua Lipa]]'s "[[Break My Heart (Dua Lipa song)|Break My Heart]]", [[the Cranberries]]' "[[Dreams (The Cranberries song)|Dreams]]", [[The Weeknd]]'s "[[Blinding Lights]]", and [[Clean Bandit]]'s "Cologne".<ref name="FVM2020-06-27" /><ref name="BB2020-07-22" /> Each member recorded their arrangement separately in their homes due to them having to quarantine because of [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19]].<ref>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Pentatonix releases 'At Home' EP recorded entirely in quarantine |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/6/24/pentatonix-releases-at-home-ep-recorded-entirely-in-quaranti.html |work=[[ABC News Radio]] |location=New York |date=June 24, 2020|access-date=July 25, 2020}}</ref><ref name="BB2020-07-22" />

On November 13, 2020, the group released their fourth Christmas-themed studio album, "[[We Need a Little Christmas (Pentatonix album)|We Need a Little Christmas]]." The album includes hits such as their covers of "[[Amazing Grace|Amazing Grace (My Chains Are Gone)]]," which was released as a single on November 25, 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|title=PENTATONIX RELEASE NEW HOLIDAY ALBUM, WE NEED A LITTLE CHRISTMAS, TODAY! â€“ Sony Music Canada|url=https://www.sonymusic.ca/press_release/pentatonix-release-new-holiday-album-we-need-a-little-christmas-today|access-date=2021-03-24|website=www.sonymusic.ca}}</ref> and "12 Days of Christmas." It also includes an original song, "Thank You." 

On August 14, 2020, Pentatonix released "Happy Now", their first original single in five years, and the first of an upcoming album of original content.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Are you "Happy Now" that Pentatonix has released a new original track? - Music News - ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/8/14/are-you-happy-now-that-pentatonix-has-released-a-new-origina.html|access-date=2020-08-16|website=abcnewsradioonline.com|language=en}}</ref> On October 14, Pentatonix released "Be My Eyes", and announced the date of ''[[Pentatonix discography|The Lucky Ones]]'', the first full-length album of originals since 2015, for February 12, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Tube|first=Stage|title=VIDEO: Watch the Video for Pentatonix's New Single 'Be My Eyes'|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/VIDEO-Watch-the-Video-for-Pentatonixs-New-Single-Be-My-Eyes-20201015|access-date=2020-10-15|website=BroadwayWorld.com|language=en}}</ref> Like ''At Home'' and ''We Need a Little Christmas'', it was recorded in isolation because of the pandemic. A music video for "Coffee in Bed" was released on the same day as the album. The album received general critical acclaim.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Lucky Ones by Pentatonix|url=https://genius.com/albums/Pentatonix/The-lucky-ones|access-date=2021-03-24|website=Genius|language=en}}</ref> The deluxe version released on 10th September 2021 with new songs like Anchor, Petals, Midnight in Tokyo (ft. Glee Monster), and more.

=== Tours ===
[[File:Pentatonix in Sydney.jpg|thumb|right|Pentatonix performing in Sydney, Australia in February 2020.]]
Pentatonix embarked on their first national headlining tour in autumn of 2012. The tour was sold out and spanned 30 cities. Opening acts consisted of Alexander Cardinale and SJ Acoustic Music.{{citation needed|date=December 2013}} The band went on their second national headlining tour from January 24, 2013, to May 11, 2013, and simultaneously wrote additional original material for their second EP, ''[[PTX, Vol. II]]''.<ref name="Moraski" /><ref name="Lawton" /><ref name="Pentatonix Interview" />

In 2014, Pentatonix undertook an extensive tour that included the United States, Canada, Europe and South East Asia.<ref>{{cite web |title=PTXofficial Website, Tour Archive|work=PTXofficial.com |url= http://ptxofficial.com/tour_archive|access-date=November 28, 2014}}</ref>

In 2015, Pentatonix began the North American leg of their "[[On My Way Home Tour]]", which began February 25, 2015, in California and ended at Grand Prairie's Verizon Theatre on March 29.<ref>{{cite web|title=PTXofficial Website, Tour Archive|work=PTXofficial.com|url=http://popcultureblog.dallasnews.com/2014/12/worlds-most-popular-a-cappella-group-pentatonix-stops-at-home-in-d-fw-in-march.html/|access-date=December 4, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141208084631/http://popcultureblog.dallasnews.com/2014/12/worlds-most-popular-a-cappella-group-pentatonix-stops-at-home-in-d-fw-in-march.html/|archive-date=December 8, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The group started a European tour in Portugal on April 9. This European leg ended in Glasgow on May 6. The Southeast Asian leg of the tour began in Seoul, South Korea on May 28. The last show of this leg was on Jun 16 in Osaka, Japan.

Pentatonix opened for [[Kelly Clarkson]] on her [[Piece by Piece Tour]]. During Clarkson's set the group performed "[[Heartbeat Song (Kelly Clarkson song)|Heartbeat Song]]" with her.<ref name="Piece by Piece Tour">{{cite web|title=Kelly Clarkson Piece by Piece Tour|url=http://pxptour.kellyclarkson.com|website=kellyclarkson.com|access-date=March 3, 2015}}</ref> Due to Clarkson's doctor's requests for vocal rest, the tour was canceled 15 shows early.<ref>{{cite web|title = Kelly Clarkson's Canadian & U.K. Tour Dates Canceled|url = http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6708097/kelly-clarksons-canadian-uk-tour-dates-canceled|access-date = October 4, 2015}}</ref>

In 2016, Pentatonix embarked on the first leg of their "[[Pentatonix World Tour]]" with [[Us The Duo]].<ref>{{cite web|title = Pentatonix Official Website : upcoming tour dates|url = http://ptxofficial.com/tour|website = Pentatonix Official Website|access-date = February 17, 2016}}</ref> The tour began on April 2, 2016, in [[Chiba, Chiba|Chiba]], Japan. The second leg ran from May 23 through June 26 in Europe before returning to Japan for two shows in August 2016. The third and fourth legs of the tour (in Oceania and Asia respectively) occurred in September 2016, followed by the fifth leg in North America from October 17 to November 22, 2016. The tour's 2017 shows kicked off in May with another five shows in Japan. The final leg of the tour began July 2, 2017 in [[Los Angeles]] and continued across the United States before concluding in Vermont on September 3, 2017.<ref name="Tour">{{cite web|url=http://ptxofficial.com/tour|title=UPCOMING TOUR DATES
|website=PTX Official Website|access-date=June 5, 2017}}</ref> The last show marked Kaplan's final performance with the group.
