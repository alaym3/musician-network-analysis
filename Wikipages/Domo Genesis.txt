'''Dominique Marquis Cole''' (born March 9, 1991), known professionally as '''Domo Genesis''', is an American rapper and songwriter. He is best known for being a member of [[Los Angeles]] [[hip hop music|hip hop]] collective [[Odd Future]]. He has since continued his solo career, releasing his debut album ''[[Genesis (Domo Genesis album)|Genesis]]'' in 2016 and founding his own record label in 2018.

==Career==

===2009–2010: ''Rolling Papers''===
Domo Genesis joined [[Odd Future]] in mid-2009 because he was very good friends with [[Tyler, the Creator]]. His debut mixtape, ''[[Rolling Papers (mixtape)|Rolling Papers]]'', was released on August 30, 2010. Production was done entirely by Tyler, the Creator, [[Left Brain]], and [[Syd tha Kyd]].

===2011–2012: ''Under the Influence'' and ''No Idols''===
His second mixtape, ''[[Under the Influence (mixtape)|Under the Influence]]'', was released on September 20, 2011. Many saw this second release as a vast improvement over his first, citing "it's focused and rarely lacking in style — or yeah, SWAG.".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/music_blog/2011/09/odd-future-domo-genesis-stays-under-the-influence-on-new-free-mixtape.html|title=Domo Genesis stays 'Under the Influence' on new mixtape|work=LA Times|author=Jeff Weiss|date=September 22, 2011|access-date=March 19, 2012}}</ref> On February 9, 2012, Domo released a track with [[Wiz Khalifa]], titled "Ground Up".<ref>[http://hulkshare.com/qc500vje3ms3 Listen to Ground Up on Hulkshare – Free Music Streaming & Download]. Hulkshare.com. Retrieved on 2012-08-20.</ref> On February 22, 2012, it was announced that Domo Genesis would have a strain of [[marijuana|medical marijuana]] named after him, ''Domo OG''.<ref>[http://www.oddfuturetalk.com/forums/topic/14150-domo-genesis-og/ Domo Genesis O.G. – Domo Genesis / Mike G – OddFutureTalk Forums]. Oddfuturetalk.com (2012-02-22). Retrieved on 2012-08-20.</ref>

Domo Genesis made his TV debut in the tenth episode of the first season of Odd Future's show, [[Loiter Squad]], as Young Gunshot, his [[gangsta rap]] alter ego. Domo Genesis and [[The Alchemist (musician)|Alchemist]]'s collaboration mixtape, ''[[No Idols]]'', was released for free download on July 31, 2012. The first official single, "[[Elimination Chamber (song)|Elimination Chamber]]", which features [[Earl Sweatshirt]], [[Vince Staples]] and [[Action Bronson]], was released on July 16, 2012.

===2013–2014: ''MellowHigh'' and ''Under the Influence 2''===
On April 4, 2013, Domo, with fellow Odd Future members Tyler, The Creator and Earl Sweatshirt, performed the song "Rusty" off of Tyler, The Creator's second studio album, ''[[Wolf (Tyler, The Creator album)|Wolf]]'', on [[The Late Show with David Letterman]]. On October 31, 2013, Domo released ''[[MellowHigh (album)|MellowHigh]]'' his collaboration album with [[MellowHype]] as [[MellowHigh]]. The album debuted at number 89 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26130|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140126120723/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26130|url-status=dead|archive-date=26 January 2014|title=Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales - HipHopDX|author=HipHopDX|date=6 November 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref>

On November 13, 2013, Domo Genesis revealed that he was working on a new mixtape ''[[Under the Influence 2]]'' and his debut studio album.<ref name="stupiddope.com">{{cite web|url=http://stupiddope.com/2013/11/13/mellowhigh-opens-up-about-their-album-having-kids-more-video/|title=MellowHigh Opens Up About Their Album, Having Kids & More - Video|work=stupidDOPE|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> Then on April 3, 2014, Genesis said that he plans to release both projects before the end of 2014.<ref name="hiphopdx">{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.28167/title.domo-genesis-on-odd-future-it-s-time-to-step-up-for-myself-|title=Domo Genesis On Odd Future: "It's Time To Step Up For Myself"|author=HipHopDX|date=3 April 2014|work=HipHopDX|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> He then hinted on Twitter that the release of ''Under the Influence 2'' would be on November 5, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/DamierGenesis/status/522110076300312577|title=The Peoples Champ. on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref>

===2015–2017: ''Genesis'' and ''Red Corolla''===
[[File:Domogenesis losangeles.jpg|thumb|right|Domo performing in [[Los Angeles]] in March 2016]]
On January 4, 2016, Domo debuted a new track over SoundCloud, "KWYM", which stands for "Keep Working Young Man". It is produced by MaffYuu.

On March 10, 2016, the lead single, "Dapper" featuring [[Anderson .Paak]], to Domo's debut studio album, ''[[Genesis (Domo Genesis album)|Genesis]]'', was released. On March 17, 2016, the second single, "Go (Gas)" featuring Wiz Khalifa, [[Juicy J]] & Tyler, The Creator, was released. ''Genesis'' was released digitally on March 25, 2016.

From May 29 to July 24, 2016, Domo embarked on the "2016 Chore Tour" in support of his album release.

On June 16, 2017, Domo released a mixtape, titled "Red Corolla". It can be streamed/downloaded through SoundCloud, HotNewHipHop, Datpiff, Mixtape Monkey and Domogenesis.com

===2018–present: ''Aren't U Glad You're U?''===
On January 19, 2018, Domo released his latest mixtape. The mixtape, titled ''Aren't U Glad You're U?'' was released independently on [[SoundCloud]]. Fellow rapper Evidence serves as the executive producer for the mixtape. That same day, the official music video for the song "Me Vs. Me" was released on the official [[Odd Future]] YouTube channel.

== Personal life ==
He briefly attended [[Arizona State University]] before leaving his studies to focus on a career in rapping. {{citation needed|date=July 2019}}
