'''In This Moment''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Los Angeles]], California, formed by singer [[Maria Brink]] and guitarist Chris Howorth in 2005. They found drummer Jeff Fabb and started the band as Dying Star. Unhappy with their musical direction, they changed their name to In This Moment and gained two band members, guitarist Blake Bunzel and bassist Josh Newell. In 2005, bassist Newell left the band and was replaced by Jesse Landry.

Their debut album, ''[[Beautiful Tragedy]]'', was released in 2007. Their follow up, titled ''[[The Dream (In This Moment album)|The Dream]]'' was released the following year debuting at number 73 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. The band's third album, ''[[A Star-Crossed Wasteland]]'' was released in 2010, and their fourth album titled ''[[Blood (In This Moment album)|Blood]]'' was released in August 2012 and debuted at number 15. Their fifth album titled ''[[Black Widow (In This Moment album)|Black Widow]]'' was released in November 2014 and debuted at number 8, their highest position in the charts to date. ''[[Ritual (In This Moment album)|Ritual]]'', their sixth, was released in the summer of 2017 and debuted at number 23. Their seventh studio album, ''[[Mother (In This Moment album)|Mother]]'' was released on March 27, 2020. They have received two nominations of Alternative Press Music Awards, including two for Best Hard Rock Artist and one for Best Live Band. They have also received a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance.

The band's line-up has changed several times. Landry was replaced by Kyle Konkiel in 2009 and Konkiel was replaced by Travis Johnson in 2010. Jeff Fabb and Blake Bunzel left the band in 2011, and were replaced by Tom Hane and Randy Weitzel respectively. In March 2016, drummer Tom Hane announced his departure from the band and was replaced by Kent Diimmel, formerly of 3 By Design.

Throughout their career, the band has performed on several notable tours and festivals including [[Ozzfest]] in 2007 and 2008; [[Warped Tour]] in 2009; [[Download Festival]] in 2009, 2013, and 2018; [[Mayhem Festival]] in 2010; [[Music as a Weapon]] V tour in 2011; [[Uproar Festival]] in 2012; [[Rock on the Range]] in 2012, 2013, and 2015; [[Carnival of Madness]] tour in 2013; [[Knotfest]] in 2014; [[Rockfest]] and [[Rocklahoma]] in 2015; [[Carolina Rebellion]] in 2018; [[Louder Than Life]] in 2017 and 2019; [[Aftershock Festival#Aftershock 2014|Aftershock]] in 2014, 2017, and 2021; and [[Welcome to Rockville]] in 2013, 2015, 2017, and 2019.

We're gonna try to replicate the magic of ''Blood'' and try to take the style and sound we had for ''Blood'' and just do it bigger and better while still keeping true to that same vibe. "The In-Between" was released on January 22, 2020.<ref name="Cos Mother"/> On January 22, 2020, the band officially announced that their upcoming seventh studio album titled, ''[[Mother (In This Moment album)|Mother]]'' is set for release on March 27, 2020.<ref name="Cos Mother"/><ref name="MetalSucks Mother"/><ref name="Kerrang">{{Cite web |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/in-this-moment-announce-new-album-mother-release-new-video/ |title=In This Moment Announce New Album Mother, Release New Video |date=January 22, 2020 |work=[[Kerrang!]] |access-date=January 22, 2020}}</ref><ref name="The PRP Mother"/> The official album art and tracklisting has been also released.<ref name="Cos Mother"/><ref name="Kerrang"/> On February 21 2020, the band released the second single of the album titled "Hunting Grounds" featuring Joe Cotela of [[Ded (band)|Ded]].<ref name="MetalSucks Mother"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/in-this-moment-hunting-grounds-joe-cotela-ded/ |title=In This Moment Reveal Groove-Filled Track "Hunting Grounds" |last1=Smith-Engelhardt |first1=Joe |website=Alternative Press |date=February 21, 2020 |access-date=February 22, 2020}}</ref> In March 2020, In This Moment cancelled their 2020 tour with [[Black Veil Brides]], Ded, and Raven Black due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="NextMosh Covid"/><ref name="The PRP Mother"/> On March 19 2020, a week before the album release, the band released their third single "As Above, So Below".<ref name="The PRP Mother"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/listen-to-new-in-this-moment-song-as-above-so-below/|title=Listen To New In This Moment Song 'As Above So Below'|date=March 20, 2020|website=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|access-date=March 20, 2020}}</ref>

In November 2020, the band was nominated for their first Grammy award. The band received a nomination in the Best Metal Performance category for the song "The In-Between."<ref>{{cite web |title=Grammys 2021 Nominees Revealed: BeyoncÃ©, Phoebe Bridgers, Fiona Apple, Taylor Swift |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/11/grammy-2021-nominees/ |website=Consequence of Sound |date=November 24, 2020|access-date=March 14, 2021}}</ref> The band [[Body Count (band)|Body Count]] won the award.<ref>{{cite web|first=Ellise|last=Shafer|title=Grammys 2021 Winners List (Updating Live)|url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/2021-grammys-winners-list-1234926947/ |website=Variety|date=March 14, 2021|access-date=March 14, 2021}}</ref>

In May 2021, the band announced "The In-Between Tour" with Black Veil Brides, Ded, and Raven Black starting in September 2021. This tour was originally scheduled for March 2020 but had to be rescheduled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref>{{cite web|first=Eli|last=Enis|title=In This Moment and Black Veil Brides Announce North American Tour|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/moment-and-black-veil-brides-announce-north-american-tour|website=Revolver Magazine|date=May 17, 2021|access-date=May 17, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Philip|last=Trapp|title=In This Moment + Black Veil Brides Announce Joint 2021 US Tour|url=https://loudwire.com/in-this-moment-black-veil-brides-2021-tour-dates/|website=Loudwire|date=May 17, 2021|access-date=May 17, 2021}}</ref>
