He began his career in 2011 when he started writing and recording his own music as a sophomore at [[Michigan State University]], before releasing his debut EP ''Change of Scenery'' in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.last.fm/music/Quinn+XCII/+wiki|title=Quinn XCII biography|publisher=[[Last.fm]]|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref> On September 15, 2017, he released his debut studio album ''The Story of Us'' under [[Columbia Records]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailynewsproject.com/blog/2017/9/15/quinn-xcii-releases-latest-album-the-story-of-us|title=Quinn XCII Releases Latest Album "The Story of Us"|publisher=Daily News Project|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref> with his second album ''From Michigan with Love'' released on February 15, 2019.<ref name="FromMichiganWithLove_2019">{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1059514803302019074|title=My sophomore album, "From Michigan With Love" coming top of 2019.|date=5 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="FromMichiganWithLove_Feb15">{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1082427876190826496|title=[image:MY SOPHOMORE ALBUM // FROM MICHIGAN WITH LOVE // COMING FEBRUARY 15]|date=7 January 2019}}</ref> His third studio album ''A Letter to My Younger Self'' was released on July 10, 2020.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=I spent the last year making my favorite body of work yet and it's finally yours this summer. ==Early life==
Mikael Temrowski was born on May 6, 1992 in Grosse Pointe, [[Michigan]].{{citation needed|date=June 2021}} He grew up listening to the city's [[Motown]] musical history.

He met his primary producer and close friend [[Ayokay]] in third grade of elementary school and remained close friends throughout high school. Temrowski began attending college at [[Michigan State University]], where he took an interest in writing and recording music as a sophomore.

==Career==
===2011â€“2016: Early career and EPs===
Temrowski originally went by the stage name "Mike T" and started performing and uploading videos to the video-sharing website [[YouTube]].<ref name="Pseudonym Week: Quinn XCII">{{Cite web|url=https://www.michigandaily.com/section/arts/pseudonym-week-quinn-xcii|title = Pseudonym Week: Quinn XCII|date = 16 April 2019}}</ref> In college, he adopted the name Quinn based on an acronym used by a professor: "Quit Unless Your Instincts Are Never Neglected."<ref name="Pseudonym Week: Quinn XCII"/><ref name="onairwithryan.iheart.com">{{Cite web|url=https://onairwithryan.iheart.com/content/2019-08-07-quinn-xcii-explains-his-stage-name-and-how-to-pronounce-i/|title = Quinn XCII Explains His Stage Name and How to Pronounce It}}</ref> Because of trademark issues, he later added the Roman numeral for 92, the year of his birth.<ref name="onairwithryan.iheart.com"/> He rapped over beats sampled from popular songs before releasing his own original song, "They Know" in 2011, followed by his first extended play ''Old Fashioned'' on November 5, 2012. During his attendance at [[Michigan State University]] around 2014, Temrowski started taking a more serious interest in writing and recording music. His longtime friend and producer, who goes by the stage name Ayokay, began producing music together, with Quinn XCII rapping over beats Ayokay found off of YouTube.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://theknockturnal.com/quinn-xcii-and-ayokay-interview/|title=Exclusive: Get To Know Quinn XCII And Ayokay [Interview]|last=Cohen|first=Sarah|work=The Knockturnal|access-date=May 23, 2018}}</ref> Quinn XCII quickly became a college mixtape sensation, and would begin to travel frequently to [[Ann Arbor]] to record new music with Ayokay.

During May 2015, Quinn XCII released his debut extended play titled ''Change of Scenery'', which garnered critical acclaim and millions of online streams. His next single, "Stung", climbed to the #1 position on [[Hype Machine]]'s Popular Charts. In January 2016, Quinn XCII featured on Ayokay's own single titled "Kings of Summer" which soared to #1 on the [[Spotify]] Global Viral Chart where it stayed there for three consecutive weeks, in addition to 25 million streams on Spotify. His collaboration with producer [[Illenium]] on a track titled "With You" reached #1 on the HypeMachine Popular Chart and reached #20 on the Spotify Global Viral Chart.

Quinn XCII released his highly anticipated second EP titled ''Bloom'' on March 10, 2016. Shortly after releasing the EP, Quinn XCII went on his first nationwide tour where he performed in 24 cities for over 20,000 fans.

===2016â€“2018: ''The Story of Us''===
On December 9, 2016, Quinn XCII released his new single "Straightjacket", taken off of his upcoming debut album ''The Story of Us''. A non-album single was released titled "Make Time", succeeded by two singles from the album that were released preceding the album's release in 2017, "Fake Denim" and "Worst". ''The Story of Us'' was released on September 15, 2017 under [[Columbia Records]]. A deluxe edition of the album was released on February 16, 2018, supported by the new single "Iron & Steel".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/entertainment/music/review-quinn-xcii-delights-on-new-radio-single-iron-steel/article/514604|title=Review: Quinn XCII delights on new radio single 'Iron & Steel'|date=12 February 2018|publisher=Digital Journal|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref>

In April 2018, it was announced that Quinn XCII was added to the management roster of [[Visionary Music Group]] through a partnership with his manager Jesse Coren.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8354278/visionary-music-group-bolsters-management-division-mutual-friends-partnership|title=Visionary Music Group Bolsters Management Division With New Partnership With Mutual Friends|last=Rys|first=Dan|work=Billboard}}</ref>

===2018&ndash;2020: ''From Michigan with Love''===
Quinn XCII released his first single since ''A Story of Us'' titled "Panama" on August 22, 2018, on the music-sharing website [[SoundCloud]]. He wrote the song in honor of his four late grandparents,<ref>{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1032413436725080064|title=Today, my final grandparent & Nana, Beverly Temrowski, passed away. I figured today would be the best time to release the most personal song Iâ€™ve ever made. This song is for my four wonderful grandparents and for anyone who has lost someone dear to them.|date=22 August 2018}}</ref> and was later released on all platforms on August 29.<ref>{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1034847184863121408|title="Panama" is now available everywhere|date=29 August 2018}}</ref>

On October 23, 2018, Quinn XCII announced a new upcoming single titled "Werewolf" featuring Yoshi Flower, and was released two days later on October 25.<ref>{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1055676022174179329|title="Werewolf" out now everywhere you listen to music: https://t.co/RXhDSN6tebâ€¦|date=25 October 2018}}</ref> A week later, he released another single "Sad Still" on November 1, centered around the struggles and feelings of dealing with [[anxiety]].<ref>{{cite Twitter|user=TeamVisionary|number=1058217179521331200|title=Part singing, part rapping. @QuinnXCII brings the best of both worlds together with a message many can relate to on his new "Sad Still" single available now. https://t.co/HEzC6Ft12lâ€¦|date=1 November 2018}}</ref> He soon announced his second album titled ''From Michigan with Love'' via social media on November 5, 2018, which contains the latter two singles. The album was teased for an early 2019 release date.<ref name="FromMichiganWithLove_2019" /> On December 11, 2018, Quinn XCII announced the third single off the album, titled "Tough" featuring Noah Kahan, and was released on December 14.<ref>{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1072572285859627008|title="Tough" with @NoahKahan out Friday ðŸ’ª|date=11 December 2018}}</ref>

On November 6, 2018, Quinn XCII announced his worldwide 2019 tour for ''From Michigan with Love'', titled "From Tour with Love". The tour includes shows at 8 cities in Europe and then 30 cities in North America with Christian French and Ashe co-headlining the tour. The tour kicks off in [[Amsterdam]], the [[Netherlands]] on February 3, and then the North American leg on February 19 in [[Indianapolis]], [[Indiana]] and ends on April 17 in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], [[Texas]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8483495/quinn-xcii-2019-tour-dates-from-michigan-with-love-album|title=Quinn XCII Announces 2019 Tour & Forthcoming Album 'From Michigan With Love': Exclusive|work=Billboard|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref>

Quinn XCII announced via social media on January 7, 2019 that ''From Michigan with Love'' is set to be released on February 15. On February 15, it was released with 12 songs.<ref name="FromMichiganWithLove_Feb15" />

Quinn XCII teased his new single "Stacy" on June 18, 2019 on Twitter.<ref>{{cite Twitter|user=QuinnXCII|number=1141066316977180673|title=Out everywhere Friday|date=18 June 2019}}</ref> The single was released that week on June 21, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Stacy - Single by Quinn XCII |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/stacy-single/1468504944 |website=Apple Music |publisher=Apple Inc. |access-date=22 December 2019 |language=en-us}}</ref>

=== 2020: ''A Letter to My Younger Self'' ===
On January 10, 2020, he released a single titled "Two 10s".<ref>{{cite web|title=Two 10s - Single by Quinn XCII|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/1491357491|website=[[Apple Music]]|publisher=[[iTunes]]|language=en-us|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref>

On April 27, 2020 Quinn XCII announced his third studio album ''A Letter to My Younger Self'', inspired by a lifetime of events and experiences. It was released on July 10, 2020.<ref name=":0" /> On April 28, 2020 he teased his new single titled "Coffee" with [[Marc E. Bassy]] that was released on Friday, May 1, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Coffee w/ @marcebassy coming Fridaypic.twitter.com/yMt3jY51nt|url=https://twitter.com/QuinnXCII/status/1255192637629435909|last=XCII|first=Quinn|date=2020-04-28|website=@QuinnXCII|language=en|access-date=2020-05-20}}</ref>

===2021: ''Change of Scenery II''===
Quinn XCII released his third studio album on March 5, 2021. Abandoning his residence in Los Angeles, the album was produced at ayokayâ€™s family home in Rhode Island. He released two singles ahead of the release, "Stay Next to Me (with Chelsea Cutler)" and "My Wife & 2 Dogs."

==Style==
Quinn XCII has been known to blend several different genres of music, including hip-hop, pop, reggae, rock, electronic and soul. His early music had him focused more on rapping than singing. He has listed [[Kid Cudi]], [[Kanye West]], [[Mos Def]], [[Jack Johnson (musician)|Jack Johnson]], [[Jon Bellion]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Sam Cooke]], and [[Chance the Rapper]] as artists who have influenced him.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/IAmA/comments/71soeg/hey_reddit_quinn_xcii_here_ama/dnd6j11/|title=Hey Reddit, Quinn XCII here. AMA.|date=22 September 2017|publisher=[[Reddit]]|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref>
