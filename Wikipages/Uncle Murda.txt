'''Leonard Grant''' (born July 25, 1980), better known by his stage name '''Uncle Murda''', is an American rapper from [[East New York, Brooklyn]]. He is currently signed to [[G-Unit Records]].<ref>{{cite web|author=by D.L. Chandler |url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.41849/title.uncle-murda-is-officially-a-member-of-50-cents-g-unit |title=Brooklyn Rapper Uncle Murda Signs With 50 Cent's G-Unit |publisher=HipHopDX |date=2016-12-30 |access-date=2017-06-23}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2007 his manager and [[DJ Green Lantern]] introduced Uncle Murda's music to [[Jay-Z]].{{citation needed|date=May 2021}} The Brooklyn-born rapper/CEO signed Uncle Murda to [[Roc-A-Fella]] Records soon after. {{citation needed|date=May 2021}} They started to promote him immediately by placing him on a track with popular label-mates [[Fabolous]] and [[Jay-Z]] for the song "Brooklyn" (featured on ''[[From Nothin' to Somethin']]'' album) and everything seemed to be going well for the rapper.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last=Lilah|first=Rose|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dj-kay-slay-the-last-hip-hop-disciple-new-mixtape.112221.html?|title=DJ Kay Slay - The Last Hip-Hop Disciple|date=2014-05-08|publisher=HowNewHipHop}}</ref>  However in late 2008 rumors that Uncle Murda left [[Def Jam]] came about.<ref name=":0" /> In an interview with DJ Vlad, Uncle Murda confirmed the rumors that he has split from [[Def Jam]] and [[Roc-A-Fella Records]] claiming everything went downhill since [[Jay-Z]]'s departure.<ref name=":0" />

On November 8, 2016, Uncle Murda signed a deal with [[50 Cent]] and [[G-Unit Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BMkWu6ZhFtv/ |title=Instagram |publisher=Instagram |date=2016-11-08 |access-date=2017-06-23}}</ref>

===Shooting===
On January 22, 2008, Uncle Murda was shot directly in the head while in a parked car on [[Linden Boulevard|Linden Blvd.]] in [[East New York]], but was not seriously hurt. Murda would later give contradictory accounts of the incident, saying he was only grazed by a bullet or hit by a piece of glass.  The shooting happened after the rapper released his mixtape entitled "Respect the Shooter". He discharged himself from the hospital less than 24 hours after he was admitted, with the bullet still in his skull. Uncle Murda called the nationally syndicated ''[[The Wendy Williams Experience]]'' radio show after the shooting and told her he was using "[[Hennessy]] and [[Newport (cigarette)|Newports]] to cope with the pain".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapbasement.com/news/roc-a-fella/uncle-murda-shot-in-the-head-using-hennessey-and-newports-for-pain.html |title=Uncle Murda Shot In The Head - Using Hennessey And Newports For Pain &#124; Roc A Fella |publisher=Rap Basement |date=2008-01-24 |access-date=2011-11-26}}</ref> No charges were filed, as Uncle Murda refused to cooperate with investigators, blaming the police for the shooting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2008/01/25/uncle-murda-shot-in-the-head-blames-police/ |title=Uncle Murda Shot In The Head, Blames Police |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=2008-01-25 |access-date=2014-08-22}}</ref>
