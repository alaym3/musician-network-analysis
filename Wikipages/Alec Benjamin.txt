'''Alec Shane Benjamin'''<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=744747089997922304|user=AlecBenjamin|title=Alec Shane Benjamin :)|author=alecbenjamin|date=June 20, 2016}}</ref> (born May 28, 1994<ref>{{Cite web|last=Donelson|first=Marcy|title=Alec Benjamin {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/alec-benjamin-mn0003523439/biography|access-date=August 20, 2020|website=[[AllMusic]]|language=en-us}}</ref>) is an American singer-songwriter from [[Phoenix, Arizona]]. His 2018 breakthrough single "[[Let Me Down Slowly]]" reached the top 40 in over 25 countries and it has amassed over 880 million streams on [[Spotify]] as of October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Tracy|first=Brianne|date=May 29, 2020|title=Alec Benjamin Talks New Album, 'Struggle' with Social Media: 'It's Harder to Just Make Music'|url=https://people.com/music/alec-benjamin-new-album-social-media-struggle/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200622105127/https://people.com/music/alec-benjamin-new-album-social-media-struggle/|archive-date=June 22, 2020|access-date=August 20, 2020|website=[[People (magazine)|People]]|language=EN}}</ref> Benjamin primarily writes and sings songs in the [[English language]] but also performs in [[Mandarin Chinese]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|title=Alec Benjamin Announces Additional North American Tour Dates|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Alec-Benjamin-Announces-Additional-North-American-Tour-Dates-20210616|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-09|website=BroadwayWorld.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Benjamin was born and raised in [[Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix]], [[Arizona]], but lived in [[Long Island]], [[New York (state)|New York]] during [[kindergarten]] and [[first grade]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Alec Benjamin Is Rising 'Slowly,' But Surely|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8509418/alec-benjamin-interview|last=Ginsberg|first=Gab|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=April 30, 2019}}</ref> He has a sister for whom he wrote his song "If We Have Each Other".<ref>{{cite web|title=â€˜These Two Windowsâ€™ successfully lights a match in the rain|url=https://thetech.com/2020/07/09/these-two-windows-review|last1=Lin|first1=Joanna|last2=Wu|first2=Wenbo|work=[[The Tech (newspaper)|The Tech]]|date=July 9, 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
Initially, Benjamin signed to [[Columbia Records]], while still enrolled at [[University of Southern California]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alec Benjamin|url=https://www.warnerchappell.com/artist-details/76|access-date=April 3, 2020|website=Warnerchappel}}</ref> However, Columbia Records ended their partnership weeks after he submitted his album.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Theatre|first=Royal Oak Music|title=Alec Benjamin {{!}} Royal Oak Music Theatre|url=https://www.royaloakmusictheatre.com/events/detail/390734|access-date=2020-03-11|website=www.royaloakmusictheatre.com|language=en}}</ref> Despite this, he went on a self-funded tour in Europe to open for Marina Kaye in France and Lea in Germany. He promoted his music by performing in parking lots outside of concert venues and handing out business cards.<ref name=":0" />

Benjamin's song "I Built a Friend" was used as backing material for Merrick Hanna's contemporary dance audition in the [[America's Got Talent (season 12)|12th season]] of ''[[America's Got Talent]]''. In addition, Hanna used Benjamin's cover of "[[Lost Boy (Ruth B song)|Lost Boy]]" by Ruth B for his live quarter-final performance later in the season.<ref>{{cite web|title=Merrick Hanna Best Dance w Judges Comments "Best Act" Quarter Finals America's Got Talent 2017 Live|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IbVJBjMe4Z0|last=|first=|work=|date=|website=YouTube}}</ref>

Benjamin's mixtape ''Narrated For You'' reached number 127 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] in 2019. The mixtape features the songs "Water Fountain" and "[[Let Me Down Slowly]]", the latter of which was rereleased as a single with [[Alessia Cara]].<ref>{{Cite web|title= Alec Benjamin Breaks Down His 'Narrated For You' Mixtape: Exclusive|last= Lukarcanin| first= Emina| url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8486042/alec-benjamin-narrated-for-you-mixtape-breakdown|date=July 2020}}</ref> To stay true to his roots while promoting the mixtape, he created "Can I Sing For You?" videos on YouTube in which he performs his written songs in acoustic to people in public.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Firman|first=Tehrene|title=Press Play on This Exclusive "Paper Crown" Music Video, Fall Totally in Love with Jersey-Wearing, Boombox-Toting Alec Benjamin|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/alec-benjamin-paper-crown-music-video-exclusive-premiere|access-date=2016-06-14|website=Teen Vogue}}</ref> In 2019, he recorded two singles with Spotify Studios NYC, one of which was a cover of Eminem's "[[Stan (Eminem song)|Stan]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Singer-Songwriter Alec Benjamin Covers Eminem's "Stan" For Spotify|url=https://genius.com/a/singer-songwriter-alec-benjamin-covers-eminem-s-stan-for-spotify|access-date=2020-03-05|website=Genius|language=en}}</ref> He performed at the music festival KAABOO in Del Mar, CA in 2019.

Benjamin's debut album ''[[These Two Windows]]'' was released on May 29, 2020. The album reached number 75 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. The album features the single "Oh My God"; the song did not enter the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart but peaked at number 32 on the [[Mainstream Top 40]] chart and at number 26 on the [[Adult Top 40]] chart.{{citation needed|date=July 2020}}. To promote the album, he brought back "The Story Behind the Story" videos on YouTube, a series he started with the original versions of "I Built a Friend" and "Water Fountain". The videos explain the background and the lyrics behind some of his biggest songs.

In addition to his solo project, Benjamin has co-written songs for artists such as [[Jon Bellion]], and has a songwriting credit on the track "New York Soul (Part II)" from Bellion's album ''[[The Human Condition (Jon Bellion album)|The Human Condition]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jon Beion - New York Soul (Part II)|url=https://genius.com/Jon-bellion-new-york-soul-part-ii-lyrics}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
In a brief interview, Benjamin says he's influenced by [[Eminem]], [[John Mayer]], [[Jason Mraz]], [[Paul Simon]], and [[Chris Martin]] of [[Coldplay]].<ref name=":1">{{Citation|title=Alec Benjamin Speaks Mandarin, Talks Musical Inspiration and Upcoming Album!|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEDXgrNCsyI|language=en|access-date=2021-06-11}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Known for his honesty and vulnerability in his songwriting and his support of mental health awareness,<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Alec Benjamin on Apple Music|url=https://music.apple.com/us/artist/alec-benjamin/632781795|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Apple Music}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=January 30, 2021|title=alecbenjamin on Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/AlecBenjamin/status/1355589467483103233?s=20|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Twitter}}</ref> Benjamin has stated in separate interviews that he has [[social anxiety]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Alec Benjamin Discusses His New Single "If We Have Each Other"|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lDaj0BE6QnU|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Youtube}}</ref> and [[OCD]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Alec Benjamin "Mind Is A Prison" Official Lyrics & Meaning &#124; Verified|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbmTwSfQxOs|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Youtube}}</ref>
