'''Justin Cole Moore''' (born March 30, 1984) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter, signed to [[Big Machine Records]] imprint Valory Music Group. For that label, he has released six studio albums: his [[Justin Moore (album)|self titled debut]] in 2009, ''[[Outlaws Like Me]]'' in 2011, ''[[Off the Beaten Path (Justin Moore album)|Off the Beaten Path]]'' in 2013, ''[[Kinda Don't Care]]'' in 2016, ''[[Late Nights and Longnecks]]'' in 2019, and ''Straight Outta the Country'' in 2021. He has also charted sixteen times on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts, including with the number 1 singles "[[Small Town USA]]", "[[If Heaven Wasn't So Far Away]]", "[[Til My Last Day]]", "[[Lettin' the Night Roll]]", "[[You Look Like I Need a Drink]]", "[[Somebody Else Will]]", "[[The Ones That Didn't Make It Back Home]]", and "[[Why We Drink]]"; and the top 10 hits "[[Backwoods (Justin Moore song)|Backwoods]]" and "[[Point at You]]".

==Music career==
Moore began performing during his junior year of high school.<ref name="augusta">[http://chronicle.augusta.com/things-do/going-out/music/2010-08-18/road-leads-augusta-moore Emerson, LaTina. (2010, August 18). ''Road leads to Augusta for Moore''. The Augusta Chronicle.] Retrieved August 19, 2010.</ref> After graduating, he joined his uncle's Southern rock band and moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], in 2002.<ref name="augusta"/> He met a young producer in Nashville, [[Jeremy Stover]], who introduced him to [[Scott Borchetta]], an industry executive who was planning to launch The Valory Music Co. Borchetta promised to give him a record deal if he would be patient.<ref name="augusta"/>

===2008–2010===
In mid-2008, Moore signed to the Valory Music Group, an imprint of the independent record label [[Big Machine Records]]. The label then released the digital single "I Could Kick Your Ass". His first radio single, "[[Back That Thing Up]]", was co-written by [[Randy Houser]] and Moore's producer, [[Jeremy Stover]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lyricsfreak.com/j/justin+moore/back+that+thing+up_20845575.htm|title=Back That Thing Up Lyrics- Justin Moore|access-date=March 16, 2013|work= Lyricsfreak.com}}</ref> It reached number 38 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart. He continued working on his debut album, which was part of a special promotion called "So You Want to Be a Record Label Executive". This promotion placed his music on social networking sites such as [[MySpace]] and [[iLike]], where fans were allowed to create playlists comprising ten of his songs; the Top 10 songs picked were then included on the final album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clevelandleader.com/node/7311 |title=Justin Moore wants you to produce his album |access-date=November 13, 2008 |last=Petruziello |first=Francis |date=October 14, 2008 |work=[[The Plain Dealer|The Cleveland Leader]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090814172842/http://www.clevelandleader.com/node/7311 |archive-date=August 14, 2009 }}</ref> His next single, "[[Small Town USA]]", entered the charts in February 2009,<ref name="allmusic"/> followed by a digital [[extended play|EP]] entitled ''The "You Asked for It" EP''.

On August 11, the label released his [[Justin Moore (album)|self-titled]] debut album, on which he co-wrote nine of the ten tracks.<ref name="boot">{{cite web|url=http://www.theboot.com/2009/08/12/justin-moore-does-big-things-with-small-town/|title=Justin Moore Does Big Things With 'Small Town'|last=Darden|first=Beville|date=August 12, 2009|work=The Boot|access-date=September 21, 2009}}</ref> This album debuted at No.&nbsp;3 on the [[Top Country Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=3178|title=Strait leads all music in sales|date=August 20, 2009|work=[[Country Standard Time]]|access-date=September 21, 2009}}</ref> He promoted the single and album on a "Small Town USA" tour which began in his hometown of Poyen, Arkansas and included several stops in small towns, as well as acoustic shows at [[Walmart]] stores.<ref name="boot"/> On the Hot Country Songs charts dated October 3, 2009, "Small Town USA" became his first No.&nbsp;1 single. "[[Backwoods (Justin Moore song)|Backwoods]]" was released as the album's third single in October 2009. It became his second Top 10 hit with a peak of No.&nbsp;6 in April 2010. The album's fourth single, "[[How I Got to Be This Way]]", reached Top 20.

===2011–present===
In February 2011, he released the song "[[If Heaven Wasn't So Far Away]]", which was originally recorded by [[Rhett Akins]] on his 2007 album ''People Like Me'' but did not chart. Justin Moore's rendition debuted at 46 on the Hot Country songs chart. In June 2011, the song became his third Top 10 hit on that chart and three weeks later, it became his second Number One. The song serves as the lead-off single to his 2011 album ''[[Outlaws Like Me]]'', which was released on June 21, 2011. Follow-up singles "[[Bait a Hook]]" and "[[Til My Last Day]]" both broke the Top 20; the latter reached Number One on the [[Country Airplay]] chart.

According to Taste of Country, a fatal accident involving Joseph Taylor and Justin Moore's tour bus occurred on May 12, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/justin-moore-bus-accident/ |title=One Dead in Justin Moore Bus Accident |website=Tasteofcountry.com |access-date=April 4, 2020}}</ref> In December 2012, Moore announced plans to embark on a headlining tour in 2013, at the time tentatively planned to begin in March.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/news/more-moore |title=More of Moore? |publisher=Country Weekly |date=December 3, 2012 |access-date=December 6, 2012 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ChMu2zaJ?url=http://www.countryweekly.com/news/more-moore |archive-date=December 6, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  Later in the month, he entered the studio to begin recording his third studio album.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://tasteofcountry.com/justin-moore-new-album/ | title=Justin Moore Begins Recording New Album | publisher=Taste of Country | date=December 19, 2012 | access-date=December 20, 2012}}</ref>  The album's first single, "[[Point at You]]", was released on March 18, 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.theboot.com/2013/02/25/justin-moore-new-album/ | title=Justin Moore New Album Shares New 'Point' of View | publisher=The Boot | date=February 25, 2013 | access-date=March 5, 2013}}</ref> It peaked at No. 2 on the Country Airplay chart in October 2013. The album, entitled ''[[Off the Beaten Path (Justin Moore album)|Off the Beaten Path]]'', was released on September 17, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|last=Paxman|first=Bob|title=Justin Moore Previews New Album, "Off the Beaten Path"|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/news/justin-moore-previews-new-album-beaten-path|access-date=June 8, 2013|newspaper=[[Country Weekly]]|date=June 6, 2013}}</ref> The album's second single, "[[Lettin' the Night Roll]]", was released on October 21, 2013, and became his fourth number one single on the Country Airplay chart in July 2014.

After that single's release, Moore sang guest vocals along with [[Thomas Rhett]] on [[Brantley Gilbert]]'s 2014 single "[[Small Town Throwdown]]". Just like Moore himself, Rhett and Gilbert are also signed to Valory. Later, he recorded a cover of [[Mötley Crüe]]'s [[Home Sweet Home (Mötley Crüe song)|"Home Sweet Home"]] as a duet with the band's lead singer [[Vince Neil]]. This song is featured on the multi-artist tribute album ''[[Nashville Outlaws: A Tribute to Mötley Crüe]]'', which was released by Big Machine on August 19, 2014. A third single from ''Off the Beaten Path'', "[[This Kind of Town]]", was released on October 20, 2014.

Moore's fourth album, ''[[Kinda Don't Care]]'', was released in August 2016. Its lead single is "[[You Look Like I Need a Drink]]", which became another No.&nbsp;1 single on the Country Airplay charts in late 2016.

Moore announced his fifth album, ''[[Late Nights and Longnecks]]'', in February 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/justin-moore-stepped-on-by-horse/ |title=Justin Moore Reveals New Album Title - and a Bad Injury |website=Theboot.com |access-date=April 4, 2020}}</ref> Its lead single is "[[The Ones That Didn't Make It Back Home]]".

Moore announced his sixth album ''Straight Outta the Country'' on March 25, 2021. The lead single is "[[We Didn't Have Much]]". The album was released on April 23.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Bernstein, Jonathan |date=March 25, 2021 |title=Justin Moore Readies ''Straight Outta the Country'' Project |work=Rolling Stone |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/justin-moore-straight-outta-the-country-1146809/ |access-date=April 3, 2021}}</ref>

==Musical styles==
Steve Leggett of [[AllMusic]] describes him as having "a ready-made image. He was that good kid from a small town with a rowdy heart of gold who just happened to be able to sing about it."<ref name="allmusic"/> He has said that he learned to write songs because, when he had first moved to Nashville, no songwriters wanted to offer him material.<ref name="boot"/> His first album received mixed reviews from music critics: [[Stephen Thomas Erlewine]] of Allmusic referred to it as "anonymous [[country rock]]",<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1621127|pure_url=yes}}|title=''Justin Moore'' review|last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|work=Allmusic|access-date=September 21, 2009}}</ref> and Jeffrey B. Remz of ''[[Country Standard Time]]'' referred to Moore as a "poseur" for name-dropping.<ref name="cst">{{cite web |last=Remz |first=Jeffrey B. |title=''Justin Moore'' review |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/cdreview.asp?xid=4246 |work=Country Standard Time |access-date=August 21, 2009}}</ref> Engine 145's Karlie Justus said that his influences were comparatively more authentic than most other acts on country radio,<ref name="engine145">{{cite web|last=Justus |first=Karlie |title=''Justin Moore'' review |url=http://www.engine145.com/album-review-justin-moore-justin-moore/ |date=August 13, 2009 |work=Engine 145 |access-date=August 21, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141218080443/http://www.engine145.com/album-review-justin-moore-justin-moore/ |archive-date=December 18, 2014 }}</ref> and Matt Bjorke of Roughstock said that his music has "a heavy dose of southern, country charm and twang."<ref name="roughstock">{{cite web|last=Bjorke |first=Matt |title=''Justin Moore'' review |url=http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/justin-moore-justin-moore |work=Roughstock |date=August 11, 2009 |access-date=August 21, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101228091406/http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/justin-moore-justin-moore |archive-date=December 28, 2010 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Justin Moore married his wife, Kate of [[Houma, Louisiana]], in 2007. They have three daughters and a son together.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/justin-moore-expecting-third-child/|title=Third Child on the Way for Justin Moore |website=People |date=November 22, 2011 |access-date=August 28, 2016}}</ref>
He is an avid supporter of the [[Arkansas Razorbacks]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/espn/thelife/music/news/story?id=7156705 |title=Justin Moore and more good sports|website=ESON |date=November 4, 2011 |access-date=August 28, 2016}}</ref>

Moore is a supporter of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. In 2016, he endorsed [[Donald Trump]] in the [[2016 United States presidential election|presidential election]], praising him as an "out of the box" candidate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/justin-moore-supports-donald-trump-president/|title=Justin Moore Supports Donald Trump for President|website=Taste of Country|access-date=December 16, 2017}}</ref>
