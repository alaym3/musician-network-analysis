'''Lontrell Dennell Williams Jr.''' (born November 8, 1999), better known by his stage name '''Pooh Shiesty''', is an American rapper from [[Memphis, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Jefferson|first=Devon|date=January 27, 2021|title=Who Is Pooh Shiesty? 'Black In Blood' Rapper Has The Likes Of Gucci Mane, Lil Durk, Charlamagne Tha God Hype|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.60258/title.who-is-pooh-shiesty-black-in-blood-rapper-has-the-likes-of-gucci-mane-lil-durk-charlamagne-tha-god-hype#|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=February 5, 2021}}</ref> He is signed to [[Gucci Mane]]'s [[1017 Records]] and [[Atlantic Records]].<ref name="XXL-Success2">{{cite web|date=December 2, 2020|title=The Break Presents: Pooh Shiesty â€“ XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/pooh-shiesty-interview-the-break/|access-date=December 2, 2020|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]}}</ref> He rose to fame in 2020 from his collaborations with the rapper and other artists within the label. He is perhaps best known for his 2020 single, "[[Back in Blood (song)|Back in Blood]]" featuring rapper [[Lil Durk]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Findlay|first=Mitch|date=November 6, 2020|title=Lil Durk Joins Pooh Shiesty on "Back in Blood"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-durk-joins-pooh-shiesty-on-back-in-blood-new-song.1989251.html|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=November 6, 2020}}</ref> Pooh Shiesty released his debut commercial mixtape, ''[[Shiesty Season]]'', on February 5, 2021, which peaked at number 3 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. His stage name was given by his late brother, and it comes from his childhood nickname "Mr. Pooh", and the name "Shiesty", referring to his lifestyle.<ref>{{Citation|title=Pooh Shiesty's ABCs|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=79rSnkxlTw8|language=en|access-date=June 22, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Pooh Shiesty Tells UPROXX When He Knew "Back In Blood" Was A Hit {{!}} How I Blew Up|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1aOGiE7sgDE|language=en|access-date=June 22, 2021}}</ref>  He received a co-sign from fellow Memphis rapper [[Juicy J]].<ref>{{cite web|date=January 28, 2021|title=Juicy J Reacts To New Rap Hits On 'The Cosign'|url=https://genius.com/a/juicy-j-reacts-to-mario-judah-cj-pooh-shiesty-on-the-cosign|work=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]|access-date=January 28, 2021}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Shiesty is from [[Memphis, Tennessee]] and grew up in the Cane Creek Apartments on the south side. He lived in [[Pflugerville, Texas]] for two years.<ref name=":0" /> When he returned to Memphis, he took summer school to help him graduate high school.<ref name=":0" /> He began focusing on music at age 18.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=POOH SHIESTY â€“ The New 1017 Records Official Website|url=https://www.thenew1017records.com/poohshiesty/|work=The New 1017 Records|date=December 18, 2020|access-date=December 18, 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2019â€“2020: Commercial debut ===
Pooh Shiesty started off his career with his debut single, "Hell Night", featuring American rapper and good friend [[Big30]], which was released on March 15, 2019, followed by the official music video on February 19, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ Hell Night feat. Big 30 (Official Music Video )|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NDllimEdhaE&list=PLJnWyCFgkJM_im9cY2rRVNEygxc6Ne95W&index=117|work=YouTube|date=February 19, 2020|access-date=September 25, 2020}}</ref> "Shiesty Summer" was then released five months later, on August 20, 2019, succeeded by the music video the following day.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty "Shiesty Summer" (Official Music Video) Dir by @Zach_Hurth|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rDKWNl5tN1o|work=YouTube|date=August 21, 2019|access-date=September 25, 2020}}</ref> His collaborative single, "Choppa Talk", with Big30, was released only three days later, on August 23, 2019, but the video was released a few months prior, on May 6, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty x Big 30 "Choppa talk" (Dir by @Zach_Hurth) (Exclusive â€“ Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cYJuenJO2Ds|work=YouTube|date=May 6, 2019|access-date=September 25, 2020}}</ref> His final release of the year came with the single, "Day One", along with the accompanying visuals, on November 25, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ Day One|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xld3cO-NAH0|work=YouTube|date=November 25, 2019|access-date=September 25, 2020}}</ref>

On March 11, 2020, Shiesty released the single "At It Again", but the visuals were released nine days prior.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ At It Again (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxQDqTqiRJM|work=YouTube|date=March 2, 2020|access-date=December 31, 2020}}</ref> Four days later, he released the single "Main Slime", on March 15, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ Main Slime [Official Lyric Video]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aOhFkJMPZP0|work=YouTube|date=June 18, 2020|access-date=December 31, 2020}}</ref> On May 29, 2020, the latter song received a remix with an accompanying music video, featuring American rapper [[Moneybagg Yo]] and American record producers [[Tay Keith]] & Evan 200k, the latters of who produced the original song as well.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ Main Slime Remix feat. Moneybagg Yo [Official Video]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jVdiNHUhxq4|work=YouTube|date=May 29, 2020|access-date=January 1, 2021}}</ref> Two days later, Shiesty released the single "ABCGE" alongside the music video on June 3, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty ft. Big 30 â€“ ABCGE [Official Video]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=biwvUE7jIqM|work=YouTube|date=June 3, 2020|access-date=January 1, 2021}}</ref>

After releasing a few singles of his own, Shiesty received attention from American rapper [[Gucci Mane]], who ended up signing him to his record label, [[1017 Records]], along with [[Atlantic Records]] sometime in April 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gucci Mane taps Pooh Shiesty for "Still Remember" video|url=https://lab.fm/genres/hip-hop/gucci-mane-taps-pooh-shiesty-for-still-remember-video/|work=[[BandLab Technologies|lab.fm]]|date=May 29, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> The two eventually ended up collaborating on Mane's single, "Still Remember", and appeared in the music video, both released on June 19, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gucci Mane â€“ Still Remember feat. Pooh Shiesty|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rpubTIE3mdY|work=YouTube|date=June 19, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> The song became Shiesty's initial rise to fame and recognition. Only six days later, he released the single "Monday to Sunday", featuring American rappers [[Lil Baby]] and Big30, alongside the visuals on June 25, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ Monday to Sunday (feat. Lil Baby & Big30)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x4U9iB4YqK4|work=YouTube|date=June 25, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> All three singles received music videos and also ended up appearing on Mane's fourth compilation album, ''So Icy Summer'', which was released on July 3, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/gucci-mane-drops-off-feature-heavy-collab-project-gucci-mane-presents-so-icy-summer-new-mixtape.120508.html|title=Gucci Mane Drops Off Feature-Heavy Collab Project "Gucci Mane Presents: So Icy Summer"|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Marie|first=Erika|date=July 3, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> The first half of the album consisted of songs by Mane and the second half consisted of songs from the rest of the label, known as the "New 1017", the latter of which is a collective of new signees to the label, which included Shiesty himself, with other labelmates [[Foogiano]], K Shiday, Enchanting, [[Big Scarr]] and Roboy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/gucci-mane-presents-so-icy-summer/1521889193|title=Gucci Mane Presents: So Icy Summer by Gucci Mane on Apple Music|work=[[Apple Music]]|date=July 3, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> Shiesty and Gucci reunited within the album on the latter's single "Who Is Him", with an accompanying music video the same day.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gucci Mane â€“ Who Is Him (feat. Pooh Shiesty)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ok2AwOs95Io&list=PLMB8YpitfrzF2pU3xu4LTF6-dbYncWlnl&index=21&t=0s|work=YouTube|date=July 3, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref>

On September 9, 2020, Shiesty released the single "Twerksum" alongside the music video.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty â€“ Twerksum [Official Video]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TlgwrRmv71c|work=YouTube|date=September 9, 2020|access-date=January 8, 2021}}</ref> The whole 1017 label (himself, Gucci Mane, Foogiano, Big Scarr, Roboy, K Shiday, and Enchanting) teamed up on Roboy's single "1017 Loaded", which was released alongside an accompanying music video exactly one month later, on October 9, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Roboy â€“ 1017 Loaded (feat. Gucci Mane, Big Scarr, Enchanting, Foogiano, K Shiday, Pooh Shiesty)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UbdFbQQkOAs|work=YouTube|date=October 9, 2020|access-date=January 8, 2021}}</ref> Both singles appeared on Gucci's fifth compilation album, ''So Icy Gang, Vol. 1'', which was released on October 16, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/gucci-mane-drops-so-icy-gang-vol-1-ft-mulatto-pooh-shiesty-key-glock-tay-keith-new-mixtape.120811.html|title=Gucci Mane Drops "So Icy Gang Vol. 1" Ft. Mulatto, Pooh Shiesty, Key Glock, Tay Keith|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Marie|first=Erika|date=October 16, 2020|access-date=January 8, 2021}}</ref> The album is similar to the second half of ''So Icy Summer'', but is instead scattered by different artists of the label on the songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/so-icy-gang-vol-1/1535808504|title=So Icy Gang, Vol. 1 by Gucci Mane on Apple Music|work=[[Apple Music]]|date=October 16, 2020|access-date=January 8, 2021}}</ref>

The mixtape also includes the previously-released singles "Twerksum" and "Back in Blood" as the respective first and second singles.<ref>{{cite web|title=Stream Pooh Shiesty's Debut Mixtape 'Shiesty Season' f/ Gucci Mane, 21 Savage, and More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/pooh-shiesty-shiesty-season-stream|website=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]|last=|first=|date=February 5, 2021|access-date=February 5, 2021}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Pooh Shiesty is influenced by [[Chief Keef]], [[Kodak Black]] and [[Lil Wayne]]. He listened to the latter for the longest and is influenced the most by him.<ref name="XXL-Success2" />

== Legal issues ==
On October 13, 2020, Williams was arrested in connection to a shooting in [[Bay Harbor Islands, Florida|Bay Harbor Islands]], Florida.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nbcmiami.com/news/local/rapper-pooh-shiesty-arrested-in-bay-harbor-islands-double-shooting/2306781/|title=Rapper Pooh Shiesty Arrested in Bay Harbor Islands Double Shooting|work=[[WTVJ|NBC Miami]]|date=October 14, 2020|access-date=December 25, 2020}}</ref> He faced several charges including armed robbery, aggravated assault and battery, and criminal theft.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2020/10/14/gucci-manes-artist-pooh-shiesty-arrested-for-alleged-shooting/|title=Gucci Mane's Artist Pooh Shiesty Arrested For Alleged Shooting|work=[[The Source]]|last=be Allah|first=Sha|date=October 14, 2020|access-date=December 25, 2020}}</ref> He was released from custody on the same day of his arrest.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newsbreak.com/florida/bay-harbor-islands/news/2082349818833/gucci-mane-signed-rapper-pooh-shiesty-arrested-for-alleged-involvement-in-double-shooting|title=Gucci Mane-Signed Rapper Pooh Shiesty Arrested for Alleged Involvement in Double Shooting|work=[[Newsbreak (magazine)|Newsbreak]]|date=October 14, 2020|access-date=December 25, 2020}}</ref>

On June 9, 2021, Williams was arrested again, this time in connection to a shooting at a strip club in Northwest [[Miami-Dade County, Florida|Miami-Dade]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ovalle |first1=David |title=Rapper Pooh Shiesty arrested in Miami over King of Diamonds strip club shooting |url=https://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/crime/article251981248.html |access-date=July 12, 2021 |work=[[Miami Herald]] |date=June 8, 2021}}</ref> Williams was held with no bond, and stayed jailed despite the victim of the shooting recanting.<ref>{{cite news |title=Despite victim recanting, Pooh Shiesty to stay jailed â€” and now he's facing federal charges |url=https://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/crime/article252177013.html |access-date=July 12, 2021 |date=June 17, 2021|work=[[Miami Herald]] |last1=Ovalle |first1=David}}</ref> On June 29, he was [[Indictment|indicted]] over the Bay Harbor Islands robbery<ref>{{cite news |title=Feds indict rapper Pooh Shiesty, use Instagram pics of cash, car as evidence in robbery case |url=https://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/crime/article252177013.html |access-date=July 12, 2021|date=June 29, 2021|work=[[Miami Herald]] |last1=Ovalle |first1=David}}</ref> and on July 8, he was ordered by the federal judge to be held without bond pending trial.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rapper Pooh Sheisty Held Without Bond In Miami On Gun And Robbery Charges |url=https://tampa.cbslocal.com/2021/07/08/rapper-pooh-sheisty-held-without-bond-in-miami-on-gun-and-robbery-charges/ |access-date=July 12, 2021 |date=July 8, 2021}}</ref>
