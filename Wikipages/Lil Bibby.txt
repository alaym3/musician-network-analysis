'''Brandon George Dickinson''' (born July 18, 1994), better known by his stage name '''Lil Bibby''', is an American rapper and record executive. Beginning his career in 2011, Bibby released his debut [[mixtape]] in 2013, titled ''[[Free Crack]]''. After signing with [[Kemosabe Records]], he followed up the project with ''[[Free Crack 2]]'' (2014) and ''[[Free Crack 3]]'' (2015). In 2017, he eventually switched focus onto his own record label, Grade A Productions. Bibby would eventually sign fellow Chicago rapper [[Juice WRLD]] and Australian rapper [[The Kid Laroi]] to the label, both signees achieving respective worldwide success.<ref name="xxl2019" />

== Career ==
=== 2011â€“2013: Career beginnings and debut mixtape ===
In 2013, Lil Bibby released his official [[mixtape]], titled ''Free Crack''. He cited [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]] and [[Jadakiss]], as two of his favorite rappers. ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'' stated that Bibby's first mixtape were cleared from its influences from both of these rappers.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Bibby: Setting up the Perfect Shot|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/lil-bibby-interview-free-crack|publisher=Vice Noisey|access-date=February 6, 2014}}</ref> The mixtape features [[guest appearance]]s from these newcomers [[Lil Herb]] and [[King L]], while the [[Record production|production]] was handled by [[Hit-Boy]], [[Young Chop]] and [[The Olympicks]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lil-Bibby-Free-Crack-mixtape.555532.html|title=Lil Bibby â€“ Free Crack Hosted by DJ Scream|work=DatPiff|access-date=February 15, 2015}}</ref> DJ Scream hosted this mixtape. The tape combines his distinctive gruff and imposing voice, along with a solid delivery and flow.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mixtape Review: Lil Bibby, Free Crack|url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2013/12/04/mixtape-review-lil-bibby-free-crack.html|publisher=Bet.com|access-date=February 6, 2014}}</ref> The [[music video]]s were released for tracks such as "How We Move" featuring King L, "Change", "Water" and "Tired of Talkin'".

Following the release of the debut mixtape, Bibby was featured on a cover in [[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]], stated that he is one of the rappers to watch in 2014. The article complimented Bibby, stating that he had what it takes to be a major music artist, due to his maturity and his rapping ability.<ref>{{cite web|title=5 Reasons Lil Bibby Will Rule 2014|url=http://www.vibe.com/photo-gallery/chicago-rapper-lil-bibby-rule-2014|publisher=Vibe.com|access-date=February 6, 2014|date=January 8, 2014}}</ref>

=== 2013â€“2015: 2014's Freshman Class, ''Free Crack 2'' and ''Free Crack 3'' ===
In December 2013, in an interview with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Bibby hinted that the next step for his career could be an [[Extended play|EP]].<ref name=xxl>{{cite web|title=Lil Bibby say Drake Scared Him|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/12/lil-bibby-says-drake-scared-him/|publisher=XXL|access-date=February 6, 2014}}</ref> In 2014, it was also stated, that the rapper Drake was a huge fan of Bibby and that they'd spoken in 2013, before the release of Bibby's debut mixtape. Bibby said it pushed him onto improve his debut release, adding the tracks "Water" and "Whole Crew".<ref name=xxl />

In January 2014, Bibby appeared on [[Sway Calloway|Sway]]'s radio show Sway in the Morning. On the show he spoke about the success of his debut mixtape, and also dropped a live freestyle.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Bibby Talks About Chicago's Violent Image, School & Drops A Freestyle On Sway In The Morning|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/lil-bibby-sway-interview|publisher=Complex|access-date=February 6, 2014}}</ref> During the same month, Bibby also featured on [[New York City|New York]]'s radio station, [[WQHT|Hot 97]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Bibby Talks Free Crack, Chicago, & More|url=http://hiphopwired.com/2014/01/15/lil-bibby-talks-free-crack-chicago-video/|publisher=Hip Hop Wired|access-date=February 6, 2014|date=January 15, 2014}}</ref> During the same month, Bibby stated in his interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', that he was working on material for an [[Extended play|EP]], but was waiting on a couple of features.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Bibby Talks Upcoming EP & Hints at Drake Collaboration|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5876798/lil-bibby-talks-upcoming-ep-hints-at-drake-collaboration|publisher=Billboard|access-date=February 6, 2014}}</ref> Bibby also appeared on the list of 2014's ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' Freshman Class.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/05/xxl-freshmen-2014-cover-revealed/|title=XXL Freshmen 2014 Cover Revealed|work=XXL|access-date=February 15, 2015}}</ref> He was the last person selected to the list, due to [[Young Thug]]'s last minute cancellation.

In July 2014, he made his appearance on the songs, collaborating with a fellow rapper and a [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]]-native [[Juicy J]], and was featured on the remix to "[[Main Chick]]" performed by [[Kid Ink]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/07/listen-to-kid-inks-main-chick-remix|title=Listen to Kid Ink's "Main Chick (Remix)" f/ French Montana, Yo Gotti, Tyga, Lil Bibby & Chris Brown|first=Zach|last=Frydenlund|work=Complex|access-date=February 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2014/07/juicy-j-featuring-lil-bibby-holy-ghost-dont-know/|title=Juicy J Featuring Lil Bibby "Holy Ghost + "You Don't Know" â€“ XXL|work=XXL|access-date=February 15, 2015}}</ref> After a few delays, Bibby's sophomore mixtape, ''Free Crack 2,'' was released on August 29, 2014. ''Free Crack 2'', which was hosted by [[DJ Drama]], includes guest appearances from Lil Herb, [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Juicy J]], [[Kevin Gates]], [[Jadakiss]] and [[Anthony Hamilton (musician)|Anthony Hamilton]], among others. The mixtape has production from Juicy J, Honorable C.N.O.T.E., Sonny Digital and [[DJ Pain 1]], among others. The mixtape was released for free digital download and rapidly accumulated over 120,000 downloads.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lil-Bibby-Free-Crack-2-mixtape.615403.html|title=Lil Bibby â€“ Free Crack 2 Hosted by DJ Drama|work=DatPiff|access-date=January 24, 2015}}</ref>

''Free Crack 2'' was received with critical acclaim. [[XXL (magazine)|XXL magazine]] awarded the mixtape an XL, praising the mixtape's authenticity, catchy hooks and production, while also commenting that the mixtape showcases Bibby's "growth as an artist."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2014/09/lil-bibby-free-crack-2-mixtape-review/|title=Lil Bibby Serves Up Potent Product On 'Free Crack II' Mixtape â€“ XXL|work=XXL|access-date=February 15, 2015}}</ref> [[RedEye]] awarded the mixtape three stars out of four, commenting on the tape's maturity and versatility and praising the ability of the song 'Dead or in Prison' to let Bibby's "emotion and natural charisma do the heavy lifting."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.redeyechicago.com/entertainment/music/redeye-lil-bibby-free-crack-2-review-20140902,0,1932581.story |title=Lil Bibby's 'Free Crack 2': The beginning of something big |access-date=February 4, 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140905172245/http://www.redeyechicago.com/entertainment/music/redeye-lil-bibby-free-crack-2-review-20140902%2C0%2C1932581.story |archive-date=September 5, 2014 }}</ref> [[BET]] gave the mixtape a positive review, awarding it five stars while commenting on its "new level of depth and introspection, detailing the difficult balance of fame and authenticity and how that effects everyday life in some of America's urban war zones."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2014/09/09/mixtape-review-lil-bibby-free-crack-2.html|title=Mixtape Review: Lil Bibby, Free Crack 2|date=September 9, 2014|work=BET.com|access-date=January 24, 2015}}</ref>

=== 2015â€“2016: ''FC3 the Epilogue'' & ''Free Crack 4'' ===
In 2015, Bibby released two singles "Ridah" and "Aww Man" for an upcoming album, supposedly the fourth installment in his Free Crack series.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.40661/title.lil-bibby-college-computer-engineering|title=Lil Bibby Enrolling Back In College To Study Computer Engineering|first=HipHopDX -|last=http://hiphopdx.com|access-date=January 15, 2017|date=September 30, 2016}}</ref>  In late 2016, Bibby released "Thought It Was A Drought" and this was produced by DJ L Beats and Roberto Mario.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopsince1987.com/2017/videos/lil-bibby-thought-it-was-a-drought-video/|title=Lil Bibby â€“ Thought It Was A Drought (Video)|date=January 13, 2017|website=Home of Hip Hop Videos & Rap Music, News, Video, Mixtapes & more|language=en-US|access-date=December 25, 2019}}</ref>  After a long period with no music, in early 2018, Bibby began using the hashtag "Free Bibby" on social media, protesting his label's refusal to allow him to release the project.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Be5X-l1nQpF/?hl=en |title = Kollege Kidd on Instagram: "#LilBibby Says 'Free Bibby' Because His Label Won't Let Him Drop #FreeCrack4 ðŸ˜® #fc4"}}</ref>

=== 2017â€“present: Grade A Productions, Juice WRLD, and The Kid LAROI ===

Bibby and his brother G-Money decided to launch their own record label known as Grade A Productions. In an interview with [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]], Bibby discussed his transition from being a rapper to starting a record label: {{quote|"I really don't know. I like to make people a lot of money. I got enough knowledge and I've studied this rap stuff and I got enough relationships to help an artist, help his family [and] I get a kick out of it 'cause I see how the label does it and my situation. I feel like just helping people."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/lil-bibby-interview/|title=How Lil Bibby Successfully Transitioned From Rapper to Hip-Hop Executive|website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Satten|first=Vanessa|date=September 25, 2019|accessdate=February 22, 2021}}</ref>}}

In 2017, they discovered then upcoming American rapper [[Juice WRLD]] after hearing his song "[[Lucid Dreams (Juice WRLD song)|Lucid Dreams]]". Under the label, Juice WRLD released his debut studio album ''[[Goodbye and Good Riddance]]'' on May 23, 2018, which was followed by 3 singles "All Girls Are The Same", "Lucid Dreams", and "Lean Wit Me". On March 8, 2019, he released his second studio album ''[[Death Race for Love]]'' which was followed by 2 singles "Robbery", and "Hear Me Calling". On December 8, 2019, Juice suffered from an overdose at an airport in Chicago which lead to his unexpected passing. On January 22, 2020, Grade A announced that posthumous projects from Juice will be released to honor his legacy. His first posthumous album ''[[Legends Never Die (Juice WRLD album)|Legends Never Die]]'' was released on July 10, 2020, which was followed by 3 singles "Righteous", "Tell Me U Luv Me" featuring [[Trippie Redd]], and "Life's A Mess" featuring [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]]. Throughout 2020, other posthumous releases included "Smile" with [[The Weeknd]], "Real Shit" with [[Benny Blanco]], "Reminds Me Of You" by The Kid LAROI featuring Juice WRLD in 2020, and recently "Bad Boy" with [[Young Thug]] in 2021.{{cn|date=November 2021}}

In 2019, Bibby later discovered upcoming Australian rapper and singer [[The Kid LAROI]] after hearing his song "Blessings" from his debut EP ''14 With A Dream''. In March, Bibby signed Laroi to Grade A. Under the label, Laroi released his debut mixtape ''[[F*ck Love|F*CK LOVE]]'' on July 24 which was followed by 2 singles "[[Go (The Kid Laroi and Juice Wrld song)|GO]]" featuring Juice WRLD, which was a posthumous verse due to his sudden death in December 2019, and "TELL ME WHY". On November 6, Laroi released a deluxe edition of ''F*ck Love'' titled ''F*ck Love (SAVAGE)'' which was followed by 1 single "SO DONE".{{cn|date=November 2021}}

According to Bibby, when opening the label, he stated during an [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] interview that: {{quote|"I really don't know. I like to make people a lot of money. I got enough knowledge and I've studied this rap stuff and I got enough relationships to help an artist, help his family [and] I get a kick out of it 'cause I see how the label does it and my situation. I feel like just helping people."}} The label is home to well known artists such as [[Juice WRLD]], and [[The Kid LAROI]].
