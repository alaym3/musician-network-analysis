'''Holly Marie Wilson''' (nÃ©e '''Miller'''; born January 3, 1997), known by her stage name '''Hollyn''', is an American Christian music singer and songwriter. Hollyn sings a mixture of pop, R&B, and Christian music. She was a contestant on ''[[American Idol (season 12)|American Idol]]'' during season 12, where she made it to the Hollywood rounds.<ref name="CR Story">{{cite web|first=Tony|last=Cummings|title=Hollyn: A new name with a Christian pop hit |publisher=[[Cross Rhythms]] |url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/music/Hollyn__A_new_name_with_a_Christian_pop_hit/57511/p1/ |accessdate=January 24, 2016|date=January 24, 2016}}</ref><ref name="CCM Story">{{cite journal|first=Jen|last=Rose Yokel|title=Hollyn â€“ Getting to know Hollyn |journal=[[CCM Magazine]] |url=https://www.ccmmagazine.com/features/hollyn-getting-to-know-hollyn/ |accessdate=March 16, 2016|date=March 15, 2016}}</ref> She released her [[extended play|EP]], ''[[Hollyn (EP)|Hollyn]]'', in 2015, and the album ''[[One-Way Conversations]]'' in 2017 and ''Bye, Sad Girl'', in 2019, both with [[Gotee Records]]. Hollyn often sings with Tobymac.

== Early and personal life ==
Hollyn was born Holly Marie Miller,<ref name="BMI">{{cite web|title=Songwriter/Composer: MILLER HOLLY MARIE|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/writer.asp?blnWriter=True&blnPublisher=True&blnArtist=True&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&querytype=WriterID&keyid=550244847&keyname=MILLER+HOLLY+MARIE&CAE=674082141&Affiliation=BMI|publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]]|author=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]]|accessdate=October 25, 2015}}{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> on January 3, 1997, in [[Waverly, Ohio]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Ohio Girl on American Idol Chasing Dreams|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePG0U3U8jIg|publisher=[[WKEF]]|author=[[WRGT-TV]]|accessdate=October 25, 2015|date=February 27, 2013}}</ref> to Jamie Drew Miller and Tammy Lynn Miller<!----The song, "Hey Mama", confirms her mothers name as Tammy.----> (''[[maiden name|nÃ©e]]'', Bales).<ref name="CR Story"/><ref name="OHIO">{{cite web |title=Holly Marie Miller (b. 1997)|url=http://birth-records.mooseroots.com/l/13016113/Holly-Marie-Miller|publisher=Ohio Birth Records|accessdate=October 25, 2015}}</ref> She has one younger brother named Michael.<ref>{{cite web |title=Michael James Drew Miller (b. 2000)|url=http://birth-records.mooseroots.com/l/13933186/Michael-James-Drew-Miller|publisher=Ohio Birth Records|accessdate=October 25, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Interview: Hollyn |url=http://www.365daysofinspiringmedia.com/interviews/hollyn/ |publisher=365 Days of Inspiring Media |accessdate=March 31, 2016 |date=March 29, 2016}}</ref>On Friday November 16, 2018, she got engaged to Dillon Wilson, Young Adults pastor at Oaks Church, Red Oak, Texas. == {{anchor|Music}} Music career ==

Hollyn started her music recording career in 2013, with her appearance on [[American Idol (season 12)|season 12 of ''American Idol'']],<ref>{{cite web |title=Holly Miller: Road To Hollywood Interviews - AMERICAN IDOL SEASON 12 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ATbz-UII4Gk|work=[[American Idol]]|author=[[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]]|accessdate=October 25, 2015|date=January 23, 2013}}</ref> where she made it to the Hollywood week rounds before she was eliminated.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stanley|first=Stephanie|title=Holly Miller eliminated during American Idol solo round|url=https://www.newswatchman.com/news/article_951e4702-765d-11e2-b33b-001a4bcf887a.html|access-date=March 12, 2021|website=The Pike County News Watchman|language=en}}</ref> Hollyn appeared on [[tobyMac]]'s ''[[This Is Not a Test (album)|This Is Not a Test]]'' album, where she was featured on "Backseat Driver" and "Lights Shine Bright". She also appeared in Funky Jesus Music by TobyMac.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Funky Jesus Music (Live) (feat. Hollyn|publisher=365 Days of Inspiring Media|accessdate=October 25, 2015|date=October 17, 2015 |url=http://www.365daysofinspiringmedia.com/reviews/hollyn-hollyn-ep/}}</ref><ref name="TCB Review">{{cite web|first=Madeleine|last=Dittmer|title=Hollyn Sure To Draw New Listeners in With Debut EP|publisher=365 Days of Inspiring Media|accessdate=October 25, 2015|date=October 22, 2015|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/exclusives/1143-hollyn-sure-to-draw-new-listeners-in-with-debut-ep|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151118030343/http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/exclusives/1143-hollyn-sure-to-draw-new-listeners-in-with-debut-ep|archive-date=November 18, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="SA Review">{{cite web|first=Andrew |last=Greenhalgh|title=Review: Hollyn â€“ Hollyn |publisher=Soul-Audio |accessdate=October 25, 2015|date=October 20, 2015 |url=http://soul-audio.com/2015/10/review-hollyn-hollyn/}}</ref> The song, "Alone", featuring the late Truett Foster, charted on the ''Billboard'' magazine [[Christian Airplay]] chart at No. 11 & No. 3 on the Christian Top 40. ,<ref name="chart search three">[http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f%5b0%5d=ss_chart_search_title%3A%22Alone%22&f%5b1%5d=itm_field_chart_id%3A1762&refine=1 search for "Alone" on Christian Airplay] at billboard.com/biz</ref> This extended play charted at No. 10 on the ''Billboard'' magazine [[Christian Albums]] chart. [[Gotee Records]]' [[TobyMac]] signed Hollyn after viewing her videos on YouTube and contacting her father through a mutual friend. The two collaborated on her song, "Alone", where she was inspired by her favorite verse of the time, Isaiah 30:21, "Whether you look to the left or the right, you will hear a voice saying, 'This is the way. Walk in it.'"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://media.focusonthefamily.com/brio/pdf/brio-sample-junejuly2017.pdf|title=Brio: Funky Jesus Music-Hollyn {{!}} Interview Information {{!}} FocusonTheFamily/BrioMagazine|website=www.focusonthefamily.com|access-date=June 1, 2017}}</ref>
