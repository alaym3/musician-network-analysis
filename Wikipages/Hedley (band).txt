'''Hedley''' is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[pop rock]] group originating in [[Abbotsford, British Columbia|Abbotsford]], [[British Columbia]], originally formed in 2003. They are named after the [[Unincorporated area|unincorporated community]] of [[Hedley, British Columbia]], a name chosen after members heard that it was for sale for $346,000.<ref>[https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/hedley-band] The Canadian Encyclopedia</ref> After lead singer [[Jacob Hoggard]] placed third on the [[Canadian Idol (season 2)|second season]] of ''[[Canadian Idol]]'' in 2004, the group signed with Universal Music Canada and released their [[Hedley (album)|self-titled debut studio album]] the following year. After signing an international recording contract with [[Capitol Records]] in 2006, which brought them an increased American following. Hedley saw commercial success with their subsequent studio albums, including ''[[Famous Last Words (Hedley album)|Famous Last Words]]'' (2007), ''[[The Show Must Go]]'' (2009), ''[[Storms (Hedley album)|Storms]]'' (2011), ''[[Wild Life (Hedley album)|Wild Life]]'' (2013),  ''[[Hello (Hedley album)|Hello]]'' (2015) and [[Cageless]] (2017).

Following the release of their seventh studio album ''[[Cageless]]'' (2017) and its accompanying tour, current members of Hedley faced accusations of [[sexual misconduct]] with young women over the course of their career.<ref name="z1035.com"/><ref name="globalnews.ca"/> After Hoggard faced additional accusations of [[sexual assault]] by two women, the group confirmed they would enter an indefinite [[wikt:hiatus|hiatus]].<ref name="CBC"/><ref name="CBC 2"/>

==History==

===2003â€“2005: Original line-up and Canadian Idol===
Hedley originally consisted of  [[Jacob Hoggard]], Kevin Giesbrecht, Kevin Heeres, Ryan Federau, and Brandon McKay. The band wrote many songs such as Gunnin' and Brave New World (which were later recorded by the current Hedley). The original band bet Jacob Hoggard $150 that he wouldn't make it past auditions for Canadian Idol. Hoggard proved them wrong making it to the top three before being eliminated from the competition. However, shortly after the members decided to go their separate ways, but the band re-formed not long after, with Hoggard as the only original member.

===2005â€“2008: ''Hedley'' and ''Famous Last Words''===
Their debut single "On My Own" reached number 1 on the [[Canadian Singles Chart]] and the follow-up "Trip" peaked at number 11. The singles "On My Own", "321", "Trip", and "Gunnin" topped the MuchMusic Countdown.<ref name=muchmusic>{{cite web|url=http://hitsofalldecades.com/chart_hits/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=1318&Itemid=52|title=CANADA'S (MUCH MUSIC) WEEKLY SINGLE CHARTS FOR 2006|access-date=June 17, 2021}}</ref>  Their last single, "Street Fight", off of their self-titled debut album was successful on both the MuchMusic Countdown and the Canadian Singles Chart.<ref name=muchmusic/> Their first single from their album ''[[Famous Last Words (Hedley album)|Famous Last Words]]'', "She's So Sorry", achieved much success on the MuchMusic Countdown as it reached number 1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sabian.com/article/chris-crippin-and-hedley-rock-the-air-canada-center/|title=Chris Crippin And Hedley Rock The Air Canada Center|access-date=June 9, 2021}}</ref>

In 2005 the band went on their first tour across Canada,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soulshine.ca/news/newsarticle.php?nid=2456|title=Hedley Tries to Shake Idol Status with New Album and Tour : Soul Shine Magazine|website=www.soulshine.ca}}</ref> with [[The Weekend (Canadian band)|The Weekend]] and Faber (the band currently known as [[Faber Drive]]). Then went on a 19-city Canadian tour with [[Simple Plan]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soulshine.ca/news/newsarticle.php?nid=2708|title=Hedley Take New Album Out On The Road : Soul Shine Magazine|website=www.soulshine.ca}}</ref> and in early 2006, headlining The Get Some Tour cross Canada club tour with [[MxPx]] and Faber.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soulshine.ca/news/newsarticle.php?nid=3067|title=Hedley Headline on Cross Canada Tour : Soul Shine Magazine|website=www.soulshine.ca}}</ref>

In the summer of 2006, they signed a record deal for the United States with [[Capitol Records]]. At the time a division of [[EMI]], [[Universal Music Group]] competitor, before being sold in the fold of EMI in 2012 to Universal. Upon signing Hedley's self-titled album was released with a new cover in the US on September 26, 2006, a year after its initial release in Canada. During June 2006, the band went on tour in the US, opening for fellow Capitol Records artist, [[Yellowcard]], with [[Matchbook Romance|Matchbox Romance]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ultimate-guitar.com/news/upcoming_tours/yellowcard_plan_early_summer_tour.html|title=Yellowcard Plan Early Summer Tour}}</ref> Subsequently, Hedley was dropped by Capitol Records in 2007.

Hedley released their second album, ''[[Famous Last Words (Hedley album)|Famous Last Words]]'', on October 30, 2007. It went platinum ten days after release.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/hedley-mn0000513860/biography|title=Hedley - Biography|access-date=June 15, 2021}}</ref>  The first single from the CD, "She's So Sorry", was released to radio on August 21, 2007. The video was shot in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], and premiered on [[Much (TV channel)|MuchMusic]] on September 20, 2007.

In late 2007, the band was chosen to open for [[Bon Jovi]] on their [[Lost Highway Tour]] at their Canadian stops for 14 shows <ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.canada.com/story.html?id=fedbd93a-6311-4034-be2a-2d629064b894 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-03-28 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140616065639/http://www.canada.com/story.html?id=fedbd93a-6311-4034-be2a-2d629064b894 |archive-date=2014-06-16 }}</ref> which forced them to postpone their own tour until early 2008, opened by [[State of Shock (band)|State of Shock]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cordovabay.com/news.php?type=article&id=77|title=Cordova Bay Records|website=www.cordovabay.com}}</ref>

[[Image:MuchMusic Video Awards 2007 635.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Hedley at the 2007 MMVA Awards]]

Famous Last Words was released on May 12, 2009 in the US with a blue cover and new name "Never Too Late". 5 songs replaced with 5 from the band's first album. Dropped by [[Capitol Records]] in 2007, Never Too Late was released by [[Fontana Distribution]], at the time owned by Hedley's Canadian record label's parent company [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]].

===2009â€“2011: ''The Show Must Go'' and ''Go with the Show''===
On November 17, 2009, Hedley released their third studio album ''[[The Show Must Go]]''. The first single "[[Cha-Ching]]" peaked at number 6 in the Canadian Hot 100.<ref name=CDN>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/hedley/chart-history/CAN|title=Hedley Chart History: Canadian Hot 100|access-date=2021-03-16}}</ref> The second single, [[Don't Talk to Strangers (Hedley song)|Don't Talk to Strangers]] peaked at number 11 on the Canadian Hot 100, and at number 1 on the Much Music Countdown.<ref name=CDN/> The third single "[[Perfect (Hedley song)|Perfect]]" peaked at number seven in the Canadian Hot 100, and also peaked at number one in the Much Music Countdown.{{Citation needed|date=September 2010}}  The fourth single off the album was "Hands Up".

The band went on [[The Show Must Go#The Show Must Go... on the Road Tour|The Show Must Go... on the Road Tour]] across 38 cities in Canada in support of the new album ''[[The Show Must Go]]''. On November 16, 2009, [[Chart Attack]] reported, "[[Fefe Dobson]] and [[Stereos]] will be along for the whole trek, while [[Faber Drive]] and [[Boys Like Girls]] will support on select dates."<ref>{{cite web|title=Hedley Announce Canadian Tour|last=Harper|first=Kate|work=[[Chart Attack]]|url=http://www.chartattack.com/news/76770/hedley-announce-canadian-tour|date=2009-11-16|access-date=2010-05-03|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091120123641/http://www.chartattack.com/news/76770/hedley-announce-canadian-tour|archive-date=2009-11-20}}</ref> A live album and documentary entitled [[Go with the Show|Go With the Show]] was recorded during this tour. Released on CD/DVD on November 9. 2010.

In 2010 the band signed an American record deal with [[Island Records]], owned by same mega-label, [[Universal Music Group]], as Hedley's Canadian label. The album was originally to be released in the US on October 12, but instead was released on December 7, 2010, containing the bonus tracks "I Do (Wanna Love You)" (a re-arrangement of the song "For the Nights I Can't Remember") and "Color Outside The Lines".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alternativeaddiction.com/musicnews/article/1871/Hedley-Hoping-Third-Times-A-Charm |title=Hedley Hoping Third Time's A Charm |publisher=AlternativeAddiction.com |date=2010-10-09 |access-date=2011-05-05}}</ref> The band also toured the US to promote the album.

The band performed Cha-Ching in a segment with other artists highlighting Canadian music at the [[2010 Winter Olympics closing ceremony|closing ceremony]] of the Vancouver [[2010 Winter Olympics|2010 Winter Olympic Games]].<ref name="2010 Olympics Closing Ceremony">[http://www.mahalo.com/2010-olympics-closing-ceremony "2010 Olympics Closing Ceremony"], ''Mahalo News''. Article, March 18, 2010</ref> The song was later included in the soundtrack to the closing ceremonies Sounds of Vancouver 2010: Closing Ceremony Commemorative Album.

===2011â€“2013: ''Storms''===
On May 5, 2011, Hedley confirmed that they were back in the studio recording a new album. The first official single from the album, entitled, "[[Invincible (Hedley song)|Invincible]]", was released on [[iTunes]] (Canada) on August 23, 2011. The single has yet to be released on iTunes in the US. An official music video for the song was released on September 6, 2011.

The band's fourth studio album, ''[[Storms (Hedley album)|Storms]]'', was released on November 8, 2011. This album consists of 12 tracks (17 on the deluxe edition).<ref name="facebook1">{{cite web|url=http://www.facebook.com/notes/universal-music-canada/studio-album-4-for-canadas-multiplatinum-hit-makers/10150321758554422 |title=Studio Album #4 for Canadaâ€™s Multiplatinum Hit-makers |publisher=Facebook |access-date=2012-02-15}}</ref> A music video was released for the album's second single "One Life" on December 14, 2011.

The album sees the group experimenting with elements of pop and rock music to create their most musically diverse soundscape yet, as well as the one with most overt pop overtones. Debuting at number two on the Canadian Albums Chart (edged out only by [[Michael BublÃ©]]'s Christmas), it is also Hedley's most successful album thus far. The album debuted at number two on the Canadian Albums Chart, selling 23,000 copies. This was the biggest debut of the week, behind Michael BublÃ©'s holiday album Christmas. This album marks the band's highest album chart peak. On November 23, 2011, the album was certified gold by Music Canada. Hedley won the Juno Award for Best Pop Album at the 2012 awards. The album produced two top-20 singles, "Invincible" (featuring rapper [[Preme|P. Reign]] on the single release) and "One Life". The album included the track "Beautiful" which was first released on Hedley's live album [[Go with the Show|Go With the Show]] in 2010.

After the success of Storms, Hedley released a new single called "[[Kiss You Inside Out]]" on May 18, 2012, included in the re-release of Storms on June 5, 2012. A  French version of Kiss You Inside Out featuring additional vocals by AndrÃ©e-Anne Leclerc was also made available on iTunes. Kiss You Inside Out was met with critical acclaim and netted the band their highest chart peak to date.

To promote Storms, the band went on the 31 city Shipwrecked tour during early 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blog.muchmusic.com/not-only-is-hedley-hitting-up-much-theyre-touring-the-entire-true-north-strong-and-free/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-03-28 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140328233309/http://blog.muchmusic.com/not-only-is-hedley-hitting-up-much-theyre-touring-the-entire-true-north-strong-and-free/ |archive-date=2014-03-28 }}</ref>

===2013â€“2015: ''Wild Life''===
On August 27, 2013, "Anything", the first single released. Followed by the video posted on September 10, 2013. The album, entitled '[[Wild Life (Hedley album)|Wild Life]],'  released on November 11, 2013 in Canada. Consisting of 11 tracks (15 on the deluxe version).

Hedley signed in late 2013 <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skopemag.com/2013/11/20/hedley-signs-to-capitol-records|title=HEDLEY SIGNS TO CAPITOL RECORDS â€“ Skope Entertainment Inc|website=www.skopemag.com}}</ref> again with [[Capitol Records]], now owned by [[Universal Music Group]], the same mega-label owner of as their Canadian label. The album was released via Capitol Records in the US on May 20, 2014. The album's second single "[[Crazy for You (Hedley song)|Crazy For You]]", released October 22, 2013 and video released February 14, 2014. The band's album, '[[Wild Life (Hedley album)|Wild Life]],' was released in the US on May 19, 2014.

Hedley headlined the [[101st Grey Cup]] Halftime show at [[Mosaic Stadium]] at [[Taylor Field (Regina, Saskatchewan)|Taylor Field]] in [[Regina, Saskatchewan|Regina]], [[Saskatchewan]] on November 24, 2013. Performing a 13-minute compilation of Hands Up, Anything, Invincible and Cha-Ching.

Hedley went on a Canadian national headlining tour, [[Wild Life (Hedley album)|Wild Live Tour]], to promote the album, in Februaryâ€“April 2014, playing 37 cities and ending in hometown, [[Abbotsford, British Columbia|Abbotsford]], [[British Columbia]]. As well as [[Webster Hall|The Studio at Webster Hall]] in [[New York City|New York]] on January 14 and [[Los Angeles]] at The Sayers Club on January 16.

===2015â€“2018: ''Hello'', departure of Chris Crippin, ''Cageless'' and hiatus===
On September 8, Hedley released "Lost in Translation" as the lead single off the album ''[[Hello (Hedley album)|Hello]]''. The sixth album was released via Universal Music Canada on November 6, 2015.

Hedley have been nominated for Group of the Year for the 2016 Juno Awards but lost to [[Walk off the Earth|Walk Off the Earth]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://junoawards.ca/nominees/|title=See the Full List of 2018 JUNO Awards Nominees - The JUNO Awards}}</ref> On July 3, 2016, they were the headliners of the post-Queen's Plate concert held at [[Woodbine Racetrack]] (in [[Toronto]]) which also featured [[The Strumbellas]] & The [[Matthew Good Band|Mathew Good Band]].

On March 31, 2017, Hedley announced on their Facebook page that their drummer, [[Chris Crippin]] had left the band after 11 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/HedleyOnline/posts/10154457059323907|title=Hedley|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

On July 21, 2017, Hedley announced their album ''[[Cageless]]'', which was released September 29, 2017.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=Hedleyonline|author=Hedley|number=888405757095546880|date=21 July 2017|title=Friends! Hedley announced February 28, 2018, via Twitter, that the band will be taking an "indefinite" hiatus.<ref name="CBC 2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/hedley-indefinite-hiatus-tour-1.4556810|title=Hedley to take 'indefinite hiatus' once current tour ends {{!}} CBC News|work=CBC|access-date=2018-03-01|language=en-US}}</ref> However, statements at concerts since this announcement indicate that the band does plan to return.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/amp/1.4591362|title=Thousands attend Hedley's final show in Kelowna before 'indefinite hiatus'|website=www.cbc.ca|language=en|access-date=June 2, 2018}}</ref>

==Controversies==
On February 13, 2018, then current Hedley members were anonymously accused of sexual misconduct with young fans, primarily female, as young as 14 years of age. As a result, the [[Juno Award|Juno Awards]] and the band mutually agreed to cancel their scheduled performance.<ref name="z1035.com">{{cite web|url=https://z1035.com/hedley-facing-sexual-assault-sexual-misconduct-allegations-fans-young-14/|title=Hedley Is Facing Sexual Assault And Sexual Misconduct Allegations By Fans As Young As 14 â€“ Z103.5|date=14 February 2018}}</ref><ref name="globalnews.ca">{{cite web|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4025968/hedley-sexual-allegations-juno-awards/|title=Hedley no longer playing Juno Awards after sexual misconduct allegations}}</ref> The band posted a Facebook message, saying that the accusations were unsubstantiated and that while the band in the past "engaged in a lifestyle that incorporated certain rock and roll clichÃ©s. However, there was always a line that we would never cross".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://nationalpost.com/pmn/news-pmn/canada-news-pmn/full-statement-by-hedley-on-sexual-misconduct-allegations|title=Full statement by Hedley on sexual misconduct allegations|date=2018-02-14|work=National Post|access-date=2018-03-03|language=en-US}}</ref> On February 28, 2018, Hedley announced that they would take an indefinite hiatus following their tour. That same day, Hoggard announced he would be taking an indefinite step back from his career.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/hedley-indefinite-hiatus-tour-1.4556810|title=Hedley to take 'indefinite hiatus' once current tour ends|work=CBC News|access-date=2018-03-03|language=en}}</ref>

The response received mixed reviews from critics, fans, and victims of assault alike with the [[hashtag]] #outhedley2k18 on [[Twitter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/ishmaeldaro/hedley-allegations|title=Hereâ€™s How The Sexual Misconduct Allegations Against Hedley Came Out}}</ref> On February 16, 2018, it was announced that Hedley was dropped by their management team effective immediately; supporting acts [[Shawn Hook]] and [[Neon Dreams]] quit from the band's Cageless Tour (on which the band remained as the only headliners). Several radio stations and media organizations (including [[Bell Media Radio]], [[Corus Entertainment|Corus Radio]] and [[CBC Music]]) removed and suspended all of their music due to these allegations.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nationalpost.com/entertainment/music/cbc-drops-hedleys-music-from-radio-streaming-service|title=Hedley dropped by management, radio, tour opener amid sexual misconduct allegations|date=16 February 2018}}</ref>

On February 25, 2018, an [[Ottawa]] woman accused lead singer Jacob Hoggard of sexual assault after the pair met on [[Tinder (app)|Tinder]] and went to a hotel to have sex in November 2016. Attorneys for Hoggard denied the allegations in a statement sent to [[CBC News]] on his behalf.<ref name="CBC">{{cite web|title='I said stop': Ottawa woman accuses Hedley singer Jacob Hoggard of rape|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/hedley-jacob-hoggard-rape-allegation-sexual-misconduct-1.4537263|website=CBC|access-date=27 February 2018}}</ref>

On March 16, 2018, the [[Toronto Police Service]] Sex Crimes Unit confirmed that it has opened an investigation into Hoggard.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/news/gta/2018/03/16/toronto-police-sex-crimes-unit-investigating-hedley-frontman-jacob-hoggard.html|title=Toronto Police Sex Crimes Unit Investigating Hedley Frontman|publisher=The Toronto Star}}</ref>

On July 23, 2018, Hoggard was charged with one count of Sexual Interference and two counts of Sexual Assault Causing Bodily Harm.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.torontopolice.on.ca/newsreleases/41705|title=Toronto Police Service :: News Release #41705|last=Service|first=Toronto Police|website=www.torontopolice.on.ca|language=en|access-date=2018-07-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180724062812/http://www.torontopolice.on.ca/newsreleases/41705|archive-date=2018-07-24|url-status=dead}}</ref>
