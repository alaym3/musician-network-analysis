'''Kane Allen Brown'''<ref name=BMI-RepertoireProfile>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=550360793&subid=2|title=Songwriter/Composer: Kane Brown; Current Affiliation: BMI CAE/IPI #: 794712210|work=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]]}}</ref><ref name=Tennesean-KaneBrown-2016>{{cite news|first1=Cindy|last1=Watts|title=Raising Kane Brown: Biracial singer forges own path in country music|url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/2016/12/01/raising-kane-brown-biracial-singer-forges-own-path-country-music/93490558/|work=[[The Tennessean]]|date=December 1, 2016}}</ref> (born October 21, 1993)<ref name=AllMusic-Bio>{{cite web|last1=Collar|first1=Matt|title=Kane Brown biography|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kane-brown-mn0003444653/biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 10, 2018}}</ref> is an American singer and songwriter. Brown first came to the attention of the public through [[social media]].<ref name=Roughstock-2015>{{cite news|first1=Matt|last1=Bjorke|title=The Rise of Kane Brown, Independent Country Sensation|url=https://roughstock.com/news/2015/10/39036-the-rise-of-kane-brown-independent-country-sensation|work=Roughstock|date=October 19, 2015}}</ref> He released his first EP, titled ''[[Closer (Kane Brown EP)|Closer]]'', in June 2015 and followed it with a new single, "[[Used to Love You Sober]]", in October 2015. After Brown signed with [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]] in early 2016, the song was included on his EP ''[[Chapter 1 (EP)|Chapter 1]]'', released in March 2016. He released his first full-length album, the self-titled ''[[Kane Brown (album)|Kane Brown]]'', on December 2, 2016. The single "[[What Ifs]]" came from this album, and in October 2017, Brown became the first artist to have simultaneous number ones on all five main ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country charts.<ref name=Billboard-5CountryCharts-2017>{{cite news|first1=Jim|last1=Asker|title=Kane Brown Becomes First Artist To Simultaneously Lead Five Country Charts|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8005648/kane-brown-what-if-country-charts-record|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date= October 17, 2017}}</ref> Brown released his second album, ''[[Experiment (album)|Experiment]]'', in November 2018, which became his first number one album on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name=caulfield />

==Early life==
Brown was raised in rural northwest [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] and in the [[Chattanooga, Tennessee]], area.<ref name=AllMusic-Bio/><ref name=RollingStone-DontGoCityOnMe-2016>{{cite news|first1=Jon|last1=Freeman|title=Kane Brown on Personal New Album, Why He Won't Sing First Hit: Ram Report|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/videos/kane-brown-on-new-album-why-he-wont-sing-first-hit-w453456|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=December 2, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.froggy101.com/blogs/exclusive-interview-kane-brown |title= Exclusive Interview: Kane Brown |first= Lauren |last=Hoffman|date=March 6, 2017|work=Froggy101 }}</ref> He is multiracial, with a white mother, Tabatha Brown,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://rarecountry.com/news/kane-brown-brings-his-mom-to-tears-in-heartwarming-video/ |title=Kane Brown brings his mom to tears in heartwarming video |first=Melinda |last=Lorge|date= October 4, 2017 |work=Rare Country}}</ref> and an African-American father who is also part [[Cherokee]].<ref name=Tennesean-KaneBrown-2016 /><ref name=NYTimes-IrvingPlaza-2016>{{cite news|first1=Jon|last1=Caramanica|title=Kane Brown Reaches to Reconcile Two Sides in His Sound|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/12/04/arts/music/kane-brown-reaches-to-reconcile-two-sides-in-his-sound.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=December 4, 2016}}</ref><ref name=WashPost-Profile-2016>{{cite news|first1=Emily|last1=Yahr|title= Kane Brown could be the future of country music. So why is the industry skeptical?|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/kane-brown-could-be-the-future-of-country-music-so-why-is-the-industry-skeptical/2016/12/07/8e1eb296-bc51-11e6-91ee-1adddfe36cbe_story.html|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=December 8, 2016}}</ref> In 2018, he told ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' he did not know he was biracial until he was 7 or 8 years old: "I thought I was full white... I found out that I was biracial, and I still wasn't thinking anything of it, but then I started getting called the [[Nigger|N-word]] ... I learned what it meant, and that's when it started affecting me. I got in fights over it when I was little."<ref name=People-Childhood-2018>{{cite news|first1=Dana Rose|last1=Falcone|title=Kane Brown Opens Up About Being Called the N-Word as a Kid: 'I Thought I Was Full White'|url=https://people.com/country/kane-brown-childhood-racism-experiment-interview/|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=November 21, 2018}}</ref> His father has been incarcerated since 1996, and he was raised by his single mother.<ref name=Billboard-CoverProfile-2018>{{cite news|first1=Marissa R.|last1=Moss|title=How Kane Brown Became the Future of Country|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/8482427/kane-brown-billboard-cover-story|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date= November 1, 2018}}</ref>

Sometimes homeless, his family moved around northwest Georgia when he was younger from [[Rossville, Georgia|Rossville]] to [[Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia|Fort Oglethorpe]] to [[LaFayette, Georgia|LaFayette]] before finally settling in [[Red Bank, Tennessee]].<ref name="cmchat">{{cite web |url=http://www.cmchatlive.com/kane-brown/ |title=Meet Kane Brown|date= November 7, 2014 |first=Jen |last=Swirsky |work= Country Music Chat}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bandpage.com/KaneBrown/bio |title=Kane Brown: Biography |work=BandPage |archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20160321195305/https://www.bandpage.com/KaneBrown/bio |archive-date=March 21, 2016 }}</ref> As a result, he attended many schools, among them [[Lakeview-Fort Oglethorpe High School]] in [[Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia]], where he sang in the choir with [[Lauren Alaina]], the runner-up on [[American Idol (season 10)|Season 10]] of ''American Idol''.<ref name="timesfreee">{{cite news |url=http://www.timesfreepress.com/news/life/entertainment/story/2015/oct/19/meet-chattanoogas-kane-brown/330954/ |title= Meet Chattanooga's Kane Brown |date=October 19, 2015 |first= Barry |last=Courter|work=Times Free Press }}</ref> He also attended [[Red Bank High School|Red Bank]], Ridgeland, and [[Soddy Daisy High School]]s.<ref name="timesfreee" /> After graduation, he lived with his grandmother, who had also helped raise him.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://rarecountry.com/news/kane-browns-visit-with-his-nana-will-melt-your-heart/ |title=Kane Brown‚Äôs visit with his Nana will melt your heart |author=Deborah Evans Price|date= August 17, 2017 |work=Rare Country}}</ref>

Brown grew up listening to country music but became interested in [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] music in middle school. However, after winning a school talent show in 11th grade with a rendition of [[Chris Young (musician)|Chris Young]]'s "[[Gettin' You Home (The Black Dress Song)|Gettin' You Home]]", he began to perform country music.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://theshotgunseat.com/get-closer-kane-brown/ |title=Get Closer to Kane Brown |first=Hope |last=Scott |date= July 2, 2015 |work= The Shotgun Seat}}</ref> He auditioned for both ''[[American Idol]]'' and ''[[The X Factor (US TV series)|The X Factor]]'' after the success of his school friend Lauren Alaina on ''American Idol''.<ref name=Billboard-YouTubeUpstart-2016>{{cite news|first1=Jewly|last1=Hight|title=YouTube Upstart Kane Brown Is Breaking Country's Rules: 'The World's Not Used to It'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/magazine-feature/7282626/youtube-upstart-kane-brown-breaking-countrys-rules|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 25, 2016}}</ref> He was chosen for ''The X Factor'' after an audition in 2013 but left the show when its producers wanted to include him in a boy band.<ref name="timesfreee" /> After that, he decided to post his cover versions of popular songs online.<ref name=Billboard-YouTubeUpstart-2016 /><ref>{{youtube|id=lVvaNCrKQsM|title=Yes, I Made It! Kane Brown ‚Äì THE X FACTOR USA 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2014, Kane Brown began posting videos of his covers of songs by [[Brantley Gilbert]], [[Billy Currington]], [[Alan Jackson]], and other singers on social media. He acquired a following for his homemade videos on Facebook. His cover of [[Lee Brice]]'s "[[I Don't Dance (Lee Brice song)|I Don't Dance]]" was one early video that gained wide attention.<ref name="cmchat"/> Released on September 30, 2015, his cover of [[George Strait]]'s "[[Check Yes or No]]" went viral and received more than seven million views.<ref name=hdd>{{cite web |url=http://hitsdailydouble.com/music_city&id=298006 |title=Brown is the New Black |date=October 8, 2015 |work=Hits Daily Double }}</ref> His number of followers on Facebook quickly reached more than a million, leading to a teaser clip for his own single "[[Used to Love You Sober]]" hitting one million views in less than three hours upon its release on October 8, 2015<ref name=hdd /> and then reaching more than 11 million views in two weeks.<ref name="cauldfield 2015">{{cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6738417/billboard-200-chart-moves-kane-brown |title=Billboard 200 Chart Moves: Indie Country Singer Kane Brown Breaks Into Top 40|first= Keith |last=Caulfield |date= October 23, 2015 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

Brown was initially signed to [[Zone 4 (record label)|Zone 4]]<ref name=NYTimes-IrvingPlaza-2016 /> with [[Jay Frank]] as his first manager.<ref name="cauldfield 2015"/> Martha Earls became his manager in 2016.<ref name=WashPost-Profile-2016 />

===2014: ''Closer'' EP===
In 2014, Brown raised funds via [[crowdfunding]] site [[Kickstarter]] to produce a six-song EP,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/kanebrown/kane-browns-debut-ep |title=Kane Brown's debut EP |work=Kickstarter }}</ref> which he recorded in Nashville at the home studio of Noah Henson, guitarist for [[Brantley Gilbert]]. The EP, titled ''[[Closer (Kane Brown EP)|Closer]]'', was released on June 2, 2015, and debuted on the Top Country Albums chart at number 22, selling 3,200 copies in its debut week.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2015/06/35685-country-album-chart-report-june-11-2015 |title=Country Album Chart Report For June 11, 2015|first= Matt |last=Bjorke |date= June 11, 2015 |work=Roughstock }} Sales figure given [https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/172kLuk5hsRAaHdkBYmwgmDq8cQPE4FyDRaZslZpUxNQ/edit?pli=1#gid=0 here]</ref> A single from the EP, "Don't Go City on Me", was first released on October 22, 2014, and reached number 43 on the Country Digital Songs chart.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2014/10/30337-country-musics-top-30-digital-singles-october-29-2014 |title=Country Music's Top 30 Digital Singles: October 29, 2014|first= Matt|last= Bjorke |date= October 29, 2014 |work=Roughstock }}</ref>

===2015: "Used to Love You Sober" and follow-up singles===
Brown released a new single "[[Used to Love You Sober]]" on his birthday (October 21) in 2015, and the song was spotlighted on [[Zane Lowe]]'s [[Beats 1]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://headlineplanet.com/home/2015/10/22/kane-browns-used-to-love-you-sober-erupts-on-itunes-sales-chart/ |title=Emerging country star Kane Brown makes a sudden ‚Äì and significant ‚Äì splash on the iTunes single sales chart|first=Brian |last=Cantor |work=Headline Planet }}</ref> It reached number two on the Country Digital Songs chart based on just two days of sales, with 38,000 copies sold.<ref name="roughstock october 2015">{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2015/10/39150-top-30-digital-singles-october-27-2015 |title=Top 30 Digital Singles: October 27, 2015|first= Matt |last=Bjorke |date= October 27, 2015 |work=Roughstock }}</ref> The release of the single also pushed his EP ''Closer'' up the chart to number 22 on the Top Album Sales chart.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/6745225/drake-returns-to-no-1-on-billboard-artist-100-demi-lovato-pentatonix-surge |title=Drake Returns to No. 1 on Billboard Artist 100; Demi Lovato & Pentatonix Surge|first= Gary |last=Trust |date= October 28, 2015 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> A second song, "[[Last Minute Late Night]]", was released on November 4, 2015, and debuted on the Country Digital Songs chart at number nine, with 26,000 copies sold.<ref name="roughstock november 2015">{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2015/11/39323-top-30-digital-singles-november-9-2015 |title=Top 30 Digital Singles: November 9, 2015|first= Matt |last=Bjorke |date= November 9, 2015 |work=Roughstock }}</ref> Another single, "I Love That I Hate You", followed three weeks later on November 30, 2015, and sold 17,000 copies in the first week.<ref name="roughstock december2015">{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2015/12/39445-top-30-digital-country-singles-december-7-2015 |title=Top 30 Digital Country Singles: December 7, 2015|first= Matt |last=Bjorke |date= December 7, 2015|work=Roughstock }}</ref>

In June 2020, Brown released the song "Worldwide Beautiful", which aims to highlight peace and equality and benefits the [[Boys & Girls Clubs of America|Boys and Girls Club of America]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-04|title=Country Star Kane Brown Drops Hopeful New Song, ‚ÄòWorldwide Beautiful‚Äô (Listen)|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/kane-brown-new-song-worldwide-beautiful-listen-1234625328/|access-date=2020-06-04|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref> In July 2020, Brown released his second single to pop radio, "[[Be Like That (Kane Brown song)|Be Like That]]" with [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] and [[Swae Lee]], another single off his EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.countrynow.com/kane-brown-teases-superstar-collaboration-be-like-that/|title=Kane Brown Teases Superstar Collaboration, ‚ÄòBe Like That‚Äô|website=Country Now|first=Lauren Jo|last=Black|date=July 7, 2020}}</ref>

In September 2020, Brown announced a drive-in concert, held at more than 200 theaters across the U.S. on September 26, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/music/2020/09/08/kane-brown-drive-concert-show-more-than-100-outdoor-venues/5747582002/|publisher=[[USA Today]]|date=8 September 2020|title=Country fans can watch Kane Brown's drive-in concert at more than 200 outdoor venues|first1=Gary|last1=Dinges}}</ref> That same week Brown appeared on stage at the [[iHeartRadio Music Festival]] in a PORTL "hologram" machine to sing "Be Like That" live from Nashville with [[Swae Lee]] and [[Khalid]] who were in the studio in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/festivals/9452484/iheartradio-music-festival-2020-6-cant-miss-moments-night-two|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=20 September 2020|title=6 Can't Miss Moments from iHeartRadio Music Festival Night 2|first1=Carl|last1=Lamarre}}</ref>

In October 2020, "[[Worship You]]" was sent to country radio as his second official single to that format off the EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/kane-brown-worship-you/|title= Kane Brown Sends Steamy Love Song ‚ÄòWorship You‚Äô to Country Radio [Listen]|website=Taste of Country|first=Billy|last=Dukes|date=October 12, 2020}}</ref>

On November 26, 2020, Brown performed in a pre-recorded halftime show in the ''Thanksgiving Thursday Night Football'' game between [[Washington Football Team]] and the [[Dallas Cowboys]] at [[AT&T Stadium]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dallas Cowboys, Kane Brown Dedicate Thanksgiving Halftime to Help Rescue Christmas|url=https://www.nbcdfw.com/news/sports/blue-star/dallas-cowboys-kane-brown-dedicate-thanksgiving-halftime-to-help-rescue-christmas/2490608/|access-date=2020-11-26|website=NBC 5 Dallas-Fort Worth|language=en-US}}</ref>

On February 11, 2021, Brown launched his own label, 1021 Entertainment, in association with Sony Nashville.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2021/2/10/kane-brown-launches-1021-entertainment-label-with-sony-music.html |title=Kane Brown launches 1021 Entertainment label with Sony Music Nashville |first=Cillea |last=Houghton|date=February 11, 2021|work=ABC News Radio}}</ref>

Also in 2021, Brown became the first black person to win Video of the Year at the [[Academy of Country Music Awards|ACM Awards]], which he won for ‚ÄúWorldwide Beautiful.‚Äù<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://wincountry.com/2021/04/16/ahead-of-the-acms-jimmie-allen-shares-his-vision-for-the-future-of-racial-equality-in-country-music/|title=Ahead of the ACMs, Jimmie Allen shares his vision for the future of racial equality in country music}}</ref> He was included in 2021's [[Time 100|''Time'' 100]], ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s annual list of the 100 most influential people in the world.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Courter |first1=Barry |title=Chattanooga native Kane Brown being named to 'Time 100' list not surprising to those who know him |url=https://www.timesfreepress.com/news/life/entertainment/story/2021/sep/21/kane-brown-time/554588/ |website=[[Chattanooga Times Free Press]] |access-date=November 14, 2021 |language=en |date=September 21, 2021}}</ref>
