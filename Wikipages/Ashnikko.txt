'''Ashton Nicole Casey''' (born February 19, 1996), known professionally as '''Ashnikko''' ({{IPAc-en|√¶| É|Àà|n|iÀê|k|o ä}} {{respell|ash|NEE|koh}}<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GbnLZlMGqvQ|title=ASHNIKKO Talks Ghosting, Sleep Paralysis and "STUPID"|publisher=[[TikTok]]|website=[[YouTube]]|date=January 7, 2020|access-date=March 8, 2021}} ''Ashnikko pronounces her name at the start of the video.''</ref>), is an American singer and rapper. This article uses feminine pronouns for consistency.}} rose to prominence with her 2019 single "[[Stupid (Ashnikko song)|Stupid]]" with [[Yung Baby Tate]], which gained viral popularity on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]] and was [[Music recording certification|certified gold]] in the United States and Canada.<ref name="Wetmore">{{Cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/miley-cyrus-cody-simpson-tik-tok-2641150452.html|title=Miley Cyrus Made a TikTok to Ashnikko With Cody Simpson|last=Wetmore|first=Brendan|date=October 29, 2019|website=[[Paper (magazine)|PAPER]]|language=en|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> Ashnikko's debut mixtape, ''[[Demidevil]]'', was released in January 2021, and spawned the singles "[[Daisy (Ashnikko song)|Daisy]]" and "Slumber Party" featuring [[Princess Nokia]].

== Early life ==
Ashton Nicole Casey<ref name="ascap">{{Cite web|last=Casey|first=Ashton Nicole|title=ASCAP Ace Search|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/writer/883442414/CASEY%20ASHTON%20NICOLE|access-date=November 23, 2019|publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP)}}</ref> was born on February 19, 1996, in [[Oak Ridge, North Carolina]],<ref name=":4" /> and raised in the city of [[Greensboro, North Carolina|Greensboro]]. Her parents exposed her to [[country music]] and [[Slipknot (band)|Slipknot]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicjournal.com.br/americana-ashnikko-celebra-sucesso-de-stupid-proximo-do-top-200-global/|title=Americana Ashnikko celebra sucesso de "Stupid", pr√≥ximo do TOP 200 Globalz|last=Reda√ß√£o|date=November 13, 2019|website=Jornal do Rap|language=pt-BR|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> She recalls becoming interested in music, specifically rap music, when she listened to ''[[Arular]]'' by [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A.]] at the age of 10,<ref name=":0" /> and says she did not listen to male musicians until she was 16 years old.<ref name="bbc" /> As a teenager, her family moved to [[Estonia]] for her father's studies, spending a year there before relocating again to [[Riga]], [[Latvia]].<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/next-wave/next-wave-862-ashnikko|title=Next Wave #862: Ashnikko|last=Martin|first=Felicity|date=August 18, 2018|website=[[Clash Magazine]]|language=en|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> At one point, she was the only American in Latvia to attend a Latvian public high school.<ref name=":2" /> At the age of 18, Ashnikko moved to [[London]], England by herself.<ref name="paper">{{cite web |last1=Wetmore |first1=Brendan |title=Ashnikko Is Ready for All of It |url=https://www.papermag.com/ashnikko-tiktok-interview-2641504657.html |website=PAPER |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=December 4, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2016‚Äì2019: Career beginnings and ''Hi It's Me'' ===
Ashnikko's first song, "Krokodil", was produced by [[Raf Riley]] and published to [[SoundCloud]] in July 2016.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ashnikko x Raf Riley - KROKODIL|date=July 31, 2016|url=https://soundcloud.com/ashnikko/ashnikko-x-raf-riley-krokodil|via=[[SoundCloud]]|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> She released her first EP, ''Sass Pancakes'', under Digital Picnic Records in 2017.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://oddchildmusic.com/ashnikko-signs-her-first-ep-to-digital-picnic-records/|title=Ashnikko signs her first EP to Digital Picnic Records|date=February 7, 2017|website=[[OddChild Music]]|language=en-US|access-date=November 23, 2019|archive-date=November 23, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191123175906/http://oddchildmusic.com/ashnikko-signs-her-first-ep-to-digital-picnic-records/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8542485/ashnikko-interview-stupid|title=Meet Ashnikko, the Rapper Carving a Lane Of Her Own With 'Stupid'|last=Spruch|first=Kirsten|date=November 4, 2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> The EP was produced by Raf Riley and features appearances from [[Avelino (rapper)|Avelino]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://listen.tidal.com/album/71979355|title=Sass Pancakes by Ashnikko|website=[[Tidal (service)|Tidal]]|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://oddchildmusic.com/ashnikko-recruits-raf-riley-avelino-for-new-track-bubblegum/|title=Ashnikko recruits Raf Riley & Avelino for New Track ‚Äì Bubblegum|date=April 4, 2017|website=[[OddChild Music]]|language=en-US|access-date=November 23, 2019|archive-date=February 26, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200226001801/http://oddchildmusic.com/ashnikko-recruits-raf-riley-avelino-for-new-track-bubblegum/|url-status=dead}}</ref> Ashnikko's second EP, ''Unlikeable'', was released in November 2018. The EP spawned the singles "Blow", "Nice Girl", "Invitation" featuring [[Kodie Shane]], and "No Brainer".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/listen-ashnikko-shares-unlikeable-ep|title=Listen: Ashnikko Shares 'Unlikeable' EP|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=November 23, 2018|website=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

Ashnikko released her third EP, ''Hi It's Me'', in July 2019.<ref>{{Citation|title=Hi, It's Me|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/1lusJhIj1nFIK4t5NaluAU|language=en|access-date=October 26, 2020}}</ref> The EP was preceded by the promotional single "Special" and was launched alongside the EP's title track and lead single, "Hi It's Me". The second official single, "[[Stupid (Ashnikko song)|Stupid]]", featuring [[Yung Baby Tate]], gained [[Viral video|viral]] popularity on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]].<ref name="Wetmore"/><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.warnermusic.co.nz/news/ashnikko-s-tiktok-hit-stupid-has-just-become-spotify-s-newest-1-viral-track|title=How Ashnikko's 'Stupid' has Just Become Spotify's #1 viral track|website=[[Warner Music New Zealand]]|language=en|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> The song reached number one on the [[Bubbling Under Hot 100|''Billboard'' Bubbling Under Hot 100]] chart, the [[Bubbling Under R&B/Hip-Hop Singles|''Billboard'' Bubbling Under R&B/Hip-Hop]] chart, and the [[Spotify Viral 50]] chart.<ref name="BU1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/bubbling-under-hot-100-singles/2019-11-16|title=Bubbling Under Hot 100 - November 16, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref><ref name="BU2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/bubbling-under-r-and-b-hip-hop-singles/2019-11-16|title=Bubbling Under R&B/Hip-Hop Singles - November 16, 2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":1" /> "Stupid" was also [[Music recording certification|certified gold]] in the United States in August 2020, as well as in Canada.<ref name="wonderland">{{cite web |last1=Morgan |first1=Maybelle |title=Ashnikko Teams Up With Beats and TikTok For #BeatsDaisyChallenge |url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2020/08/07/ashnikko-beats-tiktok-daisy/ |website=Wonderland |access-date=October 24, 2020 |language=en |date=August 7, 2020}}</ref><ref name="RIAA">{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=ashnikko#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum: Ashnikko|website=RIAA|access-date=July 16, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold/Platinum|url=https://musiccanada.com/gold-platinum/?_gp_search=ashnikko|access-date=August 17, 2020|website=Music Canada|language=en-US}}</ref> Another song from the EP, "Working Bitch", also found popularity on TikTok.<ref name="nme" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Lindsay |first1=Kathryn |title=The Songs You're Hearing All Over TikTok In 2020 |url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/02/9451416/tiktok-songs-2020-playlist#slide-7 |website=Refinery29 |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> Ashnikko embarked on a North American tour supporting American rapper [[Danny Brown]] in October 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/10/21/danny-brown-call-out-fan-ashnikko|title=Watch Danny Brown call out a male fan for disrespecting support act Ashnikko|last=Renshaw|first=David|date=October 23, 2019|website=[[The FADER]]|language=en|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mnstrmmedia.com/news/2019/10/22/ashnikko-drops-video-for-viral-fck-you-anthem-stupid-feat-yung-baby-tate|title=Ashnikko Drops Video For Viral F*ck You Anthem "Stupid" feat. Yung Baby Tate|date=October 22, 2019|website=MNSTRM MEDIA|language=en-US|access-date=November 23, 2019|archive-date=November 23, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191123175738/http://www.mnstrmmedia.com/news/2019/10/22/ashnikko-drops-video-for-viral-fck-you-anthem-stupid-feat-yung-baby-tate|url-status=dead}}</ref> She released the promotional single "Halloweenie II: Pumpkin Spice" in October 2019.<ref>{{Citation|title=Halloweenie II: Pumpkin Spice|url=https://open.spotify.com/track/0RcwpbPBHFj18vIs5yGdxg|language=en|access-date=October 26, 2020}}</ref> She co-wrote eight songs, two of which she is featured on, on [[Brooke Candy]]'s debut album, ''[[Sexorcism (Brooke Candy album)|Sexorcism]]'', which was released in October 2019.<ref name="paper" />

=== 2020‚Äìpresent: Breakthrough and ''Demidevil'' ===
Ashnikko co-wrote the song "[[Boss Bitch]]" with American rapper [[Doja Cat]], which was included on the ''[[Birds of Prey (2020 film)|Birds of Prey]]'' soundtrack, ''[[Birds of Prey (soundtrack)|Birds of Prey: The Album]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Doja Cat Flexes On Her Haters With "Boss B*tch" From The 'Birds Of Prey' Soundtrack|url=https://genius.com/a/doja-cat-flexes-on-her-haters-with-boss-b-tch-from-the-birds-of-prey-soundtrack|access-date=November 24, 2020|website=genius.com}}</ref> Prior to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Ashnikko was scheduled to accompany [[Doja Cat]] on her Hot Pink Tour throughout the United States in March 2020 before it was cancelled.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wang |first1=Steffanee |title=Doja Cat Announces Hot Pink Tour With Ashnikko |url=https://www.nylon.com/entertainment/doja-cat-tour-dates-ashnikko |website=Nylon |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=February 24, 2020}}</ref> In March 2020, she released the standalone single "Tantrum". Later that month, she performed "Tantrum" live as part of ''[[The Fader]]''{{'}}s Digital [[The Fader#The Fader Fort|Fader Fort]] event.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gwee |first1=Karen |title=FADER FORT announces nine-hour broadcast with Kesha, Ashnikko and more |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/fader-fort-announces-nine-hour-broadcast-with-kesha-ashnikko-and-more-2636063 |website=NME |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=March 27, 2020}}</ref> "[[Cry (Ashnikko song)|Cry]]", featuring Canadian musician [[Grimes (musician)|Grimes]], was then released in June 2020, alongside an animated music video.<ref name="papercry">{{cite web |last1=Wetmore |first1=Brendan |title=Ashnikko Breaks Down Her Banger 'Cry,' Featuring Grimes |url=https://www.papermag.com/ashnikko-breaks-down-new-song-cry-featuring-grimes-2646194549.html?rebelltitem=2#rebelltitem2 |website=PAPER |access-date=October 24, 2020 |language=en |date=June 17, 2020}}</ref> The song's music video was nominated for "Best Pop Video - UK" at the [[2020 UK Music Video Awards]].<ref name="ukmva" /> Following this, "[[Daisy (Ashnikko song)|Daisy]]" was released in July 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kenneally|first=Cerys|date=August 28, 2020|title=Ashnikko details new Demidevil mixtape|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/ashnikko-details-new-demidevil-mixtape|access-date=September 12, 2020|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]}}</ref> and a music video for the song was released in collaboration with [[Beats Electronics|Beats by Dre]] and TikTok a month later.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 7, 2020|title=Ashnikko Teams Up With Beats and TikTok For #BeatsDaisyChallenge|url=http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2020/08/07/ashnikko-beats-tiktok-daisy/|access-date=October 10, 2020|website=Wonderland|language=en-US}}</ref> "Daisy" became Ashnikko's breakthrough hit, charting internationally in countries including Australia, Belgium and the United Kingdom. It reached a peak of number 24 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], becoming her first charting and first top 40 hit in the UK.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ashnikko|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/57182/ashnikko/|access-date=September 28, 2020|website=www.officialcharts.com}}</ref> Ashnikko's third Halloween song, "Halloweenie III: Seven Days", was released in October 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ashnikko is celebrating Halloween with her new track, 'Halloweenie III: Seven Days' |url=https://www.readdork.com/news/ashnikko-track-halloweenie-iii-seven-days |website=Dork |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=October 23, 2020}}</ref> She was nominated for [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best Push Act|Best Push Act]] at the [[2020 MTV Europe Music Awards]].<ref name="ema" />

"Cry" and "Daisy" both appear on Ashnikko's debut mixtape, ''[[Demidevil]]'', which was released on January 15, 2021. "Deal with It" featuring [[Kelis]] and "Slumber Party" featuring [[Princess Nokia]] served as the third and fourth singles from the mixtape respectively. In support of the mixtape, Ashnikko announced, The Demidevil Tour, which is set to take place in October 2021, with shows in North America, the UK and Ireland.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|date=May 6, 2021|title=Ashnikko announces UK and Ireland tour for December 2021|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/ashnikko-announces-uk-and-ireland-tour-for-december-2021-2935055|access-date=May 16, 2021|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Ashnikko possesses a [[mezzo-soprano]] vocal range. Her musical style has been described by critics as a fusion of several genres, including [[Pop music|pop]], [[indie pop]], [[rock music|rock]],<ref name="bbc" /><ref name="vogue" /> [[alt-pop]],<ref name="pitch">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/ashnikko-demidevil/|title=Ashnikko: Demidevil Album Review|last=Bardhan|first=Ashley|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=January 21, 2021|access-date=March 19, 2021}}</ref> [[Hip hop music|hip hop]],¬†[[Bubblegum music|bubblegum pop]], [[indie rock]], [[Punk rock|punk]],<ref name="rap">{{cite web|last=Juon|first=Steve|title=Ashnikko :: Demidevil|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/2021/01/ashnikko-demidevil/|website=Rap Reviews|access-date=May 30, 2021|date=January 26, 2021|url-status=live }}</ref> and [[electropop]].<ref name="bio">{{cite web|last=Collar|first=Matt|title=Ashnikko Biography by Matt Collar|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ashnikko-mn0003594453|website=AllMusic|accessdate=July 15, 2021}}</ref> She described her style of music as "angry, punk, hip hop, sad-girl-[[Feminism|feminist]], bubblegum, poo-poo music", and has clarified that her music is not intended to be comedic or parody-based.<ref name="nme"/>

Ashnikko is known for her Tokyo-inspired street fashion.<ref name="nme">{{cite web |last1=Shutler |first1=Ali |title=Ashnikko is the Miley Cyrus-approved bubblepunk pop-star who refuses to take their self seriously |url=https://www.nme.com/features/nme-radar-breakout-ashnikko-interview-stupid-2650847 |website=NME |access-date=October 25, 2020 |language=en |date=April 22, 2020}}</ref> She is also known for her unique blue hair, which is originally brown. In the video for "Nice Girl" and some of her early [[vlog]]s, her hair is pastel green. Her hair in the "Daisy" music video is yellow, pink, and red.<ref name="vogue">{{cite web |last1=Higgins |first1=Sam |title=8 New Music Artists To Know Thanks To TikTok |url=https://www.vogue.co.uk/arts-and-lifestyle/article/tiktok-music-artists |website=British Vogue |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=July 11, 2020}}</ref><ref name="paperbrit">{{cite web |last1=Song |first1=Sandra |title=Ashnikko Is a Blue Velvet Vision at the Brit Awards |url=https://www.papermag.com/ashnikko-wesley-berryman-brits-2645186786.html?rebelltitem=5#rebelltitem5 |website=Paper |access-date=October 25, 2020 |language=en |date=February 19, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Yalcinkaya |first1=Gunseli |title=Ashnikko: 'People call me Hatsune Miku on crack' |url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/49585/1/ashnikko-interview-cry-grimes-tiktok |website=Dazed |access-date=October 25, 2020 |language=en |date=June 22, 2020}}</ref>

Ashnikko has cited her musical influences as [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A.]], [[Gwen Stefani]], [[Lil' Kim]], [[Bj√∂rk]], [[Paramore]], [[Avril Lavigne]], [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Missy Elliott]], [[Dolly Parton]], [[Janis Joplin]], and [[Joan Jett]], and has stated that her favorite song is "[[Bossy (Kelis song)|Bossy]]" by [[Kelis]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vinti |first1=Mike |title=20 Questions ‚Äì Ashnikko |url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/long-reads/20-questions-ashnikko/ |website=Crack Magazine |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=May 22, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Broster |first1=Alice |title=Ashnikko's Brand Of F*ck You Alt Pop Is The TikTok Success Story To Get Behind |url=https://www.bustle.com/p/singer-songwriter-ashnikko-takes-on-the-bustle-booth-19420141 |website=Bustle |access-date=October 26, 2020 |language=en |date=December 3, 2019}}</ref><ref name="bbc">{{cite web |last1=Holden |first1=Steve |title=Ashnikko: 'I don't take no for an answer' |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-54345411 |website=BBC News |access-date=October 24, 2020 |date=October 2, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Ashnikko is Here to Shake Things Up |url=https://vmagazine.com/article/ashnikko-is-here-to-shake-things-up/ |website=V Magazine |access-date=October 24, 2020 |language=en |date=September 17, 2020}}</ref> She has also listed [[Doja Cat]], [[Grimes (musician)|Grimes]], [[Tierra Whack]], [[Rico Nasty]], [[Princess Nokia]], [[Kim Petras]], and [[Charli XCX]] as her "peers" and artists she "really respects".<ref name="nme" />

== Personal life ==
In June 2019, Ashnikko stated on Twitter that she is [[bisexual]].<ref>{{cite tweet|author=ashnikko|author-link=ashnikko|user=ashnikko|number=1147154183981862913|date=July 5, 2019|title=HAPPY PRIDE, BABIES!!!! üè≥Ô∏è‚Äçüåàthis is the first year I have been truly honest with myself about my bisexuality and I'm really happy!!!!|language=en|access-date=May 31, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210515204150/https://twitter.com/ashnikko/status/1147154183981862913|archive-date=May 15, 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> In a 2020 interview, she clarified that she is [[Pansexuality|pansexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Russell|first=Erica|date=June 24, 2020|title=Ashnikko Has Entered The Chat|url=http://www.ladygunn.com/cover-story/ashnikko-has-entered-the-chat/|url-status=live|access-date=May 17, 2021|website=LADYGUNN|language=en-US}}</ref>

In July 2020, while discussing the meaning behind the lyrics of her single "Daisy", she said that while her family practices [[Christianity]], she does not.<ref>{{Citation|title=Ashnikko "Daisy" Official Lyrics & Meaning {{!}} Verified|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F3p8Appnu_g|language=en|access-date=July 15, 2021}}</ref>

In May 2021, Ashnikko came out as [[genderfluid]] on Twitter, saying that "i just didn't feel ready to tell the internet yet but i guess now's a good time since everyone's doing a dissection into my sexuality and gender identity".<ref>{{cite tweet |author=ashnikko |author-link=ashnikko |user=ashnikko |number=1396622094721601543 |date=May 23, 2021 |title=and to clarify i am pansexual and genderfluid i just didn't feel ready to tell the internet yet but i guess now's a good time since everyone's doing a dissection into my sexuality and gender identity |language=en |access-date=May 31, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210524115640/https://twitter.com/ashnikko/status/1396622094721601543 |archive-date=May 24, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> She uses [[She (pronoun)|she/her]] and [[singular they|they/them]] pronouns.

=== Advocacy ===
Ashnikko has described herself as a feminist, and has attributed discovering [[intersectional feminism]] through [[microblogging]] website [[Tumblr]] as a teenager as a turning point for her.<ref name="nme" /><ref name="wonderland" /> She has been vocal about a range of social justice issues, having spoken out about the commercialization of feminism in November 2019, while advocating for victims of police brutality through social media.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Subvrtmag|date=November 20, 2019|title=Meet Ashnikko, The Badass Viral Music Sensation|url=https://www.subvrtmag.com/meet-ashnikko-the-badass-viral-sensation/|access-date=June 27, 2020|website=Subvrt Mag|language=en-US}}</ref>
