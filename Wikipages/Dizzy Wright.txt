'''La'Reonte Wright''' (born November 26, 1990), known professionally as '''Dizzy Wright''', is an American rapper, record producer, and songwriter.

In December 2011, Wright signed to rapper [[Hopsin]]'s independent record label, [[Funk Volume]]. After signing, he released his debut studio album ''SmokeOut Conversations'' in April 2012. He followed that with ''The First Agreement'' in December of that year, and then a mixtape ''[[The Golden Age (Dizzy Wright album)|The Golden Age]]'' in August 2013. Following touring with Hopsin in early 2014, Dizzy Wright released ''[[State of Mind (EP)|State of Mind]]'', an EP that would peak at number 54 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. On May 22, 2015, he released his second studio album ''[[The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)|The Growing Process]]'' which peaked at number  47 on the ''Billboard'' 200.

==Early life==
Wright looked up to [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]], of which his uncles [[Layzie Bone]] and [[Flesh-N-Bone]] are members.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ballerstatus.com/2012/07/25/qa-dizzy-wright-explains-difficulty-breaking-outta-las-vegas-mom-writing-rhymes/|title=Q&A: Dizzy Wright Explains Difficulty Of Breaking Outta Las Vegas, His Mom Writing His Rhymes|date=July 26, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/2013-freshmen/dizzy-wright/|title=Dizzy Wright - XXL|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> At the age of four he moved with his mom and siblings to [[Las Vegas Valley|Las Vegas]], [[Nevada]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/who-is-dizzy-wright/growing-up-in-las-vegas |title=Growing Up In Las Vegas — Who Is Dizzy Wright? |publisher=Complex |date=2013-06-10 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref> He began rapping at just 8 years old with the group DaFuture which included his brother and a very close friend. His mother wrote his raps at the time and was a concert promoter so he was exposed to the music industry early even doing youth reporting at major awards shows like the [[BET Awards]] and interviewing [[Tyrese Gibson|Tyrese]], [[St. Lunatics]] and [[Boyz II Men]]. He credits this for being the reason he wants to stay away from [[major record label]]s and stick to independent labels.<ref name="HHDX interview">{{Cite web |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22276/title.dizzy-wright-outlines-what-it-means-to-put-las-vegas-on-the-map-new-ep |title=Dizzy Wright Outlines What It Means To Put Las Vegas On The Map, New EP {{!}} Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales {{!}} HipHop DX<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=2012-12-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131018051101/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22276/title.dizzy-wright-outlines-what-it-means-to-put-las-vegas-on-the-map-new-ep |archive-date=2013-10-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In seventh grade Wright lived in a homeless shelter with his family for five months.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/who-is-dizzy-wright/living-in-a-homeless-shelter |title=Living In A Homeless Shelter — Who Is Dizzy Wright? |publisher=Complex |date=2013-06-10 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref> Throughout the rest of high school he lived in [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] and [[California]] before moving back to Las Vegas when he was in twelfth grade.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/who-is-dizzy-wright/finishing-up-school |title=Finishing Up School — Who Is Dizzy Wright? |publisher=Complex |date=2013-06-10 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref> In 2010, Wright took part in and won the Sheikh Music Rip the Mic competition, which impressed record label [[Funk Volume]].<ref name="FunkVolumeBio">{{Cite web |url=http://myfunkvolume.com/artist/dizzy-wright/ |title=Funk Volume Music<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=2012-12-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140103070129/http://myfunkvolume.com/artist/dizzy-wright |archive-date=2014-01-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2010, he also appeared on ''[[106 & Park]]''{{'s}} Wild Out Wednesday, as Dizzy D Flashy.<ref name=autogenerated2>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2011/11/the-break-presents-dizzy-wright/|title=The Break Presents: Dizzy Wright - XXL|first=X. ==Musical career==

===2010–2012: Signing to Funk Volume and ''Smokeout Conversations''===
In 2011, Dizzy Wright released his mixtape, titled ''Soul Searchin' Next Level'', which would be a final release under Bluestar Records.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dizzy-wright-soul-searchin-next-level-mixtape.32168.html|title=Dizzy Wright - Soul Searchin Next Level|website=HotNewHipHop|date=5 November 2011}}</ref> In 2012, Wright signed to rapper [[Hopsin]]'s independent record label [[Funk Volume]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/10/funk-volume-xxl-issue-151/ |title=L.A. Indie Label Funk Volume Survives And Thrives - XXL Issue 151 - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-10-31 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref> Wright's debut studio album, ''Smokeout Conversations'', was released on [[420 (cannabis culture)|April 20]], 2012, in honor of the annual [[marijuana]] type-of "holiday" under Funk Volume.<ref name="SOC">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/smokeout-conversations/534784055|title=SmokeOut Conversations by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=April 20, 2012|access-date=December 26, 2019}}</ref> The album included the single "Can't Trust Em", and peaked at number 42 on the US [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] and number eight on the US [[Heatseekers Albums]] charts.<ref name="R&BRap">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/dizzy-wright/chart-history/BLP|title=Dizzy Wright - Chart History: Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|work=Billboard|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Heatseeker">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/dizzy-wright/chart-history/TLN|title=Dizzy Wright - Chart History: Heatseekers Albums|work=Billboard|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> Months later, Wright released a reloaded version of a mixtape, titled ''Free SmokeOut Conversations'', which has been downloaded over 170,000 times.<ref name="FunkVolumeBio"/> This mixtape features guest appearances from a fellow rapper and his label-mate Hopsin, and [[SwizZz]]. It also contains his single "Independent Living". Not withstanding the original plan to release the mixtape prior to the album, but then instead Dizzy released the album.<ref name=autogenerated1 /> The album's success allowed him to perform on a national 30-day headlining tour following its release.<ref name="FunkVolumeBio"/>

He capped off the year performing on the Funk Volume Tour 2012 with Hopsin, SwizZz and [[Jarren Benton]]. On December 3, 2012, Dizzy released his debut [[extended play]], titled ''The First Agreement''.<ref name="FunkVolumeBio"/> The EP was named after [[Don Miguel Ruiz]]'s book ''[[The Four Agreements]]''.<ref name="HHDX interview"/> Its lead single, "Fly High", was released.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/artists/dizzy-wright|title=Dizzy Wright|website=DJBooth}}</ref> The EP debuted at number 41 on the Top R&B/Hip Hop Albums chart and number 25 on the [[Top Rap Albums]] charts.<ref name="R&BRap"/><ref name="RapAlbums">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/dizzy-wright/chart-history/RLP|title=Dizzy Wright - Chart History: Top Rap Albums|work=Billboard|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> Wright had also hinted at the releases of more chapters in the series.<ref name="HHDX interview"/>

===2013–2014: ''The Golden Age'' and ''State of Mind''===
{{main|The Golden Age (Dizzy Wright album)|State of Mind (EP)}}

XXL}}</ref> Three days later he would release the music video for a new song titled, "Still Movin'" which featured cameos from his Funk Volume label mates which was followed by "Killem Wit Kindness" a few days later.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/videos/id.12767/title.dizzy-wright-still-movin- |title=Dizzy Wright - Along with production from [[DJ Hoppa]], Rikio, [[Kato (producer)|Kato]], [[6ix (record producer)|6ix]], and [[Cardo (record producer)|Cardo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/who-is-dizzy-wright/the-golden-age |title=His upcoming mixtape, The Golden Age — Who Is Dizzy Wright? |publisher=Complex |date=2013-06-10 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Baker |first=Soren |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24582/title.dizzy-wright-the-golden-age-release-date-cover-art-tracklist |title=Dizzy Wright "The Golden Age" Cover Art, Tracklist & Mixtape Download &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-08-19 |access-date=2013-11-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130818101835/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24582/title.dizzy-wright-the-golden-age-release-date-cover-art-tracklist/ |archive-date=2013-08-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The mixtape was released for free, and for purchase on [[iTunes]] on August 19, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-golden-age/id689907128?ign-mpt=uo%3D2 |title=iTunes - Music - The Golden Age by Dizzy Wright |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2013-08-19 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/08/mixtape-dizzy-wright-the-golden-age |title=Dizzy Wright "The Golden Age" Mixtape |publisher=Complex |date=2013-08-19 |access-date=2013-11-30}}</ref> Due to its retail release, the mixtape debuted on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart at number 39 becoming his highest charting project at the time.<ref name="R&BRap"/>

Wright announced plans to drop ''The Second Agreement EP'', and then his second studio album after the release of the mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/who-is-dizzy-wright/the-future |title=Future — Who Is Dizzy Wright? X. L.|last=Staff|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> However, on April 8, Wright revealed the cover artwork and release date of April 15, 2014 for the EP.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=DizzyWright|author=Dizzy Wright|number=453427410784894976|date=8 April 2014|title=#StateOfMind Dropping next Tuesday 4/15 retweet this if you ready}}</ref> ''State of Mind'' debuted at number 54 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/dizzy-wright/chart-history/TLP|title=Dizzy Wright - Chart History: Billboard 200|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref>

In July 2014, Dizzy Wright revealed that his second studio album would be released in early 2015, with a guest appearance to come from [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.29959/title.dizzy-wright-addresses-joint-project-with-logic-talks-bishop-nehru-brilliant-youth-ep- |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-07-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140728125837/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.29959/title.dizzy-wright-addresses-joint-project-with-logic-talks-bishop-nehru-brilliant-youth-ep- |archive-date=2014-07-28 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In an interview with Mr. Wavvy released on June 24, 2015, Wright confirmed that a project with [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]] is in the works. The rapper went on to confirm that the two have 4-5 songs already recorded for it.<ref>(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KBtb_h-7gu4)</ref>

===2015–2017: ''The Growing Process'' and multiple projects===
{{main|The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)}}
In February 2015, Wright released a collaborative mixtape with [[Mark Battles]] titled, ''Lost In Reality''.<ref name="LIR">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/lost-in-reality/1207404647|title=Lost In Reality by Mark Battles & Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=17 February 2015|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> On May 26, 2015, Wright released his second studio album, ''[[The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)|The Growing Process]]'', which was released through Funk Volume and distributed by [[Warner Bros. Records|Warner Bros]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Robertson|first1=Darryl|title=Dizzy Wright Drops Inspirational Album, 'The Growing Process'|url=http://www.vibe.com/2015/05/dizzy-wright-drops-inspirational-album-the-growing-process/|website=Vibe|access-date=13 July 2016}}</ref> The album went on and peaked at number 47 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart.<ref name="BB200"/>

In January 2016, Funk Volume disbanded.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Hernandez|first1=Victoria|title=Dizzy Wright Defends Damien Ritter In The Midst Of Hopsin's Funk Volume Fallout|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.37901/title.dizzy-wright-defends-damien-ritter-in-the-midst-of-hopsins-funk-volume-fallout|website=HipHopDX|date=14 March 2016|access-date=13 July 2016}}</ref> On February 5, 2016, Wright released his first project post-Funk Volume titled, ''Wisdom and Good Vibes''.<ref name="WAGV">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/wisdom-and-good-vibes/1077898660|title=Wisdom and Good Vibes by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=5 February 2016|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> On July 2, Wright released ''The 702 EP'' and was supported by two singles "East Side" and "What's In My Pot".<ref name="702ep">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-702-ep/1125832488|title=The 702 EP by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=2 July 2016|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> A few days after the release of the EP, Wright released a new single "They Know Why" commenting on controversial political issues.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Hylton|first1=Garfield|title=Dizzy Wright's 'They Know Why' Video Takes On Racism And Police Brutality|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dizzy-wright-they-know-why-video-new-video.37960.html|website=UPROXX|date=11 July 2016|access-date=13 July 2016}}</ref> In November 2016, Wright released a collaborative mixtape with [[Demrick]] titled, ''Blaze With Us''.<ref name="BWU">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/blaze-with-us/1170662759|title=Blaze With Us by Dizzy Wright & Demrick|work=Apple Music|date=2 November 2016|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref>

On August 11, 2017, Wright released his third studio album ''The Golden Age 2'' named after his 2013 mixtape ''The Golden Age''.<ref name="GA2">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-golden-age-2/1257603112|title=The Golden Age 2 by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=11 August 2017|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> On November 24, Wright released his fourth studio album, ''State of Mind 2'', named after his 2014 extended play, ''State of Mind'', with features from ILL Camille, [[Audio Push]], [[Demrick]], [[Larry June]], Reezy, [[Jon Connor]], A.D, and Chelle.<ref name="SOM2">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/state-of-mind-2/1315752674|title=State of Mind 2 by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=24 November 2017|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref>

On January 5, 2018, Still Movin record label owner Damian Ritter put together a collaboration project with Wright, Jarren Benton, [[Audio Push]], Demrick, [[Emilio Rojas]], Reezy, [[DJ Hoppa]], and [[Kato (producer)|Kato]] titled, ''One Week Notice''.<ref name="OWN">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/one-week-notice/1330225701|title=One Week Notice by One Week Notice|work=Apple Music|date=January 5, 2018|access-date=January 29, 2020}}</ref> On June 1, Wright released the extended play, ''Don't Tell Me It Can't Be Done''.<ref name="DTMICBD">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/dont-tell-me-it-cant-be-done/1385852180|title=Don't Tell Me It Can't Be Done by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=June 2018|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref> It included two singles: "Hit Em with the Pose" featuring Jazz Lazr, and "Vibe" featuring [[Kid Ink]].<ref name="HEWTP">{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/7njUl2NyHT7n3Ja6WAnN5w|title=Dizzy Wright - HIT EM WITH THE POSE (feat. On March 15, 2019, Wright released his fifth studio album titled, ''Nobody Cares, Work Harder'', which was distributed by [[Empire Distribution|Empire]].<ref name="NCWH">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/nobody-cares-work-harder/1452727666|title=Nobody Cares, Work Harder by Dizzy Wright|work=Apple Music|date=March 15, 2019|access-date=January 29, 2020}}</ref> It included two singles: "Picture Perfect" featuring [[Eric Bellinger]], and "Champagne Service".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/24cj066P7rrMV6sKETqWYi|title=Dizzy Wright - Picture Perfect (feat.
