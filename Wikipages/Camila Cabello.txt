'''Karla Camila Cabello Estrabao''' ({{IPAc-en|k|ə|ˈ|m|iː|l|ə|_|k|ə|ˈ|b|eɪ|oʊ}}; {{IPA-es|kaˈmila kaˈβeʝo|lang}}; born March 3, 1997)<ref>{{cite web |last=Braidwood |first=Ella |title=21 geeky facts about Camila Cabello |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/camila-cabello-facts-2252388 |website=[[NME]] |access-date=February 6, 2019 |date=March 6, 2018}}</ref> is a Cuban-born American <!-- Please discuss any changes to her nationality -->singer and songwriter. She rose to prominence as a member of the girl group [[Fifth Harmony]], formed on [[The X Factor (American TV series)|''The X Factor USA'']] in 2012, signing a joint record deal with [[Syco Music]] and [[Epic Records]]. While in Fifth Harmony, Cabello began to establish herself as a solo artist with the release of the collaborations "[[I Know What You Did Last Summer (song)|I Know What You Did Last Summer]]" with [[Shawn Mendes]] and "[[Bad Things (Machine Gun Kelly and Camila Cabello song)|Bad Things]]" with [[Machine Gun Kelly (rapper)|Machine Gun Kelly]], the latter reaching number four on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. After leaving the group in late 2016, Cabello released several other collaborations, including "[[Hey Ma (Pitbull and J Balvin song)|Hey Ma]]" with [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]] and [[J Balvin]] for [[The Fate of the Furious: The Album|''The Fate of the Furious'' soundtrack]] (2017) and her debut solo single "[[Crying in the Club]]".

Cabello's debut studio album, ''[[Camila (album)|Camila]]'' (2018), reached number one on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. The [[Latin music]]-influenced pop album was well-received critically and earned a Platinum certification from the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). Its lead single "[[Havana (Camila Cabello song)|Havana]]" topped charts in several countries, including the US and UK, and follow-up single "[[Never Be the Same (Camila Cabello song)|Never Be the Same]]" reached the top ten in multiple countries. Cabello's 2019 duet with Mendes, "[[Señorita (Shawn Mendes and Camila Cabello song)|Señorita]]", became her second single to top the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. Her second studio album, ''[[Romance (Camila Cabello album)|Romance]]'' (2019), peaked at number three on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart and number one in Canada. She made her acting debut in 2021, starring as the title character from the film ''[[Cinderella (2021 American film)|Cinderella]]''.

Cabello has amassed billions of streams on music platforms, and "Havana" became the [[List of best-selling singles|best-selling digital single]] of 2018, according to the [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] (IFPI). Cabello's many awards include two [[Latin Grammy Awards]], five [[American Music Awards]], and one [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Award]].

==Early life and education ==
Cabello was born in [[Havana]], Cuba,<ref name=popsugar /><ref name="All Music Bio">{{cite web |title=Biography & History |publisher=AllMusic |first=Neil Z. |last=Yeung |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/camila-cabello-mn0003392996/biography | access-date=May 1, 2017}}</ref> to Sinuhe Estrabao and Alejandro Cabello. She grew up in the town of [[Cojímar]] in [[Habana del Este|East Havana]]. Her father was born in [[Mexico City]] and is a [[Mexican immigration to Cuba|Mexican who moved to Cuba]]. She has a younger sister named Sofia.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.capitalfm.com/artists/camila-cabello/empowering-message-instagram/|title=Camila Cabello's Empowering Message About Her Sister Is So Important|last=Marti|first=Diana|date=6 September 2018|work=Capital FM|access-date=2018-09-06|language=en-UK}}</ref> For most of her early life, Cabello and her family moved back and forth between Havana and Mexico City. When Cabello was six years old, she relocated to [[Miami]], [[Florida]], in the United States, with her mother; her father was unable to obtain a visa at the time and joined the family approximately 18 months later.<ref name=popsugar>{{cite web|url=http://www.popsugar.com/latina/Camila-Cabello-Her-Cuban-Background-42239921 |title=Camila Cabello: 'Our Dreams Were Bigger Than Our Fears' |publisher=PopSugar.com |date=October 5, 2016 |first=Camila |last=Cabello |archive-date=November 30, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161130123537/http://www.popsugar.com/latina/Camila-Cabello-Her-Cuban-Background-42239921 |url-status=live |quote=...my mom and I immigrated to America. I was almost 7 at the time, born in Havana, Cuba. My papá is puro Mexicano... }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://ew.com/article/2016/09/16/camila-cabello-immigration-story/amp/|title=Camila Cabello shares immigration story|date=September 16, 2016|access-date=June 26, 2019|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://time.com/collection/american-voices-2017/4927699/american-voices-camila-cabello/|title=Cabello Going Solo|website=Time.com|date=2017|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/pop/7751677/camila-cabello-writing-music-immigrant-cuba-to-america|title=Camila Cabello Is Writing Music About Her Family's Journey From Cuba: 'I Want to Make a Love Song for Immigrants'|website=Billboard.com|date=April 5, 2017|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref> Cabello acquired American citizenship in 2008.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://time.com/collection/american-voices-2017/4927699/american-voices-camila-cabello/ |title=Cabello Going Solo |last=Bacle |first=Ariana |date=2017 |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |access-date=May 8, 2018}}</ref> She attended [[Miami Palmetto High School]] but left during the 2012–2013 school year while she was in 9th grade to pursue her singing career. She later earned her [[high school diploma]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thepalmettopanther.com/qa-with-palmetto-student-camila-cabello/|title=Q&A with Palmetto student Camila Cabello|last=Arguelles|first=Victoria|work=The Panther|access-date=2017-10-17|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Career==
===2012–2016: ''The X Factor'' and Fifth Harmony===
[[File:Camila Cabello at the 93.3 FLZ Jingle Ball Tampa Florida 2013 (3).jpg|thumb|upright|Cabello performing at the ''[[WFLZ-FM|93.3 FLZ]] Jingle Ball'' in [[Tampa]], [[Florida]] with [[Fifth Harmony]] in 2013]]
Camila Cabello auditioned for the TV talent competition show ''[[The X Factor (American TV series)|The X Factor]]'' in [[Greensboro, North Carolina]] with [[Aretha Franklin]]'s "[[Respect (song)|Respect]]";<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.nrj.fr/artistes/camila-cabello/actus/camila-cabello-de-candidate-a-the-x-factor-a-superstar-355912 |title=Camila Cabello: de candidate à "The X Factor" à superstar |first=De La Forterie |last=Sonia |date=March 15, 2018 |work=[[NRJ]] |access-date=March 18, 2018 |language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/music/fifth-harmony-member-camila-cabello-quits-group-1.12776718 |title=Fifth Harmony member Camila Cabello quits group |last=Lovece |first=Frank |date=December 19, 2016 |work=Newsday |access-date=March 18, 2018}}</ref> however, her audition was not aired because the series did not get the rights for the song. After elimination during the "bootcamp" portion of the process in Miami, Florida, Cabello was called back to the stage along with other contestants [[Ally Brooke]], [[Normani]], [[Lauren Jauregui]], and [[Dinah Jane]] to form the girl group that would later become known as [[Fifth Harmony]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fifthharmonyofficial.com/bio/|title=Bio: Fifth Harmony |publisher=Fifth Harmony Official|access-date=January 14, 2015| archive-date= January 3, 2016 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160103050655/http://fifthharmonyofficial.com/bio}}</ref> After finishing in third place on the show, they signed a joint deal with [[Syco Music]], owned by [[Simon Cowell]], and [[Epic Records]], [[L.A. Reid]]'s record label.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Phull|first1=Hardeep|title=How losing 'X-Factor' made Fifth Harmony the ultimate winners |url=https://nypost.com/2015/08/22/how-losing-x-factor-made-fifth-harmony-the-ultimate-winners/|website=New York Post|access-date=January 3, 2017|date=August 22, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Gomez|first1=Patrick|title=Simon Cowell Signs Fifth Harmony|url=http://people.com/tv/simon-cowell-signs-fifth-harmony/|website=People|access-date=January 3, 2017|date=January 17, 2013}}</ref>

The group released the EP ''[[Better Together (EP)|Better Together]]'' (2013) along with the studio albums ''[[Reflection (Fifth Harmony album)|Reflection]]'' (2015) and ''[[7/27]]'' (2016). The latter two generated the singles "[[Worth It (Fifth Harmony song)|Worth It]]" and "[[Work from Home (song)|Work from Home]]", respectively, which reached the top 10 in several international charts.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5665538/fifth-harmony/biography |title=Fifth Harmony Biography |date=December 19, 2016 |work=Billboard |access-date=December 20, 2016 |archive-date=September 22, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170922003456/http://www.billboard.com/artist/5665538/fifth-harmony/biography |url-status=dead }}</ref> From 2013 through the end of 2016, Cabello performed in various [[Fifth Harmony#Tours|tours with Fifth Harmony]].

On July 23, 2021, Cabello released "[[Don't Go Yet]]" as the lead single from her third studio album ''Familia'', announced alongside the release of the single.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/camila-cabello-dont-go-yet-song-video-1201156/|title=Camila Cabello Previews 'Familia' LP With Vibrant New Video 'Don't Go Yet'|last=Legaspi|first=Althea|date=July 23, 2021|access-date=July 24, 2021|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|language=en}}</ref> On October 15, 2021, Cabello premiered "La Buena Vida", from ''Familia'', during her [[NPR Music|NPR]] Tiny Desk Concert.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Garcia|first=Thania|title=Camila Cabello Debuts New Song 'La Buena Vida' on 'Tiny Desk Concert'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9645985/camila-cabello-tiny-desk-concert-la-buena-vida/|date=October 15, 2021|access-date=October 17, 2021|website=Billboard}}</ref> On October 29, 2021, Cabello released "[[Oh Na Na]]" with [[Myke Towers]] and [[Tainy]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Legaspi|first=Althea|date=2021-10-29|title=Camila Cabello Links Up With Myke Towers and Tainy for 'Oh Na Na'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/camila-cabello-myke-towers-tainy-oh-na-na-1249936/|access-date=2021-11-03|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> It is currently unclear whether the song is intended for Cabello's album ''Familia'' or Towers' and Tainy's future projects.

In the later half of 2021 Cabello stared in an adaptation of [[Cinderella (2021 American film)|Cinderella]], which was released in select theatres and digitally on [[Amazon Prime Video]] on September 3rd, 2021<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-03|title=How to Watch ‘Cinderella’: Where to Stream the 2021 Update Starring Camila Cabello|url=https://collider.com/how-to-watch-cinderella-2021-online-streaming-where/|access-date=2021-11-21|website=Collider|language=en-US}}</ref> The film received mixed reviews from critics, though Cabello's performance received favourable reviews. ==Philanthropy==
In February 2016, Cabello announced she had partnered with [[Save the Children]] to design a limited-edition "Love Only" T-shirt to help raise awareness of issues involving girls' equal access to education, health care and opportunities to succeed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.savethechildren.org/site/apps/nlnet/content2.aspx?c=8rKLIXMGIpI4E&b=9357115&ct=14926155&notoc=1|title=Camila Cabello Designs Limited-Edition Love Only T-shirt Benefitting Save the Children|access-date=December 28, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170804213915/http://www.savethechildren.org/site/apps/nlnet/content2.aspx?c=8rKLIXMGIpI4E&b=9357115&ct=14926155&notoc=1|archive-date=August 4, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> In June 2016, Cabello, producer [[Benny Blanco]], and members of the nonprofit arts organization OMG Everywhere helped to create the charity single "Power in Me".<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7416727/fifth-harmony-camila-cabello-benny-blanco-power-in-me|title=Watch Fifth Harmony's Camila Cabello, Benny Blanco & Six Kids Create Music With Random Instruments|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=June 28, 2016|access-date=November 23, 2019}}</ref> Cabello has also partnered with the [[Children's Health Fund]], a non-profit dedicated to providing health care to low-income families with children.<ref>{{cite web|title=Take Health into Your Hands This Holiday Season|url=https://www.childrenshealthfund.org/camila-cabello-holiday-message/|publisher=Children's Health Fund}}</ref>

On April 3, 2017, Cabello performed at [[Zedd]]'s WELCOME! Fundraising Concert which raised money for ACLU.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.axs.com/welcome-aclu-benefit-concert-at-staples-center-feat-zedd-incubus-halse-114050|title='Welcome!' ACLU benefit concert at STAPLES Center feat. ZEDD, Incubus, Halsey, Imagine Dragons + more at STAPLES Center on sale now|website=[[AXS TV]]|date=February 2, 2017|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref> Cabello sang to patients at UCLA Mattel Children's Hospital on May 8, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/radiodisney/status/861729459275157504|date=May 8, 2017|title=@Camila_Cabello spent the day at UCLA Mattel Children's Hospital singing for patients! @UCLAMCH @MusiciansOnCall|publisher=[[Radio Disney]]|via=Twitter}}</ref> In late 2017, she joined [[Lin-Manuel Miranda]] and multiple other Latin artists on the song "[[Almost Like Praying]]" for Puerto Rico hurricane relief.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/veronicavillafane/2017/10/06/lin-manuel-miranda-releases-star-studded-almost-like-praying-song-for-puerto-rico-hurricane-relief/|title=Lin-Manuel Miranda Releases Star-Studded 'Almost Like Praying' Song For Puerto Rico Hurricane Relief|last=Villafañe|first=Veronica|work=[[Forbes]]|access-date=October 10, 2017}}</ref> Cabello also announced she was donating all proceeds of "Havana" to the ACLU for DREAMers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://z100.iheart.com/alternate/amp/2017-10-04-camila-cabello-just-made-a-huge-donation-announcement/|title=Camila Cabello Just Made A HUGE Donation Announcement|date=October 4, 2017|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref>

Cabello donated portions of proceeds from VIP sale packages to the Children's Health Fund while on the 2018 Never Be the Same tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://new1027.radio.com/blogs/camila-cabello-announces-never-be-same-tour-dates|title=Camila Cabello Announces Never Be The Same Tour Dates|last=Collins|first=Robyn|date=February 14, 2018|access-date=February 14, 2018}}</ref> On July 13, 2018, she performed a concert in [[San Juan, Puerto Rico|San Juan]] and donated a portion of the concert's proceeds to Hurricane Maria Relief Fund.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.savethechildren.org/us/about-us/media-and-news/2018-press-releases/camila-cabello-san-juan-puerto-rico-concert|title=Camila Cabello for the first time in San Juan at the Coliseo de Puerto Rico; Concert to Benefit Save the Children|website=[[Save the Children]]|date=July 13, 2018|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref> In November 2018, Cabello became an ambassador for Save the Children.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.savethechildren.org/us/about-us/media-and-news/2018-press-releases/camila-cabello-new-ambassador|title=Chart-Topping Singer-Songwriter Camila Cabello Named New Ambassador for Save the Children|website=[[Save the Children]]|date=November 27, 2018|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref>

In March 2020, Cabello participated in [[iHeart Media]]'s [[iHeart Living Room Concert for America|Living Room Concert for America]], a benefit to raise awareness and funds for the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="Variety"/> In March and April 2020, Cabello participated in [[Global Citizen Festival]]'s Together at Home virtual concert to raise awareness and funds for the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.globalcitizen.org/en/content/shawn-mendes-camila-cabello-together-at-home/|title= Watch! ==Personal life==
Cabello was in a relationship with dating coach and writer [[Matthew Hussey]], whom she met on the set of ''[[Today (American TV program)|The Today Show]]''. They dated from February 2018 to June 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/music/camila-cabello-splits-from-relationship-coach-matthew-hussey-after-more-than-1-year-together/|title=Camila Cabello Splits from Relationship Coach Matthew Hussey After More Than 1 Year Together|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=June 25, 2019|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref> She began dating Canadian singer [[Shawn Mendes]] in July 2019. The relationship caused controversy, as both were accused of attempting to [[Publicity stunt|form a relationship for publicity]],<ref name="Capital 2019">{{cite web |title=Inside Shawn Mendes and Camila Cabello's relationship |website=Capital FM |date=2019-09-12 |url=https://www.capitalfm.com/artists/shawn-mendes/camila-cabello-dating-relationship/ |access-date=2019-09-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/sep/13/camila-cabello-shawn-mendes-romance-real-j-lo-drake |title=Bad fauxmance: when celebrity couples become PR stunts |last=Cooper |first=Leonie |date=September 13, 2019 |website=The Guardian |language=en-GB |access-date=September 18, 2019}}</ref> while Mendes denied it stating it is "definitely not a publicity stunt". The relationship was confirmed after the release of their song "[[Señorita (Shawn Mendes and Camila Cabello song)|Señorita]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Camila Cabello & Shawn Mendes Split After 2 Years of Relationship
 |url=https://dailyresearchplot.com/2021/11/25/camila-cabello-shawn-mendes-split-after-2-years-of-relationship/ |access-date=26 November 2021|website=Daily Research Plot|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="Capital 2019"/> In November 2021, Cabello and Mendes announced their breakup via Instagram.<ref>{{cite web|last=Atkinson|first=Katie|title=Camila Cabello & Shawn Mendes Announce Their Breakup|url=https://www.billboard.com/culture/lifestyle/camila-cabello-shawn-mendes-breakup-1234999153/|date=November 17, 2021|access-date=November 17, 2021|website=Billboard}}</ref>

Cabello has opened up about having [[Generalized anxiety disorder|anxiety]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/cover-story/7356880/fifth-harmony-billboard-cover-story-fame-challenges-new-album-the-x-factor |title=Billboard Cover: Fifth Harmony on Surviving Pop-Star Fame and 'Finally Having a Damn Voice|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=May 5, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7495993/fifth-harmony-camila-cabello-leaves-concert-early-anxiety |title=Fifth Harmony's Camila Cabello Leaves Concert Early Due to 'Anxiety'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=September 4, 2016}}</ref> and [[obsessive–compulsive disorder]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.huffpost.com/us/entry/us_58c809e8e4b0598c6699d406/amp |title=Camila Cabello Opens Up About Coping With OCD|work=Huffpost|date=March 14, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.insider.com/what-is-ocd-obsessive-compulsive-disorder-camila-cabello-2018-5?amp|title= Singer Camila Cabello opened up about living with OCD — here's what it's like to live with the disorder|work=Insider|date=May 1, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/amp28872167/camila-cabello-interview-october-2019-elle-cover/ |title=Camila Cabello Loves Like Nobody's Watching|work=Elle|date=September 5, 2019}}</ref> Cabello currently resides in [[Hollywood Hills]], [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="McClain">{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/gallery/camila-cabello-house/|title=Camila Cabello Spends Millions on Sunset Strip House|last=McClain|first=James|date=2019-05-13|work=Variety|language=en|access-date=2019-10-16}}</ref>

In December 2019, [[Tumblr]] posts that Cabello had [[Reblogging|reblogged]] between 2012 and 2013 surfaced on [[Twitter]], containing racial slurs and derogatory language, including uses of the slur "[[nigger]]" and mocking [[Rihanna#Domestic violence case|Chris Brown's assault on Rihanna in 2009]]. Her Tumblr account was deleted soon afterward and Cabello issued an apology, stating that she was "uneducated and ignorant" when younger and that she was deeply embarrassed to have ever used "horrible and hurtful" language. She added that "those mistakes don't represent" her and that she "only [stands] and [has] ever stood for love and inclusivity".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-50849419|title=Camila Cabello 'deeply ashamed' of racist language|access-date=December 19, 2019|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=December 19, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/camila-cabello-and-the-art-of-the-racism-non-apology-from-gina-rodriguez-to-shane-gillis|title=Camila Cabello and the Art of the Racism Non-Apology|work=[[The Daily Beast]]|date=December 20, 2019|access-date=December 21, 2019}}</ref> In March 2021, Cabello said she had taken part in weekly racial healing sessions administered by the racial equity group National Compadres Network, in which "you get corrected, you have homework, and you learn … Now I know better so I can do better".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/music/camila-cabello-on-providing-mental-health-support-for-activists-heal-yourself-before-you-heal-community/|title=Camila Cabello on Providing Mental Health Support for Activists: 'Heal Yourself Before You Heal the Community'|work=People|date=March 3, 2021|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref>
