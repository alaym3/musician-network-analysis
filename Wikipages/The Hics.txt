'''The Hics''' are an English [[Electronica|Electronic]] band formed in [[London]] in 2012 by Sam Paul Evans and Jacob Welsh. Originally a two piece electronic indie band, the band was soon joined by Matt Knox on bass, Roxane  Barker on vocals, Geordan Reid-Campbell on guitar and David Turay on saxophone. They released their first [[Extended play|EP]], ''Tangle'', in 2013.<ref name="hicsvincent13">{{cite web |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/music-news/10208904/The-Hics-New-Faces.html |title=The Hics - New Faces |publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|last1=Vincent |first1=Alice |date=1 August 2013}}</ref>

==History==
The band members met at Pimlico School, now [[Pimlico Academy]], whilst on the music programme.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/jul/16/the-hics |title=New band of the week |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |last1=Lester |first1=Paul |date=16 July 2013}}</ref> The Hics were originally formed as a two piece electronic indie band between Evans and Welsh, and were later joined by Matt Knox on bass. After feeling something was missing, they called on their old schoolmates Rox Barker, who joined as vocalist, and  Geordan Reid-Campbell, who joined as [[guitarist]]. The band met their sixth member, [[Saxophonist]] David Turay, through the Jazz scene. The Hics got their name from [[Hickory]], the wood from which some drumsticks are made.<ref name="hicsvincent13" />

Their first single, "Lines", was released on 30 September 2012 through their [[SoundCloud]] page.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/320383691392705 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=30 September 2012}}</ref> The song was soon picked up by [[BBC Radio 6 Music]] DJ [[Gilles Peterson]], who played the song on his radio show on 6 October 2012,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b01n6s61 |title=6 Music Live At Maida Vale | publisher  = [[BBC]] |date=6 October 2012}}</ref> and later included it on his compilation ''Brownswood Bubblers Nine'', released on 10 December 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/gilles-peterson-presents-brownswood/id579837807 |title=Gilles Peterson Presents Brownswood Bubblers Nine |website=[[iTunes]]}}</ref>

In January 2013, the band were runners-up for the Best Breakthrough Act category at Gilles Peterson's [[Worldwide Winners|Worldwide Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/591991484160189 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=20 January 2013}}</ref> Their second single, "Cold Air", premiered on 16 February 2013 on Gilles Peterson's BBC Radio 6 Music show.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/586068094754292 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=16 February 2013}}</ref> The song was released 3 days later on 19 February 2013 to The Hics' SoundCloud page.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/photos/a.612522622107075.109136602.397093723649967/612522648773739/?type=1 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=19 February 2013}}</ref> In March 2013, they were mentioned in [[NME]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/photos/a.612522622107075.109136602.397093723649967/620814124611258/?type=1 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=6 March 2013}}</ref> They released the track "Tangle" on 8 July 2013,<ref name="hicsfbjul8">{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/682951075064229 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=8 July 2013}}</ref> as a teaser for their upcoming EP of the same name. The band played their first headline show<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/684002788292391 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=10 July 2013}}</ref> at the 750 capacity [[Electrowerkz]] in London on 1 August 2013, to a sold-out crowd.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/photos/a.612522622107075.109136602.397093723649967/696613463697990/?type=1 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=2 August 2013}}</ref> Their first full-length release, the EP ''Tangle'', was released on 19 August 2013.<ref name="hicsfbjul8" /> The music video for the song "Tangle", their first music video, premiered on 3 September 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/715664948459508 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=3 September 2013}}</ref> The song "Cold Air" was featured on the in-game radio station, Worldwide FM, of the 2013 video game [[Grand Theft Auto V]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://stoneyroads.com/2013/08/grand-theft-auto-5-ft-soulwax|title=Grand Theft Auto 5 Soundtrack With a Touch of Soul(wax)|work=Stoney Roads|author=Limmer, Jack|date=26 August 2013}}</ref>

In May 2014 the band released a new song on their SoundCloud page entitled "All We'll Know".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/880691395290195 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=13 May 2014}}</ref> The music video for the song premiered on 9 June 2014<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/899520713407263 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=9 June 2014}}</ref> and the song was officially released as a single on 16 June 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/all-well-know-single/id888385896 |title=The Hics - All We'll Know - Single |website=iTunes}}</ref> The Hics collaborated with Richard Spaven on a song entitled "Whole Other*",<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/919966834695984 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=9 July 2014}}</ref> which was released on his album of the same name on 7 July 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/whole-other*/id881117574 |title=Richard Spaven - Whole Other* |website=iTunes}}</ref> On 19 September 2014<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/photos/a.612522622107075.109136602.397093723649967/966756623350338/?type=1 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=19 September 2014}}</ref> The Hics were filmed for an appearance on the [[Channel 4]] ''[[Four To The Floor (TV series)|Four To The Floor]]'' series, performing their latest single "All We'll Know" which is featured in one of Channel Four's Shorts series. The episode was broadcast on 1 October.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/photos/a.612522622107075.109136602.397093723649967/973667445992589/?type=1 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=1 October 2014}}</ref> In November 2014 The Hics' Saxophone player David Turay died.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/posts/1010790125613654 |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=22 November 2014}}</ref> In mid-2015 The Hics were signed to [[Odd Child Recordings|OddChild Music]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://oddchildmusic.com/artists/ |title=Odd Child Recordings artists}}</ref> The result of the Little Simz collaboration, the song "Gratitude", premiered on BBC Radio 1 on 9 September 2015. The Hics' first new material in over a year, it was released on Little Simz' debut album on 18 September 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/wearethehics/photos/a.725156837510319.1073741826.397093723649967/1210049852354346/ |title=The Hics on Facebook |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=9 September 2015}}</ref> In late 2015, Bass player Matt Knox departed the band.

The Hics are featured on 2 songs on [[Too High to Riot]], the second studio album by American [[Dreamville]] rapper [[Bas (rapper)|Bas]]. The album was released on 4 March 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/too-high-to-riot/id1080062359 |title=Bas - Too High to Riot |website=iTunes}}</ref> The Hics performed their song "Matches" with rapper Bas live on [[SiriusXM]] on 4 March 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BCg5pJIJljx/ |title=The Hics on Instagram |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=4 March 2016}}</ref> They again performed the song with Bas on 18 March 2016, also on SiriusXM. On 19 March 2016 The Hics performed a 15-minute set at [[Dreamville Records|Dreamville]] [[SXSW]] in [[Austin, Texas]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BDJYx_4JlmI/ |title=The Hics on Instagram |last1=Hics |first1=The |date=19 March 2016}}</ref> On 15 April 2016 it was announced that The Hics would be embarking on a tour of the [[United States]] with rapper Bas.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://fiends.nyc/live/ |title=BAS TOUR}}</ref> The 26-show Too High To Riot tour began in [[Miami, Florida]] on 3 June and ended on 13 July 2016 in [[New York City]]. The music video for the song "Matches", one of their collaborations with Bas, was released on 18 April 2016. The video was filmed in [[Los Angeles]] in late February 2016.

January 2021 saw The Hics collaborating with Bas once again, releasing the single "Smoke from Fire", which served as a theme song for Bas' [[Spotify]] podcast ''The Messenger''. They also appeared on [[BBE Music|BBE]]'s [[David Bowie]] tribute album Modern Love, in which they covered "[[The Man Who Sold the World (song)|The Man Who Sold the World]]". On October 21, 2021, The Hics released their first single in seven years entitled "Caught in a Lie"; which serves as the lead single for their upcoming EP scheduled for an early 2022 release via Bas' The Fiends imprint.
