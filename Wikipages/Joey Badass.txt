'''Jo-Vaughn Virginie Scott''' (born January 20, 1995),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1549680/joey-badass-talks-new-album-performs-with-pro-era-video |title=Joey Bada$$ Talks New Album, Performs With Pro Era: Video |publisher=Billboard |date=February 26, 2013 |access-date=August 18, 2013}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Joey Badass''' (stylized as '''Joey Bada$$'''), is an American rapper, singer, and actor. A native of [[Brooklyn]], New York, he is a founding member of the hip-hop collective [[Pro Era]], with whom he has released three mixtapes, as well as his numerous solo projects.

Joey Badass released his debut mixtape, ''[[1999 (mixtape)|1999]]'', in June 2012 to critical acclaim and recognition, followed by ''Rejex'' in September, and ''[[Summer Knights]]'' in July 2013. His debut [[studio album]], ''[[B4.Da.$$]]'', was released in January 2015. In 2016, he made his television debut on the [[USA Network]] series ''[[Mr. Robot]]''. His second studio album, ''[[All-Amerikkkan Badass]]'', was released in April 2017.

==Early life==
Jo-Vaughn Virginie Scott was born on January 20, 1995, and was the first of his immediate [[Caribbean]] family to be born in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|last=Freyman |first=Falyn |url=http://blogs.miaminewtimes.com/crossfade/2013/11/joey_badass_interview_miami_2013.php |title=Joey Badass Talks Debut Album B4.Da.$$: "Just Taking My Time, Not Forcing Anything" |publisher=Blogs.miaminewtimes.com |date=November 19, 2013 |access-date=February 18, 2014}}</ref> His 
maternal family originated from the [[Caribbean]] island of St. Lucia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/11/joey-badass-gone-home-video-diary |title=Joey Bada$$ "Gone Home" Video Diary |publisher=Complex |date=November 26, 2013 |access-date=February 18, 2014}}</ref> While his paternal family originates from the island of Jamaica,<ref>"Big Dusty" lyrics by Joey Bada$$</ref> He was born in the [[East Flatbush, Brooklyn|East Flatbush]] section of [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. He was raised in [[Bedford–Stuyvesant|Bed-Stuy]] and attended the [[Edward R. Murrow High School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/02/who-is-pro-era/joey-badass |title=Joey Bada$$ — Who Is Pro Era? |publisher=Complex |access-date=February 18, 2014}}</ref> He originally enrolled at Edward R. Murrow to study [[acting]], but transitioned towards music around the time of 9th grade, with a focus on [[rapping]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Bakare |first=Lanre |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jan/29/joey-badass-they-call-me-marxist-anti-white-gangster-rapper |title=Joey Bada$$: 'They call me a Marxist and anti-white gangster rapper – they don't know anything' |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=January 29, 2015 |access-date=February 25, 2016}}</ref>

He started rapping under the name "JayOhVee", but eventually changed it to Joey Badass. He says that the name change is due to the media's focus on rappers with more cynical names, saying it was "what sounded cool at the time, what fit [his] mood".<ref name="complex" /> He claims to have first started writing poetry and songs at the age of 11.<ref name="complex" /><ref name="Joey Badass">Gillespie, Blake [http://www.imposemagazine.com/features/joey-badass Joey Badass] Impose Magazine, 6/11/12</ref> Along with [[CJ Fly]], he is a founding member of the collective known as Progressive Era, or [[Pro Era]] for short,<ref name="complex">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/05/who-is-joey-badass/ |title=Who Is Joey Bada$$? |date=May 9, 2012 |first=Daniel |last=Isenberg |work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |access-date=September 3, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Intervew mag">{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/joey-badass-1999/ |title=Joey Bada$$ Travels Through Astral planes |date=June 6, 2012 |first=Anthony |last=Badami |work=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]] |access-date=September 3, 2016}}</ref> which was formed by his high school friends [[Capital Steez]] and Powers Pleasant.<ref name="LA Weekly">{{cite web|url=http://www.laweekly.com/music/joey-bada-and-pro-era-turn-tragedy-into-celebration-with-their-annual-steez-day-festival-7093577 |title=Joey Bada$$ and Pro Era Turn Tragedy Into Celebration With Their Annual Steez Day Festival |date=July 5, 2016 |first=Tara |last=Mahadevan |work=[[LA Weekly]] |access-date=August 30, 2016}}</ref><ref name="The Fader">{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/11/26/capital-steez-king-capital |title=Capital STEEZ: King Capital |date=November 26, 2013 |first=Eli |last=Rosenberg |work=[[The Fader]] |access-date=August 30, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160830232255/http://www.thefader.com/2013/11/26/capital-steez-king-capital |archive-date=August 30, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Career==
===2010&ndash;2014: Beginnings and breakthrough===
[[File:Joey Badass, New York's SOB's, June 2012.jpg|thumb|right|Joey Badass performing in 2012]]
In October 2010, Joey Badass uploaded a video of himself [[freestyling]] to [[YouTube]]. The video was re-posted on to American [[hip hop]] site [[World Star Hip Hop]] and caught the attention of [[Jonny Shipes]], the president of [[Cinematic Music Group]], who soon after became his manager.<ref name="complex" /><ref name="Joey Badass"/>
 
In early 2012, Joey Badass and [[Capital Steez]] released a video for "Survival Tactics" via their YouTube channel "PROfckingERA", which samples the [[2000 Fold]] song of the same name by [[Styles of Beyond]]. In February 2012, Pro Era released their debut mixtape entitled ''The Secc$ Tap.e''.<ref>[http://www.thesmokersclub.com/smokersclub/progressive-era-the-seccs-tape-mixtape/ The Seccs Tape] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130615030518/http://www.thesmokersclub.com/smokersclub/progressive-era-the-seccs-tape-mixtape/ |date=June 15, 2013 }} The Smokers Club, 2/14/12</ref> On March 21, 2012, [[MTV News]] featured Joey Badass' verse from the video during a RapFix Live segment and subsequently appeared as a featured guest on ''[[Sucker Free]]'' the following month. The song was succeeded with the video premiere of "Hardknock" featuring CJ Fly on [[MTV2]].<ref>Dukes, Rahman [http://rapfix.mtv.com/2012/05/27/joey-badass-hard-knock-video/ Hard Knock Video] MTV, 5/27/12</ref><ref>Markman, Rob [http://rapfix.mtv.com/2012/03/26/odd-future-joey-badass-rapfix-live/ RapFix Live] MTV, 3/26/12</ref><ref>Isenberg, Daniel [http://www.complex.com/music/2012/05/watch-joey-badass-perform-his-sucker-free-verse Joey Badass Sucker Free] Complex Mag, 5/2/12</ref>

On June 12, 2012, Joey Badass released his debut solo mixtape ''[[1999 (mixtape)|1999]]''. This album was only released to music streaming platforms such as Spotify and Apple Music in 2018. A video for the song "Waves" was released in June 2012. The mixtape immediately increased his popularity in the underground scene.<ref>Caramanica, Jon [https://www.nytimes.com/2012/07/29/arts/music/new-releases-from-friends-wade-bowen-and-joey-badass.html New Releases] New York Times, 7/29/12</ref><ref>Zeichner, Naomi [http://www.thefader.com/2012/07/02/video-joey-bada-waves/ Waves Video] The Fader, 6/2/12</ref> The mixtape was named the 38th best album of 2012 by ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' magazine<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/2012-year-in-review-the-50-best-albums-of-2012/joey-badass-1999 |title=38. Joey Bada$$, 1999 — The 50 Best Albums of 2012 |publisher=Complex |date=December 18, 2012 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref> and the best mixtape of 2012 by [[HipHopDX]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/editorials/id.2018/title.hiphopdxs-top-10-mixtapes-of-2012 |title=HipHopDX's Top 10 Mixtapes Of 2012 &#124; Discussing Lil' Wayne, Drake & Many More Hip Hop Artists |publisher=HipHop DX |date=December 31, 2012 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref> On September 6 of the same year, he released another mixtape titled ''Rejex'', which featured all the tracks that did not make it on the 1999 mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theproera.com/music/ |title=Music |publisher=The Pro Era |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref>

[[File:Joey Bada$$ 11.jpg|thumb|160px|left|Joey Badass performing in 2013]]
Subsequently, American rapper [[Mac Miller]] reached out to Joey Badass through Twitter to collaborate. He appeared on the song "America" from the mixtape ''[[Macadelic]]'' and opened for Miller at the [[Roseland Ballroom]] in April 2012.<ref name="complex" /> Along with headliner [[Juicy J]], Joey Badass and Pro Era performed on The Smoker's Club's One Hazy Summer Tour, presented by [[Eckō Unltd.]] and livemixtapes.com.  The 30-date tour began in July 2012.<ref>Horowitz, J, Steven [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.19986/title.juicy-j-to-headline-the-smokers-clubs-one-hazy-summer-tour-features-smoke-dza One Hazy Summer Tour] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150104205659/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.19986/title.juicy-j-to-headline-the-smokers-clubs-one-hazy-summer-tour-features-smoke-dza |date=January 4, 2015 }} Hiphopdx.com, 7/13/12</ref> He was featured on the [[posse cut]] track "1 Train" off [[ASAP Rocky]]'s album ''[[Long.Live.ASAP]]'' (2013) which also features [[Action Bronson]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Yelawolf]], [[Big K.R.I.T.]], and [[Danny Brown (rapper)|Danny Brown]].

In December 2012 it was rumored that Joey Badass would be signing to [[Jay Z|Jay Z's]] [[Roc Nation]] record label,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/joey-bada-heading-over-roc-nation |title=Joey Bada$$ Heading Over to Roc Nation? |publisher=Vibe |date=December 19, 2012 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=illseed (@illseed) |url=http://allhiphop.com/2013/01/07/hip-hop-rumors-did-jay-z-sign-joey-bada-signed-to-roc-nation/ |title=Hip-Hop Rumors: Did Jay-Z Sign Joey Bada$$ To Roc Nation? |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=January 7, 2013 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref> but in January 2013, he dispelled the rumors saying he would rather stay independent than sign to a major label for the time being.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.22574/title.joey-bada-dispels-rumors-of-signing-to-roc-nation|title=HipHopDx|date=January 17, 2013|access-date=December 6, 2017}}</ref> On December 21, 2012, Pro Era released the ''[[P.E.E.P: The aPROcalypse]]'' mixtape.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://theproera.com/music/pro-era-peep-the-aprocalypse/ |title=Pro Era - Peep The Aprocalypse |publisher=The Pro Era |access-date=May 6, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130428010005/http://theproera.com/music/pro-era-peep-the-aprocalypse/ |archive-date=April 28, 2013 }}</ref>

On November 9, 2012, Joey Badass released a track with [[Ab-Soul]] produced by [[Lee Bannon]] called "Enter the Void" along with "Waves". These songs were his first official releases to retail. On January 14, 2013, he released the first single off his second mixtape ''[[Summer Knights]]'', "[[Unorthodox (Joey Badass song)|Unorthodox]]", which was produced by [[DJ Premier]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapfix.mtv.com/2013/01/13/joey-badass-dj-premier-unorthodox/ |title=Joey Bada$$ And DJ Premier Link Up On 'Unorthodox' |publisher=Rapfix.mtv.com |date=January 13, 2013 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref> On February 8, he released a music video featuring [[Big K.R.I.T.]] and [[Smoke DZA]] titled "Underground Airplay". On March 5, 2013, he released the music video for "Unorthodox".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://buzzworthy.mtv.com/2013/03/06/joey-badass-unorthodox-video/ |title=Video Premiere: Joey Bada$$, 'Unorthodox' |publisher=Buzzworthy.mtv.com |date=March 6, 2013 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref> On March 21, 2013, he began the Beast Coast concert tour with Pro Era, [[The Underachievers]] and [[Flatbush Zombies]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/03/21/see-joey-bada-on-tour-with-pro-era-flatbush-zombies-and-the-underachievers|title=See Joey Bada$$ on Tour with Pro Era, Flatbush Zombies and The Underachievers|work=The FADER|access-date=December 7, 2017|language=en}}</ref> On March 26, 2013, it was announced that Joey Badass would be a part of ''[[XXL Magazine]]'s'' 2013 Freshman Class.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/03/xxl-freshman-class-2013-cover-revealed/2/ |title=XXL Freshman Class 2013 Cover Revealed - Page 2 of 2 - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=March 27, 2013 |access-date=May 6, 2013}}</ref>

On October 29, 2013, ''Summer Knights EP'' was released. It featured four songs from the mixtape of the same name, and three new songs including a remix.<ref>{{cite web|author=Yohance Kyles (@HUEYmixwitRILEY) |url=http://allhiphop.com/2013/10/15/joey-bada-to-release-summer-knights-ep/ |title=Joey Bada$$ To Release "Summer Knights" EP |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=October 15, 2013 |access-date=February 18, 2014}}</ref> Upon release, the EP debuted at number 48 on the US ''Billboard'' [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/r-b-hip-hop-albums?page=4 |title=WebCite query result |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=October 5, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6KxW6jBcf?url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/r-b-hip-hop-albums?page=4 |archive-date= November 7, 2013 }}</ref>

==Controversy==
===Legal issue===
In January 2015, Joey Badass was arrested and charged with assault for allegedly breaking a security guard's nose<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2015/01/joey-badass-arrested-assault-australia/ |title=Joey Bada$$ Arrested After Punching Incident In Australia |date=January 5, 2015 |work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]] |access-date=January 5, 2015}}</ref> before performing at the [[Falls Festival]] in [[Byron Bay]], [[New South Wales]], Australia.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-01-05/rapper-joey-bada-assault-charge-falls-festival-byron-bay/5999822|title=Joey Bada$$: US rapper charged with punching Falls Festival security guard|publisher=[[ABC Online]]|date=January 5, 2015|access-date=January 5, 2015}}</ref>

===Feud with Troy Ave===
In June 2015, tensions between Joey Badass and fellow Brooklyn native [[Troy Ave]] began brewing after the former declared himself the "#1 [[Independent music|independent]] hip hop artist/brand in the world" in a series of [[Twitter|tweets]]. Troy Ave responded in another tweet by pointing out that Joey Badass is signed to Cinematic Music Group, whose music is distributed by [[RED Distribution]], a subsidiary of [[Sony Music Entertainment]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2194241/joey-badass-troy-ave-indie/ |title=Joey Bada$$ And Troy Ave Are Trying To Out-Indie Each Other |date=June 23, 2015 |first=Nadeska |last=Alexis |work=[[MTV News]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> but Joey Badass later denied reports of a feud between the two of them.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6605777/troy-ave-joey-badass-twitter-beef-independent-hip-hop |title=Troy Ave & Joey Badass Duke It Out On Twitter Over Who Runs the (Independent) Rap Game |date=June 23, 2015 |first=Natalie |last=Wiener |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref>

On July 7, 2015, in an interview with [[Ebro Darden]] on [[Hot 97]], Joey Badass had words of praise for Troy Ave, stating: "At some point you gotta have some level of respect for Troy because, at the end of the day, we're both independent artists who are standing on major platforms next to a whole bunch of major artists and they know who we are."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/joey-badass-talks-troy-ave-tweets-and-steez-day-festival-on-ebro-in-the-morning-new-video.34488.html |title=Joey Bada$$ Talks Troy Ave Tweets & Steez Day Festival On Ebro In The Morning |date=July 7, 2015 |first=Kevin |last=Goddard |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> However, in February 2016, he reignited the feud in a single entitled "Ready", where he rapped: "60k, first week for the badass / 200k to this day, I know you niggas mad / With that 80-20 split, my nigga do the math / my nigga Kirk just outsold Troy Ave" (in reference to [[Kirk Knight]]'s debut studio album ''[[Late Knight Special]]'' and Troy Ave's second studio album ''[[Major Without a Deal]]'').<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/02/joey-badass-ready/ |title=Joey Badass Throws Shots at Troy Ave on "Ready" |date=February 22, 2016 |first=Justin |last=Ivey |work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/editorials/id.3239/title.joey-bada-troy-ave-beef-5-things-we-learned |title=Joey Bada$$ & Troy Ave Beef: 5 Things We Learned |date=March 6, 2016 |first=Hugh |last=Least |website=[[HipHopDX]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref>

In response to "Ready", Troy Ave released a [[diss track]] entitled "Badass" the following week, in which he not only insulted Joey Badass, but also targeted Capital Steez and his suicide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/02/troy-ave-joey-badass-diss/ |title=Troy Ave Responds to Joey Badass on "Bad Ass" |date=February 29, 2016 |first=Max |last=Weinstein |work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> Following the song's release, Troy Ave defended his actions on [[Shade 45]]'s ''Sway in the Morning'', stating: "He didn't pass away; he killed himself. There's a difference. He took his own life. God gave you life, it ain't your right to take that. That's a fact. I got niggas in jail who got life sentences, they might as well be dead. They could've traded their life for his."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/03/troy-ave-joey-badass-capital-steez-lyrics/ |title=Troy Ave Speaks on Capital Steez Diss: "Don't Play With Me" |date=March 1, 2016 |first=Sidney |last=Madden |work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> He then received heavy criticism from both fans and fellow artists, including [[ASAP Mob|A$AP Ant]], [[ASAP Twelvyy|A$AP Twelvyy]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allhiphop.com/2016/03/02/asap-mob-twelvy-ant-troy-ave-joey-badass-capital-steez/ |title=A$AP Mob Members Blast Troy Ave For Disrespecting Capital STEEZ; Troy Defends Dissing The Late Rapper |date=March 2, 2016 |first=Yohance |last=Kyles |website=[[AllHipHop]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> and [[Styles P]] who wrote, amongst a series of other tweets: "If you never experienced a family [member] committing suicide you have no fukn [sic] idea about that pain. NONE." Troy Ave then replied to Styles P's comments and apologized to his fans on Twitter.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allhiphop.com/2016/03/03/troy-ave-apologizes-for-suicide-comments-responds-to-joey-bada-exposing-his-old-tweets-about-nas/ |title=Troy Ave Apologizes For Suicide Comments & Responds To Joey Bada$$ Exposing His Old Tweets About Nas |date=March 3, 2016 |first=Yohance |last=Kyles |website=[[AllHipHop]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2016/03/03/styles-p-educates-troy-ave-on-twitter-regarding-his-suicide-comments/ |title=Styles P Educates Troy Ave On Twitter Regarding His Suicide Comments |date=March 3, 2016 |first=Rocko |last=Rathon |work=[[The Source]] |access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> In an interview on [[DJ Vlad|VladTV]], another Brooklyn rapper, [[Maino (rapper)|Maino]], stated that mentioning Capital Steez's suicide in the song was "a bit much".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C7LqI78Rh28 | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211107/C7LqI78Rh28| archive-date=2021-11-07 | url-status=live|title=Maino: Troy Ave Dissing Capital Steez's Suicide Took Joey Bada$$ Beef Too Far |date=April 5, 2016 |publisher=[[DJ Vlad|VladTV]] / [[YouTube]] |access-date=September 1, 2016}}{{cbignore}}</ref>
