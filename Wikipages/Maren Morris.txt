'''Maren Larae Morris'''<ref name=huffpost>{{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/michael-bialas/hero-worship-maren-morris_b_10273058.html |title=Hero Worship: Maren Morris Can Begin Telling Her Musical Glory Story |work=[[Huffington Post]] |date=June 3, 2016 |author=Michael Bialas}}</ref> (born April 10, 1990<ref name="Tweet Day">{{cite tweet|title=Show tomorrow at Central Mkt (Ft. Worth), my birthday is FRIDAY (whatcha getting me? lol) show @ WOODY STOCK in Burkburnett Saturday!| number= 1477681121| author= Morris, Maren| user= MarenMorris|access-date=November 18, 2015|date=April 8, 2009}}</ref><ref name="Tweet Age">{{cite tweet| title= I'd love a Jack Daniels endorsement for my 22nd birthday.| number= 189121914684448768| author= Morris, Maren| user= MarenMorris |access-date= November 18, 2015|date=April 8, 2012}}</ref>) is an American [[country (music)|country]] singer. She has released two [[Album|studio albums]]. Her 2015 [[extended play]], ''Maren Morris'', charted on two ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts. Her major label debut album, ''[[Hero (Maren Morris album)|Hero]]'', reached number five on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart and number one on the [[Top Country Albums]] chart, and was certified platinum in the United States. Morris is also a member of [[the Highwomen]], a group also consisting of [[Brandi Carlile]], [[Amanda Shires]] and [[Natalie Hemby]].

Her first album ''Walk On'' was released in 2005. Her debut single, "[[My Church]]", peaked at number one on the Country Digital Songs chart in 2016 and reached the top five on the US [[Hot Country Songs]] chart and won a [[59th Annual Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance|Best Country Solo Performance]]. Her third single, "[[I Could Use a Love Song]]", was her first to reach number one on the US [[Country Airplay]] chart. She provided vocals for "[[The Middle (Zedd, Maren Morris and Grey song)|The Middle]]", a [[Pop music|pop]] collaboration with [[Zedd]] and [[Grey (musical duo)|Grey]], released in January 2018, which reached number five on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and received three nominations at the 61st Grammy Awards.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/maren-morris |title=MAREN MORRIS |website=Grammy Awards |access-date=December 19, 2018}}</ref> Morris' second album, ''[[Girl (Maren Morris album)|Girl]]'', was released on March 8, 2019, through [[Columbia Nashville]], preceded by the lead single and title track. The album once again attained the top spot on the Top Country Albums chart and peaked at number four on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart, while Morris achieved her first top 15 entry on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart with the album's single "[[The Bones (song)|The Bones]]", which peaked at number 12.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/maren-morris-new-album-girl-track-by-track-guide-803940/|title=Maren Morris' 'Girl': Track by Track Guide|last=Crawford|first=Robert|date=March 6, 2019|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=April 1, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Morris was born in [[Arlington, Texas]] to Greg and Kellie Morris.<ref name="EO">{{cite web|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/804979/meet-my-church-singer-maren-morris-from-american-idol-reject-to-5-time-cma-award-nominee|title=Meet ''My Church'' Singer Maren Morris: From American Idol Reject to 5-Time CMA Award Nominee|author=Dill, Jennifer|date=November 1, 2016|publisher=E! News}}</ref> As a child, she spent most of her time at her parents' hair salon.<ref name="EO"/> When Maren was 12, her father bought her a guitar and she fell in love with it.<ref name="EO"/> Her music recording career started in 2005, with her debut studio album, ''Walk On'', released on June 14, 2005, by Mozzi Blozzi Music.<ref name="AM Discography">{{cite web|title=Maren Morris : Discography| url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/maren-morris-mn0000328633/discography| publisher= [[AllMusic]]| access-date= November 18, 2015}}</ref> Her subsequent release, ''All That it Takes'', was released on October 22, 2007, by Smith Entertainment.<ref name="AM Discography"/> Her third release, ''Live Wire'', was released in 2011, by Mozzi Blozzi Music.<ref name="AM Discography"/>  Her major label debut album ''[[Hero (Maren Morris album)|Hero]]'' was released to critical acclaim in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://sonymusicnashville.com/maren-morris-album-hero-available-today/|title=Sony Music Nashville {{!}} Maren Morris' Album HERO Available Today|website=sonymusicnashville.com|access-date=December 6, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220092456/http://sonymusicnashville.com/maren-morris-album-hero-available-today/|archive-date=December 20, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Maren spent several years in Nashville writing songs for other artists, including "Last Turn Home" on [[Tim McGraw]]'s 2014 album ''[[Sundown Heaven Town]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/sundown-heaven-town-mw0002703821|title=Sundown Heaven Town - Tim McGraw - Songs, Reviews, Credits - AllMusic|website=AllMusic}}</ref> and "Second Wind", which [[Kelly Clarkson]] sang on her 2015 album ''[[Piece by Piece (Kelly Clarkson album)|Piece by Piece]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.bigyellowdogmusic.com/news/maren-morris-lands-a-cut-on-kelly-clarkson-s-new-album-piece-by-piece/72/show |title={title} |access-date=December 7, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161013191220/http://bigyellowdogmusic.com/news/maren-morris-lands-a-cut-on-kelly-clarkson-s-new-album-piece-by-piece/72/show |archive-date=October 13, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Career==
===2015: ''Maren Morris'' EP===
Morris initially self-released five songs as an eponymous [[extended play]], ''Maren Morris'', on [[Spotify]] in August 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/marenmorris/status/631870841303756800 |title=Maren Morris |work=Twitter}}</ref> The songs gained 2.5 million streams on Spotify in a month, with three songs appearing on Spotify's US and Global "Viral 50" chart. The success of Morris' EP attracted the interest of major labels, and she was signed to [[Sony Music Nashville|Columbia Nashville]] in September 2015.<ref name="Music Row">{{cite web |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2015/09/sony-music-nashville-signs-singer-songwriter-maren-morris/ |title=Sony Music Nashville Signs Singer-Songwriter Maren Morris |work=Music Row |author=Jessica Nicholson |date= September 10, 2015}}</ref><ref name="leahey" /> The label re-released the five-song EP, on November 6, 2015, with "[[My Church]]" the lead single.<ref name="The Oswegonian">{{cite journal |first=Riley |last=Ackley |title=Weekly EP: country newbie sparkles  |url=http://www.oswegonian.com/2015/11/12/weekly-ep-country-newbie-sparkles/ |journal=[[The Oswegonian]] |access-date=November 18, 2015 |date=November 12, 2015}}</ref> Following airplay on country radio, along with several in-studio appearances on satellite radio, this EP charted on two ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts—number 27 on the [[Top Country Albums|Country Albums]] chart and number one on the [[Top Heatseekers]] chart<ref name="chart search">[https://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f%5b0%5d=ts_chart_artistname%3AMaren%20Morris&f%5b1%5d=ss_bb_type%3Achart_item&type=2&artist=Maren%20Morris Chart search for Maren Morris] at billboard.com/biz</ref>—and sold 2,400 copies in its debut week.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://roughstock.com/news/2015/11/39347-top-10-country-albums-chart-november-16-2015 |title=Top 10 Country Albums Chart: November 16, 2015|first= Matt|last= Bjorke |date=November 16, 2015 |work=Roughstock }}</ref> "My Church" reached number five on the [[Hot Country Songs]] chart and was certified Gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].{{Certification Cite Ref |artist=Maren Morris |title=My Church |region=United States |type=single }}

===2016&ndash;2018: ''Hero'', breakthrough, and collaborations===
With the success of "My Church", Morris released her major label studio album on June 3, 2016, the week she started a tour with [[Keith Urban]].<ref name="leahey">{{cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/maren-morris-plots-hero-album-release-20160314 |title=Maren Morris Plots 'Hero' Album Release |first= Andrew |last=Leahey |date= March 14, 2016 |work=Rolling Stone }}</ref> The album debuted and peaked at number five on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, and number one on the [[Top Country Albums]] chart.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7401269/drake-views-billboard-200-paul-simon-stranger-to-stranger|first=Keith|last=Caulfield|title=Drake's 'Views' Album No. 1 for Sixth Week on Billboard 200, Paul Simon Debuts at No. 3|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=June 2, 2016}}</ref> Her debut single from the project, "My Church", hit number one on the Country Digital Songs chart in 2016 and reached the top five on the US [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.<ref name="CountrySongs">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/maren-morris/chart-history/country-songs|title=Maren Morris Chart History (Hot Country Songs)|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=October 10, 2017}}</ref> ''Hero'' earned Morris four Grammy Award nominations at the [[59th Annual Grammy Awards]]. The album was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Country Album|Best Country Album]], "My Church" won [[Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance|Best Country Solo Performance]] and was nominated for [[Best Country Song]]. Morris was also nominated for the all-genre honor of [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], but lost to [[Chance The Rapper]].<ref name="2017GrammyResults">{{cite web|title=Grammy Awards 2017: See the Full Winners List|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/7686168/grammy-awards-2017-winners-list|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=February 12, 2017}}</ref> She was honored at ''Billboard'' Women in Music 2016 with the Breakthrough Star Award,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/women-in-music/7616902/maren-morris-breakthough-artist-award-women-in-music|title=Maren Morris Honors Country Music While Accepting Breakthrough Star Award at Billboard Women in Music|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=February 9, 2017}}</ref> and received the Breakthrough Artist Award at Music Biz 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://musicbiz.org/news/rising-country-singersongwriter-maren-morris-receive-breakthrough-artist-award-music-biz-2017/|title=Rising Country Singer/Songwriter Maren Morris to Receive Breakthrough Artist Award at Music Biz 2017 - Music Business Association|newspaper=Music Business Association|access-date=February 9, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== 2019&ndash;present: ''Girl'' and Highwomen ===
In January 2019, Morris released the lead single "Girl" from her upcoming [[Girl (Maren Morris album)|second major label studio album of the same name]] and simultaneously announced a worldwide concert, ''Girl: The World Tour'', to support the album which was released on March 8. The tour began on March 9 in [[Chicago]], and concluded on August 23, 2019 in [[Canberra]], Australia.<ref>{{cite web |last=Freeman |first=Jon |title=Maren Morris Sets Dates for Headlining Girl: The World Tour |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/maren-morris-2019-tour-new-song-girl-780360/ |website=Rolling Stone |date=January 17, 2019 |access-date=January 28, 2019}}</ref> In March, it was announced that Morris would be forming a group with [[Brandi Carlile]], [[Amanda Shires]] and [[Natalie Hemby]] called [[The Highwomen]] (in reference to [[The Highwaymen (country supergroup)|The Highwaymen]]), often joined by a rotating line-up of guests, such as [[Sheryl Crow]], [[Chely Wright]], [[Margo Price]] and [[Courtney Marie Andrews]]. A social media post by Shires' husband [[Jason Isbell]] hinted that the group had already begun recording with producer [[Dave Cobb]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/brandi-carline-maren-morris-amanda-shires-highwomen-supergroup-1203156171/|title=Brandi Carlile, Amanda Shires Add Maren Morris to 'Highwomen' Supergroup|first1=Chris|last1=Willman|date=March 6, 2019|website=Variety.com|access-date=March 9, 2019}}</ref> The Highwomen made their live debut, alongside new member [[Natalie Hemby]] on April 1, 2019 at [[Loretta Lynn]]'s 87th birthday concert performing "[[It Wasn't God Who Made Honky Tonk Angels]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/highwomen-brandi-carlile-loretta-lynn-birthday-concert-816499/|title=See the Highwomen's Live Debut at Loretta Lynn Birthday Show|first1=Marissa R.|last1=Moss|date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> "Redesigning Women" was released on July 19, 2019 as the first single from their self-titled debut album, which was released on September 6, 2019. The album had a top-ten debut on [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number one on the Country Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/the-highwomen-redesigning-women-video/|title=The Highwomen Light the Ultimate Bonfire in 'Redesigning Women' Video [Watch]|website=Taste of Country}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8530004/post-malone-hollywoods-bleeding-No1-debut-billboard-200-chart|title=Post Malone Scores Second No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 With 'Hollywood's Bleeding'|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-09-18}}</ref>

In 2020, "[[The Bones (song)|The Bones]]", the second official single from ''Girl'' peaked at No. 12 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, and topped the Country Airplay and Hot Country Songs charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Maren-Morris/chart-history/HSI|title=Maren Morris|website=Billboard}}</ref> On June 12, 2020, Morris released two songs titled "Just For Now" & "Takes Two" as part of the GIRL deluxe edition.{{citation needed|date=March 2021}} Morris is a featured vocalist on [[Taylor Swift]]'s song "[[You All Over Me]]", which was released as the second single from Swift's rerecorded album ''[[Fearless (Taylor's Version)]]'' in March 2021.<ref>{{cite web|last=Haylock|first=Zoe|date=March 24, 2021|title=Taylor Swift Recruits Maren Morris for a New Unreleased Song From Fearless|url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/03/taylor-swift-you-all-over-me-from-the-vault.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210324192708/https://www.vulture.com/2021/03/taylor-swift-you-all-over-me-from-the-vault.html|archive-date=March 24, 2021|access-date=March 24, 2021|website=[[New York (magazine)#Vulture|Vulture]]}}</ref> She also helped [[John Mayer]] record three different tracks for his 2021 album ''[[Sob Rock]]'', including a significant vocal contribution on "[[Last Train Home (John Mayer song)|Last Train Home]]", the lead single.

==Personal life==
In December 2015, Morris began dating fellow country singer [[Ryan Hurd]], whom she had first met in a co-write in 2013. ==Awards and nominations==
Morris was nominated for four Grammy Awards at the [[59th Annual Grammy Awards|2017 Grammy Awards]]. She led as the most-nominated country artist: Best New Artist, Best Country Song for "My Church", Best Country Album for ''Hero'' and Best Country Solo Performance for "My Church", the latter of which she won.<ref name="2017GrammyResults" /> In September 2018, CMT announced that their annual Artists of the Year special would honor Morris and other female performers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/maren-morris-miranda-lambert-set-for-2018-cmt-artists-of-the-year-722471/|title=Maren Morris, Miranda Lambert Set for 2018 CMT Artists of the Year|last=Betts|first=Stephen L.|date=September 11, 2018|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=September 12, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://edition.cnn.com/2018/09/11/entertainment/cmt-artists-of-the-year-all-women/index.html|title=CMT Artists of the Year all women|first=Chloe |last=Melas|work=CNN|access-date=September 12, 2018}}</ref>
