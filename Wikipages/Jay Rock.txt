'''Johnny Reed McKinzie Jr.''' (born March 31, 1985), better known by his stage name '''Jay Rock''', is an American rapper from [[Watts, Los Angeles|Watts, California]]. Rock signed to the [[independent record label]] [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE) in 2005.<ref name="MTV" /> In 2007, alongside TDE, Rock secured a recording contract with [[Warner Bros. Records]] and later [[Asylum Records]], but mergers fell through and Rock ended up leaving the label shortly after. He then signed with [[Tech N9ne]]'s [[Strange Music]], in a [[joint-venture]] record deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopmoneymagazine.com/top-dawg-entertainment-making-its-mark-tde/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203023434/http://hiphopmoneymagazine.com/top-dawg-entertainment-making-its-mark-tde/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=December 3, 2013 |title=Top Dawg Entertainment Making {{sic|I|t's|nolink=y}} Mark……..TDE |publisher=Hiphopmoneymagazine.com |date=October 27, 2012 |access-date=August 25, 2013}}</ref> Apart from his solo career, Rock is also known for being a member of the hip hop supergroup [[Black Hippy]], alongside fellow [[West Coast hip hop|West Coast rappers]] and TDE label-mates [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Schoolboy Q]] and [[Ab-Soul]]. In 2019, Jay Rock received a [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance]] for "[[King's Dead]]".

Since 2006, Rock has released several [[mixtape]]s and first becoming known for his 2008 commercial debut single, "[[All My Life (In the Ghetto)]]" (featuring [[Lil Wayne]] and [[will.i.am]]). In 2011, after signing his joint-venture deal with Strange Music, Rock's often-delayed and long-awaited debut studio album, ''[[Follow Me Home (album)|Follow Me Home]]'', was released in July of that year. The album was supported by the singles "All My Life (In the Ghetto)" and "[[Hood Gone Love It]]" (featuring [[Kendrick Lamar]]). His second studio album ''[[90059]]'' was released in September 2015. It was supported by the singles "[[Money Trees Deuce]]" (featuring [[Lance Skiiiwalker]]), "Gumbo" and the title track. In June 2018, Rock released his third studio album ''[[Redemption (Jay Rock album)|Redemption]]''. All three albums have achieved critical success.

== Life and career ==

=== 1985–2007: Early life and career beginnings ===
Jay Rock was born Johnny Reed McKinzie Jr. on March 31, 1985.<ref name="TDE Artist Page" /> Rock was raised in [[Watts, Los Angeles]], [[California]],<ref name="TDE Artist Page" /> in the [[Nickerson Gardens]] [[Public housing|projects]]. As a youth, he became a member of the [[Bounty Hunter Bloods]] street gang and attended [[Locke High School]].<ref name="billboard">{{cite news |first=David  |last=Jeffries |title=Jay Rock |url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=jay rock|chart=all}}|work=Billboard}}</ref> During this time, he was jailed on two occasions, due to the area's anti-gang injunction.<ref name="allmusic.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/jay-rock-mn0000934172/biography|title=Jay Rock – Biography – AllMusic|author=David Jeffries|work=AllMusic|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref> In 2005, Anthony "Top Dawg" Tiffith, [[chief executive officer]] (CEO) of independent record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE), found Rock and signed him to his label, after hearing a [[verse (popular music)|verse]] of his. After signing to TDE, Rock released several [[mixtape]]s throughout his neighborhood and via the internet. With the help of TDE, Rock was able to secure a recording contract with [[Asylum Records]] and later a joint venture deal with [[Warner Bros. Records]], in 2007.<ref name="allmusic.com" /><ref name="streetgangs">{{cite news |first=Alex  |last=Alonso |title= Rapper Jay Rock Talks About Livin The Nickerson Gardens, Watts |url=http://www.streetgangs.com/features/021110_jay_rock |work=Streetgangs.com}}</ref> At TDE, Jay Rock met fellow labelmate [[Kendrick Lamar]], and the two would go on multiple tours together, honing their craft. The two often feature on each other's records, and along with [[Ab-Soul]] and [[ScHoolboy Q]] would form the supergroup Black Hippy.

=== 2013–2017: ''90059'' ===
{{Main|90059}}
In November 2013, Rock told [[Power 105]] that his second studio album would be released in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/11/kendrick-lamar-powerhouse-interview|title=Kendrick Lamar: "I Have Nothing Against Drake"|author=Dharmic X|work=Complex|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref> With the release of the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards]] nominations, it was revealed Rock was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] as a featured artist for participating on Kendrick Lamar's ''good kid, m.A.A.d city''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/jay-z-rapper-macklemore-new-zealand-singer-lorde-top-nods-article-1.1540526 |title=Jay Z, Macklemore, Lorde among 2014 Grammy nods |publisher=NY Daily News |access-date=October 14, 2015}}</ref> After the release of label-mate [[Ab-Soul]]'s album ''[[These Days... (Ab-Soul album)|These Days...]]'' in June 2014, TDE's CEO confirmed that Jay Rock would be the next artist from the label to release an album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/tde-s-ceo-says-jay-rock-is-up-next-news.10988.html|title=TDE's CEO Says "Jay Rock Is Up Next"|author=Rose Lilah|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref>

In September 2014, [[Tech N9ne]] revealed that Rock had left his [[Strange Music]] imprint and is now only signed to Top Dawg Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/09/tech-n9ne-jay-rock-strange-music/ |title=Tech N9ne Says Jay Rock Is No Longer Signed To Strange Music – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=October 14, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/jay-rock-and-kutt-calhoun-leave-strange-music-news.12231.html |title=Jay Rock & Kutt Calhoun Leave Strange Music |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |access-date=October 14, 2015}}</ref> On October 29, 2014, Jay Rock released a song titled "[[Pay for It (Jay Rock song)|Pay for It]]", featuring Kendrick Lamar and [[Chantal Kreviazuk]], as a promotional single.<ref>{{cite web | title=Pay for It (feat. Kendrick Lamar & Chantal) – Single by Jay Rock | url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/pay-for-it-feat.-kendrick/id934221307 | work=[[iTunes Store]] | publisher=[[Apple Inc.|Apple]] | access-date=November 11, 2014}}</ref> On November 15, 2014, Rock appeared on [[NBC]] late-night live television sketch comedy and variety show ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'', alongside the musical guest Lamar, where they performed "Pay For It".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/kendrick-lamar-makes-a-triumphant-return-to-snl-20141116|title=Kendrick Lamar Makes a Triumphant Return to 'SNL'|work=Rolling Stone|date=November 16, 2014|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/57469-kendrick-lamar-performs-i-pay-for-it-with-jay-rock-on-saturday-night-live/|title=Kendrick Lamar Performs "i", "Pay for It" With Jay Rock on "Saturday Night Live"|work=Pitchfork|date=November 16, 2014|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref>

On February 15, 2016, Jay Rock was involved in a harsh motorcycle accident according to [[Top Dawg Entertainment]]'s CEO Anthony Tiffith.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2016/02/15/breaking--jay-rock-badly-hurt-in-motorcycle-accident.html|title=Jay Rock involved in hurtful accident|work=[[Bet.com]]|access-date=February 21, 2016}}</ref> According to Complex, he recovered very well, however he had a lot of broken bones.<ref name="Jay Rock survives accident">{{cite web|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/65751/20160221/tdes-jay-rock-healing-well-terrible-motorcycle-accident-last-week.htm|title=Jay Rock healing from accident|work=Music Times|date=February 21, 2016|access-date=February 21, 2016}}</ref> He was in surgery and successfully came out of it.<ref name="Jay Rock survives accident" />

=== 2018–present: ''Redemption'' ===
{{Main|Redemption (Jay Rock album)}}
On January 12, 2018, Jay Rock released the single "[[King's Dead]]" with [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]]. The song was produced by [[Mike Will Made It]] and [[Teddy Walton]] serving as the lead single to his third studio album and as a single from the soundtrack ''[[Black Panther (film)|Black Panther: The Album]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/kings-dead/1331041266?i=1331043072|title=Black Panther: The Album by Various Artists on Apple Music|publisher=iTunes|access-date=January 12, 2018}}</ref> "King's Dead" became Jay Rock's first song to chart on the [[Billboard Hot 100|US ''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8096084/jay-rock-first-hot-100-entry-kendrick-lamar-future-james-blake-kings-dead|title=Jay Rock Scores First Hot 100 Entry With Kendrick Lamar, Future & James Blake Collab 'King's Dead'|magazine=Billboard|access-date=May 13, 2019}}</ref> On May 16, 2018, he released the single "Win", which served as the second single from his third studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8456340/jay-rock-win-kendrick-lamar-new-song|title=Jay Rock Returns With Kendrick Lamar-Assisted Single 'Win': Listen|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Nerisha|last=Penrose|date=May 16, 2018|access-date=May 16, 2018}}</ref>

After teasing a 2018 release in January, the album, titled ''[[Redemption (Jay Rock album)|Redemption]]'', was announced on May 21, 2018, alongside the cover art and its release date, which was released on June 15, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/dangerookipawaa/status/951604954375974912|title=TOP DAWG #TDE on Twitter: "Chess Moves 2018 "King's Dead" @jayrock|publisher=Twitter|date=January 11, 2018|access-date=January 12, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/jay-rock-announces-new-album-redemption/|title=Jay Rock Announces New Album Redemption|work=Pitchfork|date=May 21, 2018|access-date=May 21, 2018}}</ref>

At the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards|2019 Grammy Awards]], both "King's Dead" and "Win" received nominations in the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance|Best Rap Performance]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song|Best Rap Song]] categories, respectively.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/8047027/grammys-2018-complete-nominees-list|title=Grammys 2018: See the Complete List of Nominees|magazine=Billboard|access-date=May 13, 2019}}</ref>

In February 2019, Rock won his first Grammy award for "[[King's Dead]]".
