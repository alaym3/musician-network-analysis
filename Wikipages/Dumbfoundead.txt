==Early life==
Park was born in [[Buenos Aires]], Argentina to [[South Korea]]n immigrants. He has one younger sister. When he was three years old, Park's family immigrated to the United States by crossing the [[Mexico–United States border]] without [[green card]]s. His family settled in the [[Koreatown, Los Angeles|Koreatown]] neighborhood of [[Los Angeles]], California.<ref name=":0" />

Park began rapping when he was 14 years old, inspired in part by the rappers he saw perform weekly at [[Project Blowed]], a local open mic workshop.<ref name="laweekly" />

Park dropped out of [[John Marshall High School (Los Angeles)|John Marshall High School]] in his sophomore year and moved into a one-bedroom apartment with his sister and a roommate at the age of 16. Before becoming a full-time rapper, he worked as a bail bondsman, among other odd jobs.<ref name="laweekly">{{cite web|url=http://blogs.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2011/11/dumbfoundead_on_doing_stand-up.php|title=Dumbfoundead On Dropping Out Of High School, Working As A Bail Bondsman, And The Advantages Of Being An Asian Rapper|last=Weiss|first=Jeff|date=2011-11-18|work=[[LA Weekly]]|access-date=2018-05-28}}</ref>

Park became a U.S. citizen when he was 19 years old.<ref name=":0" />

==Career==
Park's first solo album, ''DFD'', was released in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|first=Linda|last=Son|url=http://iamkoream.com/dumbfoundead-gets-personal-and-professional-with-dfd/|title=Dumbfoundead Gets Personal and Professional with 'DFD'|work=[[KoreAm]]|date=9 November 2011}}</ref> His second album, ''Take the Stares'', was released in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|first=Charles|last=Lam|url=http://www.nwasianweekly.com/2012/10/dumbfoundead-cant-take-the-stares/|title=Dumbfoundead can't 'Take the Stares'|work=[[Northwest Asian Weekly]]|date=26 October 2012}}</ref> In 2013, Park released his third album, ''Old Boy Jon'', which was entirely produced by Duke Westlake.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecomeupshow.com/2013/10/22/interview-dumbfoundead-talks-old-boy-joy-changing-his-name-to-parker-and-representing-for-asian-americans/|title=Interview: Dumbfoundead talks "Old Boy Jon," changing his name to Parker, and representing Asian-Americans|work=The Come Up Show|first=Matrin|last=Bauman|date=October 22, 2013|access-date=September 18, 2020}}</ref>

Dumbfoundead has been a member of [[Thirsty Fish]] along with [[Open Mike Eagle]] and [[Psychosiz]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/thirsty_fish-watergate|title=Thirsty Fish Watergate|work=[[Exclaim!]]|first=Thomas|last=Quinlan|date=May 28, 2011|access-date=September 18, 2020}}</ref> He has also collaborated with other music artists, including [[Epik High]], [[Traphik]], [[Wax (rapper)|Wax]], [[Jay Park]], [[Kahi (singer)|Kahi]], and [[Anderson .Paak]].<ref name="eph">{{cite web|url=http://popseoul.com/2009/05/22/popseoul-exclusive-interview-with-epik-high/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090523215925/http://popseoul.com/2009/05/22/popseoul-exclusive-interview-with-epik-high/|url-status=dead|archive-date=23 May 2009|title=Dumbfoundead & Epic High interview with|work=Popseoul.com|date=22 May 2009|access-date=15 January 2012}}</ref> In 2015, he was featured on the remix of [[Keith Ape]]'s song "It G Ma", alongside popular rappers [[Waka Flocka Flame]], [[ASAP Ferg]], and Father.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://owsla.com/release/keith-ape-it-g-ma-remix-ft-aap-ferg-father-dumbfoundead-waka-flocka-flame/|title=OWSLA KEITH APE – IT G MA REMIX (FT. A$AP FERG, FATHER, DUMBFOUNDEAD & WAKA FLOCKA FLAME)|website=owsla.com|access-date=July 27, 2015}}</ref>

Park began growing a large web fan base, after video clips of his rap battles were posted to [[YouTube]]. In 2015, Park returned to rap battling to participate in [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] and [[OVO Sound|OVO]]'s event ''[[King of the Dot]] Blackout 5'', and was complimented by Drake himself, who expressed his excitement at his return.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/dumbfoundead/videos/vb.81232640045/10155237030570046/?type=2&theater|title=Dumbfoundead|website=[[Facebook]]|access-date=30 April 2017}}</ref> Park competed against [[Wild 'n Out]] cast member Conceited, and the battle was the most popular English rap battle of 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OrganikHipHop/status/625377108597059584?lang=en|title=Organik - KOTDTV.com on Twitter|website=[[Twitter]]|access-date=30 April 2017}}</ref>

He has been featured on [[NBC]] for his viral video Jam Session 2.0, consisting of 8 different musicians from around the world sharing the spotlight individually via split screen but collaborating on one cohesive track.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbclosangeles.com/the-scene/events/MusicRaw__Dumbfoundead_Los_Angeles.html|title=MusicRaw: Dumbfoundead|work=[[KNBC]]}}</ref> He has also been featured by ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'',<ref>{{cite web|first=Jeff|last=Weiss|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/music_blog/2011/07/the-arrival-of-dumbfoundead-.html|title=The arrival of Dumbfoundead: Koreatown rapper's 'Are We There Yet?'|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=8 July 2011}}</ref> ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbc.com/last-call-with-carson-daly/video/dumbfoundead/1375007|title=Last Call with Carson Daly|date=17 December 2012|work=[[NBC]]|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120113034547/http://www.nbc.com/last-call-with-carson-daly/video/dumbfoundead/1375007|archive-date=13 January 2012}}</ref> MTV Hive,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtvhive.com/2011/11/11/dumbfoundead-a-rap-battle-vet-grows-up/|title=Dumbfoundead: A Rap Battle Vet Grows Up|work=[[MTV]]|date=11 November 2011}}</ref> and [[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://us.mnet.com/drupal/content/dumbfoundead|title=Dumbfoundead|work=[[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]]}}</ref>

Park played a supporting role in [[Joseph Kahn]]'s horror film ''[[Detention (2011 film)|Detention]]''<ref>{{cite web|first=Y. Peter|last=Kang|url=http://iamkoream.com/sony-buys-rights-to-joseph-kahns-indie-horror-film/|title=Sony Buys Rights To Joseph Kahn's Indie Horror Film|work=[[KoreAm]]|date=16 June 2011}}</ref> and would later appear in Kahn's 2017 film ''[[Bodied]]'' as battle-rapper Prospek.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The stars of battle rap satire 'Bodied' on the politics of wokeness: 'The whole world is in a battle rap right now'|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/la-et-mn-bodied-battle-rap-movie-20181128-story.html|last=Yamato|first=Jen|date=2018-11-28|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-26}}</ref>

In 2016, he released the music video "Safe," <ref>{{Citation|last=DUMBFOUNDEAD|title=Dumbfoundead - SAFE|date=2016-05-26|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mmvqb9Uzu8k|access-date=2017-02-15}}</ref> which gained widespread attention for superimposing Park's likeness onto the faces of white actors in famous movie scenes. The objective of this was to call attention to the fact that there were no Asian or Asian-American actors at the Oscars, and that "the only yellow men were all statues."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-dumbfoundead-safe-20160601-snap-story.html|title=Dumbfoundead breaks down the lyrics in his anti-whitewashing anthem 'Safe'|last=Thomas|first=Dexter|website=latimes.com|access-date=2017-02-15}}</ref> Furthermore, the music video was another call to "the obvious underrepresentation of people of color in Hollywood."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/10/06/dumbfoundead-rapper-asian-american-representation|title=Don't Mistake Dumbfoundead For Safe|website=The Fader|access-date=2017-02-15}}</ref> Park was also a starring member of the 2016 documentary ''[[Bad Rap (documentary)|Bad Rap]]'', which outlined the lives of four Asian-American artists trying to make it in the hip-hop scene. He also played Dylan Shin in the [[Starz]] drama [[Power (TV series)|''Power'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.badrapfilm.com/about|title=About|last=Film|first=Bad Rap|website=Bad Rap Film|language=en|access-date=2017-06-05}}</ref> In 2017, he co-wrote "Spirit Animal" and "Arrived" alongside [[Jessi (musician)|Jessi]] for her debut EP ''[[Un2verse]]''.

In April 2020, [[Peacock (streaming service)|Peacock]] began development on a half hour comedy television series based on Park's life entitled ''Big Dummie.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2020/04/rapper-dumbfoundead-comedy-inspired-life-peacock-jonathan-park-1202909189/|title=Rapper Dumbfoundead To Front Comedy Inspired By His Life For Peacock|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=2020-04-15|website=Deadline|language=en|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref>

Park's talk show with [[Sasha Grey]], ''Grey Area,'' debuted on the online [[television network]] [[VENN]] on August 5, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Armecin|first=Catherine|date=2020-07-23|title=Ex-Porn Star Sasha Grey To Tackle The Hard Questions In 'Grey Area' Series|url=https://www.ibtimes.com/ex-porn-star-sasha-grey-tackle-hard-questions-grey-area-series-3016377|access-date=2020-11-29|website=International Business Times}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Browne|first=Ryan|date=2020-08-05|title=A new TV network aims to lure a generation brought up on video games in the coronavirus era|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/08/05/venn-tv-network-launches-to-lure-gaming-generation-in-coronavirus-era.html|access-date=2020-11-29|website=CNBC|language=en}}</ref>

==Community activism==
During the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States|coronavirus pandemic]], Park promoted support for restaurant workers affected by [[COVID-19]].<ref name="LAT 2020-07-03">{{Cite news|last=Park|first=Brian|date=2020-07-03|title=Talking takeout and Koreatown with Dumbfoundead|url=https://www.latimes.com/food/story/2020-07-03/dumbfoundead-jonathan-park-koreatown-the-takeout|access-date=2020-07-04|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|language=en-US}}</ref>
