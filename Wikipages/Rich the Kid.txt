'''Dimitri Leslie Roger''' (born July 13, 1992),<ref>https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/workID/898046413</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/happy-birthday-rich-the-kid/|title=HAPPY BIRTHDAY, RICH THE KID!|work=XXL|author=Madden, Sidney|date=July 13, 2017|access-date=March 3, 2020}}</ref> professionally known as '''Rich the Kid''', is an American rapper.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/rich-the-kid/561444659 | title=Rich The Kid | publisher=[[iTunes Store|iTunes]] | access-date=December 8, 2017}}</ref> Signed to [[Rostrum Records]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://hiphop-n-more.com/2019/12/rich-the-kid-leaves-interscope-joins-republic-new-single-thats-tuff-ft-quavo/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=January 18, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200104175455/https://hiphop-n-more.com/2019/12/rich-the-kid-leaves-interscope-joins-republic-new-single-thats-tuff-ft-quavo/ |archive-date=January 4, 2020 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="Xxlmag.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/rich-the-kid-signs-interscope/ |title=Rich The Kid Signs to Interscope Records ‚Äì XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=June 10, 2017}}</ref> he released his debut studio album, ''[[The World Is Yours (Rich the Kid album)|The World Is Yours]]'', on March 30, 2018. His second studio album, ''[[The World Is Yours 2]]'', was released on March 22, 2019, and his third studio album, ''[[Boss Man (album)|Boss Man]]'', on March 13, 2020.

== Early life ==
Dimitri Leslie Roger was born in [[Queens]], New York City, on July 13, 1992.<ref name="wmag">{{cite web | url=http://www.wmagazine.com/story/new-york-fashion-week-mens-rich-the-kid | title=Following Rapper Rich the Kid Around Men's Fashion Week | work=[[W (magazine)|W Magazine]] | date=July 15, 2016 | access-date=August 16, 2016 |author=Petrarca, Emilia}}</ref> He is of [[Haitian Americans|Haitian]] descent and grew up speaking [[Haitian Creole]] fluently.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6443843/univeral-label-rap-hit-squad | title=The Lowdown on Atlanta Hip-Hop Label Quality Control's Young Hot Squad -- From Migos to OG Maco | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=January 21, 2015 | access-date=August 17, 2016 | author=Peisner, David}}</ref><ref name="xxlmag">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2013/12/the-break-presents-rich-the-kid/|last=Diep|first=Eric|title=The Break Presents: Rich The Kid|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL Magazine]]|date=December 2, 2013|access-date=November 3, 2014}}</ref> After his parents' divorce, Roger moved with his mother to [[Woodstock, Georgia]], a suburb 30 minutes north of [[Atlanta]], when he was 13.<ref name="xxlmag" /><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6415110/rich-the-kid-from-the-streets-exclusive-video-premiere | title=Rich the Kid, 'From the Streets': Exclusive Video Premiere | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=December 23, 2014 | access-date=August 17, 2016 | author=Ramirez, Erika}}</ref>

Rich the Kid said he grew up listening to [[Nas]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]], [[Notorious B.I.G.]] & [[50 Cent]], but after moving to Woodstock, he started listening to [[T.I.]] and [[Young Jeezy]].<ref name="xxlmag" /> His first rap name was Black Boy Da Kid,<ref name="wmag" /> but later changed it to Rich the Kid. He attended [[Elmont Memorial Junior ‚Äì Senior High School]] in [[Elmont, New York]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/richthekid/status/44850robynlithe9371052724224|title=@HandsomeBobby5 yea went to elmont & Alden Terrace|last=KID|first=RICH THE|date=March 25, 2014|website=@richthekid|access-date=March 2, 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Rich the Kid was married to Antonette Willis and has two children with her. In March 2018, Willis filed for divorce, seeking full physical custody while giving Rich The Kid visitation rights and joint legal custody. Willis is also seeking spousal support.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/rich-the-kid-wife-files-for-divorce/|title=Rich The Kid's Wife Files for Divorce ‚Äì XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref> This was shortly after Willis accused him of cheating on her with [[Blac Chyna]] and India Love, even alleging that he was in a relationship with the former.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/rich-the-kid-accused-of-cheating-on-wife-with-blac-chyna-india-love/|title=Rich The Kid Accused of Cheating With Blac Chyna, India Love|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref> Willis later claimed that Rich the Kid abused her and forced her to have abortions regularly, saying it was commonplace for police to be called to their residence for domestic abuse.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/05/rich-the-kid-domestic-abuse-forced-abortions/|title=Rich the Kid Accused of Domestic Abuse, Forcing Ex to Get Abortions: Report|date=May 22, 2018|work=Spin|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/10/rich-the-kid-accused-domestic-violence/|title=Rich The Kid Accused of Domestic Violence ‚Äì XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref>

Rich the Kid began dating Tori Hughes (professionally known as Tori Brixx) shortly following the filing of the divorce. On June 15, 2018, Rich the Kid was hospitalized following a home invasion at Hughes' home.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8461272/rich-the-kid-hospitalized-home-invasion|title=Rich the Kid Hospitalized Following Home Invasion: Report|work=Billboard|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref> According to a police report, several men entered Hughes' home carrying firearms and demanded money.  When Rich The Kid attempted to confront them, he was beaten and the men left with a "significant" amount of money and jewelry.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/rich-the-kid-hospitalized-after-home-invasion-attack-report/|title=Rich the Kid Hospitalized After Home Invasion Attack: Report {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|date=June 15, 2018|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref> On December 29, 2018, Rich the Kid got into a UTV accident in Calabasas, CA, when Dimitri and his friend were driving around in a Polaris RZR 4-wheeler. Based on sources from TMZ, it seems that Dimitri lost control of it and fell off of his RZR 4-wheeler and severely injured his hand. He was taken to the hospital shortly after.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rich the Kid Severely Injures His Hand in UTV Crash |url=https://www.tmz.com/2018/12/29/rich-the-kid-injures-hand-utv-accident-crash/}}</ref> In February 2019, he was robbed and shot at outside of the [[Westlake Recording Studios|Westlake Recording Studio]] in [[West Hollywood, California|West Hollywood]], California.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/music/rich-the-kid-shot-at-robbed-recording-studio-usher-inside/|title=Rapper Rich the Kid Shot at and Robbed Outside a West Hollywood Studio as Usher Records Inside|website=PEOPLE.com|access-date=February 19, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ajc.com/entertainment/rapper-rich-the-kid-robbed-outside-studio-where-usher-was-recording/fcH3gup4qBq4bXB52flnJM/|title=Rapper Rich the Kid robbed outside studio where Usher was recording|last=Nancy Clanton|first=The Atlanta Journal-Constitution|website=ajc|access-date=February 19, 2019}}</ref>

On March 26, 2018, Rich the Kid formally released "Dead Friends".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8261443/rich-the-kid-dead-friends-new-song-listen|title=Rich the Kid Takes Aim at Lil Uzi Vert on 'Dead Friends': Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/04/rich-the-kid-hints-dead-friends-video-is-about-lil-uzi-vert/|title=Rich The Kid Hints "Dead Friends" Video Is About Lil Uzi Vert ‚Äì XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref> Following the release of "Dead Friends", Rich the Kid said he thought Lil Uzi Vert would not respond.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/03/rich-the-kid-doesnt-think-lil-uzi-vert-will-respond-to-dead-friends|title=Rich the Kid Doesn't Think Lil Uzi Vert Will Respond to Diss Song "Dead Friends"|website=Complex|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref> Uzi later responded on his SoundCloud with "Rich Forever Leaked" over the [[Chief Keef]] "Where" instrumental, to which Rich the Kid responded to by saying "Fuck that nigga."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/04/rich-the-kid-has-more-to-say-about-lil-uzi-vert|title=Rich the Kid Responds to Lil Uzi Vert Diss: 'F**k That N***a'|website=Complex|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref>

In March 2016, Rich the Kid started his own record label called Rich Forever Music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.37793/title.rich-the-kid-launches-rich-forever-music-label-unveils-first-signee|title=Rich The Kid Launches Rich Forever Music; Unveils First Signee|author=Mansell, Henry|date=March 7, 2016|work=[[HipHopDX]]|access-date=August 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2016/04/rich-the-kid-rich-forever-mixtape/|title=Rich The Kid Introduces His Label With 'Rich Forever Music' Mixtape|author=Robertson, Darryl|date=April 5, 2016|work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe Magazine]]|access-date=August 17, 2016}}</ref> The label's first artist signed up was Chicago based [[Famous Dex]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/03/rich-the-kid-signs-famous-dex/|title=Rich The Kid Signs Famous Dex to His New Label|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL Magazine]]|last=Ivey|first=Justin|date=March 8, 2016|access-date=July 20, 2016}}</ref> He also signed his first producer to Rich Forever Music, The Lab Cook.<ref name="firstrelease">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rich-the-kid-rich-forever-music-new-mixtape.116351.html|title=Rich The Kid ‚Äì Rich Forever Music|author=Smith, Trevor|date=April 4, 2016|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=August 17, 2016}}</ref> In April 2016, Rich the Kid signed his second artist, rapper J $tash.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/04/04/rich-the-kid-shares-compilation-mixtape-irich-forever-musici|title=Rich The Kid Shares Compilation Mixtape Rich Forever Music|last=Dandridge-Lemco|first=Ben|date=April 4, 2016|work=The Fader|access-date=September 9, 2018}}</ref>

The record label's first release was a 15-track compilation titled ''Rich Forever Music'', and featured [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], Skippa Da Flippa, [[Lil Yachty]] and [[OG Maco]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.rapswag.com/2016/04/04/mixtape-rich-forever-music/#more-71498|title=Mixtape: Rich Forever Music ‚Äì Rap Swagger|date=April 4, 2016|work=Rap Swagger|access-date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> Shortly after the release, J $tash parted ways with the label.

In June 2016, Rich the Kid signed a deal to have [[300 Entertainment]] become a parent company for Rich Forever Music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2016/07/05/rich-the-kid-delivers-rich-forever-2-mixtape/|title=Rich The Kid Delivers 'Rich Forever 2' Mixtape|author=Lee, Craig|date=July 5, 2016|work=[[The Source]]}}</ref>

On July 4, 2016, Rich Forever Music released their second project ''Rich Forever 2,'' a collaborative mixtape between Rich the Kid and Famous Dex, with appearances from [[Wiz Khalifa]], Lil Yachty, [[Young Thug]], [[Jaden Smith]] and [[Playboi Carti]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rich-the-kid-rich-forever-2-new-mixtape.116459.html|title=Rich Forever 2|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> In November 2016, Rich the Kid signed [[Brooklyn]] rapper Jay Critch to his label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/12/rich-the-kid-and-jay-critch-got-a-hit-with-did-it-again-remix/|title=Rich The Kid and Jay Critch Got a Hit With "Did It Again (Remix)"|author=C.M Emmanuel|date=December 1, 2016|work=[[XXL Magazine]]}}</ref>

On March 17, 2017, the label released their third compilation mixtape ''[[The Rich Forever Way (Mixtape)|The Rich Forever Way]].''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rich-the-kid-the-rich-forever-way-stream-new-mixtape.116973.html|title=The Rich Forever Way [Stream]|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> The label released their fourth compilation ''[[Rich Forever 3]]'' on June 16, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rich-the-kid-reveals-tracklist-for-rich-forever-3-news.33348.html|title=Rich The Kid Reveals Tracklist For 'Rich Forever 3'|last=Goddard|first=Kevin|date=June 2, 2017|publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com|access-date=June 6, 2017}}</ref>

In February 2018, Rich the Kid signed [[Texas]] rapper YBN Almighty Jay to his label.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-almighty-jay-signs-to-to-rich-forever-ent/|title=YBN Almighty Jay signs to Rich Forever Ent.|date=January 16, 2018|work=Elevator Mag|access-date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> In March 2018, YBN Almighty Jay and Rich the Kid had a fall out when Rich the Kid released a song called ''Back Quick'' without YBN Almighty Jay's permission.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ybn-almighty-jay-attempts-to-pull-up-on-rich-the-kid-in-possibly-fake-beef-news.46245.html|title=YBN Almighty Jay Attempts To "Pull Up" On Rich The Kid In Possibly Fake Beef|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> In April 2018, YBN Almighty Jay did an interview with DJ Akademiks saying he was never signed with the label.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[YouTube]]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wLyq4gS0sjM| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211118/wLyq4gS0sjM| archive-date=2021-11-18 | url-status=live|title=YBN Almighty Jay Says he was NEVER Signed to Rich Forever & Tells why He almost Fought Rich the Kid!|author=DJ Akademiks|date=April 18, 2018|access-date=September 26, 2018}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[YouTube]]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fNBTODG2cNE| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211118/fNBTODG2cNE| archive-date=2021-11-18 | url-status=live|title=YBN Almighty Jay Says He never signed to Rich the Kid and speaks on YBN Nahmir vs Lil Wop!|website=YouTube|author=King Akademiks|date=April 18, 2018|access-date=September 26, 2018}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

In May 2018, Famous Dex stated he had left Rich Forever Music, and is an independent artist,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/famous-dex-announces-his-departure-from-rich-the-kids-rich-forever-label-news.51428.html|title=Famous Dex Announces His Departure From Rich The Kid's "Rich Forever" Label|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=May 31, 2018}}</ref> but Rich the Kid made a post through the label's Instagram saying he is still signed to the label.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BjaHxmDleAH/|title=Rich Forever Music on Instagram: "Like it or not he is Rich Forever @famousdex ‚úçüèΩü§¶üèæ‚ôÇÔ∏è"|website=Instagram|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref>
