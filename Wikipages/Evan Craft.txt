'''Evan Kenneth Craft''' (born April 8, 1991),<ref>{{cite web |author1=Thom Jurek |title=Evan Craft Biography by Thom Jurek |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/evan-craft-mn0002857663 |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=20 August 2021}}</ref> is an American [[Contemporary worship music]] singer. Evan has recorded albums in Spanish and English.<ref>{{cite web |title=Evan Craft |url=https://www.evancraftmusic.com |access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref> His 2012 release, ''[[Yo Soy Segundo]]'', ranked high on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts, reaching number one on the Christian Latin Albums chart in the United States,<ref>{{cite web |title=Evan Craft |url=https://www.capitolcmgpublishing.com/content/evan-craft |publisher=[[EMI Christian Music Group|EMI CMG]] |access-date=20 August 2021}}</ref> number 10 on the [[Latin Pop Albums]] chart<ref>{{cite web |title=Latin Pop Albums Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/latin-pop-albums/2012-10-20 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref> and number 30 on the ''Billboard'' [[Top Latin Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web |title=Top Latin Albums Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/latin-albums/2012-10-20 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref> Craft has been nominated for multiple awards.

== Biography ==
Evan Craft was born in 1991 in [[Conejo Valley]], [[California]],<ref>{{cite web |author1=Annie Gee |title=Evan Craft se destaca dentro de la m√∫sica cristiana |url=https://www.elnuevoherald.com/entretenimiento/musica/article250308444.html |publisher=[[el Nuevo Herald]] |access-date=20 August 2021}}</ref> Craft from a very young age began to play the guitar at the age of 12 and there he began to write songs. Craft expanded his linguistic studies at university, attending schools in Spain and [[Costa Rica]].<ref>{{cite web |author1=Thom Jurek |title=Evan Craft |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/evan-craft-mn0002857663/biography |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
Evan Craft began writing songs during high school. Evan has released several albums and EPs. His first album in Spanish, Craft joined other musicians such as Sean and Ryan Cook and Nico Aranda, to travel through different countries delivering evangelical messages through their performances in many Spanish churches, schools and orphanages.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Leah MarieAnn Klett |title=Jaci Velasquez, Evan Craft urge Hispanic Christians to 'vote in a way that most honors God' |url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/jaci-velasquez-evan-craft-urge-hispanic-christians-to-vote.html |work=[[The Christian Post]] |access-date=21 August 2021}}</ref>

Craft in 2012 released his first full-length studio album called ''[[Giants (Evan Craft album)|Giants]]'', under the DREAM Records label.<ref>{{cite web |title=Giants by Evan Craft |url=https://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=11360 |publisher=New Release Today |access-date=21 August 2021}}</ref>

In 2012 he released the album ''[[Yo Soy Segundo]]'', under the Dream Records label and was distributed through Universal, the album debuted in the United States at number one on the chart Latin Christian Albums, at number 10 on the [[Latin Pop Albums]] chart and number 30 on [[Top Latin Albums]] chart by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Evan Craft's Yo Soy Segundo lands the #1 Christian Latin album in the country |url=https://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2012/10/11.Evan%20Crafts%20Yo%20Soy%20Segundo%20lands%20the%20No1%20Christian%20Latin%20album%20in%20the%20country%20and%20Marlene%20Brehm%20re-enters%20the%20Billboard%20chart%20with%20a%20strong%20finish.asp |publisher=Jesusfreakhideout |access-date=19 May 2021}}</ref> In his other musical productions Evan Craft recorded songs with [[Danilo Montero]], Ingrid Rosario, [[Seth Condrey]], among others. ''Yo Soy Segundo'' reached several stations [[Christian Radio]] and on download lists.
