'''MGMT''' ({{IPAc-en|É›|m|-|dÊ’|i|-|É›|m|-|t|i:}}<ref>{{cite web |work=[[Vulture (web site)|Vulture]] |date=July 10, 2008 |url=https://www.vulture.com/2008/07/once_and_for_all_how_do_you_pr.html |title=Once and for All: How Do You Pronounce MGMT? |quote=We called the band's label, Columbia, and a publicist told us it's definitely pronounced "Em Gee Em Tee." This seems to be confirmed by this video, in which MGMT's Andrew VanWyngarden pronounces it that way.}}</ref>) is<!-- Edits changing this to ARE will be REVERTED. The band is not British.--> an American [[indie rock]] band formed in 2002 in [[Middletown, Connecticut]]. It was founded by multi-instrumentalists [[Andrew VanWyngarden]] and [[Benjamin Goldwasser|Ben Goldwasser]]. Alongside VanWyngarden and Goldwasser, MGMT's live lineup currently consists of drummer Will Berman, bassist Simon O'Connor, and guitarist and keyboard James Richardson.

Originally signed to [[Cantora Records]] by the nascent label's co-founder, NYU undergrad Will Griggs, MGMT later signed with [[Columbia Records|Columbia]] and [[RED Ink Records|RED Ink]] in 2006<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/04/11/arts/music/11mgmt.html | work=The New York Times | first=Melena | last=Ryzik | title=MGMT's Sophomore Challenges | date=April 11, 2010}}</ref><ref name="Breaking Out">{{cite magazine |first=David |last=Marchese |url=https://www.spin.com/2007/11/mgmt-0/ |title=Breaking Out: MGMT |magazine=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |date=November 23, 2007 |access-date=June 16, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Cristina Black |url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2009-08-04/music/the-wesleyan-mafia-mgmt-boy-crisis-amazing-baby/ |title=The Wesleyan Mafia: MGMT, Boy Crisis, Amazing Baby â€“ Page 1 â€“ Music â€“ New York |newspaper=Village Voice |date=August 4, 2009 |access-date=August 6, 2011}}</ref> and released their debut album ''[[Oracular Spectacular]]'' the next year. After the release of ''Oracular Spectacular'' Richardson, Berman and Matthew Asti joined the core band in the studio for ''[[Congratulations (album)|Congratulations]],'' which was released on April 13, 2010.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://spin.com/articles/exclusive-mgmt-unveil-new-album |title=EXCLUSIVE: MGMT Unveil New Album! |last=Goodman |first=William |date=February 8, 2010 |magazine=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |access-date=February 16, 2010}}</ref> In January 2011 they began work on their [[MGMT (album)|eponymous third studio album]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/music/breakdown/2841 |title=MGMT's ANDREW VANWYNGARDEN issues a vinyl shopping and coffee date invitation |publisher=Coupdemainmagazine.com |date=February 28, 2011 |access-date=August 6, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708195036/http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/music/breakdown/2841 |archive-date=July 8, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> It was released on September 17, 2013, and was released as an early exclusive on [[Rdio]] on September 9, 2013.<ref name="mgmt-rdio">{{cite web | url=https://www.theverge.com/2013/9/9/4711848/mgmts-new-album-and-bizarre-video-are-available-early-on-rdio | title=MGMT's new album and bizarre video are available a week early on Rdio | publisher=TheVerge | date=September 9, 2013 | access-date=September 9, 2013 | author=Ingraham, Nathan}}</ref> The group's fourth studio album, titled ''[[Little Dark Age]]'', was released in February 2018. Beginning in 2019, the duo began producing music independent of a label for the first time since 2006. In late 2019, the two released a new song called "In the Afternoon" as their first fully self-produced single.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rockcellarmagazine.com/mgmt-in-the-afternoon-new-song-independent-listen-stream-video/|title=MGMT Goes Independent: Watch the New Video for 'In the Afternoon'|date=2019-12-11|website=RockCellarMagazine|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-12}}</ref>

On October 5, 2007, [[Spin (magazine)|''Spin'']] named MGMT "Artist of the Day".<ref name="Artist of the Day">{{cite magazine |url=http://www.spin.com/features/band_of_the_day/2007/10/071005_mgmt/ |title=Artist of the Day: MGMT |magazine=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |first=Anthony M. |last=D'Amato |access-date=October 5, 2007}}</ref> In November ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' pegged MGMT as a top ten "Artist to Watch" in 2008<ref name="Artist to Watch">{{cite web| url = https://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/17323914/artist_to_watch_mgmt | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20071116051221/http://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/17323914/artist_to_watch_mgmt | url-status = dead | archive-date = November 16, 2007 | title = Artist to Watch: MGMT |work=Rolling Stone | first = Kevin | last = O'Donnell | access-date = November 14, 2007 }}</ref> and went on to name ''[[Oracular Spectacular]]'' number 494 in their top 500 Greatest Albums of All Time list. ==History==
===Formation (2002â€“2005)===
[[File:MGMT Burlington 2005-09-12.jpg|thumb|MGMT in 2005]]
Ben Goldwasser and Andrew VanWyngarden formed the band while attending [[Wesleyan University]] during their freshman year.<ref name="argus">{{cite web|url =http://www.wesleyan.edu/argus/archives/apr132004/dateyear/f4.html|title = Weekly Wes Celeb: ''The magmo|last = Landerman|first = Jessie|publisher = [[Wesleyan University|Wesleyan Argus]]|date = April 13, 2004|access-date = July 27, 2007|url-status = dead|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070927070512/http://www.wesleyanargus.com/article.php?article_id=2053|archive-date = September 27, 2007|df = mdy-all}}</ref> "We weren't trying to start a band," remarked Goldwasser. "We were just hanging out and showing each other music that we liked." VanWyngarden named [[Talking Heads]] and [[Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark]] (OMD) as artists who are "definitely in [their] blood".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/wj4zez/mgmt-little-dark-age-interview|title=MGMT Are Still Perfectly Strange|last=Fitzmaurice|first=Larry|date=February 18, 2018|website=Vice|access-date=May 24, 2021}}</ref> They experimented with [[noise rock]] and [[electronica]] before settling on what Paco Alvarez of ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'' calls "their current brand of shape-shifting psychedelic pop."<ref name="Breaking Out" /> In 2002, the duo attended the inaugural Bonnaroo together as fans.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://relix.com/articles/detail/mgmt-surviving-saturn-s-return/|title=MGMT: Surviving Saturn's Return|date=July 12, 2013|website=Relix Media|access-date=October 4, 2020}}</ref> The band formed under the name The Management and released two demo albums, ''We (Don't) Care'' and ''Climbing to New Lows'', under that name; but since the name was already being used by another band, they later changed it to MGMT. While at Wesleyan, they were schoolmates of [[Francis and the Lights]], with whom they have since toured.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/artist-of-the-week-francis-and-the-lights-20100805|title=Artist of the Week: Francis and the Lights|last=O'Donnell|first=Kevin|date=August 5, 2010|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=August 17, 2016}}</ref> VanWyngarden and Goldwasser graduated in 2005 and toured extensively in support of the ''[[Time to Pretend EP]]'' released in the same year, opening for indie pop band [[Of Montreal]].<ref name="2005tour">{{cite web|url= http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/34897-of-montreal-plan-late-summer-tour|title= Montreal Plan Late Summer Tour|website=Pitchfork|date=July 13, 2005|access-date= March 24, 2007|url-status= dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080308101328/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/34897-of-montreal-plan-late-summer-tour|archive-date =March 8, 2008|df= mdy-all}}</ref>

===''Congratulations'' (2010â€“2011)===
{{Main|Congratulations (album)}}
[[Image:MGMT at Voodoo 2010.jpg|thumb|MGMT performing at [[Voodoo Festival|Voodoo Festival 2010]], dressed as characters from ''[[Scooby-Doo]]'' for [[Halloween]]|alt=]]
MGMT's ''[[Congratulations (album)|Congratulations]]'' was released on April 13, 2010. The band, including Matthew Asti, James Richardson, and Will Berman from the live band, spent the summer of 2009 in [[Malibu, California]], recording the album with [[Pete Kember]] (a.k.a. Sonic Boom), formerly of [[Spacemen 3]] and Spectrum, serving as producer/guru, and long-time friend Billy Bennett (also an NCAA record-holding kicker for the University of Georgia), as engineer. MGMT originally suggested that they might not release any singles from the album.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.nme.com/news/mgmt/49280 |title=MGMT: 'We're not releasing any singles from our new album' |magazine=[[NME]]|access-date=April 2, 2010}}</ref> By July 2010, however, four single releases from the album had been confirmed. The album cover art for ''Congratulations'' was created for MGMT by [[Anthony Ausgang]] and the overall design of the packaging is by Josh Cheuse of Sony Entertainment. On March 9, 2010, MGMT released the song "[[Flash Delirium]]" for free download on their official website. On March 20, the band allowed users to stream their new album from their website.

MGMT started their Congratulations Tour on April 12, 2010, in San Francisco, California, at [[The Fillmore]]. They performed on April 23 at [[Brown University]]'s Spring Weekend; on April 27 at [[Yale University]]'s Spring Fling; on April 30 at the [[University of New Hampshire]]'s Spring Climax; and on May 1 at [[Fordham University]]'s Spring Weekend. They performed on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' on April 24, 2010, and on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'' on May 12 to promote their new album, and on June 11, MGMT played their largest headlining show anywhere in the world with a sold-out, 9,500 capacity gig at the [[Red Rocks Amphitheatre]] in [[Morrison, Colorado]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://discosalt.com/blog/2010/06/15/mgmt-its-working/ |title=Mgmt: It's Working |publisher=Discosalt |date=June 15, 2010 |access-date=September 9, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120322222152/http://discosalt.com/blog/2010/06/15/mgmt-its-working/ |archive-date=March 22, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On June 25, MGMT performed on ''[[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]]'', and on July 23, they were the musical guests on ''[[The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson]]''.

On August 25, MGMT released a music video for "Congratulations", the third video of their second album.
<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.rocktails.tv/2010/08/congratulations-el-nuevo-video-de-mgmt.html |title=Congratulations, MGMT video {{!}} Rocktails Musica Online |date=August 31, 2017 |work=ROCKTAILS MUSICA ONLINE |access-date=October 4, 2017|language=es-ES}}</ref> MGMT was one of the third tier bands for the 2010 [[Coachella Festival]] in [[Indio, California]]. The event draws nearly 120,000 attendees annually. On October 31, 2010, the band performed for the Voodoo Experience in [[New Orleans, Louisiana]]. The band decided to dress as the main characters from ''[[Scooby-Doo]]'' for Halloween; Andrew dressed up as [[Daphne Blake|Daphne]] and Ben dressed up as [[Velma Dinkley|Velma]]. They headlined at [[Fun Fun Fun Fest]] which was held at Waterloo Park in [[Austin, Texas]] on November 6, 2010. The performance was the last show in the US for MGMT in 2010. Their first 2011 gig was at [[Mar del Plata]], Argentina, in front of a crowd of more than 40,000 on the beach in a free event which they headlined.<ref name="rolling">{{cite magazine|last=Ortelli|first=Juan|title=MGMT: flash y delirio en Mar del Plata|url=https://www.rollingstone.com.ar/1343934-mgmt-flash-y-delirio-en-mar-del-plata|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=September 4, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151002130344/http://www.rollingstone.com.ar/1343934-mgmt-flash-y-delirio-en-mar-del-plata|archive-date=October 2, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> MGMT began their first Asian tour on February 22, 2011.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://asiapacificarts.usc.edu/article@apa?mgmt_starts_asian_tour_in_japan_16420.aspx | title = MGMT starts Asian tour in Japan | publisher = Asia Pacific Arts | date = February 22, 2011}}</ref> On September 22, 2011 MGMT performed the [[Pink Floyd]] song "[[Lucifer Sam]]" during ''[[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]]'''s "Pink Floyd Week".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/467339/jimmy-fallon-to-honor-pink-floyd-with-week-of-tributes |title=Jimmy Fallon to Honor Pink Floyd With Week of Tributes|magazine=Billboard.com|date=September 22, 2011|access-date=September 16, 2013}}</ref>

''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine published on January 25, 2018 an interview with MGMT regarding their fourth album ''[[Little Dark Age]]''. MGMT admitted that their two previous albums were so poorly received that they thought they could never have reached peak popularity as they had with the release of their first album. Since their separation, the two core members began working long distances on the album via e-mail. ''Little Dark Age'' was in part shaped by this unique, distanced relationship between the two musicians, who later decided to meet and jointly work together again.

The band has explained that ''Little Dark Age'' is both an expression of surprise and dismay to the current political and social climateâ€”particularly the election of [[Presidency of Donald Trump|Donald Trump as President of the United States]]â€”with an occasional reference to their personal lives. Of the election, VanWyngarden stated, "We were like, 'Wow, is it actually possible for the most impossible thing to happen?' [...] Apparently, we were more inspired to write pop music after evil took over the world." Touring guitarist James Richardson also provided inspiration, which resulted in the song 'James' being dedicated to celebrating the band's friendship. The album was released on February 9, 2018, and followed with a North American tour, part of the [[Little Dark Age Tour|''Little Dark Age'' Tour]].<ref>Andy Greene, [https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/mgmt-on-new-lp-playing-kids-live-oracular-11-years-later-w515791, "MGMT's Pop Adventure: How Duo Bounced Back 11 Years After Debut"],''Rolling Stone'',25th January, 2018</ref>

===Going independent (2019â€“present)===
On November 22, 2019, the band performed a new song titled "In the Afternoon" in [[Las Vegas]], [[Nevada]]. On December 10, a teaser for the studio version of the song was posted on the band's Instagram page. ==Legal proceedings==
In January 2009, MGMT demanded compensation from the French [[Union for a Popular Movement]] (UMP) Party. The UMP, headed by [[Nicolas Sarkozy]], the former [[President of France]], had used the song "[[Kids (MGMT song)|Kids]]" without permission at a party conference and in two online videos in 2008. In an official statement, MGMT remarked that "the fact that the UMP used our song without permission while simultaneously pushing anti-piracy legislation seemed a little wack."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2009/04/24/mgmt-settles-with-french-government-and-are-in-the-studio-recording-new-album/|title=MGMT Settles With French Government And Are In The Studio Recording New Album|date=April 24, 2009|access-date=November 26, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121206045216/http://www.thefader.com/2009/04/24/mgmt-settles-with-french-government-and-are-in-the-studio-recording-new-album/|archive-date=December 6, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> The UMP initially offered a symbolic compensation of [[euro|â‚¬]]1 to MGMT, but the band refused that offer. Finally, in April 2009, the parties settled out of court, and the UMP agreed to pay MGMT around â‚¬2,500 for legal fees and â‚¬30,000 (US $39,050) for copyright infringement. MGMT donated this money to artists' rights organizations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lepoint.fr/actualites-technologie-internet/2009-04-28/droit-d-auteur-l-ump-versera-30-000-euros-au-groupe-de-rock-mgmt/1387/0/338620|title=L'UMP s'arrange et va verser 30.000 euros au groupe de rock MGMT|date=April 28, 2009|language=fr|access-date=September 16, 2013}}</ref>

==Collaborations==
They have collaborated with rapper [[Kid Cudi]], and are featured on the single "[[Pursuit of Happiness (Kid Cudi song)|Pursuit of Happiness]]" from his album ''[[Man on the Moon: The End of Day]]''. For the 2010 MTV VMA's Kid Cudi was nominated for Best Hip-Hop video for "Pursuit of Happiness", a track which features MGMT and [[Ratatat]]. They also collaborated on the track "Worm Mountain" on [[The Flaming Lips]]' ''[[Embryonic]]''.

On September 4, 2009, [[Beck]] announced his second [[Beck's Record Club|Record Club]] covers album, ''[[Songs of Leonard Cohen]]''. MGMT contributed, alongside [[Devendra Banhart]], Andrew Stockdale of [[Wolfmother]] and Binki Shapiro of [[Little Joy]].

During the production of [[Congratulations (album)|''Congratulations'']] in 2009, VanWyngarden and Goldwasser met with [[Lou Reed]], the former lead singer and guitarist of [[The Velvet Underground]], about contributing vocals to "Lady Dada's Nightmare". VanWyngarden recalls, "he pretty much said it didn't need it. And then he also talked about how we didn't need managers."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8223033/lou-reed-turned-down-mgmt-collaboration|title=Lou Reed Turned Down An Offer to Work with MGMT, Told Them to Fire Their Managers|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-03-02}}</ref>

In 2010, they provided guest vocals on "Call from the Bank" on rapper [[Nipsey Hussle]]'s mixtape ''[[The Marathon (mixtape)|The Marathon]]'', which would be their last collaborative effort for ten years until 2020, when they contributed vocals to "The Divine Chord" and "Solitary Ceremonies" alongside guitar work by [[Johnny Marr]] on electronic group [[The Avalanches]]' third studio album ''[[We Will Always Love You]]''.
