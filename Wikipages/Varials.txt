'''Varials''' is an American [[hardcore punk]] band from [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]], formed in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fearlessrecords.com/artists/varials/Fearless|title=fearless - Fearless Records|website=Fearlessrecords.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> The band currently consists of Lead Vocalist Mitchell Rogers, guitarist James Hohenwarter, bassist Mike Foley and drummer Sean Rauchut.

==History==
The band members, all from Philadelphia or surrounding towns, had been a part of the 2009 music scene in one way or another, realizing this years later after they all met through different means.<ref name="bring">{{cite web|url=http://www.bringthenoise.com/blog/from-childhood-idols-to-metalcore-money-members|title=From Childhood Idols To Metalcore Money, Members of Varials Detail Their Start In Music / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> By 2012, they officially formed Varials. The original lineup consisted of vocalist Jared Pilieri, guitarists James Hohenwarter and Shane Lyons, bassist Mike Foley, and drummer Sean Rauchut. Pilieri was kicked out of the band in 2014, with the band releasing a statement distancing themselves from him, stating "(Pilieri) used his place in this band to manipulate others and we all sincerely apologize to anyone that was affected by his actions"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/VarialsPA/posts/341020599437626|title=Varials|website=Facebook.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> He was replaced by Travis Tabron. Lyons left around 2017 and was replaced by former [[Capture (band)|Capture the Crown]] guitarist Mitchell Rogers.

Varial's first release was the single "Trapped", released on October 7, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://varials.bandcamp.com/track/trapped|title=Trapped, by Varials|website=Varials.bandcamp.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> They released an EP, ''Recollection'' in 2014.<ref name="recol"/> In 2016 they released a split EP, ''Absolution'' with Wilmington, Delaware-based band Vicious Embrace.<ref name="sterokiller"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://new-transcendence.com/review-varialsvicious-embrace-absolution-split-ep2016/|title=REVIEW: Varials/Vicious Embrace â€“ Absolution [SPLIT EP/2016] - New-Transcendence|website=New-transcendence.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punktastic.com/album-reviews/varials-failurecontrol/|title=Varials â€“ 'Failure//Control'|website=Punktastic.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref>

They signed with [[Fearless Records]] on July 6, 2017, and announced their debut album ''[[Pain Again]]'', produced by Josh Schroeder, to be released on August 11.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bringthenoise.com/blog/varials-sign-to-fearless-records-share-heavy-new|title=Varials Sign To Fearless Records, Share Heavy New Single "Anything To Numb" / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2017/07/06/news/varials-sign-fearless-debut-anything-numb-music-video/|title=Varials Sign With Fearless, Debut "Anything To Numb" Music Video|website=Theprp.com|date=6 July 2017|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/varials_sign_to_fearless_records |title=Varials sign to Fearless Records and other news you might have missed today |last=Toney |first=Jordan |date=July 6, 2017 |website=Alt Press |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170713073458/https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/varials_sign_to_fearless_records |archive-date=July 13, 2017 |url-status=dead |access-date=May 30, 2018}}</ref> The lead single, "Anything to Numb" was released alongside a music video that day.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/video/varials-anything-to-numb-is-a-grime-filled-slowburner-of-a-track|title=VARIALS' "Anything To Numb" Is A Grime-Filled Slowburner Of A Track|website=Metalinjection.net|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> The second single, "Pain Again" was released on July 28 with another music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2017/07/28/news/varials-share-new-song-pain/|title=Varials Share New Song "Pain Again"|website=Theprp.com|date=28 July 2017|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> The song "E.D.A." had a music video released on August 10.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2017/08/10/news/varials-premiere-e-d-music-video/|title=Varials Premiere "E.D.A." Music Video |website=Theprp.com|date=10 August 2017|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/video/varials-new-song-e-d-a-is-a-painful-lesson-in-truth|title=VARIALS' New Song 'E.D.A." Is A Painful Lesson In Truth|website=Metalinjection.net|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> The third single "Empire of Dirt"'s music video was released in May 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://themoshzone.com/2018/05/16/varials-burn-it-all-down-in-new-music-video/|title=Varials burn it all down in new music video - The Mosh Zone|date=16 May 2018|website=Themoshzone.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref>

Early on in 2017, the band toured with [[For the Fallen Dreams]] and [[Deadships]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2017/04/10/news/fallen-dreams-varials-deadships-announce-u-s-tour/|title=For The Fallen Dreams, Varials & Deadships Announce U.S. Tour|website=Theprp.com|date=10 April 2017|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> In mid-2017, the band toured alongside [[Wage War]], [[Gideon (band)|Gideon]], [[Oceans Ate Alaska]] and [[Loathe (band)|Loathe]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.equalvision.com/news/gideon-announce-tour-with-wage-war/|title=GIDEON ANNOUNCE TOUR WITH WAGE WAR - Equal Vision Records|website=Equalvision.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> They also toured with [[Kublai Khan (band)|Kublai Khan]] and I Am.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2017/12/14/news/kublai-khan-varials-tour-announced/|title=Kublai Khan, Varials & I Am Tour Announced |website=Theprp.com|date=14 December 2017|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> In 2018, they toured in support of [[Emmure]] alongside [[Counterparts (band)|Counterparts]] and [[King 810]] on the Natural Born Killers tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://shockwavemagazine.com/varials-announce-north-american-tour-supporting-emmure/|title=Varials announce North American tour supporting Emmure - Shockwave Magazine|date=15 March 2018|website=Shockwavemagazine.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> In late-2018, they opened for [[Counterparts (band)|Counterparts]] and [[Being as an Ocean]], alongside [[Have Mercy (band)|Have Mercy]] on the Private Room Tour. Varials also got to play on the Atlantic City date for the 25th anniversary of the Vans Warped Tour.<ref>{{cite web|author=VARIALS |url=https://twitter.com/VarialsPA/status/1143981334811021312 |title=VARIALS on Twitter: "SATURDAY day passes available now " |website=Twitter.com |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> They were able to play both days of the festival due to technical issues they had on the first day of playing.

Varials has gained comparisons to [[Knocked Loose]] by the media, and Knocked Loose vocalist Bryan Garris guested on the Varials song "Empire of Dirt".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mindequalsblown.net/interviews/varials-on-first-album-hardcore-dynamic-and-fake-rivalry-with-knocked-loose|title=Varials Reflects on First Full-Length, Hardcore Dynamic, and Fake Rivalry with Knocked Loose - Mind Equals Blown|website=mindequalsblown.net|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/see-hardcore-upstarts-varials-haunting-pyromaniacal-new-empire-dirt-video|title=See Hardcore Upstarts Varials' Haunting, Pyromaniacal New "Empire of Dirt" Video"|date=10 May 2018|website=Revolvermag.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> Varials also toured with Knocked Loose in December 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2015/09/26/news/knocked-loose-adaliah-varials-tour-set-for-december/|title=Knocked Loose, Adaliah & Varials Tour Set For December |website=Theprp.com|date=26 September 2015|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://nocountryfornewnashville.com/2015/12/04/knocked-loose-w-orthodox-heretic-more-perform-tonight-cafe-coco/|title=Knocked Loose W/ Orthodox, Heretic, & More Perform Tonight @ CafÃ© Coco - No Country For New Nashville|website=Nocountryfornewnashville.com|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> They have also been likened to the hardcore band [[Jesus Piece (band)|Jesus Piece]], who also hail from Philadelphia, as both bands have been regarded as some of the brightest prospects in the genre.

The band released their sophomore album, ''In Darkness'', through Fearless on October 11, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metalmusicarchives.com/album/varials/in-darkness|title=VARIALS In Darkness reviews|date=24 October 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191024014201/https://www.metalmusicarchives.com/album/varials/in-darkness|access-date=25 September 2020|archive-date=2019-10-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/varials-new-track-in-darkness-music-video/|title=Varials drop heavy beatdown track "In Darkness" along with video|date=11 October 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191011132945/https://www.altpress.com/news/varials-new-track-in-darkness-music-video/|access-date=25 September 2020|archive-date=2019-10-11}}</ref> Varials supported Counterparts on their Private Room 2.0 Tour throughout the Fall of 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://lambgoat.com/news/31956/Counterparts-Stray-From-The-Path-Chamber-tour-announced |title=Counterparts, Stray From The Path, Chamber tour announced |website=Lambgoat.com |date=2019-08-26 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> Varials will be heading to Europe to support Polaris on their Death of Me Tour. Varials is also set to support Spite on their Root of all Evil Tour.

On January 23, 2020, vocalist Travis Tabron announced his departure from the band. They will still continue to tour without Travis with Mitchell Rogers taking over lead vocals.<ref>{{cite web|author=travVerified account |url=https://www.twitter.com/strawberryTrav/status/1220407148691230723 |title=trav on Twitter: "I'm no longer a member of @VarialsPA statement below ðŸŒ¹" |website=Twitter.com |date=2020-01-23 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

==Influences==
The band has listed many artists and genres as influences, such as [[Slipknot (band)|Slipknot]],<ref name="bring"/> [[Deftones]], [[Limp Bizkit]], [[Sevendust]], [[System of a Down]], [[Linkin Park]], [[Coheed and Cambria]], [[Chiodos]], [[Rise Against]], [[Keeping Destiny]], [[Rage Against the Machine]], [[Big L]],<ref name="sterokiller">{{cite web|url=http://stereokiller.com/interviews/TravisTabronofVarials32313|title=Interview with Travis Tabron of Varials, 3/23/13 - Stereokiller|website=Stereokiller.com Interviews|access-date=30 May 2018}}</ref> [[nu metal]] music, and [[shoegaze]] music.<ref name="bring"/>
