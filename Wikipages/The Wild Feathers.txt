'''The Wild Feathers''' are an American country rock band formed in 2010 in [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]], United States, by Ricky Young, Joel King, and Taylor Burns, all of whom were lead singers in previous bands. Drummer Ben Dumas joined the band following the release of their debut album in the Summer of 2013.<ref>{{cite web|author=Ed Grisamore |url=https://www.macon.com/news/local/news-columns-blogs/ed-grisamore/article215246170.html|title=Losing a coin flip led this drummer to a life-long passion and career |publisher=[[Macon Telegraph]] |date=2018-07-22 |accessdate=2020-02-05}}</ref> Multi-instrumentalist Brett Moore (formerly a member of [[The Apache Relay|Apache Relay]], a band that had opened for The Wild Feathers) began touring with the band in 2015 and recording with the band for 2018's "Greetings From The Neon Frontier" album. The band's second studio album, ''Lonely Is a Lifetime'', was released March 11, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|author=James Christopher Monger |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-wild-feathers-mn0003101327/biography |title=The Wild Feathers &#124; Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |date= |accessdate=2016-05-13}}</ref> Their third studio album titled "Greetings from the Neon Frontier" was released June 29, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8463071/the-wild-feathers-wildfire-making-of|title=The Wild Feathers Talk Making of 'Wildfire': 'It's Almost a Love Letter to the Band'|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-06-28}}</ref>

== History ==
The Wild Feathers was formed in 2010 by artists Joel King, Ricky Young, Taylor Burns, and [[Preston Wimberly]].<ref name="Reichard">{{Cite web|url=https://madison.com/daily-cardinal/ricky-young-of-the-wild-feathers-talks-about-beginnings-and/article_f6969aa6-961f-11e3-96ce-0019bb2963f4.html|title=Ricky Young of The Wild Feathers talks about beginnings and The Band|last=Reichard|first=Sean|website=madison.com|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> Burns and Wimberly were high school friends, and had previously played together in a band called Noble Dog.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dallasobserver.com/music/the-wild-feathers-grew-up-on-dallas-blues-7051068|title=The Wild Feathers Grew Up on Dallas Blues|last=Dearmore|first=Kelly|date=2014-01-24|website=Dallas Observer|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> The band's four original members all contributed both vocals and guitar to the band.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://relix.com/articles/detail/the_wild_feathers_deja_vu_all_over_again/|title=The Wild Feathers: Déjà Vu All Over Again|date=2016-05-09|website=Relix Media|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> The band was signed by Jeff Sosnow, an [[A&r|A&R]] man at [[Interscope Records]] before playing a single live session or recording any songs. They were dropped by Interscope Records before finishing their first album and signed to [[Warner Records]], where Sosnow had begun working.<ref name=":0" />

The Wild Feathers began touring in 2013, playing hundreds of show with acts like [[Bob Dylan]], [[Willie Nelson]], and [[ZZ Ward]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.guitarworld.com/acoustic-nation/acoustic-nation-wild-feathers-jimmy-kimmel-live-tonight-and-rachael-ray-performance-confirmed-december-10th|title=The Wild Feathers on 'Jimmy Kimmel Live' Tonight and 'Rachael Ray' Performance Confirmed for December 10th|last=Nation 2013-12-02T21:36:44Z|first=Acoustic|website=guitarworld|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> and playing at venues like [[Hangout Music Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.al.com/entertainment/2014/05/things_started_coming_together.html|title=Things Started Coming Together for The Wild Feathers at the 2013 Hangout Festival|last=Oldshue|first=Lynn|date=2014-05-13|website=al|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> Their self titled album, produced by [[Jay Joyce]], was released in the summer of 2013 and charted at #1 on the Billboard Heatseeker's Chart and at #109 on the ''[[Billboard 200]]'', resulting in invites to appear on various television programs, as well as acclaim from major music publications.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://countryfancast.com/country-music-bands-the-wild-feathers/|title=Country Music: Meet The Wild Feathers Band|last=steve|website=countryfancast.com|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> Their single "The Ceiling" peaked at #7 on [[Adult Alternative Songs]] in October 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://outandaboutnashville.com/february-the-month-in-music/|title=February: the Month in Music|last=Brant|first=Joseph|date=2016-02-29|website=Out & About Nashville - LGBT news, events and gay guide|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> Guitarist and vocalist Preston Wimberly left the band in late 2015.

While on tour, the band began working on their second album, ''Lonely Is A Lifetime'', which was released on March 11, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2016/03/25/wild-feathers-soar-70s-style-harmonies/82231774/|title=Wild Feathers soar with '70s-style harmonies|last=Lenahan|first=Jim|website=USA TODAY|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> On June 25, 2016 The Wild Feathers recorded ''Live At The Ryman'', played to a soldout [[Ryman Auditorium]]. ''Live At The Ryman'' celebrated the band's return to Nashville and the release of ''Lonely Is A Lifetime''. Former-member Preston Wimberly made a guest appearance on the track "Left My Woman", from ''Live At The Ryman''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/hear-the-wild-feathers-earnest-left-my-woman-at-the-ryman-105489/|title=Hear the Wild Feathers' Earnest 'Left My Woman' at the Ryman|last=Crawford|first=Robert|date=2016-12-01|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>

On June 29, 2018 The Wild Feathers released their third album ''Greetings from the Neon Frontier'', which was produced with [[Jay Joyce]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://roughstock.com/news/2018/07/43168-album-review-the-wild-feathers-greetings-from-the-neon-frontier|title=Album Review: The Wild Feathers - "Greetings From The Neon Frontier"|last=http://www.roughstock.com|first=Roughstock-|date=2018-07-05|website=RoughStock|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>

On November 20, 2020, the band independently released ''Medium Rarities'', an album featuring 11 previously unreleased songs from the band's 10-year career.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fox |first1=Randy |title=The Wild Feathers Satisfy Old Tastes and New Cravings with Medium Rarities |url=https://www.theeastnashvillian.com/the-wild-feathers-satisfy-old-tastes-and-new-cravings-with-medium-rarities/ |website=The East Nashvillian |access-date=17 May 2021}}</ref> The album includes covers of “Blue” by [[The Jayhawks]], “[[Almost Cut My Hair]],” by [[Crosby, Stills, Nash & Young]], and “[[The Guitar Man]]” by [[Bread (band)]].

== Style and influences ==
The Wild Feathers features the harmonic talents of multiple vocalists, including King, Young, and Burns.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lifeminute.tv/music/video/wild-feathers-bring-back-good-old-rock-roll|title=The Wild Feathers Bring Back 'Good Old Rock & Roll'|website=lifeminute.tv|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> The Wild Feathers have been noted for their blend of [[Southern rock]] and [[Americana (music)|Americana]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musictimes.com/articles/910/20130815/wild-feathers-album-review-nashville-band-break-past-americana-ceiling.htm|title=The Wild Feathers 'The Wild Feathers' Album Review: Can Nashville Band Break Past the Americana 'Ceiling' of Contemporaries Dawes, Houndmouth? [VIDEO]|last=Times|first=Music|date=2013-08-15|website=Music Times|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> [[blues]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nashvillescene.com/music/article/13064597/the-wild-feathers-raise-the-ceiling-at-soldout-ryman-debut|title=The Wild Feathers Raise the Ceiling at Sold-Out Ryman Debut|website=Nashville Scene|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> and [[folk music]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wfuv.org/content/wfuv-sxsw-wild-feathers-hotel-san-jose|title=WFUV at SXSW: The Wild Feathers at Hotel San Jose {{!}} WFUV|website=www.wfuv.org|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> Ricky Young acknowledged the influence of [[The Band]] on the group's sound, but pointed out that there were many other inspirations behind The Wild Feather's sound.<ref name="Reichard"/> The Wild Feathers have cited acts like [[Tom Petty]], [[The Eagles (UK band)|The Eagles]], and [[Otis Redding]] as inspirations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theboot.com/the-wild-feathers-country-music-bio-facts/|title=Who Are the Wild Feathers? 5 Things You Need to Know|last=Liptak|first=Carena|website=The Boot|language=en|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>
