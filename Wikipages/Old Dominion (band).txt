'''Old Dominion''' is an American [[country music]] band formed in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. The band consists of [[Matthew Ramsey]] (lead vocals), [[Trevor Rosen]] (guitar, keyboards), Whit Sellers (drums), Geoff Sprung (bass guitar), and Brad Tursi (guitar). Their music is contemporary country with rock instrumentation, and has pop overtones.

The band has released four full-length albums and three EPs. They released their self-titled EP in 2014, followed in the next year by their debut studio album, ''[[Meat and Candy]]'' on [[RCA Records Nashville]], which includes the singles "[[Break Up with Him]]", "[[Snapback (song)|Snapback]]", and "[[Song for Another Time]]".  Their second album ''[[Happy Endings (Old Dominion album)|Happy Endings]]'' was released in 2017, which includes the singles "[[No Such Thing as a Broken Heart]]", "[[Written in the Sand (song)|Written in the Sand]]", and "[[Hotel Key]]". The third album ''[[Old Dominion (album)|Old Dominion]]'' was released in 2019, with the singles "[[Make It Sweet]]" and "[[One Man Band (Old Dominion song)|One Man Band]]" both successful on the chart. This is followed by ''Time, Tequila & Therapy'', which was released in 2021. 

In addition to their own material, Ramsey, Rosen, and Tursi have written several hit singles for other contemporary country music artists.

==Origin==
The band members of Old Dominion are lead singer [[Matthew Ramsey]], [[Trevor Rosen]] on guitar and keyboard, Whit Sellers on drums, Geoff Sprung on bass, and Brad Tursi on guitar. The band was named Old Dominion in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://heavy.com/entertainment/2017/04/old-dominion-band-members-matthew-ramsey-trevor-rosen-whit-sellers-geoff-sprung-brad-tursi/4/ |title=Old Dominion Band Members: Meet the Group |author= S.M. Walsh  |date=April 2, 2017 |work=heavy.}}</ref>  "[[Colony of Virginia|Old Dominion]]" was chosen because it is a nickname for the state of [[Virginia]], and four members of the group have links to Virginia.<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/brandy-clark-and-old-dominion-laugh-about-haters-and-hot-rollers-20140623 | title=Brandy Clark and Old Dominion Laugh About Haters and Hot Rollers|author= Beville Dunkerley |date= June 23, 2014 |work=Rolling Stone }}</ref>

Ramsey and Sellers are both originally from the [[Roanoke Region]] of [[Virginia]], and played on [[drumline]]s for their respective, rival high schools, [[James River High School (Buchanan, Virginia)|James River High School]] and [[Lord Botetourt High School]].<ref name="Roanoke">{{cite web |url=http://www.roanoke.com/arts_and_entertainment/music/old-dominion-country-band-has-roanoke-valley-roots/article_d98905b4-ea90-11e3-9679-0017a43b2370.html |title=Old Dominion country band has Roanoke Valley roots |author=Tad Dickens |date= June 3, 2014 |work=The Roanoke Times }}</ref> After high school, Ramsey went to [[Virginia Commonwealth University]] and moved to Nashville after graduation to become a songwriter. He was introduced to Rosen in 2003 and formed a songwriting partnership for some years while working as a solo artist.<ref name="shotgun">{{cite web |url=http://theshotgunseat.com/old-dominion-discusses-new-ep-touring-and-band-mascot/ |title=Old Dominion Discusses New EP, Touring, and Band Mascot |author=Laura Spinelli |date= October 15, 2014 |work=The Shotgun Seat  }}</ref> Sellers went to [[James Madison University]] in Virginia where he met Sprung and Tursi.<ref name=about>{{cite web |url=http://olddominionband.com/about |title=About Old Dominion |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151023040427/http://olddominionband.com/about |archive-date=October 23, 2015 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Sellers and Sprung also ended up in Nashville where they joined force with Ramsey and formed Old Dominion. Tursi was once a member of the [[Washington, D.C.]] rock band [[Army of Me (band)|Army of Me]]; he joined Old Dominion in 2012.<ref name=about/> Rosen is the only band member who did not have a connection to Virginia; he was born and raised in [[Michigan]], and gradually met the other eventual bandmates in Nashville.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-new-ep-writers-regret/ |title=Old Dominion Talk Swagger, Their New EP and Writers' Regret |website=Tasteofcountry.com |date= |accessdate=2016-06-16}}</ref>

==Music career==
The band was initially formed to showcase the songs that its individual band members had written.<ref name="billboard 2015"/> Members of the band have writing credits on many songs recorded by other artists: Brad Tursi has written [[Luke Bryan]]'s "[[Light It Up (Luke Bryan song)|Light It Up]]", [[Cole Swindell]]'s "Remember Boys" and "Up" as well as [[Tyler Farr]]'s "[[A Guy Walks Into a Bar]]" in addition to songs for [[Kenny Chesney]], the [[Randy Rogers Band]], [[Michael Ray (singer)|Michael Ray]], [[Josh Turner]], [[Ryan Hurd]], and for the ABC musical drama ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]''.<ref name="rollingstone"/><ref name="ETonline">{{cite web |url=http://www.etonline.com/music/155245_kenny_chesney_taps_old_dominion_for_big_revival_tour/ |title=EXCLUSIVE: Kenny Chesney Taps Buzzy Band Old Dominion for Big Revival Tour|author= Sophie Schillaci |date= December 16, 2014 |work=Entertainment Tonight}}</ref> [[Matthew Ramsey]] co-wrote "[[Chainsaw (The Band Perry song)|Chainsaw]]" for [[The Band Perry]], [[Trevor Rosen]] co-wrote [[Kelsea Ballerini]]'s "[[I Hate Love Songs]]", [[Blake Shelton]]'s "[[Sangria (song)|Sangria]]", [[William Michael Morgan]]'s "[[I Met a Girl (William Michael Morgan song)|I Met A Girl]]", [[Chris Young (musician)|Chris Young]]'s "[[Neon (Chris Young song)|Neon]]" and The Band Perry's "[[Better Dig Two]]", Two songs written for the band by Ramsey and Rosen, "[[Wake Up Lovin' You]]" and "[[Say You Do (Dierks Bentley song)|Say You Do]]", were covered by [[Craig Morgan]] and [[Dierks Bentley]] respectively and became hits.<ref name="billboard 2015">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/6721849/old-dominion-rides-countrys-new-wave |title=Old Dominion Takes an Unusual Path to Country's Top 10 |date=October 7, 2015 |author= Tom Roland |work=Billboard }}</ref> The success of these songs led to the band self-releasing the [[extended play]] ''It Was Always Yours'' in 2012.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://naples.floridaweekly.com/articles/old-dominion-band-started-just-for-kicks/ | title=Old Dominion band started just for kicks | publisher=Florida Weekly | date=November 22, 2018 | accessdate=March 9, 2021}}</ref>

Their song "Dirt on a Road" was their first song as a band that received significant airplay,<ref name="shotgun"/><ref name="rollingstone"/> and their single "Shut Me Up" further gained them some attention.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/new-country-artists-2014-20141010/old-dominion-20141010 |title=10 New Artists You Need to Know: Fall 2014|date=October 10, 2014|work= Rolling Stone }}</ref> The band had opened for [[Alabama (American band)|Alabama]], [[Jake Owen]] and [[Chase Rice]].<ref name="rollingstone2"/>

=== 2014–2016: ''Old Dominion'' EP, '' Meat and Candy ''===
On October 7, 2014, they released their self-titled EP, produced by [[Shane McAnally]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6311898/tomorrows-hits-shakey-graves-leihoku-old-dominion |title=Tomorrow's Hits: Shakey Graves, Leihoku & Old Dominion|author=Wade Jessen |work=Billboard |date= November 7, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-ep-details/ |title=Old Dominion Reveal Debut EP Details |author=Coti Howell |date=September 1, 2014 |work=Taste of Country }}</ref> The EP debuted on the Top Country Albums chart at No.&nbsp;33 with 1,000 copies sold.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2014/10/30032-country-album-chart-recap-october-15-2014/|title=Country Album Chart Recap: October 15, 2014|author= Matt Bjorke |date= October 15, 2014 |work=Roughstock}}</ref> The lead single from the EP was "[[Shut Me Up (Old Dominion song)|Shut Me Up]]",<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-shut-me-up/ |title=Old Dominion, 'Shut Me Up' [Listen] |author= Carrie Horton |work=Taste of Country}}</ref> the music video features American country music singer and songwriter [[Whitney Duncan]].<ref name="rollingstone2">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/watch-old-dominion-get-loud-on-shut-me-up-20150105 |title=Watch Old Dominion Get Loud on 'Shut Me Up'|author= Beville Dunkerley | date=January 5, 2015 |work= Rolling Stone}}</ref> A song from the EP "[[Break Up with Him]]" first received exposure on the satellite [[Sirius XM Radio]]'s "[[The Highway (Sirius XM)|The Highway]]" channel in late 2014 which boosted its popularity, and the song was then released as the second single to radio on January 20, 2015.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2015/01/32183-old-dominion-releases-break-radio |title=Old Dominion Releases "Break Up With Him" To Radio|author= Matt Bjorke |date= January 20, 2015 |work=Roughstock}}</ref>

They signed a record deal with [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]] in late February 2015.<ref>{{citation |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-rca-nashville/ |title=Old Dominion Sign With And Good Timing |website=Billboard.com |access-date=March 10, 2017}}</ref>  It reached at number 2 on the Country Airplay in June 2016.<ref name="Country Airplay" />  The album's third single, "[[Song ===2017–present: ''Happy Endings'', ''Old Dominion'' and ''Time, Tequila & Therapy''===
On March 10, 2017, Old Dominion released "[[No Such Thing as a Broken Heart]]", the lead single to their second RCA album, which went to number one on the Country Airplay Charts.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/listen-to-old-dominions-lighthearted-new-track-no-such-thing-as-a-broken-heart/|title=Listen to Old Dominion's Lighthearted New Track, 'No Such Thing as a Broken Heart'|date=March 10, 2017|work=Sounds Like Nashville|access-date=March 10, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> The album's name, ''Happy Endings'', was announced in June, and the album was released on August 25, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|author=Annie Reuter |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-happy-endings-album/ |title=Old Dominion Announce Sophomore Album, 'Happy Endings' |publisher=Tasteofcountry.com |date=June 8, 2017 |access-date=June 9, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Liv Stecker |url=http://theboot.com/old-dominion-happy-endings/ |title=Old Dominion Share Plans for Sophomore Album, 'Happy Endings' |publisher=Theboot.com |date=June 7, 2017 |access-date=June 9, 2017}}</ref> "Written in the Sand" was the album's second single, and "Hotel Key" its third.<ref name="Country Airplay"/>

The band released "[[Make It Sweet]]" in November 2018. Make It Sweet is also the name of the corresponding tour, the band's first as a headliner. Acts joining them on this tour include [[Morgan Evans (singer)|Morgan Evans]], [[Jordan Davis (singer)|Jordan Davis]], and [[Mitchell Tenpenny]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/old-dominion-new-so | title=Old Dominion Share Breezy New Song 'Make It Sweet,' Plot Headlining Tour | work=Rolling Stone Country | access-date=February 23, 2019}}{{dead link|date=August 2021|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> Both "Make It Sweet" and "[[One Man Band (Old Dominion song)|One Man Band]]" appear on the band's third album, ''[[Old Dominion (album)|Old Dominion]]'', released in October 2019, with the latter becoming their highest-peaking [[Billboard Hot 100]] hit to date.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-2019-new-album-details/|title=Old Dominion Releasing New Album in October|first=Cillea|last=Houghton|website=Taste of Country}}</ref> However, the album's third single "[[Some People Do]]" became their first single since their commercial breakthrough to fail to chart the Billboard Hot 100, as well as their first single to peak outside the top twenty of Billboard's Country Airplay chart.<ref name="Country Airplay"/>

The fourth album, ''Time, Tequila & Therapy'', was released on October 8, 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://musicrow.com/2021/08/old-dominion-return-with-new-album-time-tequila-therapy/|title=Old Dominion Return With New Album, 'Time, Tequila & Therapy'|date=August 20, 2021|first= Lydia|last= Farthing|work=MusicRow}}</ref>
