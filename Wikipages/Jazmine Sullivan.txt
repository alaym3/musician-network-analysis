'''Jazmine Marie Sullivan''' (born April 9, 1987) is an American singer-songwriter. Born and raised in [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]], her debut album, ''[[Fearless (Jazmine Sullivan album)|Fearless]]'', was released in 2008 to commercial and critical success. The record topped [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']]'s [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart and was [[RIAA certification|certified gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). It spawned four singles, including "[[Need U Bad]]" and "[[Bust Your Windows]]", both of which peaked in the top 40 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart and the former of which became Sullivan's first and only number one on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|''Billboard'' Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.

Sullivan followed this with her second studio album, ''[[Love Me Back (album)|Love Me Back]]'', in 2010, which was received favorably by critics. After taking a three-year break, Sullivan signed with [[RCA Records]] and released her first studio album under the label, ''[[Reality Show (album)|Reality Show]]'', in 2015, and it became her second album to peak at number one on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. In 2021, she released her first EP, ''[[Heaux Tales]]''.

Sullivan has received one [[Billboard Women in Music|''Billboard'' Women in Music]] Award and two [[BET Awards]] over the course of her career. She has also received 15 [[Grammy Awards|Grammy]] nominations, tying with BjÃ¶rk as [[Grammy Award records#Most nominations without winning|the female artist with the most Grammy nominations without winning]].

=== 1987â€“2006: Early life ===
Jazmine Sullivan was born on April 9, 1987 to Don and Pam Sullivan.<ref name="birthcharts">{{cite web|url=https://birthcharts.astro-seek.com/birth-chart/jazmine-sullivan-horoscope|title=Jazmine Sullivan Birth Chart Horoscope, Date of Birth, Astro|website=birthcharts.astro-seek.com}}</ref> Her mother is a former backup singer for [[Philadelphia International Records]].<ref name="Ellis">{{cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/photos/feature-the-psychology-of-jazmine-sullivan|title=The Psychology of Jazmine Sullivan|last=Ellis|first=Stacy-Ann|date=January 13, 2015|website=Vibe}}</ref> When she was five years old, her father landed a position as a curator for the city's [[Historic Strawberry Mansion]] in the [[Strawberry Mansion, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania|Strawberry Mansion]] section, and her family moved into the historical landmark.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hSYEAAAAMBAJ&q=jazmine+sullivan+her+father+landed+a+position+as+a+curator&pg=PA54|title=Vibe|last=Group|first=Vibe Media|date=July 1, 2008|publisher=Vibe Media Group}}</ref>

==Musical style and artistry==
Sonically, Sullivan's voice alternates between "modern productions" and a "1980s-influenced sound" which music critics say gives her "[[old-school hip hop]] sound".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blackmusicscholar.com/jazmine-sullivan/|title=Jazmine Sullivan|date=November 3, 2017|website=Black Music Scholar}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last=Williams|first=Justin A.|title=The Construction of Jazz Rap as High Art in Hip-Hop Music|date=July 5, 2017|journal=From Soul to Hip Hop|pages=149â€“173|publisher=Routledge|isbn=9781315093819|doi=10.4324/9781315093819-6}}</ref> Her voice type is [[contralto]].<ref>{{cite news| url = https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2015/04/21/sullivan-shares-her-stirring-reality-royale/WOp1QlCXgmbzkzi259IgLL/story.html| title = Jazmine Sullivan shares her stirring 'Reality' at Royale| work=[[The Boston Globe]]| date=April 21, 2015| access-date=March 15, 2018| first=Maura| last=Johnston}}</ref> Her voice has a solid agility in all registers and is well connected between an [[A (musical note)|A2]] and a [[G (musical note)|G5]].{{citation needed|date=May 2019}} [[InStyle]] said that she has a "raspy voice".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instyle.com/news/jazmine-sullivan-interview-raspy-voice|title=Jazmine Sullivan on Her Famously Raspy Voice and Wanting to Sound Like Mariah Carey|date=July 15, 2015|website=InStyle.com|language=en}}</ref> She describes her writing style as "flashbacks", in reference to her songs about failed relationships that were both physically and emotionally abusive.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/1016-jazmine-sullivan-on-songwritings-labor-of-love/|title=Jazmine Sullivan on Songwriting's Labor of Love|date=February 9, 2016|website=Pitchfork|access-date=May 1, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.essence.com/news/jazmine-sullivan-opens-about-finding-joy-after-abuse/|title=Jazmine Sullivan Opens Up About Finding Joy After Abuse|website=Essence|language=en-US|access-date=August 15, 2017}}</ref> Her music displays her responses to these relationships famously with her song "[[Bust Your Windows]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2016/08/04/apparently-jazmine-sullivans-bust-windows-wasnt-poppin-song-3517d33cc0|title=Apparently Jazmine Sullivan's "Bust Your Windows" wasn't just a poppin' song|date=August 4, 2016|website=REVOLT TV|language=en}}</ref> She likes to write about how she is feeling, which in turn, taking a day or up to a month to complete.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newsok.com/article/feed/1212142/jazmine-sullivan-to-beyonce-lets-make-lemonade-together/|title=Jazmine Sullivan to Beyonce: Let's make lemonade together!|date=April 28, 2017|website=NewsOK.com}}</ref> During the recording of the album [[Reality Show (album)|Reality Show]], Sullivan spent so much time revising and re-recording, that the producers had to force her to release the album to prevent a delayed release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.greensboro.com/entertainment/jazmine-sullivan-grammy-nods-and-getting-her-groove-back/article_fc0a41c7-67da-5ca7-ab52-3097b192e53a.html|title=Jazmine Sullivan: Grammy nods and getting her groove back|last=Press|first=Melanie J. Sims The Associated|website=Greensboro News and Record|access-date=February 4, 2016}}</ref> Sullivan is well known for writing her own songs which amplifies her universal critical acclaim from critics and fans.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2016/02/toughest-song-i-wrote-jazmine-sullivan-brand-new.html|title=The Toughest Song I Wrote: Jazmine Sullivan on What Happens When a Kendrick Feature Falls Through|date=February 8, 2016|website=Vulture.com|access-date=February 14, 2016}}</ref>

Sullivan's song "[[Bust Your Windows]]" was listed at number 137 on [[National Public Radio]]'s list of The 200 Greatest Songs By 21st Century Women.<ref name="npr.org">{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/07/30/627396669/turning-the-tables-the-200-greatest-songs-by-21st-century-women-part-4|title=The 200 Greatest Songs By 21st Century Women+ (140-121)|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=October 12, 2018}}</ref>
