'''Zachary Stephen Williams''' (born March 5, 1981) is an American [[Christian rock]] artist from [[Jonesboro, Arkansas]]. From 2007 through 2012 he was a member and lead vocalist of [[Zach Williams & The Reformation]] (also acoustic guitar and harmonica). He was also the lead singer of the Christian group Brothers of Grace, renamed as Zach Williams and the Brothers of Grace. In 2016 he went solo.

==Beginnings==
{{Expand section|more details|date=August 2019}}
Zachary Stephen Williams was born in [[Pensacola, Florida]] on March 5, 1981 but grew up in [[Jonesboro, Arkansas]].<ref name="BMI">{{cite web|title=Songwriter/Composer: Williams Zachary Stephen|author=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]]|publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]]|access-date=November 15, 2016 |url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/writer.asp?fromrow=1&torow=25&keyname=WILLIAMS%20ZACHARY%20STEPHEN&querytype=WriterID&keyid=1387314&page=1&blnWriter=True&blnPublisher=True&blnArtist=True&affiliation=BMI&cae=538306844&term=}}</ref>

==Music career==
===Zach Williams & The Reformation===
He was the lead singer in [[Zach Williams & The Reformation]],<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web|title=Zach Williams : Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/zach-williams-mn0001758896/biography|first=Stephen Thomas|last=Erlewine|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 15, 2016}}</ref> an American rock band formed in [[Jonesboro, Arkansas]] in 2007 by the group of Zach Williams (acoustic guitar, harmonica and vocals), Red Dorton (bass guitar and vocals), Robby Rigsbee (slide guitar and rhythm guitar), Josh Copeland (lead guitar, rhythm guitar and vocals) and Evan Wilons (drums). The band took their name from the desire to reform or revitalize the southern rock sound. The band released two independent albums, ''Electric Revival'' in 2009 and ''A Southern Offering'' in 2011. In 2012, Williams disbanded the Reformation due to his newfound Christian beliefs.

===The Brothers of Grace / Zach Williams and the Brothers of Grace===
After Zach Williams and the Reformation disbanded, Williams and guitarist Robby Rigsbee dedicated their lives to Christ and began playing music in their church. Church band the Brothers of Grace became Zach Williams and the Brothers of Grace. Shaun Kirby, who had previously been part of southern rock band Further Down with Rigsbee, was the drummer. The group also included Bruce Ford (bass) and Kevin Rouse (keyboard). They independently released an EP, "Shine a Light," in 2014.

===Solo career===
In 2016 Williams signed to [[Essential Records (Christian)|Provident Label Group - Essential Records]] as a solo artist,<ref>{{cite web|title=Provident Label Group, Essential Records Sign Zach Williams|date=May 3, 2016|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2016/05/provident-label-group-essential-records-sign-zach-williams/|publisher=MusicRow.com}}</ref> four years after Zach Williams & The Reformation disbanded.  He released his first solo single, "Chain Breaker" in 2016<ref>{{cite web|title=From Mainstream Rock To Gospel: Zach Williams Comes Clean With New Single, Chain Breaker|date=May 5, 2016|url=http://www.breathecast.com/articles/from-mainstream-rock-to-gospel-zach-williams-comes-clean-with-new-single-chain-breaker-33886/|publisher=Breathecast.com}}</ref> which reached No. 1 on the Hot Christian Songs Chart and the Top 10 on Christian radio.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zach Williams Chart History|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7399510/zach-williams/chart|publisher=Billboard}}</ref> Following the single, Williams released a five-song EP, ''Chain Breaker'', with producers Jonathan Smith and [[Colby Wedgeworth]].{{citation needed|date=August 2017}} Williams also formerly led worship at the Refuge Campus of Central Baptist Church in Jonesboro, Arkansas, where he was  on staff from 2014–2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zach Williams: Rocker With Religion|url=http://www.kait8.com/story/31867692/zach-williams-rocker-with-religion|publisher=KAIT 8}}</ref>

On December 14, 2016, Williams digitally released his full-length album, ''Chain Breaker'', and announced a release date of January 27, 2017 for the CD.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2016/12/19.Surprise!GrammyNominatedZachWilliamsDropsNewAlbum.asp|title=JFH News: SURPRISE! GRAMMY NOMINATED ZACH WILLIAMS DROPS NEW ALBUM|website=www.jesusfreakhideout.com}}</ref>

On October 4, 2019, Williams released his second album, ''[[Rescue Story]]''. It includes the single, "[[There Was Jesus]]", with [[Dolly Parton]].
