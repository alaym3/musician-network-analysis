'''Maurice Samuel Young''' (born September 27, 1974), better known by his stage name '''Trick Daddy''', is an American [[rapper]] from Miami's [[Liberty City (Miami)|Liberty City]].<ref name="mtv.com"/>

==Early life==
Trick Daddy was born on September 27, 1974, at [[Jackson Memorial Hospital]] in [[Miami, Florida]]. His mother, Pearl Brockington of [[South Carolina]], raised him and his 10 brothers and sisters in the [[Liberty Square (Miami)|Liberty Square]] apartment complex in [[Liberty City (Miami)|Liberty City]] after relocating from Goulds, Florida. The building where he grew up was colloquially known as "Pork-N-Beans" in reference to the color of the apartment doors.<ref name="mtv.com"/> His father Charles Young was a local pimp and would take Trick and his brother Derek "Hollywood" Harris, to live with him when he was a teenager. While he lived with his father, they began to deal [[crack cocaine]] on the streets.<ref name="mtv.com"/> At 15 years old, Young was arrested for drug and firearm possession. The day he was released, Young was arrested on an attempted-murder charge for shooting a man during a street fight. He was convicted and sent to prison at 20 years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/514521/trick-daddy-scores-with-jailhouse-rap/|title=Trick Daddy Scores With Jailhouse Rap|website=Mtv.com|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref> His brother was murdered the same year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.miaminewtimes.com/news/ghetto-glorious-6358453|title=Ghetto Glorious|first=Tristram|last=Korten|date=26 August 1999|website=Miaminewtimes.com|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref> After he was released from prison, Ted Lucas, founder of [[Slip-N-Slide Records]], urged Young to focus on a rap career and stay out of the streets.

==Music career==
Trick appeared on the track "Scarred" by former [[2 Live Crew]] member [[Luther Campbell|Luther "Luke" Campbell]], produced by Darren "DJ Spin" Rudnick, from Luke's 1996 album ''[[Uncle Luke (album)|Uncle Luke]]''. The song became a hit and immediately caught the attention of fans and record producers alike. Ted Lucas, a former concert promoter and then-CEO of Slip-n-Slide Records, signed the rapper to the newly formed record label. Slip-n-Slide released Trick Daddy Dollars' debut album ''[[Based on a True Story (Trick Daddy album)|Based on a True Story]]'' in 1997; the album was popular in the Miami area.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Birchmeier|first=Jason|title=Trick Daddy : Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p413655|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=allmusic|date=2006-12-19|access-date=2008-09-11}}</ref>

In 1998, when his next album ''[[www.thug.com]]'' was released, Trick removed "Dollars" from his stage name. Club-oriented track "[[Nann Nigga]]", featuring [[Trina]], became a national hit, peaking at the third spot of the ''Billboard'' Hot Rap Singles chart.<ref name="Singles charts">{{cite web|title=Trick Daddy - Billboard Singles|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p413655|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=allmusic|access-date=2008-09-11}}</ref> [[Atlantic Records]] signed Trick Daddy to the label in 2000 and released ''[[Book of Thugs|Book of Thugs: Chapter AK Verse 47]]'' that year. "Shut Up", which Jason Birchmeier of [[allmusic]] considered Trick's "rowdiest club hit yet",<ref name="allmusic"/> it was the lead single from ''Book of Thugs''. His next single, "America" (featuring Society), was a poignant political track that writer Omar Burgess compared to other sociological tracks in Trick's catalog such as "Ain't No Santa".<ref>{{cite news|last=Burgess|first=Omar|title=Trick Daddy: Thug Life Trick Daddy: Thug Life|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.1140/title.trick-daddy-thug-life|publisher=Hip Hop DX|date=2007-04-27|access-date=2013-07-04}}</ref>

''[[Thugs Are Us]]'', released in 2001, featured the hit single "I'm a Thug" (released March 20, 2001) which reached #17 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name="Singles charts"/> His fifth album ''[[Thug Holiday]]'' boasted "In da Wind", which Birchmeier believed was Trick Daddy's most creative single.<ref name="allmusic"/> In 2004, ''[[Thug Matrimony: Married To The Streets]]'' was released, with hit single "[[Let's Go (Trick Daddy song)|Let's Go]]", produced by [[Lil Jon]], featuring [[Twista]], and sampling the guitar riffs from the [[Ozzy Osbourne]] hit "[[Crazy Train]]".<ref name="allmusic"/> That year, Trick Daddy guest-performed on the [[Ying Yang Twins]]' "What's Happnin!", which reached #30 on the Hot 100, #7 on the Rhythmic Top 40, and #9 on the Hot Rap Singles.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ying Yang Twins - Billboard Singles|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p392373|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=allmusic|access-date=2008-09-11}}</ref> "Let's Go" peaked on #7 on the Hot 100, #4 on the Rhythmic Top 40, and #4 on Hot Rap Tracks.<ref name="Singles charts"/> ''[[Back By Thug Demand]]'' followed in 2006,<ref name="allmusic"/> with singles "Bet That" and "Tuck Ya Ice" charting on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Singles & Tracks chart.<ref name="Singles charts"/> This album launched the career of Trick Daddy's Group, [[Dunk Ryders]] with their record "Damn Right" featuring [[Dray Skky]], and Trick's Joint Venture Label Deal with Cash Money Records.

Following ''Back By Thug Demand'', Trick Daddy appeared on several singles by [[DJ Khaled]]: "Born-N-Raised" in 2006 from ''[[Listennn... the Album]]'' in 2006, "[[I'm So Hood]]" from ''[[We the Best]]'' in 2007, and "Out Here Grindin'" from ''[[We Global]]'' in 2008, all among numerous other rappers. "Born-N-Raised" peaked at #83 on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Singles, and "I'm So Hood" peaked at #19 on the Hot 100 and #5 on the Hot Rap Tracks.<ref>{{cite web|title=DJ Khaled - Billboard Singles|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p672134|pure_url=yes}} |publisher=allmusic|access-date=2008-09-11}}</ref> He appeared on [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]]'s 2007 album ''[[The Boatlift]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last=Rodriguez|first=Jayson|title=Lil Jon, Trick Daddy Among Boatload Of Guests On Pitbull's Boatlift|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1558115/20070426/pitbull_2_.jhtml|publisher=MTV News|date=2007-04-27|access-date=2008-09-11}}</ref> Trick Daddy left Slip-n-Slide in 2008 and released his 8th studio album ''Finally Famous: Born a Thug Still a Thug'' on September 25, 2009, under his own Dunk Ryder Records label, which by now had grown to include fellow Miami artists Ice "Billion" Berg, Fella, Bad Guy, Kasino, A-Dot, NoLove, Dutch Dirty and Gold Rush, most of which eventually parted ways with the label to start their own independent ventures. He released a single featuring [[Young Jeezy]], called "I Can't Leave These Streets Alone", and then was featured on the track "This Is For You", from Jeezy's latest album, ''[[Thug Motivation 103: Hustlerz Ambition]]'', released in December 2011. In July 2012, Trick Daddy released a mixtape entitled ''Dick & Dynamite'' as a prequel to his upcoming album release. He continues to tour the United States, performing a mix of both his classic hits and his new releases.

==Film career==
In 2010, Trick Daddy appeared in the film ''Just Another Day'' playing Roman, a drug dealer. The film is about the intersecting lives of an aspiring rapper and an old successful rapper over the course of one day.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/trick-daddy-ja-rule-star-in-film-about-music-industry/?trackback=tsmclip |title=Trick Daddy, Ja Rule Star in Film About Music Industry |publisher=Theboombox.com |access-date=2014-06-25}}</ref> In 2018 Trick Daddy started appearing in the [[VH1]] reality series ''[[Love & Hip Hop: Miami]]''. In 2017, he started his podcast co-hosted by [[Supa cindy]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.prweb.com/releases/2017/03/prweb14105754.htm|title=Trick Daddy introduces his new podcast 'Famous & Uncensored.' Co-hosted by Supa Cindy of 99 JAMZ, the duo (Trick Daddy and Supa Cindy) sit down with some of the most intriguing hip-hop artists, sports figures, and more.}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
In 1993, Trick Daddy was convicted of cocaine possession, carrying a concealed weapon and violating probation and served 15 months in Florida state prison. Then in February 2003, he was arrested on [[gun charges]] following a dispute at a basketball game, and again in September 2003 after police found [[cocaine]] and [[marijuana]] in his pocket. He pleaded guilty to cocaine and weapons charges and received probation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6039538/trick-daddy-arrested-on-cocaine-gun-charges |title=Trick Daddy Arrested on Cocaine, Gun Charges |publisher=Billboard |date=2014-04-04 |access-date=2014-06-25}}</ref>

On April 3, 2014, he was arrested outside of his home in [[Miramar, Florida]]. According to his arrest report, he was caught by police who were surveying his home under the suspicion that he was running a grow house. Once police entered his home with a warrant and drug sniffing dogs, they reportedly found one gram of cocaine in Trick Daddy's bedroom, along with a 9mm pistol and ammunition in another bedroom. Police charged Young with possession of cocaine, possession of a firearm and ammunition by a convicted felon, and driving with a suspended license. The following morning, he was released on $6,100 bond.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/04/trick-daddy-arrested-florida-guns-drugs/ |title=Trick Daddy Arrested in Florida After Caught With Guns And Drugs - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2014-04-04 |access-date=2014-06-25}}</ref>

On January 11, 2020, Trick Daddy was pulled over in Miami by the police because they suspected he was drinking while driving. After officers smelled alcohol on his breath and he failed a field sobriety test, Trick Daddy was taken into custody at the Hammocks District Station for driving under the influence. While handing over his possessions, police found cocaine wrapped inside a dollar bill. At that point, he was also charged with cocaine possession. His bond was set at $6,000 for both charges.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/crime/article239194498.html|title=Miami rapper Trick Daddy arrested on DUI and cocaine possession charges|last=Cohen|first=Howard|date=11 January 2020|work=The Miami Herald|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Trick Daddy announced that he has [[lupus erythematosus|lupus]], which has affected his skin, he also stopped taking medication to combat the disease.<ref name="Trick Daddy Refuses Treatment For Lupus ">{{cite web|last=Arnold|first=Paul|title=Trick Daddy Refuses Treatment For Lupus|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.8832/title.trick-daddy-refuses-treatment-for-lupus|website=Hiphopdx.com|date=2009-03-30|access-date=2009-03-30}}</ref> The rapper's rationale for refusing treatment was that "Because for every medicine they gave me I had to take a test or another medicine every thirty days or so to make sure that medicine wasn’t causing side effects dealing with kidney or liver failure…I just said all together I ain’t taking no medicine."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idiomag.com/peek/73059/trick_daddy|title=Trick Daddy Refuses Treatment For Lupus|access-date=2009-04-01|date=2009-03-31|publisher=[[idiomag]]}}</ref> Trick Daddy has more recently faced financial difficulties, having declared bankruptcy three times, the latest of which came on the day his home was to be foreclosed upon and auctioned away.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopwired.com/544056/hat-trick-trick-daddy-files-3rd-bankruptcy-just-foreclosure/|title=Hat Trick: Trick Daddy Files For 3rd Bankruptcy Just Before Foreclosure|website=Hiphopwired.com|date=21 May 2017|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref> Trick Daddy's ex-wife Joy filed for a divorce from him, as she revealed on part 2 of the [[Love & Hip Hop: Miami]] [[Love & Hip Hop: Miami (season 2)|Season 2]] reunion.
