'''THEY.''' is an American [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] duo consisting of Dante Jones and Andrew "Drew Love" Neely from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] signed to Avant Garden/Island Records. The duo started their career in 2015, releasing debut extended play, ''N√º Religion'', which received critical acclaim. In the same year THEY. released the commercially successful single "Working for It" in collaboration with [[Zhu (musician)|Zhu]] and [[Skrillex]]. In 2016, the duo started touring with singer [[Bryson Tiller]]. In February 2017, the duo released their debut studio album, ''N√º Religion: Hyena''. In 2020, THEY. signed to Avant Garden Records under Island Records and released their second full-length, ''The Amanda Tape'', on October 23, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=THEY. Share New Single "Count Me In"|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/6/they-count-me-in-single-stream-lyric-video|access-date=2020-07-24|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Twitter|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/unofficialTHEY/status/1295787265398980608|access-date=2020-08-30|website=mobile.twitter.com}}</ref>

==Career==
===2015: Formation and ''N√º Religion''===
Dante Jones was born in [[Denver]], [[Colorado]], while Andrew Neely was born in [[Washington, D.C.]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/07/they-interview-the-break/ | title=The Break Presents: They. | work=XXL | date=July 8, 2016 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> The duo was formed in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://onsmash.com/music/they-nu-religion-ep-stream/ | title=THEY. ‚Äì N√º Religion [EP Stream] | work=[[OnSMASH]] | date=October 14, 2015 | access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> On October 13, 2015, the duo released their debut extended play, ''N√º Religion'', through Mind of a Genius Records and [[Warner Bros. Records]].<ref name="NR">{{cite web | url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/nu-religion-single/id1120698416 | title=N√º Religion - Single by THEY. | work=[[iTunes]] | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> The project, which includes three songs in its track listing, received favorable reviews from [[Music journalism|music critics]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.vibe.com/2015/10/they-nu-religion-ep-mp3/ | title=Thank Us Later: Stream THEY‚Äôs ‚ÄòN√º Religion‚Äô EP | work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]] | date=October 19, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref><ref name="bet">{{cite web | url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2015/10/14/they-nu-religion-ep.html | title=This New Duo Out of L.A. Just Got a Major Co-Sign By Ebro | work=[[BET]] | date=October 14, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2015/10/they-ep-nu-religion/ | title=Stream They.‚Äôs Debut EP | work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] | date=October 15, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> Additionally, the duo began gaining support from record producer [[Timbaland]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.lifedaily.com/we-now-know-why-timbaland-passed-on-bryson-tiller-he-found-they/ | title=We Now Know Why Timbaland Passed On Bryson Tiller: He Found "THEY." | work=LifeDaily | date=October 16, 2015 | access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> While talking about the project in an interview with ''[[The Fader]]'' magazine, Drew said; "''N√º Religion'' is a new way of thinking. As individuals the way we have approached life and the art of music has always been left of center; even from youth. It's been all about finding a way to incorporate that into a cohesive musical project that we are proud of and that people enjoy. We think we've done that."<ref name="thefader">{{cite web | url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/10/13/they-nu-religion-ep | title=Stream THEY.‚Äôs Debut EP, Nu Religion | work=[[The Fader]] | date=October 13, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> On October 22, 2015, "Working for It", a collaboration single by THEY., [[Zhu (musician)|Zhu]] and [[Skrillex]], was released.<ref name="WFI">{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-zhu-and-skrillexs-edgy-collaboration-working-for-it-20151022 | title=Hear Zhu and Skrillex's Edgy Collaboration, 'Working for It' | work=[[Rolling Stone]] | date=October 22, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> The song peaked at number 30 in Australia and number 71 in the United Kingdom.<ref name="WFIAUS" /><ref name="WFIUK" /> The song appeared in Zhu's EP, ''Genesis Series'' and debut studio album, ''[[Generationwhy]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/genesis-series-ep/id1054512444 | title=Genesis Series - EP by ZHU | work=iTunes | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/22216-generationwhy/ | title=Zhu: Generationwhy Album Review | work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] | date=August 9, 2016 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref>

===2016‚Äì2018: ''N√º Religion: Hyena''===
In early 2016, the duo joined singer [[Bryson Tiller]] as opening act for his [[Trapsoul|Trapsoul Tour]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/bryson-tiller-announces-trap-soul-tour-news.19217.html | title=Bryson Tiller Announces "Trap Soul" Tour | work=HotNewHipHop | date=December 16, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> In summer 2016, the duo released the singles, "Say When" and "Deep End",<ref name="SW" /><ref name="DE" /> both of which managed to enter the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Twitter Emerging Artists chart.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7522663/they/chart | title=They. - Chart history | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> On August 8, 2016, THEY. was named "New band of the week" by ''[[The Guardian]]'' newspaper.<ref name="theguardian">{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/aug/08/new-band-of-the-week-they-no-114 | title=New band of the week: THEY. (No 114) ‚Äì meditative rock for the Drake generation | work=[[The Guardian]] | date=August 8, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> In October 2016, the duo was included in ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' magazine's "Best New Musicians in 2016 Across America" list.<ref name="complex">{{cite web | url=http://www.complex.com/r/best-new-musicians/they | title=Best New Musicians - THEY. - Los Angeles - R&B | work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] | date=October 5, 2015 | access-date=October 8, 2016}}</ref> On February 24, 2017, the duo released their debut studio album, ''N√º Religion: Hyena''.<ref name="Album" /> Following the release of their album THEY. solidified their breakthrough year<ref>{{Cite web|last=Howard|first=Lindsay|title=THEY.'s collaborative new 'Fireside' EP has arrived|url=https://variancemagazine.com/sounds/9875-they-fireside-ep|access-date=2020-07-24|website=Variance Magazine|language=en-gb}}</ref> by performing at the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]].

=== 2018‚Äì2019: ''Fireside EP'' ===
In 2018, the duo also joined Grammy-nominated artist [[6lack|6LACK]] as an opening act for his From East Atlanta with Love tour. THEY. also released their collaborative [[Extended play|EP]] Fireside<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reed|first=Ryan|date=2018-11-01|title=Jessie Reyez, THEY. Unite for Breezy New Song 'Broken'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jessie-reyez-they-new-song-broken-750218/|access-date=2020-07-24|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> in 2018, which features [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Jessie Reyez]], [[Jeremih]], [[Vic Mensa]], [[Ty Dolla $ign|Ty Dolla Sign]], and Gallant. This project was influenced by 90s R&B, [[soul music]], [[trap music]], and a recent genre of hip hop known as [[emo rap]], in which the instrumental is guitar-driven.

=== 2020‚Äìpresent: ''The Amanda Tape'' ===
THEY. signed to Avant Garden Records<ref>{{Cite web|title=Avant Garden|url=https://avantgardenrecords.com/|access-date=2020-07-24|website=Avant Garden}}</ref> and in June 2020, the duo returned for the release of their new single "Count Me In" from their forthcoming album, ''The Amanda Tape'', marking their first official release of 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Premiere: THEY. Return With New Song "Count Me In"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/06/they-count-me-in|access-date=2020-07-24|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref> THEY. and [[Tinashe]] linked up in July for the throwback R&B jam "Play Fight" as the second single from ''The Amanda Tape''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=THEY. and Tinashe link up for the throwback R&amp;B jam "Play Fight"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/07/15/they-tinashe-play-fight-the-amanda-tape-premiere-stream|access-date=2020-07-24|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref>

==Influences==
The duo is influenced by artists like [[Taking Back Sunday]], [[New Edition]], [[Babyface (musician)|Babyface]] and [[Kurt Cobain]].<ref name="thefader" />

Their 2018 [[extended play|EP]] ''Fireside'', which features [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Jessie Reyez]], [[Jeremih]], [[Vic Mensa]], [[Ty Dolla $ign|Ty Dolla Sign]], and [[Gallant (singer)|Gallant]] has been influenced by 90s R&B, [[soul music]], [[trap music]], and a recent genre of hip hop known as [[emo rap]], in which the instrumental is guitar-driven.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/they-lays-down-heat-on-fireside-featuring-wiz-khalifa-ty-dolla-sign-and-more-new-mixtape.118604.html|title=THEY.
