'''Cameron Jibril Thomaz''' (born September 8, 1987), better known by his stage name '''Wiz Khalifa''', is an American rapper and singer. He released his debut album, ''[[Show and Prove]]'', in 2006, and signed to [[Warner Records|Warner Bros. Records]] in 2007. His [[Eurodance]]-influenced single "[[Say Yeah (Wiz Khalifa song)|Say Yeah]]" received [[urban radio]] airplay, charting on the [[Rhythmic Top 40]] and [[Hot Rap Tracks]] charts in 2008, becoming his first minor hit.<ref name="ErieTimesNews"/>

Thomaz parted with Warner Bros. and released his second album, ''[[Deal or No Deal (album)|Deal or No Deal]]'', in November 2009. He released the mixtape ''[[Kush and Orange Juice]]'' as a free download in April 2010; he then signed with [[Atlantic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bostonherald.com/entertainment/music/general/view.bg?articleid=1272586&srvc=home&position=also|title=Rising rap Wiz kid's got the right mix|publisher=Boston Herald('the edge' Boston Herald.Com)|date=August 6, 2010|access-date=August 8, 2010 }}</ref> Thomaz then adopted a more radio-friendly, pop-oriented approach with his debut single for Atlantic, "[[Black and Yellow]]". The song, a tribute to his hometown of [[Pittsburgh]], debuted at number 100 and eventually peaked at number 1 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The song became the lead single for his third album [[Rolling Papers (Wiz Khalifa album)|''Rolling Papers'']], which was released in March 2011, and also had the [[Top 40]] singles "[[Roll Up (Wiz Khalifa song)|Roll Up]]" and "[[No Sleep (Wiz Khalifa song)|No Sleep]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wizkhalifa.com/rollingpapers/ |title=Pre-Order Rolling Papers |publisher=Wiz Khalifa |access-date=February 20, 2011}}</ref>

The album's success was followed up with ''[[O.N.I.F.C.]]'' in December 2012, which was backed by the singles "[[Work Hard, Play Hard]]" and "[[Remember You]]" (featuring [[the Weeknd]]). Wiz released his fifth album ''[[Blacc Hollywood]]'' in August 2014, backed by the lead single "[[We Dem Boyz]]". In March 2015, he released "[[See You Again]]" (featuring [[Charlie Puth]]) for the soundtrack of the film ''[[Furious 7]]'' and the song peaked at number one on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] for 12 non-consecutive weeks.<ref name=seeyouagainbillboard>{{cite magazine|title=Wiz Khalifa's 'See You Again' Leads Hot 100 for 10th Week|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6605843/wiz-khalifa-see-you-again-hot-100-10th-week|access-date=June 30, 2015|date=June 24, 2015}}</ref>

==Early life==
Khalifa was born Cameron Jibril Thomaz on September 8, 1987 in [[Minot, North Dakota]] to parents serving in the [[Military of the United States|military]].<ref name="ErieTimesNews">{{cite news|last1=Richards|first1=Dave|title=He'll make it hot|url=http://www.goerie.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20080918/ENTERTAINMENT0301/809180341/-1/ENTERTAINMENT03|access-date=November 21, 2017|work=[[Erie Times-News]]|date=September 18, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090113003106/http://www.goerie.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=%2F20080918%2FENTERTAINMENT0301%2F809180341%2F-1%2FENTERTAINMENT03|archive-date=January 13, 2009|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="allmusic">Cordor, Cyril. "[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p843628|pure_url=yes}} Wiz Khalifa]". Allmusic. Retrieved on June 23, 2008.</ref> His parents divorced when Khalifa was about three years old. He is a [[Military brat (U.S. subculture)|military brat]] with his parents' military service causing him to move regularly. Khalifa lived in [[Germany]], the [[United Kingdom]], and [[Japan]] before settling in [[Pittsburgh]] with his mother in around 1996 where he attended [[Taylor Allderdice High School]].<ref name="ErieTimesNews"/><ref name=WizKhalifaDay>{{cite news|last=Mervis|first=Scott|title=Pittsburgh City Council makes 12-12-12 Wiz Khalifa Day|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/local/neighborhoods-city/pittburgh-city-council-makes-12-12-12-wiz-khalifa-day-665879/|newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|date=December 11, 2012}}</ref> Soon after moving to Pittsburgh, Khalifa began to write and perform his own lyrics before he was a teenager.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://go.galegroup.com/ps/i.do?p=BIC&id=GALE%7CK1606008151&v=2.1&it=r&sid=summon#|title=Gale - Product Login|website=go.galegroup.com|access-date=November 5, 2018}}</ref>

His stage name is derived from [[Khalifa]], an [[Arabic language|Arabic]] word meaning "successor", and [[wisdom]], which was shortened to Wiz when Khalifa was a young boy.<ref name="Brotha Ash">{{cite web|title=Exclusive interview: hip hop phenomenon Wiz Khalifa|url=http://www.brothaashproductions.com/WizKhalifaInterview.htm|publisher=Brotha Ash Productions|access-date=November 18, 2010|date=March 18, 2006}}</ref> Khalifa stated to [[Spinner (website)|Spinner.com]] that the name also came from being called "young Wiz 'cause I was good at everything I did, and my granddad is [[Muslim]], so he gave me that name; he felt like that's what I was doing with my music." He got a tattoo of his stage name on his 17th birthday.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[Digital Spy]]|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a311148/wiz-khalifa-i-got-name-tattoo-at-17.html|title=Wiz Khalifa: 'I got name tattoo at 17'|author=Still, Jennifer|date=March 25, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Thompson, Troy|date=March 12, 2010|url=http://www.spinner.com/2010/03/12/wiz-khalifa-interview-sxsw-2010/|title=Wiz Khalifa Interview: SXSW 2010|publisher=[[Spinner (website)|Spinner]]|access-date=March 23, 2010}}</ref>

By the age of 15 he was regularly recording his music in a studio called I.D. Labs.<ref name="Wiz Khalifa">{{Cite web|url=http://link.galegroup.com/apps/doc/K1606008151/BIC?u=ucinc_main&sid=BIC&xid=ef01bff4|title=Wiz Khalifa|website=link.galegroup.com|language=en|access-date=November 13, 2018}}</ref> The management of the studio was so impressed by his lyrics that they allowed Khalifa to record for free.<ref name="Wiz Khalifa"/>

==Career==

===2005–2008: Early mixtapes and ''Show and Prove''===
Rostrum Records president Benjy Grinberg first heard about Wiz Khalifa in 2004 when the rapper's contribution to a mixtape of various new Pittsburgh artists attracted his interest.<ref name="hitquarters.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_BGrinberg.html|title=Interview With Benjy Grinberg|publisher=[[HitQuarters]]|date=October 17, 2011|access-date=October 19, 2011}}</ref> When Grinberg finally met the 16-year-old artist, he immediately decided he wanted to work with him, later telling [[HitQuarters]]: "Even though he wasn't all the way developed you could just tell that he was a diamond in the rough, and that with some polishing, guidance and backing he could become something special." Khalifa signed to the label shortly after and began a seven-year period of artist development.<ref name="hitquarters.com"/> Khalifa released his first [[mixtape]], ''Prince of the City: Welcome to Pistolvania'', in 2005. The mixtape paved the way for his first full-length album entitled ''[[Show and Prove]]'' in 2006. Khalifa was declared an "artist to watch" that year in ''[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]'' magazine.<ref>"[https://web.archive.org/web/20061201073453/http://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/12683075/breaking_wiz_khalifa Wiz Khalifa]". November 28, 2006. ''[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]''; retrieved August 2, 2007.</ref><ref name="hiphopdx.com">{{cite web|last=Kuperstein|first=Slava|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.16814/title.wiz-khalifa-working-on-next-album|title=Wiz Khalifa Working On Next Album|publisher=HipHop DX|date=September 10, 2011|access-date=February 4, 2012}}</ref>

In 2007, Khalifa signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]] and released two mixtapes through [[Rostrum Records]]: ''Grow Season'', hosted by [[DJ Green Lantern]] and released on July 4, 2007, and ''Prince of the City 2'', released on November 20, 2007. His debut Warner Bros. single "[[Say Yeah (Wiz Khalifa song)|Say Yeah]]" reached number 25 on the ''Billboard'' [[Rhythmic Top 40]] music chart and number 20 on ''Billboard's'' [[Hot Rap Tracks]]. The song [[sampling (music)|samples]] "[[Better Off Alone (Alice DeeJay song)|Better Off Alone]]" by [[Alice Deejay]]. Khalifa's vocals from "Say Yeah" appear near the end of Pittsburgh [[Mashup (music)|mashup]] producer [[Girl Talk (musician)|Girl Talk]]'s 2008 album, ''[[Feed the Animals]]'', over music from [[Underworld (band)|Underworld's]] "[[Born Slippy]]", [[Usher (entertainer)|Usher's]] "[[Love in This Club]]", and [[the Cure]]'s "[[In Between Days]]".<ref>Breihan, Tom (June 24, 2008). "[http://www.villagevoice.com/blogs/girl-talks-pop-music-car-wreck-6386175 Girl Talk's Pop-Music Car-Wreck]". ''[[The Village Voice]]''; retrieved June 25, 2008.</ref> Khalifa appeared with [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]], [[David Banner]] and [[Play-n-Skillz]] at [[KUUU|U92's]] Summer Jam at the [[USANA Amphitheatre]] in [[West Valley City, Utah|West Valley City]], Utah on August 2, 2008.<ref>Burger, David (August 1, 2008). [http://www.sltrib.com/themix/ci_10061200 "U92's Summer Jam: Rap producers get a chance to shine"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080925004231/http://www.sltrib.com/themix/ci_10061200 |date=September 25, 2008 }}, ''[[The Salt Lake Tribune]]''; retrieved August 2, 2008.</ref> Khalifa released the mixtapes ''Star Power'' in September 2008, and ''[[Flight School (album)|Flight School]]'' in April 2009 on Rostrum Records.

Khalifa parted ways with [[Warner Bros. Records]] in July 2009 after numerous delays in releasing his planned debut album for the label, ''First Flight''. Khalifa stated to the ''[[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]]'' that, "I learned a lot during my time there and matured as an artist during the process. I'm happy to be moving on with all of my material and having the chance to be in control of my next moves".<ref name="PostGazette-July2009"/> Khalifa appeared with Girl Talk, [[Modey Lemon]], [[Donora (band)|Donora]], [[Grand Buffet]], and [[Don Caballero]] at the [[Amphitheatre at Station Square]] in Pittsburgh on July 31, 2009, where he announced that his relationship with Warner Bros. was over.<ref>Tady, Scott (August 1, 2009). [http://www.timesonline.com/articles/2009/08/01/entertainment/doc4a750232c43ca606869969.txt "A new wave hits Pittsburgh music"], ''[[Beaver County Times]]''; retrieved August 2, 2009.</ref>

On April 11, 2012, Khalifa announced that he will release his second studio album titled ''Only Nigga In First Class'' but that it will be abbreviated as ''[[O.N.I.F.C.]]'' for release to mainstream markets.<ref name="hiphopdx.com"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Markman|first=Rob|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1670545/wiz-khalifa-rolling-papers-follow-up-album.jhtml|title=Wiz Khalifa Already 'Working On' Rolling Papers Follow-Up|publisher=MTV|date=September 9, 2011|access-date=February 4, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=John Woodruff|url=http://thecelebritycafe.com/feature/wiz-khalifa-begins-work-new-album-09-10-2011|title=Wiz Khalifa begins work on new album|publisher=TheCelebrityCafe.com|date=October 9, 2011|access-date=February 4, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120125065016/http://thecelebritycafe.com/feature/wiz-khalifa-begins-work-new-album-09-10-2011|archive-date=January 25, 2012}}</ref> ''O.N.I.F.C.'' debuted at number two with 131,000 in first week sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hitsdailydouble.com/news/newsPage.cgi?news09225|title=News|publisher=HITS Daily Double|date=December 11, 2012|access-date=July 4, 2013}}</ref> The album has spawned two singles "[[Work Hard, Play Hard]]" and "[[Remember You]]" both receiving good commercial acclaim and the former going platinum. Wiz spoke with rapper [[50 Cent]], with whom he collaborated on the song "Telescope", about making a movie together.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/12/wiz-khalifa-talks-possible-movie-soundtrack-with-50-cent-video|title=Wiz Khalifa Talks Possible Movie & Soundtrack With 50 Cent|publisher=Xxlmag.com|date=December 25, 2012|access-date=July 4, 2013}}</ref>

===2013–present: ''Blacc Hollywood'' and ''Rolling Papers 2''===
In April 2013, Khalifa revealed that after having his son he decided to work on a new album that he was planning to release in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/04/11/wiz-khalifa-plans-to-release-new-album-this-year/#more-154903|title=Wiz Khalifa Plans to Release New Album This Year|publisher=Rap-Up.com|access-date=July 4, 2013}}</ref> On April 17, 2013, Wiz Khalifa and Curren$y announced that they will drop their collaboration [[extended play|EP]] ''Live in Concert'' on April 20, 2013. The EP featured seven new songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/04/in-typical-stoner-fashion-wiz-khalifa-curreny-are-dropping-live-in-concert-on-420|title=In Typical Stoner Fashion, Wiz Khalifa & Curren$y Are Dropping "Live In Concert" On 4/20|publisher=Complex|date=April 17, 2013|access-date=July 4, 2013}}</ref> On June 24, 2013, he announced that his fifth studio album would be titled ''[[Blacc Hollywood]]'' and would be released in 2013.<ref name="rap-up1">{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/06/24/wiz-khalifa-announces-new-album-blacc-hollywood|title=Wiz Khalifa Announces New Album 'Blacc Hollywood'|publisher=Rap-Up.com|date=June 24, 2013|access-date=July 4, 2013}}</ref> On September 3, 2013, Khalifa revealed he had recorded songs with [[Miley Cyrus]] and [[Juicy J]] for ''Blacc Hollywood''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Baker|first=Soren|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.25316/title.wiz-khalifa-has-collaborated-with-miley-cyrus-adele-for-blacc-hollywood-|title=Wiz Khalifa Has Collaborated With Miley Cyrus, Adele For "Blacc Hollywood"|publisher=HipHop DX |date=September 3, 2013|access-date=August 18, 2014}}</ref> In October 2013, [[Mannie Fresh]] confirmed that he provided production for the album.<ref>{{cite web|last=Rys|first=Dan|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/10/mannie-fresh-has-hot-boys-mos-def-and-wiz-khalifa-projects-coming-up|title=Mannie Fresh Has Hot Boys, Mos Def And Wiz Khalifa Projects Coming Up|publisher=Xxlmag.com|date=October 31, 2013|access-date=August 18, 2014}}</ref>

On February 11, 2014, Khalifa released ''Blacc Hollywood''{{'}}s first single titled "[[We Dem Boyz]]". On March 31, 2015, Wiz Khalifa released an [[Extended play|EP]] with fellow Taylor Gang artist [[Ty Dolla Sign|Ty Dolla $ign]], ''[[Talk About It in the Morning]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://taylorgangent.com/premiere-wiz-khalifa-x-ty-dolla-sign-talk-about-it-in-the-morning-ep |title=PREMIERE! Wiz Khalifa x Ty Dolla $ign "Talk About it in the Morning" EP - Taylor Gang |publisher=Taylorgangent.com |date=March 31, 2015 |access-date=November 16, 2015}}</ref> That same month, Khalifa and [[Charlie Puth]] released the song "[[See You Again]]", a tribute to [[Paul Walker]], who died during filming for ''[[Furious 7]]''.<ref name="seeyouagainbillboard" />

[[File:Wiz Khalifa High Road.jpg|thumb|left|Khalifa performing in [[Toronto]], July 2016]]
After releasing several "Weedmixes" on [[SoundCloud]], including "Ziplocc"<ref>{{cite web|last=Ortiz|first=Edwin|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/03/wiz-khalifa-ziplocc-weedmix|title=Wiz Khalifa Shares Hazy New Record "Ziplocc (Weedmix)"|publisher=Complex|date=March 28, 2014|access-date=August 18, 2014}}</ref> and "Maan!",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/04/wiz-khalifa-maan-weedmix-freestyle|title=Wiz Khalifa Reworks ScHoolboy Q's "Man Of The Year" For "Maan! (Weedmix)"|publisher=Complex|date=April 5, 2014|access-date=August 18, 2014}}</ref> Wiz took to Twitter to create hype about his new mixtape, ''[[28 Grams]]'', which was due to be released on May 25, 2014. However, Wiz was arrested in [[El Paso, Texas]] for marijuana possession the day before, and there were fears that the mixtape would be pushed back. After posting a "jail selfie" and [[hashtag]]ging "FreeTrapWiz" on Twitter, he was released hours later, meaning the mixtape would go ahead with its intended release date.

On January 23, 2015, Khalifa was featured on a remix release of the [[Fall Out Boy]] song "[[Uma Thurman (song)|Uma Thurman]]". On May 18, they performed the song together on the 2015 [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Awards]] show.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/bbma/6568451/fall-out-boy-wiz-khalifa-uma-thurman-2015-billboard-music-awards-video%7cFall|title=Out Boy & Wiz Khalifa Perform 'Uma Thurman' at the 2015 Billboard Music Awards: Watch|publisher=Billboard|access-date=January 5, 2018}}</ref> In the summer of 2015, he began touring the United States with [[Fall Out Boy]] and [[Hoodie Allen]] in a tour titled "[[American Beauty/American Psycho Tour#Tour dates|The Boys of Zummer Tour]]".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Leight|first1=Elias|title=Fall Out Boy & Wiz Khalifa Announce Co-Headlining Summer Tour|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6443421/fall-out-boy-wiz-khalifa-summer-tour-boys-of-zummer|website=Billboard.com|publisher=Billboard|access-date=January 15, 2015|date=January 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Mervis|first1=Scott|title=Wiz Khalifa, Fall Out Boy tour coming in July|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/music/2015/01/15/Wiz-Khalifa-Fall-Out-Boy-tour-coming-in-July-Pittsburgh/stories/201501150284|website=Post-Gazette.com|publisher=Post Gazette|access-date=January 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Fall Out Boy|title=Fall Out Boy on Facebook - "It's happening..."|url=https://www.facebook.com/falloutboy/posts/10152526900440780|website=Facebook.com/FallOutBoy|publisher=Fall Out Boy|access-date=January 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=DCD2 Records on Facebook - "you ready for the biggest tour of the zummer?..."|url=https://www.facebook.com/DCD2Records/posts/786814344738517|website=Facebook.com|access-date=January 15, 2015}}</ref> The tour featured performances of "Uma Thurman" by Fall Out Boy and Khalifa. Later that year, on December 15, 2015, Wiz released the mixtape "[[Cabin Fever 3 (mixtape)|Cabin Fever 3]]". During the Golden Globes on January 10, 2016, Wiz announced that his next album, titled ''[[Khalifa (album)|Khalifa]]'', would be released on the 22nd of that month.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Wiz Khalifa Announces "Khalifa" Release Date|url = http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/wiz-khalifa-announces-khalifa-release-date-news.19555.html|website = HotNewHipHop|date = January 11, 2016|access-date = January 11, 2016}}</ref> However, the album was released on February 5, 2016. On May 24, 2016, he released "Pull Up" , a standalone single in promotion of his sixth album, Rolling Papers 2,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7633183/wiz-khalifa-lil-uzi-vert-taylor-gang |title=Wiz Khalifa releases lead single from Rolling Papers 2 |last=DiMelgio |first=Mary |date=December 23, 2016 |website=[[Billboard.com]] |access-date=December 29, 2016}}</ref> which was released on July 13, 2018.

In 2016, Khalifa sued former manager Benjy Grinberg and [[Rostrum Records]] because of a "[[360 deal]]" that he signed which he claimed was unfair.<ref name="Former Manager">{{cite web |url=http://radio.com/2016/06/01/wiz-khalifa-sues-manager-360-deal/ |title=Wiz Khalifa sues over unfair 360 deal |last=Wright |first=Hayden |date=June 1, 2016 |website=Radio.com |access-date=June 1, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160601235309/http://radio.com/2016/06/01/wiz-khalifa-sues-manager-360-deal/ |archive-date=June 1, 2016 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> Khalifa is seeking $1 million in compensation as well as punitive damages and attorney fees. He and Grinberg split after 10 years of partnership.<ref name="Former Manager"/>

On April 20, 2020, Khalifa released a new EP, The Saga of Wiz Khalifa. The album included collaborations with [[Tyga]], [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]], [[Mustard (record producer)|Mustard]] and [[Ty Dolla Sign|Ty Dolla $ign]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/the-saga-of-wiz-khalifa-ep-stream/|title='The Saga of Wiz Khalifa' EP: Stream Here|date=April 20, 2020|website=Highsnobiety|language=en-US|access-date=April 21, 2020}}</ref>

In May 2020, Khalifa featured on a new song, ''Drums Drums Drums'' with [[Travis Barker]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=adminkccrfm|date=May 30, 2020|title=Blink-182 teams up with Powfu for "death bed" remix; Travis Barker releases new song with Wiz Khalifa|url=http://www.capitalcityrock.com/2020/05/blink-182-teams-up-with-powfu-for-death-bed-remix-travis-barker-releases-new-song-with-wiz-khalifa/|access-date=June 3, 2020|website=Capital City Rock 104.5|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2021, Khalifa competed in [[The Masked Singer (American season 5)|season five]] of ''[[The Masked Singer (American TV series)|The Masked Singer]]'' as "Chameleon". He finished in third place.

==Personal life==
Khalifa began dating model [[Amber Rose]] in early 2011. They got engaged on March 1, 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/RealWizKhalifa/status/175291615601102849|title=Wiz Khalifa On Twitter|publisher=Twitter.com|date=April 1, 2011|access-date=April 1, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Christie D'Zurilla|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/gossip/2012/03/amber-rose-wiz-khalifa-engaged.html|title=Amber Rose, Wiz Khalifa engaged; 'Love Is Everywhere'|work=Los Angeles Times|date=March 2, 2012|access-date=February 25, 2013}}</ref> and married on July 8, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Wiz Khalifa and Amber Rose Tie the Knot|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1569334/wiz-khalifa-and-amber-rose-tie-the-knot|publisher=Billboard|date=July 8, 2013|access-date=July 9, 2013}}</ref> They have one son, Sebastian Taylor (born 2013).<ref>{{cite web|title=Wiz Khalifa and Amber Rose Welcome Son Sebastian Taylor|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2013/02/21/wiz-khalifa-amber-rose-welcome-son-sebastian-taylor/|publisher=People|date=February 21, 2013|access-date=February 25, 2013}}</ref> On September 24, 2014, it was announced that Rose would be filing for divorce, citing [[irreconcilable differences]].<ref>{{cite web|author=CBS Pittsburgh|url=http://pittsburgh.cbslocal.com/2014/09/24/report-pittsburgh-native-wiz-khalifa-getting-divorced|title=Pittsburgh Native Wiz Khalifa Getting Divorced|publisher=CBS Pittsburgh|date=September 24, 2014|access-date=September 24, 2014}}</ref> As of 2015, Khalifa and Rose have [[Joint custody (United States)|joint custody]] of their son.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/amber-rose-wiz-khalifa-settle-5423734|title=Amber Rose and Wiz Khalifa 'settle for joint custody over son Sebastian'|author=Claire Rutter|publisher=Mirror|date=March 29, 2015|access-date=February 10, 2016}}</ref>

Khalifa is open about his use of [[cannabis (drug)|cannabis]], and he has claimed in many interviews that he spends $10,000 a month on cannabis, and also smokes daily.<ref>{{cite web|author=Wiz Khalifa Shirts says |url=http://www.thefader.com/2010/11/16/wiz-khalifa-spends-10000-a-month-on-weed-may-be-a-bad-guy|title=Wiz Khalifa Spends $10,000 a Month on Weed, May Be A "Bad Guy"|publisher=Thefader.com|date=November 16, 2010|access-date=August 20, 2012}}</ref> As of early 2014, Khalifa no longer pays for cannabis and is sponsored by The Cookie Company, a medical marijuana dispenser which sells his "Khalifa Kush" (KK) strain, which he partnered with RiverRock Cannabis to create.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/news/health/2015/04/07/Wiz-Khalifa-and-other-celebrities-to-market-own-brands-of-legalized-marijunana/stories/201504070014|title=Wiz Khalifa and other celebrities to market own brands of legalized marijuana|website=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|access-date=April 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.people.com/article/wiz-khalifa-partnering-with-cannabis-company |title=Khalifa partners with RiverRock Cannabis to manufacture his own line of regulated marijuana strains, products and concentrates |last=Nelson |first=Jeff |date=February 4, 2016 |website=[[People.com]] |access-date=June 24, 2016}}</ref> Also, in 2016 Khalifa was featured in a cooking show with [[Martha Stewart]] and [[Snoop Dogg]] where weed was the main ingredient.

Khalifa began training in the martial art [[Brazilian jiu-jitsu]] in May 2017 with [[Rigan Machado]], an 8th degree red and black belt.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://sports.vice.com/en_au/article/d7adpw/wiz-khalifa-is-studying-jiu-jitsu|title=Wiz Khalifa Is Studying Jiu Jitsu|date=May 11, 2017|work=Sports|access-date=May 17, 2018|language=en-au}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.jiujitsutimes.com/wiz-khalifa-jiujitsu/|title=Wiz Khalifa Is Now Training Jiu-Jitsu With Rigan Machado|date=May 9, 2017|work=Jiu-Jitsu Times|access-date=May 17, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Shortly after, he began training in [[Muay Thai]] with former [[Ultimate Fighting Championship|UFC]] bantamweight title contender [[Cat Zingano]] and at the Unbreakable Performance Center in Los Angeles.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.tmz.com/2017/07/24/wiz-khalifa-mma-training-jay-glazer/|title=Wiz Khalifa's Getting Serious About MMA Training|work=TMZ|access-date=May 17, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.mixedmartialarts.com/vault/mma/wiz-khalifa-training-mma-striking-with-ufcs-cat-zingano|title=Wiz Khalifa training MMA striking with UFC's Cat Zingano|work=The Underground|access-date=May 17, 2018}}</ref> On April 24, 2018, [[Yokkao]] hosted a private Muay Thai seminar with Thai boxing legend [[Saenchai]] for Khalifa at Unbreakable.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.criscyborg.com/hip-hop-star-wiz-khalifa-trains-muay-thai-saenchai/|title=Hip Hop Star Wiz Khalifa trains Muay Thai with Saenchai|date=April 27, 2018|work=The Official Website of Cristiane "Cyborg" Santos|access-date=May 17, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.yokkao.com/news/yokkao-private-seminar-with-saenchai-for-wiz-khalifa/|title=YOKKAO Hosts Private Seminar With Saenchai For Wiz Khalifa|date=April 25, 2018|work=YOKKAO News - Muay Thai and MMA News!|access-date=May 17, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>
