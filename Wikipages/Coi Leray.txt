'''Coi Leray Collins'''{{efn|According to Collins herself, her real name is Coi Leray Collins;<ref name="interview">{{cite web|last1=|first1=|date=March 19, 2021|title=Coi Leray How To Pronounce My Name Correctly (part 1)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mB9mGDxlY6o|access-date=May 30, 2021|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> other sources cite her name as '''Brittany Collins'''.<ref name="blair">{{cite web|last1=Blair|first1=Robert|date=May 7, 2021|title=Coi Leray & Benzino's Family History & Beef, Explained|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/coi-leray-and-benzinos-family-history-and-beef-explained-news.131113.html|access-date=May 27, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref>}} (born May 11, 1997) is an American rapper. Leray began posting music to [[SoundCloud]], releasing her breakout single "Huddy" and her debut mixtape ''Everythingcoz'' in 2018. After signing a record deal with 1801 Records and [[Republic Records]], she released her second mixtape, ''EC2'', in 2019, and her debut [[extended play]], ''Now or Never'', in 2020. In 2021, her single "[[No More Parties]]" gave Leray her first [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] entry after peaking in the top 40 and was [[RIAA certification|certified platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).

==Early life==
Collins was born on May 11, 1997<ref>{{cite web |title=Coi Leray |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/coileray/profile/ |website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref> in [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]], and raised in [[Hackensack, New Jersey|Hackensack]], [[New Jersey]].<ref name="office">{{cite web |last1=Cheng |first1=Vivian |title=Year of the Coi |url=http://officemagazine.net/year-coi |website=Office Magazine |access-date=April 14, 2021 |language=en |date=December 20, 2019}}</ref><ref name="law">{{cite web |last1=Ju |first1=Shirley |title=Coi Leray Is Feeling Cozy With Her Fame |url=https://www.laweekly.com/coi-leray-is-feeling-cozy-with-her-fame/ |website=[[LA Weekly]] |access-date=April 12, 2021 |language=en |date=February 21, 2019}}</ref> She is the daughter of rapper and media mogul [[Benzino]], and has five brothers. Leray's parents divorced after her father left ''[[The Source]]''.<ref name="paperrappers">{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Talia Tabatha Binx |title=Women in Hip-Hop: The Voices of Our Generation |url=https://www.papermag.com/women-in-hip-hop-2019-2629056759.html?rebelltitem=52#rebelltitem52 |website=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]] |access-date=April 14, 2021 |language=en |date=February 28, 2019}}</ref> She started making music at age 14.<ref name="betfetty">{{cite web |title=Coi Leray On Fetty Wap 'Better Days' Collab And Why It's Special To Be A Woman Rapper Now |url=https://www.bet.com/music/2020/06/16/coi-leray-on-fetty-wap-better-days-collab.html |website=[[BET]] |access-date=April 12, 2021 |language=en |date=June 16, 2020}}</ref> At age 16, Leray dropped out of high school and started working in sales.<ref name="law" />

==Career==
[[File:Coi Leray 2018.png|thumb|left|upright|Collins in 2018]]
In 2011, inspired by her father, she started rapping and released her first songs under the name Coi Leray (inspired by the Japanese  [[koi]] fish), "Bow Down", with her brother Taj, and "Rock Back", via YouTube.<ref name="highsnobiety">{{cite web |last1=Alston |first1=Trey |title=Try As You Might, You Can't Stop Coi Leray |url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/coi-leray-interview/ |website=[[Highsnobiety]] |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=November 11, 2021}}</ref><ref name="hotnewhiphop.com">{{Cite web|title=Coi Leray & Benzino's Family History & Beef, Explained|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/coi-leray-and-benzinos-family-history-and-beef-explained-news.131113.html|access-date=2021-09-07|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en}}</ref> However, both Leray and her brother stopped rapping shortly after.<ref name="xxlbreak" /> After quitting her job, Leray released her debut single, a response to [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]]'s song "DTB" titled "G.A.N.", in 2018 through [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="law" /><ref name="highsnobiety" /> Her breakout single, "Huddy", and her debut mixtape, ''Everythingcoz'', were both released in 2018.<ref name="xxlbreak" />

In December 2018, she was included as a featured artist alongside LouGotCash on the track "Save the Day" by American rapper [[Ski Mask the Slump God]] and singer [[Jacquees]] from [[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (soundtracks)|the soundtrack album]] for the 2018 film ''[[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse]]''. Her second mixtape, ''EC2'', was released on January 18, 2019 through 1801 Records and [[Republic Records]].<ref name="bbfame">{{cite web |last1=Cho |first1=Regina |title=Coi Leray On Her Rise to Fame & Deal With Republic Records: Exclusive |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8494039/coi-leray-interview-republic-records-deal |website=Billboard |access-date=April 14, 2021 |language=en |date=January 18, 2019}}</ref> She accompanied American rapper [[Trippie Redd]] on his Life's a Trip Tour in early 2019, and released the single "Good Days" in March 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chin |first1=Mallory |title=Trippie Redd Announces 'Life's A Trip' Tour |url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/12/trippie-redd-lifes-a-trip-tour-dates |website=Hypebeast |access-date=April 14, 2021 |language=en |date=December 23, 2018}}</ref> She appeared as a featured artist on the song "Everything BoZ" by Trippie Redd from his second studio album, ''[[! (Trippie Redd album)|!]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ju |first1=Shirley |title=Trippie Redd talks new '!' album, XXXTentacion, Lil Wayne, Coi Leray relationship and more |url=https://www.revolt.tv/2019/9/17/20870915/trippie-redd-music-xxxtentacion-interview |website=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]] |access-date=April 14, 2021 |language=en |date=September 17, 2019}}</ref> In April 2020, she released the single "Better Days" with American rapper [[Fetty Wap]], and was featured on the remix of American actress [[Keke Palmer]]'s single "Sticky" in May 2020.<ref name="betfetty" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Marie |first1=Erika |title=Keke Palmer Calls On Coi Leray For "Sticky" Remix |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/keke-palmer-calls-on-coi-leray-for-sticky-remix-new-song.1987231.html |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=April 13, 2021 |language=en |date=May 15, 2020}}</ref>
