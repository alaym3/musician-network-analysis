'''Sudan Ameer Williams''' (born November 16, 1989), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Iamsu!''' (stylized as '''IamSu!''' or '''IAMSU!'''), is an American [[Rapping|rapper]], [[Singing|singer]], [[songwriter]], and [[record producer]]. He is also a part of the production team '''The Invasion''' and lead member of the hip hop group [[The HBK Gang]]. Iamsu! has released numerous mixtapes in his career including ''Suzy 6 Speed'', ''Million Dollar Afro'' with fellow California rapper [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]] and the ''[[Kilt II|Kilt]]'' series. He is also known for featuring on [[LoveRance]]'s "[[Up! (LoveRance song)|Up!]]", [[E-40]]'s "[[Function (song)|Function]]", and [[Sage the Gemini]]'s "[[Gas Pedal]]". His debut album ''[[Sincerely Yours (Iamsu! album)|Sincerely Yours]]'' was released on May 13, 2014.

== Early life ==
Sudan Ameer Williams was born on November 16, 1989, in [[Richmond, California]], however, he moved to live in [[Pinole, California|Pinole]]. At a young age, he would be listening to artists such as [[Kanye West]], [[R. Kelly]], [[Aaliyah]], [[Marching Band (band)|Marching Band]], The Grammar Band and [[reggae]] music. At the age of 14, he began making music. Around that time, he was "always playing with instruments and messing around with music." Once he attended high school, he started learning how to make beats at a local Bay Area Youth Program "Youth Radio".<ref name=autogenerated4 />
== Career ==

=== 2009–12: Career beginnings ===
In 2009, Iamsu! started rapping under his stage name "Su", but then he decided to change it to Iamsu!, due to search engine optimization.<ref name=autogenerated5>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/iamsu-is-playing-noiseys-may-rap-party-so-we-interviewed-him |title=IamSu! Is Playing Noisey's May Rap Party, So We Interviewed Him &#124; NOISEY |publisher=Noisey.vice.com |access-date=2013-09-12}}</ref> On July 4, 2010, he released his first mixtape, titled ''Su! The Right Thing''. In 2011, Iamsu! produced, co-wrote and featured on [[LoveRance]]'s hit single "[[Up! (LoveRance song)|Up!]]", which also included fellow rapper [[50 Cent]] on a remix. The song peaked at number&nbsp;46 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] and reached at number 2 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]].<ref name=autogenerated7 /><ref name=autogenerated6>{{cite web|url=http://www.dailycal.org/2013/05/09/iamsu-revitalizes-bay-area-rap/ |title=IAmSu! revitalizes Bay Area rap - The Daily Californian |publisher=Dailycal.org |date=2013-05-09 |access-date=2013-09-12}}</ref> He followed with his appearance on [[E-40]]'s hit song "[[Function (song)|Function]]", along with rappers [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]] and [[YG (rapper)|YG]]. The song peaked at number&nbsp;62 on the US [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|''Billboard'' Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]].<ref>{{BillboardURLbyName|artist=e-40|chart=R&B/Hip-Hop Songs}}</ref> In 2012, he also featured on [[Jonn Hart]]'s track "[[Who Booty]]" and [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s track "Bout Me".<ref name=autogenerated6 />

=== 2012–13: ''$uzy 6 Speed'', ''Million Dollar Afro'' and ''Kilt II'' ===
On October 2, 2012, Iamsu! released his previous mixtapes: ''Kilt'', ''Young California'' and ''Su! The Right Thing'', on [[iTunes]] for sale. On November 12, 2012, Iamsu! released another mixtape, titled ''$uzy 6 Speed''. This mixtape features [[guest appearance]]s from [[Juvenile (rapper)|Juvenile]], Problem and [[Wiz Khalifa]]. It contained the single "Mobbin'" and received critical acclaim upon the release.<ref name="autogenerated3">{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/iamsu-talks-being-college-avoiding-groupies-and-tour-life|title=IAmSu! Million Dollar Afro |publisher=Stereogum |date=2013-04-10 |access-date=2013-09-12}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2013/03/mtv-10-hottest-mcs-rappers-in-the-game-list/130306-iamsu/ |title=Iamsu! - MTV T.R.U. ===2013&ndash;2016: ''Sincerely Yours'' and ''Kilt 3''===
{{main|Sincerely Yours (Iamsu! album)}}
Iamsu! was also featured on [[Sage the Gemini]]'s 2013's hit single "[[Gas Pedal]]", which peaked at number 29 on the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100, making it both artist's first top 40 entries and their most successful single to date. On August 12, 2013, Iamsu! and his group [[HBK Gang]], released a compilation mixtape, titled ''[[Gang Forever]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/08/iamsu-hbk-gang-releases-new-mixtape-gang-forever/ |title=Iamsu! & HBK Gang Releases New Mixtape, 'Gang Forever' - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-08-12 |access-date=2013-09-01}}</ref> On December 3, 2013, Iamsu! released "Hipster Girls as the second single from ''Kilt II''.<ref name="itunes.apple.com">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/hipster-girls-single/id746934133|title=Hipster Girls - Single|work=iTunes|access-date=March 3, 2016}}</ref> On January 3, 2014, Iamsu! released a remix to [[YG (rapper)|YG]]'s "[[Who Do You Love (YG song)|Who Do You Love]]", where he was hinted at being signed and planning to release his debut album. On January 7, 2014, he announced that his debut album would be titled ''[[Sincerely Yours (Iamsu! album)|Sincerely Yours]]'' and be released in April 2014. He also confirmed that the first single would feature Sage the Gemini.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/iamsu-reveals-title-debut-album/|title=Iamsu! Reveals Title Of Debut Album Due Out In April|author=ericdiep|date=January 7, 2014|work=XXL Mag|access-date=March 3, 2016}}</ref> On January 28, 2014, he released the album's first single, titled "[[Only That Real]]", featuring Sage the Gemini and 2 Chainz.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1646911/iamsu-only-that-real-feat-2-chainz-sage-the-gemini/mp3s/|title=Iamsu! – "Only That Real" (Feat. 2 Chainz & Sage The Gemini)|work=Stereogum|access-date=March 3, 2016}}</ref> The album was then set for a May 13, 2014, release date.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/04/heres-tracklist-cover-art-iamsus-album/|title=Here's The Tracklist and Cover Art For Iamsu!'s Album|author=emmanuel|date=April 8, 2014|work=XXL Mag|access-date=March 3, 2016}}</ref>

In 2015, he began touring worldwide to promote his second studio album, ''KILT 3''. The first single "Up All Night" followed February 14, 2016. The album was released March 24, 2016.
