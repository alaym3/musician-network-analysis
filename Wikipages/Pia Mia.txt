'''Pia Mia Perez'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/celebrities/news/a30661/who-is-pia-mia-singer-guam/|title=High Tea With Pia Mia at The Plaza|date=September 24, 2015}}</ref> (born September 19, 1996) is a Guamanian singer and actress. She began her career by posting videos of herself singing on the video sharing site [[YouTube]], and went on to star in commercials and music videos. Pia Mia signed a recording contract with [[Interscope Records]] in 2013, subsequently releasing her debut [[extended play]], [[The Gift (EP)|''The Gift'']], and a series of singles, including "[[Do It Again (Pia Mia song)|Do It Again]]" and "[[Touch (Pia Mia song)|Touch]]". In 2016, she was featured on [[will.i.am]]'s single "[[Boys & Girls (will.i.am song)|Boys & Girls]]" which reached the top 40 in the [[UK Singles Chart]]. In 2017, Pia Mia independently released an EP entitled ''The Gift 2.'' Pia would later leave Interscope Records and become an [[Unsigned artist|independent artist]] in late 2017, creating her own label Cherry Pie Records.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Go Behind the Scenes With Pia Mia for 'Bitter Love'|url=https://www.papermag.com/pia-mia-bitter-love-bts-2635643163.html?rebelltitem=18#rebelltitem18?rebelltitem=18|date=April 26, 2019|website=PAPER|language=en|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref> Pia briefly signed a record deal with [[Republic Records]] and Electric Feel Entertainment in 2020, before separating the following year.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Pia Mia Signs With Electric Feel/Republic: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9377616/pia-mia-signed-electric-feel-republic-princess-single-exclusive|date=May 14, 2020|website=Billboard|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
Pia Mia Perez was born September 19, 1996,<ref>{{cite web|title=Pia Mia Perez Celebrates Her 19th Birthday on September 19!|url=http://sciclonic.com/2015/09/pia-mia-perez-celebrates-her-19th-birthday-on-september-19/781/|website=SciClonic|publisher=Ronan Kreet|access-date=December 16, 2015}}</ref> in [[Guam]], an [[unincorporated territory]] of the United States. She is of [[Chamorro people|Chamorro]], Italian, Dutch, and Hungarian descent. She is the daughter of Angela Terlaje Perez and Peter Perez Jr. In Guam, Perez performed at weddings and other community events, before moving to Los Angeles in 2010 with her mother to pursue her music career professionally.<ref name="LA Times"/><ref name="Hot 97"/>

==Career==

===2013‚Äì2017: ''The Gift'', ''The Gift 2'' and breakthrough===
[[File:Pia Mia March 18, 2014 (cropped).jpg|thumb|left|130px|Pia Mia at the [[Los Angeles]] premiere of ''[[Divergent (film)|Divergent]]'' on March 18, 2014]]
Perez was discovered by and worked with producer [[Babyface (musician)|Babyface]] during her first year in Los Angeles.<ref name="Hot 97">{{cite web |last1=Hot 97 |title=Pia Mia Gives The TRUE Story Behind Her Relationship With Nic Nac & Kylie Jenner |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1iTmFF44sGo |website=Youtube |date=June 23, 2015}}</ref>
Eventually she was introduced to [[Chris Brown]]'s manager and record executive Abou "Bu" Thiam in 2013. She began working on material with producer [[Nic Nac]] and regularly uploaded [[cover version|covers]] to YouTube.<ref name="LA Times"/> In late 2013, [[Kylie Jenner]], a close friend, invited Perez to a family dinner, during which she was asked to sing her cover of [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]]'s single "[[Hold On, We're Going Home]]", who was also in attendance.<ref name="Power 106"/> [[Kim Kardashian]] uploaded a video of the performance to her social media, and it gained over a million views.<ref name="LA Times"/> Following the viral success, she released her first song "Red Love" on December 9, 2013, along with its music video.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ortiz |first1=Edwin |title=Pia Mia Shares Her Tale of Heartbreak in "Red Love" Music Video |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2013/12/pia-mia-red-love-video |website=Complex |date=December 9, 2013}}</ref> Her debut [[extended play]] ''[[The Gift (EP)|The Gift]]'' was released online for free later that month with eight tracks.<ref name="LA Times"/>

In February 2014, Pia Mia signed a multi-album deal with American [[record label]] [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Singer-Songwriter Pia Mia Signed to Interscope Records|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/singer-songwriter-pia-mia-signed-to-interscope-records-246288731.html|access-date=August 11, 2015|agency=Interscope Geffen A&M Records|publisher=PR Newswire|date=February 20, 2014}}</ref> The same month, Perez and [[Chance the Rapper]] released an original song "Fight For You" for the 2014 science-fiction action film ''[[Divergent (film)|Divergent]]''.<ref name=":4">{{cite web |last1=Minsker |first1=Evan |title=Listen: Pia Mia Featuring Chance the Rapper: "Fight for You" (Produced by Clams Casino) |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/54082-listen-pia-mia-featuring-chance-the-rapper-fight-for-you-produced-by-clams-casino/ |website=Pitchfork |date=February 27, 2014}}</ref> The song was featured on the film's [[Divergent: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack|original soundtrack]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ehrlich |first1=Brenna |title=Chance the Rapper and Pia Mia Go All Four and Tris on 'Divergent' Track |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1723126/divergent-track-pia-mia-chance-the-rapper-fight-for-you/ |website=MTV |date=February 27, 2014}}</ref> Perez began working on her debut album in 2014 and recorded roughly 100 songs over the next year.<ref name="Power 106">{{cite web|last1=Power 106 Los Angeles|title=Pia Mia Sang For Kanye + Drake At Kardashian Dinner|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PeRDozZrhsY|website=Youtube|date=May 6, 2015}}</ref>

In early 2015, she released the promotional single "Fuck With U" featuring American [[Hip hop music|hip hop recording artist]] and [[Hip hop production|producer]] [[G-Eazy]]. It was initially intended to be the first single from Mia's debut studio album under Interscope, but was later scrapped. On May 4, 2015, Mia released "[[Do It Again (Pia Mia song)|Do It Again]]", which featured vocals from [[Chris Brown]] and [[Tyga]], and became Mia's first charting song. It has reached No. 8 on the UK Top 40, No. 71 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, No. 19 on the Rhythmic Songs chart and No. 70 on the Canadian Hot 100.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pia Mia Chart History|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6530595/pia-mia/chart?f=378|website=Billboard}}</ref> On October 30, 2015, Mia released follow-up single called "[[Touch (Pia Mia song)|Touch]]", produced by [[Stargate (music producers)|StarGate]] and [[BloodPop]]. It reached No. 47 in Australia and the [[UK Singles Chart]].

In 2016, Perez was featured on [[will.i.am]]'s single "[[Boys & Girls (will.i.am song)|Boys & Girls]]" which peaked in the top 40 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. Perez released the promotional single "We Should Be Together" on December 15, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pia Mia Drops New R&B Jam 'We Should Be Together' - LISTEN|url=https://www.capitalxtra.com/news/pia-mia-new-song-we-should-be-together-listen/|access-date=February 17, 2021|website=Capital XTRA|language=en}}</ref>

On May 25, 2017, Pia released the single "I'm a Fan" featuring [[Jeremih]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Premiere: Pia Mia Stuns in New Video for "I'm a Fan" f/ Jeremih|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/08/pia-mia-jeremih-im-a-fan-video|access-date=February 17, 2021|website=Complex}}</ref>  Pia Mia parted ways with Interscope Records and Wolfpack Entertainment, both divisions of [[Universal Music Group]] in late 2017. She stated that she didn't have enough creative freedom under the label and that there were excessive delays with her music.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Real |first1=Evan |title=Pia Mia Talks Finding Herself as an Independent Artist, New Song 'Bitter Love': 'It Feels Like My Heart and Soul' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8503539/pia-mia-interview-bitter-love |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190322161310/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8503539/pia-mia-interview-bitter-love |url-status=dead |archive-date=March 22, 2019 |website=Billboard (archived) |date=March 22, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":3" /> On December 15, she self-released her second EP ''The Gift 2'', which was preceded by the promotional single, "Off My Feet", released on December 1, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/7672101/drop-guide-new-music-friday-vallis-alps-sasha-sloan|title=The Drop: Your Guide To New Music Friday Featuring Vallis Alps & Sasha Sloan|work=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|author=Mike Wass|date=December 1, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0786VJHSB/|title=Amazon.com: The Gift 2: Pia Mia: MP3 Downloads|publisher=[[Amazon (company)|Amazon]]|date=December 15, 2017}}</ref>

===2019‚Äìpresent: Acting and label changes===
In 2019, Perez made her acting debut, portraying Tristan in the film ''[[After (2019 film)|After]]''.<ref name="AfterBitterLove">{{cite web|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/pia-mia-shines-with-an-acting-debut-in-after/|title=Pia Mia shines with an acting debut in 'After'|date=April 12, 2019|last=Shah|first=Harshvardhan|access-date=April 27, 2019|work=[[V (American magazine)|V]]}}</ref> Correspondingly, she released the single, "Bitter Love", which served as the film's theme song.<ref name="AfterBitterLove" /> On June 20, 2019, Perez released "Crybaby" featuring Theron Theron as a promotional single.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pia Mia Shares Island-Tinged Summer Single 'Crybaby'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8516935/pia-mia-crybaby|date=June 21, 2019|website=Billboard|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref> Following this, she released promotional singles "Feel Up" with American rapper [[YG (rapper)|YG]] on September 5, 2019, and "Don't Get Me Started" with rapper [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]] and producer [[Carnage (DJ)|Carnage]] on October 2, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=A Bop! Pia Mia Teams Up With YG On New Single "Feel Up"|url=https://www.idolator.com/7875150/a-bop-pia-mia-teams-up-with-yg-on-new-single-feel-up|date=September 9, 2019|website=idolator|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Premiere: Pia Mia Connects With Gunna and Carnage for New Collab "Don't Get Me Started"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/10/pia-gunna-carnage-dont-get-me-started|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref> All four singles were be released under Perez's independent record label Cherry Pie Records.<ref name=":1" />

On May 14, 2020, it was announced that Perez had signed to Electric Feel Entertainment and [[Republic Records]].<ref name=":2" /> Perez released the single "Princess", her first single released under her new label, on May 15, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Princess by Pia Mia|url=https://music.apple.com/nz/album/princess/1512316105?i=1512316107|website=Apple Music|language=en-au|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> In an interview with ''Idolator'', Perez confirmed the release of the single "[[Hot (Pia Mia song)|Hot]]", which will feature on her upcoming debut studio album.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Interview: Pia Mia Talks "Princess" & New Music|url=https://www.idolator.com/7910884/interview-pia-mia-talks-princess-new-music|date=May 27, 2020|website=idolator|access-date=May 27, 2020}}</ref> "Hot" was released on July 24, 2020, later accompanied by a visualiser for the song, released on September 14.<ref>{{Cite web|title=üêö Princess Pia Mia ü¶ã on Instagram: "As long as you're keepin it HOT 7/24"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CCwoLMEFTP1/|access-date=July 18, 2020|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> A remix of the song featuring [[Sean Paul]] and [[Flo Milli]] was released on October 27, 2020. A visualizer for the remix version was released on October 30.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|date=October 30, 2020|title=Pia Mia Teams Up With Sean Paul & Flo Milli For "Hot" Remix|url=https://www.idolator.com/7915755/pia-mia-teams-up-with-sean-paul-flo-milli-for-hot-remix|access-date=October 30, 2020|website=idolator}}</ref>

In 2020, Perez announced that she had written a book titled ''The Princess Diaries: Sand, Glitter and Silicone'' which will be released chapter by chapter via [[Wattpad]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Pia Mia's New Book Series is Free and Worth the Read |url=https://glittermagrocks.com/connect/2020/05/28/pia-mias-new-book-series-is-free-and-worth-the-read/ |website=Glitter Magazine |date=May 28, 2020}}</ref> In September 2020, it was officially announced that Pia Mia had joined the content subscription service [[OnlyFans]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Pia Mia #WelcometoOnlyFans|url=https://www.facebook.com/OnlyFansOfficial/videos/pia-mia-welcometoonlyfans/918752875325805/|language=en|access-date=September 7, 2020}}</ref>

On February 14, 2021, Perez released the song "Miss Me" on [[SoundCloud]]. Perez announced that the song's release was [[Independent music|independent]] after parting ways with Republic Records and Electric Feel Entertainment.<ref>{{Citation|title="Miss Me" by Pia Mia|url=https://soundcloud.com/princesspiamia/miss-me-by-pia-mia|language=en|access-date=February 17, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Pia Mia Releases New Song "Miss Me" via SoundCloud|url=https://www.pmstudio.com/music/music.html?page=20210216-13128|access-date=February 17, 2021|website=pm studio world wide news|language=ja}}</ref>
