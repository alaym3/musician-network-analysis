'''Benjamin Hammond Haggerty'''<ref>{{cite news|last1=Gamp|first1=Joe|title=Watch Macklemore surprise his grandmother on her 100th birthday in heart warming Glorious video|url=http://metro.co.uk/2017/07/07/watch-macklemore-surprise-his-grandmother-on-her-100th-birthday-in-heart-warming-glorious-video-6762148/|access-date=September 22, 2017|work=[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]|date=July 7, 2017}}</ref> (born June 19, 1983), known by his stage name '''Macklemore''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|m|æ|k|.|l|ə|m|ɔr}} {{respell|MAK|lə-mor}};<ref>{{cite news | first = Chey | last = Scott | title = How to pronounce rapper Macklemore's name | date = February 22, 2013 |url=http://www.inlander.com/spokane/blog-7886-how-to-pronounce-rap.html | work = [[The Pacific Northwest Inlander]] | access-date = June 23, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | title = We are MACKLEMORE, RYAN LEWIS and FENCES. Ask us anything. | date = August 7, 2014 |url=https://www.reddit.com/r/IAmA/comments/2cwyxy/we_are_macklemore_ryan_lewis_and_fences_ask_us/cjju4pr | work = [[Reddit]] | quote = Mack-La-More is how it's pronounced. Should have picked an easier name to say. | access-date = August 8, 2014}}</ref> formerly '''Professor Macklemore'''), is an American rapper and songwriter. A native of [[Seattle, Washington]], he has collaborated with producer [[Ryan Lewis]] as the duo [[Macklemore & Ryan Lewis]]. His releases to date include one [[mixtape]], three EPs, and four albums.

Macklemore and Lewis' single "[[Thrift Shop]]" reached number one on the U.S. [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in 2013.<ref name="billboard1">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/song/macklemore/1491648/thrift-shop|title=Thrift Shop, Macklemore|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> The single was dubbed by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' as the first song since 1994 to top the Hot 100 chart without the support of a major record label; however Macklemore, in a slightly unusual [[recording contract]], pays a nominal percentage of sales to use [[Warner Bros. Records]]'s radio promotion department to push his singles.<ref>{{cite web|title=Macklemore & Ryan Lewis Crash Radio With 'Thrift Shop'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1484126/macklemore-ryan-lewis-crash-radio-with-thrift-shop|publisher=Billboard|access-date=14 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Feeney|first=Nolan|title=Macklemore's 'Thrift Shop' Is First Indie Hit to Top Charts in Nearly Two Decades |url=http://newsfeed.time.com/2013/01/25/macklemores-thrift-shop-is-first-indie-hit-to-top-charts-in-nearly-two-decades/|publisher=Time|access-date=May 23, 2013|date=January 25, 2013}}</ref> Their second single, "[[Can't Hold Us]]", also peaked at number one on the Hot 100 chart, making Macklemore and Lewis the first duo in the chart's history to have their first two singles both reach the peak position.<ref>{{cite web |title=Macklemore and Ryan Lewis's "Can't Hold Us" Makes Hot 100 History |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1560817/macklemore-ryan-lewis-cant-hold-us-tops-hot-100}}</ref> Their debut studio album ''[[The Heist (album)|The Heist]]'' was released on 9 October 2012, and charted at number 2 on the [[U.S. Billboard 200|U.S. ''Billboard'' 200]]. The duo won four [[Grammy Awards]] at the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards|2014 ceremony]], including [[Best New Artist]], [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] (''The Heist''), [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song|Best Rap Song]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance|Best Rap Performance]] ("Thrift Shop").

Macklemore and Lewis' second album, ''[[This Unruly Mess I've Made]]'', was released on February 26, 2016. In June 2017, Macklemore released "[[Glorious (Macklemore song)|Glorious]]", a single featuring [[Skylar Grey]], which marked his return to the music industry as well as his first major single produced without Lewis since his mainstream breakthrough. Macklemore released a second solo studio album, ''[[Gemini (Macklemore album)|Gemini]]'', in September that year.

==Early life and influences==
Ben Haggerty was born in [[Seattle, Washington]], on June 19, 1983, one of two sons born to Bill Haggerty and Julie Schott. He was raised with his brother Tim in Seattle's [[Capitol Hill (Seattle)|Capitol Hill]] neighborhood.<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web| url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/macklemore-mn0001015772/biography| title=Artist Biography [Macklemore]| first= David |last=Jeffries|publisher=[[AllMusic.com]]|access-date= January 4, 2015}}</ref><ref name="buzzfeed-news-2017"/> He has Irish heritage.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rapireland.com/ri-exclusive-macklemore-interview-with-rap-ireland/ |title=RI Exclusive: Macklemore Interview with Rap Ireland |publisher=RapIreland.com |date=November 21, 2011 |access-date=January 27, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190813144354/http://www.rapireland.com/ri-exclusive-macklemore-interview-with-rap-ireland/ |archive-date=August 13, 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Haggerty was six years old when [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] first came into his life by way of [[Digital Underground]].<ref name="bet.com 1">[http://www.bet.com/video/106andpark/106guestrewind/macklemore-and-ryan-lewis-106-and-park-3067.html 106 & Park : Macklemore and Ryan Lewis]. BET (November 13, 2012). Retrieved on April 11, 2013.</ref><ref name="bet.com 2">[http://www.bet.com/video/106andpark/exclusives/macklemore-and-ryan-lewis-backstage-3067.html 106 & Park : Macklemore and Ryan Lewis]. BET (November 14, 2012). Retrieved on April 11, 2013.</ref> According to a [[YouTube]] interview, he listened to "parental advisory" music from the radio when he was an underage youth.<ref name="ReferenceA">{{cite video|work=[[YouTube]]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YWe9rnu32Kk| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/YWe9rnu32Kk| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live|title=Macklemore talks Otherside, Lil Wayne, Substance Abuse, Seattle Hip Hop and more|date=April 23, 2012|access-date=December 30, 2012}}{{cbignore}}</ref> At the age of 15, he began to write lyrics.<ref name="bet.com 1"/><ref name="bet.com 2"/>

At the time he started to sing, Haggerty listened to "a lot of [[East Coast hip hop|East Coast underground hip hop]]", listing [[Hieroglyphics (group)|Hieroglyphics]], [[Freestyle Fellowship]], [[Aceyalone]], [[Living Legends (group)|Living Legends]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[Mobb Deep]], [[Nas]], and [[Talib Kweli]] as his major influences.<ref name="ReferenceA"/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/who-is-macklemore/|title=Who Is Macklemore?|author=Eric Diep|date=October 9, 2012|work=Complex|access-date=December 28, 2012}}</ref> He was interested in reaching a younger generation through his music and became part of a program focusing on education and cultural identity called "Gateways for Incarcerated Youth", where he facilitated music workshops.<ref name="matson1">{{cite news|last=Matson|first=Andrew|title=Seattle Rapper Macklemore Ready to take a shot at Pop Stardom|url=http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/musicnightlife/2014178847_macklemore10.html|publisher=The Seattle Times|access-date=December 12, 2011|date=February 9, 2011}}</ref> Haggerty attended [[Garfield High School (Seattle, Washington)|Garfield High School]] and [[Nathan Hale High School (Washington)|Nathan Hale High School]]. At Hale, he developed the stage name "Professor Macklemore" for an art project involving a made-up superhero,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/seattle-rapper-macklemore-ready-to-take-a-shot-at-pop-stardom/|title=Seattle rapper Macklemore ready to take a shot at pop stardom|date=2011-02-09|work=The Seattle Times|access-date=2017-09-10|language=en-US}}</ref> and at Garfield, he started a hip-hop group named Elevated Elements with other students. The group released an album, ''Progress'', in 2000.<ref>{{cite news|last=Davis|first=Brangien|title=2010 Spotlight Award: Macklemore|url=http://www.seattlemag.com/article/arts/spotlight-award-winners/2010-spotlight-award-macklemore|publisher=Seattle Magazine|access-date=October 19, 2011|date=July 21, 2011}}</ref>

Haggerty enrolled at the [[College of Santa Fe]] for a year, later saying in an interview that "[i]t was a very pivotal time in a lot of ways. [[Santa Fe, New Mexico|Santa Fe]] was the place where I got good at rapping".<ref name="abq-journal-2017">{{cite web |last1=Gomez |first1=Adrian |title=Back where it started: Former College of Santa Fe student and Grammy-winning rapper back in the 505 |url=https://www.abqjournal.com/1077100/rap-albuquerque-2.html |publisher=[[Albuquerque Journal]] |access-date=16 July 2019 |date=October 13, 2017 |quote=New Mexico has a special place in my heart,” he says. “I went to the College of Santa Fe for a year, spent a lot of time between Santa Fe and Albuquerque. It was a very pivotal time in a lot of ways. Santa Fe was the place where I got good at rapping; just being in my dorm room, not going to many classes and really focusing on my craft. That’s when I felt I was starting to make music that was different than just being in high school and rapping, when I was like ‘I really like this, I wanna listen to this.’ It’s awesome, Santa Fe is the place where I got my fake I.D. taken back in the day, so there’s a lot of love for that place and I always love coming back to New Mexico.}}</ref> After not getting into the music program, he moved back to Seattle.<ref name="buzzfeed-news-2017">{{cite web |last1=Petersen |first1=Anne Helen |title=How Macklemore Laid Down His White Burden |url=https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/annehelenpetersen/how-macklemore-laid-down-his-white-burden |publisher=[[BuzzFeed News]] |access-date=16 July 2019 |date=October 4, 2017}}</ref> Haggerty later enrolled at [[The Evergreen State College]] and completed his bachelor's degree in 2009.<ref name="matson1"/><ref name="behind the awesome">{{cite web|last=Shea|first=Carolyn|title=Behind the Awesome|url=http://www.evergreen.edu/magazine/2013spring/behind-the-awesome.htm|work=Evergreen Magazine|publisher=The Evergreen State College|access-date=January 27, 2014|date=Spring 2013}}</ref>

==Career==

===2000–2008: ''The Language of My World''===
[[Image:Macklemore & Ryan Lewis at Sasquatch 2011.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Macklemore & Ryan Lewis at [[Sasquatch! Music Festival]] (2011)]]
In 2000, Haggerty recorded a mixtape titled ''Open Your Eyes'' under the name Professor Macklemore, which he distributed himself and released on October 21, 2000.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/474720/macklemore-reps-talk-the-heist-debut-diy-marketing-plan | title=Macklemore & Reps Talk 'The Heist' Debut & DIY Marketing Plan | publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=October 10, 2012 | access-date=April 7, 2013 | author=Horowitz, Steven J.}}</ref> Haggerty dropped "Professor" from his name and began working on his first official full-length album, called ''[[The Language of My World]]''. The album was released on January 1, 2005, with its lead single, "Love Song", being announced the same day. "Love Song" featured singer Evan Roman, and was produced by Budo, who would later go on to produce several more tracks for Macklemore.

In 2006, Haggerty first met his future collaborator [[Ryan Lewis]].<ref name="ReferenceB" /> Lewis, who would go on to release two albums with Haggerty as [[Macklemore & Ryan Lewis]], spent a few years working on Macklemore's promotion as a photographer. The two musicians would soon become good friends but would not formalize their collaborative efforts until 2009.<ref name="ReferenceA" /> In the meantime, Macklemore kept busy as a solo artist, appearing on [[The Physics (music group)|The Physics]]' song "Good" in 2009,<ref>{{cite web|title=The Physics - "Good feat. Macklemore"|url=http://www.thatsthatish.com/2009/08/physics-good-feat-macklemore.html|publisher=That's That...|access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> as well as performing at the Seattle major arts and music festival [[Bumbershoot]] in 2006, 2009, and 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bumbershoot History|url=https://onereel.org/bumbershoot-history|publisher=One Reel|access-date=January 26, 2020}}</ref>

Macklemore's second mixtape, ''[[The Unplanned Mixtape]],'' was released on September 7, 2009. It would later reach No. 7 on the [[iTunes]] Hip Hop chart. The [[mixtape]] was accompanied by the singles "The Town" and "And We Danced", the latter of which featured singer Ziggy Stardust. "The Town" was later remixed by Sabzi of the [[Blue Scholars]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Macklemore - "The Town" (Prod. Scenik; Beats by Vitamin D)|url=http://www.thatsthatish.com/2009/09/macklemore-town-prod-scenik-beats-by.html|publisher=That's That}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Macklemore - "The Town (Sabzi Remix)"|url=http://www.thatsthatish.com/2010/03/macklemore-town-sabzi-remix.html}}</ref>

Redux'' EP. Their album ''[[The Heist (album)|The Heist]]'' was released in October 2012 and debuted on the U.S. [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] at number 2 of the week dated October 27, 2012, selling over 78,000 copies. "[[Same Love]]" was released on July 18, 2012 and after "[[White Walls]]". In January 2013, [[Music Choice]] featured Macklemore in the brand new series "Primed", which focuses on emerging artists. In May 2013, Haggerty was featured on [[Clinton Sparks]]'s single "Gold Rush", along with [[2 Chainz]] and D.A.<ref>{{cite web|title=Clinton Sparks - "Gold Rush" (feat. On January 15, 2016, Macklemore released a teaser video on his [[YouTube]] channel revealing the name of his third studio album, ''[[This Unruly Mess I've Made]]'', and announcing that it was scheduled to be released on February 26, 2016. On January 22, 2016, the duo released "[[White Privilege II]]", the second single on ''This Unruly Mess I've Made''. On February 26, 2016, Macklemore and Ryan Lewis released the album ''This Unruly Mess I've Made''.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/macklemore-and-ryan-lewis-drop-black-lives-matter-inspired-white-privilege-ii-20160122|title=Macklemore and Ryan Lewis Drop Black Lives Matter-Inspired 'White Privilege II'|first=Tessa|last=Stuart|date=January 22, 2016}}</ref> Also in 2016, Macklemore released two solo singles, "Drug Dealer" and "Wednesday Morning".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-macklemores-meditative-addiction-ballad-drug-dealer-w444468|title=Hear Macklemore's Meditative Addiction Ballad 'Drug Dealer'|date=October 12, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/macklemore-finds-post-election-courage-on-wednesday-morning-w451302|title=Macklemore Finds Post-Election Courage on 'Wednesday Morning'|date=November 18, 2016}}</ref> "Drug Dealer" features Macklemore rapping about his previous addictions and is featured in a documentary that includes clips of Macklemore discussing drug abuse with President [[Barack Obama]]. "Wednesday Morning" was released after the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 US Election]] and features Macklemore rapping about the political future of the country. The tracks were produced by [[Budo (musician)|Budo]]. On June 15, 2017, Macklemore announced via his official [[Instagram]] that the duo were on hiatus.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2017/06/15/macklemore-ryan-lewis-announce-decision-part-ways/|title=Macklemore & Ryan Lewis Announce Decision to Part Ways|date=June 15, 2017|website=BallerStatus.com|access-date=July 3, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2017/06/15/macklemore-parts-ways-with-ryan-lewis-announces-solo-album/|title=Macklemore Parts Ways with Ryan Lewis, Announces Solo Album|date= June 15, 2017|website=Rap-Up|access-date=July 3, 2017}}</ref>

===2017–present: ''Gemini''===
On June 15, 2017, Macklemore released "[[Glorious (Macklemore song)|Glorious]]", featuring American singer [[Skylar Grey]], as the lead single from Macklemore's second solo studio album ''[[Gemini (Macklemore album)|Gemini]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Anna Gaca|date=June 15, 2017|title=Macklemore Releases New Song "Glorious" Off First "Solo" Album in 12 Years|url=http://www.spin.com/2017/06/macklemore-glorious-skylar-grey-listen/|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date=July 1, 2017}}</ref> On July 26, 2017, Macklemore released "[[Marmalade (song)|Marmalade]]", featuring American rapper [[Lil Yachty]], as the second single from Macklemore's upcoming album. "[[Good Old Days (Macklemore song)|Good Old Days]]", a collaboration with [[Kesha]], was released as a single on October 9, 2017. Macklemore released ''Gemini'' on September 22. The album was produced by longtime collaborator Budo.{{citation needed|date=June 2018}}

On October 1, Macklemore performed "Same Love" in a set at the opening of [[2017 NRL Grand Final|the Grand Final]] of the [[National Rugby League]] in [[Sydney]], Australia — something that was considered by some to be controversial in the middle of a [[Australian Marriage Law Postal Survey|national survey on same sex marriage]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=ABCnews|date=October 2, 2017|title=Macklemore declares 'equality for all' after singing Same Love at NRL grand final|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2017-10-01/macklemore-performs-same-love-at-nrl-grand-final/9005752 |work=[[ABC News (Australia)|ABC]]|access-date=October 2, 2017}}</ref> Macklemore stated that it was one of his best performances due to the circumstances and thanked the fans in Sydney for the reception that he received throughout.

On December 11, 2017, Macklemore announced an upcoming co-headlining tour with Kesha titled The Adventures of Kesha and Macklemore.<ref name="Kesha Tour">{{cite web|url=http://www.keshaofficial.com/#lower|title=Tour Dates|publisher=Kesha's Official Website|access-date=December 11, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Macklemore Tour">{{cite web|url=http://macklemore.com/shows/|title=Shows|publisher=Macklemore's Official Website|access-date=December 11, 2017}}</ref> This is Macklemore's fifth headlining tour, promoting his album ''Gemini'' and Kesha's sixth headlining tour, promoting her third solo album ''[[Rainbow (Kesha album)|Rainbow]]'' (2017)<ref name="Announcement">{{cite web|last=Reed|first=Ryan|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/kesha-macklemore-plot-joint-2018-tour-w513743|title=Kesha, Macklemore Plot Joint 2018 Tour|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=December 11, 2017|access-date=December 11, 2017}}</ref> The tour began in Phoenix on June 6, 2018, and concluded in Tampa on August 5, 2018.{{fact|date=July 2021}}

==Personal life==
[[File:Macklemore at the Super Bowl XLVIII (crop1).jpg|thumb|right|Macklemore at the official [[Seattle Seahawks]] post-game party in [[Jersey City]] after [[Super Bowl XLVIII]] (February 2014)]]
Macklemore became engaged to his girlfriend of seven years, Tricia Davis, on January 21, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://instagram.com/p/UxU9qBwKy4/ |title=Photo by macklemore • Instagram |publisher=Instagram.com |access-date=February 7, 2013}}</ref> On January 3, 2015, he announced on Twitter that he and his fiancée were expecting their first child that May.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2015/01/03/macklemore-tricia-davis-pregnant-expecting-first-child/|title=Baby on the Way for Macklemore|publisher=People.com|date=January 3, 2015|access-date=January 3, 2015}}</ref> After the release of "Growing Up (Sloane's Song)", which featured English singer-songwriter [[Ed Sheeran]], the couple announced that their daughter, Sloane Ava Simone Haggerty,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/6AgWvPQK7a/|title=Ben Haggerty on Instagram|date=August 5, 2015|publisher=Instagram|access-date=July 3, 2017}}</ref> had been born on May 29.<ref>{{cite web|title=Trisha Davis Instagram|url=https://instagram.com/p/6Ai0XnSCyC/|publisher=Instagram|access-date=September 8, 2015}}</ref> Following the birth of Sloane, they married on June 27, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lynch|first1=Joe|title=Macklemore Got Secretly Married After Same Sex Marriage Ruling|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6655954/macklemore-married-wife-tricia-davis-daughter|website=Billboard|date=August 5, 2015}}</ref> In September 2017, the couple announced that they were expecting their second child.<ref>{{cite web|last=Juneau|first=Jen|title=Second Child on the Way for Macklemore and Wife Tricia|url=http://people.com/babies/macklemore-wife-tricia-pregnant-expecting-second-child/|website=People|date=September 28, 2017}}</ref> On April 3, 2018, Macklemore announced during a performance at the [[3Arena]] that his wife had given birth to their second daughter. He did not tell the crowd her name.

Macklemore voiced his support of [[LGBT rights by country or territory|LGBT rights]] and same-sex marriage in the song "[[Same Love]]", which also condemns [[homophobia]] in mainstream hip-hop, society, and mass media.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.studio360.org/2012/nov/30/macklemores-gay-anthem/ |title=Macklemore's Gay Anthem - Interview |publisher=Studio360.org |date=November 30, 2012 |access-date=February 7, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130204070807/http://www.studio360.org/2012/nov/30/macklemores-gay-anthem/ |archive-date=February 4, 2013 }}</ref>

In August 2008, Macklemore admitted himself into rehab for drug addiction and alcoholism,<ref>{{cite web|author=Vozick-Levinson, Simon |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/macklemore-opens-up-about-his-struggle-to-stay-sober-20130328 |title=Macklemore Opens Up About His Struggle to Stay Sober |work=Rolling Stone |date=March 28, 2013 |access-date=January 27, 2014}}</ref><ref name=GQMay2013>{{cite news|last=Franklin|first=Oliver|title=Pumped up on "Thrift Shop": Macklemore & Ryan Lewis storm London|url=http://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/entertainment/articles/2013-05/28/macklemore-ryan-lewis-live-review-london|access-date=June 13, 2013|newspaper=GQ|date=May 29, 2013|agency=Condé Nast UK 2013|quote=there was even bigger cheers for "Same Love", a heartfelt rap about same-sex marriage. When Haggerty prefaced "Starting Over" with a confession that he has been sober since 2008, the Empire went wild.|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130610072211/http://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/entertainment/articles/2013-05/28/macklemore-ryan-lewis-live-review-london|archive-date=June 10, 2013}}</ref> and celebrated three years of sobriety before a brief relapse in 2011, which he describes in his song "Starting Over".<ref>{{cite web|last=Matson |first=Andrew |url=http://seattletimes.com/html/matsononmusic/2019373556_macklemore.html?prmid=4939 |title=Macklemore is back with a little help from his friends |publisher=The Seattle Times |access-date=January 27, 2014}}</ref> He said in a 2012 documentary that he spent most of his twenties trying to combat his addictions and destructive way of life:{{quote| "I want to be someone who is respected and not just in terms of my music. I want to be respected in terms of the way that I treat people... Music is my creative outlet in terms of expressing what is important to me; what has importance, what has a value. And I want to be respected for that."<ref name=ReferenceB>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JErUzr8GSvU Jabari Presents: Macklemore & Ryan Lewis (Documentary)]. YouTube. Retrieved on December 30, 2012.</ref>}} On September 29, 2018, Macklemore headlined Recovery Fest in [[Pawtucket, Rhode Island]].<ref>{{Cite news |url = http://www.providencejournal.com/news/20180802/macklemore-to-headline-recovery-fest-concert-at-pawtuckets-mccoy-stadium |title = Macklemore to headline Recovery Fest concert at Pawtucket's McCoy Stadium |work = Providence Journal |date = August 2, 2018}}</ref> Recovery Fest was formed to have a drug- and alcohol-free concert and support charities that worked in combating opioid addiction.<ref>{{Cite news |url = https://www.apnews.com/067de2496d904d27993e78c3261c8524 |title = Coming clean: Public embrace for celeb addicts offers hope |work = Associated Press |date = October 20, 2018}}</ref>

Macklemore dedicated his song "My Oh My" to sportscaster [[Dave Niehaus]], who died in November 2010, with a performance before 50,000 fans who attended the 2011 Mariners [[Opening Day]] at [[Safeco Field]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Contributor |url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/681567-seattle-mariners-my-oh-my-seattle-rappers-tribute-to-dave-niehaus |title=Seattle Mariners: My Oh My; Seattle Rapper's Tribute to Dave Niehaus |publisher=BleacherReport.com |date=April 28, 2011 |access-date=January 27, 2014}}</ref> Following the performance, Macklemore and Ryan Lewis were each awarded a custom jersey embedded with a Mariners patch. Ryan Lewis announced that all proceeds from the song benefit the Rainier Vista Boys and Girls Club.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvNQWQSwmow Macklemore and Ryan Lewis – My Oh My (Official Video)]. YouTube (January 12, 2011). Retrieved on December 30, 2012.</ref> 

Macklemore is also a fan of the [[Seattle Seahawks]] and created a 12th Man promotional video as well as performed at the 2014 NFC Championship game in Seattle. Macklemore is still a fan of the now-[[Seattle SuperSonics relocation to Oklahoma City|relocated]] [[Seattle SuperSonics]], as he can be seen sporting a Sonics jersey at times.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://keyw.com/49ers-fan-jersey-autographed-but-not-they-way-they-expected/|access-date=3 February 2014|title=Macklemore Signs 49er Fan's Jersey — But Not They Way They Expected [PHOTO]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.latimes.com/sports/la-sp-macklemore-seahawks-carroll-20140126,0,7235539.story|access-date=3 February 2014|title=Macklemore, Pete Carroll are in a mutual admiration society | work=Los Angeles Times|first=Sam|last=Farmer|date=January 25, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.seahawks.com/news/2014/01/17/macklemore-ryan-lewis-perform-halftime-nfc-championship/|access-date=7 August 2015|title=Macklemore & Ryan Lewis to Perform at Halftime of NFC Championship [VIDEO]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160113052033/http://www.seahawks.com/news/2014/01/17/macklemore-ryan-lewis-perform-halftime-nfc-championship|archive-date=13 January 2016|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> In August 2019, Macklemore joined the ownership group of [[Seattle Sounders FC]], the local [[Major League Soccer]] club.<ref>{{cite news |last=Evans |first=Jayda |date=August 13, 2019 |title=Russell Wilson, Ciara, Macklemore, Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and more join Sounders ownership |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/russell-wilson-ciara-macklemore-microsoft-execs-and-more-join-sounders-ownership-group/ |work=The Seattle Times |access-date=September 1, 2019}}</ref>

Macklemore also has an eclectic taste in art, including outlandish garments spouting sequins, fringe and feathers, a kitschy velvet painting of a [[bald eagle]], an oil painting of [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] dancing and a [[Dan Lacey]] painting of a nude [[Justin Bieber]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/why-macklemore-risked-it-all-for-white-privilege-ii-20160310?page=2|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160313143349/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/why-macklemore-risked-it-all-for-white-privilege-ii-20160310?page=2|url-status=dead|archive-date=March 13, 2016|title=Why Macklemore Risked It All to Create 'White Privilege II'}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/macklemore-nude-painting-of-justin-bieber_us_56e47822e4b065e2e3d632f7|title=Macklemore Casually Owns This Bizarre Nude Painting Of Justin Bieber|first=Lily|last=Karlin|date=12 March 2016|via=Huff Post}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/748087/macklemore-owns-a-painting-of-naked-justin-bieber-balancing-pancakes-on-his-boner|title=Macklemore Owns a Very Interesting Painting of Bieber Naked|date=March 11, 2016}}</ref>

On May 14, 2016, Macklemore appeared in [[Barack Obama]]'s weekly address to talk about the dangers of [[Opioid use disorder|addiction to opioids]] and prescription [[Analgesic|painkillers]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Obama, rapper Macklemore call for more help for drug addicts|url=https://www.reuters.com/video/2016/05/14/obama-rapper-macklemore-call-for-more-he?videoId=368496570&videoChannel=1|access-date=2016-05-15}}</ref> Macklemore talked about his own experiences with abuse of painkillers, stating "When you're going through it, it's hard to imagine anything being worse than addiction. But the shame and stigma associated with the disease keeps too many people from seeking the help they actually need. Addiction isn't a personal choice or a personal failure."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/arts-and-entertainment/wp/2016/05/14/macklemore/|title=Macklemore and Obama talk opioid addiction|last=McCarthy|first=Ellen|date=2016-05-14|newspaper=The Washington Post|language=en-US|issn=0190-8286|access-date=2016-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.news.yahoo.com/obama-macklemore-pitch-better-opioid-treatment-022208750.html|title=Obama, Macklemore make pitch for better opioid treatment|website=uk.news.yahoo.com|access-date=2016-05-15|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160523194351/https://uk.news.yahoo.com/obama-macklemore-pitch-better-opioid-treatment-022208750.html|archive-date=2016-05-23}}</ref>
