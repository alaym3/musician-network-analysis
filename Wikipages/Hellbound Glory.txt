'''Hellbound Glory''' is an American [[Country music|country]] and [[roots rock]] band, featuring singer-songwriter Leroy Virgil. The band was originally formed in [[Reno]], [[Nevada]], United States, in 2008 by Virgil. Describing the band’s style as “[[Americana music|Americana]],” Virgil credits his influences as “[[Hank Williams]], [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]], and [[Hank Williams Jr.]]”<ref>{{cite web|last=Lacy|first=Eric|title=Hellbound Glory frontman on upcoming Kid Rock 'Rebel Soul' tour: 'It's going to be a hoedown!|url=http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/detroit/index.ssf/2012/12/hellbound_glory_frontman_on_up.htm|publisher=Mlive.com|accessdate=16 March 2014}}</ref>

According to The [[Oklahoma Gazette]] (February 26, 2014), "Hellbound Glory has three studio albums, and Virgil is at work on new material."<ref>{{cite news|last=Chambers|first=Kelley|title=Hell-bent|url=http://www.okgazette.com/oklahoma/article-20595-hell-bent.html|newspaper=[[Oklahoma Gazette]]|date=2014-02-26}}</ref>

Hellbound Glory is signed to The Agency Group LTD for worldwide booking representation.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Agency Group Artist Page |url=http://www.theagencygroup.com/artist/hellbound-glory/ |publisher=Theagencygroup.com |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140317033936/http://www.theagencygroup.com/artist/hellbound-glory/ |archivedate=2014-03-17 }}</ref>

==Formation and history==
The band was formed in the early 2000s after founding member, Leroy Virgil, relocated to Reno, Nevada. Originally from the town of [[Aberdeen, Washington]] where he played in the band Soylint Green; Virgil found inspiration for the band and material in his adopted new home, "It’s influenced by the Reno nightlife, experiences I’ve had, women I’ve met, fights I’ve gotten into—the local bar scene in general."<ref>{{cite web|last=Davis|first=Laura|title=Homeward bound|url=http://www.newsreview.com/reno/homeward-bound/content?oid=11634507|publisher=Reno News & Review|accessdate=16 March 2014}}</ref>

==Touring and appearances==
Hellbound Glory tours consistently in North America. In 2012 the band was tapped to perform as a featured artist on the [[Kid Rock]] Chillin’ the Most Cruise<ref>{{cite web|last=Lacy|first=Eric|title=Hellbound Glory frontman on upcoming Kid Rock 'Rebel Soul' tour: 'It's going to be a hoedown!'|url=http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/detroit/index.ssf/2012/12/hellbound_glory_frontman_on_up.html|website=MLive.com}}</ref>  and again supported Kid Rock in 2013 on the Rebel Soul Tour (28 dates) along with [[Buckcherry]];<ref>{{cite web|last=Kaufman|first=Spencer|title=Kid Rock Unveils 2013 ‘Rebel Soul’ U.S. Tour Dates Featuring Openers Buckcherry + Hellbound Glory|url=http://loudwire.com/kid-rock-2013-rebel-soul-u-s-tour-dates-openers-buckcherry-hellbound-glory/|website=Loudwire.com}}</ref> The first leg of which was publicly announced on November 27, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kid Rock Announces "Rebel Soul" Tour Dates |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2012/11/27/kid-rock-announces-rebel-soul-tour-dates.html|website=ABCNewsRadioOnline.com |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140317032257/http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2012/11/27/kid-rock-announces-rebel-soul-tour-dates.html |archivedate=2014-03-17 }}</ref>

2013 included two dates in support of [[Kid Rock]] and [[ZZ Top]] as part of the headliner's "Best Night Ever" package at [[DTE Energy Music Theatre]] in Clarkston, Michigan.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hellbound Glory support Kid Rock and ZZ Top|url=http://www.bandsintown.com/event/6454334-hellbound-glory-detroit-dte-energy-music-theater-2013?artist=Hellbound+Glory|website=Bandsintown.com|accessdate=26 July 2014}}</ref>

Subsequent touring in 2013 included a co-headline package with [[The Supersuckers]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Trigger|title=Hellbound Glory From The Empire Control Room|url=http://www.savingcountrymusic.com/hellbound-glory-from-the-empire-control-room|website=Savingcountrymusic.com}}</ref>

2014 tour dates included east coast support with [[Leon Russell]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Moser|first=John|title=REVIEW: Leon Russell takes Musikfest Cafe crowd on a musical thrill ride|url=http://blogs.mcall.com/lehighvalleymusic/2014/01/leon-russell-takes-musikfest-cafe-crowd-on-a-musical-thrill-ride-.html|website=Blogs.mcall.com}}</ref>

Hellbound Glory supported active rock band [[10 Years (band)|10 Years]] in March 2014 in support of the headliner's acoustic tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=10 Years support Dates|url=http://www.bandsintown.com/HellboundGlory/past_events?page=2|website=Bandsintown.com|accessdate=26 July 2014}}</ref>

==In the media==
[[LA Weekly]] Music Editor, Nicholas Pell, listed Hellbound Glory #2 in his list of "10 Country Artists You Should Be Listening To"] feature; describing the band's music in part as, "Think of them as evoking the energy of the wildest party Waylon, Willie and Bocephus ever had."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Pell|first1=Nicholas|title=10 Country Artists You Should Be Listening To|url=http://www.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2014/06/26/10-country-artists-you-should-be-listening-to?page=3|publisher=[[LA Weekly]]|accessdate=26 June 2014}}</ref>

[[Washington Post]] Music Editor, Chris Richards, featured Hellbound Glory's "LV" as a part of the newspaper's [https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/style-blog/wp/2014/06/25/the-months-best-music-miranda-lambert-popcaan-priests-and-more/ "This Month's Best Music"] column describing the release; "The “country” that country singers so often pine for can be a sanctuary, a playground, a paradise. But for Leroy Virgil of Hellbound Glory, it’s a twilight zone where his cell phone won’t work. “No service, so nervous,” he sings sheepishly on his confident new EP. “Nothin’ on the radio."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Richards|first1=Chris|title=The month’s best music: Miranda Lambert, Popcaan, Priests and more|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/style-blog/wp/2014/06/25/the-months-best-music-miranda-lambert-popcaan-priests-and-more/|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|accessdate=25 June 2014}}</ref>

Hellbound Glory songs receive consistent airplay on [[Outlaw Country (Sirius XM)]] Channel.

“Livin’ This Way,” from the album Scumbag Country, was featured in the major motion picture “[[Bad Grandpa]]” starring [[Johnny Knoxville]] released in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jackass Presents: Bad Grandpa (2013) Soundtracks|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3063516/soundtrack?ref_=ttspec_ql_trv_7|publisher=IMDB.com}}</ref>

In March 2013, Hellbound Glory, performed as a featured artist for CMT Edge Live in [[Nashville, TN]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Hellbound Glory - "Lost Cause (CMT Edge Live) "|url=http://www.cmt.com/videos/cmt-edge/hellbound-glory/890310/lost-cause-cmt-edge-live.jhtml|website=Cmt.com}}</ref>

In February 2013, Hellbound Glory performed exclusively at [[Elvis Presley]]'s [[Graceland]] on break from the Kid Rock Rebel Soul Tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=Photo Spotlight: Hellbound Glory Performs at Graceland|url=http://www.elvis.com/news/detail.aspx?id=6929|website=Elvis.com}}</ref>
