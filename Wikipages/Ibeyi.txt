'''Ibeyi''' is an Afro-French Cuban<ref name=Origins>{{cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iWAClRJ88-0|title=Clique x Ibeyi|date=2017-07-27|access-date=2018-09-07}}</ref> musical duo consisting of twin sisters Lisa-Kaindé Diaz and Naomi Diaz. The duo sings in English, French, [[Spanish language|Spanish]] and [[Yoruba language|Yoruba]],<ref name=Act>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/feb/15/sister-act-twins-ibeyi-music-french-cuban|title=Sister act: the twins behind Ibeyi|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|author=Rhick Samadder|date=2015-02-15|access-date=2015-02-16}}</ref> a language spoken by their ancestors before being taken to Cuba as slaves in the 1700s. In Yoruba, Ibeyi (''Ìbejì'') means "twins".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/best-sister-bands-siblings-haim-tegan-and-sara-ibeyi-beauty-bonnaroo|title=Decoding the Beauty Looks of Our Favorite Sister Bands: Haim, Tegan and Sara, and More|work=Vogue|first=Mackenzie| last=Wagoner|access-date=2018-04-13|language=en}}</ref>

Their music has elements of Yoruba, French and [[Afro-Cuban#Music|Afro-Cuban]], and fuses jazz with beats, samples with traditional instruments.,<ref name=twins>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/feb/20/ibeyi-review|title=Ibeyi review – twin-powered culture-clash charisma|newspaper=The Guardian|author=Betty Clarke|date=2015-02-20|access-date=2015-02-22}}</ref> Lisa, the lead singer,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/ibeyi-meet-the-soul-sisters-set-to-stun-this-year-s-festival-circuit-10237004.html|title=Ibeyi: meet the soul sisters set to stun this year’s festival circuit "Music has always been our way to be happy and to heal ourselves"|author=Hugh Montgomery|newspaper=The Independent|location=United Kingdom|date= May 9, 2015|access-date=October 8, 2015}}</ref> plays the piano;<ref name="Balfour">{{cite web|author=Jay Balfour |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.2429/title.ibeyi-ibeyi |title=Half Yoruba spiritual and half electronic R&B, Ibeyi’s debut is drenched in rhythm and quirky originality. |publisher=hiphopdx |date=2015-02-20 |access-date=2015-02-22}}</ref> Naomi plays the traditional Peruvian/Cuban percussion instruments [[cajón]] and [[Batá drum]].

==Early life and career==
[[File:Ibeyi by Maya Dagnino 2.jpg|thumb|Naomi Diaz (left) and Lisa-Kaindé Diaz (right)]]
The [[fraternal twins|fraternal]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=We Skyped with Ibeyi, the French-Cuban Twin Sisters Turning Their Heritage into the Sound of the Future|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/rpyemy/ibeyi-twins-interview-profile-2014|access-date=2021-10-19|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref> twins (born 13 December 1994 in [[Paris]]) lived in [[Havana]] for the first two years of their lives, then moved to Paris, where they were educated.<ref name="KEXP-FM_20150405" /> They visited Cuba yearly on holiday, but were primarily raised in Paris, where they currently reside.<ref name="Balfour" />

Their father was the famed Cuban percussionist [[Anga Díaz]], who has received a [[Grammy award]] for his work with the Latin jazz band [[Irakere]], and was also a member of [[Buena Vista Social Club]] ensemble, playing with [[Ibrahim Ferrer]], [[Rubén González (pianist)|Rubén González]] and [[Compay Segundo|Máximo Francisco Repilado Muñoz aka Compay Segundo]]. On his death in 2006, Naomi, then aged 11, learned to play his signature instrument, the cajón. Together the sisters studied Yoruba folk songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/blogs/ontherecord/2014/03/20/new-music-ibeyi-juce-akua/ |title=New Music – Ibeyi, Juce, Akua |first=Jim |last=Carroll|newspaper=Irish Times |date= 2014-03-20|access-date=2014-03-20}}</ref> Their mother is French-Venezuelan singer Maya Dagnino, who serves as their manager and encouraged Lisa-Kainde's songwriting.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/oct/30/ibeyi-french-cuban-twins-naomi-lisa-kainde-diaz-miguel-anga
|title=Meet Ibeyi: French-Cuban twins with a musical sixth sense
|newspaper=The Guardian
|date= October 30, 2014
|access-date=2015-05-01
| author=Tim Jonze}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/ibeyi-the-french-cuban-teenagers-about-to-take-the-music-world-by-storm-1.2099594 |title=Ibeyi: the French Cuban teenagers about to take the music world by storm
|newspaper=Irish Times
|date= February 15, 2015
|access-date=May 1, 2015
| author=Jim Carroll}}</ref>

In 2013, they signed to the record label, [[XL Recordings]].<ref name="Act" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lesinrocks.com/2014/04/13/musique/les-morceaux-de-la-semaine-de-jd-beauvallet-106-11495312/ |title=Les morceaux de la semaine de JD Beauvallet |publisher=Les Inrocks |access-date=2014-04-13}}</ref> Label owner [[Richard Russell (XL Recordings)|Richard Russell]] is the only other contributor to their [[Ibeyi (album)|self-titled debut album]], released in 2015.<ref name="Balfour" /> Released when they were 20, the album pays tribute to their deceased father and the track ''Yanira'' immortalizes the duo's older sister, who died in 2013. The song ends with a synth that sounds like a life-support monitor.<ref name="Balfour" />

In 2014, they received attention for the video for their album's second single, "River". The twins appear in a closeup shot throughout, taking turns having their heads forced underwater while the other sings.<ref name="Balfour" />

In 2016, the twins appeared in the short film for [[Beyoncé]]'s album ''[[Lemonade (Beyoncé album)|Lemonade]]'' as members of Beyonce's farm utopia that offers a safe space for black women. In May 2016, Ibeyi returned to Havana for live performances at the CHANEL 2016 Cruise collection in Cuba and the international music festival MUSICABANA.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musicabana.com/line-up/|title=MUSICABANA - CUBA|website=Musicabana.com|access-date=29 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160510005311/http://musicabana.com/line-up/|archive-date=10 May 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Their 2017 album ''Ash'' was nominated for [[IMPALA]]'s European Album of the Year Award.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://impalamusic.org/content/22-acts-running-best-european-independent-album |title=22 acts in the running for best European independent album |work=IMPALA |date=6 March 2018 |access-date=6 March 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180309054152/http://impalamusic.org/content/22-acts-running-best-european-independent-album |archive-date=9 March 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Influences==
As well as their father [[Anga Díaz]], the duo claims [[Frank Ocean]], [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]], and [[King Krule]] among their influences.<ref name="about">{{cite web|url=http://www.ibeyi.fr/stylesheets/images/about-text.svg|title=About|publisher=Ibeyi|access-date=2015-02-22|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150227165359/http://www.ibeyi.fr/stylesheets/images/about-text.svg|archive-date=2015-02-27}}</ref> In live shows, they have covered rapper [[Jay Electronica]]’s ''Better in Tune with the Infinite'' and others.

Their recordings also show a strong and spiritual connection to their Yoruba roots, as do the name and themes.{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} They also pay tribute to [[Santería]], a [[syncretism]] practiced by many Afro-Cubans.{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}
