'''Maurice "Verse" Simmonds''' is a Puerto Rican/Virgin Islander rapper, singer, composer, record producer, and record executive.<ref name="vswfdaf"/> Based in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]]<ref name="vsdDiscography"/> but raised in the [[Virgin Islands]], he moved to the [[United States]] after high school and he formed the production duo the Jugganauts in the 1990s. The duo has since co-written and produced tracks such as "[[Man Down (song)|Man Down]]" by [[Rihanna]]<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> and "[[Who Gon Stop Me]]" by [[Kanye West]] and [[Jay-Z]], both of which charted prominently on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/>

Around 2007, Simmonds began releasing his own solo material, and his 2011 single "[[Boo Thang]]" featuring [[Kelly Rowland]] on [[Def Jam Recordings|Def Jam]] peaked at No. 44 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.<ref name="vsbbillboard"/> He has also released a number of full-length mixtapes, rapping and singing over many of the tracks and also bringing in guest artists such as [[Young Jeezy]], [[Akon]], [[Snoop Dogg]], and [[Travis Porter]].<ref name="vsosteverave"/> Under his birth name or as Verse Simmonds he has co-written a number of charting R&B songs, such as "[[Confident (Justin Bieber song)|Confident]]" by [[Justin Bieber]] and [[Chance the Rapper]], and "[[New Flame]]" by [[Chris Brown]], [[Usher (singer)|Usher]] and [[Rick Ross]]. The latter earned Simmonds his first [[Grammy Award]] nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Song|Best R&B Song]] in 2015.<ref name="latimesgrammys"/>

==Early life and music==
Maurice Simmonds was born in [[Puerto Rico]]. Raised in the [[Virgin Islands]],<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/><ref name="vsvthebmbversimmonds"/><ref name="vstbillboard"/> he spent his youth on the island of [[Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands|Saint Thomas]].<ref name="vsdDiscography"/> Introduced to music primarily through the radio, in high school he and several friends formed the musical group 2 Xtreme, performing at local venues and talent shows.<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> He moved to [[Fort Lauderdale]] in [[Florida]] after graduating high school, where he continued to work on [[hip hop production]] and songwriting.<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> In 1996<ref name="vsdDiscography"/> in Fort Lauderdale Simmonds met and befriended the producer and songwriter Shama Joseph, and together they formed the production duo the Jugganauts.<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> The Jugganauts moved to [[Los Angeles, California]] in 2003 to work on a number of projects for major record labels.<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> Several years later Simmonds was contacted by Theron Thomas, a childhood friend and member of the production duo [[Rock City (production duo)|Rock City]]. Thomas convinced the Jugganauts<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> to move to<ref name="vsdDiscography"/> [[Atlanta, Georgia]],<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> where they began recording with producers such as [[Rodney Jerkins]] and [[Akon]].<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/>

==Music career==
===First solo releases (2009-2010)===

After moving to Atlanta Simmonds began working more intently on his own solo material, both performing and recording original material around the city.<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> In February 2007, he became associated as a solo artist with Cloud 9 and Raydar Management,<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> and one of his live shows led to a contract with [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> Simmonds released his first solo track on [[Interscope]] on June 30, 2009. Titled "Buy You a Round (Up and Down)," [[AllMusic]] called it "a cocky, percussion-heavy club single" that "enabled the upcoming singer, songwriter, and occasional MC to tour extensively."<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> "Buy You A Round (Up And Down)" peaked at No. 55 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart and at No. 63 on the  [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay]] chart.<ref name="vsbbillboard"/> An official music video for the track was uploaded to [[VEVO]] on November 22, 2009.<ref name="vshbuyround"/>

Simmonds released a mixtape in August 2009, titled ''The Sextape Chronicles.'' Sham and Simmonds served as executive producers, with Raydar, Cloud9, [[Darkchild]], and DJ Trauma listed in the credits as presenters.<ref name="vsfversandsham"/> In December 2009, a track with co-writing by Simmonds was included on the [[R. Kelly]] album ''[[Untitled (R. Kelly album)|Untitled]]''. The album ultimately went on to be nominated for a [[Grammy Award]].<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> In 2010, a number of Simmonds' recent tracks were included in the free ''V The Compilation Vol. 2'' mixtape, which was presented by DJ Ill Will.<ref name="vsgillwill"/>

==="Boo Thang" and mixtapes (2011-2012)===
He signed to [[Konvict Muzik]]<ref name="rapbupbehindvideo"/> as a singer-songwriter and producer in January 2011,<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/> also becoming associated with MVD Inc. and the Konlive imprint.<ref name="vstbillboard"/> Later in 2011<ref name="vstbillboard"/> he signed to Bu Vision, a label run by [[Akon]]'s brother Bu Thiam and affiliated with [[Def Jam]]<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> and Konlive.<ref name="vstbillboard"/> Simmonds' August 2011 mixtape ''The Sextape Chronicles 2'' was released through Bu Vision, Konlive, and Carteblanche, and listed hosts such as [[Angela Yee]].<ref name="vsjteentied"/> In 2011 he worked as The Jugganauts with his production partner Sham on both writing and producing<ref name="Juggernauts"/> the [[reggae music|reggae]]-infused song "[[Man Down (song)|Man Down]]" for [[Rihanna]]'s album  ''[[Loud (Rihanna album)|Loud]]''.<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> Also with Sham, he worked on "[[Who Gon Stop Me]]" by [[Kanye West]] and [[Jay-Z]]<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> for the ''[[Watch the Throne]]'' album.<ref name="Juggernauts"/><ref name="vserowalnds"/>

Through Bu Vision, in September 2011, Simmonds released his single "Boo Thang," featuring [[Kelly Rowland]].<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> As described by [[MTV]], apart from Rowland's vocals, "Verse starts by rapping over a midtempo piano-laced track. His raps give way to a melodic refrain that he sings, leading him all the way to the hook."<ref name="vserowalnds"/> A music video directed by [[Gil Green (director)|Gil Green]] and starring Rowlands and Simmonds was released to accompany the single.<ref name="rapbupbehindvideo"/> "Boo Thang" peaked at No. 66 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart and at No. 44 on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay chart.<ref name="vsbbillboard"/> The song was ultimately included on Simmonds' mixtape ''Sextape Chronicles 2.''<ref name="vserowalnds"/>

===Mixtapes and EPs (2012-2013)===
His April 2012 mixtape ''Sex Love & Hip Hop'' was hosted by [[DJ Drama]],<ref name="vsivibe"/> with other guest artists such as [[Yo Gotti]], Kelly Rowland,<ref name="vslvolumeone"/> [[Snoop Dogg]] and [[Akon]].<ref name="recruitsnsnoo"/> The third in his ''Sex Tape Chronicles'' series, Simmonds at the time called it his "biggest mixtape."<ref name="recruitsnsnoo"/> In August 2012, he self-released ''A Sextape Chronicle,'' a six-track EP,<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> and his October 2012 mixtape ''Fuck Your Feelings'' featured guest artists such as [[Red Cafe]], [[Gucci Mane]], and [[Young Jeezy]].<ref name="vsmfuckyoufr"/> In late 2012, he contributed to studio sessions involving recording artist [[Omotola Jalade Ekeinde]].<ref name="forgeingacts"/>

Simmonds' track "Fully Loaded," featuring [[Young Jeezy]], came out on June 11, 2013 on BuVision,<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> and also that year Simmonds released the "Who U Wit" with features from Trouble and [[Yo Gotti]].<ref name="vsaVERSEsminnondsitunes"/> Simmonds' mixtape ''Fuck Your Feelings 2'' was released for free on June 12, 2013.<ref name="vsmfuckyoufr"/> His six track EP<ref name="vsqcrerseimmonds"/> ''Circa 96: A Prelude to 1996'' came out in August 2013 as a free mixtape.<ref name="vsncirca"/> [[Drop FM]] gave it a score of 7/10 and a positive review, writing that Simmonds can "create a ’90s-inspired project that still sounds fresh and modern." The review opined the fact that Simmonds "stayed true to the 1996 concept by using traditional R&B sounds and a throwback vocal delivery," and "made songs that should be able to keep the listeners' attention throughout."<ref name="vspdesdrop"/> Also in 2013, Simmonds' collaborated with [[Cap 1 (rapper)|Cap 1]] on a mixtape titled ''Champagne Poets''. Released in November 2013, it featured artists such as [[2 Chainz]] and [[Travis Porter]].<ref name="vsosteverave"/>

===Recent releases (2013-2015)===
In 2013, he participated as a writer on a number of collaborations, for example co-writing the [[Justin Bieber]] song "[[Confident (Justin Bieber song)|Confident]]", which featured [[Chance the Rapper]] and had hip hop and reggae elements.<ref name = "Toronto Star"/> In 2013 and 2014, he co-wrote six songs on the [[Chris Brown]] album ''[[X (Chris Brown album)|X]]'', after taking part in a writing camp put on by [[RCA Records]].<ref name="vsvthebmbversimmonds"/> One of the tracks, a promo single titled "[[Don't Think They Know]]", came out in late 2013 and peaked at No. 81 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]]. Other tracks he co-wrote on the album featured artists such as [[Jhené Aiko]] and [[Trey Songz]], while "[[Love More]]" featured [[Nicki Minaj]]<ref name=allmusicx/> and reached No. 23 on the Hot 100 chart.<ref name=billboardx/> The album's track "[[New Flame]]", which featured [[Usher (singer)|Usher]] and [[Rick Ross]], went on to be nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Song|Best R&B Song]] at the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]] in 2015.<ref name="latimesgrammys"/>

In February 2014 Simmonds self-released the single "Best Sex Eva" on his own Simmonds & Company  imprint.<ref name="vsaVERSEsminnondsitunes"/> Also in February 2014, a song he co-wrote titled "[[Private Show (T.I. song)|Private]]" was included on the [[T.I.]] album ''[[Paperwork (T.I. album)|Paperwork]]''.<ref name="vscAllmusicsimmonds"/> Simmonds then released his single "Situationships" on March 24, 2015.<ref name="vsaVERSEsminnondsitunes"/> The track featured co-production from [[Needlz]] and Donut, and the publication ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' wrote that with the lyrics "Simmonds goes in on different types of relationships that go against the grain."<ref name="vswfdaf"/> The official music video for "Situationships" came out on [[VEVO]] on June 17, 2015.<ref name="vswfdaf"/>

His mixtape ''Fuck Your Feelings 3'' was released on July 15, 2015.<ref name="vsrfuckfeelings3"/> The publication ''[[Uproxx]]'' wrote that ''Fvck Your Feelings 3'' "is how it’s supposed to be done. Verse Simmonds mixes up a stiff drink and hits the club for a much needed remix to [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] and P... the remix cuts out the weakest link, keeps Drizzy and Future’s hook on blast and features a mandatory explicit sixteen from Verse."<ref name="vssuproxx"/> Through the label VVS Society, in July 2015, Simmonds released two singles: "All I Want" featuring [[Jeremih]], and "Luv In It" featuring [[Migos]].<ref name="vsaVERSEsminnondsitunes"/>

==Musical style and influences==
Simmonds has self-identified his vocal stylings as "[[R&B]], [[hip-hop]], [[reggae]] and [[calypso music|calypso]],"<ref name="vskrollout"/> and he has also described the genre of his original music as "island B," for being "a mixture of R&B, a little bit of [[Caribbean music|Caribbean]] flavor and hip hop."<ref name="mauricelinkedin"/><ref name="vsuexcluvesiveinter"/> About his lyrics, among other topics he has stated that he finds women to be "inspiration" and relationships are a commons theme throughout his mixtapes.<ref name="vskrollout"/>
