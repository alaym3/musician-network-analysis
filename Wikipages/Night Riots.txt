'''Night Riots''' (sometimes stylized as '''NIGHT RIOTS''' or '''|| NIGHT RIOTS ||'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ticketfly.com/purchase/event/1315199?_ga=1.239879360.2105792447.1475194640&__utma=1.1311559033.1475194640.1475194640.1475194640.1&__utmb=1.1.10.1475194640&__utmc=1&__utmx=-&__utmz=1.1475194640.1.1.utmcsr=bandsintown.com%7Cutmccn=(referral)%7Cutmcmd=referral%7Cutmcct=/event/12913881/buy_tickets&__utmv=-&__utmk=176323237|title=The Hunna, NIGHT RIOTS, The Shelters - Tickets - ONCE Ballroom - Somerville, MA, November 13, 2016 {{!}} Ticketfly|website=www.ticketfly.com|access-date=September 30, 2016}}</ref>) is an American [[alternative rock]] band from [[San Luis Obispo, California]]. Formed in 2010, the band consists of Travis Hawley (lead vocals), Nick Fotinakes (guitar), Matt DePauw (guitar), Mikel van Kranenburg (bass), and Rico Rodriguez (drums).

==History==
Travis Hawley, Nick Fotinakes, Matt DePauw, Kevin Menesez and Mikel van Kranenburg were raised in [[Templeton, California]],<ref name=RB>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Elliott|title=Interview With California Pop-Rock Band PK|url=http://www.redbullusa.com/cs/Satellite/en_US/Article/Interview-With-California-Pop-Rock-Band-PK-021243205571587|publisher=[[Red Bull]]|access-date=November 16, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120707140011/http://www.redbullusa.com/cs/Satellite/en_US/Article/Interview-With-California-Pop-Rock-Band-PK-021243205571587|archive-date=July 7, 2012|date=May 9, 2012}}</ref><ref name=WeHo>{{cite web|last1=Mulchin|first1=Chad Zachary|title=Straight Outta SLO: Night Riots Comes to Troubadour Sporting New Name, EP|url=http://www.wehoville.com/2013/06/06/straight-outta-slo-night-riots-come-to-troubadour-sporting-new-name-ep/|website=WeHo Ville|publisher=WHMC|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=June 6, 2013}}</ref> and they all met in middle school.<ref name=SSS>{{cite web|last1=Johnson|first1=Chandra|title=Interview: Night Riots|url=http://secretsoundshop.com/2015/04/28/interview-night-riots/|website=Secret Sound Shop|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=April 28, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151117031955/http://secretsoundshop.com/2015/04/28/interview-night-riots/|archive-date=November 17, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> They learned how to play instruments together in high school and decided to form a band.<ref name=RB/><ref name=Glide>{{cite web|last1=Bryan|first1=Kathrine|title=PK|url=http://www.glidemagazine.com/22396/pk/|website=Glide|publisher=Glide Publishing|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=January 4, 2013}}</ref> After graduating from high school, the band split for a brief period.<ref name=RB/> Rico Rodriguez replaced Kevin Menesez as the drummer in 2010. The name was inspired by the character PeeKay in Australian author [[Bryce Courtenay]]'s novel ''[[The Power of One (novel)|The Power of One]]''. Able to relate to his "[rise] from the bottom to overcome adversity", the band agreed that he was a "pretty awesome character" and were able to take away from him that they "could indeed take over the world if we just work hard and don't let anything get in our way!"<ref name=Glide/> They self-released their debut studio album ''Into the Roaring'' in 2010. The band traveled and performed at high schools and college campuses on weekdays, and music venues on weekends within the same city.<ref name=RB/> ''MTV Buzzworthy'' included PK on its "The Most Criminally Overlooked Artists of 2012" list.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Most Criminally Overlooked Artists of 2012|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2302895/overlooked-artists-music-under-the-radar-2012/|website=MTV Buzzworthy|publisher=MTV|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=December 7, 2012}}</ref>

In order to eliminate any [[trademark]] concerns with PK in the future, the band decided to change its name.<ref name=HiVo>{{cite web|last1=Green|first1=Trina N.|title=Night Riots|url=http://iamhighvoltage.com/2013/07/10/night-riots/|website=High Voltage|publisher=High Voltage|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=July 10, 2013}}</ref> The band decided on Night Riots in order to better encapsulate their sound; yearning for a "latchkey feel", they cited the energy of shows that take place at night as determining their new name.<ref name=WeHo/> They also explained that, while PK was a "great name", its meaning was difficult to interpret and failed to project any [[imagery]].<ref name=HiVo/> Night Riots sought to raise money through crowd funding website [[Indiegogo]] to release their upcoming [[extended play]] (EP), where they accumulated [[United States dollar|$]]12,000.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Eakin|first1=Marah|title=Listen to a new track from California band Night Riots|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/listen-to-a-new-track-from-california-band-night-r-100874|website=[[The A.V. Club]]|publisher=The Onion|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=July 29, 2013}}</ref> The money was used to independently release ''Young Lore'' on July 30, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Pannacione|first1=Maggie|title=Exclusive Full Album Stream: Night Riots, 'Young Lore'|url=http://www.artistdirect.com/entertainment-news/article/exclusive-fall-album-stream-night-riots-young-lore/10662753|publisher=[[Artistdirect]]|access-date=October 27, 2015|date=July 29, 2013}}</ref>

In 2014, Night Riots debuted their single "Contagious" on radio station [[KROQ-FM|KROQ]] and supported musician [[K.Flay]] on a US tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=Night Riots|url=http://kingsofar.com/2014/10/09/night-riots/|publisher=Kings of A&R|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=October 9, 2014}}</ref> On October 23, 2014 they premiered their music video for Contagious through [[MTV]] and announced their signing to [[Sumerian Records]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=Night Riots ink deal with Sumerian Records|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/night_riots_ink_deal_with_sumerian_records|website=[[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]|publisher=Alternative Press Magazine|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=October 23, 2014}}</ref> On January 20, 2015 their EP ''Howl'' was released and the band was spotlit by Live 105 in San Francisco as one of "Aaron Axelsen‚Äôs Top 16 New Acts To Watch In 2015".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Axelsen|first1=Aaron|title=Aaron Axelsen's Top 16 New Acts to Watch In 2015|url=http://live105.cbslocal.com/2015/01/15/aaron-axelsens-top-16-new-acts-to-watch-in-2015-ryn-weaver-night-riots-borns-rae-morris-lionbabe-the-amazing-snakes-flo-morrissey-years-and-years-verite-oh-wonder-urban-cone-jack-garratt-d/|website=[[KITS|Live 105]]|publisher=[[CBS Radio]]|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=January 15, 2015}}</ref> In February 2015, Night Riots embarked on their first full US headlining tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=Music &#124; An Evening with Night Riots &#124; Interview & Review|url=http://www.ultra5280.com/music/2015/3/12/music-an-evening-with-night-riots-interview-review|publisher=Ultra5280|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=March 10, 2015}}</ref> In March and April 2015, Night Riots were main support on [[The Mowgli's]] US and Canada tour.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Hoyle|first1=Hayley|title=Night Riots Added to The Mowgli's "Kids In Love Tour"|url=http://www.digitaltourbus.com/news/night-riots-added-to-the-mowglis-kids-in-love-tour/|publisher=Digital Bus Tour|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=March 25, 2015}}</ref> In May 2015, Night Riots performed for the [[Vans]] [[Warped Tour]] 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Night Riots &#124; Vans Warped Tour|url=http://vanswarpedtour.com/bands/night-riots|publisher=[[Warped Tour]]|access-date=November 16, 2015}}</ref> On September 16, 2015, Night Riots performed at the debut of the new, tenth-generation [[Honda Civic]] Sedan.{{citation needed|date=December 2015}}

In the fall of 2015 following the Vans Warped Tour, Night Riots also went to the Let The Kids Riot tour as main support to Young Rising Sons, ending the tour and gaining vast new audiences.

In the Spring of 2016, Night Riots went on tour with Blaqk Audio, opening for The Material Tour, and revisited some of the same venues in the Midwest and East Coast from the LTKR tour. For the 2016 festival season Night Riots will attend BFD, Firefly Music Festival, Osheaga Festival, and Riot Fest in both Colorado and Chicago. In October 2016, Night Riots released their new album, ''Love Gloom'', their first full-length album under a new label.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Roaring Sound of Californian Alt-Rockers Night Riots|url=http://www.provenancemag.com/art-culture/the-roaring-sound-of-californian-alt-rockers-night-riots/|publisher=Provenance Magazine|access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> Night Riots was featured on Alt Nation's 2016 Advanced Placement Tour with The Hunna and The Shelters, running November through December.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.siriusxm.com/alt-nation-presents-the-advanced-placement-tour-with-the-shelters-night-riots-the-hunna/|title=Alt Nation presents the Advanced Placement Tour with The Shelters, Night Riots + The Hunna|date=September 15, 2016|publisher=|access-date=January 5, 2019}}</ref>

In the spring of 2017, [[Andrew McMahon in the Wilderness]] toured the U.S. featuring Night Riots and [[Atlas Genius]]. In Summer 2017, Night Riots played two dates on [[All Time Low]]'s Young Renegade Tour, in replacement of [[SWMRS]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/all_time_low_announce_headlining_tour/|title=All Time Low announce headlining tour|date=February 24, 2017|publisher=|access-date=January 5, 2019}}</ref> Night Riots opened for [[The Maine (band)|The Maine]] on The Lovely Little Lonely Tour and The Modern Nostalgia tour, which ran until November 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nightriots.com/tour/|title=TOUR|website=-- NIGHT RIOTS --|access-date=January 5, 2019}}</ref> The Lovely Little Lonely tour marked the band's first European tour. Also in November 2017, Night Riots played the first night of Chicago radio station 101 [[WKQX (FM)|WKQX]]'s The Nights We Stole Christmas Music Festival, playing before [[Papa Roach]] and [[Rise Against]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.101wkqx.com/2017/12/02/night-riots-tnwsc-2017/|title=Night Riots ‚Äì #TNWSC 2017|date=December 2, 2017|website=101WKQX - WKQX-FM|access-date=January 5, 2019}}</ref>

Night Riots opened for [[The Hunna]] in early January 2018 on their U.K tour.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://nightriots.com/tourdates/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=January 3, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180103072714/http://nightriots.com/tourdates/ |archive-date=January 3, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bc-Tl-WHleq/|title=Night Riots on Instagram: "üá¨üáßUK 2018üá¨üáß shows with the FABULOUS @thehunnaband & @coastsband ! ==Musical style and influences==
Night Riots' foundations lie within [[punk rock]]; they cite bands [[AFI (band)|AFI]] and [[Thursday (band)|Thursday]] as their biggest influences.<ref name=RB/> They also draw influence from [[Billy Idol]], [[Paul Simon]], [[Peter Gabriel]], [[Arctic Monkeys]], [[Atmosphere (music group)|Atmosphere]], [[The Cure]], [[U2]], [[The Killers]], [[Vampire Weekend]], [[The Strokes]], [[Bruce Springsteen]], [[INXS]] and [[Dave Gahan]].<ref name=WeHo/><ref name=HiVo/><ref name=Aquarian>{{cite web|last1=McGrath|first1=Ryan|title=Interview with Night Riots: Howl at the Moon|url=http://www.theaquarian.com/2015/03/04/interview-with-night-riots-howl-at-the-moon/|website=[[The Aquarian Weekly]]|publisher=Arts Weekly|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=March 4, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Magalis|first1=Brooke|title=Interview with Night Riots|url=http://blackonthecanvas.com/2015/06/03/interview-with-night-riots/|website=Black on the Canvas|access-date=December 6, 2015|date=June 3, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Lisker|first1=Marlee|title=Night Riots talks new EP, musical influences|url=http://www.themiamihurricane.com/2013/08/25/night-riots-talks-new-ep-musical-influences/|website=[[The Miami Hurricane]]|publisher=[[University of Miami]]|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=August 25, 2013}}</ref> PK integrated [[pop rock]] and [[power pop]] into their sound, accompanied with "introspective and emotional" lyrics with music that "is always lively and upbeat".<ref name=RB/><ref name=Glide/> The band changed its name to Night Riots in order to represent "its darker side as it explores the deeper aspects of music with varying tempos and somber, haunting melodies juxtaposed against uplifting choruses."<ref name=WeHo/> An [[alternative rock]] band,<ref name=Aquarian/> they incorporate [[new wave music]], as well as a 1980s [[Gothic rock|Gothic pop]] feel into their current sound.<ref name=SSS/> Hawley distinguished Night Riots' music from PK by describing it as [[indie rock|indie pop rock]] with a "dark undertone", comparing the sound to U2, The Strokes, and The Killers.<ref name=WeHo/> He also describes the band's sound as "gloom pop" because of the vein of darkness that runs throughout the alternative/pop sounding music.<ref>{{Citation|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5B1fbsC_NvU|access-date = January 6, 2016|title=An Exclusive Interview with Night Riots &#124;&#124; Baeble Music}}</ref> Guitarist Matt DePauw described the band's sound as "catchy rock 'n' roll with a bit of pop and a bit of darkness".<ref name=Glide/>

Night Riots' music has been compared to The Cure and The Killers;<ref name=WeHo/><ref name=Glide/> with Hawley's "emotive vocal style" compared to that of [[Robert Smith (musician)|Robert Smith]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Night Riots|url=http://audiotree.tv/session/night-riots/|publisher=Audiotree|access-date=November 16, 2015|location=[[Los Angeles]], [[California]]|date=March 5, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Greenwald|first1=David|title=The Buzz On: PK|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2301537/pk-band/|publisher=MTV|access-date=November 16, 2015|date=July 9, 2012}}</ref> His stage presence has also been compared to [[Davey Havok]] and [[Brandon Flowers]].<ref name=Aquarian/>
