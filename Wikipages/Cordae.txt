'''Cordae Amari Dunston''' (born August 26, 1997; formerly known as '''YBN Cordae''' and '''Entendre'''), known mononymously as '''Cordae''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. Cordae gained popularity by releasing remixes of popular songs, such as "[[My Name Is]]" by [[Eminem]], and "Old Niggas" which was a response to the song "1985" by [[J. Cole]]. Videos to both remixes were uploaded to [[WorldStarHipHop]]'s YouTube channel, which led to an immense and immediate positive response from both mainstream media and the online rap community.<ref name="ComplexEverything">{{cite web|last1=McKinney|first1=Jessica|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/ybn-cordae-everything-you-need-to-know/|title=Everything You Need to Know About Cordae|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=July 24, 2019|access-date=July 25, 2019}}</ref> His debut studio album, ''[[The Lost Boy (album)|The Lost Boy]]'' was released on July 26, 2019<ref name="LostBoy">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-lost-boy/1471022583|title=The Lost Boy by YBN Cordae|via=Apple Music|date=July 26, 2019|access-date=July 26, 2019}}</ref> to widespread acclaim and received two nominations for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song|Best Rap Song]] for the single "Bad Idea" at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|62nd Grammy Awards]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/ybn-cordae|title=YBN Cordae Grammy Awards History|date=November 19, 2019|website=[[The Recording Academy]]|language=en|access-date=November 25, 2019}}</ref> Cordae was a member of the [[YBN (collective)|YBN]] collective from 2018 until he left the group in 2020, dropping the "YBN" part from his stage name.

==Early life==
Cordae was born in [[Raleigh, North Carolina]], but moved to [[Suitland, Maryland]].<ref name="ComplexEverything"/><ref name="BillTables">{{cite news | last=Lamarre| first=Carl| title =How YBN Cordae Went From Waiting Tables To Hitting the Charts| work=Billboard | date =August 9, 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8526639/ybn-cordae-up-next-debut-album-interview| access-date =December 2, 2019}}</ref> As a young child, he took an interest in music after his father would always play [[classic hip hop]] around him, from artists like [[Rakim]], [[Nas]], [[Big L]], and [[Talib Kweli]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/interviews/2018/05/ybn-cordae-interview|title=Bridging Rap's Generational Divide: An Interview With YBN Cordae|via=Pigeons & Planes|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> He began writing raps around the age of fifteen, but treated it as a hobby. As he got older, he would do research on artists through YouTube, attempting to find similar music to the music his father played.<ref name="Open Space: YBN Cordae">{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/AsNTQkKabpo?t=68|title=Open Space: YBN Cordae|via=Mass Appeal|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> As Dunston grew older, he began taking a bigger interest in pursuing a rap career. He prioritized education, but often became distracted by writing lyrics. He released three mixtapes as a teenager: "Entendre" ‚Äì Anxiety (2014), I‚Äôm So Anxious (2016), and I‚Äôm So Anonymous (2017).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ok-tho.com/2014/06/entendre-anxiety-mixtape.html|title=Entendre ‚Äì Anxiety [Mixtape]|via="OK-THO" Blog|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://musiconthedot.com/entendre-im-anxious-mixtape/|title=Entendre ‚Äì I'm So Anxious (Mixtape)|via=Music On The Dot|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/im-so-anonymous/1271420541|title=I'm so Anonymous|via=iTunes|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> He graduated from high school in 2015 and decided to go to college at [[Towson University]], later dropping out in 2018, explaining that college was "bigger than him" as a first-generation student, and he was mainly doing it for his mother. He moved to Los Angeles shortly afterward.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-cordae-is-fully-prepared-for-his-bright-future/|title=YBN CORDAE IS FULLY PREPARED FOR HIS BRIGHT FUTURE|via=Elevator Mag|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
===2018‚Äì2019: Early beginnings, record deal, and ''The Lost Boy''===
Cordae began taking his rap career seriously in the beginning of 2018. Although he hadn't been releasing any music with them, he'd often hang around [[YBN Nahmir]] and YBN Almighty Jay when visiting Los Angeles, whom he'd initially met through social media. Nahmir had taken interest in a song Dunston made with fellow rapper and brother Simba, and reached out to him.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-cordae-met-nahmir-and-almighty-jay-through-xbox-live/|title=YBN CORDAE MET NAHMIR AND ALMIGHTY JAY THROUGH XBOX LIVE|via=Elevator Mag|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kanyetothe.com/forum/index.php?topic=7780865.18|title=YBN cordae is easily the best out of the group|via=kanyetothe.com|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> Dunston is the only member of YBN that hadn't been introduced through video games, as he doesn't take gaming too seriously. He notes that he already felt like a part of YBN, with the only thing not solidifying his presence being his social media handles, which he soon changed from "Entendre" to "YBN Cordae". He also stated that Entendre had been a name that he'd wanted to change for while, stating "I was just always around Nahmir and shit, those were just like my brothers... And, I was thinking, yo, I need to change my shit to like, Cordae, and shit, my actual first name. I was already "YBN" without the social tag, you know what I'm saying?"<ref name="Open Space: YBN Cordae"/> He released his first single, which he called his "introduction to the world", in May 2018. It was a remix of the 1999 song "[[My Name Is]]" by [[Eminem]], which he released along with a music video via [[WorldStarHipHop]] on YouTube. Subsequently, he released "Old Niggas", as a response to "1985" by J. Cole, followed by the tracks "Fighting Temptations" and "Kung Fu", respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.z-pov.com/music/2018/5/19/ybn-cordae-the-outspoken-artist-declaring-the-new-generation-of-rap-has-promise|title=YBN Cordae, The Outspoken Artist Declaring The New Generation of Rap Has Promise|via=z-pov.com|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/05/17/the-10-best-new-rap-songs-right-now|title=The 10 best new rap songs right now|via=The Fader|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/07/11/ybn-cordae-releases-track-video-kung-fu-070080e9ff|title=YBN Cordae releases new track and video "Kung Fu"|via=Revolt TV|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

His debut live performance was at the annual Rolling Loud music festival in May 2018, where he performed with both YBN Nahmir and YBN Almighty Jay. He performed with the duo again for the 2018 XXL Freshman Show in New York City, after Nahmir was announced to be an inductee of the yearly freshman class.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/07/2018-xxl-freshman-show-new-york/|title=HERE'S WHAT WENT DOWN AT THE 2018 XXL FRESHMAN SHOW IN NEW YORK|via=XXL Magazine|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> Dunston was then announced to be joining [[Juice Wrld]] on his North American WRLD Domination tour from May to September 2018, along with [[Lil Mosey]] and Blake, ended up reaching 28 cities.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/juice-wrld-announces-wrld-domination-tour-dates-news.54241.html|title=Juice WRLD Announces "WRLD Domination" Tour Dates|via=HotNewHipHop|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> Soon after, it was announced that YBN would be going on a month-long tour in Europe as a collective.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/07/ybn-nahmir-ybn-almighty-jay-ybn-cordae-european-tour/|title=YBN NAHMIR SHARES DATES FOR EUROPEAN TOUR WITH YBN ALMIGHTY JAY AND YBN CORDAE|via=XXL Magazine|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> Dunston then said he would release multiple singles up until his debut project is complete.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ybn-cordae-is-bridging-the-generational-rap-gap-news.55739.html|title=YBN Cordae Is Bridging The Generational Rap Gap|via=HotNewHipHop|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> On August 2, 2018, both Nahmir and Cordae released a video for their single "Pain Away".<ref name="Pain Away">{{cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2018/08/ybns-nahmir-cordae-connect-on-pain-away/|title=YBN's Nahmir & Cordae Connect on "Pain Away"|via=Respect Magazine|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> On August 12, YBN Nahmir announced that an official YBN Mixtape, featuring the trio, would be released September 7, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/ybn-nahmir-ybn-mixtape-release-date/|title=YBN NAHMIR SETS RELEASE DATE FOR YBN MIXTAPE|via=XXL Magazine|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> He released the music video for his single, "Scotty Pippen", on August 23, 2018.<ref name="Scotty Pippen">{{cite web|url=http://digitaldripped.com/s/YBN-Cordae-Scotty-Pippen.70666|title= Alaska YBN Cordae|via=Digital Dripped|access-date=August 23, 2018}}</ref>

On January 28, 2019, Cordae released a music video for a new song titled, "Locationships" on his YouTube channel before releasing another song titled, "Have Mercy" in March 2019, with the latter song becoming the lead single from his debut studio album, ''[[The Lost Boy (album)|The Lost Boy]]''.<ref name="HaveMercy"/> He was later named as one of the members of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL's]]'' "2019 Freshman Class" on June 20, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/06/2019-xxl-freshman-cover/|title=XXL Freshman 2019 Cover Revealed|publisher=XXL|date=June 20, 2019|access-date=July 26, 2019}}</ref> Cordae later released "Bad Idea" and "[[RNP (song)|RNP]]" as the second and third singles from the album respectively.<ref name="BadIdea"/><ref name="RNP"/> ''The Lost Boy'' was released on July 26, 2019.<ref name="LostBoy"/> The album landed YBN Cordae two nominations at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|62nd Grammy Awards]]; [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song|Best Rap Song]] for the single "Bad Idea".<ref name=":0" /> In November 2019, YBN Cordae announced that he would be headlining "The Lost Boy in America Tour", which began in January 2020.<ref name="COS">{{cite news | last=Young| first=Alex| title =YBN Cordae announces 2020 headlining tour| work=Consequence of Sound | date =November 14, 2019 | url =https://consequenceofsound.net/2019/11/ybn-cordae-tour/| access-date =December 2, 2019}}</ref>

===2020‚Äìpresent: Disbanding of YBN, and ''From A Birds Eye View''===
On August 6, 2020, YBN Nahmir took to Twitter to announce that the YBN collective officially disbanded after a period of tension where he and Cordae had stopped communicating.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.60792/title.ybn-nahmir-says-cordae-talked-to-him-before-dropping-ybn-from-rap-name|title=YBN Nahmir Says Cordae Talked to Him Before Dropping YBN From Rap Name|first=Devon|last=Jefferson|work=HipHopDX|date=February 25, 2021|access-date=November 15, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=YBN Nahmir Announces YBN Crew's Disbandment|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/8/ybn-nahmir-ybn-crew-split-almighty-jay-cordae|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref> Cordae subsequently dropped the YBN from his stage name and asked YBN Nahmir for his blessing of the name change.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/cordae-asked-ybn-nahmir-for-his-blessing-before-changing-name-news.141705.html|title=Cordae Asked YBN Nahmir For His Blessing Before Changing Name|work=HotNewHipHop|first= Erika|last=Marie|date=October 27, 2021|access-date=November 15, 2021}}</ref> On August 27, Cordae released a new single, "[[Gifted (song)|Gifted]]", featuring [[Roddy Ricch]], marking Cordae's first release of 2020.<ref name="Gifted">{{cite tweet|url=https://twitter.com/cordae/status/1298768574413062146?s=19|title=New song drops tomorrow 12 EST #Gifted|number=1298768574413062146|user=Cordae|author=Cordae (confirmed account)|date=August 26, 2020|access-date=August 27, 2020}}</ref> Cordae is currently working on his second album. During an interview with [[Apple Music]], he stated, "I'm like a hundred songs deep in. Like this next one, I'm really taking everything to a whole 'nother level. I really love doing music and perfecting my craft and I'm getting better every day. And the new music from here on out is going to show that".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2020/08/27/cordae-roddy-ricch-gifted/amp/|title=Cordae and Roddy Ricch Connect on 'Gifted'|work=[[Rap-Up]]|date=August 27, 2020|access-date=September 8, 2020}}</ref>

On April 22, 2021, Cordae released the EP ''Just Until'' featuring [[Q-Tip (musician)|Q-Tip]], and [[Young Thug]], in anticipation of his forthcoming second studio album ''From A Birds Eye View''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.61725/|title=CORDAE RELEASES 'JUST UNTIL....‚Äô EP FEATURING Q-TIP & YOUNG THUG|website=[[HipHopDX]]|last=Saponara|first=Michael|date=April 22, 2021|accessdate=May 31, 2021}}</ref>

On October 7, 2021, Cordae released the highly anticipated single "Super".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-10-07|title=Cordae Drops New Single and Music Video "Super"|url=https://hypebeast.com/2021/10/cordae-super-song-music-video|access-date=2021-10-10|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
Cordae lists [[Nas]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Kid Cudi]], [[Kanye West]], [[Eminem]], [[Capital Steez]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[J. Cole]], [[Big L]], [[Travis Scott]], and [[Kendrick Lamar]] as some of his biggest influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/z6dbnZo47l8?t=1338|title=The YBN Cordae Interview|via=YouTube|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> Shortly after the release of ''The Lost Boy'', he revealed his top five favorite rappers (in no particular order) as [[Jay-Z]], [[Nas]], [[2Pac]], [[The Notorious B.I.G.]], and [[Big L]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/ybn-cordae-lists-top-5-rappers|title=YBN Cordae Lists His Top 5 Rappers|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=August 29, 2019}}</ref> He has said many times that, because of his musical background and understanding of both old school hip-hop and what hip-hop is evolving into, he can be the key in bridging the generational gap in hip-hop.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-cordae-is-bridging-the-generational-gap-in-hip-hop/|title= CORDAE IS BRIDGING HIP-HOP'S GENERATIONAL GAP|via=Elevator Mag|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/05/ybn-cordae-j-cole-1985-response-old-niggas/|title=YBN Cordae Attempts To Bridge Generational Gap In J. Cole Response Track, "Old Ni**as"|via=Vibe|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/ybn-cordae-breaks-down-old-n-ggas-on-genius-series-verified|title=YBN Cordae Breaks Down "Old N*ggas" On Genius' Series 'Verified'|via=Genius|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

''Respect Magazine'' opined that Dunston was ahead of his time, as far as rapping goes, stating: "He is a mix of the old and new school, and the deliverance and perception of his music sets himself apart from the new generation of rappers these days. We get a clear glimpse of this in his music videos for ‚ÄúOld N*ggas,‚Äù 'My Name Is,' and 'Kung Fu,' which all have over 5 million views on YouTube. He is building a solid buzz for himself day by day, and letting the world know his bars and rhymes are ahead of his time. He has yet to drop a project proving such."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2018/08/ahead-of-the-game-ybn-cordae/|title=Ahead Of The Game: Cordae|via=Respect Magazine|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

Revolt TV said "There's always going to be artists who go against the grain and manage to generate conversation without being mundane or routine.  Cordae is the result of perfect timing, a need for change and managing to bridge the gap between aesthetic and talent. He's the Gohan of the YBN collective, appearing docile and juvenile but displaying a ‚Äúmastery" over words that seems at odds with old-school "hip-hoppers" belief about his age group. It is yet to be seen if his talent warrants the praise he's receiving."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/05/31/industry-plant-conversation-longer-place-rap-music-070040ea8f|title=The 'industry plant' conversation no longer has a place in rap music|via=Revolt TV|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Before leaving college, he worked at a local [[T.G.I. Friday's]] in Maryland, explaining to [[Adam22]] of No Jumper, that he "hated" it and that he always knew he was destined for more.<ref>{{cite web|title=Who is YBN Cordae? The rapper bridging 'old heads' with the new generation|url=https://kulturehub.com/who-is-ybn-cordae/|access-date=July 30, 2018|via=Kulture Hub}}</ref>

Cordae participated in a [[Black Lives Matter]] protest in 2016, rapping among the crowd about the struggles he faced, and the issues he'd been noticing in his community.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/hiphopheads/comments/8u7jli/ybn_cordae_at_dmv_protests_2_years_ago/|title=YBN Cordae at DMV Protests 2 years ago|via=Reddit|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> In July 2020, Dunston was arrested at a [[Shooting of Breonna Taylor|Breonna Taylor]] protest in Louisville, Kentucky, along with American professional football player [[Kenny Stills]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=YBN Cordae and Kenny Stills Among Those Arrested at Breonna Taylor Protest in Louisville|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/ybn-cordae-kenny-stills-arrested-breonna-taylor-protest|access-date=July 15, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref>

He is currently dating tennis star [[Naomi Osaka]], whom he met at an [[LA Clippers]] game.<ref>{{cite web| title=YBN Cordae Discusses Girlfriend Naomi Osaka's U.S. Open Success |url=https://www.complex.com/sports/2019/09/ybn-cordae-discusses-girlfriend-naomi-osakas-us-open-success| website= Complex|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Top 5 Photos 8/26: Osaka wishes Cordae a happy birthday |url=http://baseline.tennis.com/article/90278/top-5-photos-wta-atp |website=Baseline Tennis |access-date=September 2, 2020 | date = August 26, 2020}}</ref>
