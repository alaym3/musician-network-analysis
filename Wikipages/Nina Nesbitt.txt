'''Nina Lindberg Nesbitt'''<ref>Scotland Statutory Register of Births<br>
<i>Surname: NESBITT<br>
Forename: NINA LINDA<br>
Gender: F<br>
Year: 1994<br>
Ref: 772/ 570<br>
RD Name: Livingston</i></ref> (born 11 July 1994)<ref name="Radical Look"/> is a Scottish <!--Do not change this without discussion--> singer and songwriter. She has two top 40 singles, and is known for her single "Stay Out", which peaked at 21 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in April 2013.<ref name="Stay Out">{{cite news|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/26188/nina%20nesbitt/|title=Nina Nesbitt|work=Official Charts|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref>

Her first [[Extended play|EP]], ''The Apple Tree'', was released in April 2012 and peaked at No. 6 on the [[iTunes]] download charts after receiving airplay on [[BBC Radio 1]],<ref name="recordoftheday">{{cite web | url=http://www.recordoftheday.com/record-of-the-day/track.php?trackID=6239 | title=Track – 04 May 2012 | publisher=Record of the Day | date=4 May 2012 | access-date=12 July 2012 | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120530035747/http://www.recordoftheday.com/record-of-the-day/track.php?trackID=6239 | archive-date=30 May 2012 | df=dmy-all }}</ref> and also peaked at the top of the iTunes singer/songwriter chart.<ref name="scotsman" /> The ''Way in the World'' EP and single were released on 23 July 2013, as a follow-up to "Stay Out". In August 2013, she recorded a version of [[Fleetwood Mac]]'s "[[Don't Stop (Fleetwood Mac song)|Don't Stop]]", for a new [[John Lewis (department store)|John Lewis]] advertising campaign, subsequently charting at number 61. Nesbitt released her debut album, ''[[Peroxide (Nina Nesbitt album)|Peroxide]]'', on 17 February 2014.

As a songwriter, she has written for artists including [[Jessie Ware]], [[Olivia Holt]], [[Don Diablo]] and [[The Shires (duo)|The Shires]]. In February 2019, Nesbitt released her second studio album, ''[[The Sun Will Come Up, the Seasons Will Change]]''.

==Early life==
Nesbitt was born in [[Livingston, West Lothian|Livingston]],<ref>{{cite news |title=Nina Nesbitt: The Scottish singer-songwriter on taking on America, and winning |url=http://www.ludlowadvertiser.co.uk/news/16289771.Nina_Nesbitt__The_Scottish_singer-songwriter_on_taking_on_America__and_winning/ |access-date=6 August 2018 |publisher=Ludlow Advertiser}}</ref> and attended the small village school in Bellsquarry, [[Livingston, West Lothian|Livingston]]. At 12, she moved to [[Balerno]], a suburb of Edinburgh, and attended [[Balerno Community High School]]. Neither of her parents are musical - her father, Mike, worked in the electronics industry and her mother Caty, who is originally from Sweden, was a childcare worker.

Nesbitt plays the guitar, piano and flute. She began playing the guitar at the age of fifteen. After hearing [[Taylor Swift]]’s ''[[Fearless (Taylor Swift album)|Fearless]]'' album, she uploaded her first cover to [[YouTube]]. The site's positive feedback inspired her to continue writing and recording songs in her bedroom, and uploading the videos to her YouTube channel.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nina Nesbitt will convince you break-ups are actually dope |url=https://galoremag.com/nina-nesbitt-music-breakups-scotland/ |website=Galore Mag|date=2017-11-21 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Nina Nesbitt made £100k before debut album |url=https://www.edinburghnews.scotsman.com/news/entertainment/nina-nesbitt-made-100k-before-debut-album-1-3329490 |access-date=6 August 2018 |publisher=Edinburgh Evening News}}</ref> As a child, she competed in regional and national gymnastic competitions for both Pentland Rhythmic Gymnastics Club and Scotland. She was also part of the Scottish Team training for the Commonwealth.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2017/09/21/artist-of-the-day-2109-nina-nesbitt-is-one-of-the-most-exciting-pop-artists-to-release-music-this-year-6942223/|title = Nina Nesbitt is the most exciting pop artist to release music this year|date = 21 September 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
===2011–2012: Early career===
Nesbitt met musician [[Ed Sheeran]] before a radio gig he was scheduled to perform in [[Edinburgh]]. After asking him for advice for aspiring singer-songwriters, and playing him a song on his guitar, she was invited to support him on his European tour. She was also invited to support [[Example (musician)|Example]] after he heard her cover of "[[Stay Awake (Example song)|Stay Awake]]".<ref name="guardian"/><ref name="scotsman">{{cite news | url=http://www.scotsman.com/edinburgh-evening-news/edinburgh/17-year-old-edinburgh-singer-nina-nesbitt-rides-high-in-charts-1-2257967 | title=17-year-old Edinburgh singer Nina Nesbitt rides high in charts | work=[[Edinburgh Evening News]] | date=26 April 2012 | access-date=12 July 2012}}</ref> She also appeared in the music video for Sheeran's single "[[Drunk (Ed Sheeran song)|Drunk]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/entertainment/music/nina-nesbitt-could-duet-boyfriend-1215018|title=Using our Eds: Scots singer Nina Nesbitt hints at duet with boyfriend Sheeran|work=Daily Record|access-date=3 December 2015}}</ref>

===2012–2013: ''The Apple Tree'', ''Boy'', ''Stay Out'' and ''Way in the World''===
[[File:NINA NESBITT FIB 2014.jpg|thumb|200px|right|Nesbitt performing at the [[Festival Internacional de Benicàssim|FIB Festival]] in 2014]]
Her second EP, ''The Apple Tree'', was released in April 2012 via [[AWAL]] and reached No. 6 on the [[iTunes]] download charts after receiving airplay on [[BBC Radio 1]],<ref name="recordoftheday"/> and also reached No. 1 on the iTunes singer/songwriter chart.<ref name="scotsman" /> On 9 October 2012, Nesbitt embarked on her second UK headline tour, to support the release of her debut single, "Boy", under new label, [[Island Records]]. The tour included support from singer-songwriter [[Josh Kumra]] and singer-songwriter Billy Lockett. The tour commenced on 9 October 2012 at Oran Mor, Glasgow, Scotland and finished on 18 October 2012 at [[Dingwalls]], London, England.<ref>{{cite news | title = Nina Nesbitt BOY UK Tour | date = 2 August 2012 | url = http://www.clixie.co.uk/nina-nesbitt-announces-new-single-and-uk-tour/ | archive-url = https://archive.today/20130419231719/http://www.clixie.co.uk/nina-nesbitt-announces-new-single-and-uk-tour/ | url-status = dead | archive-date = 19 April 2013 | work = Clixie | access-date = 21 September 2012 }}</ref> Her third EP, ''Stay Out'', was released on 8 April 2013; its title track of the same name entered the charts at No. 21 in April 2013. Her next EP, ''Way in the World'', was released on 21 July 2013. The title track was released as a single, and the music video released on 12 June 2013. Nesbitt's next single was a cover of the [[Fleetwood Mac]] song, "[[Don't Stop (Fleetwood Mac song)|Don't Stop]]", and is used in an advert for the department store [[John Lewis (department store)|John Lewis]]. On 6 September 2013, Nesbitt sang "[[Flower of Scotland]]" at [[Hampden Park]] before [[Scotland National Football Team|Scotland]] faced [[Belgium National Football Team|Belgium]] in a [[2014 FIFA World Cup qualification|Group A World Cup Qualifier]].

===2013–2014: ''Peroxide''===
{{Main|Peroxide (Nina Nesbitt album)}}
On 25 November 2013, Nesbitt announced on her Twitter and Facebook pages that her debut album, ''[[Peroxide (Nina Nesbitt album)|Peroxide]]'', would be released on 17 February 2014 in the United Kingdom. Nesbitt did an album signing tour around the UK, starting in Edinburgh on the day the album was released. ''Peroxide'' entered the [[UK Albums Chart|UK Album Charts]] at number 1 in Scotland and 11 in the rest of the UK. The lead single from the album, "[[Selfies (song)|Selfies]]" was released on 9 February 2014. She released her fifth EP, ''Nina Nesbitt'', exclusively in the US on 1 April 2014.

===2016–2017: ''Modern Love'' EP and ''Life in Colour''===
In 2016, Nesbitt announced the release of her newest [[Extended play|EP]], ''Modern Love'', featuring her "radical new look."<ref name="Radical Look"/> She also announced a three-date UK Tour, that took place from 26 to 28 January.<ref name="Chewing Gum">{{cite news|url=https://m.axs.com/news/nina-nesbitt-announces-new-ep-modern-love-streams-single-chewing-gum-73846|title=Nina Nesbitt announces new EP Modern Love, streams single 'Chewing Gum'|last=Law|first=Tarynn|date=11 January 2016|access-date=2 February 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180622192923/https://m.axs.com/news/nina-nesbitt-announces-new-ep-modern-love-streams-single-chewing-gum-73846|archive-date=22 June 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> Following its World Exclusive on [[BBC Radio 1|Radio 1]], her single "Chewing Gum" became available for download on [[Apple Music]] and [[Spotify]] on 10 January.<ref name="Chewing Gum"/> On 27 April, Nesbitt performed at the [[Young Scot Awards]] at Edinburgh's International Conference Centre.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/nina-nesbitt-cant-wait-perform-7315906#xor78Sp0mGcafW19.97|title=Nina Nesbitt can't wait to perform at our Young Scot Awards in Edinburgh|work=Daily Record|date=7 February 2016|access-date=10 February 2016}}</ref> In June, she started the project ''Songs I Wrote For You'', where she asked her fans to send her their personal love stories and she would write a song about it. In October 2016, she released a five-track EP titled ''Life in Colour'' with the songs of the project under her own label.

===2016–present: ''The Sun Will Come Up, the Seasons Will Change''===
In May 2016, Nesbitt disclosed that she had left her record label and was now an independent artist.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://twitter.com/ninanesbitt/status/729733202558988288|title=Nina Nesbitt – Twitter|date=9 May 2016|access-date=9 May 2016}}{{Primary source inline|date=December 2017}}</ref> In November 2016, Nesbitt signed a record deal with indie label [[Cooking Vinyl]].<ref name="Nina Nesbitt – Twitter" /> On 2 July 2017, she served as a supporting act for [[Justin Bieber]] during [[British Summer Time (concerts)|British Summer Time]] in [[Hyde Park, London|Hyde Park]], [[London]]. She released a new single, "The Moments I'm Missing", on 14 July 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ninanesbittmusic.com/|title=The Moments I'm Missing|website=Ninanesbittmusic.com}}</ref> On 8 September 2017 she released her next single, "The Best You Had''"'', which was made [[Greg James]]' 'Tune Of The Week' on Radio 1<ref>{{cite web |title=Want some free merch? |url=https://www.facebook.com/ninanesbittmusic/videos/1500517116696643/ |website=Facebook |access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> and added to the Radio 1 playlist. [[Taylor Swift]] later added "The Best You Had" to her 'Favourite Songs' playlist on [[Apple Music]] and [[Spotify]], while actress [[Chloë Grace Moretz]] tweeted about the song to her followers.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nina Nesbitt shares brand new track 'Somebody Special' |url=http://cookingvinyl.com/nina-nesbitt-shares-brand-new-track-somebody-special/ |website=Cooking Vinyl |access-date=6 August 2018 |date=2018-01-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180806210935/http://cookingvinyl.com/nina-nesbitt-shares-brand-new-track-somebody-special/ |archive-date=6 August 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Nesbitt then released "[[Somebody Special]]" as the third single from her second album.

In October 2017, she played two intimate shows in [[New York City]] and [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Scottish Songwriter Nina Nesbitt Announces US Tour Dates |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Scottish-Songwriter-Nina-Nesbitt-Announces-US-Tour-Dates-20171003 |website=Broadway World}}</ref> In December 2017, Nesbitt won the 'Evolution Award' at the SSE Scottish Music Awards.<ref>{{cite web |title=Emeli Sande and Lewis Capaldi big winners at SSE Scottish Music Awards |url=https://www.glasgowlive.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/emeli-sande-lewis-capaldi-big-13987571 |website=Glasgow Live|date=2017-12-03 }}</ref>

Between March and April 2018, Nesbitt toured the United States with [[Jake Bugg]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Nina Nesbitt To Tour With Jake Bugg In The States |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Nina-Nesbitt-To-Tour-With-Jake-Bugg-In-The-States-20180110 |website=Broadway World}}</ref> She also revealed new track "Psychopath" for the launch of [[Spotify]]'s ‘Louder Together’, a program bringing artists together to collaborate on an original Spotify Singles song in the spirit of community, empowerment and inspiration.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sasha Sloan, Nina Nesbitt and Charlotte Lawrence launch first multi-artist Spotify Single |url=http://www.musicweek.com/digital/read/sasha-sloan-nina-nesbitt-and-charlotte-lawrence-launch-first-multi-artist-spotify-single/071871 |website=Music Week}}</ref>

The titular track "The Sun Will Come Up, the Seasons Will Change" was released as a promotional single in May. Nesbitt has said that it is "like a self-help song and she hopes that people can listen to it in times of need and be reminded that things are constantly changing". The song was also featured in an episode of ''[[Life Sentence (TV series)|Life Sentence]]'', an American television series.<ref>{{cite news |title=Songs for Screens: Hear Nina Nesbitt's New Single, Appearing on CW's 'Life Sentence' |url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/songs-for-screens-hear-nina-nesbitts-new-single-appearing-on-cws-life-sentence-exclusive-1202802656/ |access-date=6 August 2018 |publisher=Variety}}</ref>

In summer 2018, Nesbitt played twelve festivals in the UK, and also performed at SXSW, MIDEM and The Great Escape.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nina Nesbitt – A Fiery Performance At The Great Escape |url=https://essentiallypop.com/epop/2018/05/nina-nesbitt-a-fiery-performance-at-the-great-escape/ |website=Essentially Pop |access-date=6 August 2018|date=2018-05-24 }}</ref> She later sold out three consecutive London shows (at [[Heaven (nightclub)|Heaven]], [[The Islington]] and [[The Barfly|Camden Assembly]]), and supported acts such as [[James Arthur]], [[Jesse McCartney]], MAX and [[Lewis Capaldi]] on tours.

On 10 August 2018, Nesbitt released her fourth single from the album, "[[Loyal to Me]]", which was produced by [[Fraser T. Smith|Fraser T Smith]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Pre-Save Loyal To Me On Spotify |url=http://www.ninanesbittmusic.com/ |website=Ninanesbittmusic.com |access-date=6 August 2018}}</ref> In October 2018, Nesbitt announced that her second album, ''[[The Sun Will Come Up, the Seasons Will Change]]'' will be released on 1 February 2019 through [[Cooking Vinyl]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/ninanesbitt/status/1057211488384049152|title=THE SUN WILL COME UP THE SEASONS WILL CHANGE the new album. february 1st.|first=Nina|last=Nesbitt|date=30 October 2018|publisher=[[Twitter]]|access-date=1 February 2019}}</ref>

''The Sun Will Come Up, The Seasons Will Change'' was released on 1 February 2019, to a positive critic reaction. [[The Independent]] awarded the record four stars<ref>{{cite web |title=Album reviews: Nina Nesbitt – The Sun Will Come Up, the Seasons Will Change, and Highasakite – Uranium Heart |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/nina-nesbitt-the-sun-will-come-up-album-review-tracklist-release-date-highasakite-uranium-heart-a8756201.html |website=independent.co.uk |date=31 January 2019 |access-date=7 February 2019}}</ref> and [[The Times]] gave it three,<ref>{{cite news |title=Nina Nesbitt: The Sun Will Come Up, Seasons Will Change review |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/nina-nesbitt-the-sun-will-come-up-seasons-will-change-review-whdd5x2r7 |website=thetimes.co.uk |access-date=7 February 2019|last1=Hodgkinson |first1=Will }}</ref> whilst CelebMix awarded the album five stars saying, "February is far too early to talk about albums of the year. But, really, is it going to get better than this?".<ref>{{cite web |title=ALBUM REVIEW: Nina Nesbitt's 'The Sun Will Come Up, The Seasons Will Change' |url=https://celebmix.com/album-review-nina-nesbitts-the-sun-will-come-up-the-seasons-will-change/ |website=celebmix.com |date=February 2019 |access-date=7 February 2019}}</ref> On the same day, Nesbitt also released a new music video for her fifth single from the album, titled "Is It Really Me You're Missing?". Nesbitt then embarked on a headlining Tour to support ''The Sun Will Come Up, the Seasons Will Change'', with the North American and UK legs spanning from February to April. She also toured in Australia and New Zealand from 28 May to 1 June.

In March 2019, it was announced that Nesbitt would be supporting [[Jess Glynne]] on the American leg of the Always In Between Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kentonline.co.uk/medway/news/nina-nesbitt-to-perform-at-concert-200457/|title=Nina Nesbitt to perform at concert|date=2019-03-11|website=Kent Online|access-date=2019-03-11}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
Nesbitt was part of the [[mothers2mothers]] fund raising dinner, raising half a million pounds for the charity. She also attended the [[Women in the World]] Summit, discussing the impact of body image in the Internet era.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://nytlive.nytimes.com/womenintheworld/2015/10/07/selfies-the-high-cost-of-low-confidence/|title=Selfies: The high cost of low confidence|work=The New York Times|date=7 October 2015|access-date=9 December 2015}}</ref>

She has worked with brands such as [[Calvin Klein (company)|Calvin Klein]] and [[American Eagle Outfitters|American Eagle]] and was chosen to attend the Women In The World Summit alongside [[Nicole Kidman]] and [[Cara Delevingne]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Scots singer Nina Nesbitt: I've learned to love my body despite abuse from trolls |url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/lifestyle/fashion-beauty/scots-singer-nina-nesbitt-ive-6592877 |access-date=6 August 2018 |publisher=Daily Record}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Nesbitt was in a relationship with singer-songwriter [[Ed Sheeran ]] and she appeared in his music video for "[[Drunk (Ed Sheeran song)|Drunk]]" in 2012.<ref name="Nesbitt">{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/nina-nesbitt-ed-sheeran-wrote-3845502|title=Nina Nesbitt: Ed Sheeran wrote a song about our love life and I'm giving him a miss at T in the Park|work=Daily Record|date=11 July 2014|access-date=16 October 2015}}</ref> She wrote most of her album, ''[[Peroxide (Nina Nesbitt album)|Peroxide]]'', about Sheeran, while she was the subject of Sheeran's songs "Nina" and "[[Photograph (Ed Sheeran song)|Photograph]]".<ref name="Nesbitt"/>

In 2016, Nesbitt said that two people were stalking her.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.edinburghnews.scotsman.com/inspire-me/nina-nesbitt/nina-nesbitt-being-hounded-by-stalkers-1-4089282|title=Nina Stalking Trouble|website=Edinburghnews.scotsman.com}}</ref>
