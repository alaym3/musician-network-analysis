'''Celeste Epiphany Waite''' (born 5 May 1994)<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/celeste_epiphany_waite_born_1994_23375512|title=Celeste Epiphany Waite, Born 05/05/1994 in California|website=[[California Birth Index]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191127164342/https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/celeste_epiphany_waite_born_1994_23375512|archive-date=27 November 2019|access-date=27 November 2019}}</ref> is an American-British singer and songwriter. In 2019, she became the fifth artist to top the [[BBC]]'s annual [[Sound of...]] poll and win the [[Brit Award for Rising Star|Rising Star Award]] at the [[2020 Brit Awards|Brit Awards]] in the same year. Her debut album ''[[Not Your Muse]]'' was released in 2021 and debuted at No. 1 on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. It earned her nominations for [[Brit Award for British Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[Brit Award for British Female Solo Artist|Best Female Solo Artist]] and [[Brit Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] at the [[2021 Brit Awards]], as well as the [[Mercury Prize|2021 Mercury Prize]]. In the same year, Celeste was also nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]] for co-writing "Hear My Voice" from the film ''[[The Trial of the Chicago 7]]'' (2020).

Celeste began her career in 2014 providing vocals for [[Electronic music|electronic]] producers such as [[Avicii]], [[Tieks]] and [[Real Lies]], while also [[self-publishing]] content onto [[SoundCloud]] on the side. She made her solo debut via [[Lily Allen]]'s [[vanity label]] Bank Holiday Records with the [[Extended play|EP]] ''The Milk & the Honey'' (2017). After signing with [[Polydor Records]] in 2018, she released her second EP ''Lately'' (2019).
