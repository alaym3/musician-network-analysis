'''Waax''' (stylised as '''WAAX'''; pronounced "wax") are an Australian rock band from [[Brisbane]], formed in 2012. The band consists of guitarists James Gatling and Ewan Birtwell, drummer Tom Bloomfield, bassist Tom Griffin, and vocalist Marie 'Maz' DeVita. Waax have released two independent EPs: ''[[Holy Sick]]'' (2015) and ''Wild & Weak'' (2017). Their debut album, ''[[Big Grief]]'', was released in 2019. They also won the [[Triple J Unearthed]] BIGSOUND competition in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/waax|title=WAAX|publisher=[[Triple J Unearthed]]|accessdate=17 December 2018}}</ref> Their single "Labrador" won the No. 1 spot on the independent Brisbane radio station [[4ZZZ]]'s Top 100 in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/4zzzradio/status/1080040848086028290|title=4ZZZ - "#ZedHot100 2018 #01 WAAX @waaxband "Labrador" CONGRATS! New #01 Band in #ZedHot100 historyâ€¦ "|website=Twitter|date=1 January 2019|accessdate=11 January 2019}}</ref> In 2019, the single was voted into [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2018|Triple J's Hottest 100 of 2018]] at No. 88.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/18/|title=Hottest 100 2018|publisher=Triple J|date=27 January 2019|accessdate=27 January 2019}}</ref>

==History==
===Early years and ''Holy Sick'' (2012â€“2015)===
Waax was formed in June 2012 by Marie DeVita on vocals, bass and keys; Elijah Gall on lead guitar; Ewan Birtwell on rhythm guitar; and Tom Bloomfield on drums. Shortly after forming, DeVita moved into a strictly vocal role, and the band recruited Ariana Pelser as their full-time bassist.

Their debut single "Wisdom Teeth", released on 23 May 2014, earned the band a spot on the Triple J Unearthed stage at that year's BIGSOUND festival and lifted the band to national prominence.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://archive.aaabackstage.com/movies/6369-waax-release-debut-single.html|title=WAAX Release Debut Single|publisher=AAA Backstage|accessdate=26 February 2019}}</ref>

Pelser left the band in February 2015, followed by Gall shortly thereafter. The pair were replaced with Tom Griffin on bass and Chris Antolak on lead guitar. Waax's next single, "I For an Eye", was released in June and added to the band's popularity. An accompanying music video was released a month later.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0iNf-1Gfq_s|title=WAAX - I For An Eye - (Official Video)|author=WAAX|date=15 July 2015|accessdate=25 February 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> Their newfound visibility in the mainstream also landed them opportunities to perform as a support act for Australian bands such as [[Kingswood (band)|Kingswood]], The Delta Riggs, [[Ecca Vandal]], Emperors and [[Stonefield (band)|Stonefield]], and international bands like [[Guitar Wolf]] and [[Upset (band)|Upset]].

Throughout their touring, the band found time to record and release their first EP, ''[[Holy Sick]]''. The EP was released to SoundCloud on 29 September 2014 with no prior announcement. The EP was met with praise from fans and positive reviews from critics. On 14 December, the EP was made commercially available as a digital download and on CD.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://waax.bandcamp.com/album/holy-sick-ep|title=Holy Sick EP {{!}} WAAX|website=BandCamp|date=14 December 2015|accessdate=17 July 2019}}</ref> Also in December, the band embarked on their first headlining tour throughout Australia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://archive.aaabackstage.com/reviews/7936-review-waax-debut-ep-holy-sick.html|title=Review: WAAX Debut EP "Holy Sick"|first=Jack|last=Doonar|publisher=AAA Backstage|accessdate=26 February 2019}}</ref>

===Restructuring and ''Wild & Weak'' (2016â€“2017)===
After a brief period of restructuring, Waax released the single "Same Same" on 21 November 2016, with a music video following on 6 February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xeh3iM2DcT0|title=WAAX - Same Same (Official Video)|author=WAAX|date=6 February 2017|accessdate=9 July 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> Another single, "Nothing Is Always", was released on 8 March,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://waax.bandcamp.com/track/nothing-is-always|title=Nothing is Always {{!}} WAAX|website=Bandcamp|date=8 March 2017|accessdate=9 July 2019}}</ref> followed shortly thereafter by the title track from their second EP, ''Wild & Weak''. The EP was released independently on 7 June. Drummer Tom Bloomfield described the band's new sound as "something spicy, like a really hot burrito".<ref name="LunchBox" /> To promote the release of the EP, the band performed a ten-date national tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2017/06/24/get-wild-weak-with-waax/|title=Get 'Wild & Weak' with WAAX|first=Paul|last=Brown|publisher=Wall of Sound|date=24 June 2017|accessdate=25 February 2019}}</ref>

===Antolak's departure and ''Big Grief'' (2018â€“present)===
During [[Fall Out Boy]]'s Australian ''Mania'' tour, Waax opened for them as a support band at four shows from February to March 2018.<ref name="Overdrive" /> Signing a label deal with Universal imprint [[Dew Process]], Waax released their single "Labrador" on 27 April, a month after announcing they would perform a twelve-date national tour in its support. They also supported Scottish rock band [[Biffy Clyro]] on their Australian tour.<ref name="Overdrive" >{{cite news|url=https://overdrive-mag.com/2018/09/17/get-to-know-the-good-things-2018-line-up-waax/|title=Get To Know The Good Things 2018 Line Up â€“ WAAX|first=Shannon-Lee|last=Sloane|publisher=Overdrive Mag|date=17 September 2018}} Retrieved 17 December 2018</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/waax-announce-2018-labrador-national-tour/|title=WAAX Announce 2018 'Labrador' National Tour|magazine=Music Feeds|first=Tom|last=Williams|date=26 March 2018}} Retrieved 17 December 2018</ref> During Waax's performance at [[Splendour in the Grass]] in July, former [[Powderfinger]] frontman [[Bernard Fanning]] joined them onstage to sing "Don't Wanna Be Left Out" by Powderfinger.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.triplem.com.au/story/bernard-fanning-popped-up-for-a-surprise-cameo-at-splendour-in-the-grass-102196|title=Bernard Fanning Popped Up For A Surprise Cameo At Splendour In The Grass|publisher=Triple M|first=Rudi|last=Edsall|date=22 July 2018|accessdate=9 July 2019}}</ref> In December, Waax performed at all three dates of the inaugural [[Good Things (Australian music festival)|Good Things]] festival.<ref>{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20181217092716/https://www.goodthingsfestival.com.au/artist/waax/|title=Artist: WAAX|publisher=[[Good Things (Australian music festival)|Good Things Festival]]|url=https://www.goodthingsfestival.com.au/artist/waax/|archivedate=17 December 2018|accessdate=17 December 2018}}</ref>

In January 2019, during the band's performance at the [[Unify Gathering|2019 UNIFY festival]], DeVita announced that their then-untitled debut album would be released in June. On 24 February, the band posted to their social media accounts hinting at a new single release.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/WAAXBAND/photos/a.653355058029961/2327159527316164/|title=WAAX - 27.02.2019|website=Facebook|date=24 February 2019|accessdate=25 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/waaxband/status/1099777315951730688|title=WAAX - 27.02.2019|website=Twitter|date=24 February 2019|accessdate=25 February 2019}}</ref> Later revealed to be titled "FU", the track was premiered a day early on [[Triple J]]'s ''Good Nights'' program.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/waaxband/status/1100139377575915520|title=WAAX - Tune in to @triplej Good Nights w/ @BHustwaite tonight from 6pm to hear our new single 'FU'|website=Twitter|date=26 February 2019|accessdate=26 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2019/02/27/waax-finally-debut-that-fu-song-theyve-been-playing-at-shows-recently/|title=WAAX finally debut that 'FU' song they've been playing at shows recently|first=Paul|last=Brown|date=26 February 2019|website=Wall Of Sound|language=en-US|access-date=26 February 2019}}</ref> On 9 April, a music video for the single was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QKGN9NT5Pr0|title=WAAX - FU|author=WAAX|date=9 April 2019|accessdate=10 April 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> On 18 April, the band announced their debut North American tour, which was scheduled for July 2019. For unknown reasons, the tour was quietly cancelled.

On 21 April, during a performance at The Cambridge Hotel in Newcastle, frontwoman Maz DeVita was reportedly assaulted by a crowd member. She later took to social media to discuss her outrage.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/kira-puru-alex-the-astronaut-waax-were-all-harassed-and-assaulted-at-their-gigs-this-weekend/|title=Kira Puru, Alex The Astronaut & WAAX Were All Harassed/Assaulted At Their Gigs This Weekend|magazine=Music Feeds|first=Jackson|last=Langford|date=22 April 2019|accessdate=3 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BwhPUDqB3-e/|title=W A A X on Instagram: "PSA"|website=Instagram|date=21 April 2019|accessdate=3 July 2019}}</ref> On 10 May, the band announced that they had parted ways with guitarist Chris Antolak.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/WAAXBAND/posts/2441551015877014|title=WAAX - Hey everyone, Just wanted to flag a change within...|website=Facebook|date=10 May 2019|accessdate=16 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2019/05/10/waax-part-ways-with-guitarist-chris-antolak/|title=WAAX part ways with guitarist Chris Antolak|publisher=Wall of Sound|first=Paul|last=Brown|date=10 May 2019|accessdate=15 May 2019}}</ref> In an interview with ''Australian Guitar Magazine'', guitarist Ewan Birtwell refused to answer a question regarding Antolak's departure. "Iâ€™m sure in the future something will be said, but I donâ€™t really know how to tackle that one right now," he said.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.avhub.com.au/features/australian-guitar/waax-good-grief-529903|title=WAAX: Good Grief|website=AVHub|access-date=2019-09-10}}</ref>

On 14 May, Waax released a new single, "I Am". DeVita described the song as being "about wanting to please someone so much that you forget who you are in the process." A music video for the track was released on 18 June,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wGrvzfmYeIs|title=WAAX - I Am|author=WAAX|date=18 June 2019|accessdate=21 June 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> and the following day, the band's debut album was officially announced with the title ''Big Grief'', and a release date of 23 August.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/waaxband/status/1141107753303175168|title=WAAX - "ðŸ“žwell, this is emotional: we finally have the pleasure of announcing that our debut album...|website=Twitter|date=19 June 2019|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref> The band also announced their new lead guitarist, James Gatling.<ref name="WOS James">{{cite web|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2019/06/19/waax-finally-announce-debut-album-big-grief/|title=WAAX FINALLY Announce Debut Album 'Big Grief' + Reveal New Member|publisher=Wall of Sound|first=Paul|last=Brown|date=19 June 2019|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/WAAXBAND/photos/a.545278748837593/2510272355671546/|title=WAAX - We are really stoked to introduce our newest WAAX...|website=Facebook|date=19 June 2019|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref> During the week leading up to the album's release, it was spotlighted as Triple J's Feature Album, which saw all 12 of its tracks receive airtime.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/featured-music/feature-albums/waax---big-grief/11425776|title=WAAX - Big Grief - Feature Albums|publisher=ABC|work=Triple J|date=18 August 2019|accessdate=23 August 2019}}</ref> ''Big Grief'' premiered on the [[ARIA Charts]] at #11 (#3 Australian album, #2 on vinyl).

At a secret performance in Brisbane on 6 September, WAAX debuted a new song titled "However" (or "One Good Day").<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/waax/2019/crowbar-black-brisbane-australia-239cb07f.html|title=WAAX Setlist at Crowbar Black, Brisbane|website=setlist.fm|language=en|access-date=2019-09-10}}</ref> Written following the release of ''Big Grief'', the track alludes directly to the struggles faced by the band in the months preceding the albumâ€™s release and in the aftermath of Antolakâ€™s firing. A studio version has not been confirmed; however, a live recording of the song surfaced on YouTube shortly after the performance.<ref>{{Citation|title=WAAX - "One Good Day" (Live at Crowbar Brisbane, 06/09/2019)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4pcVzF8s0GM|language=en|access-date=2019-09-10}}</ref>

On 22 April 2020, Waax released a cover of [[Julia Jacklin]]'s "Pool Party".<ref>{{cite web| url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/listen-to-waax-covering-julia-jacklins-pool-party/ | title=Listen To WAAX Covering Julia Jacklin's 'Pool Party'| first=Alex| last=Gallagher| publisher=Music Feeds| date=22 April 2020| accessdate=23 April 2020}}</ref> 

==Musical style==
Frontwoman Marie DeVita describes the band's style as "a mixture of post-punk and alternative rock".<ref name="Visit Brisbane">{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20181217100938/https://www.visitbrisbane.com.au/the-city-sounds/city-sounds-interviews-and-features/meet-waax?sc_lang=en-au|title=Meet WAAX|publisher=Visit Brisbane|work=The City Sounds|url=https://www.visitbrisbane.com.au/the-city-sounds/city-sounds-interviews-and-features/meet-waax?sc_lang=en-au|archivedate=17 December 2018|accessdate=17 December 2018}}</ref>

DeVita is the band's primary songwriter with prior assistance from then-lead guitarist Chris Antolak.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.avhub.com.au/features/australian-guitar/waax-the-wild-wild-waax-473121|title=WAAX: The Wild, Wild WAAX|first=Matt|last=Doria|publisher=AV Hub|date=23 October 2017}} Retrieved 17 December 2018</ref> On the creation of their ''Wild & Weak'' EP, drummer Tom Bloomfield shared how DeVita, while writing the lyrics, "was really getting into delving into her internal struggles and [he thought] that's what has inspired the record. Really emotive both lyrically and musically."<ref name="LunchBox" >{{cite news|url=https://www.lunchboxtv.com.au/waax-calling-help-toms-drunken-mistake/|title=WAAX: On Calling For Help & Tom's Drunken Mistake|first=Clair|last=Wainwright|publisher=LunchBox TV|date=24 May 2017}} Retrieved 17 December 2018</ref>
