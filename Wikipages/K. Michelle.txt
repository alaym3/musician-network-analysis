'''Kimberly Michelle Pate''' (born March 4, 1982) is an American singer and television personality.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/interview-k-michelle-talks-%E2%80%9Crebellious-soul%E2%80%9D-winning-once-she-stopped-caring-reality-tv/|title=Interview: K. Michelle Talks "Rebellious Soul", Winning Once She Stopped Caring, Reality TV|work=New R&B Music, R&B Videos, R&B Interviews, R&B Concert Coverage - YouKnowIGotSoul|access-date=January 12, 2015}}</ref> She rose to fame as the breakout star of [[VH1]]'s ''[[Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta]]'' after appearing as a regular cast member for two seasons on the reality television series.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/ratings/2016/01/27/vh1-love-and-hip-hop-breakout-stars-rule-the-monday-night-scene-577514/20160127vh101/ |title=Ratings - VH1 "Love & Hip Hop" Breakout Stars Rule the Monday Night Scene |website=TheFutonCritic.com |date=January 27, 2016 |access-date=March 6, 2020}}</ref> Michelle subsequently signed with [[Atlantic Records]]. Her debut studio album, ''[[Rebellious Soul]]'', debuted at number two on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and number one on the US [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] charts.<ref name="Elliott"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://singersroom.com/content/2013-08-21/K-Michelles-Rebellious-Soul-Album-Sales-Lead-To-Impressive-Debut/|title=News: K. Michelle's Rebellious Soul Album Sales Lead To Impressive Debut|work=Singersroom|date=August 21, 2013|access-date=January 12, 2015}}</ref> Her second studio album ''[[Anybody Wanna Buy a Heart?]]'' was released on December 9, 2014. The album debuted at number six in the US and spawned three singles "[[Love 'Em All]]", "[[Maybe I Should Call]]" and "[[Hard to Do]]". The album sold 87,000 copies in its first week in sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://singersroom.com/content/2014-12-17/k-michelle-tops-the-rb-chart-with-new-album-anybody-wanna-buy-a-heart/|title=K. Michelle Tops The R&B Chart With New Album, 'Anybody Wanna Buy A Heart'|first=Gary |last=Gentles|work=[[Singersroom]]|date=December 17, 2014}}</ref>

In March 2016, Michelle's third studio album, ''[[More Issues Than Vogue]]'', was released to positive reviews. Singles from the album included "[[Not a Little Bit]]" and "[[Ain't You]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/more-issues-than-vogue/k-michelle|title=More Issues Than Vogue by K. Her fifth  studio album, ''[[All Monsters Are Human]]'' was released on January 31, 2020. The album was preceded by two singles "Supahood" and "[[The Rain (K. Michelle song)|The Rain]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2019/09/19/k-michelle-yung-miami-kash-doll-supa-hood/|title=K. ==Early life==
Kimberly Michelle Pate was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]]. As a child, Michelle learned to play piano and guitar, and took voice lessons from Bob Westbrook, who also trained pop singers [[Justin Timberlake]] and [[Britney Spears]]. She graduated from [[Overton High School (Memphis, Tennessee)|Overton High School]] in Memphis in 2000.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theteenappeal.wordpress.com/2015/05/05/overton-high-visited-by-former-student-k-michelle/ |title=Overton High visited by former student K. She was crowned the Freshman Attendant of the [[Homecoming Court]] at the age of 18.<ref name="rattler">{{cite book|url=http://ufdc.ufl.edu/AM00000319/00013/30j|title=Rattler|publisher=[[Florida A&M University]]|date=2001|access-date=January 12, 2015}}</ref> She is a member of [[Delta Sigma Theta]] sorority having pledged FAMU's Beta Alpha chapter in fall 2001.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://s2smagazine.com/78938/k-michelle-talks-delta-sigma-theta-being-a-role-model/ |title=K.Michelle talks Delta Sigma Theta, being a role model |publisher=S2SMagazine.com |access-date=January 12, 2015}}</ref> She reports that she stopped attending classes for a short time to have a son born September 2004, then returned to finish college.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eurweb.com/2014/10/k-michelle-on-beef-with-tamar-and-not-sexing-chad-ochocinco-watch/ |title=K.
