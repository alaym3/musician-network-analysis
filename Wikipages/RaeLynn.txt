'''Racheal Lynn Woodward''' (born May 4, 1994),<ref name=billboard-rovi>{{cite news| url = http://www.billboard.com/artist/277332/raelynn/biography | title = RaeLynn > Biography| publisher=[[Rovi]] via [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | first=Steve |last=Leggett | access-date= January 10, 2016 | archive-date= July 25, 2015 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150725073223/http://www.billboard.com/artist/277332/raelynn/biography| url-status=live}}</ref><!--Billboard also verifies birth name Racheal Lynn Woodward--> better known as '''RaeLynn''', is an American singer and songwriter who was a contestant on ''[[The Voice (U.S.)|The Voice]]'' in [[The Voice (U.S. season 2)|season two]] (2012). She was eliminated in the quarterfinals.

==Early life==
RaeLynn was born and raised in [[Baytown, Texas]],<ref name=billboard-rovi /><ref name="toc">{{cite web|last=Dukes|first=Billy|title=RaeLynn Dishes on Record Deal, New Music and Advice From Blake Shelton|url= http://tasteofcountry.com/raelynn-record-deal-new-music-blake-sheltoninterview/|publisher=TasteOfCountry.com|date=July 19, 2012|access-date=December 22, 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151003194036/http://tasteofcountry.com/raelynn-record-deal-new-music-blake-sheltoninterview/ | archive-date= October 3, 2015| url-status=live}}</ref> and at age 15 wanted a career in music. She graduated high school early, in 2010.{{citation needed|date=April 2017}}

==Career==
===2012: ''The Voice''===
RaeLynn auditioned for the second season of ''The Voice'' after being encouraged by a contestant on Team Adam who wrote songs with her.<ref>{{cite web|last=Scarpa|first=Gina|title=The Voice Season 2: Exclusive Interview with RaeLynn|url=http://www.realitywanted.com/newsitem/5282-the-voice-season-2-exclusive-interview-with-raelynn#.UNWiTW9t7ng|work=RealityWanted|access-date=22 December 2012}}</ref> Her audition song was "[[Hell on Heels]]" by [[Pistol Annies]], [[Miranda Lambert]]'s group. Both [[Adam Levine]] and [[Blake Shelton]] wanted her on their team, but she eventually chose Team Blake. She sang "[[Free Fallin']]" against Adley Stump in the Battle Rounds and won. Her victory was, however, highly contested, and Blake publicly defended his choice in a Twitter war.{{citation needed|date=December 2018}} She then went on to the live shows to perform "[[Wake Up Call (Maroon 5 song)|Wake Up Call]]" by [[Maroon 5]] and "[[She's Country]]" by [[Jason Aldean]] and the latter later became her best selling cover on the show.<ref>{{cite web|last=Dunkerley|first=Beville|title=RaeLynn, 'The Voice': Singer 'Picks' on Blake Shelton & Predicts Season 3 Winner|url=http://www.theboot.com/2012/12/12/raelynn-the-voice/ |work=the Boot|access-date=22 December 2012}}</ref> For her last-chance performance, she sang "[[If I Die Young]]" by [[The Band Perry]], but was not chosen over Erin Willett and thus eliminated.<ref>{{cite web|title=Blake Shelton Explains Decision to Eliminate RaeLynn on The Voice|first=Becca|last=Walls|date=April 18, 2012|work=The Country Vibe with Chuck and Becca|url=http://www.thecountryvibe.com/blog/blake-shelton-explains-decision-to-eliminate-raelynn-on-the-voice/|access-date=January 10, 2016|archive-date=January 10, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160110225541/http://www.thecountryvibe.com/blog/blake-shelton-explains-decision-to-eliminate-raelynn-on-the-voice/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2012–2018: After ''The Voice'' and ''WildHorse''===
As of 2012, she was signed to [[Republic Nashville]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Gallo|first=Phil|title=Three 'Voice' Alums at Work on New Albums |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1481360/three-voice-alums-at-work-on-new-albums |access-date=22 December 2012|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=December 17, 2012}}</ref> She has written songs with [[Natalie Hemby]], [[Jon Randall]], John Wiggins, and fellow ''Voice'' contestants [[Nicolle Galyon]] and Hailey Steele.<ref name="toc" />

She has performed a song co-written with Miranda Lambert entitled "Lie",<ref>{{cite web|title=Miranda Lambert and The Voice Contestant RaeLynn Perform New Co-Written Song "Lie" (VIDEO)|url=http://www.wetpaint.com/the-voice/articles/miranda-lambert-and-the-voice-contestant-raelynn-perform-new-co-written-song-lie-video|access-date=22 December 2012}}</ref> and returned with fellow finalists [[Chris Mann (singer)|Chris Mann]] and [[Juliet Simms]] to perform at the semifinal results show on the [[The Voice (U.S. season 3)|third season]] of ''The Voice'', performing her new single, "[[Boyfriend (RaeLynn song)|Boyfriend]]". "Boyfriend" later charted on the [[Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles]] chart, which acts as a 25-song extension to the [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]], thus making her the first contestant to chart on one of Billboard's overall singles charts for post-Voice activity. The single sold 27,000 copies in its debut week.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bjorke|first=Matt|title=Country Chart News - The Top 30 Digital Singles: The Week of December 19, 2012: Cassadee Pope Debuts with "Over You"; RaeLynn Debuts with Boyfriend|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/country-chart-news-the-top-30-digital-singles-the-week-of-december-19-2012-cassadee-pope-debuts-with-over-you-raelynn-debuts-with-boyfriend|access-date=22 December 2012|newspaper=Roughstock|date=December 20, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130108023659/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/country-chart-news-the-top-30-digital-singles-the-week-of-december-19-2012-cassadee-pope-debuts-with-over-you-raelynn-debuts-with-boyfriend|archive-date=8 January 2013}}</ref>

She performed backing vocals for [[Blake Shelton]]'s 2013 single, "[[Boys 'Round Here]]",<ref>{{cite news|last=Addison|first=Daryl|title=GAC Album Review: Blake Shelton's Based On A True Story…|url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/03/19/gac-album-review-blake-sheltons-based-on-a-true-story/|access-date=28 May 2013|newspaper=Great American Country|date=March 19, 2013}}</ref> which debuted at No. 67 on the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot 100]] chart, and peaked at No. 16.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Blake Shelton Album & Song Chart History|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=Blake Shelton |chart=Hot 100}}|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Hot 100]] for [[Blake Shelton]]  |publisher=Prometheus Global Media|access-date=28 May 2013}}</ref> The song is also featured on Shelton's album, ''[[Based on a True Story…]]''. She was signed to [[Dr. Luke|Dr. Luke's]] publishing label Prescription Songs in April 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/1558798/raelynn-inks-deal-with-dr-luke-on-eve-of-recording-debut-album|title=RaeLynn Inks Deal With Dr. Luke on Eve of Recording Debut Album|date=2013-04-18}}</ref>

RaeLynn's debut single to country radio, "[[God Made Girls]]", was released on July 1, 2014.<ref>https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/god-made-girls-single/id893395273</ref> On December 2, 2014, she performed her single on [[The Voice (U.S. season 7)|season seven]] of ''The Voice''. It went on to be a Top 10 hit on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart and a Top 20 hit on the ''Billboard'' [[Country Airplay]] charts, and was certified [[Gold single|Gold]] by the [[RIAA]]. On January 13, 2015, RaeLynn released an EP through [[Valory Music Group|Valory Music Co.]] titled ''Me'', which debuted at No. 49 on the ''Billboard'' 200, and No. 7 on the Top Country Albums chart, with 5,500 copies sold in its first week.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://roughstock.com/news/2015/01/32254-country-album-chart-report-january-21-2015/|title=Country Album Chart Report For January 21, 2015 |author= Matt Bjorke |date=January 21, 2015 |work=Roughstock }}</ref> "[[For a Boy]]" was released on March 23, 2015 as RaeLynn's second single to country radio. It was less successful than its predecessor and a year later, in April 2016, it was announced that RaeLynn had parted ways with Valory Music Co., leaving her debut album delayed until 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/raelynn-wildhorse-album-delay-2017/|title=RaeLynn Pushes Back 'WildHorse' Release to 2017|website=Taste of Country|access-date=2017-01-17}}</ref>

On June 7, 2016, it was announced that RaeLynn had signed a new record deal with [[Warner Bros. Records|Warner Bros. Nashville]]. "[[Love Triangle (song)|Love Triangle]]" was released on July 11, 2016 as her first single for the label. It served as the lead-off to her debut album, ''[[WildHorse]]'', which was released on March 24, 2017. [[SpinMedia|Sounds Like Nashville]] praised RaeLynn's "standout debut" and called its lead single, "Love Triangle", "country music storytelling at its finest and country radio is better because of it."<ref>{{Cite news|last=Hostelley|first=Laura|url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/reviews/album-review-raelynns-wildhorse/|title=Album Review: RaeLynn's ''WildHorse''|date=March 24, 2017|work=Sounds Like Nashville|access-date=March 24, 2017}}</ref>

''WildHorse'' landed within the Top 10 on ''Billboard’s'' All-Genre Album Sales Chart and debuted at No.1 on the Billboard Country Albums Chart, marking the first female country artist to do so since [[Maren Morris]]' debut ''[[Hero (Maren Morris album)|HERO]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/raelynn-celebrates-chart-topping-debut-album-wildhorse/|title=RaeLynn Celebrates Chart-Topping Debut Album, 'WildHorse'|date=2017-04-05|work=Sounds Like Nashville|access-date=2017-04-05|language=en-US}}</ref>

===2019—present: Label change===
On November 8, 2019, she released the single "Bra Off" and announced that she had signed to [[Florida Georgia Line|Florida Georgia Line's]] Round Here Records.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/2019/11/08/raelynn-inks-deal-florida-georgia-lines-round-here-records/2525426001/|title=RaeLynn teams with Florida Georgia Line's Round Here Records for new deal, music|last=Watts|first=Cindy|website=The Tennessean|language=en|access-date=2019-11-08}}</ref> "Keep Up" followed in February 2020, and was sent to country radio as her first single under the label in April 2020.<ref name="keep up"/>

==Personal life==
In October 2015, RaeLynn became engaged to then-financial advisor Joshua Davis.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/country/raelynn-engaged-to-josh-davis-see-the-ring/|title=RaeLynn Is Engaged to Josh Davis – See the Ring!|last=Anderson|first=Danielle|work=People|date=October 13, 2015|access-date=October 24, 2017}}</ref> They were married on February 27, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/country/raelynn-marries-joshua-davis-wedding-photo/|title=Voice Alum RaeLynn Marries Joshua Davis|last=Anderson|first=Danielle|work=People|date=February 27, 2016|access-date=October 24, 2017}}</ref> A year after their wedding, RaeLynn revealed that Davis had enlisted in the military.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/raelynn-husband-josh-davis-military/|title=RaeLynn's Husband Joins the Military|last=Stefano|first=Angela|work=The Boot|date=February 14, 2017|access-date=October 24, 2017}}</ref> In May 2021, the couple announced that they are expecting their first child, a daughter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/raelynn-pregnant-expecting-first-baby-husband-josh-davis-daughter-exclusive/|title=RaeLynn Is Pregnant! Country Singer Expecting First Baby, a Daughter: 'The Best Birthday Present'|last=Michaud|first=Sarah|work=People|date=May 4, 2021|access-date=June 6, 2021}}</ref>

She has [[type 1 diabetes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.diabetesdaily.com/blog/singer-raelynn-shares-her-life-on-the-road-with-diabetes-459471/|title=Singer RaeLynn Shares Her Life on the Road with Diabetes|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180112161455/https://www.diabetesdaily.com/blog/singer-raelynn-shares-her-life-on-the-road-with-diabetes-459471/|archive-date=2018-01-12|access-date=January 11, 2018|date=2017-08-09}}</ref>
