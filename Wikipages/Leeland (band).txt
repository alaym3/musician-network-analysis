'''Leeland''' is a [[Christian rock]] band from [[Baytown, Texas|Baytown]], [[Texas]]. The band was formed in 2004, although lead singer Leeland Mooring had been writing songs beforehand. Leeland consists of [[Leeland Dayton Mooring|Leeland Mooring]] (lead vocals, guitar), and Casey Moore (guitar, background vocals). The band's original line-up included Jeremiah Wood (guitar), who left the band in late 2006 and was replaced by guitarist Matt Campbell in early 2007, Jack Mooring (piano), Mike Smith (drums) and Jake Holtz (bass).

Leeland has released five studio albums, the latest in July 2016.  The band received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination and two [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Award]] nominations for their debut album ''[[Sound of Melodies]]'' (2006).<ref name="canada">{{cite web| url=http://www.soulshine.ca/news/newsarticle.php?nid=4196 |title=Leeland Ready for Grammys, Visit Canada  |date=February 9, 2007 |work=Soul Shine Magazine |access-date=April 2, 2008}}</ref><ref name="GMA Albums of the Year" /> The band's third album, ''[[Love Is on the Move]]'', was released on August 25, 2009.

Leeland's fourth studio album, ''[[The Great Awakening]]'', was released on September 20, 2011, and received a nomination to the [[54th Grammy Awards]] for [[Best Contemporary Christian Music Album]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.grammy.com/nominees |title=58th Annual GRAMMY Awards Winners & Nominees |access-date=July 12, 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120201120225/http://www.grammy.com/nominees |archive-date=February 1, 2012 |df=mdy }}</ref> 

After a five-year hiatus, Leeland released their fifth studio album in July 2016, ''[[Invisible (Leeland album)|Invisible]]'', via the [[Bethel Music]] label.

==Band history==

=== Formation and initial years ===
Lead singer [[Leeland Dayton Mooring|Leeland Mooring]] wrote his first song, "Shine," at the age of 11.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soulshine.ca/features/featuresarticle.php?fid=215 |title=A Sound of Melodies Rises Up |date=July 19, 2006 |publisher=Soul Shine Magazine |author=Whitfield, Lindsay |access-date=February 15, 2008}}</ref> After giving a successful performance of the song at a church in [[Illinois]], the Mooring family began touring the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.worshiptogether.com/resources/worship.aspx?iid=491871 |title=Featured Worship Leader |publisher=www.worshiptogether.com |access-date=May 25, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080608131817/http://www.worshiptogether.com/resources/worship.aspx?iid=491871 |archive-date=June 8, 2008 |df=mdy }}</ref> When he was 14, Mooring entered into Embassy Music's Ultimate Talent Search contest in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], becoming a finalist in the songwriting and artist divisions.<ref name="ccmmag" /> Although he did not win the competition, one of the contest's judges, Kent Coley, took interest in the young teen.<ref name="ccmmag" /> A year later, [[Eddie DeGarmo]], president of EMI CMG Publishing, also expressed interest in Mooring, and the two signed a songwriting contract on [[EMI]]/[[Capitol Music Group]].<ref name="ccmmag" /> Less than a year later, Leeland Mooring, his older brother Jack Mooring, their cousin Jake Holtz, and friends Jeremiah Wood and Mike Smith, whom the Moorings had known for several years,<ref name="cmt-bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/artists/leeland.html |title=Leeland - Music |work=[[Christianity Today|Christian Music Today]] |access-date=May 30, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080516173814/http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/artists/leeland.html |archive-date=May 16, 2008 |url-status=dead |df=mdy }}</ref> officially signed as a band onto [[Essential Records (Christian)|Essential Records]], a subsidiary of [[Sony BMG]]'s [[Provident Label Group]].<ref name="reuters">{{cite web |url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/musicNews/idUKN0124006720080303 |title=Touring fuels songwriting for Christian band Leeland |date=March 2, 2008 |author=Price, Deborah Evans |publisher=Reuters |access-date=May 30, 2008}}</ref> The five members began practicing at night after youth group in their church's building, which doubled as a [[funeral home]].<ref name="ccmmag" /><ref name="1cubed">{{cite web |url=http://www.1cubed.com/display.php?ID=261&sectionID=26 |title=Leeland Biography |publisher=1 Cubed |access-date=May 11, 2008}}</ref> Leeland Mooring's first name was used as the band's name because the members said that "it sounded cool".<ref name="cmt">{{cite web |url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/interviews/2006/leeland-mini-0806.html |title= Leeland, 08/06 - Interview|date=August 21, 2006 |work=Christian Music Today |author=Breimeier, Russ |access-date=April 2, 2008}}</ref>

[[File:Leeland-MikeSmith.jpg|thumb|right|Drummer Mike Smith in 2007]]
Leeland began recording for their debut release, ''[[Sound of Melodies]]'', in 2005, with [[Matt Bronleewe]], [[Marc Byrd]], and [[Steve Hindalong]] producing the album. It was released in the United States on August 15, 2006. At the [[49th Grammy Awards]] on December 7, 2006, the album was nominated for Best Pop/Contemporary Gospel Album.<ref name="cmspin">{{cite web|url=http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=4033&z=26|title=Leeland Receives Grammy Nomination for Sounds of Melodies|website=Cmspin.com|date=December 8, 2007|access-date=February 15, 2008|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708170636/http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=4033&z=26|archive-date=July 8, 2011|df=mdy-all}}</ref> 
In 2007 the band received five [[GMA Dove Award]] nominations, including "New Artist of the Year"<ref>{{cite web |url=http://christianmusic.about.com/od/doves/a/gma07awards.htm |title=38th Annual GMA Awards Nominations & Winners |author=Jones, Kim |publisher=[[About.com]] |access-date=February 15, 2008}}</ref> and two nominations for their debut album ''[[Sound of Melodies]]''.<ref name="GMA Albums of the Year">[http://christianmusic.about.com/od/doves/a/07gmaAOY.htm 38th Annual GMA Dove Awards Album of the Year Nominees & Winners] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707074839/http://christianmusic.about.com/od/doves/a/07gmaAOY.htm |date=July 7, 2011 }}, About.com: Christian Music</ref> The album was also released in Japan on March 21, 2007, with the title song, "Sound of Melodies," peaking at No.&nbsp;6 on the general Japan radio chart.<ref name="Japan">{{cite web |url=http://www.breathecast.com/Christian.Music.Artist-LEELAND/Article-564_1569.htm |title=Leeland's Debut Album Drops in Japan |publisher=BREATHECast.com |date=March 30, 2007 |access-date=April 2, 2008}}</ref>

The band has worked closely with Christian music legend [[Michael W. Smith]], who remarked, "Leeland is the best thing I have heard in a long, long time".<ref name="artistdirect">{{cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/nad/music/artist/bio/0,,3705992,00.html |title=Biography |publisher=Artist Direct |access-date=May 28, 2008}}</ref> Leeland Mooring co-wrote six songs on Smith's album  ''[[Stand (Michael W. Smith album)|Stand]]'', and wrote one song by himself.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.crosswalk.com/root/music/1463815/page0/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120730014723/http://www.crosswalk.com/root/music/1463815/page0/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=July 30, 2012 |title=Michael W. Smith and Leeland Mooring: Eye 2 Eye |work=[[CCM Magazine]] |publisher=Crosswalk.com |author=Swartzendruber, Jay |access-date=February 15, 2008 }}</ref> Jack Mooring married Smith's daughter Whitney Smith-Mooring on June 1, 2007.<ref name="scoop">{{cite web |url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/news/2007/thescoop-0709.html |title=The Scoop |publisher=Christian Music Today |date=July 9, 2007 |author=Farias, Andree |access-date=April 13, 2008}}</ref>

After four years as a member of Leeland, Jeremiah Wood left the band in October 2006 to pursue other endeavors.<ref name="guitaristleaves">{{cite web| url=http://www.soulshine.ca/news/newsarticle.php?nid=3998 |title=Leeland Guitarist Departs |date=December 5, 2006 |work=Soul Shine Magazine |access-date=April 2, 2008}}</ref> Austin Tirado was hired as a temporary guitar player until February 2007. In February, Matt Campbell was hired as a temporary guitarist; he was added as an official band member after three months.

Leeland's second studio album, ''[[Opposite Way]]'', was recorded in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref name="newalbum" /> In November 2007, the band announced that the album would release on February 26, 2008.<ref name="newalbum">{{cite web|url=http://www.soulshine.ca/news/newsarticle.php?nid=4864|title=Leeland Announce New Album Details|publisher=Soul Shine Magazine|date=November 20, 2007|access-date=May 25, 2008}}</ref> Lead singer [[Leeland Dayton Mooring|Leeland Mooring]] commented, "[''Opposite Way''] is primarily a call to our generation to passionately walk the 'opposite way' from the world, that it's OK to live the [[Christianity|Christian]] life and to be on fire for God, even if it makes us look different."<ref name="indiebands">{{cite web|url=http://www.independentbands.com/cd/leeland/oppositeway.html |title=Leeland : Opposite Way CD |publisher=IndependentBands.com |access-date=May 30, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080616025004/http://www.independentbands.com/cd/leeland/oppositeway.html |archive-date=June 16, 2008 |df=mdy }}</ref><ref name="bc">{{cite web |url=http://www.breathecast.com/Christian.Music.Artist-LEELAND/AlbumView-564_1890.htm |title=Opposite Way - LEELAND |publisher=BREATHEcast.com |access-date=May 30, 2008}}</ref> In its debut week of release, ''Opposite Way'' peaked at No.&nbsp;72 on the [[Billboard 200|Billboard Top 200]] albums chart,<ref name="CCMrightdirection">{{cite web|url=http://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/headlines/11570104/ |title=Leeland's Opposite Way Going in the Right Direction |publisher=CCM Magazine |date=March 5, 2008 |access-date=April 6, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080312034134/http://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/headlines/11570104/ |archive-date=March 12, 2008 |df=mdy }}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=leeland|chart=all}} |title=Opposite Way |publisher=Billboard.com |access-date=April 7, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130614070324/http://www.billboard.com/artist/306870/leeland/chart |archive-date=June 14, 2013 |df=mdy }}</ref> and also reached No.&nbsp;1 on the [[iTunes Store|iTunes]] Christian Albums chart.<ref name="iTunes#1">{{cite web |url=http://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/headlines/11569738/ |title=Leeland's Opposite Way Reaches No.&nbsp;1 at iTunes Christian Albums Sales Chart During Street Week |publisher=CCM Magazine |date=February 29, 2008 |access-date=April 2, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080306113201/http://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/headlines/11569738/ |archive-date=March 6, 2008 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Starting in September 2007, Leeland began touring with [[Grammy Award]]-winning band [[Casting Crowns]] on their ''[[The Altar and the Door]]'' tour. The tour continued until May 1, 2008,<ref name="CMI video">{{cite web |url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2008/01/08.Leeland%20Shoots%20Music%20Video%20for%20Count%20Me%20In.asp |title=Leeland Shoots Music Video for "Count Me In" |work=Provident Label Group |publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout |date=January 7, 2008 |access-date=February 15, 2008}}</ref> and was named the "highest-grossing Christian tour of the year" touring through 83 cities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/headlines/11575311/ |title=Casting Crowns' The Altar and the Door Tour Named Highest-Grossing Christian Tour of the Year |publisher=CCM Magazine |date=May 8, 2008 |access-date=May 25, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080515053723/http://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/headlines/11575311/ |archive-date=May 15, 2008 |df=mdy }}</ref>

The band's song "Tears of the Saints" from their debut album received two [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Award]] nominations in 2008.<ref name="dovenominees2008" /> Lead singer Leeland Mooring was nominated for "Songwriter of the Year" and co-writer of the song "Be Lifted High" with [[Michael W. Smith]].<ref name="dovenominees2008">{{cite web|url=http://www.doveawards.com/doveawards/nominees.php |title=Dove Awards 2008 |website=DoveAwards.com |access-date=April 2, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080406132108/http://www.doveawards.com/doveawards/nominees.php |archive-date=April 6, 2008 |url-status=dead |df=mdy }}</ref>

Their song "Brighter Days," from the ''Opposite Way'' album, was featured in the June 22, 2008, episode of the television show ''[[Army Wives]]''.<ref name="armywives">{{cite web|url=http://www.hmmagazine.com/exclusive/hm_news_update_week_ending_629200806/index.php |title=Internet Exclusives: Leeland's Brighter Days used on Lifetime Network |publisher=[[HM Magazine]] |date=June 29, 2008 |access-date=July 16, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080828205909/http://www.hmmagazine.com/exclusive/hm_news_update_week_ending_629200806/index.php |archive-date=August 28, 2008 }}</ref> The show's music supervisor, Frankie Pine, commented, "We thought the use of 'Brighter Days' in this episode of ''Army Wives'' was moving, and was the perfect fit for what we were looking for".<ref name="armywives" /> The song was also featured on the soundtrack of ''[[Fireproof (film)|Fireproof]]'', a film featuring actor Kirk Cameron.

In December 2008, the band's ''[[Opposite Way]]'' album was nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] in the category Best Pop/Contemporary Gospel Album.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://christianmusic.about.com/od/grammys/a/2008grammys.htm |title=2009 GRAMMY Awards |publisher=[[About.com]] |access-date=January 4, 2009 |author=Kim Jones}}</ref>

Leeland's third studio album, titled ''[[Love Is on the Move]]'', was released on August 25, 2009, in the United States.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/artists/Leeland.asp |title=Leeland Discography |publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout |access-date=April 22, 2009}}</ref> The album's [[lead single]] "Follow You", which includes vocals from singer-songwriter [[Brandon Heath]], was released to Christian radio on August 7.<ref name="tennessean">{{cite news|url=http://www.tennessean.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20090721/TUNEIN/90720050 |title=Christian pop crew Leeland links up with Nashville's Brandon Heath for album, tour |newspaper=[[The Tennessean]] |date=July 21, 2009|access-date=August 2, 2009}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> In September, the band launched their "Follow You" Tour, co-headlining with Brandon Heath and featuring [[Francesca Battistelli]] as an opening act.<ref name="tennessean" /><ref>[http://direct.crossrhythms.co.uk/product/Love-Is-On-The-Move/Leeland/82416.asp]{{dead link|date=September 2011}}</ref>

=== Changes in membership and label ===
In early 2011, only a few months before the release of their fourth studio album, bassist Jake Holtz left the band to pursue a career with the US Army.<ref name="jake">{{cite web |url=http://yourmusiczone.com/news.php?area=&aid=595 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304060921/http://yourmusiczone.com/news.php?area=&aid=595 |url-status=dead |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |title=Jake Holtz Departs Leeland |date=February 17, 2011|access-date=December 10, 2012}} </ref>

Leeland's fourth studio album, ''[[The Great Awakening]]'', was released on September 20, 2011.{{Citation needed|date=December 2012}}

In September 2015, Leeland announced via YouTube that they would be joining [[Bethel Music]] as part of the Bethel Music Collective,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KuT2wDUuGb0 |title=Meet Leeland |access-date=2016-07-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/810-leeland-joins-bethel-music-collective |title=Leeland Joins Bethel Music Collective |author=Herb Longs |access-date=July 11, 2016}}</ref> after Leeland Mooring and Casey Moore established an organic friendship with Bethel's founding member, [[Brian Johnson (American musician)|Brian Johnson]].<ref name="Invisible CCM article">{{cite web |url= http://www.ccmmagazine.com/features/leeland-inside-invisible/ |title= Leeland – Inside Invisible – CCM Magazine |last= Williams | first = Lindsay | date = July 31, 2016 |website= [[CCM Magazine]] |access-date= May 9, 2017}}</ref>

Leeland's fifth studio album, ''[[Invisible (Leeland album)|Invisible]]'', was released on July 22, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thep.com/Project.aspx?ProductID=4306297991|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817074729/https://thep.com/Project.aspx?ProductID=4306297991|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-08-17|title=Provident Distribution &#124; Products|date=August 17, 2016|access-date=September 16, 2020}}</ref> It is the first album the band has released under the Bethel label, with Brian Johnson featuring on the song "Son Was Lifted Up".<ref name="Invisible CCM article" />

== Musical style ==
Leeland's musical style has been defined as "[[Alternative rock|alternative]] [[Contemporary Christian music|CCM]]"<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p820312|pure_url=yes}} |title=Leeland > Overview |website=[[AllMusic]] |author=Monger, James |access-date=May 21, 2008}}</ref> and "[[progressive rock]]".<ref name="artistdirect" /> The band's songs have been defined as "melodic" with "[[Britpop|Brit pop]]/rock" influences,<ref name="ct-oppositeway" /><ref name="ct-som" /> being compared to [[Coldplay]], [[Keane (band)|Keane]], [[Travis (band)|Travis]] and [[U2]].<ref name="ct-oppositeway">{{cite web |url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/reviews/2008/oppositeway.html |title=Leeland - Opposite Way |author=Breimeier, Russ |work=[[Christianity Today|Christian Music Today]] |access-date=May 21, 2008}}</ref><ref name="ct-som">{{cite web |url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/reviews/2006/soundofmelodies.html |title=Leeland - Sound of Melodies |work=[[Christianity Today|Christian Music Today]] |author=Breimeier, Russ |access-date=May 21, 2008}}</ref>

Their first album, ''[[Sound of Melodies]]'', has been described as "sometimes [[Contemporary worship music|worshipful]], always melodic".<ref name="ct-som" /> Leeland's style moved in a more [[rock music|rock]] direction with their second album ''[[Opposite Way]]''. [[Allmusic]] reviewer Jared Johnson noted that the band "cemented their sound by creating electrifying walls of guitar" with their second release, having the same "energetic and melodic" sound as their first album.<ref name="allmusic-ow">{{cite web |url={{Allmusic|class=album|id=r1314576|pure_url=yes}} |title=Opposite Way > Overview |website=[[AllMusic]] |author=Johnson, Jared |access-date=May 22, 2008}}</ref> Of their third album ''[[Love Is on the Move]]'', Christian youth magazine StreetBrand said the album shows "just how much they have matured musically as a group, creatively as songwriters and spiritually as a band with a penchant for God-pleasing worship."<ref name="streetbrand-01">{{cite web|url=http://www.streetbrand.com/200908071038/culture/leeland-love-is-on-the-move-christian-music-review.html |title=Love Is on the Move > Review |website=StreetBrand.com |access-date=August 7, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519081826/http://www.streetbrand.com/200908071038/culture/leeland-love-is-on-the-move-christian-music-review.html |archive-date=May 19, 2010 }}</ref>
