'''5 Seconds of Summer''', often shortened to '''5SOS''' (pronounced as '5 sos'), are <!-- DO ''not'' CHANGE to 'is'. 'Are' is correct Australian English grammar, which is what this article is written in. See WP:ENGVAR for more info. --> an Australian [[pop rock]] band from [[Sydney]], [[New South Wales]], formed in late 2011. The group consists of lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist [[Luke Hemmings]], lead guitarist [[Michael Clifford (musician)|Michael Clifford]], bassist [[Calum Hood]], and drummer [[Ashton Irwin]]. Originally beginning their career as [[YouTube celebrities]], they rose to international fame while touring with English-Irish boy band [[One Direction]] on their [[Take Me Home Tour (One Direction)|Take Me Home Tour]]. Since 2014, 5 Seconds of Summer have sold more than 10 million albums, sold over 2 million concert tickets worldwide, and the band's songs streams surpass 7 billion, making them one of the most successful Australian musical exports in history.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-17|title=Australia's Annual Music Exports Worth $137M: Report|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/8520286/australia-annual-music-exports-report|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Why 5SOS are finally feeling the love in Australia|url=https://www.sunshinecoastdaily.com.au/news/why-5sos-are-finally-feeling-the-love-in-australia/3769636/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Sunshine Coast Daily|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=5 SECONDS OF SUMMER: Umusic|url=http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/5-seconds-of-summer-announce-new-album-calm-out-march-27/|access-date=2020-07-27|website=Umusic|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-31|title=5 Seconds of Summer|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/live/8549774/5-seconds-summer-north-american-no-shame-tour-dates|access-date=2020-07-27|website=Billboard}}</ref>

In early 2014, the band released "[[She Looks So Perfect]]" which topped the charts in four countries. Their [[5 Seconds of Summer (album)|self-titled debut album]] was released in 2014, peaking at number one in 11 countries. The band released their second album ''[[Sounds Good Feels Good]]'' in 2015, topping the charts in 8 countries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/the_10_best_5_seconds_of_summer_b_sides/|title=THE 10 BEST 5 SECONDS OF SUMMER B-SIDES|work=Alternative Press|date=7 February 2017|access-date=29 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180729171043/https://www.altpress.com/features/the_10_best_5_seconds_of_summer_b_sides/|archive-date=29 July 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> The band's third album ''[[Youngblood (5 Seconds of Summer album)|Youngblood]],'' released in 2018, was a commercial success and became their third number one album in their home country. In the US, 5 Seconds of Summer became the first Australian act to achieve three number one albums on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] album chart. They also became the first band (not vocal group) to have their first three full-length albums debut at the top in the US.<ref>{{cite ==History==

===2011–2014: Origin and debut===
[[File:5 Seconds of Summer Metro Theatre 25 Nov 2012.jpg|thumb|5 Seconds of Summer at their EP launch gig at [[The Metro Theatre]] on 25 November 2012]]
5 Seconds of Summer formed in late 2011 when [[Luke Hemmings]], [[Michael Clifford (musician)|Michael Clifford]], and [[Calum Hood]], who all attended the same high-school: Norwest Christian College, began posting videos of themselves performing covers of popular songs together on Hemmings' [[YouTube]] channel. In December 2011, the trio invited drummer [[Ashton Irwin]],<ref name=autogenerated2>{{cite news |title=5 Seconds of Summer Australian Tour |url=http://www.liveguide.com.au/Tours/768360/5_Seconds_of_Summer_Australian_Tour |date=May 2012 |access-date=10 August 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120801001305/http://www.liveguide.com.au/Tours/768360/5_Seconds_of_Summer_Australian_Tour |archive-date=1 August 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and the four-man lineup was completed.<ref>{{cite news |title=the Au interview - 5 Seconds of Summer |url=http://www.theaureview.com/interviews/the-au-interview-5-seconds-of-summer-sydney |date=1 August 2011 |access-date=19 February 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160326115920/http://www.theaureview.com/interviews/the-au-interview-5-seconds-of-summer-sydney |archive-date=26 March 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The band's name was created by Clifford.<ref>7 July 2019

As their fame and dedication to the band grew, the band members eventually dropped out of school, with Clifford having left school two years prior in Year 10 and later completing a year at [[TAFE]]. Hood dropped out in Year 11 in 2012 due to the band's move to London. Irwin had already graduated from [[Richmond High School (Richmond, New South Wales)|Richmond High School]] in 2012, and studied music performance at a TAFE college, before choosing to leave to pursue the band. Hemmings continued with [[Distance learning|distance education]] up until 2013, dropping out in the final year of secondary schooling: year 12, due to the commitment of the band.<ref name=":32">{{cite web|title=5SOS on leaving school to pursue music: 'We had to prove to our parents it was worth it'|url=https://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/londonnewsdesk/view/108848/?isworld=y|website=Pressparty|access-date=2020-05-22}}</ref><ref name=":42">{{cite web|title=The songs may change but the story of a hit remains the same|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/the-songs-may-change-but-the-story-of-a-hit-remains-the-same-20130309-2fsb2.html|last=Creswell|first=Toby|date=2013-03-09|website=The Sydney Morning Herald|access-date=2020-05-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172433/http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/the-songs-may-change-but-the-story-of-a-hit-remains-the-same-20130309-2fsb2.html|archive-date=21 October 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2014: ''5 Seconds of Summer'' and ''LIVESOS''===
In late March 2014, the band's single "She Looks So Perfect" was released in UK. Despite spending only one day on top in the British [[iTunes store]] and ending its week in the lower end of the top 10, 5 Seconds of Summer became the fourth Australian band to have a UK number-one single, and the first to do so in 14 years.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-26813060 |title=5SOS triumph in UK singles charts |date=30 March 2014 |access-date=30 March 2014 |work=BBC News |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140330202111/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-26813060 |archive-date=30 March 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 9 April 2014, their third EP ''She Looks So Perfect'' debuted at number 2 on the [[Billboard 200]] Chart.<ref>{{cite news |title=5 Seconds of Summer Debuts at No 2 in USA |last=Cashmere |first=Paul |url=http://www.noise11.com/news/5-seconds-of-summer-debuts-at-no-2-in-usa-20140409 |date=9 April 2014 |access-date=10 April 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140413124825/http://www.noise11.com/news/5-seconds-of-summer-debuts-at-no-2-in-usa-20140409 |archive-date=13 April 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 9 May 2014, they released their second single, "[[Don't Stop (5 Seconds of Summer song)|Don't Stop]]". ===2015–2016: ''Sounds Good Feels Good''===
In May 2015, the band embarked on their first headlining world tour, the [[Rock Out With Your Socks Out Tour]], taking place across arenas in Europe, Australia, New Zealand and North America.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|title='ROCK OUT WITH YOUR SOCKS OUT' 2015 UK & EUROPEAN TOUR !|url=http://www.5sos.com/rock-socks-2015-uk-european-tour/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141106041233/http://www.5sos.com/rock-socks-2015-uk-european-tour/|url-status=dead|archive-date=6 November 2014|website=5sos.com|access-date=10 July 2014|date=7 July 2014}}</ref><ref name="auto1">{{cite web |url=http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/92087205293/ |title=5 SECONDS OF SUMMER |publisher=5sos-official.tumblr.com |date=17 July 2014 |access-date=4 August 2014 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140806191159/http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/92087205293/ |archive-date=6 August 2014 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref name="auto2">{{cite web |url=http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/93313551973/ |title=5 SECONDS OF SUMMER |publisher=5sos-official.tumblr.com |date=30 July 2014 |access-date=4 August 2014 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140806191158/http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/93313551973/ |archive-date=6 August 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 17 July 2015, the band released "[[She's Kinda Hot]]" as the first single from their second studio album. On 12 August, they announced that their second studio album was titled ''[[Sounds Good Feels Good]]''. On 28 August, they released their seventh EP, ''She's Kinda Hot'', in the UK and Ireland only.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sugarscape.com/music/news/a1080593/5sos-shes-kinda-hot-ep/|title=5SOS announce 'She's Kinda Hot' EP with two brand new songs|work=Sugarscape|access-date=22 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150910162323/http://www.sugarscape.com/music/news/a1080593/5sos-shes-kinda-hot-ep/|archive-date=10 September 2015|url-status=live}}</ref> On 9 October, they released "[[Hey Everybody!]]" as the second single and announced their [[Sounds Live Feels Live World Tour]]. ''Sounds Good Feels Good''  was released worldwide on 23 October 2015. It became the band's second number one in their home country and the first in the United Kingdom. In the United States, 5 Seconds of Summer became the first band (not vocal) to have their first two full-length albums debut at the top of the charts. The band released their third single "[[Jet Black Heart]]" on 17 December 2015, together with a music video which featured some of their fans. In 2016, the band embarked on their Sounds Live Feels Live World Tour which included sold-out stadiums and arenas across North America, Europe, Australia, and Asia.

On 3 June 2016, the band announced their single "[[Girls Talk Boys]]". The song was included on the ''[[Ghostbusters (2016 film)|Ghostbusters (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)]]'' and was released on 15 July.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.5sos.com/ghostbusters-girls-talk-boys/|title=GHOSTBUSTERS // GIRLS TALK BOYS - 5 Seconds of Summer|work=5sos.com|access-date=15 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161116001812/http://www.5sos.com/ghostbusters-girls-talk-boys/|archive-date=16 November 2016|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> On 2 December 2016, the band announced the release of their B-sides and rarities, titled This Is Everything We Ever Said, to celebrate their fifth anniversary as a band.

After years of intense touring and the completion of the successful Sounds Live Feels Live World Tour in October 2016, the band announced that "2016 for 5SOS [had] ended," and that they would be taking the remainder of the year to rest and develop the sound for their next studio album.<ref>{{cite web|title=5SOS Are Taking a Break After Never-Ending Tour|url=https://fuse.tv/2016/10/5-seconds-of-summer-break|last=Break|first=Sounds Break Feels|website=Fuse|access-date=2020-05-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190301143127/https://www.fuse.tv/2016/10/5-seconds-of-summer-break|archive-date=1 March 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The break lasted nearly two years, with Hood later stating "After six years of being on the road, we needed to get our heads right for the longevity of the band."<ref name=":11">{{cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer on Why They Needed That Long Break|url=https://z100.iheart.com/content/2018-02-27-5-seconds-of-summer-on-why-they-needed-that-long-break/|website=Z100|access-date=2020-05-22}}</ref> Hemmings later revealed that the band had briefly considered breaking up due to their intense schedules, stating: "'Seven years as a band may seem like a long time, but we were teenagers when we started." He further went on to say: "We had to look at each other and say 'are we going to stop now, or do this forever? Because if we continue, then we have to go further than we ever have before.'"<ref name=":11" /> Ultimately the band chose to continue to release new music and explore new musical directions.<ref>{{Citation|title=5 Seconds of Summer on Why They Took an 'Extended Break'|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bfasSnoUXPI| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/bfasSnoUXPI| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live|access-date=2020-05-22}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

5 Seconds of Summer has been labelled a [[boy band]] by the media.<ref name="RollingStone20142">{{cite journal|last=Knopper|first=Steve|date=9 April 2014|title=On the Charts: Aussie Boy Band 5 Seconds of Summer Almost Top 'Frozen'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/on-the-charts-aussie-boy-band-5-seconds-of-summer-almost-top-frozen-20140409|journal=Rolling Stone|access-date=23 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171015203613/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/on-the-charts-aussie-boy-band-5-seconds-of-summer-almost-top-frozen-20140409|archive-date=15 October 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> Largely due to their past affiliation with English-Irish boy band [[One Direction]] and previously having a mostly female audience.<ref>{{cite news|last=Lewak|first=Doree|date=22 April 2014|title=5 Seconds of Summer uber-fans storm NYC in boy band mania|work=New York Post|url=https://nypost.com/2014/04/22/5-seconds-of-summer-uber-fans-storm-nyc-in-boy-band-mania/|access-date=9 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170517183429/http://nypost.com/2014/04/22/5-seconds-of-summer-uber-fans-storm-nyc-in-boy-band-mania/|archive-date=17 May 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> Members of the group have long rebutted claims that they are a boy band,<ref>{{cite journal|last=Smirke|first=Richard|date=8 April 2014|title=5 Seconds of Summer Goes Global - But They're Not The Next One Direction|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6042193/5-seconds-of-summer-goes-global-but-theyre-not-the-next-one|journal=Billboard|access-date=25 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140410211954/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6042193/5-seconds-of-summer-goes-global-but-theyre-not-the-next-one|archive-date=10 April 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> with Irwin, in the past, comparing the band's fanbase to [[Fall Out Boy]], who also boast a large female fanbase. [[John Feldmann]], frontman of [[Goldfinger (band)|Goldfinger]], attributed 5 Seconds of Summer's fanbase to "a fundamental change in the pop punk demo[graphic]", mentioning its gradual shift away from the majorly-male audiences of early 1990s pop punk acts, such as [[Blink-182]] and [[Green Day]].<ref>{{cite journal|last=Shultz|first=Cara Lynn|date=4 August 2014|title=5 Seconds of Summer: 'If Anyone Puts Us Down, We Don't Care'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6205128/5-seconds-of-summer-if-anyone-puts-us-down-we-dont-care|journal=Billboard|access-date=4 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140805025632/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6205128/5-seconds-of-summer-if-anyone-puts-us-down-we-dont-care|archive-date=5 August 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> Hemmings previously described the band as being a pop rock band with a boyband fanbase.<ref name="billboard2">{{cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer Want You Back: 'Our Purpose Is Very Different Than Other Bands'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8462361/5-seconds-of-summer-interview-youngblood-2018|last=Payne|first=Chris|work=Billboard|access-date=6 December 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181206222800/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8462361/5-seconds-of-summer-interview-youngblood-2018|archive-date=6 December 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> In a 2018 interview with [[Vice (magazine)|''Vice'']], in response to a question about the band's thoughts on being mistakenly referred to as a boy band, Irwin responded:{{quote|"Some of the bands we look up to have been amazing vocalists but I think the term "boyband" embodies youth. And when we're not a youthful band anymore, that's when we'll have problems. Labels are for people who are scared of themselves, and we're not".<ref name="I Took Every Member of 5 Seconds of Summer on a Date to Nando's">{{cite web|title=Even in An Ever-Changing Pop Scene, 5 Seconds Of Summer Are On A Mission To Stand Out|url=https://www.vice.com/en_ca/article/ywx57y/5-seconds-summer-5sos-first-date-interview-2018|last=Jones|first=Daisy|date=2018-04-16|website=Vice|access-date=2020-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191214071917/https://www.vice.com/en_ca/article/ywx57y/5-seconds-summer-5sos-first-date-interview-2018|archive-date=14 December 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>|Ashton Irwin of 5 Seconds Of Summer|title=|source=}}

The band's third studio album, ''Youngblood'', cited inspiration from the likes of [[Gorillaz]], [[The Beatles]], and Queen.<ref name="billboard2" /><ref>{{cite web|title=5 SECONDS OF SUMMER HONOR FREDDIE MERCURY WITH 'KILLER QUEEN' COVER|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/5-seconds-of-summer-killer-queen-cover/|last=Casteel|first=Beth|work=Alternative Press|access-date=6 December 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181105012231/http://www.altpress.com/news/5-seconds-of-summer-killer-queen-cover/|archive-date=5 November 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Hemmings described the title track single of the album as the lifestyle the band were living at the time of writing the song and further referred to the Youngblood 'persona' as dynamic waves of  "give and take".<ref>{{cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer Breaks Down "Youngblood" On Genius' Series 'Verified'|url=https://genius.com/videos/5-seconds-of-summer-breaks-down-youngblood|website=Genius|access-date=2019-07-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190702003353/https://genius.com/videos/5-seconds-of-summer-breaks-down-youngblood|archive-date=2 July 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The distinct musical differences from their previous albums to ''Youngblood'' was credited to the band's desire to move out of their sonic comfort zone of pop punk. Clifford later stated that in order for the band to feel satisfied as artists, they had to push themselves "to make something new" and that the unpredictability that resulted was "the best thing" for the band.<ref>{{cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer Want You Back: 'Our Purpose Is Very Different Than Other Bands'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8462361/5-seconds-of-summer-interview-youngblood-2018|last=Payne|first=Chris|date=23 June 2018|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-07-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181206222800/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8462361/5-seconds-of-summer-interview-youngblood-2018|archive-date=6 December 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

For their fourth studio album, ''Calm,'' the band stated that there was an intentionally "darker sound" and that they were "inspired by dark, synth-heavy groups of the Eighties and Nineties" including [[Depeche Mode]], [[Tears for Fears]], and [[Nine Inch Nails]]' song "[[Closer (Nine Inch Nails song)|Closer]]".<ref name="RS42">{{cite web|title=Watch 5 Seconds of Summer's Gloomy Video for Nine Inch Nails-Inspired 'Easier'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/5-seconds-of-summer-easier-interview-new-song-837008/amp/|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=23 May 2019|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=7 June 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190523133135/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/5-seconds-of-summer-easier-interview-new-song-837008/amp/|archive-date=23 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> They also stated that the direction of the album had been influenced by their listening to [[Gesaffelstein]], [[Health (band)|Health]], [[St. Vincent (musician)|St. Vincent]], [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]] and [[Bob Moses (band)|Bob Moses]].<ref name="RS42" /> The band's lead single for their fourth album "Easier" was inspired by Nine Inch Nails' song "Closer with the band citing inspiration to the "derivative of the driving drum groove".<ref>{{cite web|title=Why Trent Reznor of Nine Inch Nails Is Having a Pop Moment in 2019|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8515031/trent-reznor-reznorssance-nine-inch-nails-pop-influence-2019|date=2019-06-10|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200513214522/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8515031/trent-reznor-reznorssance-nine-inch-nails-pop-influence-2019|archive-date=13 May 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> The second single off the fourth album, "Teeth" featured Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine, who provided a guitar riff solo on the outro of the track.<ref name="nme.com2"/>

On the band's diverse and ever-changing musical style change, Hemmings has stated:{{quote|"I think all artists change their sound because they're not satisfied in the art they're making. We started out, very angsty, trying to get out of the hometown, and people called us a boy band, but we played pop punk music, it was written like that. As you get older, your music taste becomes more diverse and you get a bit smarter about it. You just bring in those kind of elements and you want to remain happy in what you're making".<ref name="Nash"/>|Luke Hemmings of 5 Seconds of Summer|title=|source=}}

In one of their first interviews as a band, 5 Seconds of Summer cited American rock band All Time Low as one of their biggest inspirations and dream collaboration.<ref name="auto3">{{cite web|date=2012-05-14|title=Meet 5 Seconds Of Summer, Your Hosts For This Evenings Entertainment|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/features/meet-5-seconds-of-summer-your-hosts-for-this-evenings-entertainment/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180923212331/http://musicfeeds.com.au/features/meet-5-seconds-of-summer-your-hosts-for-this-evenings-entertainment/|archive-date=23 September 2018|access-date=2020-05-24|website=Music Feeds}}</ref> In 2020, it was announced that on the 5 Seconds of Summer's headlining No Shame Tour, All Time Low would be the opening act for the European leg of the tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=5 Seconds Of Summer Announce Coin and All Time Low As Supports For No Shame Tour - Stereoboard|url=https://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/227474/9|last=Stickler|first=Jon|website=Stereoboard.com|access-date=2020-05-24}}</ref>

=== Net worth ===
5 Seconds of Summer have established two known and confirmed companies, both London-based. The initial 5 SECONDS OF SUMMER LLP was created in January 2013, followed by the 5SOS LLP which was established in June 2014. The band also has multiple known [[Australia]]n touring and business companies.<ref>{{cite web|title=5SOS LLP - Overview (free company information from Companies House)|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/OC393608|publisher=Companies House, Government of the United Kingdom|access-date=2020-05-23}}</ref>

The secondary company, 5SOS LLP was established in association with company ONE MODE PRODUCTIONS LLP, with One Direction members, [[Liam Payne]] and [[Louis Tomlinson]], signed as directors to the latter.<ref>{{cite web|title=5SOS LLP - Officers (free information from Companies House)|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/OC393608/officers|publisher=Companies House, Government of the United Kingdom|access-date=2020-05-23}}</ref>  In late 2014, ''Billboard Magazine'' revealed that all five One Direction members: [[Niall Horan]], [[Harry Styles]], Liam Payne, [[Zayn Malik]] and Louis Tomlinson, collectively held a 20% stake in the 5SOS LLP.<ref>{{cite web|title=One Direction's Investment in 5 Seconds of Summer|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6273836/one-direction-secret-investment-5-seconds-summer|date=2014-10-03|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200423233700/https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6273836/one-direction-secret-investment-5-seconds-summer|archive-date=23 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> It was also reported that the 20% stakes were further split evenly between the members of One Direction and London-based Modest Management, with 5 Seconds of Summer owning the remainder 80% of the company's shares.<ref>{{cite web|title=One Direction's Secret Financial Investment In Aussie Band|url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/one-directions-secret-financial-investment-aussie-band/|date=2014-10-06|website=Tone Deaf|access-date=2020-05-23}}</ref> It was estimated that within the span of three months, the members of One Direction had earned $25,000 each, based on 5 Seconds of Summer's music sales alone.<ref>{{cite web|title=Are 5SOS Making One Direction Rich? (Spoiler: The Answer Is Yes)|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1952765/one-direction-5sos-financial-investment/|last=Walker|first=John|publisher=MTV News|access-date=2020-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160713092019/http://www.mtv.com/news/1952765/one-direction-5sos-financial-investment/|archive-date=13 July 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

In January 2015, it was revealed that both Payne and Tomlinson had resigned as directors of One Mode Production.<ref>{{cite web|title=ONE MODE PRODUCTIONS LIMITED - Officers (free information from Companies House)|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/08217709/officers|publisher=Companies House, Government of the United Kingdom|access-date=2020-05-23}}</ref>

On 27 January 2014, the band announced that they would be setting up their own record label, Hi or Hey Records.<ref>[http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/74762309071/so-excited-to-announce-the-record-label-were "5SOS Tumblr"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150418091347/http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/74762309071/so-excited-to-announce-the-record-label-were |date=18 April 2015 }} (27 January 2014). Retrieved 10 April 2015.</ref> The record label's name was chosen via a fan voting system on Twitter.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-04-18|title=5 SECONDS OF SUMMER|url=http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/74222293315|access-date=2020-07-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150418091533/http://5sos-official.tumblr.com/post/74222293315|archive-date=18 April 2015}}</ref> On the label's website, the band stated that aside from releasing all of their music through Hi or Hey Records in conjunction with Capitol Records, they would also be signing bands via the label.<ref>[http://www.hiorheyrecords.com/about "Hi or Hey Records website"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402145540/http://www.hiorheyrecords.com/about |date=2 April 2015 }} Retrieved 10 April 2015.</ref> On 24 March 2015, the label signed pop rock band [[Hey Violet]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://popcrush.com/5-seconds-of-summer-hey-violet-record-label-video/|title=5 Seconds of Summer Sign Hey Violet to Record Label [VIDEO]|website=PopCrush|access-date=2016-09-30|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160601192429/http://popcrush.com/5-seconds-of-summer-hey-violet-record-label-video/|archive-date=1 June 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

5 Seconds of Summer's first three studio albums: ''5 Seconds of Summer'' (2014), ''Sounds Good Feels Good'' (2015), and ''Youngblood'' (2018) were all released through the Hi or Hey Records imprint.<ref>{{Cite web|title=5SOS/5 SECONDS OF SUMMER: CAPITOL RECORDS|url=https://www.capitolrecords.com/5-seconds-summer-returns-new-single/|access-date=2020-07-29|website=www.capitolrecords.com}}</ref>

In May 2019, it was announced that 5 Seconds of Summer had departed from Hi or Hey Records/Capitol Records and signed with current record label, Interscope Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.interscope.com/artists/5-seconds-summer|title=5 Seconds of Summer {{!}} Interscope Records|website=[[Interscope Records]]|date=20 May 2019|location=United States|access-date=21 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190621071928/https://www.interscope.com/artists/5-seconds-summer|archive-date=21 June 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=HITS Daily Double : Rumor Mill - SECONDS OF JOY|url=https://hitsdailydouble.com/news&id=316427&title+SECONDS-OF-JOY|access-date=2020-07-29|website=HITS Daily Double|language=en}}</ref>

As of 2020, Hi or Hey Records remains active as all of Hey Violet's [[Hey Violet discography|recordings]] are released via Hi or Hey Records in conjunction with Capitol.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hi or Hey Records/Capitol: Hey Violet|url=https://www.capitolrecords.com/hey-violet-release-new-single-better-by-myself/|access-date=2020-07-22|website=www.capitolrecords.com}}</ref>

==Philanthropy and supported causes==

In October 2018, prior to the scheduled premiere of Queen's biopic,"[[Bohemian Rhapsody (film)|Bohemian Rhapsody]]", 5 Seconds of Summer released a cover of British rock band Queen song, "[[Killer Queen]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8481847/5-seconds-of-summer-killer-queen-cover|title=5 Seconds Of Summer Pay Homage to Freddie Mercury With 'Killer Queen' Cover|website=Billboard|date=26 October 2018|access-date=8 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190402203522/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8481847/5-seconds-of-summer-killer-queen-cover|archive-date=2 April 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>  All proceeds from the cover were presented to the Mercury Phoenix Trust, an organization founded by Queen's band members aiming to provide support against HIV/AIDS. In response, Queen's manager, [[Jim Beach]], expressed his gratitude and excitement towards the band for supporting the cause.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ew.com/music/2018/10/26/5-seconds-summer-covers-killer-queen-honor-bohemian-rhapsody/|title=5 Seconds of Summer covers 'Killer Queen' in honor of Bohemian Rhapsody|access-date=26 October 2018|first=Maureen Lee|last=Lenker|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181026161816/https://ew.com/music/2018/10/26/5-seconds-summer-covers-killer-queen-honor-bohemian-rhapsody/|archive-date=26 October 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

In December 2018, the band appeared on ''[[The Climate Reality Project|24 Hours of Reality: Protect Our Planet, Protect Ourselves]]'', a two-day web-telecast organized by ''The Climate Reality Project'', an organization led by former US Vice President [[Al Gore]], to help raise awareness about the effects of climate change.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.climaterealityproject.org/blog/see-who-joining-us-24-hours-reality|title=SEE WHO'S JOINING US FOR 24 HOURS OF REALITY|work=The Climate Reality Project|date=30 November 2018|access-date=6 December 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181206235254/https://www.climaterealityproject.org/blog/see-who-joining-us-24-hours-reality|archive-date=6 December 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

In a June 2019, 5 Seconds of Summer starred in a season 6 episode of [[Celebrity Family Feud]], a televised game show in which celebrity teams compete against each other for a $50,000 top prize to be donated to the charity of the winning team's choice. The band competed against The Chainsmokers for the [[Make-A-Wish Foundation]], an organization granting the wishes of children with life-threatening illnesses.<ref>{{Citation|last=FamilyFeud|title=Steve Harvey meets The Chainsmokers and 5SOS! {{!}} Celebrity Family Feud|date=2019-06-30|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xEW2dwJSTuU|access-date=2019-07-02}}</ref>''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://america.wish.org/|title=Make-A-Wish America|website=Make-A-Wish America|access-date=1 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190701173840/https://america.wish.org/|archive-date=1 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>''

In February 2020, the band performed at Fire Fight Australia, a televised fundraising concert for Australian Bush-fire Relief held at the ANZ Stadium. The concert grossed a total of $9.85 million (AUD).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://7news.com.au/entertainment/music/fire-fight-australia-concert-raises-almost-10-million-for-bushfire-relief-c-699673|title=Staggering amount of money raised through Fire Fight concert revealed|date=2020-02-17|website=7NEWS.com.au|access-date=2020-03-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200316131326/https://7news.com.au/entertainment/music/fire-fight-australia-concert-raises-almost-10-million-for-bushfire-relief-c-699673|archive-date=16 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> The band's performance was widely praised both by critics and viewers and dubbed the "best performance" of the show,<ref name=":4" /> with ''Billboard'' writing "They were raised playing stadiums and as such were, perhaps surprisingly, the perfect warm-up act for Queen -- though such a term diminishes the impact they had in fifteen minutes".<ref name=":5" /> In March 2020, a compilation album titled ''[[Artists Unite for Fire Fight]]'' was released, consisting of performances from the concert. The album includes 5 Seconds of Summer's live performance of "Youngblood."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/fire-fight-australia-releases-compilation-cd-to-raise-more-funds-for-bushfire-relief/|title=Fire Fight Australia Releases Compilation CD To Raise More Funds For Bushfire Relief|date=2020-03-13|website=Music Feeds|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref>

=== Friends of Friends ===
On 13 May 2019, the band announced the creation of their [[Philanthropy|philanthropic]] brand, '''Friends of Friends''', with the release of a limited-edition clothing collection.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wearefof.com/|title=WeAreFoF – Friends of Friends|access-date=2019-07-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190620163541/https://wearefof.com/|archive-date=20 June 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://shop.wearefof.com/|title=Friends of Friends Merch|website=Friends of Friends Merch|access-date=2019-07-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190516180947/https://shop.wearefof.com/|archive-date=16 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> All profits from the first initiative were donated to Safe Place for Youth, a project that focuses on helping and empowering homeless youth in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.safeplaceforyouth.org/about|title=About us - Safe Place for Youth|website=SPY|access-date=2019-07-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190702003354/http://www.safeplaceforyouth.org/about|archive-date=2 July 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> On 21 May 2019, the band announced they would be hosting their first benefit concert under the Friends of Friends name. On 29 May 2019, the first-ever Friends of Friends show was held in a sold-out venue in [[Venice, Los Angeles|Venice, California]], with 5 Seconds of Summer as featured headliners and All Time Low, [[Sierra Deaton]], among various other artists as supporting acts. All proceeds from the event were donated to the Safe Place for Youth project.<ref>{{cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer Presents FRIENDS OF FRIENDS|url=https://www.eventbrite.com/e/62151880949?aff=efbneb|website=Eventbrite|access-date=2020-05-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=All Time Low Are Playing 5 Seconds Of Summer's Charity Show|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/all-time-low-are-playing-5-seconds-of-summers-charity-show|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=2020-05-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190526215145/https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/all-time-low-are-playing-5-seconds-of-summers-charity-show|archive-date=26 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 26 June 2019, Friends of Friends released another limited edition clothing range with the announcement of their second benefit concert. This time taking place in Sydney, Australia on 3 July 2019, the sold-out concert featured 5 Seconds of Summer as headliners, with [[Amy Shark]], [[Genesis Owusu]], and several other artists as support acts.<ref>{{Cite web|title=/|url=https://themusic.com.au/news/5-seconds-of-summer-and-amy-shark-to-play-special-benefit-gig-in-sydney-next-week/DS8VAQADAgU/27-06-19/|access-date=2020-06-10|website=The Music|language=en}}</ref> All proceeds from the show and the clothing range were donated to Blacktown Youth Services Association, an Australian organization that works to inspire young people to positively impact their community.<ref>{{cite web|title=Blacktown Youth Services Association|url=http://www.bysa.org.au/|website=bysa.org.au|access-date=2019-07-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190702003359/http://www.bysa.org.au/|archive-date=2 July 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 23 January 2020, in response to the [[2019–20 Australian bushfire season|2019-20 Australian bushfires]], the band stated that they were "devastated by the tragic fires that [had] affected so many [...] in Australia," and wanted "to contribute as best [they could] to relief efforts". The band later announced the release of a limited-edition clothing range designed by artist, Micah Ulrich. All proceeds from the clothing collection were donated to the [[Australian Red Cross]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Friends|first=Friends of|date=2020-01-22|title=To aid recovery from the Australia wildfires, we've released a limited merch range, featuring designs from our friend @micahulrich. All profits will benefit the Australian Red Cross. Thank you for continuing to spread awareness and calling for action|url=https://twitter.com/wearefof/status/1220130297460723713|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200122235029/https://twitter.com/wearefof/status/1220130297460723713|archive-date=22 January 2020|access-date=2020-03-22|website=@wearefof}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=COLLABORATIONS {{!}} Micah Ulrich|url=https://www.micahulrichart.com/collaborations|access-date=2020-06-10|website=Mysite 1|language=en}}</ref>

On 27 July 2020, amid the [[George Floyd protests|George Floyd Protests]], the band collaborated with [[Haitian Americans|Haitian-American]] [[transgender]] artist Sanyu Nicolas to release a charitable clothing collection with all proceeds being donated to [[Marsha P. Johnson|The Marsha P. Johnson Institute]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=5SOS on Twitter: "Black Trans Lives Matter. We stand with the Black transgender community in their fight for equality, recognition, and justice."|url=https://twitter.com/5sos/status/1287856755112906754|access-date=2020-07-27|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> Along with the launch of the clothing collection the band stated that they "stand with the Black transgender community in their fight for equality, recognition, and justice".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-27|title=5SOS team with Sanyu Tattoo, releasing merch to benefit Black Trans Lives Matter|url=https://dontboreus.thebrag.com/5sos-sanyu-tattoo-black-trans-lives-matter/|access-date=2020-07-28|website=Don't Bore Us|language=en-AU}}</ref> The band also added additional resources to the Friends of Friends website and the brand's official newsletter, which detailed the [[Black Lives Matter|Black Lives Matter Movement]] and [[LGBT social movements|LGBT Movement]] with petitions related to the movements.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-28|title=This 5SOS Merch Benefits the Marsha P. Johnson Institute|url=https://www.papermag.com/5sos-marsha-p-johnson-2646818834.html?rebelltitem=1#rebelltitem1?rebelltitem=1|access-date=2020-07-29|website=PAPER|language=en}}</ref>

For the [[2020 United States presidential election|2020 American presidential election]], Friends of Friends released a clothing collection, accompanied by an appeal urging people to vote, stating, "You've been given the privilege to use your voice; now it is your job to show up to the polls." All proceeds from the collection were donated to Vote Save America, to "benefit [their] efforts to ensure fair & equal access to the polls."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Friends Of Friends on Instagram: "Please vote. ==Awards==
{{main|List of awards and nominations received by 5 Seconds of Summer}}

5 Seconds of Summer have received [[List of awards and nominations received by 5 Seconds of Summer|numerous accolades and awards]] over the course of the band's active career including ten [[MTV Europe Music Award|MTV European Music Awards]], five ARIA Awards, three [[APRA Music Awards of 2019|APRA Awards]], three [[BMI Awards]] and two [[MTV Video Music Award]]s. In 2019, the band was awarded the prestigious [[APRA Awards (Australia)|APRA Music Awards]]' ''Outstanding International Achievement Award'' for their excellence and outstanding worldwide success in sales and airplay performance.<ref>{{cite web|title='Youngblood' wins 'Outstanding International Achievement'award at the APRA Music Awards|url=https://reservoir-media.com/5047-2/|date=2019-04-30|website=Reservoir Media|access-date=2020-05-23}}</ref> In 2020, 5 Seconds of Summer were awarded and honored in the APRA AMCOS' ''The 1,000,000,000 List'', which recognizes excellence among Australian songwriting artists who have achieved massive international success. The band was awarded for their single, "Youngblood", reaching over a billion streams. There are currently only 16 artists in history who have been awarded a position on the list.<ref name="AMCOS">{{cite web|last=AMCOS|first=APRA|title=5 Seconds of Summer joins The 1,000,000,000 List for 'Youngblood'|url=http://apraamcos.com.au/awards/the-1-000-000-000-list/5sos-youngblood/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200328154347/https://apraamcos.com.au/awards/the-1-000-000-000-list/5sos-youngblood/|archive-date=28 March 2020|access-date=2020-05-23|website=apraamcos.com.au}}</ref><ref name="au.rollingstone.com">{{cite web|date=2020-02-19|title=5 Seconds of Summer Join APRA AMCOS' 1,00,000,000 List for "Youngblood"|url=https://au.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/5-seconds-of-summer-apra-amcos-billion-list-youngblood-7686/|access-date=2020-05-23|website=Rolling Stone Australia}}</ref>
