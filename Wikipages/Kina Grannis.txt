'''Kina Kasuya Grannis''' (born August 4, 1985) is a Japanese-American singer-songwriter, guitarist and [[Internet personalities|YouTuber]]. Grannis was the winner of the 2008 [[Doritos]] [[Crash the Super Bowl]] contest,<ref>{{cite web | url=https://usatoday30.usatoday.com/money/advertising/adtrack/2008-02-10-ad-track_N.htm | title=Ad Track | publisher=USA Today | access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://dailytrojan.com/2013/03/31/singer-alumna-embraces-her-multicultural-roots/ | title=Singer, alumna embraces her multicultural roots | publisher=Daily Trojan | access-date=April 4, 2021}}</ref> earning a recording contract with [[Interscope Records]] and having her music video played during the commercials of [[Super Bowl XLII]] on February 3, 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.informationweek.com/blog/main/archives/2008/02/rating_the_supe.html |title=InformationWeek's ratings of Super Bowl Commercials Published 3 February 2008 |work=InformationWeek |access-date=November 29, 2010}}</ref>

==Early life and career beginnings==

Grannis grew up in [[Mission Viejo, California|Mission Viejo]], California. Grannis attended Viejo Elementary School, Newhart Middle School and, from 1999 to 2003, [[Capistrano Valley High School]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Chang |first=Richard |url=http://www.ocregister.com/articles/grannis-329155-video-album.html |title=O.C.'s Kina Grannis crashes the spotlight again |publisher=Ocregister.com |date=November 19, 2011 |access-date=November 12, 2016}}</ref> Grannis enrolled at the [[University of Southern California]] (USC) in Los Angeles in 2003. Two years later, staff members of the University's [[USC Thornton School of Music|Thornton School of Music]] asked her to produce an album in their music and music industry departments.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lee |first=Jen |url=http://dailytrojan.com/2010/02/22/alumna%E2%80%99s-new-album-an-homage-to-%E2%80%99sc// |title=Alumna's New Album an Homage to SC |publisher=Daily Trojan |date=February 22, 2010 |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> That album, ''Sincerely, Me.'', was released the same year. In 2007, she graduated ''[[summa cum laude]]'' in [[social science]] with an emphasis on [[psychology]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Paisley |first=Laura |url=https://dornsife.usc.edu/news/stories/2069/in-her-element-kina-grannis/ |title=In Her Element: Kina Grannis |publisher=dornsife.usc.edu |date=June 1, 2015 |access-date=August 28, 2017}}</ref> While attending USC, she was inducted into academic society [[Phi Beta Kappa]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dornsife.usc.edu/phi-beta-kappa-epsilon-inductees-by-year/ |title=2006 Inductees |publisher=dornsife.usc.edu |access-date=August 28, 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2006, Grannis recorded and self-released two more albums, ''One More in the Attic'' and ''In Memory of the Singing Bridge.'' In early 2007, she recorded "Ours to Keep" written by Rachael Lawrence and Deborah Ellen. This song would be featured regularly on ''[[General Hospital]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kinagrannis.com/blog/2007/09/24/general-hospital/ |title=bla(h)g entry, Sept. 24, 2007 |publisher=Kinagrannis.com |access-date=November 29, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120311200016/http://www.kinagrannis.com/blog/2007/09/24/general-hospital/ |archive-date=March 11, 2012 }}</ref> and was in an episode of the [[ABC Family]]'s mini-series ''[[Samurai Girl (TV miniseries)|Samurai Girl]]'' in September 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.kinagrannis.com/2008/09/08/ |title=bla(h)g entry, Sept. 8, 2008 |publisher=Kinagrannis.com |date=September 8, 2008 |access-date=November 29, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713152213/http://www.kinagrannis.com/2008/09/08/ |archive-date=July 13, 2011 }}</ref>

===World in Front of Me Tour (2011)===
In April 2011, Grannis launched her first world tour. She performed in the eastern regions of Canada and in the eastern and southern regions of the United States. She continued in May through the northwest region of North America which included stops in Vancouver, [[British Columbia]], and Los Angeles. Shows in Europe and southeast Asia followed into the fall of 2011.

===In Your Arms Tour (2011)===
In late 2011 and into 2012, Grannis launched her In Your Arms Tour in Europe, Asia (Indonesia, Singapore, Philippines, Malaysia, Hong Kong), Australia, and North America.

In March 2013, Grannis gave a talk at [[TEDx]] Hollywood<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ted.com/tedx/events/6620 |title=TEDxHollywood ‚Äì Entertaining and Connecting With Audiences &#124; TEDx |publisher=TED.com |access-date=May 10, 2014}}</ref> titled 'Finding Community Through the Internet'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cqg0HHp6W2U |title=Finding Community Through the Internet: Kina Grannis at TEDxHollywood |publisher=YouTube |date=April 27, 2013 |access-date=May 10, 2014}}</ref>

===''Elements'' (2014)===
''Elements'' was released on May 6, 2014,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/elements-deluxe-version/id849318884 |title=Elements |publisher=iTunes |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> and was produced by Matt Hales (a.k.a. Aqualung).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.discogs.com/Kina-Grannis-Elements/release/5909408 |title=Elements |publisher=Discogs |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> Starting in November 2013, Grannis began ramping up for the forthcoming album with the launch of "Kina Mondays", which promised a weekly video via Kina's YouTube channel.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JDbEgQdUsyM |title=Let Her Go |publisher=YouTube |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> Nearly six months later, she continued to deliver on that promise and released a video for "The Fire", the first single from ''Elements''. The song showcases acoustics and folk-driven vocals. The following week another album track was released via video with a stripped-down version of "My Own" featuring her sisters. "Dear River", an upbeat track, premiered on ''Glamour'''s "Obsessed Blog".  ''Elements'' debuted at No. 48 on the Billboard 200,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/305901/kina-grannis/chart |title=Kina Grannis Chart History |publisher=Billboard |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> No. 4 on the Billboard Americana/Folk chart,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/305901/kina-grannis/chart?f=1071 |title=Kina Grannis Chart History |publisher=Billboard |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> and No. 7 on the Billboard Independent Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/305901/kina-grannis/chart?f=326 |title=Kina Grannis Chart History |publisher=Billboard |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> On its release day, ''Elements'' hit No. 2 on iTunes Singer Songwriter Chart, and No. 1 on iTunes Canada.

Grannis announced that she would be celebrating the release of her sophomore studio album ''Elements'' with fans at a handful of intimate shows across North America, kicking off with a show at the [[Troubadour (West Hollywood, California)|Troubadour]] in Hollywood on May 6 ‚Äì ''Elements''{{'}} release date.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.troubadour.com/event/529447-kina-grannis-los-angeles/ |title=Kina Grannis at the Troubadour |publisher=Troubadour |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://rachelannc.com/2014/05/07/kina-grannis-let-the-fire-out-and-created-sentimental-space-in-the-troubadour-elements-album-release-and-kick-off-tour/ |title=Elements Album Release and Kick-Off Tour |publisher=Beauty Within |access-date=September 20, 2017}}</ref> From there, she performed in San Francisco, New York City, Brooklyn, DC, Boston, Toronto, and Chicago. The select dates were the first time fans could hear ''Elements'' live and also served as her first official headline run since the spring of 2012.

In late 2014 and 2015, Grannis launched her Elements tour in Europe and North America. She embarked on a tour of Southeast Asia, but was only able to play two shows before she and her band were detained by Indonesian authorities for having the wrong type of visas. Grannis and her crew were deported from the country after three months.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Harvey |first1=Adam |title=Kina Grannis: Indonesia warns foreigners over visas after US singer detained in Jakarta for 100 days |url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-05-26/kina-grannis-detained-in-jakarta-on-visa-fraud-charges/7446580 |publisher=ABC News |date=May 25, 2016}}</ref>

===Patreon===
Grannis joined Patreon, a site created by Jack Conte to provide artists a living via direct support, in April 2014.<ref name="Kina Grannis"/>

In April 2017, Grannis created a record label entirely composed of her patrons and began creating an album entirely funded by her supporters.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=kinagrannis |number=850375274118041600 |date = April 7, 2017 |author=Kina Grannis |title=YOU Are My Record Label}}</ref> On advice of these supporters, the record label was named KG Records. Grannis began recording, producing, and releasing songs from the upcoming album throughout 2017.

===''In the Waiting'' (2018)===
Her first major release under KG Records was the album ''In The Waiting'', on June 29, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/in-the-waiting/1401061648 |title=In The Waiting |publisher=iTunes |access-date=July 7, 2018}}</ref>

==Collaborations==
Grannis has been a part of several Wong Fu Productions short films including The Last, as well as the series Funemployed and Single by 30. She has also appeared in other YouTube stars' videos. One of the bands Grannis has performed with in YouTube videos is Boyce Avenue. ==Personal life==
Grannis has two sisters, Misa and Emi, both of whom have occasionally been featured in her [[video blog]]s, as well as accompanying her at concerts and on tour. Her father, Gordon, is a [[chiropractor]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grannischiropractic.com/ |title=Gordon H. Grannis Chiropractic Clinic, Inc |publisher=Grannischiropractic.com |access-date=November 29, 2010}}</ref> and her mother, Trish, is a [[graphic designer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dailytrojan.com/2013/03/31/singer-alumna-embraces-her-multicultural-roots/ |title=Singer, alumna embraces her multicultural roots |last=Kawatra|first=Isha|publisher=[[Daily Trojan]]|date=March 31, 2013 |access-date=August 18, 2018}}</ref>

On August 31, 2013, Grannis married frequent musical collaborator [[Jesse Epstein]]{{citation needed|date=January 2020}} in a small ceremony in Los Angeles, California, after they had dated for 11 years. They met while attending Capistrano Valley High School in 2002; Jesse was in the grade below Kina.<ref>{{cite web|last=Curio|first=Nicholas|url=http://dailybruin.com/2012/05/15/performing_as_imaginary_friend_ucla_alumnus_jesse_epstein_hits_the_road_with_singer_kina_grannis/|title=Performing as Imaginary Friend, UCLA alumnus Jesse Epstein hits the road with singer Kina Grannis|work=[[Daily Bruin]]|date=May 15, 2012}}</ref> Grannis used footage from her wedding to create the music video she released for her song "My Dear".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2014/09/09/kina-grannis-marks-wedding-anniversary-with-my-dear-video-exclusive-premiere/ |title=Kina Grannis Marks Wedding Anniversary With My Dear Video Exclusive Premiere |last=Danton |first=Eric R. |publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]] |date=September 9, 2014 |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> In June 2021, after years of struggling with infertility,<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=kinagrannis|last=Grannis|first=Kina|number=1284175183872356353|title=infertility is rough. like really rough. i first shared about my experience a year ago. since then it has become an even bigger part of my day-to-day existence, but at some point i stopped knowing how to talk about it and now it feels like i've been going through the world mute.|access-date=2021-06-04|date=July 17, 2020}}</ref> Grannis announced that she and her husband were expecting a daughter.<ref>{{cite instagram|user=kinagrannis|last=Grannis|first=Kina|postid=CPtJA40FKxU|title=wellllllll. it's happening üò≠üéâ what on earth.‚Å£ we've got a tiny human coming our way this october and we can't wait to meet her ‚ô•Ô∏è‚Å£|date=June 4, 2021|access-date=June 4, 2021}}</ref>

On September 14, 2015, Grannis and her band left Los Angeles heading to Jakarta, Indonesia. It would have been the first concert in a tour of Southeast Asia. Due to complications with her work visa, she was detained for 100 days in the country.<ref name="100 Days in Jakarta"/><ref name="The Straits Times"/><ref name="ABC News Australian"/> During this time she was told to not talk about her struggles or create any YouTube videos. While waiting each day for an update she was able to write two songs, ''California'' and ''For Now''.

===Philanthropy===
An avid supporter of cancer research, Grannis has performed at several Southern California [[Relay For Life]] functions over the years.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.kinagrannis.com/2007/04/relay-for-life/ |title=bla(h)g entry, Apr. 2, 2007 |publisher=Kinagrannis.com |date=April 2, 2007 |access-date=November 29, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713151745/http://www.kinagrannis.com/2007/04/relay-for-life/ |archive-date=July 13, 2011 }}</ref>

In late 2007, she contributed her music to the Band Together: To Fight Measles benefit album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://redcrossyouth.org/2008/01/07/using-new-technology-to-solve-old-problems/ |title=Using New Technology to Solve Old Problems |publisher=American Red Cross |date=January 7, 2008 |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.venturacountystar.com/news/2007/dec/23/local-musicians-album-funds-fight-on-measles/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090415073834/http://www.venturacountystar.com/news/2007/dec/23/local-musicians-album-funds-fight-on-measles/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=April 15, 2009 |title=Local musicians' album funds fight on measles |last=Volkmann |first=Michelle |publisher=[[Ventura County Star]] |date=December 23, 2007}}</ref>

In October 2008, she participated in the Nike Women's Marathon in San Francisco<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marathonguide.com/results/browse.cfm?MIDD=2224081019 |title=Nike Womens Marathon Race Results 2008 |publisher=Marathon Guide |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> to support the [[Leukemia & Lymphoma Society|Leukemia Lymphoma Society]] in honor of her mother,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://popdirt.com/kina-grannis-nike-womens-marathon-highlight-video/69689/ |title=Kina Grannis Nike Womens Marathon Highlight Video |publisher=popdirt |date=October 28, 2008 |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> raising $6,000 for the organization.

In 2009, the philanthropic organization Sister to Sisters adopted her song ''Message from Your Heart.''

In 2011, Grannis contributed her song ''Message from Your Heart'' to a benefit album<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/1N0GkhbtvugDEF3n2PUWdd |title=SXSW4JAPAN by Various Artists |publisher=spotify |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> released as part of a broader relief effort<ref>{{cite web|url=http://a2im.org/news/sxsw4japan-benefit-album-available-re-stream-of-live-performances-today/ |title=SXSW4JAPAN Benefit Album Available |publisher=American Association of Independent Music |date=April 5, 2011 |access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> for the [[2011 T≈çhoku earthquake and tsunami]].

In 2012, she launched a charity project called Run Team Kina dedicated to the promotion of personal health and wellness and to raise money for the [[Leukemia & Lymphoma Society|Leukemia Lymphoma Society]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.runteamkina.com/ |title=Run Team Kina |publisher=runteamkina.com |access-date=April 5, 2012}}</ref> In 2012, Run Team Kina raised $71,886 for The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society.
