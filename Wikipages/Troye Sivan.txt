'''Troye Sivan Mellet''' ({{IPAc-en|t|r|…î…™|_|s|·µª|Àà|v|…ëÀê|n}} {{respell|TROY|_|sih|VAHN}}; born 5 June 1995)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/troyesivan/status/739352032461524992 |title=troye sivan on Twitter: "IM 21" |website=[[Twitter]] |date=5 June 2016 |access-date=5 June 2018}}</ref> is an Australian singer-songwriter, actor and [[YouTuber]]. After gaining popularity as a singer on YouTube and in Australian talent competitions, Sivan signed with [[Universal Music Australia|EMI Australia]] in 2013 and released his third extended play, ''[[TRXYE]]'' (2014), which peaked at number five on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. Its lead single, "[[Happy Little Pill]]", reached number ten on Australian music charts. In 2015, he released his fourth extended play ''[[Wild (Troye Sivan EP)|Wild]]'' followed by his debut studio album ''[[Blue Neighbourhood]]'', whose lead single "[[Youth (Troye Sivan song)|Youth]]" became Sivan's first single to enter the top 40 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, peaking at number 23.<ref name="twitter.com">{{cite tweet |user=troyesivan |number=653975462452965376 |title=üíô BLUE NEIGHBOURHOOD ‚Äì PREORDER OCTOBER 15 ‚Äì OUT DECEMBER 4 üíô |date=14 October 2015 |access-date=28 May 2018 |last=Sivan |first=Troye}}</ref> His second studio album ''[[Bloom (Troye Sivan album)|Bloom]]'' (2018) reached number three in Australia, and number four on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart. Its lead single, "[[My My My! (Troye Sivan song)|My My My!]]", became Sivan's second number-one single on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Dance Club Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8457748/troye-sivan-my-my-my-no-1-dance-club-songs-chart |title=Troye Sivan's 'My My My' Moves to No. 1 on Dance Club Songs Chart |website=Billboard.com |date=24 May 2018 |access-date=2 September 2018}}</ref> "[[Take Yourself Home]]" served as the first single from his EP ''[[In a Dream (EP)|In a Dream]]'' (2020).<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=15 July 2020|title=Troye Sivan Kicks Off 'In A Dream' EP Rollout With Hypnotic New Single 'Easy': Listen|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/9418720/troye-sivan-announces-ep-easy|access-date=17 July 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

As an actor, Sivan portrayed the younger [[Logan (film series character)|Wolverine]] in ''[[X-Men Origins: Wolverine]]'' (2009) and starred as the title character in the ''[[Spud (film)|Spud]]'' film trilogy. As a YouTube personality, Sivan used to upload [[video blog]]s regularly and, as of 2021, has over 7.3{{nbsp}}million subscribers and over 1.3{{nbsp}}billion total views.<ref name="Social Blade">{{cite web|title=Troyesivan18 YouTube Stats, Channel Statistics|url=https://socialblade.com/youtube/user/troyesivan18|access-date=30 October 2019|work=Social Blade}}</ref> His video "The 'Boyfriend' Tag", with fellow vlogger [[Tyler Oakley]], earned them a [[2014 Teen Choice Awards#Choice Web|Teen Choice Award]] in the "Choice Web Collaboration" category. In 2014, ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' named Sivan as one of the "25 Most Influential Teens of 2014".<ref>{{cite news |title=The 25 Most Influential Teens of 2014 |url=http://time.com/3486048/most-influential-teens-2014/ |magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |date=13 October 2014 |access-date=3 January 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150102175933/http://time.com/3486048/most-influential-teens-2014/ |archive-date=2 January 2015 |url-status=live}}</ref> In 2018, he received a [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe]] nomination for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] for "[[Revelation (Troye Sivan and J√≥nsi song)|Revelation]]", from the film ''[[Boy Erased]]'', in which he also had a supporting acting role.

== Career ==
=== Music ===
Sivan's musical career started when he sang at the 2006, 2007 and 2008 [[Channel Seven Perth Telethon]].<ref name="PerthNow">{{cite news |last=Symonds |first=Kristy |title=Perth boy making it big in Hollywood |url=http://www.perthnow.com.au/entertainment/perth-boy-making-it-big-in-hollywood/story-e6frg30c-1226321060650 |access-date=15 January 2014 |newspaper=[[Perth Now]] |date=7 April 2012}}</ref><ref name="stm">{{cite news |last=Harvey |first=Shannon |title=Perth boy to play young Hugh Jackman in Wolverine movie |url=http://www.perthnow.com.au/news/perth-boy-to-play-young-hugh/story-e6frg12c-1111115680990 |access-date=15 January 2014 |newspaper=[[Perth Now]] |date=28 February 2008}}</ref> His 2006 performance included a duet with ''[[Australian Idol]]'' winner [[Guy Sebastian]].<ref name="stm" /> Sivan made it to the finals of ''StarSearch'' 2007.<ref name="PerthNow" /> In 2009 he released "For Them" to raise awareness and funds to help homeless children. His debut EP, ''[[Dare to Dream (EP)|Dare to Dream]]'', was released in June 2007. In February 2010, Sivan opened "[[We Are the World 25 for Haiti (YouTube edition)|We Are the World 25 for Haiti (YouTube Edition)]]", the collaborative music charity video produced by [[Lisa Lavie]] to help raise money for the victims of the [[2010 Haiti earthquake]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.yourmusics.in/artist/biography/troye-sivan-35225.html |title=Free Troye Sivan biography {{!}} SONG365|website=Yourmusics.in |access-date=6 October 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171006211539/https://www.yourmusics.in/artist/biography/troye-sivan-35225.html|archive-date=6 October 2017}}</ref>

On 5 June 2013, Sivan was signed to EMI Australia, a [[Universal Music Australia]] label, but kept it a secret until a year later. On 15 August 2014 he released a five-song EP entitled ''[[TRXYE]]'', led by its first single "[[Happy Little Pill]]", which was released on 25 July 2014.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Caulfield |first1=Keith |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6140809/youtube-star-troye-sivan-signs-with-emi-australia-announces-ep |title=YouTube Star Troye Sivan Signs With EMI Australia, Announces EP |magazine=Billboard |date=27 June 2014 |access-date=28 June 2014}}</ref> ''TRXYE'' debuted at No. 1 on iTunes in over 55 countries.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stern |first1=Bradley |url=http://www.idolator.com/7531054/troye-sivan-shoots-to-1-on-itunes-with-debut-ep-trxye |title=Troye Sivan Shoots To #1 On iTunes (In 55 Countries!) With Debut EP, 'TRXYE' |website=Idolator |date=15 August 2014 |access-date=25 September 2014}}</ref> The EP debuted at No. 5 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] the following week, scoring Sivan his first Top 10 album.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Caulfield |first1=Keith |url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/chart-alert/6221699/troye-sivan-set-for-top-10-debut-on-billboard-200 |title=Troye Sivan Set for Top 10 Debut on Billboard 200 |magazine=Billboard |date=15 August 2014 |access-date=25 September 2014}}</ref> "Happy Little Pill" peaked at number 10 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Singles Chart]] and was certified gold by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] for shipments exceeding 35,000 copies.<ref name="ARIAcert">{{cite certification |region=Australia |type=single |certyear=2014 |access-date=15 October 2014}}</ref> Sivan released his second major-label EP, ''[[Wild (Troye Sivan EP)|Wild]]'', on 4 September 2015. The EP was supported by a music video trilogy entitled ''Blue Neighbourhood'', comprising the three songs "[[Wild (Troye Sivan song)|Wild]]", "Fools" and "[[Talk Me Down]]" and released from September 2015 to December 2015. Additionally, the EP served as an introduction to his debut studio album ''[[Blue Neighbourhood]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://renownedforsound.com/index.php/album-review-troye-sivan-blue-neighbourhood/ |title=Album Review: Troye Sivan ‚Äì Blue Neighbourhood |last=Smith |first=Michael |date=5 December 2015 |website=Renowned for Sound |access-date=11 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180121182531/http://renownedforsound.com/index.php/album-review-troye-sivan-blue-neighbourhood/ |archive-date=21 January 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> which was released on 4 December 2015.<ref name="twitter.com" /> The single "[[Youth (Troye Sivan song)|Youth]]" reached number 23 in the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=Troye Sivan's 'Youth' Soars Into Top 40|work=Fuse|url=https://www.fuse.tv/2016/03/troye-sivan-youth-goes-top-40|access-date=6 October 2017}}</ref> Sivan supported ''Blue Neighbourhood'' and ''Wild'' with his first tour, 2015's [[Troye Sivan Live]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dailydot.com/entertainment/troye-sivan-fools-video-us-tour/ |title=Troye Sivan releases music video for 'Fools,' announces first U.S. tour |last=Votta |first=Rae |date=25 September 2015 |website=[[The Daily Dot]] |access-date=26 September 2015}}</ref> He further supported ''Blue Neighbourhood'' with 2016's [[Blue Neighbourhood Tour]] and Suburbia Tour.{{citation needed|date=July 2021}} On 26 May 2017, Troye collaborated with [[Martin Garrix]] to produce the song "[[There for You (Martin Garrix and Troye Sivan song)|There for You]]".<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.out.com/music/2017/5/26/why-troye-sivans-martin-garrix-collab-there-you-important-lgbtq-listeners |title=Why Troye Sivan's Martin Garrix Collab 'There For You' is Important for LGBTQ Listeners |date=26 May 2017 |access-date=30 April 2018}}</ref>

The artists, who Sivan met on Instagram, also designed t-shirts for the single, and all net proceeds were donated to the WHO [[COVID-19]] Solidarity Response Fund and Spotify's COVID-19 Music Relief Project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/troye-sivan-is-paying-freelance-artists-to-help-him-drop-a-new-song-2633143|title=Troye Sivan is paying freelance artists to help him drop a new song |date=22 March 2020|website=NME|access-date=2 April 2020}}</ref> On 15 July, Sivan released the single "[[Easy (Troye Sivan song)|Easy]]" and announced the release of the [[concept EP]] ''In a Dream''. The EP was released on 21 August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=21 August 2020|title=Troye Sivan drops new concept EP 'In A Dream'|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/troye-sivan-drops-new-concept-ep-in-a-dream-2734033|access-date=28 June 2021|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref>

On 27 August 2021, Sivan announced his single "[[Angel Baby (Troye Sivan song)|Angel Baby]]" which was later released on 10 September.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sivan|first=Troye|date=August 27, 2021|title=ANGEL BABY out sept 10 üëºüèº #fyp|url=https://www.tiktok.com/foryou?referer_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.nme.com%2F&referer_video_id=7000924021181222149&refer=embed&is_copy_url=1&is_from_webapp=v1&item_id=7000924021181222149#/@troyesivan/video/7000924021181222149|url-status=live|access-date=August 30, 2021|website=[[TikTok]]}}</ref>

==== Music videos ====
Sivan's music videos frequently feature LGBTQ relationships between the characters. ''Blue Neighbourhood''{{'}}s trilogy followed the narrative of two gay teenagers in a secret relationship while "Youth" featured Sivan with another male love interest. His music video for "[[Heaven (Troye Sivan song)|Heaven]]" features footage of historical LGBTQ movements and couples, as well as sensual shots with him and an anonymous man with the running taglines "Without losing a piece of me, how do I get to Heaven?" and "If I'm losing a piece of me, maybe I don't want Heaven". The singer says these portrayals are important to him, especially when he considers his younger life when he remembers "such vivid memories of the few times I saw any type of LGBTQ relationship on TV or in music videos".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.kitchenerpost.ca/whatson-story/6955732-q-a-troye-sivan-on-coming-out-canadian-popstars/ |title=Q&A: Troye Sivan on coming out, Canadian popstars |last=Friend |first=David |date=9 November 2016 |work=Kitchener Post |access-date=16 November 2016 |archive-date=12 April 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190412190739/https://www.kitchenerpost.ca/whatson-story/6955732-q-a-troye-sivan-on-coming-out-canadian-popstars/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On 16 July 2020, Sivan released a self-directed<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sivan|first=Troye|date=July 16, 2020|title=Troye Sivan - Easy (Official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bCHqIhiFmEY&ab_channel=TroyeSivanVEVO|url-status=live|access-date=August 30, 2021|website=[[YouTube]]|quote="Director:  Troye Sivan"}}</ref> music video for his second song, "Easy", from his EP ''In a Dream''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Legaspi|first=Althea|date=17 July 2020|title=Troye Sivan's Heartbreak is Set Aflame in New 'Easy' Video|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/troye-sivan-easy-video-1030149/|access-date=17 July 2020|magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref> A remix of "Easy", featuring country singer-songwriter [[Kacey Musgraves]] and producer [[Mark Ronson]], was released alongside a second music video in December 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Haylock|first=Zoe|date=10 December 2020|title=Troye Sivan and Kacey Musgraves Make It 'Easy' to Love Their New Music Video|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/12/troye-sivan-kacey-musgraves-easy-music-video.html|access-date=21 January 2021|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref>

=== YouTube ===
[[File:Troye Sivan at the 2014 VidCon (1).jpg|thumb|Sivan at the 2014 [[VidCon]] in [[Anaheim, California]]|alt=]]
In September 2012, Sivan began creating [[video blog]]s on the video sharing site [[YouTube]], after having only sung on the site since 2007. At the time of posting his first vlog, Sivan had accumulated 27,000 subscribers in his five years since joining YouTube on 1 October 2007. As of April 2016, Sivan had over 4{{nbsp}}million subscribers and over 243{{nbsp}}million total views across his YouTube videos.<ref name="Social Blade" /> His YouTube channel is the eighth most subscribed channel in Australia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://socialblade.com/youtube/top/country/AU/mostsubscribed|title=Top 100 YouTubers in Australia Filtered by Subscribers ‚Äì Socialblade Youtube Stats &#124; Youtube Statistics|work=Social Blade|access-date=22 July 2019}}</ref>

A YouTube video Sivan made with fellow YouTuber [[Tyler Oakley]], titled "The 'Boyfriend' Tag", earned them a [[2014 Teen Choice Awards#Choice Web|Teen Choice Award]] in the "Choice Web Collaboration" category.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/teen-choice-awards-2014-winners-724423 |title=Teen Choice Awards: The Complete Winners List |last=Nordyke |first=Kimberly |date=10 August 2016 |work=The Hollywood Reporter |access-date=29 December 2016}}</ref>
