'''Alexander Igorevich Rybak'''  ({{lang-ru|–ê–ª–µ–∫—Å–∞–Ω–¥—Ä –ò–≥–æ—Ä–µ–≤–∏—á –†—ã–±–∞–∫}}) or '''Alyaxandr Iharavich Rybak''' ({{lang-be|–ê–ª—è–∫—Å–∞–Ω–¥—Ä –Ü–≥–∞—Ä–∞–≤—ñ—á –†—ã–±–∞–∫}}; born 13 May 1986) is a Belarusian-Norwegian singer-composer, violinist, pianist and actor.<ref name="√∏p">{{cite web|url=http://www.op.no/kultur/article4317559.ece|title=Rybaks ukjente fortid i Larvik |last=Hansen|first=Kjeld-Willy|date=9 May 2009|publisher=[[√òstlands-Posten]]|language=no|access-date=12 July 2009}}</ref>

Though based in [[Oslo]], Rybak extensively worked on television programmes and on tours in [[Russia]], [[Belarus]], [[Ukraine]] and [[Sweden]] throughout the early 2010s. Singing in mainly [[English language|English]] and [[Russian language|Russian]], Rybak, in his early twenties, held on to a [[teen idol]] status in [[Scandinavia]], [[Eastern Europe]], and to a certain extent in [[Western Asia]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Where can I get...? |url=https://www.alexanderrybaknews.com/site/music/wherecaniget/page/2/ |website=Alexander Rybak News}}</ref> His debut album, ''[[Fairytales (Alexander Rybak album)|Fairytales]]'', charted in the top 20 in nine European countries, including a top position in Norway and Russia. After two pop albums in 2009 and 2010, Rybak desired to be more of a family-orientated artist, focusing on children's and classical music alongside and frequently performed with youth orchestras throughout the world.<ref name="eurostory.nl">{{Cite web|url=https://eurostory.nl/alexander-rybak-kinderboekenschrijver/|title=Alexander Rybak: kinderboekenschrijver|date=2016-02-10|website=Eurostory|language=en|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref>

Rybak is known for his extensive involvement in the [[Eurovision Song Contest]]. Representing Norway in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009|2009 contest]] in Moscow, Russia, he won the competition with 387 points‚Äîthe highest tally any country has achieved in the history of Eurovision under the then voting system‚Äîwith "[[Fairytale (Alexander Rybak song)|Fairytale]]", a song he wrote and composed. Winning at the age of 23, Rybak remains the youngest solo male winner of the contest and the only Belarusian-born winner. His win was celebrated all across Europe for crushing stereotypes about the contest, such as needing an over-the-top performance or the negative influence of [[Eurovision Song Contest#Political and geographical voting|neighbour voting]].<ref>{{cite web |title=–ï–≤—Ä–æ–≤–∏–¥–µ–Ω–∏–µ-2020: –ª—É—á—à–∏–µ –≤—ã—Å—Ç—É–ø–ª–µ–Ω–∏—è –ù–æ—Ä–≤–µ–≥–∏–∏ –Ω–∞ –∫–æ–Ω–∫—É—Ä—Å–µ |url=https://eurovision.stb.ua/ru/2020/05/18/evrovidenie-2020-luchshie-vystupleniya-norvegii-na-konkurse/ |website=STB.ua |access-date=22 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Sprookje komt uit voor Noorse winnaar |url=http://radio.nl/63425/sprookje-komt-uit-voor-noorse-winnaar |website=Radio.nl}}</ref>

Since then Rybak has been involved several times in the contest. He represented Norway again in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2018]] in [[Lisbon]], [[Portugal]], with the song "[[That's How You Write a Song]]", winning the second semi-final and finishing in 15th place in the Grand Final. He provided as an opening act to the Grand Final in 2010 and as an interval act in 2012 and 2016. Rybak has frequently provided commentary on the contest, working as a journalist in 2011, as once-off judge on the Belgian preselection in 2016 and as commentator on his personal social media.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UV2EfAbePig | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211102/UV2EfAbePig| archive-date=2021-11-02 | url-status=live|website=YouTube |publisher=Eurovision Song Contest}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Hadise en Alexander Rybak adviseren Eurosongkandidaten |url=https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2015/12/17/hadise_en_alexanderrybakadvisereneurosongkandidaten-1-2525944/ |website=VRT|date=17 December 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Rybak |first1=Alexander |title=Login ‚Ä¢ Instagram |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BFegwPsmFiP/ |website=Instagram}}</ref>

==Early life ==
Rybak was born in [[Minsk]], Belarus, which at that time was the [[Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic|Belarusian SSR]] in the [[Soviet Union]]. His parents and most of his other family hail from the town [[Vitebsk]], in Northern Belarus. His father [[Igor Rybak (violinist)|Igor Rybak]], a well-known classical violinist who performed alongside [[Pinchas Zukerman]], [[defection|defected]] to Norway in 1991 after a concert tour of a Belarusian chamber orchestra of which he was part of. Rybak's father lived with a musical family who gave him shelter and food in exchange for violin lessons for their son. Alexander Rybak and his mother Natalia Rybak (n√©e Gurina), who worked as a music journalist and a piano teacher, arrived in Norway on a tourist visa and were initially refused a residence permit.<ref name="op.no">{{Cite web|url=https://www.op.no/kultur/article4317559.ece?ns_campaign=article&amp;ns_mchannel=recommend_button&amp;ns_source=facebook&amp;ns_linkname=facebook&amp;ns_fee=0|title=Rybaks ukjente fortid i Larvik|date=2009-05-08|website=www.op.no|language=no|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref> Eventually, the Rybak family settled in [[Nesodden]] in the early 1990s.<ref name="early">{{cite web |url=http://www.nrk.no/programmer/tv/kjempesjansen/1.1087124 |title=Alexander Rybak |last=Horne |first=Birte Nj√∏sen |date=7 October 2006 |publisher=nrk.no |language=no}}</ref> Rybak and his family received Norwegian citizenship after seven years of residing in Norway.<ref name="op.no"/>

At the age of five, Rybak began to play piano and later violin, but eventually picked up violin as his main instrument.<ref name="early" /><ref>[http://www.emimusic.de/info/84880.html Biography: April 2009] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090520174505/http://www.emimusic.de/info/84880.html |date=20 May 2009 }} Alexander Rybak, EMI Music, Germany</ref> He stated "I always liked to entertain and somehow that is my vocation".<ref name="escinterview">{{cite web|url=http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/13180 |title='Being a big favourite makes me nervous' ‚Äî esctoday.com met with Alexander Rybak |last=Espen Hansen |first=Tom |date=9 February 2009 |publisher=esctoday.com |access-date=13 February 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090212175522/http://esctoday.com/news/read/13180 |archive-date=12 February 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He became a student at the [[Barratt Due Institute of Music]] in [[Oslo]] at the age of 10. As a result of his success in the 2009 [[Eurovision Song Contest]] he took a break from his [[bachelor's degree]] studies at the institute, but in 2011 he returned to his studies and in June 2012 he graduated from the institute with a Bachelor of Music in violin performance.<ref>{{cite web|title=Alexander Rybak Main Stage |url=http://www.operaen.no/Default.aspx?ID=27246&ProductID=PROD73 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/5gwJcPhrL?url=http://www.operaen.no/Default.aspx?ID=27246&ProductID=PROD73 |publisher=[[The Norwegian Opera and Ballet]] |archive-date=21 May 2009 |access-date=21 May 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|first=P√•l Fredrik |last=Pedersen |title=Rybak ble stor stjerne |url=http://www.tv2.no/gmn/article2732289.ece |publisher=[[TV 2 (Norway)]] |location=Norway |date=14 May 2009 |access-date=21 May 2009 |language=no |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/5gwJMm2N8?url=http://www.tv2.no/gmn/article2732289.ece |archive-date=21 May 2009 |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2004, Rybak was awarded the [[Anders Jahre]] Culture Prize presented to him by Viktoriya Rybak at the annual culture festival in Madrid, Spain.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Anders Jahres Humanit√¶re Stiftelse ‚Äì Pressemeldinger {{!}} sandefjord {{!}} skipsreder {{!}} vestfold {{!}} prisvinnere |url=http://www.ajhs.no/scripts/ingress/read.pl?id=040630091306-683 |date=2009-03-04 |access-date=2015-11-21 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090304005649/http://www.ajhs.no/scripts/ingress/read.pl?id=040630091306-683 |archive-date=4 March 2009}}</ref> In 2005, he entered the [[Idol (Norway)|Norwegian version of Idol]], ''Idol: Jakten p√• en superstjerne'', reaching the semifinal. ===Eurovision 2009===
[[File:Alexander Rybak at the Eurovision press conference.jpg|thumb|right|Rybak after he had won Eurovision 2009]]
{{Main|Norway in the Eurovision Song Contest 2009}}
Rybak won the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009|54th Eurovision Song Contest]] in Moscow, Russia, with a record 387 points, singing "[[Fairytale (Alexander Rybak song)|Fairytale]]",<ref name="rybaktoeurovision">{{cite web|url=http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/13325 |title=Norway: Alexander Rybak to Eurovision |last=Klier |first=Marcus |date=21 February 2009 |publisher=ESCtoday |access-date=21 February 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090224053412/http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/13325 |archive-date=24 February 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref> a song inspired by Norwegian folk music. The song was composed and written by Rybak<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=1891 |title=Norway: Alexander Rybak and Ovi to final! |last=Schacht |first=Andreas |date=7 February 2009 |publisher=eurovision.tv |access-date=10 February 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090208163910/http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=1891 |archive-date=8 February 2009 |url-status=live}}</ref> and was performed with the modern [[folk dance]] company [[Frikar Dance Company|Frikar]]. The song received good reviews with a score of 6 out of 6 in the Norwegian newspaper ''[[Dagbladet]]'',<ref name="escinterview"/> and, in an ''ESCtoday'' poll, he scored 71.3%, making him the favourite to get into the final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.escfans.com/news/read/13167 |title=Third semi final results Norway: Another two acts chosen for the national final |last=Klier |first=Marcus |date=7 February 2009 |publisher=escfans.com |access-date=13 February 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110710201457/http://www.escfans.com/news/read/13167 |archive-date=10 July 2011}}</ref>

In the 2009 Norwegian national heats, Rybak achieved a clean sweep, gaining the top score from all nine voting districts and ending with a combined televote and jury score of 747,888, while the runner up, [[Tone Damli Aaberge]], received a combined score of only 121,856 (out of a total population of less than 5 million)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/13325 |title=Norway: Alexander Rybak to Eurovision |publisher=Esctoday.com |access-date=18 September 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090224053412/http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/13325 |archive-date=24 February 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

The song then competed in the second semi-final and won the place in the Eurovision final. Rybak later won the Eurovision final with a landslide victory, receiving votes from all the other participating countries. Rybak finished with a total of 387 points, breaking the previous record of 292 points scored by [[Lordi]] in [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006|2006]] and scoring 169 points more than the runner-up, [[J√≥hanna Gu√∞r√∫n J√≥nsd√≥ttir|Iceland]].

===2016‚Äì2017===
[[File:ESC2016 Grand Final Interval Act 09 (crop).jpg|thumb|Rybak performing in the interval act of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]]]]
In early 2016, Rybak was a one-off jury member in Eurosong, Belgium's selection for Eurovision 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2015/12/17/belgium-vrt-experts-eurosong-alexander-rybak/111391/|title=Belgium: VRT reveals experts for Eurosong, including Alexander Rybak|date=2015-12-17|website=wiwibloggs|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref> Rybak also performed as a part of the interval act for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]], in a musical number satirising Eurovision songs. Next to that, he held several concert shows under the title "Entertainer".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alexanderrybak.com/2016/04/05/alexander-rybak-presents-summer-show-entertainer/|title=Alexander Rybak presents the Summer-show "Entertainer"|date=2016-04-05|website=Alexander Rybak|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref>

=== Eurovision 2018 ===
[[File:Alexander Rybak Melodi Grand Prix 2018.jpg|thumb|Rybak performing "That's How You Write A Song" at the final of Melodi Grand Prix 2018.]]
In January 2018, it was revealed that Rybak would be taking part in the Norwegian national selection ''[[Melodi Grand Prix 2018]]'', to represent [[Norway in the Eurovision Song Contest 2018]] in [[Lisbon]], [[Portugal]], with the song "[[That's How You Write a Song]]". The song initially received very mixed reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2018/03/10/melodi-grand-prix-2018-aleksander-walmann-is-our-favourite-to-win-in-oslo/219377/|title=Melodi Grand Prix 2018: Aleksander Walmann is our favourite to win in Norway|date=2018-03-10|website=wiwibloggs|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref> Rybak stated that he wanted to take part mostly for pleasure, rather than wanting to win the competition, stating that Norwegian singer [[Jahn Teigen]], who participated fourteen times in the Norwegian selection and won three times, was his inspiration.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nrk.no/kultur/alexander-rybak-kan-bruke-flere-timer-pa-a-kjope-et-brod-1.14029302|title=Alexander Rybak kan bruke flere timer p√• √• kj√∏pe et br√∏d|last=Tolfsen|first=Caroline|date=2018-05-01|website=NRK|language=nb-NO|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref>

Rybak was not the bookmakers and experts' favourite to win the selection,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eurovisionworld.com/odds/norway-mgp-2018|title=Odds Norway Melodi Grand Prix 2018|website=Eurovisionworld|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref> but topped several public polls ahead of the competition. To his own surprise and to the surprise of both of the presenters, Rybak won the selection.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nrk.no/mgp/alexander-rybak-med-ny-mgp-seier-1.13954913|title=Alexander Rybak med ny MGP-seier|last=Aune|first=Oddvin|date=2018-03-10|website=NRK|language=nb-NO|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref> During the show, Rybak topped four international juries ‚Äì Russia, Estonia, North Macedonia and Czechia ‚Äì more than any other contestant. Rybak proceeded to the last four, where he first beat [[Aleksander Wallman]] in a duel and then proceeded to the final duel with singer [[Rebecca (Norwegian singer)|Rebecca]], which he also won.

After the show, several news outlets claimed that Norwegian broadcaster NRK had favourised Rybak, putting him last in the running order.<ref name="dagbladet.no">{{Cite web|url=https://www.dagbladet.no/a/69602175|title=NRK sl√•r tilbake mot kritikken:-Dette er en sv√¶rt dr√∏y p√•stand|last=Grindem|first=Karianne|date=2018-03-13|website=Dagbladet.no|language=no|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref> NRK denied this, pointing to the voting statistics that showed that Rybak had won with a large margin.<ref name="dagbladet.no"/> He won his first duel with 94.3% of the votes and his final duel by 71% of the votes, receiving a total of 306,393 votes, the third-highest number of votes an entry ever reached in ''Melodi Grand Prix'' history.

Rybak was drawn to perform first in second semi-final on 10 May and finished first overall, becoming the first ever Eurovision performer to win two semi-finals after he also won the second semi-final in 2009. In the final, on 12 May 2018, Rybak performed seventh in the running order and finished in fifteenth place, receiving a top jury score from Italy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurovisionworld.com/eurovision/2018|title=Eurovision 2018 Results: Voting & Points|publisher=eurovisionworld.com|access-date=13 May 2018}}</ref>
