'''We the Kings''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Bradenton, Florida]]. The band's [[We the Kings (album)|self-titled full-length debut album]], released in 2007, included the platinum single "[[Check Yes Juliet]]", and went on to sell over 250,000 copies in the US. The group's second album ''[[Smile Kid]]'' (2009) included Top 40 singles "Heaven Can Wait" and "[[We'll Be a Dream]]" (featuring [[Demi Lovato]]), as well as the single "She Takes Me High".<ref name="We the Kings Bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/we-the-kings-mn0000727318/biography|title=We the Kings Bio|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>

The band's third album ''[[Sunshine State of Mind]]'' was released in 2011 and featured the [[MTV Video Music Award]] winner for Most Innovative Music Video of the Year for the group's interactive music video and song "Say You Like Me". The band then completed a worldwide tour with Canadian band [[Simple Plan]] and performed across the US as a main stage headliner on the [[Vans Warped Tour]]. The group's fourth album ''[[Somewhere Somehow (album)|Somewhere Somehow]]'' was released in late 2013 and includes singles "[[Just Keep Breathing]]", "Find You There", "Any Other Way", and "Art of War". Their fifth studio album, ''[[Strange Love (We the Kings album)|Strange Love]]'', came out on November 20, 2015. It featured the single "Runaway".<ref name="altpress">{{cite web|last=Bazley|first=John|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/we_the_kings_release_title_track_from_upcoming_album_strange_love|title=We The Kings release title track from upcoming album, 'Strange Love'|date=October 30, 2015|publisher=altpress|access-date=August 6, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/strange-love-mw0002899928|title=We the King|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>

==History==

===Early years, formation, ''Broken Image'' and ''We the Kings'' (2005‚Äì2008)===
{{Main|We the Kings (album)}}

Friends since childhood, the four bandmates (singer/guitarist [[Travis Clark (singer)|Travis Clark]], guitarist Hunter Thomsen, his brother bassist Drew Thomsen, and drummer Danny Duncan) formed the group while attending high school. The band got its name from the middle school that all the members attended called Martha B. King Middle School.<ref>Taylor, Chuck. "We The Kings: Check Yes Juliet (Run Baby Run)." ''Billboard'', June 14, 2008. Web. September 30, 2014.</ref> Before the band was known as We the Kings, the group went by the name Broken Image.<ref>Trippy, Charles. "OUR HOUSE TOUR! (7.2.15 - Day 2256)" 'CTFXC', July 2, 2015. Web. 3. July 3, 2015.</ref> The band's first tour was with Don't Die Cindy in the summer of 2005 where the group went by Broken Image, then later went under the moniker De Soto. The band A Heartwell Ending (later renamed Call the Cops) supported for the final leg of the tour. While teaming with Bret Disend in Fall of 2005, We the Kings went on its first tour under that name as a five piece, with the then-unknown [[Boys Like Girls]] as the group supported the new release of the "Great Escape" music video. We the Kings played on its first headlining tour Long Hair Don't Care with [[Valencia (band)|Valencia]], [[The Cab]], [[Sing It Loud]], and [[Charlotte Sometimes (musician)|Charlotte Sometimes]] during late March and throughout April. In May and June, We the Kings supported [[Cute Is What We Aim For]] and [[Boys Like Girls]] on the group's UK tour.<ref name="concertarchives">{{cite web|url=http://www.concertarchives.org/bands/we-the-kings--5|title=We the Kings 's Concert History|publisher=concertarchives}}</ref>

Under the guidance of manager Bret Disend, the band placed a number of tracks on the social networking site Purevolume in 2007 to build online buzz. Meanwhile, the band also pursued a deal with EMI's imprint S-Curve Records, which eventually signed the group. We the Kings' self-titled debut album was produced by Sam Hollander and mixed by Lou Giordano, and was released in October 2007. The album peaked at number 151 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and spent fifteen weeks on the chart. The second single from the album, "[[Check Yes Juliet]]", became a minor hit, peaking at number seventy on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and number twenty-five on the [[Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)|Pop Songs]] chart.<ref name="US-singles">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=we the kings|chart=Hot 100}} | title=We the Kings ‚Äì Chart History: Hot 100 | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | access-date=April 8, 2013}}</ref><ref name="US-Pop-singles">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=we the kings|chart=Pop Songs B}} | title=We the Kings ‚Äì Chart History: Pop Songs | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | access-date=April 8, 2013}}</ref> The single was later released in [[Australia]] in 2011 where it peaked at number twenty-six and was certified platinum by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA).<ref name="AUS-singles">{{cite web | url=http://www.australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=We+The+Kings | title=Discography We The Kings | work=australian-charts.com | publisher=Hung Medien | access-date=April 8, 2013}}
* "Just Keep Breathing": {{cite web|url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/23790/20140517-0000/Issue1261.pdf|title=ARIA Report 1261}}</ref><ref name="ARIA" /> The album was also released in 2011 and was certified platinum by the ARIA for sales of 70,000 copies.<ref name="ARIA">{{cite web | url=http://www.aria.com.au/pages/aria-charts-accreditations.htm | title=ARIA Charts ‚Äì Accreditations | publisher=[[Australian Recording Industry Association]] | access-date=April 12, 2013}}</ref>

For part of the fall of 2007, the band played a tour called Tourzilla with headliner Boys Like Girls, [[All Time Low]], and [[The Audition (band)|The Audition]]. The group toured in support of [[Cobra Starship]] in early 2008 along with Metro Station and [[The Cab]]. We the Kings performed all dates of the Warped Tour 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://warpedtour.com/warpedtour/bands.asp |title=2013 Vans Warped Tour / Date And Band Details Coming This Fall |publisher=Warpedtour.com |access-date=October 29, 2012}}</ref> On August 30, 2008 the band was a part of The Rays Summer Concert Series, playing on the field after a Tampa Bay Rays game. In late 2008, after completion of the Warped Tour, the band toured the US and UK with [[The Academy Is...]] (the tour was known as Bill & Trav's Bogus Journey), along with supporting bands including [[The Maine (band)|The Maine]], [[Hey Monday]] and [[Carolina Liar]].<ref name="concertarchives"/>

The Kings Carriage are skits that We The Kings film and post on YouTube. The King's Carriage detail the band's life on the road and give the fans insight into the band members' lives. These skits have generated over 300 million views.<ref>Lipshutz, Jason. "Music: Happening Now-Pop: Kingdom Come." ''Prometheus Global Media'', January 16, 2010. Web. October 30, 2014.</ref>

===''Smile Kid'' (2009‚Äì2010)===
{{Main|Smile Kid}}

In February 2009, the band headlined a tour called The Secret Valentine Tour with [[The Maine (band)|The Maine]], [[The Cab]], [[There for Tomorrow]], and [[VersaEmerge|Versa]]. We the Kings played in the 2009 Bamboozle Roadshow Tour. It began on April 3 at Hoodwinked in California, touring from April through May 2, ending at [[The Bamboozle]] in East Rutherford, NJ. Bands also playing the Bamboozle Road Show tour included [[Forever the Sickest Kids]], The Cab, [[Never Shout Never]], and Mercy Mercedes. On July 8, 2009, the band began its tour as support for [[All Time Low]] along with [[Cartel (band)|Cartel]] and [[Days Difference]]. The band also performed at [[Warped Tour 2009]]. The band was on tour in Fall 2009, again supporting [[All Time Low]] on the Glamour Kills Tour, with [[Hey Monday]] and [[The Friday Night Boys]]. The group's sophomore album, which was released in December 2009, reached 112 on the Billboard 200 and spent three weeks on the chart.<ref name="US-albums">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=we the kings|chart=Billboard 200}} | title=We the Kings ‚Äì Chart History: Billboard 200 | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | access-date=April 8, 2013}}</ref> It spawned the [[lead single]], "Heaven Can Wait", which peaked at number thirty on the Pop Songs chart,<ref name="US-Pop-singles" /> and a second single featuring [[Demi Lovato]] titled "[[We'll Be a Dream]]", which peaked at seventy-six on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and number twenty-three on the Pop Songs chart.<ref name="US-singles" /><ref name="US-Pop-singles" />

The band's second album, ''Smile Kid'', was released on December 8, 2009 <ref>Lipshutz, Jason. "Music Happening Now-Pop: Kingdom Come." ''Billboard,'' January 16, 2010. Web. October 1, 2014.</ref>

In early 2010, the band headlined the Hot Topic Presents: Take Action Tour with There for Tomorrow, [[A Rocket to the Moon]], [[Mayday Parade]], and [[Stereo Skyline]]. We the Kings also played with New Found Glory at Fitchburg State College's annual spring concert. In March 2010, We the Kings supported [[You Me at Six]], an English pop rock band, on the group's UK headline tour along with [[Forever the Sickest Kids]]. The band has also played in Warped Tour 2010 from June 26 to August 2. After Warped Tour, the band plan on a world tour in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://amplified.tv/2010/03/21/we-the-kings-interview/ |title=Amplified. ‚Äì We The Kings Interview |publisher=Amplified.tv |date=March 21, 2010 |access-date=October 29, 2012}}</ref> In April 2010 the band performed a benefit concert with fellow pop punk band Voted Most Random at a local venue in [[New Haven, Connecticut]]. The event raised thousands of dollars for ClearWater Initiative and was the organization's biggest fundraiser up to that date. In June 2010, We the Kings performed at 93Q Summer Jam, a concert in [[Baldwinsville]], New York at the Papermill island. The band performed with Cartel, [[Jaicko]], [[Mayday Parade]], [[New Boyz]], [[Shontelle]] and [[Spose]]. The group performed "Secret Valentine" along with a few other songs and closed with "Check Yes Juliet".<ref name="concertarchives"/>

===''Sunshine State of Mind'' and lineup changes (2011‚Äì2012)===
{{Main|Sunshine State of Mind}}

From February 4‚Äì15, 2011, We the Kings toured the UK in ten different cities, with the Manchester performance being one of the first to sell out. The band was supported by Versaemerge, All Forgotten and I See Stars throughout the whole tour. Later that same month, the band toured in the Philippines with [[The Maine (band)|The Maine]] and Never Shout Never. The group toured Australia in early March 2011, including performing at the Soundwave festival. The band also headlined two sold-out sideshows in Sydney and Melbourne with The Maine and [[Never Shout Never]]. The band went out on the Friday Is Forever Tour in Summer 2011 in support of ''Smile Kid''. [[The Summer Set]], [[The Downtown Fiction]], [[Hot Chelle Rae]] and Action Item provided support. In November 2011 at an industry conference hosted by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine, Clark joined S-Curve founder and record producer [[Steve Greenberg (record producer)|Steve Greenberg]] to demonstrate an interactive video game based on the band's music and images.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://venturebeat.com/2011/11/09/billboards-futuresound-conference-is-coming-up-fast/ |title=Billboard's FutureSound conference is coming up fast! |work=Venture Beat |date=November 9, 2011 |access-date=November 18, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/industry/digital-and-mobile/futuresound-keynotes-pandora-ceo-joe-kennedy-1005544372.story |title=FutureSound Keynotes: Pandora CEO Joe Kennedy, Superfly Co-Founder Rick Farman, S-Curve Founder Steve Greenberg |date=November 18, 2011 |last1=Dahud |first1=Hisham |last2=Downs |first2=David |location=San Francisco |work=Billboard |access-date=November 21, 2011}}</ref>

Almost two years after the release of the band's second album, a follow-up, titled ''[[Sunshine State of Mind]]'', was released. Preceded by the single "Friday Is Forever", the album became the group's highest charting effort in the United States, debuting and peaking at number 45 on the ''Billboard'' 200. The album spawned a second single, "Say You Like Me", which peaked at number 32 on the Pop Songs chart,<ref name="US-Pop-singles" /> and number 29 in Australia.<ref name="AUS-singles" /> The single was later certified gold in Australia for shipments exceeding 35,000.<ref name="ARIA" />

In October 2011, Charles Trippy and Coley O'Toole joined We the Kings with Trippy playing bass guitar (replacing Drew Thomsen who left the group earlier that year) and O'Toole playing keyboard and rhythm guitar. Throughout much of 2011 and 2012, the band toured various countries on numerous tours including the End of the World Tour, performing with Anarbor, The Downtown Fiction and Mayday Parade. The group then toured countries in Europe, including Sweden, Denmark, Spain, Finland, Switzerland, The Netherlands, Germany, France, Austria, Hungary, Serbia, Italy, Portugal and the United Kingdom. The band also toured the east coast of Australia as a supporting act for Simple Plan. We the Kings played tour dates at the Vans Warped Tour 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=We The Kings {{!}} Tour Dates|url=http://www.wethekingsmusic.com/tour|publisher=Wethekingsmusic.com|access-date=June 23, 2012}}</ref>

The band performed at NoCAPRICHO 2012 in September alongside bands [[We Are the In Crowd]] and Before You Exit in S√£o Paulo, Brazil.<ref>{{cite web|title=NoCAPRICHO 2012: We the Kings, We Are the in Crowd e Before You Exit est√£o confirmados!|url=http://capricho.abril.com.br/blogs/nocapricho2012/nocapricho-2012-we-the-kings-we-are-the-in-crowd-e-before-you-exit-estao-confirmados/|publisher=Capricho Magazine|access-date=August 12, 2012}}</ref> The band also made a stop in Rhode Island to perform at Roger Williams University.<ref name="concertarchives"/>

===''Somewhere Somehow'' and ''Stripped'' (2013‚Äì2015)===
{{Main|Somewhere Somehow (album)|Stripped (We the Kings album)}}

In January 2013, We the Kings began recording the group's fourth album. Clark recorded parts in California with Duncan, Hunter Thomsen, and O'Toole, while Trippy recorded bass tracks in Florida using [[Boyce Avenue]]'s studio. During February, the band headlined another tour throughout Europe, visiting numerous countries including the United Kingdom, The Netherlands, Germany, Italy and Switzerland. By March 2013, the band members announced that a new song would be released in the first week of April. The song, "[[Just Keep Breathing]]", is about how frontman Clark had been bullied as a kid. "Just Keep Breathing", released on April 5, reached number 101 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and number 13 in the Official Alternative chart after only three days of sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/we_the_kings_release_new_single_just_keep_breathing|title=We The Kings release new single, "Just Keep Breathing"|work=Alternative Press|author=Common, Tyler |date=April 5, 2013 |access-date=September 5, 2015}}</ref><ref name="smith" /> The single climbed 63 places to number 38 on the mid-week chart for April 10.<ref name="smith">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/need-u-100-holds-on-to-official-singles-chart-update-number-1-2155/|title=Need U 100% holds on to Official Singles Chart Update Number 1|work=officialcharts.com}}</ref> The second single, "Find You There", was released on May 3. The band's third single, "Any Other Way" was released on June 14. On September 1, 2013, the band finished the Summerfest 2013 tour with [[Breathe Carolina]], [[T. Mills]], and [[The Ready Set]].<ref name="concertarchives"/>

On November 22, 2013, We the Kings announced the title of the group's upcoming fourth album would be ''Vitam Regum''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2013/11/we-kings-reveal-new-album-vitam-regum.html|title=Alter The Press!: We The Kings Reveal New Album 'Vitam Regum' Release Date|work=alterthepress.com}}</ref> On November 26, Charles Trippy announced on Twitter that ''Vitam Regum'' would be released on December 16, 2013. On December 2, however, the band announced via Twitter the name of the album had changed to ''Somewhere Somehow''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/CharlesTrippy/status/405447935501926401|title=Charles Trippy on Twitter|work=[[Twitter]]|access-date=November 19, 2014}}</ref> The band released the fourth single for ''Somewhere Somehow'', entitled "Art of War", on December 9, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2013/12/we-kings-release-new-song-art-of-war.html|title=Alter The Press!: We The Kings Release New Song "Art Of War"|website=Alterthepress.com|access-date=November 19, 2014}}</ref> After its first week of release as an iTunes exclusive the album debuted at 44 on the ''Billboard'' 200 and at 88 on the UK Albums Chart.<ref name=Bill200>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431174/we%20the%20kings/chart?f=305|title=We the Kings - Chart history (Billboard 200)|work=billboard.com|access-date=September 8, 2015}}</ref>

On February 18, 2014, Clark announced via YouTube that the band would be traveling on Warped Tour 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fuse.tv/2013/12/warped-roadies-s2-warped-tour-2014-bands-lineup |title=Warped Tour 2014 Lineup: See All the Bands Playing This Year! - News - Fuse |website=Fuse.tv |access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref> We the Kings also performed on the SlamDunk Fest 2014 alongside bands such as [[The All-American Rejects]].<ref name="concertarchives"/>

After Warped Tour 2014, Clark started working on new songs as well as re-recording songs from the previous album for an acoustic album. The album, [[Stripped (We the Kings album)|Stripped]], featured acoustic versions of songs from Somewhere Somehow, as well as some new material, including "Stone Walls" and the bonus track, "Is This the End?". The album was fully released on November 24, 2014. We the Kings completed a short, Florida-only tour for this album, and released a lyric video for "Stone Walls".<ref name="itunes.apple">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/stripped/id942328832|title=Stripped We the Kings|website=[[iTunes]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/we_the_kings_to_release_new_acoustic_album_stripped|title=We The Kings to release new acoustic album, 'Stripped'|publisher=altpress}}</ref>

===''Self-Titled Nostalgia'', and ''Six'' (2016‚Äìpresent)===
In September 2016, Travis Clark announced that he was writing music for a new We the Kings album in his studio in Orlando, Florida. He also posted pictures to Instagram with the caption "This is what writing/recording through all hours of the night looks like for new We The Kings music. Yay for more/new songs! Yay for the studio being in my house! Who's excited?"

On December 5, 2016, We the Kings announced a tour that will commemorate the ten year anniversary of their debut album, ''We the Kings'', that was released in 2007. The tour will go from February to May 2017 with 32 dates in the US, 3 dates in the UK, and 1 date in Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iconvsicon.com/2016/12/06/we-the-kings-announce-wtk-10-tour-to-celebrate-10th-anniversary-of-their-debut-album/|title=We the Kings Announce WTK 10 Tour to Celebrate 1oth Anniversary of Their Debut Album|publisher=iconvsicon}}</ref><ref name="billboard 2">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7694130/we-the-kings-travis-clark-10th-anniversary-tour|title=We the Kings' Travis Clark on the Band's 10th Anniversary Tour: 'I Will Remember This For the Rest of My Life'|work=Billboard}}</ref>

On September 12, 2017, We the Kings released a tenth anniversary album called ''Self-Titled Nostalgia''. The album is composed of reworked versions of all the songs that appeared on the band's debut studio album, ''We the Kings''. It also includes one new song called "Planes, Trains, and Cars".

On December 1, 2017, We the Kings released a new single called "Festival Music".

In December 2017, We the Kings released a video announcing the start of production for their sixth studio album. The album was announced to include "Planes, Trains, and Cars", "Festival Music", and eight to ten more songs. Like the previous two We The Kings records, this album was released without a label. To fund the album, the band offered multiple packages for fans to purchase on PledgeMusic. The packages included items such as a vinyl record of the album, the ability to name a song on the album, a bass guitar used by Charles Trippy, a guitar used by Travis Clark, and more. Each person who purchased a package had their face placed on the album cover along with the band members' faces in a yearbook format. The album was released July 6, 2018.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8461838/we-the-kings-new-album-six-on-my-love-song|title="We the Kings" Announce Fan-Driven New Album 'Six|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>
