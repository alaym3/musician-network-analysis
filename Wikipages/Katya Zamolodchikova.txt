'''Brian Joseph McCook''', known by his [[Drag queen|drag persona]] '''Yekaterina Petrovna Zamolodchikova''' ({{lang-ru|–ï–∫–∞—Ç–µ—Ä–∏–Ω–∞ –ü–µ—Ç—Ä–æ–≤–Ω–∞ –ó–∞–º–æ–ª–æ–¥—á–∏–∫–æ–≤–∞}}), or [[Mononymous person|mononymously]] as '''Katya''' ({{lang-ru|–ö–∞—Ç—è}}), is an American [[drag queen]], actor, author, recording artist, and comedian.{{citation needed|date=September 2021}} {{lang|ru-Latn|Katya|italic=no}} is best known for placing fifth on the [[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 7)|seventh season of ''RuPaul's Drag Race'']] and placing as runner-up on the [[RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars (season 2)|second season of ''RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars'']], as well as for appearing in the [[World of Wonder (production company)|World of Wonder]] web series ''[[UNHhhh]]'' and the Viceland series ''[[The Trixie & Katya Show]]'' with co-host and fellow season 7 alum [[Trixie Mattel]].<ref>{{cite web|title=UNHhhh|url=https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLhgFEi9aNUb2BNrIEecCGXApgeX7Yjwz8|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Pandaraboyina|first1=Vennela|title=Queens, wigs, and RuPaul - Campus Times|url=http://www.campustimes.org/2017/04/17/queens-wigs-and-rupaul/|website=Campustimes.org|date=April 17, 2017|access-date=April 24, 2017}}</ref> In June 2019, a panel of judges from ''[[New York (magazine)|New York]]'' magazine placed {{lang|ru-Latn|Katya|italic=no}} 13th on their list of "the most powerful drag queens in America", a ranking of 100 former ''Drag Race'' contestants.<ref name="nymag">{{cite news |url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/06/most-powerful-drag-queens-in-america-ranked.html# |title=The Most Powerful Drag Queens in America: Ranking the new establishment. |author=The Editors |date=June 10, 2019 |access-date=June 10, 2019 |magazine=[[New York (magazine)|New York]] |archive-date=June 10, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190610184458/https://www.vulture.com/2019/06/most-powerful-drag-queens-in-america-ranked.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Early life==
McCook is of [[Irish People|Irish descent]] and was raised in a [[Catholic]] household with an older brother and a younger sister.<ref>{{Citation|title=Episode 76 - Katya|url=https://soundcloud.com/rupaul/episode-76-katya|access-date=December 15, 2016}}</ref> McCook grew up in [[Marlborough, Massachusetts]] and graduated from Marlborough High School in 2000. He attended [[Boston University]] for one year before transferring to [[Massachusetts College of Art and Design]], where he studied video and performance art in the [[Studio for Interrelated Media]] (SIM) program with a minor in psychology, and first became interested in drag.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Parks-Ramage|first1=Jonathan|url=https://broadly.vice.com/en_us/article/43n84p/keeping-up-with-katya-the-russian-queen-racing-toward-fame|title=Keeping up with Katya, the Russian Queen Racing Toward Fame|work=broadly.vice.com|access-date=November 15, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Infante|first1=Victor D.|url=http://www.telegram.com/article/20150313/COLUMN86/303139957|title=Marlboro-born performer Katya shoots for 'Drag Race' fame|work=telegram.com|access-date=November 27, 2015}}</ref>
In a 2019 interview, McCook described growing up as "boring, nondescript, [and] a boiler-plate white suburb".<ref>{{Citation |title=KATYA: EXPOSED (THE FULL INTERVIEW) - Part 1|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sqM0WpnzpBI |website=YouTube |date=October 17, 2019 |access-date=March 16, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Early drag career ===
McCook created the Russian drag character {{lang|ru-Latn|Yekaterina Petrovna Zamolodchikova|italic=no}} in 2006, taking the stage name from a combination of Russian names and one of his favorite gymnasts, {{lang|ru-Latn|[[Elena Zamolodchikova]]|italic=no}}.<ref name="RC">{{cite web|url=http://russianchicagomag.com/katya_zamo_eng/|title=His name is Katya|author=Andre Akimov|work=RUSSIAN CHICAGO MAGAZINE|access-date=November 27, 2015}}</ref> When creating his drag character, McCook stated that he was inspired by "female comedians and just funny interesting women like [[Tracey Ullman|Tracy Ullman]], [[Maria Bamford]], [[Lana del Rey]] [and] [[Amy Sedaris]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.frenchbeautytime.com/Star%20of%20RuPaul's%20Drag%20Race-%20interview%20with%20Katya%20Zamo%20-%20Examiner.com.pdf|title=Interview with Katya Zamo - Examiner.com|website=examiner.com|access-date=June 5, 2015}}</ref> McCook stated on ''[[Rupaul's drag race|Drag Race]]'' that the Russian-based aspect of his persona was inspired by a professor he had while studying at the [[Massachusetts College of Art and Design]], who "never left the house without a full face of makeup [with] six inch stilettos in the snow."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.logotv.com/video-clips/b84pew/rupauls-drag-race-katya-gets-to-work#id=1734527|title=Katya Gets To Work - Video Clip from RuPaul's Drag Race {{!}} S7, E1 {{!}} LOGOTV.com|website=Logo TV|access-date=April 18, 2016}}</ref> McCook is not Russian himself, but took several Russian language courses and used a cassette tape called "Pronounce It Perfectly" to master the accent.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/06/02/rupauls-drag-race-miss-congeniality-winner-katya-speaks|title='RuPaul's Drag Race': Miss Congeniality winner Katya speaks!|magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |access-date=April 18, 2016}}</ref> In addition to speaking Russian, he can also speak fluent French and is known to speak Japanese and Portuguese.<ref>{{Cite web|title=RuPaul's Drag Race: 10 Things You Didn't Know About Katya|url=https://screenrant.com/rupauls-drag-race-things-didnt-know-katya/amp/|date=2019-08-11|website=ScreenRant|access-date=2020-05-24}}</ref>

As Katya, McCook hosted a monthly [[drag show]] called "Perestroika" at the Jacques Cabaret.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://archive.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2011/04/29/night_watch_perestroika/|title=Night Watch: Perestroika|last1=Oliver|first1=Maria|date=April 29, 2011|access-date=April 26, 2016|agency=Boston Globe}}</ref> Katya became known on the local drag scene in Boston for performing songs by popular Russian artists such as [[Alla Pugacheva]], [[t.A.T.u.]] and [[Glukoza]].

===''RuPaul's Drag Race''===
Katya auditioned for ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race]]'' on [[Logo TV]] four times before being chosen to participate in [[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 7)|season 7]].<ref name="Juengling">{{cite news|first=Konrad|last=Juengling|authorlink= Konrad Juengling|title=PQ MONTHLY GRILLS KATYA OF "RUPAUL'S DRAG RACE" SEASON 7|url=http://www.pqmonthly.com/pq-monthly-grills-katya-rupauls-drag-race-season-7/21949|access-date=October 16, 2016|publisher=PQ Monthly|date=March 6, 2015}}</ref> She is seen auditioning for the show back in [[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 3)#Episodes|Season 3's Casting Extravaganza]]. She won two challenges before being eliminated in episode 11, finishing in fifth place after losing a lip-sync against [[Kennedy Davenport]]. The decision to eliminate her was controversial, as she was popular among viewers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2015/06/02/justice-for-katya-inside-the-rupaul-s-drag-race-season-7-finale.html|title=Justice for Katya: Inside the 'RuPaul's Drag Race' Season 7 Finale|last=Fallon|first=Kevin|website=The Daily Beast|date=June 2, 2015|access-date=April 18, 2016}}</ref> During the Season 7 reunion, she was voted Miss Congeniality by fans. On May 16, 2016, Katya appeared at the finale of ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 8)|RuPaul's Drag Race Season 8]]'' to crown the next Miss Congeniality: [[Cynthia Lee Fontaine]]. On June 17, 2016, Katya was announced as one of the cast members on the [[RuPaul's Drag Race: All Stars (season 2)|second season of ''RuPaul's Drag Race: All Stars'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/06/17/rupauls-all-stars-drag-race-season-2-cast|title=RuPaul's All Stars Drag Race season 2 cast revealed ‚Äî exclusive|website=Entertainment Weekly|access-date=June 27, 2016}}</ref> She placed in the top three times throughout the season, and eventually finished as a runner-up along with fellow contestant [[Detox Icunt|Detox]].

===Other projects===
[[File:Trixie and Katya's High School Reunion 2.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Katya and Trixie Mattel in 2017]]
Katya has uploaded several [[web series]] on her YouTube channel, "welovekatya", including ''RuGRETs'', ''RuFLECTIONS'', ''Drag 101'', ''Total RuCall'', and ''Irregardlessly Trish'', the last of which is about a Bostonian hairdresser who lives in a dumpster. Katya improvises some of her material and also writes with a creative partner, Avi Paul Weinstein.

In November 2015, Katya appeared on the ''[[Christmas Queens]]'' album, singing a modified version of the song "[[The Twelve Days of Christmas (song)|12 Days of Christmas]]".<ref>{{cite journal|url=http://www.people.com/article/'rupauls-drag-race-holiday-album-christmas-queens|title=Queens from RuPaul's Drag Race to Release a Holiday Album: Hear One of the Tracks|journal=[[People Magazine]]|access-date=November 27, 2015}}</ref> Katya also features in [[Trixie Mattel]]'s album ''[[Homemade Christmas]]'' in the song "The Night Before Contact".<ref name="Lopez">{{cite news |last1=Lopez |first1=David |title=Trixie Mattel Serves Up A 'Homemade Christmas' with Holiday Tracks |url=http://instinctmagazine.com/post/trixie-mattel-serves-homemade-christmas-holiday-tracks |access-date=January 27, 2019 |publisher=Instinct Magazine |date=December 1, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180317231953/http://instinctmagazine.com/post/trixie-mattel-serves-homemade-christmas-holiday-tracks |archive-date=March 17, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Along with fellow ''Drag Race'' contestant Trixie Mattel, Katya co-hosted a web series on the World of Wonder YouTube channel called ''[[UNHhhh]]'',<ref name="newnownext.com">{{cite web|title=Trixie Mattel And Katya Are A Laugh Riot In Their New Web Series, "UNHhhh"|url=http://www.newnownext.com/trixie-mattel-katya-series/03/2016/|work=[[Next Magazine (New York City)|Next]]|access-date=July 3, 2016|date=March 25, 2016}}</ref> which debuted in March 2016. On August 21, 2017, it was announced that Katya and Mattel would have their own show on [[Viceland]], titled ''[[The Trixie & Katya Show]]'', which premiered on November 15, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vulture.com/2017/08/trixie-mattel-and-katya-are-getting-their-own-show-honey.html|title=RuPaul Contestants Trixie Mattel and Katya Are Getting Their Own Show, Honey|first=Jackson|last=McHenry|website=Vulture.com|date=August 18, 2017|access-date=October 9, 2018}}</ref>

Katya had a role in the 2018 film ''[[Hurricane Bianca 2: From Russia with Hate|Hurricane Bianca 2: From Russia With Hate]]'', directed by Matt Kugelman and starring [[Bianca Del Rio|Bianca del Rio]] in the lead role. In 2018, Katya co-hosted a podcast called ''Whimsically Volatile'' with Craig MacNeil.<ref name="bbwv">{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8256890/katya-hiatus-rehab-drag-race-podcast|title='Drag Race' Star Katya Reveals She Attended Rehab During Hiatus on New Podcast 'Whimsically Volatile'|last=Daw|first=Stephen|date=March 21, 2018|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 31, 2018|publisher=Lynne Segall}}</ref> In November 2019, it was announced on the podcast that Katya will be leaving the show.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://whimsicallyvolatile.libsyn.com/70-news|title=Whimsically Volatile Ep. 70: News!|date=November 9, 2019|access-date=November 14, 2019}}</ref> In the fall of 2018, Katya and Mattel revitalized their web series ''UNHhhh'' on WOWPresents Plus as well as YouTube due to the overall popularity and success of the show.

In the spring 2019, Katya announced her touring comedy show, "Help Me, I'm Dying". The tour was filmed for a comedy special of the same name that aired on [[OutTV]] in Canada in October 2019.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=http://www.newnownext.com/drag-race-comedy-specials-out-tv-trixie-mattel/07/2019/|title="Drag Race" Alums Return to TV in New Documentary, Comedy Specials|last=Rudolph|first=Christopher|publisher=NewNowNext|date=July 24, 2019|access-date=October 1, 2019}}</ref> It was available [[video on demand|on demand]] in February 2020 and aired on [[Logo TV]] on March 4, 2020.<ref name="Rudolph">{{cite web|url=http://www.newnownext.com/drag-race-comedy-specials-logo/02/2020/|title="Drag Race" Legends Laugh It Up on Logo With New Comedy Specials|last=Rudolph|first=Christopher|website=NewNowNext|date=February 26, 2020|access-date=March 3, 2020}}</ref> In November 2019, Katya and Mattel cohosted a new show on the Netflix YouTube channel called ''[[I Like to Watch]]'' where they react to various shows on Netflix.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.out.com/television/2019/11/21/trixie-and-katya-just-launched-show-netflix|title=Trixie and Katya Just Launched a New Show for Netflix|date=November 21, 2019|website=www.out.com}}</ref>

She released her first book, ''[[Trixie and Katya's Guide to Modern Womanhood]]'', on July 14, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/616908/trixie-and-katyas-guide-to-modern-womanhood-by-trixie-mattel-and-katya/|title=Trixie and Katya's Guide to Modern Womanhood By TRIXIE MATTEL and KATYA |website=penguinrandomhouse.com|access-date=July 16, 2020}}</ref> Published by Plume Books, it was co-written with fellow ''Drag Race'' contestant Mattel, debuting at number two on [[The New York Times Best Seller list]] for "Advice, How-To, and Miscellaneous".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/advice-how-to-and-miscellaneous/|title=Advice, How-To & Miscellaneous|newspaper=The New York Times|access-date=July 22, 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, Katya and co-host Trixie Mattel launched the weekly podcast "The Bald and The Beautiful."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Studio71 to launch The Bald and the Beautiful podcast with Trixie Mattel & Katya Zamo|url=https://redarrowstudios.com/studio71-to-launch-the-bald-and-the-beautiful-podcast-with-trixie-mattel-katya-zamo/|access-date=2020-10-09|website=Red Arrow Studios|language=en-GB}}</ref> In October Katya confirmed she would release her debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[Vampire Fitness]]'' on November 13.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/katya_zamo/status/1317186800922750976?ref_src=twsrc%5Egoogle%7Ctwcamp%5Eserp%7Ctwgr%5Etweet|title=Katya on Twitter: "A musical from my mouth to your ears! Pre-save now! Do it!"|website=Twitter|access-date=October 16, 2020}}</ref> In December, it was announced that Katya and Mattel would co-host the [[10th Streamy Awards]] in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/youtube-streamy-awards-trixie-mattel-katya-1234845280/|title=YouTube Streamy Awards 2020 to Be Hosted by 'RuPaul's Drag Race' Alums Trixie Mattel and Katya|last=Spangler|first=Todd|work=Variety|date=December 3, 2020|access-date=December 8, 2020}}</ref>

In August 2021, Katya and Mattel launched an advice newsletter, ''Gooped''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff|first=Queer Forty|date=2021-08-20|title=Trixie & Katya announce GOOPED newsletter on Substack|url=https://queerforty.com/trixie-katya-announce-gooped-newsletter-on-substack|access-date=2021-08-21|website=Queer Forty|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In January 2018, McCook announced a hiatus from drag for mental health reasons, and his comedy tour as {{lang|ru-Latn|Katya|italic=no}}, "Help Me, I'm Dying", was postponed until spring 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.pinknews.co.uk/2018/01/16/rupauls-drag-race-star-katya-to-take-a-break-from-drag-after-opening-up-about-mental-health/|title=RuPaul's Drag Race star Katya to take a break from drag after opening up about mental health|work=PinkNews|access-date=February 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gaytimes.co.uk/culture/97142/read-katyas-heartfelt-message-fans-cancelling-tour-dates/|title=Read Katya's heartfelt message to fans after postponing tour dates|date=January 18, 2018|work=Gay Times|access-date=February 8, 2018}}</ref> Returning to Twitter in March 2018, McCook announced that the tour would likely be renamed due to his recovery.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/katya_zamo/status/975747099781251072|title=Katya on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=March 24, 2018}}</ref>

In the first episode of ''Whimsically Volatile'' in March 2018, McCook discussed the hiatus in detail. He described suffering a [[psychotic break]] after a [[methamphetamine]] relapse and briefly returning to live with his family in Massachusetts before entering a rehabilitation center in Arizona.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://metro.co.uk/2018/03/23/drag-race-star-katya-complete-psychotic-break-reality-crystal-meth-7411267/|title=Drag Race star Katya had 'complete psychotic break from reality' on crystal meth|last=Kelly|first=Emma|date=March 23, 2018}}</ref>
