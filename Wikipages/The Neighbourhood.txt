'''The Neighbourhood''' (sometimes rendered as "'''THE NBHD'''") is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band formed in [[Newbury Park, California]], in 2011. The band is composed of vocalist [[Jesse James Rutherford|Jesse Rutherford]], guitarists Jeremy Freedman and Zach Abels, bassist Mikey Margott, and drummer Brandon Alexander Fried. After releasing two EPs, ''[[I'm Sorry...]]'' and ''Thank You'', The Neighbourhood released its first full-length album ''[[I Love You. (The Neighbourhood album)|I Love You.]]'' on April 23, 2013, via [[Columbia Records]].<ref>{{cite web| author=Gigwise| title=The Neighbourhood announce details of debut album, I Love You|url=http://www.gigwise.com/news/79992/the-neighbourhood-announce-details-of-debut-album-i-love-you | access-date=March 26, 2013}}</ref> The same year, the EP "The Love Collection" was released; and in November 2014, a [[mixtape]] titled ''#000000 & #FFFFFF''. A second album, ''[[Wiped Out!]]'' was released on October 30, 2015. On March 9, 2018, a [[The Neighbourhood (album)|self-titled]] third studio album came out, preceded by the release of two EPs: ''Hard'' on September 22, 2017, which briefly charted on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and ''To Imagine'' on January 12, 2018. After the release of the album, the tracks from the extended plays not included on the final tracklisting were collected in another EP, ''Hard to Imagine''. All song names use the British spelling of words hence the name of the band.

==History==
===Formation and ''I Love You.'' (2011‚Äì2013)===
The Neighbourhood was formed in August 2011 by singer Jesse Rutherford, guitarists Zachary Abels and Jeremiah Freedman, bass guitarist Michael Margott and drummer Bryan "Olivver" Sammis. The members of The Neighbourhood chose the [[American and British English spelling differences|British spelling]] of "neighbourhood" on the advice of their manager, in order to distinguish themselves from a band already using the American spelling.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/the-neighbourhood-announce-2018-tour-dates-127344|title=The Neighbourhood announce 2018 tour dates|work=[[AXS (company)|AXS]]|author=Kareem Gantt|date=January 18, 2018}}</ref>

In early 2012, The Neighbourhood released "[[Female Robbery]]" and "[[Sweater Weather]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://schedule.sxsw.com/2013/events/event_MS21560 |title=The Neighbourhood - Schedule|work=[[South by Southwest|SXSW]]|access-date=2013-08-09}}</ref> In May 2012, the band unveiled their debut self-released [[extended play]] titled ''I'm Sorry...'' for free [[Music download|download]], produced by Justyn Pilbrow.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/2012/05/10/the-neighbourhood-im-sorry-ep/|title=The Neighbourhood ‚Äì I'm Sorry‚Ä¶ [EP]|work=EARMILK|author=Jake Blair|date=May 10, 2012}}</ref> In December 2012, The Neighbourhood released their second EP, ''Thank You,''.<ref name="ThankYouEP">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/thank-you-single/584841510|title=Thank You, - Single by The Neighbourhood|work=[[Apple Music]], [[iTunes]] (US)|date=January 14, 2013}}</ref>

The band performed at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] in 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1557381/backbeat-the-neighbourhood-talks-new-album-backstage-at-coachella-2013|title=Backbeat: The Neighbourhood Talks New Album Backstage at Coachella 2013|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|author=Nicole Pajer|date=April 12, 2013}}</ref> anticipating their debut album ''I Love You.'', which was premiered on April 16, 2013, via [[Rolling Stone]]<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/the-neighbourhood-reach-for-the-stratosphere-on-i-love-you-album-premiere-20130416 |title=The Neighbourhood Reach for the Stratosphere on 'I Love You' - Album Premiere|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=April 16, 2013}}</ref> and officially released on April 23, 2013, via [[Columbia Records]]. It debuted at number 39 on the US [[Billboard 200]] albums chart, selling 9,000 units in its first week.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1560539/chart-moves-the-neighbourhood-debuts-van-halens-1984-returns-after-20-plus |title=Chart Moves: The Neighbourhood Debuts, Van Halen's '1984' Returns After 20-Plus Years|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> It was preceded by the lead single "[[Sweater Weather]]", which music video was released on March 5, 2013.<ref>{{cite web| author=Alternative Press| title=The Neighbourhood release "Sweater Weather" video| url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_neighbourhood_release_sweater_weather_video| access-date=March 26, 2013| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151117015334/http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_neighbourhood_release_sweater_weather_video| archive-date=November 17, 2015| url-status=dead| df=mdy-all}}</ref> They made their first televised performance of the song on June 27, 2013, at ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://pigeonsandplanes.com/news/2013/06/the-neighbourhood-jimmy-kimmel |title= Watch The Neighbourhood's Late Night Television Debut |work=[[Complex (magazine)#Complex|Pigeons and Planes]] |date= June 28, 2013}}</ref> "Sweater Weather" topped the charts in early June 2013, reaching number one on ''Billboard'' [[Alternative Songs]] chart<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/1559249/the+neighbourhood/chart?f=377|title=The Neighbourhood : Chart History|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2013-08-09}}</ref> and breaking the top ten on ''Billboard'' [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/1559249/the+neighbourhood/chart?f=887|title=The Neighbourhood : Chart History|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2013-08-09}}</ref>

The band went on tour opening for [[Imagine Dragons]] in July and September 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.redbull.com/us-en/the-nbhd-tour-dates|title=Tour Dates: The Neighbourhood Is On The Road|work=[[Red Bull GmbH]]|author=Elliott Sharp|date=July 1, 2013}}</ref> The Neighbourhood also made two appearances in Eastern Canada during the summer of 2013. They played as one of the headliners of Toronto's [[Edgefest]] on July 31, 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nationalpost.com/entertainment/music/edgefest-2013-lineup-includes-the-lumineers-and-band-of-horses-headlining-toronto-festival|title=Edgefest 2013 lineup includes The Lumineers and Band of Horses headlining Toronto festival|work=[[National Post]]|author=Lilian Asante|date=April 8, 2013}}</ref> and then a few days later played at Canada's largest music festival, [[Osheaga]], in Montreal on August 4.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2013/05/osheaga-2013-lineup-montreal-festival/|title=Beck, Frank Ocean, the Breeders Join Osheaga Festival's 2013 Lineup|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|author=Kyle McGovern|date=May 8, 2013}}</ref> The Neighbourhood performed at Atlanta's [[Music Midtown]] on September 21, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.midtownatl.com/do/music-midtown|title=Music Midtown - Midtown Alliance - Atlanta, GA|publisher=[[Midtown Atlanta]]|access-date=2018-03-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180311081208/https://www.midtownatl.com/do/music-midtown|archive-date=2018-03-11|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===''#000000 & #FFFFFF''  and ''Wiped Out!'' (2014‚Äì2015)===
On January 16, 2014, the band revealed via social media that drummer Bryan Sammis was leaving the band.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://idobi.com/news/the-neighbourhood-part-ways-with-drummer/| title=The Neighbourhood part ways with drummer|work=[[idobi Radio]]|author=Alexa Gallo|date=January 16, 2014}}</ref>

In April 2013, The Neighbourhood announced their summer 2013 tour called The Love Collection Tour along with Lovelife, [[the 1975]], and [[JMSN]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://idobi.com/news/the-neighbourhood-embark-on-the-love-collection-tour/|title=The Neighbourhood embark on "The Love Collection" tour|work=idobi Radio|author=Tarynn Law|date=April 24, 2013}}</ref> as well as that they were planning the release of a [[mixtape]].<ref name="LurkPremiere">{{cite web |url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/2014/04/the-neighbourhood-lurk-video-mixtape/ |title= Watch The Neighbourhood's "Lurk" Video, and Check Out Their Plans For Rock's First Real Mixtape|work=Pigeons and Planes |date= April 21, 2014}}</ref> On December 10, 2013, they released a new EP called ''The Love Collection''.<ref name="TheLoveCollectionEP">{{cite web |url= https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-love-collection-single/766049325|title=The Love Collection - Single by The Neighbourhood|work=Apple Music, iTunes (US) |date= December 10, 2013}}</ref> On November 28, 2014, the band finally released the project, titled ''#000000 & #FFFFFF'' (the [[Web colors#Hex triplet|hexadecimal color codes]] for black and white, respectively) for free.<ref>{{cite web|date=November 28, 2014|title=Download The Neighbourhood's #000000 & #FFFFFF Mixtape|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2014/11/download-the-neighbourhoods-000000-ffffff-mixtape|access-date=October 17, 2021|work=Complex}}</ref> It was hosted by [[DJ Drama]] and features guest appearances by [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Dej Loaf]], [[French Montana]], [[Danny Brown (rapper)|Danny Brown]], [[G-Eazy]] and others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/the-neighborhood-000000-and-ffffff-new-mixtape.115603.html|title=#000000 & #FFFFFF|work=HotNewHipHop|author=Trevor Smith|date=November 28, 2014}}</ref> The mixtape was officially released for streaming and download on November 22, 2017.<ref name="000000andFFFFFF">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/000000-ffffff/1316105951|title=#000000 & #FFFFFF by The Neighbourhood|work=Apple Music, iTunes (US)|date=November 28, 2014}}</ref>

In August 2015, the band announced the upcoming October 30 release of their second album ''[[Wiped Out!]]''. It was preceded by the lead single "[[R.I.P. 2 My Youth]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/the-neighbourhood/news/the-neighbourhood-drop-cheery-rip-2-my-youth-video|title=The Neighbourhood Drop Cheery 'R.I.P. 2 My Youth' Video|work=[[MTV UK]]|author=Georgina Langford|date=September 17, 2015}}</ref> and reached the number 13 position on the US Billboard 200.<ref name="Billboard200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-neighbourhood/chart-history/billboard-200|title=The Neighbourhood Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> The band embarked on a European tour in November 2015<ref>{{cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2015/10/30/the-neighbourhood-talk-growing-up-and-second-album-wiped-out|title=The Neighbourhood talk growing up, and second album 'Wiped Out!'|work=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|author=Natasha West|date=October 30, 2015}}</ref> and on an American tour in May and June 2016 to support the album.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Neighbourhood Announce Summer 2016 Tour Dates|url=http://music.mxdwn.com/2016/02/09/news/the-neighbourhood-announce-summer-2016-tour-dates/|website=mxdwn|author=Julia Naman|date=February 16, 2016}}</ref>

=== ''Hard to Imagine'', self-titled album, and ''Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones'' (2017‚Äìpresent) ===
On September 21, 2017, The Neighbourhood released the EP ''Hard''<ref name="HardEP">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7973750/the-neighbourhood-hard-surprise-ep-listen|title=The Neighbourhood Drops Sinister (And Seductive) Surprise EP 'Hard': Listen|work=Billboard |author=Sadie Bell|date=September 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="HardEPiTunes">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/hard-ep/id1285207093 |title=Hard ‚Äì EP by the Neighbourhood |work=Apple Store, iTunes (US)|access-date=September 22, 2017}}</ref> which reached the number 183 position on the US Billboard 200 chart.<ref name="Billboard200"/> Another EP called ''To Imagine'' was released on January 12, 2018.<ref name="ToImagineEP">{{cite web|url=https://www.theodysseyonline.com/to-imagine-ep|title=The Neighbourhood Adds Some Color With "To Imagine EP"|work=[[Odyssey (publication)|Odyssey]]|author=Jonathan C. S. Pettyjohn|date=January 12, 2018|access-date=March 10, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180623170233/https://www.theodysseyonline.com/to-imagine-ep|archive-date=June 23, 2018|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name="ToImagineEPiTunes">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/to-imagine-ep/1331640088|title=To Imagine ‚Äì EP by the Neighbourhood |work=Apple Store, iTunes (US)|access-date=January 12, 2018}}</ref> The band later announced their self-titled third studio album ''[[The Neighbourhood (album)|The Neighbourhood]],'' released March 9, 2018, which included tracks from the previous extended plays, including the lead single: "Scary Love".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-neighbourhood/1347353698|title=The Neighbourhood by The Neighbourhood|work=Apple Music, iTunes (US)|date=March 9, 2018}}</ref> After the release, the tracks from the extended plays that weren't included on the final tracklisting were collected in another EP called ''Hard to Imagine''.<ref name="HardToImagineEP">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/hard-to-imagine-ep/1355215517|title=Hard to imagine - EP by The Neighbourhood|work=Apple Music, iTunes (US)|date=March 9, 2018}}</ref> The band then released the complete edition of the album, titled ''Hard To Imagine The Neighbourhood Ever Changing'' which featured all songs released from ''Hard'', ''To Imagine'', ''The Neighbourhood'', and ''Ever Changing'', except the two tracks "Revenge" and "Too Serious". 

On August 16, 2019, they premiered a music video along with their single, "Middle of Somewhere".<ref>{{Citation|title=The Neighbourhood - Middle of Somewhere|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6tU52kEKuq0|language=en|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref> On October 10, 2019, The Neighbourhood's single, "Yellow Box" was released and featured on an action game, [[Death Stranding]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variancemagazine.com/sounds/10631-the-neighbourhood-yellow-box|title=Hear The Neighbourhood's haunting new track 'Yellow Box'|last=Schmitt|first=Tyler|website=Variance Magazine|language=en-gb|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref> They released a lyric video for "Yellow Box" on November 7, 2019.<ref>{{Citation|title=The Neighbourhood - Yellow Box (Lyric Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QiATQ25E5nk|language=en|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref>

On July 31, 2020, the band announced the September 25 release of their fourth album ''[[Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones]]''. It was preceded by a single "Cherry Flavoured", which they released in a playlist named after the album via various streaming services. The playlist is a compilation of the band's essential songs, plus the single. In description of the playlist was, quote: "Album out 9/25".

In late 2020, their songs "[[Sweater Weather]]", from their album, "[[I Love You.]]" and "Daddy Issues" from their album, ''[[Wiped Out!]]'', went viral on [[TikTok]], garnering new streaming highs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9477079/neighbourhood-sweater-weather-daddy-issues-streaming-gain|title=The Neighbourhood Score Best On-Demand Streaming Weeks Yet For 'Sweater Weather' & 'Daddy Issues'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Unterberger|first=Andrew|date=November 2, 2020|accessdate=January 26, 2021}}</ref>

==Musical style==
The Neighbourhood's musical style has been described as [[alternative rock]],<ref name="AllMusic Genres">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-neighbourhood-mn0002690553/biography|title=The Neighbourhood &#124; Biography & History|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=26 September 2020}}</ref><ref name="Sptnik Genres">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/bands/The-Neighbourhood/42815/|title=The Neighbourhood Sputnik|access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref> [[indie rock]],<ref name="AllMusic Genres"/><ref name="Sptnik Genres"/> [[indie pop]],<ref name="Sptnik Genres"/> [[pop rock]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://sites.bu.edu/wtbu/2018/10/10/review-the-neighbourhood-house-of-blues-10-02/|title=Review: The Neighbourhood @ House of Blues 10/02|author=Christian Jaeger|access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref> with elements of [[Electronic music|electronic]], [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Pop music|pop]], and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]].<ref name="AllMusic Genres"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/the-neighbourhood-the-neighbourhood/|title=The Neighbourhood The Neighbourhood|author=Ian Cohen|date=16 March 2018|access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref>
