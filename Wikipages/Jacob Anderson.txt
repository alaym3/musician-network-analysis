'''Jacob Basil Anderson''' (born 18 June 1990) is an English actor and musician. As an actor, he is known for his role as [[Grey Worm]] in the television series ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', and his recurring appearances in the first seasons of ''[[Episodes (TV series)|Episodes]]'' and ''[[Broadchurch]]''.<ref name="anderson_game_thrones">{{cite web |last1=Bustos |first1=Kristina |title=''Game of Thrones'' casts Ed Skrein, Jacob Anderson |url=http://www.digitalspy.com/tv/s151/game-of-thrones/news/a400671/game-of-thrones-casts-ed-skrein-jacob-anderson.html#~oGmEMgzv58eNsM |website=[[Digital Spy]] |date=20 August 2012 |publisher=Hearst Magazines UK |access-date=6 June 2014 }}</ref> As a musician, he uses the alias '''Raleigh Ritchie''' (named after his favourite characters from ''[[The Royal Tenenbaums]]''); his debut album, ''[[You're a Man Now, Boy]]'', was a [[soul music|soul]] and [[trip hop]] album released in 2016 to positive reviews. Anderson's second studio album, ''[[Andy (Raleigh Ritchie album)|Andy]]'', was released in 2020.

==Early life==
Anderson was born and raised in [[Bristol, England]]. His father is of [[Afro-Caribbean]] descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/mar/04/raleigh-ritchie-my-family-values |title=Raleigh Ritchie: my family values |work=[[The Guardian]] |first=Angela |last=Wintle |date=4 March 2017 |access-date=23 July 2017}}</ref> At the age of 17, Anderson moved to [[London]] in order to launch his music career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/raleigh-ritchie-game-of-thrones-star-jacob-anderson-talks-about-balancing-his-acting-and-music-careers-10006691.html|title=Raleigh Ritchie: Game of Thrones star Jacob Anderson talks about|first=Neela |last=Debnath|date=17 February 2015|work=The Independent}}</ref>

==Acting career==
Anderson played Angelo in the film ''[[4.3.2.1.]]'' He starred in the [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] drama series ''[[Injustice (British TV series)|Injustice]]'' as Simon, a teenager in a [[Her Majesty's Young Offender Institution|youth offender institution]]. He also had a one-episode guest role on ''[[Outnumbered (UK TV series)|Outnumbered]]''. He also appeared in an episode of ''[[Skins (UK TV series)|Skins]]'', and appeared in the feature film ''[[Comedown (film)|Comedown]]'', co-starring ''[[Adulthood (film)|Adulthood]]''{{'}}s [[Adam Deacon]] and directed by ''[[Kidulthood]]'' director [[Menhaj Huda]]. Anderson featured in various [[Play (theatre)|plays]], including ''[[Dunsinane (play)|Dunsinane]]'' for the [[Royal Shakespeare Company|RSC]], ''[[King Lear]]'' (with [[Pete Postlethwaite]]) at the [[Young Vic|Young Vic Theatre]], and ''[[War Horse (play)|War Horse]]'' at the [[Royal National Theatre|National Theatre]]. He was seen in the teen slasher film ''[[Demons Never Die]]'', playing Ricky, and appeared in the [[BBC Two]] comedy ''[[Episodes (TV series)|Episodes]]'', alongside [[Matt LeBlanc]] and [[Stephen Mangan]].

In 2012, Anderson was cast for the [[Game of Thrones (season 3)|third season]] of [[HBO]]'s television series ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' as Grey Worm, the leader of [[Daenerys Targaryen]]'s Unsullied ==Music career==
===2005‚Äì2009: Early career===
In 2006, Anderson was featured as a vocalist on [[Typesun]]'s "[[Typesun#The PL|The PL]]". They released another track in 2007, titled "Let Me Know." Anderson recorded a song with [[Plan B (musician)|Plan B]], which was featured on the ''[[Adulthood (film)|Adulthood]]'' soundtrack, titled "[[Plan B (musician)|Plan B]]".
[[File:Jacob Anderson (48427303962).jpg|alt=|thumb|Anderson in 2019]]

===2010‚Äì2017: ''You're a Man Now, Boy''===
In 2013, Anderson signed to [[Columbia Records]], and released a free three-track EP, ''The Middle Child'', under the stage name Raleigh Ritchie. A second EP, ''Black and Blue'', followed in 2014.

Anderson's musical influences include [[Erykah Badu]], [[David Bowie]], [[Jill Scott]], [[The Smiths]], and [[Stevie Wonder]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/25851579 |title=Introducing... Raleigh Ritchie |work=[[Newsbeat]] |date=23 January 2014 |access-date=7 March 2015 }}</ref> In March 2014, his track "[[Stronger Than Ever (Raleigh Ritchie song)|Stronger Than Ever]]" from ''Black and Blue'' was remixed by [[UK garage]] producer [[MJ Cole]] and released as a standalone single. "Stronger Than Ever" has been used in television adverts promoting the launch of [[ITV Encore]], as well as in montage videos during [[Sky Sports]]' coverage of [[Professional Darts Corporation]] events. The song reached number 30 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in June 2014.<ref name="OCC">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/31325/raleigh-ritchie/|title=Raleigh Ritchie - Full Official Chart History|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=14 July 2021}}</ref> Anderson supported [[George Ezra]] on his February 2015 UK tour.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=george_ezra|first=George|last=Ezra|number=559757798246842369|date=27 January 2015|title=Announcement time: My main man @RaleighRitchie will be supporting me on my UK tour this Feb!|access-date=14 July 2021}}</ref>

His debut album, ''[[You're a Man Now, Boy]]'', was released on 26 February 2016; it charted at number 32 on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name="OCC"/> He also featured on Stormzy's debut album ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]'' track 13 ‚Äì "Don't Cry For Me" which was released on 24 February 2017.

=== 2020‚Äìpresent: ''Andy'' ===
On 6 May 2020, Anderson released a new single called "Aristocrats".<ref>{{Cite web |title=06.05.20 (or 05.06.20 to my friends in US) |url=https://twitter.com/RaleighRitchie/status/1256224644740374534 |last=Ritchie |first=Raleigh |date=1 May 2020 |website=@RaleighRitchie |language=en |access-date=4 May 2020 }}</ref> It was the second single to promote Anderson's second studio album ''[[Andy (Raleigh Ritchie album)|Andy]]'', after a 2018 single "Time In A Tree" also included on the record. In June, "Aristocrats" was followed by the songs "Party Fear" and "Squares". ''Andy'' was released on 26 June 2020 via Alacran Records.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/andy/1508175685 |website=Apple Music |title=Andy by Raleigh Ritchie |date=26 June 2020 |access-date=29 June 2020}}</ref>

On 30 July 2021, Anderson released the single Say What You Mean.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://music.apple.com/gb/album/say-what-you-mean-single/1575591984|title=Raleigh Ritchie Say What You Mean (single)|date=30 July 2021|access-date=26 October 2021}}</ref> Anderson commented,  ‚ÄúI write songs for myself, to get things off my chest and process my emotions, but then I release them and I don‚Äôt own them anymore [‚Ä¶] I hope there is someone who listens to it and [‚Ä¶] I hope that makes them feel less lonely.‚Äù <ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.clashmusic.com/news/raleigh-ritchie-shares-new-single-say-what-you-mean|title= Raleigh Ritchie Shares New Single 'Say What You Mean' |website=Clash|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=30 July 2021|access-date=26 October 2021}}</ref>
