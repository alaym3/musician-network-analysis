'''Little Brother''' is an [[United States|American]] hip hop duo from [[Durham, North Carolina|Durham]], [[North Carolina]] that consists of [[Rapping|rappers]] [[Phonte]] and [[Big Pooh]]. [[Disc jockey|DJ]] and [[Hip hop production|Producer]] [[9th Wonder]] was an original member of the group but departed in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Strauss |first1=Matthew |title=Little Brother Release First New Album in 9 Years |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/little-brother-release-first-new-album-in-9-years-listen/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref> The group has produced five acclaimed studio albums and six [[mixtape]]s.

Little Brother is highly regarded among hip hop fans and critics.<ref name="Leftback Review">{{cite web | last = Rinaldi | first = Matt | url = {{Allmusic|class=album|id=leftback-r1742551/review|pure_url=yes}} | title = ''Leftback'' Review | work = Rinaldi, Matt | publisher = AllMusic.com | access-date = 13 October 2010}}</ref>

==History==

===Early career and ''The Listening'' album===
The individual members of the group, rappers [[Phonte]] (Phonte Coleman), and [[Big Pooh|Rapper Big Pooh]] (Thomas Jones), and DJ/producer [[9th Wonder]] (Pat Douthit), met in 1998 while enrolled as college students at [[North Carolina Central University]] in [[Durham, North Carolina]]. The group began as a trio. Little Brother were members of the North Carolina-based alternative hip hop [[Musical collective|collective]], [[Justus League]].<ref name="Little Brother Biography">{{cite web | last = Huey | first = Steve | url = {{Allmusic|class=artist|id=little-brother-p556916/biography|pure_url=yes}} | title = Little Brother Biography | work = Huey, Steve | publisher = AllMusic.com | access-date = 13 October 2010}}</ref>

In a February 2003 interview with ''MVRemix.com'', Phonte explained the origins of the group's name:

{{quote|[[A Tribe Called Quest|Tribe]], [[De La Soul|De La]], [[Public Enemy (group)|Public Enemy]] ... were like our big brothers in the game so now we are the little brothers of that movement ... carrying on the tradition of good music.<ref name="MVRemix Urban Interviews Phonte of Little Brother">{{cite web | last = Phayde | url = http://www.mvremix.com/urban/interviews/littlebrother.shtml | title = MVRemix Urban Interviews Phonte of Little Brother | work = Phayde | publisher = mvremix.com | access-date =1 February 2003}}</ref>}}

The official debut for Little Brother came in August 2001 with their first recording, "Speed".<ref name="Little Brother Biography"/>

According to 9th Wonder in an interview with Complex, another rapper Median was actually supposed to be on the track before they got a hold of Big Pooh:

{{quote|The Listening was done in Missie Ann. Cesar Comanche, who I met when I was attending North Carolina State University, lived not far from the school. He stayed in a two-bedroom apartment, and that's basically our studio Missie Ann. [Comanche] named it after his sister. And we pretty much did that album in Comanche's other room besides the bathroom. Every song on The Listening has a story to it, which is crazy. We started recording The Listening in August of 2001, which will make this August the ten-year anniversary when we started Little Brother. So the story goes like this. At the time Phonte was doing his 9 to 5 at Blue Cross Blue Shield Association, the insurance company, and he would come into the studio, and then going back to his 9 to 5, sometimes without even sleeping. That was a repeating fashion. One day, Phonte and I were doing a song called 'Speed.' Phonte did his part, and we took 'Take A Look' by Joc Max and Grap Luva, put out by DJ Spinna, which has a part that goes, 'I put my JBs on when I hustle with speed,' and I really loved that part, so I put that in there. We asked Median to be on it. He said he was down to do it, but on the weekend we wanted to record it, we couldn't find Median. Pooh, who was originally from Virginia, was in town at the time with his friends in Charlotte, North Carolina. Matter of fact, I think around March or April of 2001, Pooh asked Phonte to be a group, and Phonte said, 'No.' But Median wasn't around, so we were like, 'Let's just ask Pooh, he's in town.' So Pooh came in, and recorded the song, and we listened to the song like 18 times, and I looked at Phonte afterward and said, 'Let's try to be a group, man. Let's see what it sounds like.' Median could have been a member of Little Brother. 'Speed' was the first song we recorded as a group, because Median didn't show up. But we thought it sounded good, so we just said, 'Let's be a group.' And that's the first song in Little Brother's existence.}}

They continued to work the local, [[Research Triangle|Raleigh-Durham]]-area scene and were eventually signed by [[independent record label]] ABB Records. In 2002, they released the [[Cult following|cult hit]] 7" single "Atari 2600", with lyrics centering on [[video game]]s.

In 2003, the group released their first full-length studio album, ''[[The Listening (Little Brother album)|The Listening]]'' to critical acclaim. National recognition for the group, and particularly 9th Wonder, came when [[Jay-Z]] tapped 9th Wonder for the song "Threat", from his eighth studio album ''[[The Black Album (Jay-Z album)|The Black Album]]''.

Following the release of ''The Listening'', Little Brother opened for [[Oakland, California|Oakland]]-based [[alternative hip hop]] collective [[Hieroglyphics (group)|Hieroglyphics]] on the latter's ''[[Full Circle (Hieroglyphics album)|Full Circle]]'' national tour.

Little Brother's first commercially distributed "mixtape", which featured the song by  [[Consequence (rapper)|Consequence]] titled I See Now featuring [[Kanye West]], off of the album Take 'em To The Cleaners. Released on June 21, 2005 ahead of their sophomore album ''[[The Minstrel Show]]''.

===''The Minstrel Show'' album===
Little Brother's second album, ''[[The Minstrel Show]]'', released in September 2005, saw increased success for the group due to their raised popularity and praise for the album from critics. The album's theme compared present day rap music and the music industry to the [[minstrel shows]] of the late 19th century. A music video for the single, "Lovin' It" featuring Joe Scudda, was directed by Joey Boukadakis.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/25528219|title=Little Brother - "Lovin' It"|website=Vimeo|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref>

In 2005, Little Brother became embroiled in a short-lived feud, when [[Young Jeezy]]'s protÃ©gÃ© Slick Pulla challenged the group to a [[Battle rap|battle]] after hearing an interview in which Phonte voiced some disapproval concerning "drug rap" and Young Jeezy's "snowman mascot". Little Brother did not respond, and instead, Phonte called Slick Pulla and the pair resolved the misunderstanding.{{Citation needed|date=March 2009}}

===''Getback'' album and 9th Wonder's departure===
In late 2006, Little Brother began recording their third album, and second for [[Atlantic Records]], ''[[Getback]]''. However, in January 2007, the group announced both their departure from Atlantic and that producer [[9th Wonder]] had left the group.<ref name="9th Wonder and Little Brother Part Ways; Rapper Pooh Clears Up Little Brother Rumors">{{cite web | last = Kayser | first = Brian | url = http://hiphopgame.ihiphop.com/news.php3?id=1510 | title = 9th Wonder and Little Brother Part Ways; Rapper Pooh Clears Up Little Brother Rumors | work = Kayser, Brian | publisher = HipHopGame.com | access-date = 27 January 2007}}</ref>

The move from Atlantic was due to the commercial failure of ''[[The Minstrel Show]]'', and creative differences over the group's future material. [[Rapper Big Pooh]] said:

{{quote|We as a group just felt that it was not in our best interest to remain in a situation where our needs were constantly being overlooked. We didn't have an [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] for ''The Minstrel Show''. We don't have any type of relationship with our current A&R. There are just a lot of internal issues that ended up working against us.<ref name="9th Wonder and Little Brother Part Ways; Rapper Pooh Clears Up Little Brother Rumors"/>}}

The group's [[Talent manager|manager]] Big Dho added that there were no "ill feelings towards their (Atlantic) company."<ref name="9th Wonder and Little Brother Part Ways; Rapper Pooh Clears Up Little Brother Rumors"/>

The group's reason for the break from Atlantic and for 9th Wonder's departure was ultimately due to creative differences, but in both cases, the break and departure were described as amicable.

As to 9th Wonder's departure from the group, Rapper Big Pooh was quoted as saying:

{{quote|Little Brother has decided, in the best interest of the group, for Little Brother and 9th Wonder to part ways. There are no hard feelings and no [[Hip hop feud|beef]]. This is just a decision that had to be made so all three of us could move forward and continue to provide the world with dope music.<ref name="9th Wonder and Little Brother Part Ways; Rapper Pooh Clears Up Little Brother Rumors"/>}}

On October 23, 2007 Little Brother released ''[[Getback]]'', the long-awaited follow-up to ''The Minstrel Show''. With no major video or radio airplay, ''Getback'' sold approximately 9,600 copies during its first week in stores and landed at No. 89 on the ''Billboard 200'' chart. The album consisted of 11 songs, and featured several guests, most notably [[New Orleans]]-based rapper [[Lil Wayne]]. Lil Wayne had been a longtime fan of the group's work and was invited to rap on the third verse for "Breakin My Heart".

Following the release of ''Getback'' in late 2007, Little Brother toured with [[Los Angeles]]-based alternative hip hop artist [[Evidence (musician)|Evidence]] of [[Dilated Peoples]].

The remainder of 2007 found Little Brother working on becoming completely independent artists and putting out records on their own. Big Pooh told ''[[L.A. Record]]'' in an April 2008 interview that "For the next record, we definitely want ownershipâ€”the first record we'll have 100% ownership of."<ref name="Little Brother: I Got Hands That Heal">{{cite web | last = McGarry | first = Joe | url = http://larecord.com/interviews/2008/04/27/little-brother-i-got-hands-that-heal/ | title = Little Brother:  I Got Hands That Heal | work = McGarry, Joe | publisher = L.A. Record | access-date = 27 April 2008}}</ref>

===''...And Justus For All'' mixtape===
Following the release of ''Getback'', in mid-2008, Little Brother officially released their 2007 [[mixtape]] ''...And Justus For All'' with DJ [[Mick (DJ)|Mick Boogie]] taking over production duties for the departed [[9th Wonder]]. The mixtape was released through the Hall of Justus collective with Little Brother controlling the project.

In a June 2008 interview with ''[[HipHopDX]].com'', [[Rapper Big Pooh]] said:

{{quote|We decided to officially release ''...And Justus For All'' for the fans. Since all of our mixtapes are more like actual albums, many people have been requesting us for deejay free versions of these mixtapes. So to all of the fans out there that's been asking for it, you're welcome!<ref name="Little Brother: Brother's Gonna Work It Out">{{cite web | last = Paine | first = Jake | url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.1152/title.little-brother-brothers-gonna-work-it-out | title = Little Brother: Brother's Gonna Work It Out | work = Paine, Jake | publisher = HipHopDX.com | access-date = 24 June 2008}}</ref>}}

''...And Justus For All'' featured 5 new songs as well as enhanced mastered versions of songs on their 2007 mixtape with DJ Mick Boogie. The CD version contained 5 new songs for the album and the [[iTunes]] version had 7 new songs. The iTunes version linked former groupmate 9th Wonder to the group with the single, "Black Light Special".

===Individual member side-projects===
Outside of Little Brother, [[Phonte]] began a collaboration with [[Netherlands]]-based [[Record producer|producer]] [[Nicolay (musician)|Nicolay]] as the group [[The Foreign Exchange]], that ultimately resulted in the release of their debut album, ''[[Connected (The Foreign Exchange album)|Connected]]'', in 2004. The Foreign Exchange released their follow-up album entitled ''[[Leave It All Behind (The Foreign Exchange album)|Leave It All Behind]]'', in October 2008.

[[Rapper Big Pooh]] released a solo album entitled ''[[Sleepers (album)|Sleepers]]'' in 2005. In 2009, he released two albums, ''The Delightful Bars'', and ''Rapper's Delight''. In early 2010, Rapper Big Pooh released ''The Purple Tape'' mixtape as a free [[Music download|download]]. This mixtape featured Big Pooh rhyming over [[Detroit]]-based producer [[Black Milk]]'s instrumentals from a collection that used [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] ''[[Purple Rain (album)|Purple Rain'' album]] [[Sampling (music)|samples]].

[[9th Wonder]] has produced songs for major artists like [[Jay-Z]], [[De La Soul]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]] and [[Destiny's Child]], as well as [[Alternative hip hop|underground]] artists such as [[Sean Price]] and [[Rapsody]]. 9th Wonder has produced entire, full-length studio albums for [[Jean Grae]] (''[[Jeanius]]''), [[Murs (rapper)|Murs]] (''[[Murs 3:16: The 9th Edition]],'' ''[[Murray's Revenge]], [[Sweet Lord]]'' and ''[[The Final Adventure]],'' just to name a few), [[Buckshot (rapper)|Buckshot]] (''[[Chemistry (Buckshot and 9th Wonder album)|Chemistry]]'' and ''[[The Formula (album)|The Formula]]''), as well as a remix album of [[Nas]]' ''[[God's Son (album)|God's Son]]'' entitled ''[[God's Stepson]]''.

===''Leftback'' album, hiatus, and disbandment===
During a May 2008 interview with ''HipHopGame.com'', [[Rapper Big Pooh]] hinted that he and [[Phonte]] may never record another album together:

{{quote|That's so far in the future. We don't know if we are even gonna do another Little Brother album at this point. We are working on our personal projects right now.<ref name="Rapper Pooh Interview">{{cite web | last = Kayser | first = Brian | url = http://hiphopgame.ihiphop.com/index2.php3?page=rapperpooh | title = Rapper Pooh Interview | work = Kayser, Brian | publisher = HipHopGame.com | access-date = 22 May 2008}}</ref>}}

In June 2009, Phonte announced the forthcoming Little Brother album, ''[[Leftback LP|Leftback]]'', but also announced that the group would subsequently take a [[Black Star (rap duo)|Black Star]]-esque hiatus, wherein he and Big Pooh would continue to collaborate on projects, but that another group album would not be released for a long while.<ref name="Phonte Hints At Little Brother Hiatus">{{cite web | last = Harling | first = Danielle | url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.9245/title.phonte-hints-at-little-brother-hiatus | title = Phonte Hints At Little Brother Hiatus | work = Harling, Danielle | publisher = HipHopDX.com | access-date = 3 June 2009}}</ref>

Shortly before the April 2010 release of what would become Little Brother's final studio album, ''Leftback'', Phonte and [[9th Wonder]] exchanged pointed [[Twitter]] messages regarding Little Brothers' release of a previously unreleased 9th Wonder produced single, "Star", on the ''Leftback'' album.<ref name="Little Brother Wants Fans">{{cite web | last = Jacobs | first = Alan | url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.10906/title.phonte-and-9th-wonder-rehash-the-little-brother-break-up | title = Phonte And 9th Wonder Rehash The Little Brother Break-Up | work = Jacobs, Allen | publisher = HipHopDX.com | access-date = 28 March 2010}}</ref>

Shortly after the release of ''Leftback'' on April 10, 2010, Little Brother formally announced the group's breakup.<ref name="Little Brother Wants Fans 2">{{cite web | last = Harper | first = Rosario | url = http://www.sohh.com/2010/04/little_brother_want_fans_to_understand_b.html | title = Little Brother Wants Fans to Understand Break-Up, 'You Don't Want Your Favorite Group to Force a Relationship' | work = Harper, Rosario | publisher = SOHH.com | access-date = 23 April 2010}}</ref><ref name="Little Brother's Retirement Party">{{cite web | last = Soderberg | first = Brandon | url = http://www.villagevoice.com/2010-04-27/music/little-brother-s-retirement-party/ | title = Little Brother's Retirement Party | work = Soderberg, Brandon | publisher = VillageVoice.com | access-date = 27 April 2010}}</ref> Rapper Big Pooh noted:

{{quote|I was just thinking about our own situation and then I realized, when groups leave, it's just like when a person dies. Every person dies and a baby is born. So, as Little Brother calls it quits, there are other groups to not necessarily take our place but to keep the tradition going ... That's what it's all about -- you don't want your favorite group to force a relationship. Like, you don't want Tribe Called Quest ... If they don't really want to be together, you want them to make another album. If they make an album just because you asked for it, it's not going to be the same Tribe Called Quest you fell in love with. It's going to be something forced.<ref name="Little Brother Wants Fans"/>}}

Phonte added:

{{quote|If you're doing business with a friend, you gotta decide, well, do I end this business relationship and keep my friendship? Or do I continue this business relationship and end up wrecking both?<ref name="Little Brother's Retirement Party"/>}}

===Reunion & ''May The Lord Watch''===
The reunion of Little Brother was, in part, due to the death of [[Phife Dawg]] in 2016. At this time [[Rapper Big Pooh]] and [[Phonte]] had not spoken in 5 years. Pooh sent Phonte a short message of love and appreciation. Phonte followed this text with a phone call where the two were said to have spoken for several hours. This and many followup conversations allowed the duo to rekindle their friendship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://shows.pippa.io/itsthereal/episodes/270-little-brother|title=#270: Little Brother &#124; A Waste Of Time with ItsTheReal|via=shows.pippa.io}}</ref>

[[Phonte]], [[Rapper Big Pooh]] and [[9th Wonder]] reunited on stage together for the first time in eleven years at their hometown in the 2018 Art of Cool Festival. This reunion was the result of [[Royce da 5'9"]] being unable to attend the festival. According to Phonte, he was contacted by the promoters to substitute for Royce and called Pooh and 9th to collaborate with him. The group had not performed together in over a decade, so they performed many of their known hits including "Say it Again," "Loving It", "The Way You Do It," and "Dreams." Both artists felt that the energy from the show was a good omen, and after leaving the stage they knew they had something special. Following this performance, Phonte invited Pooh to a barbeque at his home where they decided to continue the collaboration.<ref name="tidal.com">{{Cite web|url=https://tidal.com/browse/video/118638179|title=Rap Radar - Little Brother, Episode 74|via=tidal.com}}</ref>

In May 2019, it was revealed that Little Brother was to return and release new music later in the year, announcing their appearance at the upcoming [[Hopscotch Music Festival]] at September 7, 2019 in [[Raleigh, North Carolina]]. However, it was also revealed that 9th Wonder would not be returning as a third member of the group, with Phonte and Rapper Big Pooh continuing under the name; as they have been doing since 2007.

On August 19, 2019, it was announced that Little Brother will be releasing a new album, titled "May the Lord Watch", which was released the following day on the 20th.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B1WT1RLgXyt/|title=Phonte Coleman on Instagram: "Little Brother. May The Lord Watch. Midnight. #LBbizness #MTLW ðŸ“·: @toinejameson"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2019-08-19}}</ref> The album continues and updates the fictional UBN channel concept that was originally used for their well-known sophomore effort [[The Minstrel Show]], and features no production from 9th Wonder; instead, working with producers such as [[Khrysis]], [[Nottz]], [[Focus...|Focus]] and [[Black Milk]]. The album was completed in secret with many artists who featured on the album not knowing that they were contributing to a Little Brother album. The album was also remarkable for killing the singer Percy Miracles, who was an alter ego of Phonte. The first skit on the album features DJ [[Peter Rosenberg]] discussing the death of Percy. This concept was a tribute to the fact that the death of [[Phife Dawg]] was the event that brought the Phonte and Pooh back together.<ref name="tidal.com"/> It was revealed in an interview on the ''Premium Pete Show'' that 9th Wonder was to have been a part of the reunion album, under its original title ''Homecoming''. The title was changed due to [[Beyonce]] releasing ''[[Homecoming: The Live Album]]'' to coincide with her concert film ''[[Homecoming (2019 film)|Homecoming]]''. Phonte and Pooh decided to continue the project without 9th after several disagreements over production and touring commitments.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.52769/title.little-brother-reveals-why-9th-wonder-wasnt-part-of-reunion-album|title=Little Brother Reveals Why 9th Wonder Wasn't Part Of Reunion Album|first=HipHopDX-|last=https://hiphopdx.com|date=September 10, 2019|website=HipHopDX}}</ref>
