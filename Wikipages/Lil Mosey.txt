'''Lathan Moses Stanley Echols''' (born January 25, 2002), known professionally as '''Lil Mosey''', is an American rapper. He rose to fame in late 2017 with the release of his single "Pull Up". Mosey's debut studio album ''[[Northsbest]]'' (2018) included his first [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] charting single, "[[Noticed (song)|Noticed]]". His second studio album, ''[[Certified Hitmaker]]'' (2019), peaked at number 12 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. In 2020, Mosey released the highest-charting single of his career, "[[Blueberry Faygo]]", which peaked at number 8 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.

In April 2021, Echols was charged with [[Rape|second degree rape]] in [[Lewis County, Washington]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/music/seattle-rap-star-lil-mosey-charged-with-rape-in-lewis-county/|title = Seattle rap star Lil Mosey charged with rape in Lewis County|date = April 22, 2021}}</ref> He is currently out on bail, and his trial is expected to start in January 2022.<ref name="Third_defendant_named" />

== Early life ==
Lathan Moses Stanley Echols was born on January 25, 2002, in [[Mountlake Terrace, Washington]], to a white mother and a half-Puerto Rican, half-black father.<ref name="AllMusic">{{Cite web|title=Lil Mosey {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lil-mosey-mn0003754346/biography|access-date=July 31, 2020|website=AllMusic}}</ref><ref name="Revolt">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/2020/9/21/21449691/lil-mosey-music-interview|title=Lil Mosey on being a "certified hitmaker," his upcoming music, his background and more|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Ju|first=Shirley|date=September 21, 2020|access-date=November 14, 2020}}</ref> He was raised by his mother in the north side of [[Seattle]]. He began rapping in his early teens and started his music career in the eighth grade. Echols first attended [[Mountlake Terrace High School]], then transferred to [[Shorecrest High School]] in the tenth grade. He later dropped out of school following the success of his song "Pull Up", and went on to pursue his career, heading to [[Los Angeles]] to record.<ref name="AllMusic" /><ref name="Revolt" />

== Career ==

=== 2016‚Äì2018: Career beginnings and ''Northsbest'' ===
In 2016, Lil Mosey uploaded his first song, titled "So Bad", to the music streaming service [[SoundCloud]], quickly receiving 50,000 views. On November 13, 2016, Mosey competed and placed fourth at the Coast 2 Coast Live Seattle All Ages Edition.

"Pull Up" was Lil Mosey's first released track and served as his debut commercial single.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2018/03/new-artist-recommendations-march/lil-mosey |title=If you like YBN Nahmir, listen to Lil Mosey. |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Pigeons and Planes]] |author1= Moore, Jacob |author2=Gardner, Alex |access-date=July 28, 2018}}</ref> Its music video reached over 25 million views on [[YouTube]] in the first 16 months upon its release.<ref name="ST1" /> On March 14, 2018, Mosey released his second commercial single "Boof Pack". Its music video has received over 13 million views on YouTube in just over a year after its release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JF8xgg7APuk|title=Lil Mosey "Boof Pack" (WSHH Exclusive ‚Äì Official Music Video) ‚Äì YouTube|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref> Around 4 months later, he released "Noticed" as his third commercial single alongside a music video directed by [[Cole Bennett]], also with the help of Jesus Sevilla, his cousin. The music video was viewed just short of 10 million times within the first two weeks of its release.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/lil-mosey-interview-the-break/|title=The Break Presents: Lil Mosey ‚Äì XXL|last=III|first=Robby Seabrook|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=April 12, 2019}}</ref> In the video, "Mosey and his friends live the good life as they relax in a luxury apartment, which overlooks a beautiful beach".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/7/lil-mosey-enjoys-the-good-life-in-noticed-video |title=Lil Mosey Enjoys the Good Life in "Noticed" Video |website=Hypebeast |author=Maduakolam, Emmanuel |date=July 25, 2018 |access-date=July 28, 2018}}</ref>

On October 19, 2018, he released his debut studio album titled ''[[Northsbest]]'', which includes all three of his commercial singles and eight other tracks.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.thehypemagazine.com/2018/11/lil-mosey-northsbest/|title=Lil Mosey ‚Äì Northsbest|website=The Hype Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2019}}</ref> The only artist featured is [[BlocBoy JB]].

== Musical style ==
Lil Mosey is known for his melodic flow and lyrics that are tailored to his life.<ref name=":1" /> When asked if the term "[[mumble rap]]" applies to him in an interview with Complex, Lil Mosey says "I wouldn't consider myself a mumble rapper, because I don't know what that is. But when I talk, I mumble."<ref name=":2">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/08/lil-mosey-interview|title=Lil Mosey Isn't a Mumble Rapper: 'I Don't Know What That Is'|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=April 12, 2019}}</ref> Although Lil Mosey does not consider his sound to be similar to [[Meek Mill]]'s, he does consider the rapper an influence as in an interview with XXL Magazine, he describes how he would always listen to Meek Mill's song "Dreams and Nightmares" when he was younger.<ref name=":0" /> When asked in the same interview about what comparisons he has drawn, Mosey responded "Music-wise, they be comparing me to [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]".<ref name=":0" />

== Live performances ==
Lil Mosey has toured with rappers Smooky MarGielaa, [[Lil Tjay]], [[Polo G]] and [[Smokepurpp]].<ref name=":2" /> In 2018, Lil Mosey was on tour with [[Juice Wrld]] and [[YBN Cordae]], serving as the opening act for Juice Wrld's WRLD Domination Tour.<ref name=":1" /> He has also received a co-sign from [[Lil Xan]], and he says his first real live performance as an established artist came when Lil Xan brought him on stage during his concert in Seattle.<ref name=":2" /> The rapper also performed at Rolling Loud in 2018. == Legal issues ==
=== Rape charge ===
On April 2, 2021, Echols was charged with one count of second-degree rape in [[Lewis County, Washington]], for engaging in sexual intercourse with an individual that was incapable of giving consent.<ref name=":4">{{cite news |last1=Rosenbaum |first1=Claudia |title=Lil Mosey Wanted for Rape Charge After Missing Court Date |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9562216/lil-mosey-wanted-rape-charge-arrest |access-date=April 24, 2021 |work=Billboard.com |date=April 23, 2021}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{cite news |last1=Curto |first1=Justin |title=Rapper Lil Mosey Wanted for Rape Charge After Missing Court Date |url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/04/lil-mosey-charged-rape-wanted-missing-court-date.html |access-date=April 24, 2021 |work=Vulture |date=April 22, 2021}}</ref> He has pleaded not guilty.<ref name=":7" /> The charge stems from an alleged incident on the night of January 5, 2020, at a cabin in [[Randle, Washington]], where Echols had hosted a party.<ref name=":4" /> Echols was not an adult at the time of the incident, as he turned 18 on January 25, 2020. In accordance with a pre-trial sexual assault [[Restraining order|protective order]], Echols is ordered not to contact or come within 500 feet of the victim, and is prohibited from possessing firearms or using drugs.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cummings-Grady |first1=Mackenzie |title=Lil Mosey Ordered To Stay Away From Alleged Rape Victim |url=https://www.complex.com/music/lil-mosey-ordered-to-stay-away-alleged-rape-victim |access-date=August 2, 2021 |work=Complex |date=May 22, 2021}}</ref> If convicted, Echols faces a minimum of 78 to 102 months and a maximum of life in prison.<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":6" />

Upon being contacted by the [[Morton, Washington|Morton]] Police Department on January 6, 2020, the alleged victim told a detective that she attended the party with a friend and [[Blackout (drug-related amnesia)|blacked out]] after drinking [[White Claw Hard Seltzer|White Claw]] alcoholic beverages and [[champagne]] provided at the party. The woman stated that she could not clearly remember the events of the rest of the night, and could only remember walking in her underwear to an upstairs bedroom, being told by individuals that she had sex, and being told that there was video recording of the intercourse. However, she had no memory of the rape. She stated that upon waking up the following morning, she and the friend she came with left the cabin.<ref name=":4" />

On January 9, 2020, the Morton Police Department interviewed the woman's friend, who stated that she had consensual sex with Echols in his car, during which she felt a pain in her legs. She stated that she had a "fuzzy" memory of what happened afterwards, but that the next thing she remembered was multiple men forcing themselves onto her, including a man identified as Francisco P. Prater saying "wake up, wake up, suck this dick, bitch." Francisco P. Prater was also charged with one count of second-degree rape, in tandem with Echols' rape charge.<ref name=":8">{{Cite news|last=The Chronicle Staff|date=April 22, 2021|title=Arrest Warrants Issued for Two Men Accused of Raping Woman at Party in Randle|work=The Chronicle|url=https://www.chronline.com/stories/arrest-warrants-issued-for-two-men-accused-of-raping-woman-at-party-in-randle,262871|access-date=April 23, 2021}}</ref> She also stated that she had bruises on her arm, neck, and inner knee that she did not know the origin of. In a later interview with police, the woman stated that she remembered waking up to Echols raping her in an upstairs bedroom after she had blacked out.<ref name=":4" />

On April 21, 2021, a $50,000 [[Arrest warrant#United States|warrant]] was issued for Echols' arrest after he failed to show up for a required court appearance at a preliminary hearing.<ref name=":5" /><ref name=":6">{{cite news |last1=Williams |first1=Aaron |title=Lil Mosey Has Reportedly Been Accused Of Rape And A Warrant Has Been Issued For His Arrest |url=https://uproxx.com/music/lil-mosey-accused-rape/ |access-date=April 24, 2021 |work=UPROXX |date=April 22, 2021}}</ref> An attorney for Echols claimed that he had not shown up for his appearance because the court summons had been sent to an address Echols had not lived at for five years, and that he had no knowledge of the preliminary hearing until after the warrant was issued.<ref name=":7">{{cite news |last1=Fitzgerald |first1=Emily |title=Rapper 'Lil Mosey' Pleads Not Guilty to Second-Degree Rape Charge in Lewis County |url=https://www.chronline.com/stories/rapper-lil-mosey-pleads-not-guilty-to-second-degree-rape-charge-in-lewis-county,263036 |access-date=April 28, 2021 |work=The Chronicle |date=April 27, 2021}}</ref> After a deal was made between Echols and the Lewis County Prosecutor's Office to quash the warrant, Echols appeared in court on April 27.<ref name=":7" /> On July 13, Echols appeared at a pre-trial [[omnibus hearing]].<ref name="Third_defendant_named" />

Francisco P. Prater, who was allegedly present at the party on January 5, 2020, was also charged with one count of second-degree rape in tandem with Echols' rape charge. Like Echols, Prater did not show up for his scheduled court appearance on April 21, 2021, leading to a warrant being issued for his arrest.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Rietmulder|first=Michael|date=April 23, 2021|title=Seattle Rap Performer Lil Mosey Charged With Rape in Lewis County|work=[[The Seattle Times]]|agency=The Chronicle|url=https://www.chronline.com/stories/seattle-rap-performer-lil-mosey-charged-with-rape-in-lewis-county,262901|access-date=April 23, 2021}}</ref><ref name=":8" /> Prater failed to appear in court after Echols' appearance on April 27, and as of that date the warrant for his arrest remains active.<ref name=":7" /> Joshua D. Darrow was named as a third defendant in the rape case in July 2021. Darrow is accused of restraining the victim as Echols and Prater raped her.<ref name="Third_defendant_named">{{cite news |last1=Fitzgerald |first1=Emily |title=Third Defendant Charged in Lewis County Rape Case Involving Rapper 'Lil Mosey' |url=https://www.chronline.com/stories/third-defendant-charged-in-lewis-county-rape-case-involving-rapper-lil-mosey,269078 |access-date=August 2, 2021 |work=The Chronicle |date=July 14, 2021}}</ref> Darrow is currently out on unsecured bail and has also been issued a pre-trial sexual assault protective order.<ref name="Third_defendant_named" />

Echols is currently out on $50,000 unsecured bail.<ref name=":7" /> He is expected to go on trial [[Joinder|alongside]] Prater and Darrow on January 24, 2022.<ref name="Third_defendant_named" />
