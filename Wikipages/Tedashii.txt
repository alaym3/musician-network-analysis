'''Tedashii Lavoy Anderson''' (born March 8, 1977), known simply as '''Tedashii''', is an American [[Christian hip hop]] artist and member of the hip-hop troupe, [[116 Clique]]. He also hosted the [[NGEN Radio]] show "Serium". Tedashii has released five solo albums, ''[[Kingdom People]]'', ''[[Identity Crisis (Tedashii album)|Identity Crisis]]'', ''[[Blacklight (Tedashii album)|Blacklight]]'', ''[[Below Paradise]]'', and ''Never Fold'' on [[Reach Records]]. Tedashii's delivery style is characterized by a deep voice that he can manipulate for rapping a wide range of flow schemes and patterns.

In 2008, Tedashii toured with the 116 Clique during the "Unashamed Tour".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapzilla.com/rz/news/38-backstage/889-uk-alert-unashamed-tour-coming-to-you |title=UK Alert: Unashamed Tour coming to you |publisher=Rapzilla |access-date=June 15, 2014}}</ref> The "Don't Waste Your Life Tour" followed in 2009, after which he took a brief break.{{Citation needed|date=December 2012}} In late 2010 he returned for the "Unashamed: The Movement Tour".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dasouth.com/news/24-news/3748-reach-records-announces-unashamed-2010-the-movement-tour-now-booking |title=Reach Records announces Unashamed 2010: The Movement Tour - Now booking |publisher=DaSouth.com |date=August 5, 2010 |access-date=June 15, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708233730/http://www.dasouth.com/news/24-news/3748-reach-records-announces-unashamed-2010-the-movement-tour-now-booking |archive-date=July 8, 2011 }}</ref> As soon as his album ''Blacklight'' was released in 2011 he went on a brief tour to promote it.{{Citation needed|date=December 2012}} He joined the "Man Up Tour" in 2011, taking a brief break in early 2012.{{Citation needed|date=December 2012}} In late 2012 116 Clique toured 30 cities with the "Unashamed Tour: Come Alive".<ref>{{cite web|title=UNASHAMED TOUR 2012: COME ALIVE|url=http://reachrecords.com/blog/post/Unashamed-Tour-2012-Come-Alive|publisher=reachrecords.com|access-date=June 15, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130414193659/https://reachrecords.com/blog/post/Unashamed-Tour-2012-Come-Alive|archive-date=April 14, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Unashamed 2012: Come Alive (@reachrecords)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UGVOvVRM4i4|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=June 15, 2014|author=Reach Records|format=Video/Web|date=August 1, 2012}}</ref> Then in December, [[Natalie Sims|Suzy Rock]] and [[KB (rapper)|KB]] joined him for the "No Boundaries Concert Series".{{Citation needed|date=December 2012}} Tedashii appeared in [[Family Force 5]]'s song "Chainsaw" on the remix album ''[[Reanimated (Family Force 5 album)|Reanimated]]'' released in 2013. In 2015, Tedashii appeared on the song "I Have a Dream" for Manafest's album ''[[Reborn (Manafest album)|Reborn]]''.

== Personal life ==

Tedashii Lavoy Anderson was born in Lufkin, Texas on March 8, 1977 to mother Vera Louise Anderson.<ref name="Public Records">{{cite web |title=U.S. Public Records Index |url=https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:V823-MSX |author=Texas Birth Index |year=2002 |publisher=Family Search |access-date=September 22, 2015}}</ref><ref name="DRC"/>

Tedashii currently lives with his wife in [[Denton, Texas]], serving at The Village Church.<ref name="DRC">{{cite news|url=http://www.dentonrc.com/local-news/local-news-headlines/20110530-present-imperfect.ece|title=Present imperfect|last=Breeding|first=Lucinda|date=30 May 2011|publisher=Denton Record-Chronicle|access-date=January 31, 2014}}</ref> In March 2013 Tedashii's one-year-old son died. He recorded much of his spiritual struggle during that time and based his fourth studio album, ''[[Below Paradise]]'', off of his experiences.<ref>{{cite web|title=Below Paradise|url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/news/Below_Paradise_/53708/p1/|publisher=[[Cross Rhythms]]|access-date=June 15, 2014|date=May 6, 2014}}</ref><ref name=ivm>{{cite web |url=http://www.indievisionmusic.com/2014/05/26/tedashii-below-paradise/ |title=Tedashii – Below Paradise &#124; Reviews |last=Peronto |first=Anthony |date=May 26, 2014 |publisher=Indie Vision Music |access-date=June 15, 2014 }}</ref>
