'''Trivium''' is an American <!-- Please reach consensus on the talk page before changing the genre of the band -->[[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band from [[Orlando, Florida]], formed in 1999.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Trivium/posts/10153706121131896|title=Trivium - EST. 1999 - Get the tee here: bit.ly/TRonidmnd|publisher=[[Facebook]]|access-date=21 October 2017}}</ref> The band comprises vocalist and guitarist [[Matt Heafy]], guitarist [[Corey Beaulieu]], bassist [[Paolo Gregoletto]], and drummer [[Alex Bent]].<ref name="AlexBentMetalSucks">{{cite web|url=https://www.metalsucks.net/2017/01/23/drummer-paul-wandtke-is-out-of-trivium-alex-bent-is-in/|title=Drummer Paul Wandtke is Out of Trivium, Alex Bent is In|author=Vince Neilstein|date=23 January 2017|publisher=Metal Sucks}}</ref>

The band's debut album ''[[Ember to Inferno]]'' was their only album released through [[Lifeforce Records]] in 2003.<ref name="Pheonix Times" /> After getting signed to [[Roadrunner Records]] in 2004, the band has released [[Trivium discography|nine studio albums]] and over twenty singles. Their tenth studio album, ''[[In the Court of the Dragon]]'', was released on October 8, 2021. The band has sold over one million albums worldwide and was nominated for a Grammy for the song "Betrayer" at the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards]] in 2019 for [[Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance|Best Metal Performance]].

==History==
===Formation and ''Ember to Inferno'' (1999â€“2004)===
The band formed in 1999. At his eighth-grade talent show, [[Matt Heafy]] performed a cover version of "[[No Leaf Clover]]" by [[Metallica]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Hellraisers: A Complete Visual History of Heavy Metal Mayhem |chapter=Foreword |author=Matt Heafy |publisher=Race Point |editor=Axl Rosenberg, Christopher Krovatin |date=October 24, 2017 |isbn=9781631064302 |page=7}}</ref> Singer Brad Lewter noticed Heafy and later asked him to try out for his band as lead guitarist. The pair went over to drummer [[Travis Smith (musician)|Travis Smith]]'s house where they played a rendition of Metallica's "[[For Whom the Bell Tolls (Metallica song)|For Whom the Bell Tolls]]". Impressed with Heafy's performance, they accepted him into the band known as "Trivium", which is a Latin three-way intersection they used to explain their music as combining [[metalcore]], [[melodic death metal]] and [[thrash metal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lordsofmetal.nl/nl/interviews/view/id/370 |title=Trivium |website=Lords of Metal|first=Marcel|last=Zuurmond |access-date=March 4, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171021221041/http://www.lordsofmetal.nl/nl/interviews/view/id/370|archive-date=October 21, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Lewter played a number of local gigs with the band before departing about a year later. In mid-2001 Trivium parted ways with its bassist. Trivium asked Richie Brown from local black metal band [[Mindscar]] to fill in on bass duties until a full-time member could be brought in. A string of successful shows followed and then a search was conducted for a permanent bassist.{{citation needed|date=January 2018}}

In late 2002, Trivium went into the recording studio to record its first high-quality demo disc.<ref>{{cite web|title=Trivium Releases|website=Trivium.org|url=http://www.trivium.org/releases.php |access-date=November 2, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080822023347/http://www.trivium.org/releases.php |archive-date=August 22, 2008}}</ref> A copy of the demo was heard by German label [[Lifeforce Records|Lifeforce]] which signed Trivium, and the band entered a studio to record its debut album, ''[[Ember to Inferno]]''.<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|title=Official biography|url=http://www.trivium.org/bio.php|website=Trivium.org|access-date=March 25, 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070323015401/http://www.trivium.org/bio.php |archive-date=March 23, 2007}}</ref> Lead guitarist [[Corey Beaulieu]] joined after the recording of the album. In 2004, [[Paolo Gregoletto]] joined as the band's bassist, replacing Brent Young, before a tour with [[Machine Head (band)|Machine Head]].<ref name="RR">{{cite web| url= http://www.fourteeng.net/Roadrage.html| title= Road Rage 2004 review| access-date= June 30, 2007| last= Lumpkin| first= Sharitta| year= 2003| website= FourteenG.net| url-status= dead| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20070928040412/http://www.fourteeng.net/Roadrage.html| archive-date= September 28, 2007| df= mdy-all}}</ref> The album ''Ember to Inferno'' managed to garner the interest of [[Roadrunner Records]] representatives, who later signed Trivium to the label. The band then began writing songs for their major label debut.<ref name="Bio"/>

===''Ascendancy'' (2004â€“2006)===
In 2004, Trivium recorded its second album, ''[[Ascendancy (album)|Ascendancy]]'', in Audiohammer Studios and Morrisound Recording in [[Florida]].<ref>{{cite AV media notes| others=Trivium |year=2005 |title=Ascendancy |publisher=[[Roadrunner Records]]}}</ref> Produced by Heafy and [[Jason Suecof]], the album was released in March 2005. The album debuted at No. 151 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and at No. 4 on the ''[[Top Heatseekers]]'' chart.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite web| title=Album chart history |publisher=[[Billboard charts]] |url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=trivium|chart=all}} |access-date=March 16, 2008}}</ref> [[Allmusic]] reviewer Johnny Loftus stated on ''Ascendancy'', Trivium are a "ridiculously tight quartet, unleashing thrilling dual guitar passages and pummeling kick drum gallops as surely as they do melodic breaks and vicious throat screams."<ref>{{cite web| author=Loftus, Johnny |url={{Allmusic|class=album|id=r733299|pure_url=yes}} |title=''Ascendancy'' overview |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=March 29, 2008}}</ref> and Rod Smith of [[Decibel Magazine|''Decibel'' magazine]] praised "Smith's impeccably articulated beats, bassist Paolo Gregoletto's contained thunder, and, especially, Heafy and guitarist Corey Beaulieu's liquid twin leads."<ref>{{cite magazine| author=Smith, Rod |url=http://www.decibelmagazine.com/features/dec2005/trivium.aspx |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060506010110/http://www.decibelmagazine.com/features/dec2005/trivium.aspx |archive-date=May 6, 2006 |title=Trivium, they are the youth gone wild |magazine=[[Decibel Magazine|Decibel magazine]] |access-date=March 26, 2008}}</ref> The album was also recognized as the "Album of the Year" by ''[[Kerrang!]]'' magazine. Later in 2007 the band received their first Gold Record in the UK for more than 100,000 Sales.<ref>{{cite web| author=Roadrunner AU |url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com.au/page/News?&news_page=182&news_id=44449 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111001000229/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com.au/page/News?&news_page=182&news_id=44449 |archive-date=October 1, 2011 |title=TTRIVIUM score a gold record! |publisher=[[Roadrunner Records]] |access-date=August 11, 2011}}</ref>

In 2005, Trivium played the first Saturday set on the main stage at [[Download Festival]] in [[Castle Donington]], England, credited by Matt Heafy as the gig that really launched Trivium on the world stage. Singles and music videos were released for "[[Like Light to the Flies]]", "[[Pull Harder on the Strings of Your Martyr]]", "[[A Gunshot to the Head of Trepidation]]", and "[[Dying in Your Arms]]". The videos for these songs gained rotation on [[Scuzz|Scuzz TV]] and [[MTV2]]'s [[Headbangers Ball]] and "Pull Harder on The Strings of Your Martyr" has become one of the band's most well known songs and is the song that they usually close their sets with.<ref name="Roadrunner Records">{{cite web| url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/artists/Trivium/ |title=Roadrunner Records artists&nbsp;â€“ Trivium |website=Roadrunnerrecords.com |access-date=March 19, 2008}}</ref> In support of the album, Trivium played on numerous tours with well-known artists. The band opened for [[Killswitch Engage]], [[Iced Earth]], [[Fear Factory]], and [[Machine Head (band)|Machine Head]], who were one of Heafy's largest influences.<ref>{{cite journal| author=J. Bennett |date=December 2007 |title=Rebel Meets Rebel&nbsp;â€“ Robb Flynn and Matt Heafy |journal=[[Revolver Magazine]]}}</ref> Trivium also played at Road Rage 2005<ref>{{cite web| title = Interview with Road Rage 2005 US bands| publisher = Metal Underground|date= April 12, 2005| url = http://www.metalunderground.com/interviews/details.cfm?newsid=13069| access-date =June 24, 2007}}</ref> and [[Ozzfest]].<ref>{{cite web| url=http://ozzfest.com/static_content/view/alumni.html |title=Ozzfest alumini |website=Ozzfest.com |access-date=January 5, 2008}}</ref>

''Ascendancy'' was re-released in 2006 with four bonus tracks and a DVD containing all of the band's music videos and live footage.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=48344 |title=Trivium: ''Ascendancy''&nbsp;â€“ Special Edition CD/DVD Package Details Revealed |work=[[Blabbermouth.net]] |access-date=March 29, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080227235150/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=48344 |archive-date=February 27, 2008 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In an interview with the UK's ''[[Metal Hammer]]'' magazine in May 2008, Heafy said that their new album was to have "more thrash influences, more screaming."<ref>{{cite journal|date=May 2008 |author=Head, Richard |title=Trivium Discuss Upcoming Album |journal=[[Metal Hammer]]}}</ref> He told [[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]] magazine, "For the first time, we can't look at our songs and say who the riffs sound like. Trivium toured extensively throughout 2009 in support of ''[[Shogun (Trivium album)|Shogun]]'', with two headline legs of the US, a headline slot on the [[Mayhem Festival]] second stage, an Australia/Japan leg,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/trivium-guitarist-on-touring-and-shogun/|title=TRIVIUM Guitarist On Touring And 'Shogun'|last=Blabbermouth|date=April 26, 2009|website=BLABBERMOUTH.NET}}</ref> as well as a conclusive UK run in March 2010. Throughout their headline runs, they were supported by [[Chimaira]], [[Darkest Hour (band)|Darkest Hour]], [[Dirge Within]], [[Whitechapel (band)|Whitechapel]], [[Rise to Remain]] and [[Black Tide]]. They were also booked as a co-headliner for Australia's [[Soundwave (Australian music festival)|Soundwave Festival]] in February 2010.

Travis Smith unofficially left the band on the second leg of the "Into the Mouth of Hell We Tour" tour. On February 4, 2010, the band announced that Travis had indeed officially left the band and fill-in drummer, ex-drum tech, Nick Augusto, was replacing him. Augusto is a former member of Maruta and Metal Militia, in which he played with Gregoletto. The band has not yet stated the reason of his departure.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.trivium.org/board_posts/trivium-announce-nick-augusto-as-new-drummer |title=Trivium Announce Nick Augusto As New Drummer |website=Trivium.org |date=February 4, 2010 |access-date=April 10, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100208101319/http://www.trivium.org/board_posts/trivium-announce-nick-augusto-as-new-drummer |archive-date=February 8, 2010 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> While Trivium's relationship with Smith had deteriorated over the years, the band had no immediate plans to replace him when he announced in late October 2009 that he would be sitting out the 'Into the Mouth of Hell We March' tour to take care of "some personal business."

===''In Waves'' (2010â€“2012)===
In an interview with ''Guitar World'', it was stated that for their fifth studio album, Trivium will move forward by looking back. The group will forgo the complex epic compositions, tricked-out leads and seven-string guitars that characterized its past two albums. Trivium will take an approach similar to that of its second album ''Ascendancy'' (2005) by using uncluttered riffs, Drop C# tuning, and more straightforward solos. "When we did ''Ascendancy'', we were writing specifically for the songs, not to show how well we could play," says frontman and guitarist Matt Heafy. "That wasn't exactly the case with our next two records."<ref name="guitarworld.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.guitarworld.com/article/trivium039s_pursuit |title=Trivium: In the Studio â€“ Guitar World |website=Guitarworld.com |date=January 18, 2011 |access-date=October 26, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110511210355/http://www.guitarworld.com/article/trivium039s_pursuit |archive-date=May 11, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In the bonus content for the November 2011 issue of Guitar World, Matt Heafy talked about why the band decided to record this album in Drop Dâ™­ as opposed to Drop D. "For the whole record of 'In Waves' we're in Drop Dâ™­, so it's kind of like (Ascendency), just a half step lower, and everything that we do from all of the albums is half a step flat. It's mainly for the vocals. (It's) not that I can't sing higher, it's just easier with the amount of shows we do."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guitarworld.com/trivium-waves-video-lessons?page=0,1|title=Trivium: 'In Waves' Video Lessons|website=GuitarWorld.com|date=September 21, 2011|access-date=January 11, 2012}}</ref>

On June 6, ''[[Metal Hammer]]'' revealed that the band have decided to entitle their upcoming album ''[[In Waves]]'' and that it would be released on August 9, 2011.<ref name="metalhammer.co.uk">{{cite magazine |url=http://www.metalhammer.co.uk/news/trivium-name-new-album-2/ |title=Blog Archive Â» Trivium Name New Album |magazine=[[Metal Hammer]] |date=June 6, 2011 |access-date=July 9, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130722092309/http://www.metalhammer.co.uk/news/trivium-name-new-album-2/ |archive-date=July 22, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> On June 19, Trivium debuted two new songs live in Birmingham entitled "Dusk Dismantled" and "Black". On the June 28 "Dusk Dismantled" was released to TriviumWorld users only; it was released to general public on the 29th. On July 13, the premiere of "Inception of the End" was released to the general public on the website [[Hot Topic]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://community.hottopic.com/content/world-premiere-hear-triviums-new-track-inception-end |title=World Premiere! Hear Trivium's New Track "Inception Of The End" &#124; Hot Topic |website=Community.hottopic.com |access-date=October 26, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111003225026/http://community.hottopic.com/content/world-premiere-hear-triviums-new-track-inception-end |archive-date=October 3, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

''In Waves'' was released in August 2011 worldwide, and received generally positive reviews. Kerrang! described the album as "draws from the band's entire repertoire and shapes what it finds into a defining and definitive set". A special edition was released featuring the tracks: "Ensnare the Sun", "A Grey So Dark", "Drowning in Slow Motion", "Slave New World" cover and the single from the God of War III soundtrack "Shattering the Skies Above".

Trivium participated in a co-headlining trek with Swedish melodic death metal band [[In Flames]] in both Europe and North America. European support came from [[Ghost (Swedish band)|Ghost]], [[Rise to Remain]] and Insense. North American support came from [[Veil of Maya]] and Kyng. Additionally, Miss May I and The Ghost Inside joined Trivium during the [[In Waves]] 2011 European tour. 
They also performed at the [[Metaltown Festival]] and [[Download Festival]] in June 2012. The second disk included 13 bonus tracks which consisted of the early demos ''Ruber'' (the "Red Demo"), ''Caeruleus'' (the "Blue Demo") and ''Flavus'' (the "Yellow Demo").<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/trivium-to-re-release-hard-to-find-debut-ember-to-inferno/|title=TRIVIUM To Re-Release Hard-To-Find Debut 'Ember To Inferno'|last=Blabbermouth|date=October 14, 2016|website=BLABBERMOUTH.NET|access-date=December 26, 2018}}</ref>

Trivium's music mixes "soaring" and "crushing" [[riffs]], dual guitar harmonies, [[double bass drum]] patterns and occasional [[blast beat]]s and breakdowns that one can expect from the [[metalcore]] genre. Vocally Trivium combines both [[singing]] along with heavy [[screaming (music)|screaming]] and [[death growl|growls]]. Trivium is one of the notable [[New wave of American heavy metal]] acts.<ref name="Blabbermouth">{{cite news |title='New Wave Of American Heavy Metal' Book Documents Over 600 Bands |url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=44955 |work=[[Blabbermouth.net]] |access-date=April 27, 2008}}</ref>

Their style has evolved over the years: from their earliest work on ''[[Ember to Inferno]]'' right through to ''[[In Waves]]'', there is a clear thrash influence from [[Metallica]] and [[Machine Head (band)|Machine Head]], as well as a melodic death metal influence from early [[In Flames]].<ref name="MetalHammer">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.metalhammer.co.uk/news/albums-of-the-decade-trivium-ascendancy/|title=Ascendancy Named as album of the decade|date=July 15, 2009|magazine=[[Metal Hammer]]|access-date=July 15, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130412235749/http://www.metalhammer.co.uk/news/albums-of-the-decade-trivium-ascendancy/|archive-date=April 12, 2013|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

Upon the release of their second album ''[[Ascendancy (album)|Ascendancy]]'',<ref name="Ascendancy Received Gold Status">{{cite web|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.co.uk/release/The+Crusade|title=Ascendancy Received Gold Status|date=July 15, 2005|website=RoadrunnerRecords.co.uk|access-date=July 15, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090701034150/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.co.uk/release/The+Crusade|archive-date=July 1, 2009|df=mdy-all}}</ref> Trivium were identified as [[melodic metalcore]] with strong elements of thrash metal, with the third track on the album "[[Pull Harder on the Strings of Your Martyr]]" becoming a permanent fixture in the band's set lists and the rest of the album selling itself to gold status.<ref name="Ascendancy Received Gold Status" /> ''Ascendancy'' was even featured as one of [[Metal Hammer|Metal Hammer's]] Albums of the Decade.<ref name="MetalHammer" /> Later releases have marked changes in the band. ''[[The Crusade (album)|The Crusade]]'' was seen as a major shift in musical direction due to the change in vocal style, namely the absence of screaming, and some of the melodies featured.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.co.uk/release/The+Crusade|title=Crusade Changes Direction|date=July 15, 2005|website=RoadrunnerRecords.co.uk|access-date=July 15, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090701034150/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.co.uk/release/The+Crusade|archive-date=July 1, 2009|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

''The Crusade'' is a much more thrash-oriented album and lyrical content was also different in direction, citing current affairs, such as famous killings. In Autumn 2008, Trivium released ''Shogun'', which has a heavy Japanese influence on its title track as well as the first single release "Kirisute Gomen", which translates to "[[Kiri sute gomen|authorization to cut and leave]]". Acknowledging Matt Heafy's Japanese heritage,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thrashhits.com/2008/08/album-trivium-shogun/|title=Thrashhits Review on Shogun|date=July 15, 2009|publisher=Thrash Hits|access-date=July 15, 2009}}</ref> the album also was described more favorably as more their own style, as previous references to Trivium sounding like Metallica had been made on the back of ''The Crusade''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.stylusmagazine.com/reviews/trivium/the-crusade.htm|title=The Crusade Review (Hetfield Reference)|date=July 15, 2009|magazine=Stylus Magazine|access-date=July 15, 2009}}</ref> ''The Crusade'' made sparing use of [[seven-string guitar]]s, which were featured heavily on ''Shogun''.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Trivium - The Crusade (album review 9) {{!}} Sputnikmusic|url = https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/9696/Trivium-The-Crusade/|website = www.sputnikmusic.com|access-date = 2015-09-26}}</ref> Seven-string guitars once again returned on ''[[Silence in the Snow]]'', ''[[The Sin and the Sentence]]'' and ''[[What the Dead Men Say (album)|What the Dead Men Say]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.trivium.org/spotifystories|title=The Story And Sound Behind Silence In The Snow|website=Trivium.org|access-date=21 October 2017}}</ref>

On ''In Waves'', the band featured a sound closer to ''Ascendancy'' than ''The Crusade'' and ''Shogun''. The guitar tuning instead of being in drop D, they went half a step lower to drop C#. The album has several songs, such as 'Built to Fall' or 'Dusk Dismantled', featuring solely clean vocals or screamed vocals from Matt Heafy.

===Lyrical themes===
The band's lead singer, [[Matt Heafy]], has also been the primary lyricist since his early days as a member. He is known for his poetic style of lyrics and the usage of archaic words. Growing up, Heafy experienced [[anxiety]], teenage [[angst]], and occasional thoughts of [[suicide]]; several songs from the album ''Ascendancy'', such as "Rain", "Suffocating Sight", and "Departure", deal with these issues. Although not a victim himself, he also wrote the song "A Gunshot to the Head of Trepidation" on the topic of [[domestic violence]] and [[child abuse]], inspired by the stories of some of his peers.

Lyrical themes on the album ''The Crusade'' includes famous killings. "Entrance of the Conflagration" is about [[Andrea Yates]]'s murder of her five children, "Unrepentant" is about Nazir Ahmad's murder of his three daughters and stepdaughter, "Contempt Breeds Contamination" is about the death of [[Amadou Diallo]], and "And Sadness Will Sear" is based upon the murder of [[Matthew Shepard]].

Many of the songs on ''Shogun'' draw lyrical inspiration from [[Greek mythology]]. "[[Into the Mouth of Hell We March]]" and "Torn Between Scylla and Charybdis" detail the story of [[Odysseus]] choosing whether to face the giant whirlpool [[Charybdis]] or the six-headed monster [[Scylla]]. "He Who Spawned the Furies" is about the [[Titan (mythology)|Titan]] [[Cronus|Cronos]] devouring his children and castrating his father [[Uranus (mythology)|Uranus]], creating [[Aphrodite]] and the [[Erinyes|ErinÃ½es]] (the Furies). "Of Prometheus and the Crucifix" references the daily torment of [[Prometheus]] and alludes to [[crucifixion of Jesus]]. "Like Callisto to a Star in Heaven" is written from the perspective of the [[nymph]] [[Callisto (mythology)|Callisto]], detailing her rape and impregnation by [[Zeus]] and her transformations into a bear and into [[Ursa Major]]. Mythological lyrics returned on ''[[In the Court of the Dragon]]''. Inspired by fantasy literature, the band made up its own mythology on the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://knotfest.com/culture/matt-heafy-talks-overcoming-negativity-and-the-new-trivium-album-in-the-court-of-the-dragon/|title=Matt Heafy Talks Overcoming Negativity and the New Trivium Album 'In the Court of the Dragon'|work=[[Knotfest]]|last=Delgadillo|first=NicolÃ¡s|date=September 9, 2021|accessdate=October 10, 2021}}</ref> Lyrical content on ''[[The Sin and the Sentence]]'' and ''[[What the Dead Men Say (album)|What the Dead Men Say]]'' were inspired by modern aspects of life, such as [[social media]] and [[artificial intelligence]] as well as general themes such as [[war]], [[history]] and [[religion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teamrock.com/feature/2017-10-19/triviums-track-by-track-guide-to-the-sin-and-the-sentence|title=Trivium's track-by-track guide to The Sin And The Sentence|date=October 19, 2017|website=Teamrock.com|access-date=November 23, 2017}}</ref>
