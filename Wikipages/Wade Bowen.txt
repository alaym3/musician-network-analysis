'''Wade Bowen''' (born 1977) is an American [[Texas Country]]/[[Red Dirt (music)|Red Dirt]] singer from [[Waco, Texas]], United States.

Bowen was a member of the band West 84 with friend Matt Miller until 2001 when the group became known as Wade Bowen and West 84.<ref name="am">{{cite news |title=Wade Bowen Biography |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p326461|pure_url=yes}}}}, [[AllMusic]]</ref> He released his first album in 2002, ''Try Not To Listen'', which became a regional hit in Texas.<ref>{{cite news |title=Biography |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p326461|pure_url=yes}}}}, [[AllMusic]]</ref> He released his first live album in 2003, recorded live at The Blue Light in [[Lubbock, Texas]], followed by studio albums ''Lost Hotel'' in 2006 and ''[[If We Ever Make It Home]]'' in 2008.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wade Bowen: If We Ever Make It Home Review |url=http://www.slantmagazine.com/music/music_review.asp?ID=1542|website=Slantmagazine.com}}</ref> On November 21, 2009, Bowen recorded his second live album at [[Billy Bob's Texas]] in [[Fort Worth, Texas|Fort Worth]]. The album was released on April 27, 2010, as a CD/DVD combo.<ref>{{cite news |title=WADE BOWEN GEARS UP FOR "LIVE AT BILLY BOB’S" ALBUM AND DVD ON NOV. 21, 2009 |url=https://app.e2ma.net/app/view:CampaignPublic/id:7018.2550101837/rid:2848afec78311ce4e9bfd8c3c5d361a5|website=App.e2ma.net}}</ref>

Bowen released his fourth studio album, ''The Given'', in 2010.<ref name="am"/>  It was his first on a major label, [[Sony Music|Sony]] imprint [[BNA Records]], though he returned to releasing music independently after BNA closed.<ref name="am"/>  He released a self-titled studio album in 2014, followed by a duets album in 2015 with singer Randy Rogers of the [[Randy Rogers Band]], titled ''[[Hold My Beer Vol. 1]]''.<ref name="am"/>  Bowen's next album was '' Then Sings My Soul: Songs for My Mother'', a solo [[gospel music|gospel]] influenced studio release.<ref name="am"/> He has released 3 more albums: Watch This, with Randy Rogers, Solid Ground, released in 2018, and a follow up to Hold My Beer Vol. 1, Hold My Beer Vol. 2 with Randy Rogers.

During the [[COVID-19 pandemic|Covid-19 Pandemic]] of 2020, Wade started a web series of live shows called Wade's World, where he hosted other prominent Texas musicians to play and tell stories about their songs every Friday night. Guests included [[Bruce Robison]], [[Cody Canada]], [[Jack Ingram]], and others.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hennessey|first=Katie|title=Texas Americana Artist Wade Bowen Hosts Quaranstream Concert Series on Friday Nights|url=https://www.sacurrent.com/sa-sound/archives/2020/05/13/texas-americana-artist-wade-bowen-hosts-quaranstream-concert-series-on-friday-nights|access-date=2021-01-20|website=San Antonio Current|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Bowen is the brother-in-law of [[Cross Canadian Ragweed]] frontman [[Cody Canada]]<ref>{{cite news |title=Galleywinter Interview with Cody Canada |url=http://www.galleywinter.com/main/News/article/sid=402.html|website=Galleywinter.com}}</ref> and is a graduate of [[Texas Tech University]], where he was a member of the Epsilon Nu chapter of the [[Sigma Chi]] fraternity.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wade Bowen |website=CMT.com |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/wade_bowen/artist.jhtml}}</ref>
