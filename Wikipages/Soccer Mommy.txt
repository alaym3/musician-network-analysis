'''Sophia Regina Allison''' (born May 27, 1997), better known by her stage name '''Soccer Mommy''', is an American singer-songwriter and musician from [[Nashville]], Tennessee. Allison released her first studio album, ''[[Clean (Soccer Mommy album)|Clean]]'', on March 2, 2018. Her follow up, ''[[Color Theory (album)|Color Theory]]'', was released on February 28, 2020.

==History==
Allison was born in Switzerland on May 26, 1997,<ref name="Ross">{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/dannyross1/2019/02/04/behind-the-scenes-with-indie-rocks-soccer-mommy/|title=Behind The Scenes With Indie Rock's Soccer Mommy|last=Ross|first=Danny|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref><ref name="rolling stone">{{cite news |last1=Vozick-Levinson |first1=Simon |title=Soccer Mommy: Indie Rock's New 'Chill But Kinda Sad' Hero |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/soccer-mommy-indie-rocks-new-chill-but-kinda-sad-hero-253710/ |accessdate=20 May 2019 |agency=Rolling Stone Magazine |date=2018-03-09 |quote="20-year-old singer-songwriter Sophie Allison"}}</ref> and grew up in Nashville, Tennessee. She attended [[Nashville School of the Arts]], a specialty high school where she studied guitar and played in the swing band.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nashvillescene.com/music/features/article/20994028/on-clean-soccer-mommy-showcases-how-disillusionment-can-lead-to-growth|title=On Clean, Soccer Mommy Showcases How Disillusionment Can Lead to Growth|work=Nashville Scene|access-date=2018-03-06|language=en}}</ref>

She first began playing guitar at age six and started making music.<ref name="auto">{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/01/29/soccer-mommy-clean-cool-scorpio-rising-interview-sophie-allison|title=Soccer Mommy is 2018's chillest new rock star|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-03-05|language=en}}</ref> She began posting home-recorded songs to [[Bandcamp]] as Soccer Mommy in 2015, during the summer when she was about to leave for college. She then attended [[New York University]], where she studied music business at the [[Steinhardt School of Culture, Education, and Human Development]]. While in college, Allison played her first show as Soccer Mommy at the community art space [[Silent Barn]] in Bushwick, Brooklyn, and shortly after landed a record deal with [[Fat Possum Records|Fat Possum]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://uproxx.com/music/soccer-mommy-clean-interview/|title=An Interview With Soccer Mommy On Tender, Insolent Indie Rock Debut|date=2018-03-05|work=UPROXX|access-date=2018-03-06|language=en-US}}</ref> She dropped out of college after two years in 2017 to return to Nashville and pursue her music career.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/28/arts/music/soccer-mommy-clean-review.html|title=Soccer Mommy Confronts All the Disappointments of Love on 'Clean'|last=Pareles|first=Jon|date=2018-02-28|work=The New York Times|access-date=2018-03-05|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

Her first full-length album as Soccer Mommy, ''For Young Hearts'', was released in 2016 on [[Orchid Tapes]].<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web|last1=Rettig|first1=James|title=Stream Soccer Mommy For Young Hearts|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1883096/stream-soccer-mommy-for-young-hearts/mp3s/|publisher=[[Stereogum]]|accessdate=2 December 2017|date=2016-06-21}}</ref> Her second album, ''[[Collection (Soccer Mommy album)|Collection]]'', was released in 2017 on Fat Possum Records.<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Rytlewski|first1=Evan|title=Soccer Mommy: Collection|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/soccer-mommy-collection/|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|accessdate=2 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Lorusso|first1=Marissa|title=Songs We Love: Soccer Mommy, 'Out Worn'|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/05/16/528466112/songs-we-love-soccer-mommy-out-worn|publisher=[[NPR Music]]|accessdate=2 December 2017}}</ref><ref name="thefader">{{cite web|last1=McDermott|first1=Patrick|title=Soccer Mommy Debuts "Allison," A Lonely-Sounding Song With A Video To Match|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/06/20/soccer-mommy-allison-video-collection|publisher=[[The Fader]]|accessdate=2 December 2017}}</ref><ref name="popmatters">{{cite web|last1=Duricy|first1=Brian|title=Soccer Mommy: Collection|url=https://www.popmatters.com/soccer-mommy-collection-2495384382.html|publisher=[[PopMatters]]|accessdate=2 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Daly|first1=Rhian|title=What's the deal with Soccer Mommy?|url=https://www.timeout.com/london/music/whats-the-deal-with-soccer-mommy|publisher=Time Out|accessdate=5 December 2017|date=28 July 2017}}</ref> Her debut album proper, titled ''[[Clean (Soccer Mommy album)|Clean]]'', was released on March 2, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2018/01/09/soccer-mommy-new-album-clean-your-dog-watch|title=Soccer Mommy announces new album 'Clean'|website=DIY|accessdate=10 April 2018}}</ref>

She has toured with [[Stephen Malkmus and the Jicks|Stephen Malkmus]], [[Mitski]], [[Kacey Musgraves]], [[Jay Som]], [[Slowdive]], [[Frankie Cosmos]], [[Liz Phair]], [[Phoebe Bridgers]] and others.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://fatpossum.com/artists/soccer-mommy/|title=Soccer Mommy|work=Fat Possum Records|access-date=2018-03-06|language=en-US}}</ref> She joined [[Paramore]] and [[Foster the People]] on the first half of their 2018 summer tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/paramore-touring-with-jay-som-soccer-mommy/|title=Paramore Touring With Jay Som, Soccer Mommy {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-04-13}}</ref> Soccer Mommy opened for [[Vampire Weekend]] in the fall.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8497367/vampire-weekend-tour-opening-acts-soccer-mommy-chicano-batman/|title=Vampire Weekend Tour Adds Soccer Mommy, Chicano Batman & More as Opening Acts {{!}} Billboard|website=billboard.com|language=en|access-date=2019-01-02}}</ref> She also opened for a few shows for [[Wilco]] in the fall of 2019. In February 2020, she played at one of [[Bernie Sanders]]'s presidential rallies and endorsed his [[Bernie Sanders 2020 presidential campaign|2020 presidential campaign]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=D'Souza |first1=Shaad |title=Watch Soccer Mommy perform at a Bernie Sanders rally in Houston |url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/02/24/watch-soccer-mommy-perform-at-a-bernie-sanders-rally-in-houston |website=thefader.com |publisher=The Fader |accessdate=February 24, 2020 |date=February 24, 2020}}</ref>

==Influences==
Allison cites musicians [[Natalie Imbruglia]], [[Mitski]], [[Slowdive]], [[The Chicks]], [[Taylor Swift]] and [[Avril Lavigne]] as well as the cities of [[Nashville]] and [[New York City|New York]] as influences.<ref name="billboard">{{cite web|last1=Kaplan|first1=Ilana|title=Soccer Mommy on How Nashville, Mitski & Taylor Swift Influenced Her Sound|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7905079/soccer-mommy-interview|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=2 December 2017}}</ref><ref name="auto"/> Lavigne's ''[[Under My Skin (Avril Lavigne album)|Under My Skin]]'' was the first CD she ever owned.<ref name="auto"/>

Allison has said that her music is influenced by pop music and she strives to include catchy elements in her songs.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://uproxx.com/music/soccer-mommy-clean-interview/3/|title=An Interview With Soccer Mommy On Tender, Insolent Indie Rock Debut|date=2018-03-05|work=UPROXX|access-date=2018-03-06|language=en-US}}</ref>
