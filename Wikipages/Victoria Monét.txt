'''Victoria Mon√©t McCants''' (born May 1, 1993)<ref>{{Cite web|title=Victoria Monet's schedule for ASCAP EXPO|url=https://ascapexpo2015.sched.com/speaker/victoria_monet.1tgjbkx9|access-date=2021-02-11|website=ascapexpo2015.sched.com}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. She soon took to writing and began working with producer [[Rodney Jerkins]]. Previously signed to [[Atlantic Records]], Mon√©t released her debut EP ''Nightmares & Lullabies: Act 1'' in 2014 and her follow-up EP ''Nightmares & Lullabies: Act 2'' in 2015. Part one of her debut album, ''[[Jaguar (album)|Jaguar]]'', was released on August 7, 2020.

Mon√©t has written songs for a number of artists including [[Nas]], [[Ariana Grande]], [[Fifth Harmony]], [[T.I.]], [[GOOD Music]], [[Lupe Fiasco]], [[Chrisette Michele]], [[Coco Jones]], [[Chris Brown]], and [[Dirty Money (duo)|Diddy ‚Äî Dirty Money]]. ==Career==

===2010‚Äì2015: Songwriting and ''Nightmares & Lullabies''===
Outside of dance rehearsals, Mon√©t began exchanging poetry with an older cousin and found a new passion. This soon translated into her writing her own music. Meanwhile, she had been educating herself on the production world, learning about producers, and making music at a local recording studio. She learned about [[Rodney Jerkins]] (aka Darkchild) and decided to add him on [[Myspace]].<ref name="IGotSoul">{{cite web|last1=YKIGS|title=Interview: Victoria Mon√©t Talks Nightmares & Lullabies Project, Working With Ariana Grande, & Signing With Atlantic Records|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/interview-victoria-monet-talks-nightmares-lullabies-project-working-with-ariana-grande-signing-with-atlantic-records|website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|access-date=April 13, 2015}}</ref> Shortly after that, he invited her to come to [[Los Angeles]] and audition for a new girl group he was forming, Purple Reign.<ref name="SoulTrain">{{cite journal|date=December 12, 2014|title=Profile: Victoria Monet |journal=[[Soul Train]]|url=http://soultrain.com/2014/12/12/profile-victoria-monet/}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-06-13|title=Victoria Mon√©t on her 'Friendship-Based' Writing Success With Ariana Grande|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8515625/victoria-monet-billboard-cover-story-2019|access-date=2020-09-19|website=Billboard}}</ref> The group landed a record deal with [[Motown]] a year later, but was dropped before releasing any music. After the group's disbandment, Monet turned to songwriting to earn money while she waited for her own music career to build up.<ref name="Wonderland">{{cite web |last1=Kaplan |first1=Ilana |title=Singer-songwriter Victoria Monet is on Her Own Path to Greatness |url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2020/01/10/victoria-monet-winter-issue/ |website=Wonderland Magazine |date=January 10, 2020}}</ref> The first song she worked on was a track for [[Diddy Dirty Money]]'s 2010 album, ''[[Last Train to Paris]]''.<ref name="SingersRoom"/> She continued to write song [[Hook (music)|hooks]] and sang the demos herself before sending them out. Her hope was that the labels would like her version enough that they would keep her on the track as a feature.<ref name="IGotSoul"/><ref name="The Guardian">{{cite web |last1=Cragg |first1=Michael |title=Victoria Mon√©t: the Ariana Grande songwriter making bed-quaking R&B |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/dec/27/victoria-monet-the-ariana-grande-songwriter-making-bed-quaking-rb |website=The Guardian |date=December 27, 2019}}</ref>

[[Atlantic Records]] CEO [[Craig Kallman]] heard one of her demos in a meeting and offered her a record deal later that day.<ref name=ASCAP>{{cite web|title=Victoria Monet|url=https://ascapexpo2015.sched.org/speaker/victoria_monet.1tgjbkx9#.VTKDBy2B41I|website=ASCAP EXPO|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150418161502/https://ascapexpo2015.sched.org/speaker/victoria_monet.1tgjbkx9#.VTKDBy2B41I|access-date=April 13, 2015|archive-date=April 18, 2015}}</ref><ref name="IGotSoul"/> Even though she was signed, Monet acknowledged that her songwriting "took off" before her music, while she was working on both.<ref name="Wonderland"/> Her songwriting remained her main form of income during the beginning of her music career.<ref name="The Guardian"/> On October 30, 2014, Monet released her debut EP ''Nightmares & Lullabies: Act 1'' and on June 17, 2015, she released her follow-up EP ''Nightmares & Lullabies ‚Äì Act 2'' under Atlantic Records.<ref name=RnB>{{cite web|last1=ThisIsRnB|title=New Music: Victoria Monet - Nightmares & Lullabies (Act 1) EP|url=http://www.thisisrnb.com/2014/10/new-music-victoria-monet-nightmares-lullabies-act-1-ep/|website=THISISRnB|date=October 30, 2014|access-date=April 13, 2015}}</ref><ref name=SingersRoom>{{cite web|last1=JUSMUSIC|first1=DJ|title=Atlantic Recording Artist Victoria Monet Drops 'Nightmares & Lullabies' ACT 1|url=http://singersroom.com/content/2014-10-30/Atlantic-Recording-Artist-Victoria-Monet-Drops-Nightmares-Lullabies-ACT-1/|website=Singers Room|date=October 30, 2014|access-date=April 13, 2015}}</ref> Monet chose the name as it represented the sound of the EPs: a dark beat mixed with soft melodies.<ref name="IGotSoul"/>

Mon√©t has a long history of writing songs for other artists.
