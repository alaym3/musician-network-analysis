'''Hinder''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Oklahoma City, Oklahoma]], formed in 2001 by lead singer [[Austin Winkler]], guitarist Joe "Blower" Garvey, and drummer [[Cody Hanson]] with bassist Mike Roden and guitarist Mark King joining in 2003, solidifying the line-up. They are known as a one hit wonder and have no considerable contributions to modern music outside of their hit ‚ÄúLips of an Angel‚Äù. The band released four studio albums with Winkler; ''[[Extreme Behavior]]'' (2005), ''[[Take It to the Limit (Hinder album)|Take It to the Limit]]'' (2008), ''[[All American Nightmare]]'' (2010) and ''[[Welcome to the Freakshow (Hinder album)|Welcome to the Freakshow]]'' (2012).  Cody Hanson, along with former lead singer Austin Winkler, wrote the majority of the band's music on their first four albums.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ultimate-guitar.com/interviews/interviews/hinder_we_bring_the_fun_back_into_rock-n-roll.html|title=Hinder: 'We Bring The Fun Back Into Rock-n-Roll!'|website=Ultimate-Guitar.com|date=23 September 2006|access-date=1 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100505085315/http://www.ultimate-guitar.com/interviews/interviews/hinder_we_bring_the_fun_back_into_rock-n-roll.html|archive-date=5 May 2010}}</ref> After Winkler left the band in 2013, they looked for a new lead vocalist, and added [[Marshal Dutton]]. They have since released: ''[[When The Smoke Clears]]'' (2015) and ''[[The Reign (Hinder album)|The Reign]]'' (2017) with their new vocalist. Their seventh studio album is currently in the works. The band was inducted into the [[Oklahoma Music Hall of Fame]] in 2007.

==History==
===Formation and ''Far From Close'': 2001‚Äì2004===
Before the band formed, Austin Winkler sang in an [[Oklahoma City]] cover band until July 2001,<ref name="songwriter interview"/> when he met drummer Cody Hanson and lead guitarist Joe Garvey at a college party.<ref name="yahoo">{{cite web|url=http://new.music.yahoo.com/hinder/biography/|title=Hinder Biography|publisher=Yahoo! Music|access-date=10 May 2010}}</ref> Soon after meeting, the three formed Hinder. Hanson commented on Austin Winkler shortly after the band formed saying he was "blown away" and "he has the kind of charisma very few people have and that unique voice. You can't really compare him to anybody." After recruiting bassist Cole Parker, they recorded a 4 track demo disc, with the track listing "Someday", "Like Me", "Broken", and "Worthless Home". The tracks "Someday" and "Broken" would later be re-recorded and put on their debut EP ''[[Far From Close]]'' and the other two tracks "Like Me" and "Worthless Home" still remain unreleased, only being able to find them on their demo CDs, although they are now posted on [[YouTube]]. The band began performing at an Oklahoma City club called The Blue Note, building a local fan base. Profits from the shows went to advertising, as well as paying the expenses for the group's first release. In April 2003, Hinder entered the March Bandness contest for [[Oklahoma City]] radio station [[KREF-FM|KHBZ-FM]] (94.7). They made it to the Final Four out of a field of thirty-two, ultimately losing to the OKC group Falcon Five-O. After saving enough money from local concerts the band's debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''Far From Close'' was released in 2003 on the independent label Brickden Records and sold around 5,000 copies.<ref name="Hall of Fame">{{cite web|url=http://www.omhof.com/Inductees/BYYEAR/tabid/86/ItemID/11/Default.aspx|title=Hinder - 2007 Inductee|publisher=Oklahoma Music Hall of Fame|access-date=21 March 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715001929/http://www.omhof.com/Inductees/BYYEAR/tabid/86/ItemID/11/Default.aspx|archive-date=15 July 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> Bassist Mike Rodden was recruited from Oklahoma City band Shade Seven and rhythm guitarist Mark King joined the band later that year.<ref name="songwriter interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.songwriteruniverse.com/hinder123.htm|title=Austin Winkler Talks About The Band's Success Story|publisher=Songwriter Universe|access-date=10 May 2010}}</ref>

===''Extreme Behavior'' and ''Take It to the Limit'': 2005‚Äì2009===
After the release of ''Far from Close'', Hinder was offered record deals by [[Atlantic Records]], [[Roadrunner Records]], and [[Universal Records]], eventually signing with Universal Records in 2005;<ref name="Hall of Fame"/> later that year, their major-label debut album, ''[[Extreme Behavior]]'', was released.<ref name="allmusic bio">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p741890|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=5 June 2010}}</ref> The album was produced by [[Brian Howes]], who, along with Cody Hanson and Austin Winkler, wrote most of the material on the album.<ref name="allmusic review">{{cite web|url = {{Allmusic|class=album|id=r795114|pure_url=yes}}|title = Review|publisher = Allmusic|access-date = 5 June 2010}}</ref> The album was engineered by [[Mike Fraser (record producer)|Mike Fraser]] and [[Jay Van Poederooyen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/jason-jvp-vanpoederooyen-mn0000652771 |title=Jason "JVP" VanPoederooyen {{pipe}} Credits |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=2015-05-19}}</ref> The album reached triple-platinum certification for sales,<ref name="allmusic bio"/><ref>Falina, Melanie.[http://www.unratedmagazine.com/articles/Archive/2009/Hinder_Joe-Garvey.cfm An Interview with Hinder's Joe "Blower" Garvey] unratedmagazine.com. Retrieved on 2010-07-16.</ref>

Hinder toured and released singles in support of their first album. Hinder's debut single, "[[Get Stoned]]", was released in October 2005. The band's second single, "[[Lips of an Angel]]", entered charts in the US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and Singapore; the song lasted for 41 weeks in the New Zealand charts, two weeks of which were spent at the number one spot. The band's third single, "[[How Long (Hinder song)|How Long]]", was released in September 2006 and appeared at number six on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Mainstream Rock Tracks]] chart. Their second song to be released in New Zealand was "[[Better Than Me (Hinder song)|Better Than Me]]", which spent three weeks on the New Zealand charts, peaking at number 16. In 2007, Hinder was inducted into the [[Oklahoma Music Hall of Fame]],<ref name="Hall of Fame"/> and in October of the same year, the band released their limited edition CD/DVD of ''Extreme Behavior'' entitled ''You Can't Make This S**t Up''.

The first single from the band's second album, "[[Use Me (Hinder song)|Use Me]]", was released on July 15, 2008 and peaked at No.&nbsp;3 on the US Mainstream Rock Tracks chart. The second album, ''[[Take It to the Limit (Hinder album)|Take It to the Limit]]'', was released on November 4, 2008; the album marked a change in style in Hinder's music in that it moved the band in a slightly more [[glam metal]]-influenced direction. The title track features [[M√∂tley Cr√ºe]]'s [[Mick Mars]] on guitar. They announced the release of the album's second single, "[[Without You (Hinder song)|Without You]]," through their official MySpace page on September 13, 2008. ''Take It To the Limit'' debuted at number four on the Billboard 200 with 81,000 sales its first week and ended up going Gold in the US

Through late 2008, Hinder headlined the [[J√§germeister Music Tour]] with [[Trapt]] and [[Rev Theory]] also on the bill. In early 2009, Hinder embarked on [[M√∂tley Cr√ºe]]'s [[Saints Of Los Angeles Tour]], which also included [[Theory of a Deadman]] and [[The Last Vegas]]. In July, Hinder embarked on [[Nickelback]]'s [[Dark Horse Tour]], which ran through [[Live Nation]] outdoor amphitheaters; other bands involved on the tour were [[Papa Roach]], [[Breaking Benjamin]] and [[Saving Abel]]. On March 27, 2013 ''Take It To The Limit'' was certified gold in the US.

===''All American Nightmare'' and ''Welcome to the Freakshow'': 2010‚Äì2013===
During early 2010, the band was engaged in writing and recording material for their third album,<ref name="rockstar"/> titled ''All American Nightmare''.<ref name="nightmare tunelab">{{cite web|url=http://tunelab.com/2010/05/28/hinder-title-new-album/|title=Hinder Title New Album|website=Tunelab.com|date=28 May 2010|access-date=17 June 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100603040052/http://tunelab.com/2010/05/28/hinder-title-new-album/|archive-date=3 June 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album was released on December 7, 2010, with the first single, the title track, released on September 14, 2010.<ref name="rockstar">{{cite web|url=http://www.rockstarweekly.com/hinder-halfway-through-recording-new-album.html|title=Hinder Halfway Through Recording New Album|publisher=Rockstar Weekly|access-date=17 July 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100728204524/http://www.rockstarweekly.com/hinder-halfway-through-recording-new-album.html|archive-date=28 July 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=HINDER - LATEST NEWS|url=http://www.hindermusic.com/home/index.shtml|publisher=Hinder official website|access-date=8 September 2010|date=1 September 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100910014302/http://www.hindermusic.com/home/index.shtml|archive-date=10 September 2010}}</ref> Although the album was originally planned to be produced by [[Howard Benson]], [[Kevin Churko]] was later announced to be the producer.<ref name="rockstar"/>

Winkler said that the group started recording during the touring cycle for ''Take It To The Limit''. "We wrote 70 or so songs, recorded about 50 and cut it down to 12," the singer explained. "It's something that we've never done before, so it's like our ultimate baby. A lot of the [other] songs could be shit, too, we don't know. You don't really know until you put it out there and have more than two people's opinion on it." Both he and drummer Cody Hanson mentioned the song "Memory" as one that was a strong contender for the final cut. "It's about being with your chick ‚Äî the chick you've been with for a while, not some chick you've met on the road ‚Äî and about being able to still have a good time with your wife or long-time girlfriend," said Hanson. "I've been in a relationship myself for nine years, so it was cool to write a song like that."<ref>{{cite web|title=Hinder Write A Box Set's Worth Of Material For New Album|url=http://www.bryanreesman.com/blog/2010/11/18/hinder-write-a-box-sets-worth-of-material-for-new-album/|publisher=Attention Deficit Delirium|access-date=18 January 2011|date=18 November 2010}}</ref>

On August 9, 2012, Hinder announced via Facebook and Twitter that their then-upcoming album would be titled ''[[Welcome to the Freakshow (Hinder album)|Welcome to the Freakshow]]''. It was released on December 4, 2012. Hinder released their first single, "Save Me", on August 30.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hinder Music on Facebook|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151106909180489&set=a.442162565488.204000.41532430488&type=1|publisher=Hinder via Facebook|access-date=August 9, 2012|date=August 9, 2012}}</ref> On July 10, 2013 it was announced that lead singer Austin Winkler had entered rehab, and would not be touring with the band for the remainder of the year. Marshal Dutton, one of the band's producers, was brought in to fill in on lead vocals for a couple shows until Jared Weeks, of [[Saving Abel]], joined the band to fill in for Winkler for the rest of the tour.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://loudwire.com/hinder-austin-winkler-rehab-saving-abel-jared-weeks-fills-in-tour/ |title=Hinder Frontman Austin Winkler Enters Rehab, Saving Abel's Jared Weeks Fills In On Tour |website=Loudwire.com |date=July 12, 2013 |access-date=July 15, 2014 |first=Mary |last=Ouellette}}</ref> On November 20, 2013, it was announced via [[Loudwire]] that Austin Winkler had left the band.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://loudwire.com/hinder-part-ways-frontman-austin-winkler/ |title=Hinder Officially Part Ways With Frontman Austin Winkler |website=Loudwire.com |date=November 20, 2013 |access-date=December 26, 2013 |first=Mary |last=Ouellette}}</ref>

===''When the Smoke Clears'' and ''The Reign'': 2014‚Äì2017===
On July 7, 2014 the band posted a video on their social media sites apologizing to fans for their inactivity and to announce that they had been writing new songs for the new album while searching for a new lead singer. They also announced a string of shows throughout the rest of the summer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/video.php?v=10152549979990489&set=vr.10152549979990489&type=2&theater |title=Hinder |website=Facebook.com |access-date=2015-05-19}}</ref> Although never announced officially, the band played their shows with Nashville-based singer Nolan Neal on lead vocals.

On December 9, 2014 Hinder officially announced their new album, and that it would be titled ''[[When The Smoke Clears]]''. The album is slated to be released in the spring of 2015.<ref name="facebook1">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Hinder?fref=nf |title=Hinder |website=Facebook.com |access-date=2015-05-19}}</ref> On January 20, 2015 Hinder officially announced that Marshal Dutton would be their new lead singer, via social media sites. Dutton has been affiliated with the band since his former band [[Faktion]] toured with Hinder and [[Rev Theory]] on the Girls Gone Wild tour back in 2006. He had also been writing and recording for the band since 2009 when they were going through the writing and recording sessions for ''All American Nightmare''. Dutton had been working on every Hinder album since, even co-producing ''Welcome to the Freakshow'' alongside drummer Cody Hanson. On March 17, 2015 the band released a preview of the first song on their record, "Rather Hate Than Hurt" via loudwire, making it the second song to be released since founding and former vocalist Austin Winkler departed the band in November 2013. The band also revealed their new album ''[[When The Smoke Clears]]'' will be released on May 12, 2015.<ref name="loudwire1">{{cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/hinder-rather-hate-than-hurt-exclusive-song-premiere/ |title=Hinder, 'Rather Hate Than Hurt' - Exclusive Song Premiere |website=Loudwire.com |date=2015-03-16 |access-date=2015-05-19}}</ref> The band went on tour in support of the new album starting in March 2015, they did a Summer tour throughout the U.S. with [[Full Devil Jacket]] and AGES APART supporting them. In November 2015, Hinder announced their Winter tour with [[Shaman's Harvest]], [[Within Reason]], Sons Of Texas and AGES APART.

After playing a show at the venue "Ziggy's By The Sea" in [[Wilmington, North Carolina]] the group did an interview with The Baltimore Sun and talked about the band's place over the course of the past year. The group stated that they had no choice but to find a new lead singer in order to keep the band alive.<ref name="baltimoresun">{{cite web|url=http://www.baltimoresun.com/entertainment/music/midnight-sun-blog/bs-ae-hinder-20160106-story.html|title=Settling in with new frontman, Hinder ignores all the hate|author=Baltimore Sun|date=7 January 2016|work=baltimoresun.com|access-date=3 April 2016}}</ref> The band went on to say that the change in singers was a fresh start for both Hinder and Austin Winkler, and that they are searching for another hit song as big as "Lips of an Angel".<ref name="baltimoresun"/>

On March 7, 2016 the band announced that their upcoming acoustic EP would be titled ''[[Stripped (Hinder album)|Stripped]]'' and will be released May 13, 2016. The EP will feature six tracks with one newly recorded track titled "[[Not an Addict]]", which was originally recorded by [[K's Choice]].<ref name="blabbermouth.net">{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/hinder-to-release-stripped-acoustic-ep/|title=Hinder To Release 'Stripped' Acoustic Ep - Blabbermouth.net|work=BLABBERMOUTH.NET|access-date=3 April 2016}}</ref>

The band went out on tour in support of the new EP on the "Stripped Tour" with new rock band [[Like a Storm]] as the opening act. the tour consisted of 22 shows over the course of June through July 2016.<ref name="blabbermouth.net"/> On March 2, 2017 the band announced a summer tour with [[Nonpoint]]. On June 12, 2017 the band finally announced the title of their sixth studio album ''[[The Reign (Hinder album)|The Reign]]'', which was released on August 11, 2017. On August 29, 2017 it was revealed the remaining original members of Hinder were suing former lead singer Austin Winkler for [[trademark infringement]]. The lawsuit alleges Winkler is currently promoting his own performances for his fall 2017 solo tour and music by the "unlawful use and exploitation of the Hinder trademark," and the group are asking Winkler to cease infringement and to pay over "all gains, profits and advantages realized from his infringing."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/hinder-sues-former-lead-singer-austin-john-winkler/|title=Hinder Sue Former Lead Singer Austin John Winkler|website=[[Loudwire]]|access-date=1 April 2018}}</ref> In support of their new album ''[[The Reign (Hinder album)|The Reign]]'', on September 22, 2017 Hinder announced a fall co-headlining tour with [[Josh Todd & the Conflict]]. The tour will run from October through December with Wayland and [[Adelitas Way]] supporting.<ref name="loudwire.com">{{cite web|author=Chad Childers |url=http://loudwire.com/josh-todd-the-conflict-hinder-co-headlining-tour-wayland-adelitas-way/ |title=Josh Todd & the Conflict + Hinder Announce Co-Headlining Tour |website=Loudwire.com |access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref>

===Break from touring and new music: 2018‚Äìpresent===
Throughout the winter and spring of 2018 drummer [[Cody Hanson]] and vocalist [[Marshal Dutton]] have been working on new music for a yet-untitled project. They've released numerous song clips throughout social media including a new Hinder song titled "Halo".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BiRw5XDhsN9/ |title=Hinder on Instagram: "Here's a quick preview of a new song we're working on! Do you like it or love it? Let us know! üòÅ #hinder #studiolife #studioflow" |website=Instagram.com |access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://intriguemag.com/2018/06/21/cody-hanson-and-marshal-dutton-of-hinder-discuss-new-music-and-their-latest-approach/|title=Cody Hanson and Marshal Dutton of Hinder Discuss New Music and Their Latest Approach ‚Äì Intrigue|date=19 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180719024319/https://intriguemag.com/2018/06/21/cody-hanson-and-marshal-dutton-of-hinder-discuss-new-music-and-their-latest-approach/|access-date=11 October 2020|archive-date=2018-07-19}}</ref>

In December 2018, the band announced a winter 2019 tour with [[Soil (American band)|Soil]]. In early January 2019, the band announced their new single titled "Halo" would be released January 25; this single serves as their lead single for their untitled upcoming seventh studio album which was to be released later that year. In July 2019 the band released a cover of the hit song [[Life in the Fast Lane]]. The song was released as a single to purchase and stream wherever music is sold.

In February 2020 Hinder announced an Extreme Behavior 15th-anniversary tour for the summer and fall of 2020. The tour was delayed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].
