'''Marece Benjamin Richards'''<ref>{{cite web| url= https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/work/371531316| title= ACE Repertory: 371531316 "Good Things"| website= [[ASCAP]].com| access-date= 2020-01-06}}</ref> (born September 2, 1983),<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.discogs.com/artist/Rich+Boy| website= Discogs.com |title= Rich Boy Discography }}</ref> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Rich Boy''', is an American [[rapper]] from [[Mobile, Alabama]]. He is best known for the hit single "[[Throw Some D's]]" (featuring [[Polow da Don]]) released in August 2006. It was the lead single for his [[Rich Boy (album)|eponymous debut album]], released a year later in March under Polow da Don's [[Zone 4 (record label)|Zone 4]] and [[Interscope Records]].

In 2009, his follow-up single "[[Drop (Rich Boy song)|Drop]]" became popular online through remixes and freestyles from then-upcoming internet rappers, including [[Donald Glover|Childish Gambino]], [[Kid Cudi]], and [[Earl Sweatshirt]].

==Early life and education==
Richards is from [[Alabama]].<ref name= reclaiming>{{cite news| url= https://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.2066/title.rich-boy-explains-reclaiming-his-creative-control-on-break-the-pot| title= Rich Boy Explains Reclaiming His Creative Control On 'Break The Pot'| date= March 27, 2013 | first= Sha'Linda |last= Jeanin| access-date= January 6, 2020}}</ref> He was studying mechanical engineering at [[Tuskegee University]], but he dropped out to concentrate on pursuing a rap career.

== Career ==
Rich Boy signed a contract with [[Zone 4 (record label)|Zone 4]] through [[Interscope Records]] and started to work on his debut album.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Kellman|first=Andy|title=Rich Boy: Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p732818/biography|pure_url=yes}}| work= Allmusic |year= 2007| access-date= January 22, 2010}}</ref> In the meantime, he appeared on one of Ludacris' [[Disturbing tha Peace]] compilations and on a few mixtapes, such as ''[[Comeback Season (mixtape)|Comeback Season]]'' by Canadian rapper [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]] and ''Da Bottom, Vol. 5'' by DJ Ideal and [[Jermaine Dupri]].

On March 13, 2007, Rich Boy released his self-titled debut album, with the singles "[[Throw Some D's]]," "[[Boy Looka Here]]," "''Good Things''", and "[[Let's Get This Paper]]". "Throw Some D's" reached #6 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], #3 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]], and #2 on the [[Hot Rap Tracks]] chart. "Boy Looka Here" and "Good Things" both peaked around #50 on the R&B chart.<ref name="Billboard singles">{{cite web| url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p732818/charts-awards/billboard-singles|pure_url=yes}}|title=Rich Boy: Charts & Awards: Billboard Singles| work= Allmusic| access-date= January 22, 2010}}</ref> In 2009 he released a single, "Top of the World", which was part of the album [[Music Inspired by More Than a Game|Music Inspired by]] ''[[More Than a Game]]''.

Rich Boy's second album was intended in 2010 to be titled ''Resurrected in Diamonds'' and was slated for a 2013 release.<ref name= Dre-Diamonds>{{cite web| url= https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.11833/title.dr-dre-to-produce-on-rich-boys-album | title= Dr. Dre To Produce On Rich Boy's Album| date= August 1, 2010| first= Roman | last= Cooper| website= hiphopdx.com| access-date= January 6, 2020}}</ref> The intended first single released off the album was "[[Drop (Rich Boy song)|Drop]]" which had been released in the beginning of early 2009. Another track "She Luvs Me (She Luvs Mi Knot)" featuring [[Polow da Don]] was released on June 21, 2010. Both [[Dr. Dre]] and [[Polow da Don]] were confirmed contributors or producers for the album.<ref name= Dre-Diamonds />

On March 18, 2013, Rich Boy released the mixtape ''Back to Class'' in promotion of his second studio album, ''[[Break the Pot]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://rapradar.com/2013/03/18/new-mixtape-rich-boy-back-to-class/ |title=New Mixtape: Rich Boy Back To Class |publisher=Rap Radar |date=March 18, 2013 |access-date= March 22, 2013}}</ref> On January 14, 2013, the title track was released as a single in promotion of the album, featuring Hemi.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/break-pot-feat.-hemi-single/id593073146 |title=iTunes - Music - Break the Pot (feat. Hemi) - Single by Rich Boy |website= Itunes.apple.com |date=January 14, 2013 |access-date=March 22, 2013}}</ref> On February 25, 2013, it was announced that ''Break the Pot'' would be released on April 9, 2013, and would feature guest appearances from [[Maino (rapper)|Maino]], Mista Raja, [[Bobby V]], Doe B, Playboi Lo and Smash.<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.xclusiveszone.net/2013/02/rich-boy-announces-break-the-pot-album-release-date/ |title=Rich Boy Announces "Break The Pot" Album Release Date |publisher= Xclusives Zone |date=February 25, 2013 |access-date=March 22, 2013}}</ref> On March 17, the music video for "Break the Pot" premiered on ''[[MTV Jams]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/videos/rich-boy/888932/break-the-pot.jhtml |title=Break The Pot &#124; Rich Boy &#124; Music Video |publisher=MTV |date=March 17, 2013 |access-date=March 22, 2013}}</ref> ''Break the Pot'' was released on April 9, 2013, and included the single "Pimp On", which featured Doe B, Playboi Lo and Smash.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/break-the-pot/626044007|title=Break the Pot by Rich Boy|via=music.apple.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rich-boy-pimp-on-song.1308813.html| title= Rich Boy - Pimp On Feat. Doe B, Playboi Lo & Smash| first= Trevor| last= Smith |date= 2013-04-03 | website= hotnewhiphop.com| access-date= 2020-01-06}}</ref>
