'''Ice Nine Kills''' (sometimes stylized as '''ICE NINE KILLS''' or abbreviated to '''INK''', and formerly known as '''Ice Nine''') is an American [[heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band from [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]] who are signed to [[Fearless Records]]. Best known for its [[Horror and terror|horror]]-inspired lyrics, Ice Nine Kills formed in its earliest incarnation in 2000 by high school friends Spencer Charnas and Jeremy Schwartz. Charnas is currently the only remaining founding member.

To date, Ice Nine Kills has released three EPs along with six full-length studio albums: ''[[Last Chance to Make Amends]]'', ''[[Safe Is Just a Shadow]]'', ''[[The Predator Becomes the Prey]]'', their most successful albums to date; ''[[Every Trick in the Book]]'', which peaked at number 122 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]; ''[[The Silver Scream]]'', which peaked at number 29;<ref>{{cite web

== History ==

=== 2000â€“2009: Formation and ''Last Chance to Make Amends'' ===
Ice Nine Kills was founded in 2000 under the name "Ice Nine" by high school friends Spencer Charnas and Jeremy Schwartz, before later recruiting drummer Grant Newsted in 2003 and swapped out their then-bassist (Patrick Morse) for Hobie Boeschenstein in 2004,<ref>{{cite web |title=Norma Jean This Or The Apocalypse, Ice Nine Kills |url=https://www.thecastletheatre.com/event/134583-norma-jean-bloomington/ |access-date=June 23, 2018}}</ref> but changed their name to Ice Nine Kills just before releasing their debut independent album ''Last Chance to Make Amends'' on April 20, 2006.<ref name="lctmaitunes">{{cite web|title = Last Chance to Make Amends by ICE NINE KILLS|url = https://www.amazon.com/Last-Chance-Make-Amends-Kills/dp/B000JVS4W6|work = Amazon|publisher = Amazon}}</ref> However, they did record a demo, entitled "The Pop-Punk-Ska Years", in 2002, which would eventually be released in 2009.<ref name="AM">{{cite web |last1=Heaney |first1=Gregory |title=Ice Nine Kills Biography by Gregory Heaney |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ice-nine-kills-mn0002003366/biography |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=June 23, 2018}}</ref>

The following year on November 20, 2007, Ice Nine Kills released their EP ''The Burning'' through Red Blue Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Nine Kills- The Burning |url=http://redbluerecords.myshopify.com/products/ice-nine-kills-the-burning |work=Red Blue Records Online Store |publisher=Red Blue Records |access-date=June 5, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110316093514/http://redbluerecords.myshopify.com/products/ice-nine-kills-the-burning |archive-date=March 16, 2011 }}</ref> The band then set out on several national tours in support of the EP, including opening slots on tours with [[I See Stars]] and [[Eyes Set to Kill]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Gauntlet - Ice Nine Kills|url=http://www.thegauntlet.com/bio/3956/Ice-Nine-Kills.html|work=The Gauntlet|access-date=June 5, 2011}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=August 2014}} By 2008, the group had been invited to open for well-known acts such as [[As I Lay Dying (band)|As I Lay Dying]], [[A Day to Remember]], [[Thursday (band)|Thursday]], [[Paramore]] and a one-off performance on the [[Taste of Chaos]] 2009 tour.<ref name=ferretdebut/> That same year, Ice Nine Kills released 2 acoustic tracks ("The Simple Act of Giving Up" and "Lifetime in a Week") on an EP titled ''2 Song Acoustic''.<ref>{{cite web|title=2 Song Acoustic: Ice Nine Kills|url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B003C39XGO|website=Amazon.com|access-date=June 5, 2011}}</ref>

In mid-2009, Schwartz decided to leave the band after struggling with life on the road, leaving Charnas as the only original member.<ref>{{cite web|title = Interview with Ice Nine Kills frontman Spencer Charnas|url = http://www.hellhoundmusic.com/interview-with-ice-nine-kills-front-man-spencer-charnas/|website = Hellhound Music|access-date = February 6, 2016}}</ref> Charnas then recruited former members of the Rochester NY based [[post-hardcore]] band Remember Tomorrow which had recently disbanded<ref>{{cite web|title = Ice Nine Kills signs with Fearless Records|url = http://marblehead.wickedlocal.com/article/20151022/news/151027969|website = Marblehead Reporter|access-date = February 21, 2016|first = Mary Reines|last = mreines@wickedlocal.com}}</ref> and stopped playing rhythm guitar. This shifted Ice Nine Kills' sound into more of an experimental metalcore sound to which [[Ferret Music]] took notice and signed the band in the spring of 2009 and after their appearance on [[Warped Tour 2009]] the band began writing and recording their next record.<ref name=ferretdebut>{{cite web|title=Ferret Music to release Ice Nine Kills album|url=http://www.lambgoat.com/news/view.aspx?id=12649|website=Lambgoat.com|access-date=June 5, 2011}}</ref>

=== 2010â€“2013: ''Safe Is Just a Shadow'' and ''The Predator'' ===

Ice Nine Kills released their second full-length album ''Safe Is Just a Shadow'' on July 12, 2010.<ref>{{Citation|title = Safe Is Just A Shadow|url = https://www.amazon.com/Safe-Is-Just-A-Shadow/dp/B003UNKFQ8|publisher = RED BLUE RECORDS|date = July 2, 2010|access-date = February 6, 2016|last = Ice nine kills}}</ref> Vocalist Dave Sieling, who has since left the band, contributed clean vocals along with Spencer Charnas. The album received praise from heavy metal magazine, ''[[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]]'' in the 2010 Hottest Chicks in Rock Issue and subsequently the band was invited to attend the ''Revolver'' Golden Gods awards in Los Angeles CA on April 20, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Nine Kills at the Golden Gods released|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yfd6Ocv7XOI |work=AllMusicRelated|access-date=April 5, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Nine Kills new album "Safe is just a shadow" released|url=https://54.254.31.231/|work=AllMusicRelated|access-date=June 5, 2011}}{{dead link|date=November 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The band supported ''Safe Is Just a Shadow'' with a two-week appearance on [[Warped Tour 2010]],<ref name=warpedtour2010>{{cite web|last=Gannaio|first=Anthony|title=The Vans Warped Tour 2010 Experience|url=http://thenewreview.net/show-reviews/the-vans-warped-tour-2010-experience|work=The NewReview|publisher=TheNewReview|access-date=June 5, 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110224153744/http://thenewreview.net/show-reviews/the-vans-warped-tour-2010-experience|archive-date=February 24, 2011}}</ref> and a supporting slot with Michigan-based [[nu metal]] band [[Taproot (band)|Taproot]].{{citation needed|date=August 2014}} The band performed on a headlining tour in the summer of 2011 as well as a performance at the Darien Lake NY stop of [[Warped Tour 2011]] on the Dzambo Stage.<ref>{{cite web|title=Exclusive Warped Tour Dzambo Stage Announced|url=http://rewritethescene.com/news/rts-exclusive-warped-tour-dzambo-stage-announced/ |date=June 2011}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=August 2014}} The band released a music video inspired by the film ''[[Inglourious Basterds]]'' for their song "The People Under the Stairs" on June 20, 2011. That same day the band announced their partnership with Outerloop Management who represent [[We Came as Romans]], [[Refused]], and [[Periphery (band)|Periphery]] among others.{{citation needed|date=August 2014}}

On May 12, 2012, it was announced that the band would be the opening act for the 2012 edition of The All Stars Tour featuring [[Suicide Silence]], [[The Word Alive]], [[Dance Gavin Dance]], [[I See Stars]], and [[Attila (metalcore band)|Attila]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Nine Kills Added to All Stars Tour|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2747362 |work=AbsolutePunk|access-date=May 23, 2012}}</ref> Prior to The All Stars Tour the band supported [[Like Moths To Flames]] on their first headlining tour in Canada in early June as well as a small midwest run supporting [[Norma Jean (band)|Norma Jean]] in July.{{citation needed|date=August 2014}}

It was announced on August 16, 2012, that the band would be supporting [[We Came As Romans]] and  [[Abandon All Ships]] for a Canadian Tour taking place September 11 through the 17th.{{citation needed|date=August 2014}} The band would close out 2012 by taking part in the "Party with The Devil" tour with [[Attila (metal band)|Attila]], Make Me Famous, [[Issues (band)|Issues]] and Adestria from October 26 to November 15, followed by a short run with [[Like Moths To Flames]] from December 2 to 9.{{citation needed|date=August 2014}}

On November 22, 2012, Ice Nine Kills released an official lyric video for their new single "The Coffin is Moving".<ref>{{Citation|title = ICE NINE KILLS - The Coffin Is Moving (Official Lyric Video)|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LnFnAJhJip4|date = November 22, 2012|access-date = February 6, 2016|last = iceninekilled}}</ref> On that same date, they launched a [[Kickstarter]] campaign to raise money to fund their New EP. On December 22 the band announced that they had raised over $21,000 for their Kickstarter campaign and that their new EP "The Predator" would be released on January 15, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title = Ice Nine Kills EP/CD RELEASE FUND!|url = https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/2039010930/ice-nine-kills-ep-cd-release-fund|website = Kickstarter|access-date = February 6, 2016|language = en-US}}</ref> To celebrate this announcement they released a second single from the EP entitled "What I Never Learned In Study Hall" featuring guest vocals from [[Tyler Carter]] vocalist of [[Issues (band)|Issues]].<ref>{{Citation|title = ICE NINE KILLS - What I Never Learned In Study Hall (ft. === 2018â€“2021: ''The Silver Scream'' and ''The Silver Scream: Final Cut'' re-issue===

On June 20, 2018, Ice Nine Kills released "The American Nightmare", the first single from their upcoming album ''[[The Silver Scream]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bringthenoise.com/blog/ice-nine-kills-debut-eerie-new-standalone-single|title=Ice Nine Kills Debut Eerie New Standalone Single "The American Nightmare" / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=2020-03-29}}</ref> It was accompanied by a music video inspired by the film ''[[A Nightmare on Elm Street]]'' (1984).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bringthenoise.com/blog/ice-nine-kills-debut-nightmare-on-elm|title=Ice Nine Kills Debut 'Nightmare On Elm Street'-Inspired&nbsp;Visual For "The American Nightmare" / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=2020-03-29}}</ref> ''The Silver Scream'' was released on October 5, 2018, and marked the band's highest chart debut to date. Less Than Jake provided the brass instrument section of the song to give it a "''sinister, carnival, circus vibe"''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2018/09/24/spencer-charnas-ice-nine-kills-thrills-chills-and-silver-scream-spills/|title=Spencer Charnas â€“ Ice Nine Kills 'Thrills, Chills and Silver Scream Spills'|last=Hurcom|first=Carys|date=September 24, 2018|website=Wall of Sound}}</ref> The album also features a track called "Rocking the Boat", in which the band namedrops all of their previous releases and former singer Jeremy Schwartz makes a guest appearance.

=== 2021â€“present: ''The Silver Scream 2: Welcome to Horrorwood'' ===
{{main|The Silver Scream 2: Welcome to Horrorwood}}
When teasing their single "Hip to Be Scared", inspired by the cult classic thriller ''[[American Psycho (film)|American Psycho]]'', the title of Ice Nine Kills' new album was listed in the credits as ''[[The Silver Scream 2: Welcome to Horrorwood]].''<ref name="Brown">{{Cite web|last=Brown|first=Paul 'Browny'|date=2021-07-08|title=Looks Like Ice Nine Kills Are Announcing A Sequel to The Silver Scream!|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2021/07/08/looks-like-ice-nine-kills-are-announcing-a-sequel-to-the-silver-scream/|access-date=2021-07-08|website=Wall Of Sound|language=en-US}}</ref> The album was released on October 15, 2021.

The single, which features [[Papa Roach]] frontman [[Jacoby Shaddix]], was released on July 9, along with an accompanying music video.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Brown|first=Paul 'Browny'|date=2021-07-08|title=Ice Nine Kills Drop â€˜Hip To Be Scaredâ€™ feat. Jacoby Shaddix|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2021/07/09/ice-nine-kills-drop-hip-to-be-scared-feat-jacoby-shaddix/|access-date=2021-08-04|website=Wall Of Sound|language=en-US}}</ref>

On August 3, 2021, the band released a teaser video featuring the album's track listing, leading fans to predict and speculate the horror/thriller movies that inspired the songs on the album.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Brown|first=Paul 'Browny'|date=2021-08-04|title=Let The Silver Scream 2 Song Inspiration Speculation Begin...|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2021/08/04/let-the-silver-scream-2-song-inspiration-speculation-begin/|access-date=2021-08-04|website=Wall Of Sound|language=en-US}}</ref>

The band started releasing music videos leading up to the release of the album on July 9 with "Hip to Be Scared". The video started a storyline for the album that continued on August 9 with "Assault & Batteries", which is inspired by the original ''[[Child's Play (1988 film)|Child's Play]]'' film. The video continued the storyline and revealed more than "Hip to Be Scared". The storyline continued on September 9 with "Rainy Day", which is inspired by the ''[[Resident Evil (game)|Resident Evil]]'' game series. Like "Assault and Batteries". the video showed more to the storyline and even gave a plot. The plot of the storyline is that the lead singer of the band is arrested for murders he committed in the videos and an officer is trying to get evidence before the trial happens. The storyline segments of the videos also have cameos by James A. Janisse and Chelsea Rebecca from the YouTube channel Dead Meat, along with [[Bill Moseley]]. The final single that was released was "Funeral Derangements" which was based on the cult-classic book [[Pet Sematary]] by [[Stephen King]], and the [[Pet Sematary (1989 film)|movie]] which it inspired. It features the original actor of Gage Creed, [[Miko Hughes]], who this time plays the truck driver instead. This continues the story of the music videos being released, showing that The Silence killed Nadia with three stabs to the chest, two to the stomach, and one to the back.{{Citation needed|date=November 2021}}

==Musical style==
The group originally pursued a style of [[ska-punk]]<ref name="AM"/> influenced by [[alternative rock]] and [[pop punk]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://iceninekills.bandcamp.com/album/last-chance-to-make-amends|title=Last Chance To Make Amends, by Ice Nine Kills|website=Ice Nine Kills|access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref> but they have since shifted into a style which has been described as [[metalcore]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/listen-to-the-new-ice-nine-kills-album|title=Listen To The New Ice Nine Kills Album - News - Rock Sound Magazine|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=January 18, 2018}}</ref> [[melodic hardcore]],<ref name="AM"/> [[post-hardcore]],<ref name="SWM">{{cite web|url=https://shockwavemagazine.com/ice-nine-kills-release-silver-scream-via-fearless-records-october-5/| title=Ice Nine Kills to Release The Silver Scream via Fearless Records on October 5|website=Shockwave Magazine| date= June 29, 2018}}</ref> [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Newton |first1=Caleb |title=Album Review: Ice Nine Kills â€“ The Silver Scream |url=https://newnoisemagazine.com/review-ice-nine-kills-the-silver-scream/ |access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref> and [[symphonic metal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.courant.com/entertainment/music/hc-ice-nine-kills-webster-underground-20161123-story.html |title=Ice Nine Kills Plays Webster - Hartford Courant |website=Courant.com |access-date=January 18, 2018}}</ref>

The band's latest album, ''[[The Silver Scream 2: Welcome to Horrorwood]]'', retains its predecessor's metalcore sound,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://distortedsoundmag.com/album-review-the-silver-scream-2-welcome-to-horrorwood-ice-nine-kills/|title=ALBUM REVIEW: The Silver Scream 2: Welcome To Horrorwood â€“ Ice Nine Kills|website=Distorted Sound|last=Ray|first=Graham|date=15 October 2021|access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> but otherwise is described as [[deathcore]] and [[emo pop]],<ref name="Kerrang!">{{Cite web|url=https://www.kerrang.com/reviews/album-review-ice-nine-kills-the-silver-scream-2-welcome-to-horrorwood/|title=Album review: Ice Nine Kills â€“ The Silver Scream 2: Welcome To Horrorwood|website=[[Kerrang!]]|last=Richardson|first=Jake|date=12 October 2021|access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> adding onto the less prominent post-hardcore, heavy metal, and symphonic metal sound of its predecessor.
