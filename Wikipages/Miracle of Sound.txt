'''Gavin Dunne''' (born May 6, 1980), better known by the name of his music project "'''Miracle of Sound'''", is an Irish [[indie music]]ian known for his music inspired by [[video games]], films, and TV shows.<ref name="nique">{{Cite web |url=http://nique.net/entertainment/2015/10/23/gavin-dunne-enchants-video-game-enthusiasts/ |title=Gavin Dunne enchants video game enthusiasts |last=Rule home country of Ireland, his music is very popular with the online [[gamer|gaming community]], and he has been described as Ireland's most successful indie musician.<ref name="examiner">{{Cite web ==History==
===Early life and origins of Miracle of Sound===
Dunne is the son of late Irish poet [[Sean Dunne (poet)|Se√°n Dunne]] and is from [[Cork (Ireland)|Cork]], Ireland.<ref name="thejournal" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://twitter.com/miracleofsound/status/596797249201516544 |title=Twitter comment by Gavin Dunne |date=8 May 2015 |website=Twitter |access-date=5 January 2017 |archive-date=30 August 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210830154734/https://twitter.com/miracleofsound/status/596797249201516544 |url-status=live }}</ref> Before starting Miracle of Sound, Dunne spent 15 years playing in various groups.<ref name="Russian Interview">{{cite web | url=http://old.mirf.ru/Articles/art5938.htm&usg=ALkJrhiXB0PU-i667AWlX07F4W-EZ0APvQ | title=–ú—É–∑—ã–∫–∞–ª—å–Ω—ã–π —Ü–µ–Ω—Ç—Ä | work=Issue 123 | publisher=[[Mir Fantastiki]] (Fantasy World Magazine) | date=1 November 2013 | access-date=18 October 2016 | archive-date=18 October 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018203309/http://old.mirf.ru/Articles/art5938.htm%26usg%3DALkJrhiXB0PU-i667AWlX07F4W-EZ0APvQ | url-status=live }}</ref>

As part of his previous band, ''Lotus Lullaby'', he and his bandmates competed in and won the Bank of Ireland National Student Music Awards in 2006,<ref name="Lotus Lulluby 1">{{cite web | url=http://www.hotpress.com/archive/2852903.html | title=Bank of Ireland National Student Music Awards' acts revealed | publisher=The Hot Press | date=2 March 2006 | access-date=23 October 2016 | archive-date=22 October 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161022224659/http://www.hotpress.com/archive/2852903.html | url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Lotus Lullaby 2">{{cite web | url=http://www.hotpress.com/National-Student-Music-Awards/music/reviews/live/Bank-Of-Ireland-National-Student-Music-Awards-live-at-TBMC-Dublin/2861588.html | title=Bank of Ireland National Student Music Awards live at TBMC, Dublin | publisher=The Hot Press | date=21 April 2006 | access-date=23 October 2016 | author=Cummins, Steve | archive-date=19 May 2017 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170519143838/http://www.hotpress.com/National-Student-Music-Awards/music/reviews/live/Bank-Of-Ireland-National-Student-Music-Awards-live-at-TBMC-Dublin/2861588.html | url-status=live }}</ref> as well as the Murphy's Battle of the Bands earlier the same year.<ref name="murphys">{{cite web | url=http://www.donal.ie/2006/04/04/murphys-battle-of-the-bands-final-2006/ | title=Murphy's Battle of the Bands Final 2006 | date=4 April 2006 | access-date=23 October 2016 | archive-date=23 October 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161023210701/http://www.donal.ie/2006/04/04/murphys-battle-of-the-bands-final-2006/ | url-status=live }}</ref>

After the break-up of ''Lotus Lullaby'', Dunne became inspired by the video games he was playing and wrote several songs based on his favourite games, intended on conveying the atmosphere of the game or the mood of the characters.<ref name="Russian Interview" /><ref name="Interview with gavin" /> He began with the song "Gordon Freeman Saved My Life",<ref name="Interview with gavin" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qMUyjvXpMV0 |title=Gordon Freeman Saved My Life! Half Life song by Miracle of Sound |location=Youtube |date=8 September 2010 |access-date=10 January 2017 |via=YouTube |archive-date=16 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161016145624/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qMUyjvXpMV0 |url-status=live }}</ref> based on the character from the [[Half-Life (series)|''Half-Life'' franchise]]. The song amassed 15,000 views in one night on YouTube, something Dunne had struggled to do for years.<ref name="Interview with gavin" /> He continued with "The Ballad of Clay Carmine", which had another positive reception, and Dunne realised that he had found a significant niche audience and he continued to write songs based on video games.<ref name="examiner"/><ref name="Russian Interview"/><ref name="Interview with gavin" />

One of these early songs, "Commander Shepard", based on the [[Commander Shepard|character]] from the ''[[Mass Effect]]'' series of the same name, went [[viral video|viral]] and eventually amassed millions of views, leading to a rapid rise in popularity for the musician.<ref name="examiner"/><ref name="Interview with gavin" /><ref name="Escapist-BioWare"/>

===Coordination with game developers===
Following his success with "Commander Shepard", Dunne eventually met with the marketing team from [[BioWare]], the creative team behind the ''Mass Effect'' series, while he was at [[gamescom]] in Germany in August 2011.<ref name="Interview with gavin">{{cite web | url=http://nightmaremode.thegamerstrust.com/2011/09/03/interview-with-gavin-dunne-of-miracle-of-sound/ | title=Interview with Gavin Dunne of Miracle of Sound | publisher=The gamer trust | date=3 September 2011 | access-date=23 October 2016 | author=Hedges, Jordan | archive-date=9 October 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161009184703/http://nightmaremode.thegamerstrust.com/2011/09/03/interview-with-gavin-dunne-of-miracle-of-sound/ | url-status=live }}</ref> There he released another ''Mass Effect'' song, "Normandy", based on the ending of ''[[Mass Effect 2]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.escapistmagazine.com/news/view/112180-Miracle-of-Sound-to-Premere-Mass-Effect-Song-Live-with-BioWare |title=Miracle of Sound to Premere &#91;sic&#93; Mass Effect Song Live with BioWare |last=Tito |first=Greg |date=11 August 2011 |publisher=The Escapist |access-date=23 October 2016 |archive-date=24 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161024024020/http://www.escapistmagazine.com/news/view/112180-Miracle-of-Sound-to-Premere-Mass-Effect-Song-Live-with-BioWare |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Escapist-BioWare">{{Cite web |url=http://www.escapistmagazine.com/news/view/112458-Miracle-of-Sound-Talks-to-BioWare |title=Miracle of Sound Talks to BioWare |last=Westbrook |first=Logan |date=2 March 2015 |publisher=The Escapist |access-date=19 September 2016 |archive-date=30 January 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160130204421/http://www.escapistmagazine.com/news/view/112458-Miracle-of-Sound-Talks-to-BioWare |url-status=live }}</ref> Coordinating with BioWare, Dunne became involved in the marketing campaign for the next game in the series, ''[[Mass Effect 3]]''. ===Music and reception by critics===
In traditional video game style, his albums are named in increasing levels ‚Äì ''Level 1'', ''Level 2'' and so on. However, he has also released two other non-gaming-related albums. His [[instrumental]] album called ''Vistas'' had a mixed reception, receiving 3.5 out of 5 stars from Nick Mongiardo of ''Sputnik Music'' who said "''Vistas'' has some issues with pacing and contains some jarring shifts in style, but anyone who enjoys chilled out instrumental music will love this."<ref name="Vistas">{{cite web | url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/60913/Miracle-of-Sound-Vistas/ | title=Miracle of Sound Vistas | publisher=Sputnik Music | date=10 February 2014 | access-date=18 October 2016 | author=Mongiardo, Nick | archive-date=30 August 2021 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210830154631/https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/60913/Miracle-of-Sound-Vistas/ | url-status=live }}</ref> His seventh album, ''[[Metal Up]]'', a [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] compilation with some songs based on Irish folklore, topped the iTunes Metal chart for 2 days following its release in early 2015.<ref name="Metal up for everyone">{{Cite web |url=http://www.sbindependent.org/metal-up-metal-for-everyone/ |title=Metal Up: Metal For Everyone! |last=Matyi |first=Kevin |date=7 April 2015 |publisher=Stony Brook Independent |access-date=19 September 2016 |archive-date=18 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018230824/https://sbindependent.org/metal-up-metal-for-everyone/ |url-status=live }}</ref> ''Metal Up'' was described as mix of classic rock, hard rock, and metal, and was praised by ''Firstherd'' for its Celtic influences in some songs which were "very original when placed in a metal framework".<ref name="Metal up review firstherd">{{Cite web |url=https://firstherd.wordpress.com/2015/04/21/first-listen-review-miracle-of-sound-metal-up/ |title=First Listen Review: Miracle of Sound ‚Äì "Metal Up" |last=Firstherd |date=21 April 2015 |publisher=Firstherd |access-date=18 October 2016 |archive-date=19 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019100631/https://firstherd.wordpress.com/2015/04/21/first-listen-review-miracle-of-sound-metal-up/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The album was also given a 5 out of 5 score by ''Rock Review''.<ref name="Rock review metal up">{{Cite web |url=http://rock-review.ru/reviews/18184-retsenziya-na-albom-miracle-of-sound-metal-up.html |title=–†–µ—Ü–µ–Ω–∑–∏—è –Ω–∞ –∞–ª—å–±–æ–º Miracle of Sound ‚Äì Metal Up |last=–î–æ–∫—Ç–æ—Ä –§–æ—Ä–∫–æ—Å–∏–≥–∞–Ω |date=25 May 2015 |publisher=Rock Review |access-date=20 October 2016 |archive-date=15 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161015171807/http://rock-review.ru/reviews/18184-retsenziya-na-albom-miracle-of-sound-metal-up.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

Shortly before the [[Wasteland 2#Director.27s Cut|''Wasteland 2 Director's Cut'']] re-release in 2015, Dunne released a [[musical parody|parody]] of the ''[[Dire Straits]]'' song "[[Money for Nothing (song)|Money for Nothing]]", titled "I want my RPG". The lyrics of the song criticise modern [[free to play]] games, [[Micropayment#Online gaming|microtransactions]] and [[downloadable content]] and express the desire for more games of the old [[Role-playing video game#Tactical RPGs|CRPG]] style.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://nichegamer.com/2015/06/big-wasteland-2-kickstarter-update-talks-names-changes-music-and-perks |title=Big Wasteland 2 Kickstarter Update Talks Names Changes, Music, And Perks |last=Batchelor |first=Carl |date=23 June 2015 |publisher=Niche Gamer |access-date=19 September 2016 |archive-date=2 February 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160202234356/http://nichegamer.com/2015/06/big-wasteland-2-kickstarter-update-talks-names-changes-music-and-perks/ |url-status=live }}</ref> He did not include the song on any album, saying on ''Twitter'' that "It's a parody of someone else's song. Wouldn't feel right to monetise it."<ref name="Twitter RPG">{{cite web | quote=@TheFerfles @apbusch It's a parody of someone else's song. Wouldn't feel right to monetise it. | url=https://twitter.com/miracleofsound/status/676534562919419905 | title=Twitter comment by Gavin Dunne | publisher=Twitter | date=14 December 2015 | access-date=20 October 2016 | author=Dunne, Gavin | archive-date=7 March 2021 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210307081655/https://twitter.com/miracleofsound/status/676534562919419905 | url-status=live }}</ref> Dunne has also used his music to criticise [[quick time event]]s in his ''[[Resident Evil 6]]'' song "Wigglesticks".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cinemablend.com/games/Resident-Evil-6-Parody-Song-Makes-Fun-Quick-Time-Events-48119.html |title=Resident Evil 6 Parody Song Makes Fun of Quick Time Events |last=Haas |first=Pete |date=October 2013 |publisher=Cinemablend |access-date=19 September 2016 |archive-date=30 August 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210830154654/https://www.cinemablend.com/games/Resident-Evil-6-Parody-Song-Makes-Fun-Quick-Time-Events-48119.html |url-status=live }}</ref> It has been suggested that other indie games on limited budgets could improve the quality of their music by employing indie musicians such as Miracle of Sound.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://diehardgamefan.com/2016/01/08/review-amplitude-sony-playstation-4/ |title=Review: Amplitude (Sony Playstation 4) |last=B. |first=Mark |date=8 January 2016 |publisher=Diehard Gamefan |access-date=19 September 2016 |archive-date=28 May 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160528181225/http://diehardgamefan.com/2016/01/08/review-amplitude-sony-playstation-4/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

However, he has collaborated with other artists at times for vocals or other instruments that he cannot play or create electronically.<ref name="Interview with gavin" />

===Sale and distribution of music===
From 2011 until February 2015, Dunne released new music videos on ''[[Escapist Magazine|The Escapist]]'' (an online video gaming website) every two to four weeks, in addition to releasing them on his YouTube channel.<ref name="Escapist-BioWare"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.twitlonger.com/show/n_1skrne2 |title=Hi lovelies |last=Dunne |first=Gavin |date=23 February 2015 |location=Twitter |access-date=5 January 2016 |archive-date=11 January 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160111061811/http://www.twitlonger.com/show/n_1skrne2 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Views on YouTube for Dunne have continued to grow, with around 4 million YouTube views per month, and with total views of over 254&nbsp;million as of July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.noxinfluencer.com/youtube/channel/UCSfoxYTlCPFfglckBLrjpsA|title=miracleofsound YouTube Channel Analytics and Report|website=www.noxinfluencer.com|language=en|access-date=2019-07-17|archive-date=11 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190711041641/https://www.noxinfluencer.com/youtube/channel/UCSfoxYTlCPFfglckBLrjpsA|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://socialblade.com/youtube/user/miracleofsound |title=miracleofsound YouTube Stats, Channel Statistics |website=Socialblade |access-date=10 January 2017 |archive-date=6 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161006104455/http://socialblade.com/youtube/user/miracleofsound |url-status=live }}</ref> He makes the majority of his income with sales of his songs and albums on [[Bandcamp]] and [[iTunes]], as well as monetisation of his YouTube videos.<ref name="examiner"/><ref name="thejournal"/> He has taken an unusual approach to piracy, and has said that he is fine with people pirating his music if they are poor or broke, and encourages them to tell their friends about him, credit him if they use his music, and buy it when they can.<ref name="Metal up for everyone" /><ref name="Twitter Piracy">{{cite web | url=https://twitter.com/miracleofsound/status/583810972323860480?lang=en | title=Twitter comment by Gavin Dunne | publisher=Twitter | date=2 April 2015 | access-date=21 October 2016 | author=Dunne, Gavin | quote=Hey. Listen. If you are poor or broke, pirate my music. Take it. Enjoy it. But tell your buddies about it & please ‚Äì buy it when you can <3 | archive-date=30 August 2021 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210830154736/https://twitter.com/miracleofsound/status/583810972323860480?lang=en | url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="gavs faq. ">{{Cite web |url=http://miracleofsound.net/2013/10/gavs-tricky-questions-faq/ |title=Gav's 'Tricky Questions' Miracle of Sound FAQ! |last=Dunne, Gavin |date=31 October 2013 |access-date=21 October 2016 |archive-date=20 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161020174047/http://miracleofsound.net/2013/10/gavs-tricky-questions-faq/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In late 2013, multiple [[YouTube copyright strike|copyright strikes]] were issued by YouTube against Dunne's videos for use of his own music, as well as against other users who had used his music in their videos with permission. All of the strikes were initiated by INDMUSIC, on behalf of [[TuneCore]], the company that distributed Miracle of Sound's songs at the time. Though the incident was resolved successfully, Dunne commented that although TuneCore was trying to help artists, their efforts did more damage than good and that he depends on [[let's plays]] and gaming videos to spread and popularise his music by word of mouth.<ref name="copyright strikes">{{Cite web |url=http://www.escapistmagazine.com/news/view/130667-YouTube-Issued-Copyright-Claims-Against-Miracle-of-Sound |title=YouTube Issued Copyright Claims Against Miracle of Sound |last=Lemon, Marshall |date=19 December 2013 |publisher=The Escapist |access-date=23 October 2016 |quote="I think TuneCore has the artist's best interests at heart but their methods need massive changes," Dunne said. "People using our songs in lets plays/gaming videos is free advertising. I depend on this kind of word of mouth spread to popularize my music as I do not have the money for advertising and that kind of thing. They are trying to help us by collecting what we 'are owed', but they are doing more damage than help." |archive-date=24 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161024024024/http://www.escapistmagazine.com/news/view/130667-YouTube-Issued-Copyright-Claims-Against-Miracle-of-Sound |url-status=live }}</ref>
