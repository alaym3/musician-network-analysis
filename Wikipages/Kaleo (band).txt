'''Kaleo''' (stylized as '''KALEO''') is an Icelandic [[blues rock]] band which formed in [[Mosfellsbær]] in 2012. It consists of lead vocalist and guitarist JJ, drummer David Antonsson, bassist Daniel Kristjansson, lead guitarist Rubin Pollock and harmonicist Þorleifur Gaukur Davíðsson. They have released three studio albums, ''Kaleo'' (2013), ''[[A/B (album)|A/B]]'' (2016) and ''Surface Sounds'' (2021) as well as an EP, ''Glasshouse'' (2013). ''A/B'' has sold over one million albums worldwide. One of the singles from ''A/B'', "[[Way Down We Go]]", was certified double platinum in the US and reached number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Alternative Songs]] chart on 20 August 2016.<ref name="RIAA"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7469309/kaleo-ealternative-songs-no-1-way-down-we-go|title=Kaleo Earns First Alternative Songs No. 1 with 'Way Down We Go'|website=Billboard|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref> Kaleo received a Grammy nomination in 2017 for Best Rock Performance for the song "No Good".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/kaleo|title=Kaleo|date=15 February 2019|website=GRAMMY.com|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref>

''Surface Sounds'', their third studio album, was released on 23 April 2021.<ref name="Kaleo-SS-site">{{cite web |title=KALEO {{!}} Surface Sounds {{!}} Official Website |url=https://www.officialkaleo.com/ |website=officialkaleo.com |access-date=2 April 2021}}</ref>

==History==

===2012–2013: Early years and self-titled album===
Best friends since attending elementary school outside of Reykjavik, band leader JJ, drummer David Antonsson, and bassist Daniel Kristjansson began playing together at the age of 17 before adding guitarist Rubin Pollock to the mix in 2012. They named the band Kaleo, which means "the voice" in Hawaiian, and started their career with a handful of well-received shows at the 2012 Iceland Airwaves music festival, which was the band's first major public appearance.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |title=ABOUT KALEO |url=http://www.officialkaleo.com/about |website=officialkaleo.com |access-date=1 January 2019}}</ref>
Kaleo gained fame after covering the song "Vor í Vaglaskógi" which received heavy rotation on Icelandic state broadcaster [[RÚV]]'s radio station [[Rás 2]] and was featured on the station's top 10 list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.icelandreview.com/icelandreview/daily_news/?cat_id=29314&ew_0_a_id=401466|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130718210016/http://www.icelandreview.com/icelandreview/daily_news/?cat_id=29314&ew_0_a_id=401466|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 July 2013|title=Top 10 in Iceland|first=Iceland|last=Review|date=10 July 2013}}</ref> This song is also featured in the first episode of the TV series ''[[Trapped (Icelandic TV series)|Trapped]]''. By year's end, Kaleo had signed with Sena, the country's main record label. Their self-titled debut went gold in their home country, spawning five number-one hits and inspiring their first European tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kaleo-mn0003393991/biography|title=KALEO - Biography & History|website=AllMusic|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref>

=== 2013–2019: major label record deal, departure to America, ''A/B'' and touring ===
{{Main|A/B (album)}}
In 2014, the band gained further attention with their single "All the Pretty Girls", which garnered over 87 million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://play.spotify.com/album/4Q4O06TIoYrl7zzVIOq3OF?play=true|title=All The Pretty Girls|date=20 January 2015}}</ref> In early 2015, they signed with [[Atlantic Records]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atlanticrecords.com/artists/kaleo/|title=Atlantic Records KALEO|website=Atlantic Records Official Website}}</ref> and subsequently relocated to [[Austin, Texas]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mystatesman.com/news/entertainment/music/iceland-band-kaleo-puts-down-americana-roots-in-au/nkQ8C/Iceland|title=Band Puts Down American Roots in Austin}}{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Throughout 2015, Kaleo received increasing attention in the United States. The band's [[South by Southwest]] (SXSW) debut in 2015 garnered press from ''[[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]]''{{'}}s "40 Bands You Need to Hear"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/music/a33670/sxsw-2015-music-preview-best-bands/ |title=SXSW Preview: 40 Bands You Need to Hear |first1=Andy |last1=Langer |first2=John |last2=Hendrickson |first3=Ryan |last3=Bort |date=16 March 2015 |website=Esquire |archive-url=https://archive.today/20160616094606/http://www.esquire.com/entertainment/music/a33670/sxsw-2015-music-preview-best-bands/|archive-date=16 June 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> as well as [[NPR]]'s "The Austin 100: SXSW 2015" feature and ''All Songs Considered''{{'}}s "SXSW 2015 Music Preview". "All the Pretty Girls" peaked at number 9 on ''Billboard'''s [[Adult Alternative Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/triple-a|title=Alternative Music: Top Alternative Songs Chart|website=Billboard |date=16 March 2015 |access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref>

Another three singles were released to promote their upcoming major label debut album ''[[A/B (album)|A/B]]'': "[[Way Down We Go]]", "No Good", and "I Can't Go On Without You". The single "[[Way Down We Go]]", was released on August 2015 and gained critical acclaim. The single was certified gold in the United Kingdom, Belgium, Ireland, South Africa and Germany, Platinum in Russia, Australia and the United States, and triple platinum in Canada. The single "No Good" was released shortly after and was featured in the promo for HBO's ''Vinyl''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusicenthusiast.tumblr.com/post/134539185097/single-review-no-good-by-kaleo|title=Single Review: 'No Good' by Kaleo|website=The Music Enthusiast - Dallas Music Blog|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref> The fourth single, "I Can't Go On Without You", was released on 18 February 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teamcoco.com/node/93277|title=WORLD PREMIERE: Kaleo "I Can't Go On Without You"|access-date=2 June 2019|via=teamcoco.com}}</ref> The album was released worldwide later that year on 10 June 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialkaleo.com/news/announcing-our-debut-album-ab-20986|title=Announcing our debut album A/B - KALEO Official Blog|website=KALEO Official Website|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref>

The album peaked at number 16 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/kaleo|title=Kaleo Chart History|website=Billboard|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref> and the band embarked on a tour, the Handprint Tour,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thescenestar.typepad.com/ss/2016/06/kaleo-schedules-show-at-fonda-theatre.html|title=Kaleo Schedules Show At Fonda Theatre|website=The Scenestar|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref> in order to promote it. The tour began on 11 September 2016 and concluded on 30 November 2016. The band embarked on a second tour, the fall Express Tour,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialkaleo.com/news/kaleo-express-fall-2017-tour-announcement-23201|title=KALEO Express Fall 2017 Tour Announcement - KALEO Official Blog|website=KALEO Official Website|access-date=2 June 2019}}</ref> which started on 25 August 2017 in [[Anaheim, California|Anaheim]], California, and ended on 25 November in [[Moscow]], Russia.

=== 2020–present: ''Surface Sounds'' ===
On 15 January 2020, KALEO released two singles, "I Want More" and "Break My Baby", the first music released since ''A/B''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-15|title=KALEO Returns with "I Want More" and "Break My Baby" |url=https://musicmayhemmagazine.com/kaleo-returns-with-i-want-more-and-break-my-baby/|access-date=2021-02-11|website=Music Mayhem Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> They followed this up with the announcement of the album's title and track list on 13 March, with the album scheduled for 5 June,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kaleo announces new album, 'Surface Sounds' - Music News - ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/3/13/kaleo-announces-new-album-surface-sounds.html|access-date=11 February 2021 |website=abcnewsradioonline.com |language=en}}</ref> along with dates for the Fight or Flight Tour, to take place July–September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kaleo Announces North American Tour: See Which Cities They'll Hit|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9324213/kaleo-tour-dates|access-date=2021-02-11|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> The third single "Alter Ego" was also released on 13 March, with JJ describing it as "the most classic rock and roll song".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Admin|first=S. P.|date=17 March 2020 |title=Grammy nominated Icelandic band KALEO drop 'Alter Ego' single|url=http://sportplaylists.com/kaleo-alter-ego-single|access-date=12 February 2021 |website=Sport Playlists|language=en-US}}</ref> The fourth single "Backbone" was released on 17 April,<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 April 2020 |title=KALEO Releases 'Backbone' from Highly-Anticipated 'Surface Sounds' |url=https://musicmayhemmagazine.com/kaleo-releases-backbone-from-highly-anticipated-surface-sounds/|access-date=11 February 2021 |website=Music Mayhem Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> although on 27 May, they delayed the album's release until further notice due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kaleo delays upcoming 'Surface Sounds' album - Music News - ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/5/26/kaleo-delays-upcoming-surface-sounds-album.html|access-date=11 February 2021 |website=abcnewsradioonline.com |language=en}}</ref> They were also forced to postpone their Fight or Flight tour twice, first to April–July 2021,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kaleo announces 2021 dates for postponed Fight or Flight tour - Music News - ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/6/25/kaleo-announces-2021-dates-for-postponed-fight-or-flight-tou.html|access-date=11 February 2021 |website=abcnewsradioonline.com|language=en}}</ref> and then to February–May 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=KALEO Announces 'Fight or Flight' Tour for 2022 |url=https://thunderbirdarena.ubc.ca/2021/02/kaleo-announces-fight-or-flight-tour-for-2022/|access-date=24 February 2021 |website=thunderbirdarena.ubc.ca}}</ref>

On 5 February 2021, they released a live version of "Break My Baby", recorded from Þrídrangar Lighthouse on 5 July 2020, the 78th anniversary of the lighthouse.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=5 February 2021|title=KALEO - Break My Baby (LIVE from Þrídrangar, Iceland)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y8q4zPjXd0M|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> Fifth single "Skinny" was released on 1 April 2021, with the announcement of the album's new release date of 23 April. 

==Musical style==
Kaleo's music has been influenced by genres such as [[blues rock]], [[indie folk]] and [[alternative rock]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=KALEO {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kaleo-mn0003393991/biography|access-date=2021-04-23|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', frontman JJ explained, "Growing up I was listening to a lot of American music and blues artists and such and that was what kind of connected us."<ref>{{cite web |title=Meet Kaleo: The All-American Rock Band from Iceland |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jtu-GM_xp1I |website=YouTube |publisher=Rolling Stone |access-date=5 January 2021 |date=June 30, 2016}}</ref><ref name="RS-Watch Kaleo">{{cite news |title=Watch Kaleo Talk 'Vinyl' Spot, Icelandic Fans, American Blues |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/watch-kaleo-talk-vinyl-spot-icelandic-fans-american-blues-77346/ |access-date=5 January 2021 |work=Rolling Stone |date=June 29, 2016}}</ref> Their blues influences are mainly drawn from [[Delta blues]] which was the target for some of the heavier songs in their album ''A/B''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Everything about the Icelandic Band Kaleo|url=https://adventures.is/information/kaleo/|access-date=2021-04-23|website=Arctic Adventures|language=en}}</ref> Their music prominently features a distinctive [[resonator guitar|resonator]] sound.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Taylor|first=Luke|title=The Current's Guitar Collection: Kaleo's JJ, 1935 National Guitar|url=https://www.thecurrent.org/feature/2016/03/08/the-currents-guitar-collection-kaleo-jj-juliusson-1935-national-guitar|access-date=2021-04-23|website=www.thecurrent.org}}</ref>

Lead singer JJ has given influences such as [[Jimi Hendrix]], [[Carlos Santana]] and [[Jim Morrison]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Everything about the Icelandic Band Kaleo|url=https://adventures.is/information/kaleo/|access-date=2021-04-23|website=Arctic Adventures|language=en}}</ref> while critics liken their music to that of [[Hozier (musician)|Hozier]], [[Ray LaMontagne|Ray LaMontagne]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=KALEO {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kaleo-mn0003393991/biography|access-date=2021-04-23|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> [[Led Zeppelin]], [[Cream (band)|Cream]] and [[ZZ Top]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bharadwaj|first=Nitin|date=2020-04-05|title=KALEO: A Modern Rock ‘n Roll Gem|url=https://nitinbharadwaj.medium.com/kaleo-a-modern-rock-n-roll-gem-36ee7dce5e5a|access-date=2021-04-23|website=Medium|language=en}}</ref>
