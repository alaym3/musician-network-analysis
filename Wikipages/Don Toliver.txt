'''Caleb Zackery "Don" Toliver'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=1963671&subid=0|title=BMI â€“ Repertoire Search|website=Repertoire.bmi.com|access-date=October 24, 2018}}</ref> ({{IPAc-en|t|É’|l|Éª|v|É™|r}}; born June 12, 1994) is an American rapper and singer. He released his first major label mixtape, ''Donny Womack'', in August 2018. He is known for his singles "[[No Idea (song)|No Idea]]" and "[[After Party (Don Toliver song)|After Party]]", from his debut studio album, ''[[Heaven or Hell (album)|Heaven or Hell]]'' (2020), which gained popularity on the video-sharing app [[TikTok]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/chrislambert/2019/12/14/don-toliver-and-no-idea-houston-to-tiktok-to-billboards-hot-100/|title=Don Toliver And 'No Idea'â€”Houston To Grindr To Billboard's Hot 100|last=Lambert|first=Chris|date=December 14, 2019|website=[[Forbes]]|language=en|access-date=December 16, 2019}}</ref> as well as his August 2018 feature on fellow rapper and label-boss [[Travis Scott]]'s song "[[Can't Say (Travis Scott song)|Can't Say]]", from Scott's third studio album, ''[[Astroworld (album)|Astroworld]]''. In August 2020, he was featured alongside [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]] and [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]] on the song "[[Lemonade (Internet Money and Gunna song)|Lemonade]]" by [[Internet Money]].

== Early life ==
Toliver was born and raised in [[Houston]], [[Texas]]. He had no musical experience prior to releasing music in 2017.<ref name="Piff">{{cite web |url=http://www.datpiff.com/Yungjosh93-Don-Toliver-Playa-Familia-mixtape.844648.html |title=Playa Familia Mixtape by Yungjosh93 & Don Toliver Hosted by n/a|website=DatPiff.com |access-date=September 7, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Dream">{{cite news | last=Kease| first=Kevin| title =The Polished Gem That Is Don Toliver| website=Day & A Dream | date =March 8, 2018 | url =http://dayandadream.com/2018/03/08/don-toliver-feature/| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Fader">{{cite news | last=Hussein| first=Wandera| title =Don Toliver talks working with Travis Scott, William Albrechtsen and Mike Dean in Hawaii, shares new video for "Diamonds"| website = [[The Fader]] | date =August 2, 2018 | url =http://www.thefader.com/2018/08/02/don-toliver-travis-scott-diamonds-video-premiere| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> His father was a rapper during the [[Swishahouse]] movement in the early 2000s, and would commonly play music around him growing up.<ref>{{Citation|title=Don Toliver On Kanye West, Relationship w/ Kali Uchis, How His Father Inspired Him + 'Life Of A Don'|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ovbZXtk4sTk|language=en|access-date=October 8, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2017: ''Playa Familia'' and singles ===
He released a collaborative mixtape, titled ''Playa Familia'', with Yungjosh93. In late 2017, Toliver released the solo singles, "Diva" and "I Gotta".<ref name="Elevator">{{cite news | last=Markow| first=Jake| title =Houston artist Don Toliver may have a hit on his hands with 'I Gotta'| website=Elevator| date =December 21, 2017 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/houston-artist-don-toliver-may-have-a-hit-on-his-hands-with-i-gotta/| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Respect">{{cite news | last=Buford| first=London| title =Don Toliver â€“ Diva| website = Respect | date =December 20, 2017 | url =https://respectmag.com/2017/12/don-toliver-diva/| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> In March 2018, he signed to [[Atlantic Records]] and We Run It Entertainment. Also in early 2018, Toliver released "Make Sumn" and "Checks",<ref name="Dream" /><ref name="Trend">{{cite news | last=Keise| first=Kevin| title =Don Toliver Drops Money-indulged Visual for "Checks"| work = Houston Trend Magazine | date =March 25, 2018 | url =http://www.houstontrendmag.com/home/2018/03/25/don-toliver-drops-money-indulged-visual-for-checks/| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> with a music video.

=== 2018â€“2020: Breakthrough, ''Cactus Jack'' and ''Heaven or Hell'' ===

In July 2018, Toliver released the single "Holdin' Steel" featuring Dice Soho. The song was also accompanied by a music video.<ref name="HNHHSteel">{{cite news | last=Findlay| first=Mitch| title =Don Toliver & Dice Soho Are "Holdin Steel" In New Visuals| website = HotNewHipHop | date =July 6, 2018 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/don-toliver-and-dice-soho-are-holdin-steel-in-new-visuals-new-video.44077.html| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> On August 2, Toliver released his major label debut mixtape, ''Donny Womack'',<ref name="DXMix">{{cite news | last=Ivey| first=Justin| title =Don Toliver Debuts "Donny Womack" Mixtape| website = [[HipHopDX]] | date =August 2, 2018 | url =https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47892/title.don-toliver-debuts-donny-womack-mixtape| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> along with a music video for the song "Diamonds".<ref name="Fader" />

The following day, on August 3, 2018, [[Travis Scott]] released his third studio album, ''[[Astroworld (album)|Astroworld]]'', on which Toliver was featured on the thirteenth track, "[[Can't Say (Travis Scott song)|Can't Say]]".<ref name="BB" /><ref name="Complex">{{cite news | last=Price| first=Joe| title ='ASTROWORLD' Standout Guest Don Toliver Drops New Mixtape 'Donny Womack'| work = [[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] | date =August 3, 2018 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2018/08/don-toliver-donny-womack-mixtape-stream| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> The single was later accompanied by a video that was sponsored by [[Yves Saint Laurent (brand)|Yves Saint Laurent]]. On August 6, it was announced that Toliver had signed to Scott's [[Cactus Jack Records|Cactus Jack]] imprint label.<ref name="DXCactus">{{cite news | last=Harling| first=Danielle| title =Don Toliver Signs To Travis Scott's Cactus Jack "Family"| website = HipHopDX | date =August 6, 2018 | url =https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47923/title.don-toliver-joins-travis-scotts-cactus-jack-family#| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> On August 9, [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]] released the music video for his single "[[Champion (Nav song)|Champion]]", which Scott was featured on. The song is from Nav's debut studio album ''[[Reckless (Nav album)|Reckless]]''. Toliver appeared in the video alongside Nav, Scott, and American rappers [[Sheck Wes]] and [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]]. Toliver and all rappers were featured on songs from ''Astroworld''.<ref name="Snob">{{cite news | last=Sawyer| first=Jonathan| title =Travis Scott & NAV Jet Set to Hawaii in New "Champion" Video | website = Highsnobiety | date =August 9, 2018 | url =https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/nav-travis-scott-champion-video/| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> In September, his single "Diva" was later remixed by rapper [[Kevin Gates]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=A |first1=Aaron |title=Kevin Gates Drops Remix To Don Toliver's Diva |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kevin-gates-drops-remix-to-don-tolivers-diva-new-song.1980196.html |access-date=September 7, 2018 |website=HotNewHipHop |date=September 5, 2018}}</ref>

In November 2019, Toliver accompanied Scott during the second [[Astroworld Festival]].<ref name="Astroworld Festival 2019">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-pictures/travis-scott-astroworld-fest-2019-houston-910584/astroworld-festival-2019-01865/|title=Travis Scott's Astroworld Fest in Houston|last=Wadsworth|first=Chad|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=November 11, 2019|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref> On December 13, Toliver released the music video for "Can't Feel My Legs".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S1GxqV65Tsg|title=Don Toliver â€“ Can't Feel My Legs (Official Music Video)|via=YouTube|date=December 13, 2019|access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref> On December 27, Toliver along with other Cactus Jack members, known as JackBoys, released the compilation album ''[[JackBoys]]''. Toliver, alongside Scott and Luxury Tax, contributed on the songs "Gang Gang" with Wes, "[[Had Enough (Don Toliver song)|Had Enough]]" by Toliver featuring [[Quavo]] and [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]] of the hip hop trio [[Migos]], and "What To Do?" by JackBoys and Scott featuring Toliver.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/jackboys/1492785997|title=JACKBOYS by JACKBOYS & Travis Scott on Apple Music|date=December 27, 2019|via=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=February 23, 2020}}</ref>

On January 17, 2020, Toliver was featured on American rapper [[Eminem]]'s eleventh studio album, ''[[Music to Be Murdered By]]'', on the song "No Regrets".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/music-to-be-murdered-by/1495267282|title=Music To Be Murdered By by Eminem on Apple Music|website=[[Apple Music]]|date=January 17, 2020|access-date=February 25, 2020}}</ref> On February 21, Toliver was announced to open for [[The Weeknd]]'s [[After Hours til Dawn Stadium Tour|After Hours Tour]] alongside [[Sabrina Claudio]].<ref name="After Hours Tour">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/02/the-weeknd-after-hours-tour-schedule|title=The Weeknd Reveals After Hours Tour Schedule Featuring Don Toliver and Sabrina Claudio|last=Price|first=Joe|website=[[Complex (website)|Complex]]|date=February 21, 2020|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref>

On March 10, 2020, Toliver announced his debut studio album, ''[[Heaven or Hell (album)|Heaven or Hell]]'', on social media and announced its release date.<ref name="Heaven Or Hell">{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B9j6Y8ppNnx/?igshid=4l9t8ul4frsl|title=HEAVEN OR HELL. 3/13. On May 8, 2020, Toliver was featured on Nav's song "Recap", from the latter's third studio album, ''[[Good Intentions (album)|Good Intentions]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://music.apple.com/us/album/good-intentions/1511575331|title=Good Intentions by NAV on Apple Music|via=[[Apple Music]]|date=May 8, 2020|access-date=May 11, 2020}}</ref>

On July 24, 2020, Toliver and his DJ Chase B released a collaborative single titled "Cafeteria", featuring Gunna, from their upcoming collaborative project, ''Escapism''. Exactly one week later, on July 31, he released a song titled "Clap", for the soundtrack of the film ''[[F9 (film)|F9]]'', in which the soundtrack was released on the same day and the song became the seventh single.

On August 14, 2020, Toliver was featured alongside Nav on "[[Lemonade (Internet Money and Gunna song)|Lemonade]]" by [[Internet Money]] and Gunna. The song earned him his highest-charting song on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], eventually peaking at number 6 on the chart. On the same day, he was featured on Kaash Paige's song "Grammy Week", from her debut studio album, ''Teenage Fever''. Exactly one week later, on August 21, he was featured alongside [[Big Sean]] on [[Nas]]' song "Replace Me", from his thirteenth studio album, ''[[King's Disease]]''. On October 22, he was featured alongside [[Gucci Mane]] on [[Rico Nasty]]'s single "[[Don't Like Me]]", from the latter's debut studio album, ''[[Nightmare Vacation (album)|Nightmare Vacation]].''

=== 2021: ''Life of a Don'' ===
In February 2021, Toliver teased his next project, titled ''[[Life of a Don]]'', on [[Twitter]].<ref>{{cite tweet|user=DonToliver|number=1356664240296218624|title=L.O.A.D|date=February 3, 2021|access-date=March 5, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=DonToliver|number=1363574664690348036|title=L.O.A.D Next. Love Ya.|date=February 22, 2021|access-date=March 5, 2021}}</ref> On May 4, Toliver released the lead single, "[[What You Need (Don Toliver song)|What You Need]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Don Toliver Shares New Track and Video for "What You Need"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/don-toliver-what-you-need|access-date=October 3, 2021|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref> One month later on June 18, Toliver released the second single from the album, "Drugs N Hella Melodies", featuring his girlfriend, American singer [[Kali Uchis]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Don Toliver and Kali Uchis team up on "Drugs N Hella Melodies"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/06/18/don-toliver-kali-uchis-drugs-n-hella-melodies|access-date=October 3, 2021|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref>

On August 20, 2021, Toliver released the single "[[Don't Go (Skrillex, Justin Bieber and Don Toliver song)|Don't Go]]", a collaboration with American record producer [[Skrillex]] and Canadian singer [[Justin Bieber]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Skrillex, Justin Bieber & Don Toliver Team Up for 'Don't Go': Stream It Now|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/9617353/skrillex-justin-bieber-don-toliver-dont-go-listen|date=August 20, 2021|access-date=August 20, 2021|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Bain|first=Katie}}</ref> On August 29, Toliver appeared on [[Kanye West]]'s album ''[[Donda]]'' on the track "[[Moon (Kanye West song)|Moon]]", alongside [[Kid Cudi]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 26, 2021|title=Inside Look at Kanye West's Third 'DONDA' Listening Party in Chicago|url=https://hypebeast.com/2021/8/watch-kanye-west-donda-chicago-soldier-field-listening-event-inside-look|access-date=October 5, 2021|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref> On September 28, Toliver revealed the release date and cover art for ''Life of a Don''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Blistein|first=Jon|date=September 28, 2021|title=Don Toliver Teases New Song 'Xscape' in Mysterious Trailer for New Album 'Life of a Don'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/don-toliver-life-of-a-don-album-announcement-1233978/|access-date=September 28, 2021|magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref> ''Life of a Don'' was released on October 8, 2021. The album included features from Travis Scott, Kali Uchis, [[SoFaygo]], [[Baby Keem]], and HVN.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Stream Don Toliver's New Album 'Life of a Don' f/ Travis Scott, Baby Keem, and More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/don-toliver-life-of-a-don-stream|access-date=October 8, 2021|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>
