'''Kelsea Nicole Ballerini''' (born September 12, 1993)<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kelsea Ballerini|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm7322413/|access-date=2021-06-09|website=IMDb}}</ref> is an American [[country pop]] singer and songwriter.<ref>{{cite web|author=Sellers, Morgan |title=Kelsea Ballerini - Spotify|url= https://open.spotify.com/artist/3RqBeV12Tt7A8xH3zBDDUF?si=Rcqt7gO3Qv6wbs-47OsTWg|website=[[Spotify]]|access-date=March 24, 2020}}</ref> She began songwriting as a child and signed with [[Black River Entertainment]] in 2014,<ref>{{Cite web|title=About {{!}} Black River Entertainment|url=https://www.blackriverent.com/about|access-date=2021-06-09|website=www.blackriverent.com|language=en}}</ref> releasing her debut studio album the following year, ''[[The First Time (Kelsea Ballerini album)|The First Time]]''. Her second studio album, ''[[Unapologetically]]'', followed in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Abbey|first=Alison|date=2017-10-13|title=Kelsea Ballerini Unapologetically Talks New Music, Love and Growing Up|url=https://parade.com/608697/alison-abbey/kelsea-ballerini-unapologetically-talks-new-music-love-and-growing-up/|access-date=2021-06-09|website=Parade: Entertainment, Recipes, Health, Life, Holidays|language=en}}</ref> Ballerini's first two albums have accounted for seven charted songs on the [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts. She has four No.&nbsp;1 singles on the latter, starting with her debut single "[[Love Me Like You Mean It]]", which made her the first female artist to send a debut single to the top of that chart since [[Carrie Underwood]] in 2006. It was followed by "[[Dibs (song)|Dibs]]" and "[[Peter Pan (Kelsea Ballerini song)|Peter Pan]]", whose peaks also made her the first female country artist to send her first three singles to the top of that chart since [[Wynonna Judd]]. Ballerini's fourth No.&nbsp;1 single is "[[Legends (Kelsea Ballerini song)|Legends]]". Her third and fourth studio albums, ''[[Kelsea (album)|Kelsea]]'' and ''[[Ballerini (album)|Ballerini]]'', were both released in 2020.

== Early life ==
Ballerini grew up in [[Knoxville, Tennessee]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/kelsea-ballerini-mn0003312551/biography|title=Kelsea Ballerini biography|last=Leggett|first=Steve|work=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=December 9, 2014}}</ref> Her father, Ed, a former sales manager for a country radio station, is of part [[Italian people|Italian]] descent.<ref name="cmt1">{{cite web|last1=Shelburne|first1=Craig|title=Next Women of Country: Kelsea Ballerini|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1746335/next-women-of-country-kelsea-ballerini/|website=CMT|access-date=June 23, 2015|date=December 5, 2014}}</ref><ref name="995qyk">{{cite web|title=Would Kelsea Ballerini ever change her name? |url=http://995qyk.com/2015/04/18/would-kelsea-ballerini-ever-change-her-name/ |website=99.5 QYK |access-date=June 23, 2015 |date=April 18, 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150623152240/http://995qyk.com/2015/04/18/would-kelsea-ballerini-ever-change-her-name/ |archive-date=June 23, 2015 }}</ref> Her mother, Carla, has worked in marketing for [[Thomas Nelson (publisher)|Thomas Nelson Bible publishing]] and a sponsorship company.<ref name="995qyk"/> She is an only child. She started taking dance lessons at Premiere Dance Studio in Seymour, Tennessee when she was three and stopped ten years later.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Hudelson|first1=Molly|title=Country singer Kelsea Ballerini talks her debut EP, upcoming NYC show and more|url=http://www.axs.com/news/country-singer-kelsea-ballerini-talks-her-debut-ep-upcoming-nyc-show-a-40000|website=AXS|access-date=June 23, 2015|date=February 9, 2015}}</ref> She sang in the church and school choirs.<ref name="cmt1"/> She wrote her first song at 12 for her mother<ref>{{cite web|last1=Mansfield|first1=Brian|title=On the Verge: Kelsea Ballerini means it|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/ontheverge/2015/04/05/kelsea-ballerini-love-me-like-you-mean-it-on-the-verge/70635632/|website=[[USA Today]]|access-date=June 23, 2015|date=April 5, 2015}}</ref> and moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], three years later.<ref name="allmusic"/> She attended [[Central High School (Knoxville, Tennessee)|Central High School]] in Knoxville, Tennessee; [[Centennial High School (Tennessee)|Centennial High School]] in Franklin, Tennessee; and then [[Lipscomb University]] for two years until she pursued a musical career.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kelsea Ballerini experiences several firsts, headlines SGA Spring Concert |url=http://www.luminationnetwork.com/2015/04/09/kelsea-ballerini-experiences-several-firsts-headlines-sga-spring-concert/|website=Lumination Network|access-date=June 23, 2015|date=April 9, 2015|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150623142315/http://www.luminationnetwork.com/2015/04/09/kelsea-ballerini-experiences-several-firsts-headlines-sga-spring-concert/ |archive-date=June 23, 2015 }}</ref>

==Career==
===2014–2016: Self-titled EP and ''The First Time''===
At 19, she signed a deal with [[Black River Entertainment]].<ref name="allmusic"/> In late 2014, she released her debut single "[[Love Me Like You Mean It]]", which debuted on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in October 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6281325/tomorrows-hits-lillywood-robin-schulz-logic-kelsea-ballerini|title=Tomorrow's Hits: Lillywood & Robin Schulz, Logic, Kelsea Ballerini|date=October 10, 2014|work=Billboard|access-date=December 9, 2014}}</ref> She released a [[Kelsea Ballerini (EP)|self-titled extended play]] for the label in November.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/kelsea-ballerini-e.p./id940713706 |title=Kelsea Ballerini EP |work=iTunes Store |access-date=December 9, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150109222114/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/kelsea-ballerini-e.p./id940713706|archive-date=January 9, 2015}}</ref> She was named one of [[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]]'s Next Women of Country in 2014. She performed for the first time at the [[Grand Ole Opry]] on February 14, 2015, and sang "Love Me Like You Mean It".<ref name="cmt1"/> On May 19, 2015, Ballerini's debut album ''[[The First Time (Kelsea Ballerini album)|The First Time]]'' was released through Black River. For the chart dated July 4, 2015, "Love Me Like You Mean It" , the album's first single, reached number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Country Airplay]] chart, making Ballerini the first solo female country music artist to score a number one hit with her debut single since [[Carrie Underwood]] did so with "Jesus, Take the Wheel" in 2006, and only the eleventh in history to achieve the feat.<ref>{{cite web|last=Moss|first=Marissa R.|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/kelsea-ballerini-joins-carrie-underwood-in-elite-number-one-club-20150622 |title=Kelsea Ballerini Joins Carrie Underwood in Elite Number One Club|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=June 22, 2015 |access-date=March 2, 2016}}</ref> Ballerini also became only the fifth solo female country music artist to score back-to-back number one hits with her first two singles when "[[Dibs (song)|Dibs]]" reached the top of the Country Airplay chart and the first to do it since [[Jamie O'Neal]] in 2001.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6889333/kelsea-ballerini-has-dibs-on-second-country-airplay-no-1|title=Kelsea Ballerini Has 'Dibs' on Second Country Airplay No. 1|author=Jim Asker|date=February 23, 2016|publisher=Billboard.com|access-date=February 23, 2016}}</ref>

The album's third single, "[[Peter Pan (Kelsea Ballerini song)|Peter Pan]]", was released to country radio on March 21, 2016. "Peter Pan" reached number one on both the Country Airplay and [[Hot Country Songs]] charts in September, making Ballerini the first solo female country music artist to top both country music charts simultaneously.<ref name="same time">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7503747/kelsea-ballerini-tops-two-country-charts-same-time-peter-pan|title=Kelsea Ballerini Is the First Woman to Top Hot Country Songs & Country Airplay Charts at the Same Time|last=Asker|first=Jim|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|date=September 12, 2016|access-date=September 15, 2016}}</ref> This achievement also made her the first solo female to send her first three singles to number one since [[Wynonna Judd]] accomplished the same feat in 1992.<ref name="same time"/> The album's fourth single, "[[Yeah Boy]]", was released to country radio on October 10, 2016.

== Influences ==
Ballerini has been influenced by artists in both the pop music and country music fields. Originally influenced by pop music, Ballerini states "I grew up on top 40 pop. I didn't know what country music was, which is so funny because I grew up on a farm in East Tennessee."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://radio.com/2015/05/14/kelsea-ballerini-the-first-time-interview-positive-songs-taylor-swift-new-music-to-know/|title=Kelsea Ballerini on Her New Album, Taylor Swift & Staying 'Positive': New Music to Know|access-date=April 30, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170817081204/http://radio.com/2015/05/14/kelsea-ballerini-the-first-time-interview-positive-songs-taylor-swift-new-music-to-know/|archive-date=August 17, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Ballerini lists [[Britney Spears]], [[Christina Aguilera]], and [[NSYNC]] among these early artists influencing her.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.argusleader.com/story/blogs/scotthudson/2015/08/19/chart-topping-kelsea-ballerini-loves-pizza-songwriting/31991065/|title=Chart-topping Kelsea Ballerini loves pizza, songwriting|website=Argus Leader|access-date=April 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/see-kelsea-ballerini-cover-britney-spears-nsync-in-nineties-medley-20150723|title=See Kelsea Ballerini Cover Britney Spears, N'Sync in Nineties Medley|date=July 23, 2015|website=Rolling Stone|access-date=April 30, 2016}}</ref> Ballerini also cites [[Justin Bieber]] as an early pop musical inspiration.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Watch Kelsea Ballerini Sing Justin Bieber, Taylor Swift, and Cher in a Game of Song Association|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/music/a31187981/kelsea-ballerini-song-association/|last=ELLE.com|date=2020-03-03|website=ELLE|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-17}}</ref> It was not until Ballerini heard "[[Stupid Boy (Keith Urban song)|Stupid Boy]]" by [[Keith Urban]] that she decided to delve deeper into country music by listening to albums by [[Taylor Swift]], [[Sugarland]], and [[Dixie Chicks]]. She credited Swift's [[Taylor Swift (album)|self-titled debut studio album]] for introducing her to country music. Ballerini also cites [[Shania Twain]] as her greatest influence.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/kelsea-ballerini-interview|title=Kelsea Ballerini Says She'll Never Stray From Her Country Roots|last=Nesvig|first=Kara|website=Teen Vogue|date=February 23, 2016|access-date=April 30, 2016}}</ref> She has also performed songs originally recorded by [[Alison Krauss]] and sang "[[Ghost in This House]]" alongside Krauss at the CMT Artists of the Year Awards in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PU_4E_sF8W8 |title=Kelsea Ballerini - "Ghost In This House" &#124; Live at the Grand Ole Opry &#124; Opry |publisher=YouTube |date=August 10, 2016 |access-date=June 9, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://vimeo.com/296307613 | title=Kelsea Ballerini & Alison Krauss &#124; Ghost in This House &#124; 2018 CMT Artists of the Year Performance}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
[[File:Morgan And Kelsea CMC Awards 2016.jpg|upright=1.1|alt=Morgan Evans and Kelsea Ballerini in March 2016|thumb|[[Morgan Evans (singer)|Morgan Evans]] and Kelsea Ballerini during a [[Planet Country with Big Stu & MJ|Planet Country]] interview on the [[Country Music Channel|CMC Awards]] red carpet in March 2016 around the time their relationship started.]]

In March 2016, Ballerini began dating Australian country singer [[Morgan Evans (singer)|Morgan Evans]]. They became engaged on Christmas Day 2016<ref>{{cite web|author=Sarah Michaud |url=http://people.com/country/kelsea-ballerini-engaged-morgan-evans-see-the-ring/ |title=Kelsea Ballerini Engaged to Morgan Evans - See the Ring |publisher=People.com |date=December 25, 2016 |access-date=June 9, 2017}}</ref> and were married on December 2, 2017, in [[Cabo San Lucas|Cabo San Lucas, Mexico]].

Ballerini was a student at [[Central High School (Knoxville, Tennessee)|Central High School]] during the 2008 Shooting, which she witnessed. She has said she suffers from [[Post-traumatic stress disorder|PTSD]] due to the experience.<ref>{{Cite web|last=November 17|first=Jeff Nelson|last2=Pm|first2=2021 04:15|title=Kelsea Ballerini Talks About PTSD from Witnessing School Shooting, Says Concerts Can Trigger Her|url=https://people.com/country/kelsea-ballerini-ptsd-triggers-after-witnessing-school-shooting/|access-date=2021-11-26|website=PEOPLE.com|language=en}}</ref>
