'''Tyrone William Griffin Jr.''' (born April 13, 1982), better known by his stage name '''Ty Dolla Sign''' (stylized as '''Ty Dolla $ign''' or '''Ty$'''), is an American singer, songwriter and [[record producer]].<!--occupation(s) in the lead intro are only those the subject is notable for per reliable sources. DO NOT ADD RAPPER as he is not a rapper/is not notable as one.-->  He first gained major recognition in 2010 for his feature on American rapper [[YG (rapper)|YG]]'s single "[[Toot It and Boot It]]", which he had co-written and produced for [[Def Jam Recordings]]. In the summer of 2013, he signed a record deal with American rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s [[Taylor Gang Entertainment|Taylor Gang Records]]. In November 2015, he released his debut studio album, ''[[Free TC]]'', which peaked at number 14 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

Ty Dolla Sign is known for his songs "[[Paranoid (Ty Dolla Sign song)|Paranoid]]", "[[Or Nah (Ty Dolla Sign song)|Or Nah]]", and "[[Blasé (song)|Blasé]]"; as well as his writing contributions to "[[Loyal (Chris Brown song)|Loyal]]" by [[Chris Brown]], "[[Post to Be]]" by [[Omarion]] featuring Brown and [[Jhené Aiko]], and "[[FourFiveSeconds]]" by [[Rihanna]], as well his various features on singles such as "[[Work from Home (song)|Work from Home]]" by [[Fifth Harmony]], "[[Swalla]]" by [[Jason Derulo]], featuring [[Nicki Minaj]], "[[Hot Girl Summer]]" by [[Megan Thee Stallion]], also featuring [[Nicki Minaj]], and "[[Psycho (Post Malone song)|Psycho]]" by [[Post Malone]], which was his first track to hit number one on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/sign-language-5-songs-you-didn-t-know-ty-dolla-sign-penned-news.14180.html|title=Sign Language: 5 Songs You Didn't Know Ty Dolla $ign Wrote|date=March 24, 2015|publisher=Hot New Hip Hop}}</ref> Aside from his solo music career, Ty Dolla Sign is also a member of production team D.R.U.G.S., alongside Chordz 3D, Buddah Shampoo, Nate 3D, James Koo, Fuego, HbarzDidIt and [[DJ Dahi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/11/25-new-producers-to-watch-out-for/drugs|title=25 New Producers To Watch Out ForD.R.U.G.S.|publisher=Complex}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2007–2011: Career beginnings ===
Ty Dolla Sign began his music career by learning how to play the [[bass guitar]] and later learned how to play drums, guitars, keyboards, and the [[Akai MPC|MPC]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Leight|first1=Elias|title=Ty Dolla $ign Talks 'New Black Punk,' Loving Babyface, Gritty 'Free TC' LP|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/ty-dolla-ign-talks-new-black-punk-loving-babyface-gritty-free-tc-lp-20151116|website=rollingstone.com|date=November 16, 2015|publisher=Rolling Stone|access-date=January 24, 2016}}</ref> Ty Dolla Sign and his partner Kory were signed a record deal with [[Venus Brown]] and Buddah Brown Entertainment, subsequently releasing a [[mixtape]], titled ''Raw & Bangin Mixtape Vol 2''. The duo also made [[guest appearance]]s on [[studio album]]s by other artists such as [[Sa-Ra|Sa-Ra Creative Partners]] and [[Black Milk]]. They also sang on songs such as "U", which was featured on the latter 2007's recording ''[[Popular Demand]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Chau|first1=Danny|title=Los Angeles Plays Itself: The Surprising Rise of Ty Dolla $ign|url=http://grantland.com/hollywood-prospectus/the-surprising-rise-of-ty-dolla-sign/|website=Grantland.com|date=May 28, 2014|access-date=January 23, 2016}}</ref> Eventually, however, the partners had a falling out related to these ventures.

After the dispute, Ty Dolla Sign would go on to collaborate with [[Los Angeles]]-based rapper [[YG (rapper)|YG]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/who-is-ty-dolla-sign/getting-into-music |title=Hanging With 50 Cent and Breaking Into the Industry — Who Is Ty Dolla $ign? |website=Complex.com |date=October 26, 2012 |access-date=February 22, 2014}}</ref> Ty Dolla Sign's initial claim to fame was with the success of YG's single "[[Toot It and Boot It]]",<ref>{{cite web|last=Sittner|first=Brian|title=Eclipse Magazine interviews Tydolla$ign|url=http://eclipsemagazine.com/Music/24041/|website=Eclipsemagazine.com|access-date=April 27, 2011}}</ref> a song he both wrote and produced with YG.<ref>{{cite web|last=Torres|first=Gina|title=Producer Tydolla$ign Releases New Mixtape "House on the Hill"|url=http://allhiphop.com/stories/reviewsmusic/archive/2011/04/26/22691195.aspx|website=Allhiphop.com|access-date=April 26, 2011}}</ref> He then became affiliated with YG's group Pu$haz Ink. In 2011, Ty Dolla Sign released his first solo song, titled "All Star" featuring Joe Moses, and produced by Fuego. Ty Dolla Sign then released "My Cabana", featuring [[Jeezy|Young Jeezy]], which was ranked on ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]''{{'}}s Best 50 Songs of 2012 list at number 23.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/2012-year-in-review-the-50-best-songs-of-2012/ty-dolla-sign-my-cabana |title=23. Ty Dolla $ign "My Cabana" — The 50 Best Songs of 2012 |website=Complex.com |date=December 11, 2012 |access-date=February 22, 2014}}</ref> Ty Dolla Sign co-founded the production team D.R.U.G.S. alongside longtime friends and fellow music producers Chordz 3D and G Casso, the latter of which was later murdered. Other producers later joined D.R.U.G.S., including Nate 3D, Buddah Shampoo, Fuego, [[Mustard (record producer)|DJ Mustard]] and [[DJ Dahi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/who-is-ty-dolla-sign/making-music-with-drugs|title=Who Is Ty Dolla $ign?Producing With His Crew, D.R.U.G.$.|website=Complex.com}}</ref>

=== 2012–2014: ''Beach House'' mixtapes ===
[[File:TyDolla2013 (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|190px|Ty Dolla Sign in November 2013.]]
In 2012, Ty Dolla Sign signed a [[recording contract]] with [[Atlantic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/12/ty-dolla-ign-on-singing-producing-and-screwing-your-girlfriend/ |title=Ty Dolla $ign on Singing, Producing and Screwing Your Girlfriend – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 5, 2012 |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref> After signing with Atlantic Records, he released his first solo mixtape, titled ''Beach House'', on October 1, 2012.<ref>{{cite web |last=Bacher |first=Danielle |url= https://www.laweekly.com/i-hung-out-with-ty-the-other-night-things-got-out-of-hand/ |title=I Hung Out With Ty$ the Other Night. ''Beach House 2'' features guest appearances from [[Too Short]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Juicy J]] and [[Kirko Bangz]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/the-most-anticipated-summer-albums-of-2013/ty-dolla-sign-beach-house-2 |title=19. Ty Dolla Sign, Beach House 2 — The Most Anticipated Albums of Summer 2013 |publisher=Complex |date=June 13, 2013 |access-date=June 23, 2020}}</ref> The following day, it was revealed that Ty Dolla Sign signed a deal with Wiz Khalifa's [[Taylor Gang Entertainment|Taylor Gang Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/ty-dolla-sign-gets-signed-taylor-gang |title=Ty Dolla $ign Officially Joins Taylor Gang |publisher=Vibe |date=July 2, 2013 |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref> In mid-2013, he toured with Khalifa and [[ASAP Rocky]], among others, on the "Under the Influence of Music 2" concert tour.<ref name="theboombox1">{{cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/ty-dolla-sign-beach-house-tracklist/ |title=Ty Dolla $ign Reveals 'Beach House EP' Tracklist |publisher=Theboombox.com |date=December 12, 2013 |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref>

On September 10, 2013, Ty Dolla Sign released a single, titled "[[Paranoid (Ty Dolla Sign song)|Paranoid]]", which features a verse from Atlanta rapper [[B.o.B]] and production from [[Mustard (record producer)|DJ Mustard]]. The song was later announced to be the first single off his upcoming [[extended play]] (EP). On October 22, 2013, the Ethan Lader-directed [[music video]] for "Paranoid", premiered via [[Sean Combs|Diddy]]'s ''[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]''.<ref name="thesource1">{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2013/10/22/it-doesnt-end-well-for-ty-dolla-ign-in-new-video-paranoid/ |title=It Doesn't End Well For Ty Dolla $ign In New Video, "Paranoid" |publisher=TheSource |date=October 22, 2013 |access-date=December 28, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Dyer |first=Deidre |url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/10/22/video-ty-dolla-ign-f-b-o-b-paranoid/ |title=Video: Ty Dolla $ign f. B.o.B., "Paranoid" |publisher=The Fader |date=October 22, 2013 |access-date=December 28, 2013}}</ref> The single became highly popular, peaking at number 29 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and eventually being [[RIAA certification|certified platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/the-wrap-up/blog/interview-ty-dolla-ign-on-krept-konan-the-uk-scene-more |title=Interview: Ty Dolla $ign on Krept & Konan, The UK Scene & More |work=MTV |date=April 7, 2016 |access-date=October 17, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018221606/http://www.mtv.co.uk/the-wrap-up/blog/interview-ty-dolla-ign-on-krept-konan-the-uk-scene-more |archive-date=October 18, 2016 |url-status=dead  }}</ref>

On January 7, 2014, Ty Dolla Sign released his second single for his debut EP, titled "[[Or Nah (Ty Dolla Sign song)|Or Nah]]", featuring Wiz Khalifa and DJ Mustard.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Nah-feat-Khalifa-Mustard-Explicit/dp/B00HBHLIBU |title=Or Nah (feat. On January 21, 2014, Ty Dolla Sign released his [[major record label|major label]] debut project, ''[[Beach House EP]]''. The EP features guest appearances from [[Casey Veggies]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Twista]], [[Jay Rock]], [[Trey Songz]], [[French Montana]], [[Travis Scott]] and [[Fredo Santana]], while the [[Record producer|production]] was primarily handled by Ty Dolla Sign himself, along with [[Mustard (record producer)|DJ Mustard]],<ref name="XXL">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/12/ty-dolla-ep-tracklisting/ |title=Ty Dolla $ign EP Release Date & Tracklisting – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 11, 2013 |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref><ref name="allhiphop1">{{cite web|author=timsanchez818 |url=http://allhiphop.com/2013/12/10/exclusive-ty-dolla-ign-sounds-off-on-new-ep-and-imitators/ |title=Exclusive: Ty Dolla $ign Sounds Off On New EP And Imitators |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=December 10, 2013 |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref> Cardo and [[Young Chop]], among others. He stated that the EP would feature a new sound from him, and that it would be on a whole other level compared to his mixtapes.<ref name="allhiphop1" /> Ty Dolla Sign described the EP as being a preview for his debut studio album, at the time due to be released during 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/ty-dolla-sign-wants-to-rule-the-summer-again/ |title=Ty Dolla $ign Wants To Rule The Summer Again – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=January 3, 2014 |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref> The remix to "Paranoid", featuring Trey Songz, French Montana, and DJ Mustard, was also included on the EP.<ref>{{cite web|author=Miranda J. |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/12/ty-dolla-ep-tracklisting/ |title=Ty Dolla $ign EP Release Date & Tracklisting – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 11, 2013 |access-date=February 22, 2014}}</ref>

===2016–2018: ''Beach House 3'' and ''MihTy''===
On June 19, 2016, Ty Dolla Sign announced that his next project following ''Campaign'' would be ''[[Beach House 3]]'' and that this installment would be an album rather than a mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ty-dolla-sign-suggests-beach-house-3-is-on-the-way-news.22347.html|title= Ty Dolla $ign Suggests "Beach House 3" Is On The Way |date= June 19, 2016 |publisher=HotNewHipHop|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref> The lead single "[[Love U Better]]" featuring [[Lil Wayne]] and [[The-Dream]] was released July 10, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/love-u-better-feat-lil-wayne-the-dream-single/id1256400644|title=Love U Better (feat. Lil Wayne & The Dream) – Single by Ty Dolla $ign on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=July 10, 2017}}</ref> The second single "[[So Am I (Ty Dolla Sign song)|So Am I]]" featuring [[Damian Marley]] and [[Skrillex]], was released September 1, 2017. The album was released on October 27, 2017, and debuted at number 11 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.45108/title.hip-hop-album-sales-chris-browns-heartbreak-on-a-full-moon-eclipses-almost-everyone-else|title=Hip Hop Album Sales: Chris Brown's "Heartbreak On A Full Moon" Hits #3 On Billboard 200|work=HipHopDX|last=Eustice|first=Kyle|date=November 6, 2017|access-date=November 12, 2017}}</ref>

In February 2018, Ty Dolla Sign was featured on [[Post Malone]]'s single "[[Psycho (Post Malone song)|Psycho]]", which peaked at number one on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8231969/drake-gods-plan-post-malone-psycho-hot-100|title=Drake's 'God's Plan' No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100 for Sixth Week, Post Malone's 'Psycho' Debuts at No. 2|work=Billboard|access-date=March 17, 2018}}</ref> In May 2018, he was featured on [[Christina Aguilera]]'s single "[[Accelerate (Christina Aguilera song)|Accelerate]]" along with [[2 Chainz]], the first single from Aguilera's eighth album ''[[Liberation (Christina Aguilera album)|Liberation]]''.{{citation needed|date=December 2019}} On June 1, he was featured on "All Mine" by [[Kanye West]], on West's ''[[Ye (album)|ye]]'' album. On June 8, he was featured on "Freeee (Ghost Town, Pt. 2)" by [[Kids See Ghosts]], on their album ''[[Kids See Ghosts (album)|Kids See Ghosts]]''. On June 14, he was featured on the [[Bhad Bhabie]] single "Trust Me". On June 16, he was featured on "Boss" on [[Beyoncé]] and [[Jay-Z]]'s joint album ''[[Everything Is Love]]''. In 2018, he was featured on [[Tinashe]]'s single "[[Me So Bad]]" with [[French Montana]], which had moderate success in Japan, Australia, and New Zealand. He was also featured on [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s album, ''[[Scorpion (Drake album)|Scorpion]]'', He was also featured on the song "After Dark". He also featured on [[Lecrae]]'s album ''[[All Things Work Together]]'' on the song "[[Blessings (Lecrae song)|Blessings]]".

On October 26, 2018, Ty Dolla Sign and [[Jeremih]] released a collaborative album under the joint pseudonym MihTy (a [[portmanteau]] of both the artists' names) through [[Def Jam Recordings]] and [[Atlantic Records]], their respective labels. It was entitled ''[[MihTy]]'', and features guest appearances from [[French Montana]], [[Chris Brown]] and [[Wiz Khalifa]]. The duo had released several singles throughout the fall season of 2018 in anticipation of the album's release, and performed "The Light" on ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]'' in the week leading up to the album's debut. The project also contained the hit single "Goin Thru Some Thangs" which reached #15 on the Apple Music top charts.

=== 2019–present: ''Featuring Ty Dolla Sign'' and ''Cheers to the Best Memories'' ===
Ty Dolla Sign released an album preview in February 2019 (via [[Instagram]]) featuring a new song produced by [[D.A. Got That Dope]] along with a verse from [[Tyga]]. On May 20, 2019, Ty Dolla Sign released the single "Purple Emoji", featuring American rapper [[J. Cole]] as the intended first single. On August 9, 2019, he released another single titled "Hottest in the City", featuring American rappers [[Juicy J]] and [[Project Pat]]. He later announced that his third studio album was originally called ''Tyrone'' (named from his birth name) and was set to release fall 2019. However, he stated "I had a meeting one time with [[Kanye West|Kanye]], and played him the album, and he was like, 'Bro nah, you need to do what you do. Add more bass, add more drums, add more, the real shit, that's what no one else is doing. Be the best you, bring that shit all the way up to the forefront...That conversation definitely inspired me, and made me go back and go crazy with the live instruments".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8526180/ty-dolla-sign-spin-cover-story-interview|title=Ty Dolla $ign On Upcoming "Tyrone" album...|publisher=Billboard|access-date=August 17, 2020}}</ref>

On July 1, 2020, Ty Dolla Sign released "[[Ego Death (song)|Ego Death]]" featuring [[Kanye West]], [[FKA Twigs]] and [[Skrillex]] as the official lead single to his third studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/ty-dolla-sign-announces-new-song-with-kanye-skrillex-fka-twigs-serpentwithfeet/|title=Ty Dolla $ign Announces New Song With Kanye, Skrillex, FKA twigs, serpentwithfeet|publisher=Pitchfork|date=June 30, 2020|access-date=June 30, 2020}}</ref> On July 3, 2020, Ty Dolla Sign announced to [[Big Boy (radio host)|Big Boy]] that his third studio album is called ''Dream House'' and would be released "sooner than you think".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ty-dolla-sign-announces-new-album-dream-house-news.113685.html|title=Ty Dolla Sign announces new album "Dream House"|date=July 3, 2020|publisher=Hot New Hiphop|access-date=August 17, 2020}}</ref> The second single "[[Expensive (Ty Dolla Sign song)|Expensive]]", featuring [[Nicki Minaj]], was released on August 28, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-25|title=Ty Dolla $ign Announces New Track "Expensive" Featuring Nicki Minaj|url=https://www.boss-sound.com/ty-dolla-ign-announces-new-track-expensive-featuring-nicki-minaj/|access-date=2020-08-25|website=Boss Sound}}</ref> On October 14, Ty Dolla Sign revealed that he changed the album's title to ''[[Featuring Ty Dolla Sign]]'', a reference to his work on other artists' projects and songs since his last release. He said the album title came from his gift of collaborating, and features many of his industry friends.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2020/10/15/ty-dolla-ign-releases-artwork-for-forthcoming-album-featuring-ty-dolla-ign|title=Ty Dolla $ign Releases Artwork For Forthcoming Album 'Featuring Ty Dolla $ign'|work=[[The Source]]|last=Johnson|first=Jasmine|date=October 15, 2020|access-date=October 15, 2020}}</ref>

''[[Featuring Ty Dolla Sign]]'' was released on October 23, 2020. The album features artists including [[Kid Cudi]], [[Post Malone]], [[Kanye West]], [[Anderson .Paak]], [[Quavo]], [[Lil Durk]], [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Big Sean]], [[Jhené Aiko]], [[Kehlani]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Musiq Soulchild]], [[FKA Twigs|FKA twigs]] and [[Skrillex]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Caraan |first1=Sophie |title=Ty Dolla $ign Delivers Comprehensive New Album 'Featuring Ty Dolla $ign' |url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/10/featuring-ty-dolla-sign-album-stream |access-date=23 October 2020 |publisher=Hypebeast |date=23 October 2020}}</ref> Ty Dolla Sign was also featured on the song "Safety Net" with American singer [[Ariana Grande]] for her sixth studio album, ''[[Positions (album)|Positions]]'' on October 30, 2020.

On 11 August 2021, Ty Dolla Sign and Canadian R&B duo [[dvsn]], composed of singer Daniel Daley and producer [[Nineteen85]], posted the artwork from a forthcoming collaborative album titled ''Cheers to the Best Memories'' to their respective Instagram pages, captioned with the 20 August 2021 release date.<ref>https://www.instagram.com/p/CScrTfNrPvi/</ref><ref>https://www.instagram.com/p/CSc04NupxWe/</ref> The album featured [[Rauw Alejandro]] and longtime Ty Dolla Sign associate [[YG (rapper)|YG]], as well as a posthumous guest appearance from [[Mac Miller]], with whom Ty Dolla Sign had [[The Divine Feminine|collaborated in the past]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/cheers-to-the-best-memories/1581704122|title = Cheers to the Best Memories by DVSN & Ty Dolla $ign|date = August 20, 2021}}</ref> The release preceded Ty Dolla Sign's appearances in the following two weeks on "Junya pt 2" from [[Kanye West]]'s ''[[Donda (album)|Donda]]'' and "Get Along Better" from [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s ''[[Certified Lover Boy]]''.

==Artistry==
[[File:Ty Dolla Sign 2018 March.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Ty Dolla Sign performing in March 2018.]]
Ty Dolla Sign sings and raps, combining elements of [[contemporary R&B]] and [[hip hop music]]. When asked if he was a rapper, he stated, "People would call it rap, but I really don't feel like I rap. There's so many great rappers, like if we had to [[Battle rap|battle]] or some shit, they would just cream me. I don't really consider myself a rapper, I just happen to have [[Bar (music)|bars]], or something like that. I still sing man, I'm a singer, ya feel me?"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/who-is-ty-dolla-sign/influences |title=Who Is Ty Dolla $ign? |website=Complex.com|date=October 26, 2012 |access-date=November 14, 2014}}</ref>

He cited [[Nate Dogg]] and [[Chris Brown]] as main influences for his musical style.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/ty-dolla-ign-says-hes-honoured-to-be-hailed-the-new-nate-dogg-by-snoop-dogg-2800703|title=Ty Dolla $ign "honoured" to be hailed "the new Nate Dogg" by Snoop Dogg|website=[[NME]]|date=October 27, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1838428/chris-brown-ty-dolla-sign-conversation-jail/|title = What Did Chris Brown Talk to Ty Dolla $ign About the Night He Got Out of Jail?|website = [[MTV]]}}</ref> When asked about his influences, Ty Dolla Sign responded, "[[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]] is my favorite artist of all time. I liked [[Slum Village]] a lot back then, like [[J Dilla]], he's one of my favorite producers and artists. I'm like a hip-hop head type of dude. [[Mos Def]], [[Talib Kweli]], that whole [[Rawkus Records]] scene, that's my type of shit." He continued, "I also like cats like [[Prince (musician)|Prince]]. Just like his production, he's kind of like the same thing as me. He sings, he produces, he [[Multi-instrumentalist|plays all types of instruments]]. That's what I'm after. I wouldn't wear that type of clothes or wear my hair like that. [Laughs.] But as far as artistry, that's the same type of dude I am."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/who-is-ty-dolla-sign/influences|title=Who Is Ty Dolla $ign?Influences and His Style of Music|website=Complex.com}}</ref> In an interview he revealed that [[Kim Burrell]] was his absolute favorite vocalist, and that [[Brandy Norwood|Brandy]] was right there with her.<ref name=MalcolmMusic/>

==Personal life==
Ty Dolla Sign has a daughter named Jailynn Crystal.<ref name="thefader" /> He was in a relationship with Fifth Harmony member [[Lauren Jauregui]] from 2017 to 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rap-up.com/features/sound-bites-ty-dolla-sign/|title=Food & Conversation With Ty Dolla $ign|first=Andres|last=Tardio|work=[[Rap-Up]]|access-date=December 15, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.etonline.com/ty-dolla-ign-confirms-relationship-fifth-harmonys-lauren-jauregui-90303|title=Ty Dolla $ign Confirms Relationship With Fifth Harmony's Lauren Jauregui|work=[[Entertainment Tonight]]|date=November 1, 2017|access-date=January 8, 2019}}</ref>

On December 10, 2018, it was revealed that Ty Dolla Sign was facing up to fifteen years in jail in relation to [[cocaine]] and [[marijuana]] possession charges.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ty-dolla-sign-indicted-felony-drug-charges-766789/|title=Ty Dolla $ign Indicted on Felony Drug Possession Charges After Atlanta Arrest|last=Kreps|first=Daniel|date=December 10, 2018|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=December 10, 2018}}</ref> These charges resulted from a traffic stop in [[Atlanta, Georgia]] where in a car with multiple people, Ty Dolla Sign was the only one charged with the drugs due to him being in close proximity to them.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/09/skrillex-ty-dolla-sign-arrest-marijuana-cocaine/|title = Skrillex Present During Ty Dolla $ign Arrest|date = September 7, 2018}}</ref> Later, all charges were dropped upon completion of a drug prevention program.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2019/02/27/ty-dolla-sign-drug-possession/|title=Ty Dolla Sign' Drug Possession Charges Will be Dropped as Long as he Stays Clean|last=Miss2Bees|date=February 27, 2019|website=The Source|language=en-US|access-date=September 9, 2019}}</ref>

In 2020, Ty Dolla Sign formally endorsed the [[Kanye West 2020 presidential campaign|presidential campaign]] of fellow rapper and frequent collaborator [[Kanye West]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Chuck D, 2 Chainz, Ty Dolla $ign And More Sound Off On Kanye's Presidential Bid|url=https://www.bet.com/music/2020/07/06/hip-hop-reacts-to-kanye-running-for-president.html?cid=facebook|access-date=2021-01-18|website=BET.com}}</ref>
