'''Duncan de Moor''' ({{IPA-nl|Ààd è≈ãk…ôn d…ô moÀêr}}; born 11 April 1994),<ref name=whois/> known professionally as '''Duncan Laurence''', is a Dutch singer-songwriter. He [[Netherlands in the Eurovision Song Contest 2019|represented the Netherlands]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]] with his song "[[Arcade (song)|Arcade]]" and went on to win the competition, giving the Netherlands its first Eurovision win since [[Eurovision Song Contest 1975|1975]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/duncan-laurence-netherlands-2019|title= Duncan Laurence will represent The Netherlands this year|last=Groot|first=Evert|date=21 January 2019|website=eurovision.tv}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurovoix.com/2019/03/06/the-netherlands-duncan-laurence-to-sing-arcade-in-tel-aviv/|title= The Netherlands: Duncan Laurence To Sing 'Arcade' In Tel Aviv|date=6 March 2019|access-date=9 June 2019|last=McCaig|first=Ewan|work=Eurovoix}}</ref> "Arcade" became one of the most successful [[List of Eurovision Song Contest winners|Eurovision Song Contest winning entries]] on streaming platforms and international charts in recent history. Prior to Eurovision, Laurence was a semi-finalist in the [[The Voice of Holland (season 5)|fifth season]] of ''[[The Voice of Holland]]''.

==Career==
===Early life and career===
[[File:DuncanLaurence2014.jpg|thumb|Laurence during his time at the Rock Academy in [[Tilburg]] in 2014.]]
Born in [[Spijkenisse]], Laurence grew up in [[Hellevoetsluis]]. He began writing his own songs as a teenager, as an escape from being bullied.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eurovision.tv/participant/duncan-laurence|title=Duncan Laurence|work=eurovision.tv|access-date=25 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190506225621/https://eurovision.tv/participant/duncan-laurence|archive-date=6 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He started his musical career at the Rock Academy in [[Tilburg]], playing in a number of school bands,<ref name=whois>{{Cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2019/05/16/who-is-the-netherlands-2019-eurovision-entrant-duncan-laurence-9444114/|title=Who is The Netherlands 2019 Eurovision entrant Duncan Laurence?|date=16 May 2019|newspaper=Metro|access-date=25 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190517075657/https://metro.co.uk/2019/05/16/who-is-the-netherlands-2019-eurovision-entrant-duncan-laurence-9444114/|archive-date=17 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> including his own, The Slick and Suited. Formed in 2013, the band went on to perform at [[Eurosonic Noorderslag]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vestingpop.nl/|title=VestingPop {{!}} Duncan Laurence|access-date=21 January 2019|publisher=VestingPop}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://duncandemoor.wordpress.com/the-slick-and-suited/|title=The Slick and Suited|access-date=21 January 2019|date=15 June 2014|author=Duncan de Moor|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190121213558/https://duncandemoor.wordpress.com/the-slick-and-suited/|archive-date=21 January 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> In February 2020, he revealed that he had [[Perinatal asphyxia|oxygen deprivation at birth]], and thus has had a motor disorder affect his right hand.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B8t1vQ3gB5a/|title=Duncan Laurence on Instagram|date=18 February 2020|access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref>

===2014‚Äì2018: ''The Voice of Holland'' and independent work===
Laurence participated in the [[The Voice of Holland (season 5)|fifth season]] of ''[[The Voice of Holland]]'', choosing [[Ilse DeLange]] as his coach. He advanced to the Cross Battles/Semi Finals before being eliminated.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://escunited.com/netherlands-duncan-laurence-on-his-way-to-tel-aviv/|title= Netherlands: Duncan Laurence on his way to Tel-Aviv!|last=Popescu|first=David|date=21 January 2019|website=escunited.com}}</ref> In March 2016, he left The Slick.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/theslickofficial/photos/a.595105770570087/1011706535576673|title=The Slick on Facebook|date=24 March 2016|website=Facebook|language=nl|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210225100209/https://www.facebook.com/theslickofficial/photos/a.595105770570087/1011706535576673|archive-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> Laurence graduated from the Rock Academy in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.radiotimes.com/news/tv/2019-05-16/eurovision-2019-netherlands-holland-duncan-laurence/amp/|title=Duncan Laurence Information - meet the Dutch star|website=Radio Times|access-date=17 May 2019}}</ref> He, with Jihad Rahmouni, wrote the music to K-pop duo [[TVXQ]]'s song "Closer" for their 2018 album ''[[New Chapter No. 1: The Chance of Love|New Chapter #1: The Chance of Love]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nu.nl/songfestival/5696852/duncan-laurence-naar-songfestival-wie-is-de-24-jarige-zanger.html|title= Duncan Laurence naar Songfestival: Wie is de 24-jarige zanger?|last=Koeleman|first=Danja|date=21 January 2019|website=nu.nl|language=nl}}</ref>

===2019‚Äì2021: Eurovision Song Contest, ''Worlds on Fire'' EP and ''Small Town Boy''===
{{See also|Netherlands in the Eurovision Song Contest 2019}}
[[File:Semi2Dress2019-041-20190516-EuroVisionary.jpg|thumb|Laurence performing "[[Arcade (song)|Arcade]]" during the second semi-final of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]].]]
In January 2019, Laurence was internally selected to represent the Netherlands in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]], held in [[Tel Aviv]], [[Israel]]. He was nominated by DeLange, with whom he remained in contact after ''The Voice''. His song, "[[Arcade (song)|Arcade]]", was released in March 2019. Arriving as the main favourite to win, Laurence eventually went on to win the contest, having received a total of 498 points. He is the fifth Dutch entrant to win the competition, and the first since [[Teach-In (band)|Teach-In]] won the [[Eurovision Song Contest 1975]] with the song "[[Ding-a-dong]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-48324831|title=Eurovision 2019: Netherlands wins song contest|work=BBC News|date=19 May 2019}}</ref> As a result of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2020|following year's contest]] being cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Laurence is the longest reigning Eurovision winner, having held the title for two consecutive years.

After his Eurovision victory, Laurence embarked on a concert tour across the Netherlands and Europe.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Val√©rie|first=Charlotte|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2019/05/18/the-netherlands-duncan-laurence-announces-first-european-tour/239189/|title=The Netherlands: Duncan Laurence announces first European tour|work=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=18 May 2019|accessdate=22 May 2021|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20210522111112/https://wiwibloggs.com/2019/05/18/the-netherlands-duncan-laurence-announces-first-european-tour/239189/|archivedate=22 May 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> On 10 June 2019, he performed at the 50th anniversary of [[Pinkpop Festival]] in Landgraaf, the first Dutch Eurovision winner to perform at the event. He replaced the Swedish duo [[First Aid Kit (band)|First Aid Kit]] who dropped out due to health reasons.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pinkpop.nl/2019/en/eurovision-song-contest-winner-duncan-laurence-at-pinkpop-2019/|title=Eurovision Song Contest Winner Duncan Laurence At Pinkpop 2019|date=2019-05-27|publisher=Pinkpop|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190528112033/https://www.pinkpop.nl/2019/en/eurovision-song-contest-winner-duncan-laurence-at-pinkpop-2019/|archive-date=2019-05-28|url-status=live}}</ref> On 23 October 2019, Laurence released his second single, "[[Love Don't Hate It]]". It was also announced that he signed a record deal with [[Capitol Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://escbubble.com/2019/10/duncan-laurence-releases-follow-up-single-love-dont-hate-it|title=Duncan Laurence Releases Follow-Up Single 'Love Don't Hate It'|last=Clues|first=David|website=ESC Bubble|date=2019-10-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191023221502/https://escbubble.com/2019/10/duncan-laurence-releases-follow-up-single-love-dont-hate-it/|archive-date=2019-10-23|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1187088736841945088|user=CapitolRecords|title=We‚Äôre thrilled to welcome the supremely talented @dunclaurence to the #CapitolFamily|author-link=Capitol Records|date=2019-10-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191023195153/https://twitter.com/CapitolRecords/status/1187088736841945088|archive-date=2019-10-23|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2020, the artists' entrance at [[Rotterdam Ahoy]] was re-named "Door Duncan" in his honour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://escxtra.com/2020/01/28/rotterdam-ahoy-renames-a-part-of-the-arena-in-honour-of-duncan-laurence/|title=üá≥üá± Rotterdam Ahoy renames a part of the arena in honour of Duncan Laurence|date=January 28, 2020}}</ref> On 13 May 2020, Laurence released his first [[extended play]] (EP), ''[[Worlds on Fire (EP)|Worlds on Fire]]'', which included the singles "Arcade", "Love Don't Hate It", and "[[Someone Else (Duncan Laurence song)|Someone Else]]", along with two new tracks. His first studio album, ''[[Small Town Boy (album)|Small Town Boy]]'', followed on 13 November 2020. It was [[List of music recording certifications|certified platinum]] in the Netherlands three days after its release.<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1328472882654089216|user=BeauRTL|title=Een grote verrassing voor @dunclaurence!
