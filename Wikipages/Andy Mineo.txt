'''Andrew Aaron Mineo'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://exposingdarkness.bandcamp.com/track/stop-the-traffic|title=Stop The Traffic, by Andy Mineo|website=Exposing Darkness|access-date=September 15, 2017}}</ref> (born April 17, 1988), is an American [[Christian hip hop]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://narrative.ly/andy-mineo-raps-about-christ-just-dont-call-him-a-christian-rapper/|title=Andy Mineo Raps About Christ. Just Don't Call Him a Christian Rapper. - Narratively|website=narrative.ly|access-date=November 11, 2017|date=July 3, 2014}}</ref> artist, [[record producer|producer]], [[music executive]], and [[music video director|video director]] based in [[New York City]]. He is signed to [[Reach Records]] and his creative initiative Miner League. In addition to his solo work, he is a member of Reach Records' hip hop collective [[116 Clique]].

Originally from [[Syracuse, New York|Syracuse]], Mineo worked as a producer in high school at [[Henninger High School]] in [[Upstate New York]], and joined the hip-hop group Fat Camp, signed to [[Syracuse University]]'s Marshall Street Records. After moving to New York City, he realized his weak spiritual condition, re-dedicated his life to Christ and closed down his production studio in order to restart his career. He independently released his first mixtape ''Sin is Wack''  in 2009 and joined a community outreach program created by [[Nicky Cruz]] called T.R.U.C.E. where he met long-time friends and collaborators Alex Medina, Wordsplayed, Rich Perez and others. Upon meeting, Andy began collaborating with Alex Medina on a record called "Background" that would later be placed on [[Lecrae]]'s ''[[Rehab (Lecrae album)|Rehab]]'' album and was Andy's introduction to Reach Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_ORR_38LLv0|title=Andy Mineo talks meeting Alex Medina, TRUCE Ministry & Uno Uno Seis Response (@andymineo @rapzilla)|work=[[Rapzilla]]|date=August 6, 2014|access-date=November 14, 2017|via=YouTube}}</ref>

In 2011, Andy released "In My City", a single featuring Efrain from Doubledge, that continued to build momentum and buzz. After rebranding from C-Lite, Andy signed with Reach Records and released, ''[[Formerly Known]]'', a full-length studio album, ''[[Heroes for Sale (Andy Mineo album)|Heroes for Sale]]'' on April 16, 2013. On January 28, 2014, he released an EP titled ''[[Never Land (EP)|Never Land]]''. His sophomore album, ''[[Uncomfortable (album)|Uncomfortable]]'', was released on September 18, 2015.

Andy began building his own creative initiative, Miner League and signed [[John Itiola|Wordsplayed]] along with L.A.-based alternative artist Willow Stephens that featured on his "Strange Motions" track on ''[[Uncomfortable (album)|Uncomfortable]]''. In 2016, the first project from the initiative, ''Clowntown'', was released by Wordsplayed<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapzilla.com/rz/news/38-backstage/12980-wordsplayed-announces-his-brand-new-album-clowntown|title=wordsplayed announces his brand new album 'clowntown'|first=David|last=Daniels|access-date=November 14, 2017|work=[[Rapzilla]]|date=March 22, 2016}}</ref> and followed by Willow Stephens' self-titled EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/news_detail.php?newsid=2348|title=Soulful Vocalist Willow Stephens To Release Debut EP|website=newreleasetoday.com|access-date=November 14, 2017}}</ref> In 2017, Miner League and Reach Records partnered to release a collaborative mixtape titled, ''Andy Mineo & Wordsplayed Present Magic & Bird'' on August 4, 2017.

His video and television work includes "Saturday Morning Car-Tunez", "League Chumps Season 1", and co-direction on numerous of his own music videos. As a producer, his work includes tracks from his self-released "In My City", ''Formerly Known'', ''Saturday Morning Car-Tunez'', and ''Heroes for Sale''. He also produced the single "Destiny" by Sheena Lee, in which he was a featured performer, and, along with rapper [[Derek Minor]], aided [[DJ Official]] in producing "Power Trip" by [[Lecrae]], featuring Andy Mineo, Derek Minor, and [[Sho Baraka]], from the album ''[[Gravity (Lecrae album)|Gravity]]''. ''Gravity'' won the [[Best Gospel Album]] category in the [[2013 Grammy Awards]].

== Early life ==
Mineo was raised in a single-parent home in [[Syracuse, New York]].<ref name=bio>{{cite web|title=Andy Mineo's Biography|url=http://reachrecords.com/artists/show/Andy-Mineo-|work=Reach Records Website|publisher=Reach Records|access-date=December 5, 2012|format=Web}}</ref><ref name=unyc>{{cite web|title=I AM UNYC - Andy Mineo (@iamunyc @AndyMineo @reachrecords @AP_Films)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Js6a9GjEB8I|work=I Am UNYC|via=[[YouTube]]|author=I Am UNYC|format=Web/Video|date=May 2, 2012}}</ref> In the summer before entering the 8th grade, Mineo went to a Summer camp at which his sister worked as a counselor and was exposed to the [[Gospel]] and his life had changed.<ref name=bio /><ref name=unyc /> However, after his conversion, Mineo went into high school with no Christian support, and he soon drifted away from his faith.<ref name=bio /><ref name=unyc /> While in high school, Mineo acquired recording equipment and started selling studio time out of his home.<ref name=bio /> He had formed a friendship with Chris "Oxburg" Leonard, and the pair recorded a song together in 2001.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url = http://blog.syracuse.com/listenup/2009/11/syracuse_rapper_oxburg_takes_h.html|title = Syracuse rapper Oxburg takes his time to get 'Premonition' just right|date = November 5, 2007|access-date = January 12, 2015|website = [[The Post-Standard]]|publisher = [[Advance Publications]]|last = Bialczak|first = Mark}}</ref>

== Career ==
After graduating from [[The City College of New York]] (CCNY),<ref>{{cite web|last=Hauptman|first=Amy|title=Andy Mineo: Minister & Rapper|url=http://www.intervarsity.org/news/andy-mineo-minister-rapper|publisher=Intervarsity Christian Fellowship USA|format=Web|date=June 25, 2012}}</ref> Mineo, along with Leonard, joined a rap group called Fat Camp, which subsequently won a song contest for [[WJPZ-FM]] and signed to [[Syracuse University]]'s Marshall Street Records.<ref name=":0" /> The group opened up for major acts such as [[Jadakiss]], [[The Roots]] and [[Common (rapper)|Common]], as well as members of the underground scene, such as [[Immortal Technique]] and [[Dead Prez]], when said artists performed at the University.<ref name="unyc" /> In 2006, the group recorded and released a studio album entitled ''The Food''.<ref name=":0" />

In his first year of college, Mineo met the producer Alex Medina and through him discovered the urban evangelism project T.R.U.C.E.<ref name=bio /><ref name=unyc /> After hearing the song "Price Tag" by [[Da' T.R.U.T.H.]], Mineo decided to close his studio and rededicate his life to Christ.<ref name=introduction>{{cite web|title=Andy Mineo - Introduction (@andymineo @reachrecords @rapzilla)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USF-FMsVo1A|work=[[Rapzilla]]|via=[[YouTube]]|access-date=December 10, 2012|format=Web/Video|date=October 5, 2011}}</ref> He also left Fat Camp, which promptly dissolved.<ref name=":0" /> Although he is a Christian, he chooses to not to identify as a "Christian rapper", because he feels the label turns some people off.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://narrative.ly/andy-mineo-raps-about-christ-just-dont-call-him-a-christian-rapper|title=Andy Mineo Raps About Christ. Just Don't Call Him a Christian Rapper|date=July 3, 2014|access-date=September 15, 2017}}</ref>

Following the closure of his studio, Mineo went on tour with T.R.U.C.E. He released his first mixtape, ''Sin is Wack''.<ref name=bio /> Mineo explained in an interview that he came up with the concept of the "Sin is Wack" title through his work with T.R.U.C.E.<ref name=introduction /> Following this release, Mineo sang on the Lecrae song "Background" from ''[[Rehab (Lecrae album)|Rehab]]'' in 2010 and "Reverse" from ''[[Blacklight (Tedashii album)|Blacklight]]'' by [[Tedashii]] in 2011.<ref name=bio /><ref name=introduction /> Mineo explained to [[Rapzilla]] "Background" was his first attempt at singing, and following the success of that song he received subsequent requests.<ref name=introduction /> ''The Christian Manifesto'' claimed that Mineo's hook for "Background" made ''Rehab'' one of the best Christian albums to date, and stated that Mineo became one of the most sought after artists, particularly for singing hooks.<ref>{{cite web|last=Wildes |first=Michael |title=Formerly Known |url=http://www.thechristianmanifesto.com/main/index.php/2011/10/12/music-review-andy-mineo-formerly-known/ |work=The Christian Manifesto |access-date=December 12, 2012 |format=Web |date=October 12, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140219114048/http://www.thechristianmanifesto.com/main/index.php/2011/10/12/music-review-andy-mineo-formerly-known/ |archive-date=February 19, 2014 }}</ref> When asked about his recognition as a singer instead of an [[emcee]], Mineo stated that "I enjoy making melodies", and said he wasn't too concerned about rapping when he supplied hooks for songs and was content with the opportunity to serve in any capacity.<ref name=introduction /> Mineo contributed to "Put On" from ''Captured'' by [[Flame (rapper)|Flame]] and "The Reunion Cypha" on ''[[Stop the Funeral]]'' by [[The Ambassador (rapper)|The Ambassador]].<ref name=bio/><ref name=introduction /> He released the single "In My City" in 2011 as part of the God in My City Movement [[prayer walk]] that Mineo was involved in. The song was reported by Rapzilla to become an "anthem for a lot of people."<ref name=introduction />

On July 28, 2011, at Legacy Conference 2011, Reach Records announced that Mineo had signed with the label.<ref>{{cite web|title=Reach Records announces signing of Andy "C-Lite" Mineo at Legacy Conference|url=http://www.dasouth.com/news/24-news/6534-reach-records-signs-andy-qc-liteq-mineo-announces-at-legacy-conference|work=DaSouth|access-date=December 5, 2012|author=DaSouth.com|format=Web|date=July 28, 2011|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130121080924/http://www.dasouth.com/news/24-news/6534-reach-records-signs-andy-qc-liteq-mineo-announces-at-legacy-conference|archive-date=January 21, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> Upon signing with the label, Mineo abandoned the stage name C-Lite, stating that he "never really liked the name" and that his signing marked a good time to transition to his legal name.<ref>{{cite web|title=Andy Mineo fka C-Lite Signs To Reach Records (@andymineo @reachrecords @rapzilla)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1d0_quVBgao|work=[[Rapzilla]]|via=[[YouTube]]|access-date=December 5, 2012|format=Web|date=July 29, 2011}}</ref> He released his debut mixtape on Reach Records, ''[[Formerly Known]]'', on September 29, 2011.

In 2016, Mineo released a music video for his song "Hear My Heart", which describes his guilt for not learning [[sign language]] to communicate with his [[deaf]] sister for 25 years. Mineo said he worked for a year to make translate every sound in the song into a [[three-dimensional]] experience so that deaf viewers could experience the rhythm of the music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/09/andy-mineo-hear-my-heart-video/|title=Andy Mineo Communicates to the Deaf in 'Hear My Heart' Video - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=September 15, 2017}}</ref>

Mineo founded Miner League, a brand he had explained was going to give him new an outlet to make music and expand his reach. [[Wordsplayed]] was the first artists signed to the brand. Mineo had experienced a creative halt while writing his upcoming album. He reached out to Wordsplayed, and together they came up with the concept of ''Magic and Bird'', a five-song [[extended play]] of songs from Mineo and Wordsplayed. They spent three weeks in [[Atlanta, Georgia]] to further develop the project. It grew from a five-song E.P. to a ten-song [[mixtape]] with one bonus song and two skits. Mineo and Wordsplayed called their song, "Dunk Contest", a summarization of their time in Atlanta.<ref>Madden, Sidney. "Andy Mineo and Wordsplayed Discuss Their New Mixtape 'Magic & Bird'." XXL, Townsquare Media, August 3, 2017, www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/08/andy-mineo-wordsplayed-magic-and-bird-mixtape-interview/.</ref>

The pre-release claimed No. 1 on the iTunes' all genres slider. ''Andy Mineo & Wordsplayed Present Magic & Bird'' peaked at No. 1 on Top Christian Albums. "The set moved with 11,000 equivalent album units earned in its first week, with 8,000 in traditional sales." said journalist Timothy Yap from Christian music portal Hallels. The mixtape also peaked at No. 3 on Rap Album Sales and No. 23 on Top Rap Albums. "Dunk Contest" entered Spotify's Viral Top 50 in [[Canada]], [[the United Kingdom]], [[Norway]], [[Australia]], [[New Zealand]], [[Taiwan]], [[Spain]], [[France]], and [[Turkey]]. On September 21, 2017, Mineo and Wordsplayed launched the Friends and Family Tour.<ref>Yap, Timothy. "'Andy Mineo and Wordsplayed Present Magic & Bird' Is the #1 Christian Album This Week." Hallels.com, Hallels, August 20, 2017, www.hallels.com/articles/17971/20170820/andy-mineo-and-wordsplayed-present-magic-bird-is-the-1-christian-album-this-week.htm.</ref>

Mineo scored his first RIAA certification when "You Can't Stop Me" went gold on January 17, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=you+can't+stop+me#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum |work=RIAA|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> The rapper kept the news quiet until he released the remix featuring Messiah on April 11, 2018.

On April 27, 2018, Mineo released an extended play called ''I: the Arrow''. Four days before his EP's scheduled release, Mineo said that he was "unsure [of] when [he] began to feel stuck in this cloud or when it will end, but [he knew] there [were] others who feel the same." He featured the electronic group Weathrman his songs, "Family Photo" and "Anxiety". Mineo revealed that the six-track release is the first of four extended plays culminating into his third studio album. The E.P. peaked at No. 2 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Top Christian Albums chart earn 6,000 equivalent album units in its first week.<ref>Asker, Jim. Billboard, Universal Music Publishing, May 10, 2018, ''Billboard''/articles/columns/chart-beat/8455411/andy-mineo-arrow-no-2-top-christian-albums-chart.</ref>

On September 21, 2018, he released another EP, ''II: The Sword'', which is the second of four EPs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news-2/5258-andy-mineo-s-new-project-arrives-today|title=Andy Mineo's New Project 'II: The Sword' Arrives|last=Clarks|first=Jessie|website=TheChristianBeat.org|access-date=April 3, 2019}}</ref> The EP, peaked at No. 15 on the ''Billboard'' Digital Albums Chart for the week of October 6, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/digital-albums|title=Digital Albums Chart|website=Billboard|access-date=April 3, 2019}}</ref>
