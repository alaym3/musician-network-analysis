'''Sarah Faith Griffiths''' (born 21 January 2001), known professionally as '''Griff''', is an English singer and songwriter. In 2019, she released her debut single, "Mirror Talk", through [[Warner Records]]. This was followed by her debut extended play of the same name, released later that year. Griff was named the [[Brit Award for Rising Star|Rising Star]] at the [[2021 Brit Awards]], becoming one of the youngest winners in the category at 20. Later that year, she released her debut mixtape, ''[[One Foot in Front of the Other]]'', to commercial success and critical acclaim.

==Early life and education ==
Griff was born on 21 January 2001 as Sarah Faith Griffiths,<ref name="UK">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/58980/griff/|title=Griff {{!}} full Official Chart History|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=23 June 2021|archive-date=7 December 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201207071859/https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/58980/griff/|url-status=live}}</ref> and was raised in [[Kings Langley, Hertfordshire]].<ref name="id">{{cite news|url=https://i-d.vice.com/amp/en_uk/article/paj9xb/griff-is-the-uk-teen-talking-you-out-of-negative-body-image-with-her-pop-music|title=griff is the uk teen talking you out of negative body image with her pop music|work=[[i-D]]|date=8 July 2019|access-date=23 October 2020|archive-date=26 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201026113927/https://i-d.vice.com/amp/en_uk/article/paj9xb/griff-is-the-uk-teen-talking-you-out-of-negative-body-image-with-her-pop-music|url-status=live}}</ref> She was born to a Jamaican father and a Chinese mother, as well as having Jamaican grandparents who were part of the "[[Windrush generation]]". Griff has stated that she feels "more in touch" with her Chinese ancestry, since her mother is a first generation migrant who migrated to England during the [[Vietnam War]].<ref name="id" /> Griff's parents [[foster care|foster]] children as well, and her experiences with the children were reflected in her song "Good Stuff".<ref name="Complex">{{cite news|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2020/02/premiere-griff-good-stuff|title=Premiere: Multi-Disciplined Soul Singer Griff Copes With Separation By Remembering The "Good Stuff"|work=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]|date=11 February 2020|access-date=23 October 2020|archive-date=26 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201026130438/https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2020/02/premiere-griff-good-stuff|url-status=live}}</ref>

Griff stated that her earliest memory of loving music was being gifted an [[iPod Shuffle]] with the [[Taylor Swift]] album ''[[Fearless (Taylor Swift album)|Fearless]]'' (2008) downloaded to it,<ref name="MTV">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push/news/get-to-know-griff|title=get to know: griff|date=29 August 2019|access-date=23 October 2020|archive-date=27 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201027100557/http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push/news/get-to-know-griff|url-status=live}}</ref> when she was around the age of eight.<ref name="Notion">{{cite news|url=https://notion.online/notion-86-griff/|title=Notion 86: Griff|work=[[Notion (magazine)|Notion]]|date=12 August 2020|access-date=23 October 2020|archive-date=27 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201027023304/https://notion.online/notion-86-griff/|url-status=live}}</ref> She then taught herself to record and produce music on her brother's laptop, and used tutorials on [[YouTube]] to inform her on techniques.<ref name="id" /> While attending school, Griff kept her musical life private, due to not knowing if her musical career would be successful, stating: "no one likes that person at school who talks about how they want to sing".<ref name="id" /> After finishing her [[A Levels]], she told teachers that she was taking a [[gap year]], rather than informing them on her music ventures.<ref name="id" /> On using the [[stage name]] Griff, she stated: "Sarah Griffiths isn't that glamorous, I sound like I have a mortgage and 4 kids, it's very corporate".<ref name="Notion" />

==Career==

[[Annie Mac]] featured the song on [[BBC Radio 1]] under the ''New Name'' playlist.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Griff-Releases-Debut-Track-And-Video-MIRROR-TALK-20190709|title=Griff Releases Debut Track And Video MIRROR TALK|work=[[BroadwayWorld.com]]|date=9 July 2019|access-date=23 October 2020|archive-date=27 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201027002807/https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Griff-Releases-Debut-Track-And-Video-MIRROR-TALK-20190709|url-status=live}}</ref>
