'''Vanessa Joy Amorosi'''<ref name="BackOnTrack">Sharon Verghis: [https://web.archive.org/web/20160306102530/http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=SHD09101827I3P5EDRTO "Back on track"], ''The Sun-Herald'', 18 October 2009, Archived from [http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=SHD09101827I3P5EDRTO ''the original''] on 6 March 2016.</ref> (born 8 August 1981) is an Australian singer-songwriter and recording artist. Her combined album and single sales have reached over two million worldwide. She is known for her wide [[Belting (music)|belting]] [[Vocal range|range]] ( being able to reach a C#6 with ease ) {{citation needed|date=August 2020}} and her ability to vocally cover various genres of music including pop, rock, [[blues]], [[jazz]] and [[Gospel music|gospel]].{{citation needed|date=August 2020}} 

Amorosi rose to fame in 1999 with the release of her debut single, "[[Have a Look]]", which reached gold status in Australia.<ref name="HonestWoman"/> The following year, she achieved international success with her debut studio album, ''[[The Power (album)|The Power]]''. She performed at both the [[2000 Summer Olympics opening ceremony|2000 Olympic Games opening ceremony]] and its accompanying [[2000 Summer Olympics closing ceremony|closing ceremony]] in [[Sydney]]. Her performance of "Heroes Live Forever" at the opening ceremony gained international acclaim and recognition due to the enormous live vocal range displayed. {{citation needed|date=August 2020}} Performed at the closing ceremony, "[[Absolutely Everybody]]", which became an unofficial anthem of the games, went on to be a major hit in Australia and many European countries including the United Kingdom and Germany.<ref name="Absolutely_Everybody_Charts"/> After a second studio album, ''[[Change (Vanessa Amorosi album)|Change]]'', in 2002<ref name="Change_Album_Charts"/> and several years from the spotlight, Amorosi returned in 2008 with the release of her third studio album ''[[Somewhere in the Real World]]''.<ref name="Somewhere_in_the_Real_World_release"/> The album's most successful single, "[[Perfect (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Perfect]]", marked her comeback to the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA]] top 5 singles<ref>[http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=Perfect&cat=s ''Vanessa Amorosi â€“ "Perfect".''] australian-charts.com, Retrieved 15 November 2017.</ref> and was the most played Australian song on Australian radio for 2008. Her fourth studio album, ''[[Hazardous (album)|Hazardous]]'', followed in 2009<ref>[http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/23790/20091205-0000/issue1029.pdf "Top 100 Albums â€“ Week Commencing 16th November 2009 (PDF)"], ARIA, 16 November 2009, Retrieved 15 November 2017.</ref> and is Amorosi's second-best selling album in Australia behind her debut, ''The Power''. It produced her first number one-charting single, "[[This Is Who I Am (song)|This Is Who I Am]]".<ref>[http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=This+Is+Who+I+Am&cat=s "Vanessa Amorosi â€“ 'This Is Who I Am'"], australian-charts.com, Retrieved 15 November 2017.</ref> In October 2016, Amorosi recorded her fifth studio album at [[Royal Studios]] in [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]],<ref name="Recorded_New_Album_2016"/> that is slated to be released in 2019.<ref name="New_Album_release_date">[http://www.5mu.com.au/shows/afternoon-swing/68316-jon-stevens] {{dead link|date=April 2020}}</ref>

To date Amorosi has received a total of 16 ARIA and [[APRA Awards (Australia)|APRA]] nominations. She has won an APRA award with "[[Shine (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Shine]]",<ref name="2001APRA"/> a [[VIVA Germany#Annual Comet Awards|VIVA Comet Award]] for "Best International Newcomer"<ref name="Die Comet-Preistraeger 2001"/> and an [[Australian Recording Industry Association#ARIA No. 1 Chart Awards|ARIA No.1 Chart Award]] with "This Is Who I Am".

==Early life==
Amorosi was born in [[Melbourne]], in a [[Roman Catholic]] family of Italian origin, the daughter of singers Frank and Joy Amorosi.<ref name="BackOnTrack"/> When Amorosi was four, along with her younger sisters, Mellissa and Natasha, would go to tap, jazz and classical ballet classes, which were being run by her uncle. She attended [[Emerald, Victoria|Emerald]] Primary School and Emerald Secondary College.<ref>Paul Connolly: [https://web.archive.org/web/20160307021440/http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+emerald+primary&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=SHD081012L42OP494KRI "Rewind 1987"], ''The Sun-Herald'', 12 October 2008, Archived from [http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+emerald+primary&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=SHD081012L42OP494KRI ''the original''] on 7 March 2016.</ref><ref>Roslyn Guy: [https://web.archive.org/web/20160307184822/http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+emerald+secondary&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=news990317_0323_7275 "A chorus line"], ''The Age'', 17 March 1999, Archived from [http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+emerald+secondary&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=news990317_0323_7275 ''the original''] on 7 March 2016.</ref> At the age of 12, Amorosi started to perform in shopping centres and local council concerts under the supervision of her family. She also performed on Young Talent Time.<ref name="Milestones">{{cite web|title=Milestones|url=http://vanessaamorosi.com/mstonesb.htm|work=Vanessa Amorosi|access-date=1 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20010124103200/http://www.vanessaamorosi.com/mstones.htm|archive-date=24 January 2001}}</ref> Amorosi was discovered performing at Matrioshka,<ref>{{cite news|last=Dent|first=Nick|title=Making tracks|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/lifestyle/making-tracks/story-e6frf039-1111114471654|access-date=4 August 2013|newspaper=Daily Telegraph|date=23 September 2007}}</ref> a Russian restaurant in [[Carnegie, Victoria|Carnegie]], by Jack Strom (After being referred by Leigh Hope) and signed a recording contract in 1997. After being signed to MarJac Productions (co-owned by managers [[Mark Holden]] and Jack Strom) and recording a promo CD titled "[[Get Here]]", she secured a deal with Transistor Music Australia.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mark Holden|url=http://www.abc.net.au/profiles/content/s3396694.htm|work=[[Australian Broadcasting Corporation|ABC]]|access-date=1 November 2013|date=22 December 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
===1999â€“2002: ''The Power''===
[[File:201000 - Opening Ceremony Vanessa Amorosi performs - 3b - 2000 Sydney opening ceremony photo.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Amorosi shown singing at the opening ceremony of the 2000 Summer Paralympics in Sydney, Australia]]
In 1999, Amorosi released her debut single "[[Have a Look]]" which became a Top 20 hit and went gold in Australia.<ref name="HonestWoman">Kelly Stoner: [https://web.archive.org/web/20160306224336/http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+have+a+look&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=NCH0111229B38P1K1MGU "An honest woman"], ''Newcastle Herald'', 22 November 2001, Archived from [http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+have+a+look&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=NCH0111229B38P1K1MGU ''the original''] on 6 March 2016.</ref> At the end of this year she performed at [[Carols in the Domain]]. "Have a Look" was followed by her second single "[[Absolutely Everybody]]", an anthemic dance song, which reached the Top 10. It was certified double platinum and became the longest charting Australian single by an Australian female artist.<ref name="HonestWoman"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.amo.org.au/artist.asp?id=1245|title=Australian Music Online :: Artists :: Vanessa Amorosi|date=12 September 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060912181240/http://www.amo.org.au/artist.asp?id=1245|access-date=17 April 2020|archive-date=12 September 2006}}</ref> It remained in the [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] Top 50 Singles Chart for over six months after its release.<ref name="Absolutely_Everybody_Charts">[http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=Absolutely+Everybody&cat=s "Vanessa Amorosi â€“ 'Absolutely Everybody'"], australian-charts.com, Retrieved 13 November 2017.</ref> "Absolutely Everybody" was released internationally and reached the Top 10 in many European countries including the UK and Germany where it received a gold certification. Amorosi performed at many large events such as the [[National Rugby League|NRL Grand Final]], the opening of the Motorcycle Grand Prix at Phillip Island and the new millennium on New Year's Eve at Darling Harbour.

Following the success of the first two singles, Amorosi released two more singles in 2000. "[[Shine (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Shine]]" and the double A-sided single "[[The Power (Vanessa Amorosi song)|The Power]]"/"[[Every Time I Close My Eyes (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Every Time I Close My Eyes]]" became major Australian hits.<ref>[http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=Shine&cat=s ''Vanessa Amorosi â€“ "Shine".''] australian-charts.com, Retrieved 17 November 2017.</ref><ref>[http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=The+Power+%2F+Every+Time+I+Close+My+Eyes&cat=s ''Vanessa Amorosi â€“ "The Power/Every Time I Close My Eyes".''] australian-charts.com, Retrieved 17 November 2017.</ref> Both were accredited gold by the ARIA Australian music industry. The rights to create a remixed version of "Shine" were purchased by the airline [[Ansett Australia]], who used this version for a commercial.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20160306164206/http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+shine+and+Ansett&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=AGE130426G015N6FBK69 ''I was there.''] The Age, 26 April 2013, Archived from [http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+shine+and+Ansett&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=AGE130426G015N6FBK69 ''the original''] on 6 March 2016.</ref> Amorosi was one of many celebrities to appear in the commercial. In Europe, "Every Time I Close My Eyes" was released as the fourth single from ''The Power'' and became her most successful song in Germany behind "Absolutely Everybody".<ref>[http://hitparade.ch/song/Vanessa-Amorosi/Every-Time-I-Close-My-Eyes-5007 ''Vanessa Amorosi â€“ "Every Time I Close My Eyes".''] hitparade.ch, Retrieved 17 November 2017.</ref>

From 29 April to 25 May, Amorosi supported [[Savage Garden]] for 16 shows on the Australian leg of their [[Affirmation (Savage Garden album)#Affirmation World Tour|''Affirmation'' World Tour]].<ref name="Milestones"/>

Her album ''[[The Power (album)|The Power]]'' reached number one on the Australian album charts. Amorosi received six ARIA nominations in the year 2000 awards<ref>{{cite web|title=Winners by Year â€“ 2000|url=http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history/year/2000|work=ARIA Awards|publisher=ARIA|access-date=19 August 2013}}</ref> and ''The Power'' was nominated<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070908205315/http://www.sonybmg.com.au/cd/releaseDetails.do?catalogueNo=CCBK7042 ''The Power, Nominated in the 15th ARIA Awards.''] sonybmg.com.au, Archived from [http://www.sonybmg.com.au/cd/releaseDetails.do?catalogueNo=CCBK7042 ''the original''] on 8 September 2007.</ref> for "Best Female Artist" and "Best Pop Release" in the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2001]].
Her largest audience came in September 2000 with performances at the opening and closing ceremonies of the [[2000 Summer Olympics|2000 Sydney Olympics]] and at the [[Paralympic Games]]. At the opening ceremony of the aforementioned Olympics she sang the Olympic song titled "Heroes Live Forever". Amorosi won the [[APRA Awards (Australia)|APRA Award]] in 2001 for "Most Played Song" on Australian Radio with "[[Shine (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Shine]]",<ref name=2001APRA>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090918021105/http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/apraawards/musicawards/history/2001Winners.aspx ''2001 Winners.''] apra-amcos.com.au, Archived from [http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/apraawards/musicawards/history/2001Winners.aspx ''the original''] on 18 September 2009.</ref> and received international acclaim in Germany where she won "Best International Newcomer" at the 2001 [[VIVA Germany#Annual Comet Awards|VIVA Comet Awards]].<ref name="Die Comet-Preistraeger 2001">[http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/musik/auszeichnung-die-comet-preistraeger-2001-a-150847.html ''Die "Comet"-PreistrÃ¤ger 2001.''] spiegel.de, 18 August 2001, Retrieved 7 November 2017.</ref> Later that year she also made a special appearance at the [[Goodwill Games]] Opening and Closing ceremonies in Brisbane. In 2001, she performed at the [[AFL Grand Final]].

In October 2001, Amorosi released ''[[Turn to Me]]'', a compilation album with new songs and tracks from ''The Power'', which were previously unreleased in Australia. It also contains the studio version of the Olympic song "Heroes Live Forever". ''Turn to Me'' was released in Australia only and achieved a top 30 peak.<ref>[http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=Turn+To+Me&cat=a ''Vanessa Amorosi â€“ "Turn to Me" (Album).''] australian-charts.com, Retrieved 17 November 2017.</ref> The same titled lead single from the album reached number 80 on the ARIA singles chart.<ref>[http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/23790/20020221-0000/www.aria.com.au/issue615.pdf ''Top 100 Singles â€“ Week Commencing 10th December 2001 (PDF).''] ARIA, 10 December 2001, Retrieved 17 November 2017.</ref>

In August 2002, Amorosi traveled to [[Manchester]] where she was one of the stars of the [[Commonwealth Games]] Hand-Over Ceremony, performing her single "Shine" and a new song written especially for the event, "I'll Always Be a Melbourne Girl".<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20131104013823/http://www.take40.com/artists/6/vanessa-amorosi/news/1781/vanessa-sings-at-the-games ''Vanessa Sings At The Games.''] take40.com, 28 June 2002, Archived from [http://www.take40.com/artists/6/vanessa-amorosi/news/1781/vanessa-sings-at-the-games ''the original''] on 4 November 2013.</ref><ref name=biography>[https://web.archive.org/web/20110223233906/http://www.theharbouragency.com/file_download/2374 ''Vanessa Amorosi Biography (PDF).''], Theharbouragency.com, Archived from [http://www.theharbouragency.com/file_download/2374 ''the original''] on 23 February 2011.</ref>

===2003â€“2009: ''Somewhere in the Real World''===
On 25 January 2006, it was revealed that Amorosi signed with manager Ralph Carr, having completed her seven-year management with MarJac Productions.<ref>{{cite news|last=Eliezer|first=Christie|title=Ralph Carr Management Signs Amorosi|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1358795/ralph-carr-management-signs-amorosi|access-date=1 November 2013|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=24 January 2006}}</ref> She is quoted on the website as saying, "I have been with my previous management for seven years and thought both parties had gone as far as we could together... Moving over to Ralph Carr Management seemed like a natural progression, given the acts he has successfully managed. I am excited and enthused." On 22 November, it was announced that Amorosi had signed to [[Universal Music]] Australia.

Amorosi's involvement with [[Kids Help Line]] and the [[Variety Club]] led to a number of honours,<ref>Michael Gadd: [https://web.archive.org/web/20160306085501/http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+variety+club&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=NCH041125EJ3PB78U0QM ''Absolutely versatile.''] Newcastle Herald, 25 November 2004, Archived from [http://newsstore.fairfax.com.au/apps/viewDocument.ac?page=1&sy=smh&kw=vanessa+amorosi+and+variety+club&pb=all_pubs&dt=selectRange&dr=entire&so=relevance&sf=text&sf=author&sf=headline&rc=150&rm=200&sp=nrm&clsPage=1&docID=NCH041125EJ3PB78U0QM ''the original''] on 6 March 2016.</ref> including a 2003 nomination for Australian of the Year. She still has the farm where endangered animals can find refuge, although she does not get to visit much; her friends run it for her. She supported [[Kiss (band)|Kiss]] on their 2008 tour of Australia.

In 2008, Amorosi released the album ''[[Somewhere in the Real World]]'', through Universal Music Australia, on 24 May 2008.<ref name=heraldsunhit>{{Cite web|url=http://www.heraldsunhit.com.au/product.asp?intProductID=733252&intArtistID=5371|title=SOMEWHERE IN THE REAL WORLD (AMOROSI, VANESSA): Music: Herald Sun HiT|date=20 December 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071220184226/http://www.heraldsunhit.com.au/product.asp?intProductID=733252&intArtistID=5371|access-date=17 April 2020|archive-date=20 December 2007}}</ref><ref name="Somewhere_in_the_Real_World_release">{{cite news|title=Delta circus keeps Vanessa on hold|url=http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/celebrity/delta-circus-keeps-vanessa-on-hold/story-e6frfmqr-1111114743429|access-date=4 August 2013|newspaper=News.com.au|date=29 October 2007}}</ref> The album tracks "Perfect" and "Start It" have both been used in promotions for the Seven Network. "Start It" is Channel 7's AFL song for 2008 and was used again in 2009. Released digitally and in stores on 24 May 2008, ''Somewhere in the Real World'' reached gold sales in Australia.

The single "[[Kiss Your Mama!]]" was serviced to radio on 3 August and released 8 September 2007.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070901174026/http://www.accessallareas.net.au/data/EElluuAAlFlzCiYJfF.php ''Vanessa Amorosi hits the road!''] accessallareas.net.au, 31 August 2007, Archived from [http://www.accessallareas.net.au/data/EElluuAAlFlzCiYJfF.php ''the original''] on 1 September 2007.</ref>

On a special edition of ''Australian Idol'' she sang her hit song "Kiss Your Mama!".<ref>{{cite web|title=Doin' It for the Kids |work=Australian Idol |publisher=Network Ten |date=20 October 2007 |url=http://www.australianidol.com.au/news-doin-it-for-the-kids-night.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080722045228/http://www.australianidol.com.au/news-doin-it-for-the-kids-night.html |archive-date=22 July 2008 }}</ref> This marked Amorosi's returned to the [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] charts the first single in many years to be released, the song peaked at No. 15.

Amorosi released her second single, "[[Perfect (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Perfect]]", digitally on 5 April and physically on 26 April 2008.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20080323071101/http://www.accessallareas.net.au/data/EkpVlAZuVAvzCbVBTy.php ''Vanessa Amorosi's Perfect new single!''] accessallareas.net.au, 19 March 2008, Archived from [http://www.accessallareas.net.au/data/EkpVlAZuVAvzCbVBTy.php ''the original''] on 23 March 2008.</ref> The single made it to number 1 on the digital iTunes charts on 7 June 2008, and a then equal highest charting singles of her career at No. 4. "Perfect" reached Platinum sales in Australia and was the most played Australian song on Australian radio for 2008. "Perfect" was nominated for the 2009 APRA awards for 'Most Played Australian Work'.

Following the success of her first two singles a third single was released from the album, the song "[[The Simple Things (Something Emotional)]]" was released on 20 September 2008. The Song received a video and achieved a top 40 peak. In December 2008 a video for the song "My House" was released onto her website, but never released as a single.

On 20 April 2009, an Amorosi fan website revealed details of a collaboration with US band [[Hoobastank]] on a single titled "[[The Letter (Hoobastank song)|The Letter]]". ===2009â€“2010: ''Hazardous''===
Amorosi's album ''[[Hazardous (album)|Hazardous]]'' became her third ARIA Top 10 album. Debuting at No. 7, it was also the week's highest album debut by an Australian artist and has been accredited platinum by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]]. The album has produced the singles "[[This Is Who I Am (song)|This Is Who I Am]]" (Amorosi's first No. 1), top 30 hit "[[Hazardous (song)|Hazardous]]" and third single "[[Mr. Mysterious]]" featuring [[Seany B (artist)|Seany B]]. Amorosi released the album to the German market in early June 2010.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20110728002141/http://schattenblick.org/infopool/musik/fakten/mflb2462.html ''Universal International Newsletter â€“ 13. Kalenderwoche.''] schattenblick.org, 31 March 2010, Archived from [http://schattenblick.org/infopool/musik/fakten/mflb2462.html ''the original''] on 28 July 2011.</ref>

On 28 August 2009, Amorosi's new song "[[This Is Who I Am (song)|This Is Who I Am]]" was serviced to Australian radio. On 31 August 2009, Universal Music Australia released a press release for Amorosi's new single with details of new single and album. {{cquote|The first single to spawn from her forthcoming album is the fierce pop-rock song "This Is Who I Am". The undoubtable hit single was produced by MachoPsycho (Pink) and mixed by Chris Lord-Alge (Green Day) and was serviced to Australian radio this week. The video for "This Is Who I Am" was recently shot in Los Angeles and directed by Christopher R Watson. The video was predominately shot on green screen and will launch on 14 September.}}

"This Is Who I Am" debuted at number 1 on the [[ARIA Charts]] for the week beginning 18 October 2009. This is Amorosi's first [[List of number-one singles of 2009 (Australia)|number 1 single]]. The single held the top spot for a second week in a row â€“ becoming the first Australian artist to debut at No. 1 and hold No. 1 single since [[Silverchair]]'s "[[Straight Lines (song)|Straight Lines]]" in 2007. "This Is Who I Am" has achieved 2Ã— platinum sales in Australia making it her most successful single to date in her homeland.

With the success of her album and first single "This Is Who I Am", Amorosi released her second single and title track from the album ''Hazardous''.
Amorosi flew to Los Angeles to film the video for "[[Hazardous (song)|Hazardous]]" with director Paul Brown ([[Prince (musician)|Prince]], Alicia Keys), which was serviced Thursday, 17 December 2009. The single peaked at No. 29 on the [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] Charts â€“ a solid follow-up single to her first number 1.

It was announced that Amorosi would collaborate with Mary J. Blige, so Amorosi flew to Los Angeles on 1 February to film the music video for her duet with [[Mary J. Blige]] for the upcoming single "Each Tear". The new version of the track will feature on an international re-release of Blige's latest record ''[[Stronger with Each Tear]]''.<ref>Kathy McCabe: [https://web.archive.org/web/20120905094734/http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/sydney-confidential/aussie-duo-vanessa-amorosi-and-orianthi-oblige-soul-queen-mary-j-blige/story-e6frewz0-1225828022527 ''Aussie duo Vanessa Amorosi and Orianthi oblige soul queen Mary J Blige.''] dailytelegraph.com.au, 9 February 2010, Archived from [http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/sydney-confidential/aussie-duo-vanessa-amorosi-and-orianthi-oblige-soul-queen-mary-j-blige/story-e6frewz0-1225828022527 ''the original''] on 5 September 2012.</ref>

On 4 March 2010, Amorosi announced "[[Mr. Mysterious]]" as the third single from the ''Hazardous'' album via newsletter.<ref>[http://www.myreg.net/emailarchive?id=0000026353&field=html_template]{{Dead link|date=July 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> During March 2010 Vanessa Amorosi supported [[Rob Thomas (musician)|Rob Thomas]] on his Australian leg of the [[Cradlesong Tour]], as well as performing headline shows for her fans separately.

"[[Holiday (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Holiday]]" was released as the fourth single off ''Hazardous'' and charted in the top 50 on the ARIA charts.<ref>[http://www.australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Vanessa+Amorosi&titel=Holiday&cat=s ''Vanessa Amorosi â€“ "Holiday".''] australian-charts.com, Retrieved 14 November 2017.</ref> The song is one of her favourite tracks on the album: "Holiday is inspired by a fantasy of escaping to a far off island, warm weather, with sand at my feet, partying all night and dancing till dawn", Amorosi explains.

===2010â€“2012: ''V'' and its delays===
In September 2010, Amorosi revealed on her official Twitter account she was back in the studio again with MachoPsycho in London. In October 2010, she wrote to her mailing list announcing she was writing and recording in Los Angeles for her new studio album,<ref>[http://www.myreg.net/emailarchive?id=0000027085&field=html_template]{{Dead link|date=August 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> which is described to "push boundaries" and "may even shock people".<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20110716190439/http://www.take40.com/news/22057/interview---vanessa-amorosi-thanks-fans-for-support-as-favourite-aussie-artist ''INTERVIEW â€“ Vanessa Amorosi Thanks Fans For Support As Favourite Aussie Artist.''] take40.com, 1 February 2011, Archived from [http://www.take40.com/news/22057/interview---vanessa-amorosi-thanks-fans-for-support-as-favourite-aussie-artist ''the original''] on 16 July 2011.</ref>

Amorosi's first single "[[Gossip (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Gossip]]" from her forthcoming album ''[[V (Vanessa Amorosi album)|V]]'' was released on 1 July 2011 and became the No. 1 added song on Australian radio in the first week,<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20110917135757/http://auspop.blogspot.com/2011/07/most-added_11.html ''MOST ADDED.''] auspop.blogspot.com, 11 July 2011, Archived from [http://auspop.blogspot.com/2011/07/most-added_11.html ''the original''] on 17 September 2011.</ref> but did not chart in the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA]] Top 100 Singles. The second single "[[Amazing (Vanessa Amorosi song)|Amazing]]" was released 9 September 2011 and peaked at No. 83 on the ARIA Singles Chart.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20131024054020/http://auspop.blogspot.com/2011/10/chart-watch_16.html ''CHART WATCH.''] auspop.blogspot.com, 16 October 2011, Archived from [http://auspop.blogspot.com/2011/10/chart-watch_16.html ''the original''] on 24 October 2013.</ref> The song was featured on Channel 7's AFL Grand Final ad which also resulted in Vanessa Amorosi performing at the AFL Grand Final in 2011. 2011 also saw Amorosi work on such campaigns as Proactiv, Headspace and Kids Helpline.

The tracklist and previews were already available in the Australian [[iTunes]] store,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://forum.popjustice.com/threads/vanessa-amorosi-v-5th-album-singles.23002/|title=Vanessa Amorosi â€“ V (5th Album & Singles)|website=The Popjustice Forum}}</ref> but on 3 November 2011, Amorosi announced her album due for release on 11 November would be delayed until further notice.<ref>{{cite web|title=V Vanessa Amorosi|url=http://www.sanity.com.au/products/2204654/V_Limited_Edition%20www.sanity.com.au/products/2204654/V_Limited_Edition|publisher=Sanity|access-date=4 August 2013}}</ref> In August 2012, Amorosi announced she was no longer with Universal Music, and would be looking to rebuild her career as an album artist.<ref>{{cite news|last=McCabe|first=Kathy|title=Vanessa Amorosi leaves Universal to rebuild career|url=http://www.news.com.au/national-news/vanessa-amorosi-leaves-universal-for-creative-future/story-fndo4eg9-1226456645346|access-date=4 August 2013|newspaper=[[News.com.au]]|date=23 August 2012}}</ref> The album is still unreleased to this day.

==Personal life==
Following the release of ''Hazardous'' in 2009, Amorosi relocated to Los Angeles, California, where she currently lives.<ref name=MorningBulletin/> In October 2017 she married martial arts trainer Rod Busby. The couple have a son, Killian.
