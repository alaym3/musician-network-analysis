'''Amanda Lee''' (born March 13, 1992), also known as '''AmaLee''', is an American singer and voice actress. She is known for her English-language interpretations of songs from [[anime]] and [[video games]] on YouTube,<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web|last=Deming|first=Mark|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/amalee-mn0003502907/biography|title=AmaLee {{!}} Biography & History|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> which have been viewed over 600 million times and has acquired her over 1&nbsp;million subscribers.<ref name="LeeandLie YouTube">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/LeeandLie/about|title=LeeandLie YouTube Channel|via=YouTube|access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref> In [[voice acting]], she has voiced in titles such as ''[[Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2]]'', ''[[Rio: Rainbow Gate!]]'', and ''[[Yandere Simulator]]''.

Lee has also provided vocals for many independent video-game soundtracks and theme songs ‚Äì including a collaboration with [[Porter Robinson]] on "Fellow Feeling".<ref name="AmaLee Robinson">{{cite news|last=Greene|first=Scott|url=https://www.youredm.com/2014/10/31/edm-exclusive-interview-porter-robinson-worlds/|title=Your EDM Exclusive Interview: Porter Robinson on 'Worlds'|website=Your EDM|date=October 31, 2014|accessdate=April 11, 2018}}</ref> In early 2017, she released her debut album, ''Nostalgia'', which brought together English-language versions of 12 songs beloved by gaming and anime fans. The album charted at No. 6 on [[Top Heatseekers|''Billboard'' Heatseekers Albums]],<ref name="Heat Nostalgia">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/amalee/chart-history/heatseekers-albums/song/1010559|title=AmaLee "Nostalgia" Heatseekers chart|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 28, 2017|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> No. 27 on [[Independent Albums|''Billboard'' Independent Albums]],<ref name="Ind Nostalgia">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/amalee/chart-history/independent-albums/song/1010559|title=AmaLee "Nostalgia" Independent chart|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 28, 2017|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> No. 43 on [[Billboard charts#Top Rock Albums|''Billboard'' Top Rock Albums]],<ref name="Top Rock Nostalgia">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/amalee/chart-history/rock-albums/song/1010559|title=AmaLee "Nostalgia" Top Rock chart|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 28, 2017|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> and No. 12 on [[UK Independent Singles and Album Breakers Charts|UK Independent Album Breakers]].<ref name="Indie Breaker Nostalgia">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/independent-albums-breakers-chart/20170113/255/|title=Official Independent Album Breakers Chart Top 20|website=[[Official Charts Company|Official Charts]]|date=January 13, 2017|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2006, AmaLee began posting homemade videos of her covers of tunes popular with anime followers and gamers. Later in 2010, she and her friend Annalie created YouTube channel called '''LeeandLie'''.<ref name="AmaLee post on Twitter">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/amaamaleelee/status/693201174745464832?lang=en|title=AmaLee post on Twitter|website=Official Twitter|date=January 29, 2016|access-date=April 11, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=October 2019}}</ref> According to AmaLee, their channel name is a combination of "Lee" from her name, and "Lie" from Annalie's name.<ref name="AmaLee post on Twitter" /> On December 14, 2010, LeeandLie uploaded their first video, a [[Cover version|cover]] of the song "Scarlet" from the anime ''[[Ceres, Celestial Legend|Ayashi no Ceres]]'', translated and performed by AmaLee.

In 2011, AmaLee joined AX Idol, a singing competition hosted by [[Bang Zoom! Entertainment]] and [[Viz Media]], affiliated with [[Anime Expo]], a convention for anime fans held annually in Los Angeles and won the grand prize.<ref name="AllMusic" /><ref>{{cite news|author=Lizzy|url=https://honeysanime.com/amalee-and-mari-iijima-ax-2017-press-conference/|title=AmaLee and Mari Iijima AX 2017 Press Conference|website=HoneysAnime|date=July 7, 2017|accessdate=April 11, 2018}}</ref> Since then, she has also had the occupation of providing her voice to shows and titles such as ''[[Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2]]'', ''[[One Piece]]'', ''[[Yandere Simulator]]'', ''[[Gosick]]'', ''[[Rio: Rainbow Gate!]]'', ''[[K-On!!]]'', and ''[[Show by Rock!!]]'', among others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.behindthevoiceactors.com/Amanda-Lee/|title=Amanda Lee (Visual voices guide)}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1967053/|title=AmaLee}}</ref> Amanda has also provided vocals for many independent [[Video game music|video game soundtracks]] and theme songs ‚Äì including a collaboration with [[Porter Robinson]].<ref name="AmaLee Robinson" /> She is also known for singing the English version of "Lagrima", the eleventh ending theme of ''[[Dragon Ball Super]]''.

On September 2, 2012, they uploaded their most viewed video, a cover of the song "Crossing Field" from the anime ''[[Sword Art Online]]'', with over 18 million views as of April 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MO60ahwc8FI|title=Sword Art Online ‚Äì "Crossing Field" (Opening) {{!}} English ver {{!}} AmaLee|via=YouTube|publisher=LeeandLie|date= September 2, 2012|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> In 2014, Annalie left the channel, leaving it the sole property of AmaLee, though the channel name remains "LeeandLie".<ref>{{cite web|date=October 19, 2014|title=LiveChat No. 4 // Annalie's last livestream!|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzeSb3H70to|access-date=April 11, 2018|publisher=LeeandLieTV|via=YouTube}}</ref>

On January 7, 2017, she released her first album, ''Nostalgia'', which brought together English-language versions of 12 songs beloved<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://animemotivation.com/most-popular-anime-songs/|title = The MOST Popular Anime Songs, According to YouTube}}</ref> by gaming and anime fans.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/nostalgia/1184663698|title=Nostalgia by AmaLee|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> The album charted at No. 6 on [[Top Heatseekers|''Billboard'' Heatseekers Albums]],<ref name="Heat Nostalgia" /> No. 27 on [[Independent Albums|''Billboard'' Independent Albums]],<ref name="Ind Nostalgia" /> and No. 43 on [[Billboard charts#Top Rock Albums|''Billboard'' Top Rock Albums]].<ref name="Top Rock Nostalgia" /> On September 22, 2017, she released an [[Extended play|EP]] titled ''Hourglass'' which contains five original songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/hourglass-ep/1270504890|title=Hourglass ‚Äì EP by AmaLee|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/amalee-mn0003502907/biography|title = AmaLee &#124; Biography & History}}</ref>

In July 2017, Lee performed in [[Los Angeles Convention Center]] as part of the 2017 [[Anime Expo]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Amo|first=Meg|url=https://www.anime-expo.org/2016/11/23/amalee-joins-ax-2017-for-10th-anniversary-of-late-comedy-showdown-2/|title=AmaLee joins AX 2017 for 10th Anniversary of Late Comedy Showdown|publisher=[[Anime Expo]]|date=November 23, 2016 |accessdate=April 11, 2018}}</ref> On August 22, 2017, it was announced that AmaLee would be joining YouTube stars NateWantsToBattle and MandoPony on "The Cool and Good Tour" across America.<ref>{{cite news|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|url=https://www.fuse.tv/2017/08/nathan-sharp-natewantstobattle-tour-amalee-mando-pony|title=Nathan Sharp announces Fall Tour with fellow YouTube stars|publisher=[[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse TV]]|date=August 22, 2017|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> AmaLee provides vocals and lyrics on multiplayer online battle arena game ''[[League of Legends]]'' 2019 Ranked Season trailer.<ref>{{cite news|last=Morrissy|first=Kim|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/interest/2019-01-23/p.a-works-produces-league-of-legends-video-game-ad/.142399|title=P.A.
