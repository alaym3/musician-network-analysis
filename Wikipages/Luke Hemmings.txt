'''Luke Robert Hemmings''' (born 16 July 1996) is an Australian singer-songwriter and musician, best known for being the lead vocalist of the Australian [[pop rock]] band [[5 Seconds of Summer]]. Since 2014, 5 Seconds of Summer have sold more than 10 million albums, sold over 2 million concert tickets worldwide, and the band's songs streams surpass 7 billion, making them one of the most successful Australian musical exports in history.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-17|title=Australia's Annual Music Exports Worth $137M: Report|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/8520286/australia-annual-music-exports-report|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Why 5SOS are finally feeling the love in Australia|url=https://www.sunshinecoastdaily.com.au/news/why-5sos-are-finally-feeling-the-love-in-australia/3769636/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Sunshine Coast Daily|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=5 SECONDS OF SUMMER: Umusic|url=http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/5-seconds-of-summer-announce-new-album-calm-out-march-27/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200212064035/http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/5-seconds-of-summer-announce-new-album-calm-out-march-27/|archive-date=12 February 2020|access-date=2020-07-27|website=Umusic|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-31|title=5 Seconds of Summer|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/live/8549774/5-seconds-summer-north-american-no-shame-tour-dates|access-date=2020-07-27|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

His debut studio album, ''[[When Facing the Things We Turn Away From]]'', was released through [[Sony Music Australia]] on 13 August 2021. ''When Facing the Things We Turn Away From'' debuted atop the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]] and peaked within the top 20 in the [[Ultratop Flanders|Belgium]], [[Scottish Singles and Albums Charts|Scotland]], and [[Official Albums Chart|the UK]].

== Early life ==

Hemmings was born on 16 July 1996, and raised in the [[Freemans Reach, New South Wales|Freemans Reach]] suburb of the [[Hawkesbury, New South Wales|Hawkesbury]] district in [[New South Wales]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|title=Hey, Lets Make a Band!|year=2014|isbn=9780062366443|pages=15|last1=Summer|first1=5. [...] I grew up in a small house in the middle of nowhere".<ref name=":022"/> When he was 10 years old, Hemmings' brothers taught him how to play guitar, later prompting him to take professional lessons and learning from [[YouTube]] tutorial videos to learn how to play the instrument.<ref name=":0" /> Eventually, Hemmings began [[busking]] throughout his tween years.<ref name=":22">{{cite web|title=5 Seconds Of Summer on their addiction to rally hot girls. In [[Year Seven|Year 7]], Hemmings switched schools, attending Norwest Christian College, where he met future band-mates, [[Calum Hood]] and [[Michael Clifford (musician)|Michael Clifford]], later revealing that he initially disliked and was "enemies" with Clifford.<ref name=":12">{{cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer Band Members Were Once Frenemies|url=https://people.com/celebrity/5-seconds-of-summer-band-members-were-once-frenemies/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170515044235/http://people.com/celebrity/5-seconds-of-summer-band-members-were-once-frenemies/|archive-date=15 May 2017|access-date=2020-05-24|website=People}}</ref> Hemmings eventually befriended Hood after they performed a [[Secondhand Serenade]] cover at a school talent show, and later befriended Clifford after learning they had similar music taste.<ref name=":0" />
Following the band's rise to fame, Hemmings continued with his high school education, opting to switch to [[distance learning]]. In 2013, he chose to not finish [[Year Twelve|Year 12]], the final year of secondary schooling in Australia, due to his commitment to the band.<ref name=":32">{{cite web|title=5SOS on leaving school to pursue music: 'We had to prove to our parents it was worth it'|url=https://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/londonnewsdesk/view/108848/?isworld=y|access-date=2020-05-22|website=Pressparty}}</ref><ref name=":42">{{cite web|last=Creswell|first=Toby|date=2013-03-09|title=The songs may change but the story of a hit remains the same|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/the-songs-may-change-but-the-story-of-a-hit-remains-the-same-20130309-2fsb2.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172433/http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/the-songs-may-change-but-the-story-of-a-hit-remains-the-same-20130309-2fsb2.html|archive-date=21 October 2016|access-date=2020-05-22|website=The Sydney Morning Herald}}</ref>

== Career ==
===2011-present: 5 Seconds of Summer===
{{Main|5 Seconds of Summer}}
In 2011, at the age of 14, Hemmings began posting [[YouTube]] song cover videos, under the username "hemmo1996". Hemmings' first video, a cover of [[Mike Posner]]'s "[[Please Don't Go (Mike Posner song)|Please Don't Go]]", was posted on 3 February 2011. As Hemmings' covers began gaining traction on the platform, he invited Hood and Clifford to join his videos. The trio eventually added mutual friend [[Ashton Irwin]] to their videos, forming the current [[5 Seconds of Summer]] lineup.<ref name="auto3">{{cite web|date=2012-05-14|title=Meet 5 Seconds Of Summer, Your Hosts For This Evenings Entertainment|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/features/meet-5-seconds-of-summer-your-hosts-for-this-evenings-entertainment/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180923212331/http://musicfeeds.com.au/features/meet-5-seconds-of-summer-your-hosts-for-this-evenings-entertainment/|archive-date=23 September 2018|access-date=2020-05-24|website=Music Feeds}}</ref> &nbsp;After months of posting song covers together, the band began attracting interest from major music labels and publishers and signed a publishing deal with [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite news|last=Creswell|first=Toby|date=30 July 2012|title=Smells Like Teen Spirit|work=The Age|location=Melbourne|url=http://www.theage.com.au/entertainment/music/smells-like-teen-spirit-20120730-2397m.html|access-date=11 August 2012}}</ref> Hemmings has since released four studio albums with the band, each met with worldwide success: ''[[5 Seconds of Summer (album)|5 Seconds of Summer]]'' (2014), ''[[Sounds Good Feels Good]]'' (2015), ''[[Youngblood (5 Seconds of Summer album)|Youngblood]]'' (2018), and ''[[Calm (album)|Calm]]'' (2020).<ref>{{Cite news|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=2018-06-24|title=5SOS on demons, lies and gun control|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-44574089|access-date=2020-07-27}}</ref>

===2021: Solo career: ''When Facing the Things We Turn Away From''===
{{main|When Facing the Things We Turn Away From}}
In June 2021, Hemmings announced his debut solo album ''[[When Facing the Things We Turn Away From]]'', which was released on 13 August 2021. The album was preceded by the singles "Starting Line", "Motion" and "Place in Me".
