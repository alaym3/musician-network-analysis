'''Emilie Autumn Liddell'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/emilieautumn/status/11670577995|title=There have been questions...|date=April 5, 2010|author=Autumn, Emilie|publisher=Twitter|access-date=February 2, 2011|quote=There have been questions about my legal name for some reason lately ‚Äì It's in the book. Emilie Autumn Liddell.}}</ref> (born September 22, 1979), better known by her stage name '''Emilie Autumn''', is an American singer-songwriter, poet, author and violinist. Autumn's musical style is described by her as "Fairy Pop", "Fantasy Rock" or "Victoriandustrial". It is influenced by [[glam rock]] and from plays, novels, and history, particularly the [[Victorian era]]. Performing with her all-female backup dancers The Bloody Crumpets, Autumn incorporates elements of classical music, cabaret, electronica, and glam rock with theatrics, and [[burlesque]].<ref name="Bizarre mag">{{cite web |last1=Stanborough |first1=Denise |title=Emilie Autumn |url=http://www.bizarremag.com/entertainment/interviews/9292/emilie_autumn.html |website=Bizarre Magazine |access-date=1 August 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100604060630/http://www.bizarremag.com/entertainment/interviews/9292/emilie_autumn.html |archive-date=4 June 2010 |date=April 2010}}</ref><ref name=gazette>{{Cite news|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/09337/1017976-437.stm |work=[[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]] |date=December 3, 2009 |title=Weekend Hotlist |access-date=January 3, 2010 }}</ref>

Growing up in [[Malibu, California]], Autumn began learning the violin at the age of four and left regular school five years later with the goal of becoming a world-class violinist; she practiced eight or nine hours a day and read a wide range of literature. Progressing to writing her own music, she studied under various teachers and went to [[Indiana University]], which she left over issues regarding the relationship between classical music and the appearance of the performer. Through her own [[independent label]] Traitor Records, Autumn debuted with her classical album ''[[On a Day...|On a Day: Music for Violin & Continuo]]'', followed by the release in 2003 of her album ''[[Enchant (album)|Enchant]]''.

Autumn appeared in singer [[Courtney Love]]'s backing band on her 2004 ''[[America's Sweetheart (Courtney Love album)|America's Sweetheart]]'' tour and returned to Europe. She released the 2006 album ''[[Opheliac]]'' with the German label [[Trisol Music Group]]. In 2007, she released ''[[Laced/Unlaced]]''; the re-release of ''On a Day...'' appeared as ''Laced'' with songs on the [[electric violin]] as ''Unlaced''. She later left Trisol to join New York based The End Records in 2009 and release ''Opheliac'' in the United States, where previously it had only been available as an import. In 2012, she released the album ''[[Fight Like a Girl (Emilie Autumn album)|Fight Like a Girl]]''. She played the role of the Painted Doll in [[Darren Lynn Bousman]]'s 2012 film ''[[The Devil's Carnival]]'', as well as its 2015 sequel, ''[[Alleluia! The Devil's Carnival]]''.

==Life and career==

===1979‚Äì2000: Beginnings===
[[File:Pachelbel-canon-colors.png|thumb|right|Autumn attributes her ability to write music in her mind to the fact that as a child, she played ''[[Pachelbel's Canon in D]]'' (pictured) mentally every night.<ref name="vampirefreaks"/>]]
Emilie Autumn was born in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]],<ref name="The End Records">{{cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100203020458/http://www.theendrecords.com/artists/emilie-autumn|archive-date=February 3, 2010|url-status=dead|url=http://theend.dreamhosters.com/artists/emilie-autumn/|title=Emilie Autumn|publisher=The End Records|access-date=February 16, 2011|df=mdy-all}}</ref> on September 22, 1979.<ref name = "Bio" /> Autumn grew up in [[Malibu, California]].{{#tag:ref|Autumn has also said that she grew up in Los Angeles.<ref name="Birthday chat"/><!-- Answer to Question 57: "I adore Chicago, from the moment I got off the plane. It is an amazing city, and growing up in LA, I didn't know what that was like." -->|group="note"}} She has stated that "being surrounded by nature and sea had a lot to do with [her] development as a 'free spirit.'"<ref name="MD"/> Her mother worked as a [[seamstress]],<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic | class =artist|id=p530236|pure_url=yes}}|title=Emilie Autumn|author=Wilson, MacKenzie|publisher=Allmusic|access-date=August 11, 2010}}</ref> and she has said that her father was a German immigrant<ref name = "Teatime suffering"/> with whom she did not share a close relationship.<ref name="Dad">{{cite web|url=http://forum.emilieautumn.com/viewtopic.php?f=59&t=364 |title=My dad's gone, and more pleasant notes on the world today... |date=September 28, 2004 |author=Autumn, Emilie |publisher=Emilie Autumn Ent |access-date=September 21, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111005080850/http://forum.emilieautumn.com/viewtopic.php?f=59&t=364 |archive-date=October 5, 2011 |df=mdy }}</ref> While not musicians, her family enjoyed various genres of music.<ref name="MD"/>

When Autumn was four years old, she started learning the violin, and later commented: "I remember asking for a violin, but I don't remember knowing what one was. I might have thought it was a kind of pony for all I know, but I don't remember being disappointed."<ref name="vampirefreaks">{{cite web|url=http://vampirefreaks.com/content/comment.php?entry=237 debut as a solo violinist performing with an orchestra, and won a competition.<ref name="MD"/> At the age of nine or ten,{{#tag:ref|In a 2003 interview, she stated that she was ten years old when she becoming a world-class violinist.<ref name="Shred News interview">{{cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716064712/http://www.shrednews.com/chat-with-emilie-autumn/|archive-date=July While convinced that she would only play violin, eighteen-year-old Autumn decided to sing on one of her songs as a way of demonstrating to a major music producer, who wanted to sign her on a label, how it should sound.<ref name="MD"/> She became unhappy with the changes done to her songs, and decided to break away from the label and create her own [[independent record label]], Traitor Records.<ref name="MD"/> Through it, she debuted with her classical album ''[[On a Day...|On a Day: Music for Violin & Continuo]]'', which she recorded in 1997 when she was seventeen years old;<ref name="Bio"/><ref name="MD"/> its title refers to the fact that the album took only a day to record.<ref name="MD"/> It consists of her performing works for the [[baroque violin]] accompanied by Roger Lebow on the [[baroque cello]], Edward Murray on [[harpsichord]], and Michael Egan on [[lute]].<ref name="MD"/><ref>{{cite book|title=On a day-- : music for violin and continuo|publisher=[[Worldcat]]|oclc = 123125566}}</ref> She considered it "more of a demo despite its length", and released it as "a saleable album" after fans who enjoyed her "rock performances starting asking for a classical album so that they could hear more of the violin."<ref name="MD"/> She also debuted with her poetry book ''Across the Sky & Other Poems'' in 2000, later re-released in 2005 as ''[[Your Sugar Sits Untouched]]'' with a music-accompanied [[audiobook]].<ref name="YSSU">{{cite web|url=http://www.asylumemporium.com/collections/music/products/your-sugar-sits-untouched|title=Your Sugar Sits Untouched|publisher=The Asylum Emporium (Emilie Autumn Ent. ===2001‚Äì04: ''Enchant'' and collaborations===
As part of a recording project, Autumn traveled to [[Chicago, Illinois]], in 2001, and decided to stay because she enjoyed the public transportation system and music scene there.<ref name="MD"/> She released the 2001 [[extended play|EP]] ''[[Chambermaid (EP)|Chambermaid]]'' while finishing ''Enchant''‚Äîshe alternatively labeled the musical style on ''Chambermaid'' as "fantasy rock" and [[cabaret]]‚Äîand wrote the 2001 [[charity single]] "[[By the Sword (EP)|By the Sword]]" after the events of September 11, 2001.<ref name="Bio"/><ref name="MD"/> According to her, the song is about strength, not violence; the act of swearing by the sword represents "an unbreakable promise to right a wrong, to stay true".<ref name="MD"/>

On February 26, 2003,{{#tag:ref|While Autumn's official site lists the release date as 2002,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.asylumemporium.com/collections/music/products/enchant|title=Enchant|publisher=The Asylum Emporium (Emilie Autumn Ent. LLC)|access-date=April 4, 2012}}</ref> newsletters published by Autumn and her interview with Musical Discovery point to the release date as February 26, 2003, with a free download of the complete album offered in January 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.batteredrose.com/newsletters/2003/01_14_03.html |title=Emilie Autumn Newsletter for 01/14/03 |date=January 14, 2003 |publisher=Zoe French |access-date=June 20, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723181436/http://www.batteredrose.com/newsletters/2003/01_14_03.html |archive-date=July 23, 2011 |df=mdy }}</ref>|group="note"}} Autumn released her [[concept album]] ''[[Enchant (album)|Enchant]]'', which spanned multiple musical styles: "[[New-age music|new-age]], pop and [[trip hop]] [[chamber music]]".<ref name="Bizarre mag"/> Written during her late teenage years, ''Enchant'' revolved around the supernatural realm and its effect on the modern-day world. Autumn labeled it as "fantasy rock", which dealt with "dreams and stories and ghosts and faeries who'll bite your head off if you dare to touch them".<ref name="MD"/> The faery-themed "Enchant Puzzle" appeared on the artwork of the album; her reward for the person who would solve it consisted of faery-related items.<ref name="MD"/> Her bandmates consisted of cellist Joey Harvey, drummer Heath Jansen, guitarist Ben Lehl, and bassist Jimmy Vanaria, who also worked on the electronics.<ref name="MD"/> At the same time of ''Enchant''{{'}}s release, Autumn had several side projects: Convent, a musical group for which she recorded all four voices; Ravensong, "a classical baroque ensemble" that she formed with friends in California; and The Jane Brooks Project, which she dedicated to the real-life, 16th-century Jane Brooks‚Äîa woman executed for witchcraft.<ref name="MD">{{cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718133329/http://musicaldiscoveries.com///reviews/emilieautumn.htm|archive-date=July 18, 2011|url-status=live|url=http://www.musicaldiscoveries.com/reviews/emilieautumn.htm|title=Emilie Autumn at Musical Discoveries|author=Elliot, Russell W.|date=October 15, 2003|publisher=Musical Discoveries|access-date=August 12, 2010}}</ref>{{#tag:ref|{{As of|2012}}, no albums from any of her side-projects have been released. In 2003, Autumn had plans to release Ravensong's album live as "a radio broadcasted concert", so that the listeners would have "something to enjoy while we finish the studio recording".<ref name="MD"/> The album from The Jane Brooks Project, tentatively titled ''The Jane Brooks Songbook Volume I'' and later ''The Jane Brooks Project: Volume I'', was slated for release "early next year", according to a 2003 interview;<ref name="MD"/><ref name="August 2004">{{cite web|url=http://www.batteredrose.com/newsletters/2004/8_25_04.html |title=Emilie Autumn Newsletter:for 8/25/04 |date=August 25, 2004 |publisher=Zoe French |access-date=July 31, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110930173903/http://www.batteredrose.com/newsletters/2004/8_25_04.html |archive-date=September 30, 2011 |df=mdy }}</ref> near the end of July 2004, Autumn commented that the album "is very near complete and we are mixing at present".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://forum.emilieautumn.com/viewtopic.php?f=59&t=315 |title=Answer to InkyDusts's Question |date=July 30, 2004 |author=Autumn, Emilie |publisher=Emilie Autumn Ent. ==Influences and musical style==
[[File:Emilie Autumn at Nachtleben 2007 ter.jpg|thumb|Autumn in Frankfurt, 2007]]
Her music encompasses a wide range of styles.<ref name=gazette/> Autumn's vocal range is [[contralto]], but also has the ability to perform in the [[dramatic soprano]] range.<ref name="Birthday chat">{{cite web |url=http://batteredrose.com/info/transcripts/eas-birthday-chat/ |title=EA's Birthday Chat |date=September 22, 2006 |publisher=Zoe French |access-date=July 11, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100626002446/http://batteredrose.com/info/transcripts/eas-birthday-chat/ |archive-date=June 26, 2010 |df=mdy }}</ref> Her vocal work has been compared to [[Tori Amos]],<ref name="Enchant"/><ref name="Allmusic review"/> [[Kate Bush]],<ref name="Allmusic review"/> and [[The Creatures]].<ref name="Enchant"/> She has released two instrumental albums (''On a Day...'' and ''Laced/Unlaced''), and four featuring her vocals: ''Enchant'', ''Opheliac'', ''A Bit o' This & That'', and "Fight Like a Girl". The 2003 album ''Enchant'' drew on "new age chamber music, trip hop baroque, and experimental space pop".<ref name="Enchant">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r602979|pure_url=yes}}|title=Enchant|author=Spano, Charles|publisher=Allmusic|access-date=September 11, 2010}}</ref> Autumn layers her voice frequently, and incorporates electronics and electronic effects into her work on ''Enchant''; she also combines strings and piano for some songs, while others feature mainly the piano or violin.<ref name="MD"/> The 2006 release ''Opheliac'' featured "cabaret, electronic, symphonic, new age, and good ol' rock & roll (and heavy on the theatrical bombast)".<ref name="Allmusic review">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r937668|pure_url=yes}}|title=Opheliac|author=Prato, Greg|publisher=Allmusic|access-date=August 12, 2010}}</ref>

A classically trained musician, Autumn is influenced by plays, novels, and history, particularly the [[Victorian era]].<ref name="Teatime suffering">{{cite web |url=http://batteredrose.com/info/transcripts/teatime-suffering/ |title=Battered Rose >> Teatime Suffering |publisher=Zoe French |access-date=December 10, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110211054133/http://batteredrose.com/info/transcripts/teatime-suffering/ |archive-date=February 11, 2011 |df=mdy }}</ref><ref name="LA Weekly"/> She enjoys the works of [[Shakespeare]], [[Elizabeth Barrett Browning]] and husband [[Robert Browning|Robert]], and [[Edgar Allan Poe]].<ref name="Teatime suffering"/> She incorporates sounds resembling Victorian machinery such as [[locomotives]], which she noted was "sort of a steampunk thing".<ref name="LA Weekly"/> While a young Autumn cited [[Itzhak Perlman]] as an influence because of the happiness she believed he felt when he played, her main musical influence and inspiration is the English violinist [[Nigel Kennedy]].<ref name="MD"/> Her favorite singer is [[Morrissey]] from The Smiths.<ref name="Shred News interview"/> She takes inspiration for her songs from her life experiences and mixes in "layers and layers of references, connections, other stories and [[metaphor]]s".<ref name="Teatime suffering"/> Autumn has variously described her music and style as "Psychotic Vaudeville Burlesque",<ref name="Green Planet">{{cite web|url=http://planetgreen.discovery.com/feature/instrumental/vaudevilleviolinist-emilie-autumn-interview.html|title=Interview with a Vegan Vaudeville Violinist: Talking Music, Justice, and Rodent Love with the One and Only Emilie Autumn|author=Z., Mickey|publisher=Green Planet|access-date=August 12, 2010}}</ref> "Victoriandustrial'", a term she coined, and [[glam rock]] because of her use of glitter onstage.<ref name="LA Weekly"/><ref name="ReGen">{{cite web|url=http://www.regenmag.com/Interviews-317-Emilie-Autumn.html |title=Interview: Emilie Autumn‚ÄìEverything and Nothing |author=Y√ºcel, Ilker |date=April 28, 2010 |work=ReGen Magazine |access-date=November 17, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100503134717/http://www.regenmag.com/Interviews-317-Emilie-Autumn.html |archive-date=May 3, 2010 }}</ref><ref name="March 2010 Metal discovery">{{cite web|url=http://www.metal-discovery.com/Interviews/emilieautumn_interview_sheffield_2010_pt2.htm|title=Metal Discovery: Interview with Emilie Autumn|author=Holmes, Mark|date=March 9, 2010|publisher=Metal Discovery|pages=1‚Äì4|access-date=July 5, 2010}}</ref> According to Autumn, her music "wasn't meant to be cutesy" and is labeled as "industrial" mainly because of her use of drums and yelling.<ref name="LA Weekly"/> Her adaption of "[[Feste#O Mistress Mine|O Mistress Mine]]" was praised by author and theater director
[[Barry Edelstein]] as "a ravishing, guaranteed tearjerker".<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JikT2vM5YZ0C&q=Emilie+Autumn&pg=PA96|title=Bardisms: Shakespeare for All Occasions|author=Edelstein, Barry|year=2009|author-link=Barry Edelstein|location=New York|publisher=HarperCollins|pages=95‚Äì6|isbn=978-0-06-149351-5|oclc=232977957}}</ref>

For her live performances, which she calls [[dinner theatre]] because of her practice of throwing tea and tea-time snacks off of the stage, Autumn makes use of [[burlesque]]‚Äî"a show that was mainly using humour and sexuality to make a mockery of things that were going on socially and politically"<ref name="March 2010 Metal discovery"/>‚Äîto counterbalance the more morbid topics discussed in her music, such as abuse, suicide and self-mutilation.<ref name="Bizarre mag"/> Her shows feature handmade costumes,<ref name="March 2010 Metal discovery"/> fire tricks, theatrics, and her all-female backing band, The Bloody Crumpets, a group whose members have variously included burlesque performer [[Veronica Varlow]] as The Naughty Veronica, performance artist Maggie Lally as Captain Maggot, Jill Evyn as Lady Amalthea (or Moth), actress and performer Beth Hinderliter as The Blessed Contessa, actress Aprella Godfrey Barule as Lady Aprella, German musician Lucina as Little Lucina, cellist Sarah Kim as Lady Joo Hee, German costume designer Vecona as Captain Vecona, Jesselynn Desmond as Little Miss Sugarless, and [[Ulorin Vex]]. Her intention is for the live shows to be a statement of "anti-repression" and empowerment.<ref name="Metal discovery pg 2"/>

==Personal life==
She keeps a ritual of drawing a heart on her cheek as a symbol of protection.<ref name="Bizarre mag"/>

Autumn became vegetarian at age eleven after being unable to rationalize why she should eat farm animals but not her pet dog; in her late-teens, she became [[vegan]].<ref name="PETA">{{cite web|url=http://www.peta2.com/OUTTHERE/o-EmilieAutumn.asp|title=Pro Violinist Emilie Autumn's Interview|author=Harkens, Ray|publisher=Peta2|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120527141740/http://www.peta2.com/OUTTHERE/o-EmilieAutumn.asp|archive-date=May 27, 2012|access-date=November 12, 2014}}</ref> She has stated she believes that there is a link between the treatment of women and animals in society.<ref name="PETA"/> In August 2014, Autumn said she had developed [[copper toxicity]] and was no longer vegan, although still a committed vegetarian.<ref>{{cite web|title=Matcha Steamed Buns (Gluten Free)|url=http://www.emilieautumn.com/rollingscone/matcha-steamed-buns/|website=www.emilieautumn.com|publisher=Emilie Autumn's Rolling Stone|access-date=June 20, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160204055415/http://www.emilieautumn.com/rollingscone/|archive-date=February 4, 2016 |date=August 31, 2014}}</ref>

In 2021, Autumn adopted a [[Toy Manchester Terrier]], who she named Darjeeling.<ref>{{Cite instagram |author=Emilie Autumn |author-link= |user=emilieautumnofficial |postid=CLQjomKBWZK |date=13 February 2021 |title=Back in bed where we started, except that there is a PUPPY in the room! |script-title= |trans-title= |language= |location= |access-date=1 August 2021 |link=https://www.instagram.com/p/CLQjomKBWZK/ |url-status= |archive-url= |archive-date= |ref=}}</ref>

She has endorsed companies such as [[Manic Panic (brand)|Manic Panic]] and Samson Tech.<ref name="Links">{{cite web|url=http://www.emilieautumn.com/links.html |title=Links |year=2010 |publisher=Emilie Autumn Ent. LLC |access-date=January 26, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100921122518/http://emilieautumn.com/links.html |archive-date=September 21, 2010 |df=mdy }}</ref>

Autumn has [[bipolar disorder]], which she has discussed in a number of interviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalblast.net/interviews/emilie-autumn-interview/|title=Emilie Autumn Interview|website=Metal Blast|author=J Salmeron |date=April 17, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bryanreesman.com/2009/12/01/emilie-autumns-personal-asylum-part-two/|title=Emilie Autumn's Personal Asylum: Part Two
|website=Attention Deficit Delerium |author=Bryan Reesman |date=December 1, 2009}}</ref>
