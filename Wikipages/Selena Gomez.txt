'''Selena Marie Gomez''' ({{IPAc-en|s|ə|'|l|iː|n|ə|_|'|g|oʊ|m|ɛ|z}} {{respell|sə|LEE|nə|_|GOH|mez}}; born July 22, 1992) is an American singer, actress, and producer.<!--NO LAUNDRY LIST. ONLY NOTE OCCUPATION(S) ARTIST IS *NOTABLE* FOR. NO [[WP:ORIGINAL RESEARCH]]; [[MOS:BEGIN]], [[MOS:LEADBIO]]--> Born and raised in Texas, Gomez began her acting career on the children's television series ''[[Barney & Friends]]'' (2002–2004). In her teenage years, she rose to prominence for her lead role as [[Alex Russo]] in the [[Disney Channel]] television series ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place]]'' (2007–2012).

Alongside her television career, Gomez appeared in the films ''[[Another Cinderella Story]]'' (2008), ''[[Princess Protection Program]]'' (2009), ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place: The Movie]]'' (2009), ''[[Ramona and Beezus]]'' (2010), ''[[Monte Carlo (2011 film)|Monte Carlo]]'' (2011), ''[[Spring Breakers]]'' (2012), ''[[Getaway (2013 film)|Getaway]]'' (2013), ''[[The Fundamentals of Caring]]'' (2016), ''[[The Dead Don't Die (2019 film)|The Dead Don't Die]]'' (2019), and ''[[A Rainy Day in New York]]'' (2019). She voiced the character Mavis in the [[Hotel Transylvania|''Hotel Transylvania'' film franchise]] (2012–present). Gomez executive produced the [[Netflix]] television series ''[[13 Reasons Why]]'' (2017–2020) and ''[[Living Undocumented]]'' (2019) through her production company, July Moonhead Productions. She also executive produces and stars in the [[HBO Max]] cooking series ''[[Selena + Chef]]'' (2020–present) and the [[Hulu]] mystery-comedy series ''[[Only Murders in the Building]]'' (2021–present).

Gomez released three albums with her former band, [[Selena Gomez & the Scene]], all of which reached the top ten of the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and were certified [[Music recording certification|gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]]: ''[[Kiss & Tell (Selena Gomez & the Scene album)|Kiss & Tell]]'' (2009), ''[[A Year Without Rain]]'' (2010), and ''[[When the Sun Goes Down (Selena Gomez & the Scene album)|When the Sun Goes Down]]'' (2011). Additionally, Gomez's three solo studio albums—''[[Stars Dance]]'' (2013), ''[[Revival (Selena Gomez album)|Revival]]'' (2015), and ''[[Rare (Selena Gomez album)|Rare]]'' (2020)—each debuted atop the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Selena Gomez Earns Third No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart With 'Rare'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8548614/selena-gomez-rare-number-one-billboard-200-albums-chart|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=January 21, 2020|magazine=Billboard|access-date=May 4, 2020|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210126023533/http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8548614/selena-gomez-rare-number-one-billboard-200-albums-chart|archive-date=January 26, 2021}}</ref> She also released the Spanish-language EP ''[[Revelación]]'' (2021), for which she received a nomination for the [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Melendez|first=Miguel A.|url=https://www.etonline.com/selena-gomez-lands-first-ever-grammy-nomination-with-revelacion-175622|title=Selena Gomez Lands First-Ever GRAMMY Nomination With 'Revelación'|work=[[Entertainment Tonight]]|date=November 23, 2021|access-date=November 23, 2021|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211123190443/https://www.etonline.com/selena-gomez-lands-first-ever-grammy-nomination-with-revelacion-175622|archive-date=November 23, 2021}}</ref> A number of Gomez's singles have charted inside the top ten of the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], including "[[Come & Get It (Selena Gomez song)|Come & Get It]]", "[[The Heart Wants What It Wants]]", "[[Good for You (song)|Good for You]]", "[[Same Old Love]]", "[[Hands to Myself]]", "[[We Don't Talk Anymore (Charlie Puth song)|We Don't Talk Anymore]]", "[[It Ain't Me]]", and her first number-one song "[[Lose You to Love Me]]".

{{As of|2017}}, Gomez has sold over seven million albums and 22 million singles worldwide according to ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. She has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Selena Gomez|various accolades]] and was named ''Billboard''<nowiki/>'s [[Billboard Women in Music|Woman of the Year]] in 2017. She has a large following on social media and was at one point the most-followed individual on [[Instagram]]. Gomez's other ventures include a makeup line, a clothing line, a handbag line, and a fragrance line. She has worked with many charitable organizations and served as a [[UNICEF]] ambassador since age 17.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Charity, Action, Now! (C.A.N.)|url=https://www.unicefusa.org/supporters/celebrities/ambassadors/selena-gomez/can|website=UNICEF USA|language=en|access-date=May 6, 2020}}</ref>
