'''Ari Staprans Leff''' (born August 8, 1994), known professionally as '''Lauv''' ({{ipac-en|l|a ä|v}}<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=lauvsongs|author=Lauv|number=848731357132050432|title=like the word "loud" but with a v at the end|date=April 3, 2017|access-date=February 21, 2021}}</ref>), is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer, best known for his breakout hit "[[I Like Me Better]]"; included in his [[compilation album]] ''[[I Met You When I Was 18 (The Playlist)]]'', which was released in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=On The Rise: An Interview with Lauv|url=http://www.theaquarian.com/2016/04/13/on-the-rise-an-interview-with-lauv/}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=Lauv - Biography & History - AllMusic|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/lauv-mn0003441724/biography|access-date=May 27, 2017|website=AllMusic}}</ref> His debut album, ''[[How I'm Feeling]]'', was released on March 6, 2020, and debuted at number 16 on the US [[Billboard 200]]. Lauv has also written songs for other artists such as "[[Boys (Charli XCX song)|Boys]]" by [[Charli XCX]] and "[[No Promises (Cheat Codes song)|No Promises]]" by [[Cheat Codes (DJs)|Cheat Codes]] and [[Demi Lovato]].

==Early life==
Leff was born in [[San Francisco]], [[California]]. He spent his early childhood in a suburb of [[Atlanta, Georgia]], before his family relocated to a suburb of [[Philadelphia, Pennsylvania]]. He went to [[Radnor High School]].<ref name="time">{{cite news |last1=Bruner |first1=Raisa |title=Meet Lauv, the New Voice in Pop Making Hits Out of Heartbreak |url=http://time.com/5335106/lauv-interview/ |access-date=April 30, 2019 |newspaper=Time |date=August 17, 2018}}</ref> As a child, he was interested in music and took piano and viola lessons before learning how to play the guitar at the age of 11.<ref name=":0" /> Leff studied guitar, recording, sound design, production, voice, and songwriting with Jason Brook Zimmerman, co-owner and founder of the music lesson organization, Traveling Musician.<ref name="Traveling Musician">{{Cite web|url= https://travelingmusician.com/2019/11/01/zoe-spring-recital/|title=Student News|date=November 1, 2019|website=Traveling Musician|access-date=February 9, 2021}}</ref> He started writing songs in middle school, at around 14 years old.<ref name=":1"/> Many of his songs during this period focused on heartbreak and breakups, despite at the time, never having had a girlfriend.<ref name="time" /> During his time in high school, he played in several bands and studied jazz before moving on to electronic music.<ref name=":0" /> He was so "fully obsessed" in making music and playing shows that he had no interest to "drink, or do drugs, or anything".<ref name="Zack Sang Show">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MZVvdoN57BI|title=Lauv Talks Drugs & The Internet, Depression and New Music|last=Zack Sang Show|date=May 3, 2019|website=YouTube|access-date=February 24, 2020}}</ref> He has said that his sister, Aiva would drive him and his friends on tour, where they often played "to zero people".<ref name="trib">{{cite news |last1=Stewart |first1=Allison |title=Jingle stuff, youthful pop stars, Sabrina Carpenter, Lauv and all that fame |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-sabrina-carpenter-lauv-jingle-ball-1207-story.html |access-date=April 30, 2019 |newspaper=Chicago Tribune |date=December 3, 2018}}</ref> His early musical influences were [[Owl City]] and [[Never Shout Never]]. He would listen to Owl City's "[[Maybe I'm Dreaming]]" and study the production style and the abstract lyrical themes that use "dreamy" and "whimsical" images to describe love. He also adopted the artist name, ''Somersault Sunday'', and published some songs and [[extended plays]] on his [[Myspace]] account.<ref name="Zack Sang Show"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://myspace.com/somersaultsunday|title=Somersault Sunday on Myspace|last=Somersault Sunday|website=Myspace|access-date=February 24, 2020}}</ref>

After graduating high school, Leff decided to pursue a behind-the-scenes role in music seeing that his career was not taking off. He chose to study [[music technology]] at [[New York University]]'s [[Steinhardt School]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1"/> While at university, he studied abroad in [[Prague]] and was a member of the [[Zeta Psi]] fraternity.<ref name="nyu">{{cite news |title=NYU's Own Lauv Hits Big With First Single, "The Other" |url=https://nyulocal.com/nyus-own-lauv-hits-big-with-first-single-the-other-a9b7a6579971 |access-date=April 30, 2019 |newspaper=NYU Local |date=April 23, 2015}}</ref> He spent two of his four years at NYU as a studio intern for [[Jungle City Studios]], where artists such as [[Jay-Z]], [[Justin Timberlake]], and [[Alicia Keys]] recorded.<ref name="time"/> During his junior year, he signed a publishing deal after the success of his song "The Other" but he decided to finish university and "enjoy it" having only a year left and because he was also feeling "fortunate enough to be able to go there".<ref name="Zack Sang Show"/>

==Career==
===2008‚Äì2012: Early years and ''Somersault Sunday''===
Leff began writing music at an early age. When he was 12, he started his first rock duo with his friend Mike Wagenheim, and by age 13, the two teens started a metal band. After starting to try and write music, Leff began experimenting with an acoustic guitar and some synth sounds. A few months later, he wrote his own solo acoustic song called ''Parting Ways''. Leff then formed his first solo music project, ''Somersault Sunday'', when he was 14 years old on April 28, 2008. He started it first "as a side project, just for fun," but continued it after receiving enthusiastic online response.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sonicbids.com/band/somersaultsunday/|title=Somersault Sunday on Sonicbids|website=Sonicbids|access-date=August 16, 2020}}</ref>

''Somersault Sunday'' is an electro-infused pop rock solo-project. Under the ''Somersault Sunday'' project, he was able to release three [[extended plays]] namely, ''Parting Ways EP'' (2009), ''Intermission - EP'' (2010), and ''Phone Calls & Weekends'' (2010). Trevor Dahl of [[Cheat Codes (DJs)|Cheat Codes]] (who was then making music under the moniker ''Plug In Stereo'') featured on his track "Wishful Thinking" from the ''Intermission'' [[extended play|EP]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://myspace.com/somersaultsunday/|title=Somersault Sunday on Myspace|website=Myspace|access-date=August 15, 2020}}</ref> The ''Phone Calls & Weekends'' EP, was produced by Kevin Gates, who also mixed and produced some of the songs of [[Never Shout Never]], [[The Ready Set]], and [[Cady Groves]]. He posted his songs on [[MySpace]] along with some song covers, and kept close contact with his fans through the platform. He had a band playing with him during live shows and concerts. Leff was known for his basement concerts in his early middle and high school years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.reverbnation.com/somersaultsunday/|title=Somersault Sunday on ReverbNation|website=ReverbNation|access-date=August 15, 2020}}</ref>

===2014‚Äì2016: Beginnings and ''Lost in the Light''===
Leff focused on writing and producing for other artists during college, straying from his personal songwriting style. During his sophomore year, he stumbled upon an interview with [[Paul Simon]], in which Simon described his songwriting approach as a process of uncovering his innermost buried feelings. The interview helped Leff to get in touch with the way he used to write.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Han |first1=Alex |title=Review: LAUV discovers himself through his music |url=https://eagleeye.news/artsandleisure/review-lauv-discovers-himself-through-his-music/ |access-date=April 30, 2019 |publisher=Eagle Eye |date=March 18, 2018}}</ref> Afterwards, Leff adopted the stage name Lauv, [[Latvian language|Latvian]] for "lion" (lauva), as a nod to his mother's heritage. His first name, Ari, means "lion" in [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]], and his zodiac sign is Leo.<ref name="nyu"/><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2017-07-10-interview-lauv-reveals-the-touching-inspiration-behind-i-like-me-better/|title=INTERVIEW: LAUV Reveals The Touching Inspiration Behind 'I Like Me Better'|last=Mastrogiannis|first=Nicole|date=July 10, 2017|website=iHeartRadio|access-date=April 13, 2018}}</ref>

While getting over a break-up in fall of 2014, he co-wrote "The Other" with Michael Matosic.<ref name="time"/><ref name="nyu"/> Although he was focused on writing potential songs for other artists, he felt "The Other" worked better as his own song. It would be the first song he released under the name Lauv.<ref name="nyu"/> With "The Other", he also discovered his stripped-down electronic pop sound.<ref name="time"/> The song is a fusion of [[rhythm and blues]] and indie-pop with influence from jazz guitar. The song gained blog attention and, after he uploaded it to [[SoundCloud]] in 2015,<ref name="time"/> went viral, peaking at number three on blog [[Poll aggregator|aggregator]] [[Hype Machine]],<ref name="nyu"/><ref>{{cite web|title=I'm watching Lauv at Ones To Watch!|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/artist/lauv|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170705060959/http://www.onestowatch.com/artist/lauv|url-status=dead|archive-date=July 5, 2017|website=[[Ones to Watch]]|access-date=July 22, 2017}}</ref> and hitting the Global Top 100 on [[Spotify]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://foundationsmusic.com/lauv/|title=Lauv - Foundations|website=foundationsmusic.com|access-date=April 14, 2019}}</ref>

After graduating from NYU, Lauv signed to the publishing company Prescription Songs.<ref name=":2" />

On September 25, 2015, Lauv released his debut [[extended play|EP]], ''Lost in the Light'', which featured "The Other".<ref name="auto">{{cite web|last=Lauv|date=October 3, 2015|title=Lauv - "Lost In The Light - EP" (Official Full Stream)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eig98TG5fo0|access-date=May 27, 2017|via=YouTube}}</ref> Then, apart from releasing the single "Question" featuring Travis Mills in 2016, Lauv worked on writing and producing songs for other artists.

Most notably, he co-wrote and co-produced "[[No Promises (Cheat Codes song)|No Promises]]" for [[Cheat Codes (DJs)|Cheat Codes]] and [[Demi Lovato]],<ref name=":1" /> which reached number 7 on the [[Pop Songs (Billboard chart)|Billboard Pop Songs]] chart.<ref name="people">{{cite news |last1=Sands |first1=Nicole |title=5 Things to Know About 'I Like Me Better' Singer Lauv |url=https://people.com/music/lauv-singer-five-things-to-know/ |access-date=April 30, 2019 |work=People |date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> In an interview with [[iHeartRadio]], Lauv stated that he's been friends with Trevor Dahl from [[Cheat Codes (DJs)|Cheat Codes]] since "back in the [[Myspace]] days."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2017-07-10-interview-lauv-reveals-the-touching-inspiration-behind-i-like-me-better/|title=INTERVIEW: LAUV Reveals The Touching Inspiration Behind 'I Like Me Better'|last=Mastrogiannis|first=Nicole|date=July 10, 2017|website=www.iheart.com|access-date=October 29, 2019}}</ref>

===2017‚Äì2018: Breakthrough===
On May 19, 2017, Leff released the single "[[I Like Me Better]]",<ref name="billboard">{{cite web|title=Lauv Premieres New Song 'I Like Me Better' Before Upcoming Tour: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7800919/lauv-i-like-me-better-premiere-tour|website=Billboard|access-date=July 22, 2017}}</ref> an up-tempo track inspired by his relationship that began shortly after he moved to New York City.<ref name=":1" /> It peaked at number 27 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]],<ref name="billboard1">{{cite news |last1=Cirisano |first1=Tatiana |title=Lauv On Touring With 'Nicest Person' Ed Sheeran, Pulling All-Nighters in Tokyo & His Favorite Moments of 2018 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/year-in-music-2018/8490260/lauv-touring-ed-sheeran-2018-highlights-interview |access-date=April 30, 2019 |work=Billboard |date=December 24, 2018}}</ref> earned platinum certification in seven countries (including the US), and gold certification in three.<ref name="people"/> As of March 9, 2021, the song has amassed over 1.1 billion streams on [[Spotify]].

In 2017, he headlined his own ''Late Nights, Deep Talks'' tour, in which he performed in eight cities across the United States. The tour began on May 23 in Los Angeles and concluded on June 7 in New York City. On July 9, he also performed at [[Summerfest]] in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.<ref name="billboard"/>

It was announced on September 4, 2017, that Leff would be joining [[Ed Sheeran]] as the supporting act on his [[Divide Tour]] in Asia.<ref name="divide tour">{{cite web|title=Independent singer, songwriter and producer Lauv will join Ed Sheeran through his Asia tour, 14 date run commencing in Osaka.|url=https://www.facebook.com/AEGPresentsAsia/posts/1585680418156206|access-date=September 4, 2017}}</ref> Several of Ed Sheeran's Asia tour dates were cancelled or rescheduled as a result of injuries he sustained from a bicycle accident, but Leff proceeded with promotional tours in cities such as Manila, Philippines,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/lauv-alabang-town-center-manila-philippines-concert-ed-sheeran-asia-tour-up-town|title=LAUV to perform in Ayala Malls|last=Words by Bandwagon|date=October 16, 2017|website=Bandwagon|access-date=April 13, 2019}}</ref> officially kicking off the [[√∑ Tour|Divide Tour]] with [[Ed Sheeran]] in Singapore on November 11, 2017.

Leff embarked on his first world tour, ''I Met You When I Was 18'', in the first half of 2018. The tour began on January 18 in Seattle, Washington and concluded on April 29 in Oslo, Norway. Artists [[Jeremy Zucker]] and [[Ashe (singer)|Ashe]] joined him as supporting acts in most of the North American tour dates.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8007018/lauv-2018-tour-dates-live-spotify-playlist|title=Fast-Rising Pop Artist Lauv Announces 2018 Headlining Tour|last=Payne|first=Chris|date=October 23, 2017|website=Billboard|access-date=April 13, 2019}}</ref> During the tour, he had a box named "My Blue Thoughts", in which he told fans to write down anything they wanted to and put it inside. He commemorated the notes on his blog mybluethoughts.world.<ref name="time"/> On August 30, 2018, Leff released the single "Superhero", a song inspired by a note from the box.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.j-14.com/posts/lauv-superhero-165918/|title=<3: The True Story Of How A Fan's Anonymous Note Inspired Lauv's Song 'Superhero'|last=Maldonado|first=Jennifer|date=September 7, 2018|website=J-14|access-date=April 13, 2019}}</ref> He performed at [[Lollapalooza]] in Chicago on August 3, 2018.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sunkel |first1=Cameron |title=FIVE UNMISSABLE SETS TO CATCH AT LOLLAPALOOZA 2018 |url=https://edm.com/events/lollapalooza-2018-artists-to-watch |access-date=May 13, 2019 |publisher=edm.com |date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018, Leff released ''[[I Met You When I Was 18 (The Playlist)]]'', a 17-track collection of new and previously released tracks, including the singles "I Like Me Better" and "The Other", that he had been working on over the course of a few years.<ref name="trib"/><ref name="billboard1"/><ref>{{cite news |last1=Hoffman |first1=Lauren |title=EXCLUSIVE: 5 Things You Don't Know About Lauv |url=https://947thewave.radio.com/blogs/lauren-hoffman/exclusive-5-things-you-dont-know-about-lauv |access-date=April 30, 2019 |publisher=94.7 The Wave |date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> It has also been described as a "playlist" that Lauv updated gradually until completing it in May 2018.<ref name="lofficiel">{{cite news |last1=Sahai |first1=Fred |title=Q+A with Hopeless Romantic Lauv |url=https://www.lofficielusa.com/music/q-a-lauv-2019 |access-date=April 30, 2019 |publisher=L'Officiel |date=April 12, 2019}}</ref> It chronicles his move to New York City and the first time he fell in love.<ref name="people"/><ref name="lofficiel"/> By August 2018, it had been streamed over 1 billion times on [[Spotify]].<ref name="time"/>

In June 2018, Leff ranked number one on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Emerging Artists chart, dated June 9. As of September 29, he had been at the top spot of the chart for a total of 13 weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/emerging-artists/2018-06-09|title=Billboard Emerging Artists|work=Billboard|access-date=October 2, 2018}}</ref>

He released the single "[[There's No Way (Lauv song)|There's No Way]]", a collaboration with [[Julia Michaels]], on September 27, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/lauv-julia-michaels-theres-no-way-video-729913/|title=Watch Lauv, Julia Michaels Miss Out on Love in 'There's No Way' Video|last=Blistein|first=Jon|date=September 27, 2018|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=April 14, 2019}}</ref>

In November 2018, Lauv announced his first headlining tour starting the next year in Asia, including India, Singapore, Thailand, Philippines, Hong Kong, Indonesia, and Japan. It began on May 13, 2019, in Mumbai, India and concluded on May 30 in Tokyo.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/8486098/lauv-2019-asia-tour-dates-schedule|title=Lauv Announces 2019 Asia Tour: See Dates|last=Billboard Radio China|date=November 20, 2018|website=Billboard|access-date=April 13, 2019}}</ref>
