'''Andrew Charles Grammer''' (born December 3, 1983)<ref>{{cite web |url=http://fresh1027.cbslocal.com/2011/09/26/20-things-you-didnt-know-about-andy-grammer/ |title=20 Things You Didn't Know About Andy Grammer |work=Fresh 102.7 |publisher=CBS Local |date=September 26, 2011 |access-date=March 9, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://hollywoodlife.com/celeb/andy-grammer/ |title=Andy Grammer |last=Fuller |first=Bonnie |work=Hollywood Life |date=2016 |access-date=March 9, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Californiabirthindex.org |url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/andrew_grammer_born_1983_15837234 |access-date=July 26, 2017}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. He is signed to [[S-Curve Records]].<ref name="spinner.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.spinner.com/2011/03/08/andy-grammer-keep-your-head-up/|title=Andy Grammer Goes From Busker to Pop Phenom, With Rainn Wilson's Help|publisher=Spinner|date=March 8, 2011|access-date=May 9, 2012|last=Tacopino|first=Joe}}</ref>

His debut album, ''[[Andy Grammer (album)|Andy Grammer]]'', was released in 2011<ref name="hollywoodreporter.com">{{cite web|last=Rome|first=Emily|title=How Andy Grammer Graduated from Street Busker to the Majors|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/how-andy-grammer-graduated-street-200925|work=HollywoodReporter.com|publisher=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=May 2, 2013|date=June 13, 2011}}</ref> and spawned the singles "[[Keep Your Head Up (Andy Grammer song)|Keep Your Head Up]]" and "[[Fine by Me (Andy Grammer song)|Fine by Me]]". His second album ''[[Magazines or Novels]]'' was released in 2014, and featured "[[Honey, I'm Good]]" which is his most successful song to date, peaking at number 9 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. This single has been [[RIAA certification#Singles|certified triple-platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) and was ranked one of the ten best-selling songs of 2015 by [[Nielsen SoundScan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://live.wtmx.com/listen/artist/9e60ea29-9607-4f7d-aa96-2092ef41f0d3?slide=19|title=101.9 THE MIX LIVE Stream &#124; Chicago|website=101.9 THE MIX LIVE Stream &#124; Chicago}}</ref>
The ''Magazines or Novels'' album also featured the certified gold single "[[Good to Be Alive (Hallelujah)]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bmg.com/us/news/Andy-Grammer-announces-new-album-single-and-tour.html|title=US: Andy Grammer announces new album and single|date=November 3, 2017|website=BMG}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Andrew Charles Grammer was born in Los Angeles, the son of Kathryn Willoughby<ref>{{cite web|title=Andy Grammer Launches Campaign for Women's Equality|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Andy-Grammer-Launches-Campaign-for-Womens-Equality-20130510|publisher=Broadwayworld.com|access-date=December 12, 2013|date=May 10, 2013}}</ref> and recording artist [[Red Grammer|Robert Crane "Red" Grammer]].<ref name="tjcnewspaper.com">{{cite web|last=Smith|first=Haley|title=Performer Andy Grammer Brings Hollywood to TJC|url=http://mytjcnews.com/arts-entertainment/performer-andy-grammer-brings-hollywood-to-tjc/|work=MyTJCNews.com|publisher=MyTJCNews.com|access-date=May 2, 2013|date=December 2, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212215357/http://mytjcnews.com/arts-entertainment/performer-andy-grammer-brings-hollywood-to-tjc/|archive-date=December 12, 2013|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.copyrightencyclopedia.com/rolling-along-singing-a-song-by-robert-crane-grammer-red/ |title=Rolling along, singing a song. By Robert Crane Grammer (Red Grammer) & Kathryn Willoughby Grammer |website=Copyrightencyclopedia.com |access-date=2016-06-02}}</ref> He is of [[German people|German]] and [[English people|English]] descent. He grew up in [[Chester (town), New York|Chester, New York]] and  graduated from [[Monroe-Woodbury High School]].<ref name=20things>{{cite web|title=Andy Grammer: 20 Things You Didn't Know...|url=http://965tic.cbslocal.com/2013/03/27/20-things-you-didnt-know-about-andy-grammer/|work=96.5 TIC FM|access-date=December 12, 2013|date=March 27, 2013}}</ref> As a child, he learned to play the trumpet and later the guitar and piano.<ref name=20things/> He began writing songs at 15.<ref>{{cite web|last=Herman|first=Emily|title=Andy Grammer Visits Children's Hospital Of Philadelphia!|url=http://ryanseacrestfoundation.org/2012/02/21/andy-grammer-visits-childrens-hospital-of-philadelphia/|publisher=Ryanseacrestfoundation.org|access-date=December 12, 2013|date=February 21, 2012}}</ref> At 20, he left [[Binghamton University]] in [[Vestal, New York]], and returned to Los Angeles,<ref name="nycartscene.com">{{cite web|last=Morelli|first=Christina|title=Andy Grammer: Sincere, Soulful, and All Smiles|url=http://www.nycartscene.com/?p=136|work=NYCArtScene.com|publisher=NYC Art Scene|date=November 19, 2009|access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> where he currently resides.<ref name="starpulse.com">{{cite web|last=Demchak|first=Erin|title=Andy Grammer Chats With Starpulse Before His Show At The Troubadour|url=http://www.starpulse.com/news/Erin_Demchak/2011/02/24/andy_grammer_chats_with_starpulse_befo|work=Starpulse.com|publisher=Starpulse.com|date=February 24, 2011|access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> In 2007, he graduated from the [[California State University, Northridge]], with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in [[music industry]] studies.<ref>{{cite web|title=Andy Grammer Keeps His Head Up|url=http://www.grammy.com/news/andy-grammer-keeps-his-head-up|publisher=Grammy.com|access-date=December 12, 2013|date=June 30, 2011}}</ref>

==Musical career==
[[File:Andy Grammer Charlotte.jpg|thumb|Grammer performing in [[Charlotte, North Carolina]], June 2012]]
Grammer started as a [[busker]] on the [[Third Street Promenade]] in [[Santa Monica]].<ref name="spinner.com"/> He was discovered by his manager Ben Singer in July 2009 and signed with [[S-Curve Records]] in April 2010.<ref name=usatoday>{{cite web|last=Lopez|first=Korina|title=On the Verge: Andy Grammer|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2011-06-09-on-the-verge-andy-grammer-taylor-swift-los-angeles_n.htm|work=[[USA Today]]|access-date=December 12, 2013|date=June 13, 2011}}</ref> Grammer's popular songs include: "[[Honey, I'm Good.]]", "[[Keep Your Head Up (Andy Grammer song)|Keep Your Head Up]]", "[[Fine by Me (Andy Grammer song)|Fine by Me]]", "Lunatic", "Ladies", "[[Fresh Eyes]]", "Biggest Man in L.A.", "Numbers", "Stepping Stone", "Couple More Sleeves", "Fireflies", and "The Pocket".

His debut video "[[Keep Your Head Up (Andy Grammer song)|Keep Your Head Up]]", which featured actor [[Rainn Wilson]],<ref name="spinner.com" /><ref name=billboardvideo>{{cite web|last=Bruno|first=Antony|title=Andy Grammer, Rainn Wilson Create Viewer-Interactive Video|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/viral-videos/952686/andy-grammer-rainn-wilson-create-viewer-interactive-video|work=Billboard.com|publisher=Billboard|access-date=May 2, 2013|date=October 25, 2010}}</ref> was an iTunes Video of the Week in 2010, and won MTV's "O Music Awards" for the most innovative video on April 25, 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/viral-videos/952686/andy-grammer-rainn-wilson-create-viewer-interactive-video|title=Andy Grammer, Rainn Wilson Create Viewer-Interactive Video|date=October 25, 2010|website=Billboard}}</ref> He performed the song on ''[[The Rachael Ray Show]]'' on November 17, 2010.<ref name=rachaelrayshow>{{cite web|title=Andy Grammer Performance|url=http://www.rachaelrayshow.com/show/segments/view/andy-grammer-performance/|work=RachaelRayShow.com|publisher=KWP Studios Inc.|access-date=May 2, 2013|date=November 17, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121110104705/http://www.rachaelrayshow.com/show/segments/view/andy-grammer-performance/|archive-date=November 10, 2012|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> It has since debuted at No. 94 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]<ref>{{cite web|last=Trust|first=Gary|title=Adele's Still 'Deep' Atop Hot 100, Coldplay Makes a Splash|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/470794/adeles-still-deep-atop-hot-100-coldplay-makes-a-splash|work=Billboard.com|publisher=Billboard|access-date=May 3, 2013|date=June 8, 2011}}</ref> Grammer's debut album sales for the first week earned him the No. 1 spot on the Billboard Heatseekers Albums Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/archive/charts/2011/heatseekers-albums|title=Heatseekers Albums - 2011 Archive|publisher=Billboard Magazine|access-date=December 22, 2017}}</ref> He opened for [[Plain White T's]] for the second leg of their "[[Wonders of the Younger]]" tour in the spring of 2011.<ref name="soundspike.com">{{cite web|last=Fuoco-Karasinski|first=Christina|title=Plain White T's add more "Wonders of the Younger" dates|url=http://www.soundspike.com/news/tour/1800-plain_white_t_s_tour_plain_white_t_s_add.html|work=SoundSpike.com|publisher=SoundSpike Media|access-date=May 3, 2013|date=March 22, 2011}}</ref> In January 2011, he was named an "Artist to Watch" by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine.<ref name=daily>{{cite web|title=Andy Grammer concert at Bay|url=http://www.dailypress.net/page/content.detail/id/528676/Andy-Grammer-concert-at-Bay.html?nav=5001|work=DailyPress.net|publisher=Daily Press (Michigan)|access-date=May 3, 2013|date=February 21, 2011}}</ref> He performed at [[South by Southwest|SXSW 2011]], playing BMI's Acoustic Brunch,<ref name="bmi.com">{{cite web|last=Kennedy|first=Drew|title=SXSW Ready: Andy Grammer|url=http://www.bmi.com/musicworld/entry/sxsw_ready_andy_grammer/|work=BMI.com|publisher=Broadcast Music Inc.|access-date=May 3, 2013|date=March 11, 2011}}</ref> where he was one of the artists contributing to a recording produced by [[Hanson (band)|Hanson]] to benefit the victims of the [[2011 Japan earthquake]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Chancellor|first=Jennifer|title=Hanson to release digital album for Japan quake victims|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/article.aspx/Hanson_to_release_digital_album_for_Japan_quake_victims/20110323_296_0_lastwe515765|work=TulsaWorld.com|publisher=Tulsa World|access-date=May 3, 2013|date=March 23, 2011}} {{dead link|date=December 2019}}</ref>

Grammer credits his music influences to three genres of music: guitar players like [[Jason Mraz]], [[John Mayer]] and [[Jack Johnson (musician)|Jack Johnson]]; modern pianists like [[Coldplay]], [[The Fray]], and [[OneRepublic]]; and hip-hop vocalists like [[Common (rapper)|Common]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Jay-Z]], and [[Kanye West]].<ref name=usatoday/>

===2011–2013: ''Andy Grammer''===
His debut album, ''[[Andy Grammer (album)|Andy Grammer]]'', was released on June 14, 2011, on [[S-Curve Records]].{{citation needed|date=September 2021}} The self-titled album was produced by [[Matt Wallace]] from [[Maroon 5]], [[Matt Radosevich]], [[Dave Katz (songwriter)|Dave Katz]], and [[Sam Hollander]] of [[Gym Class Heroes]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Hrabkovska|first=Silvia|url=https://www.boomsbeat.com/articles/70972/20150924/50-facts-andy-grammer-joined-dad-stage-sing-promoting-fierce.htm |title=50 facts about Andy Grammer: he often joined his dad on-stage to sing, promoting his fierce ambition to succeed |publisher=boombeats.com |date=September 24, 2015 |access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref>

On the new album, Grammer said:
{{blockquote|My ultimate goal is to try to be real. It just so happens that I'm usually more happy than sad when I'm writing. And in general I think that life is pretty great, and it's cool to be here, so that comes through in my music. I don't have a dismal outlook, but I don't aim to make positive music. It's just what I am.<ref name="hollywoodreporter.com"/>}}

He joined [[Natasha Bedingfield]] on her [[Less is More Tour]], which began June 2011, and [[Colbie Caillat]] on her US and Canada tour, which began August 2011.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.natashabedingfield.com/us/news/us/2011/04/20/natasha_announces_less_is_more_tour |publisher=[[Sony Music Entertainment]] |date=April 19, 2011 |title=NATASHA BEDINGFIELD ANNOUNCES "LESS IS MORE" SUMMER TOUR PRESENTED BY FRESCHETTA® |access-date=May 7, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110522034303/http://www.natashabedingfield.com/us/news/us/2011/04/20/natasha_announces_less_is_more_tour |url-status=dead |archive-date=May 22, 2011 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.andygrammer.com/news/entry/on_tour_with_colbie_caillat_now |title=Andy Grammer on tour with Colbie Caillat |publisher=andygrammer.com |date=August 8, 2011 |access-date=July 5, 2012}}</ref> [[Grammy Museum at L.A. Live|The Grammy Museum]], in Los Angeles, featured him in the first installment of its homegrown local artist series starting in May 2011.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lewis|first=Andy|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/music_blog/2011/05/grammy-museum-andy-grammer-homegrown-may-31.html |title=Grammy Museum to launch Homegrown series spotlighting L.A.-area music on May 31 with Andy Grammer* (Updated) |publisher=Latimesblogs.latimes.com |date=May 17, 2011 |access-date=May 9, 2012}}</ref>

===2014–2016: ''Magazines or Novels''===
On March 25, 2014, he debuted "Back Home", the lead single from his second album, ''[[Magazines or Novels]]''. The song, co-written with Ryan Met from the group [[AJR (band)|AJR]], was released to iTunes on April 8, 2014. The album was released August 5, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ryanseacrest.com/2014/03/25/andy-grammer-unveils-new-feel-good-single-back-home-listen/ |title=Andy Grammer Unveils New Feel-Good Single 'Back Home': Listen |publisher=Ryanseacrest.com |date=2014-03-25 |access-date=2015-05-22}}</ref>

On November 11, 2014, he released his biggest single to date, "[[Honey, I'm Good.]]" which peaked at #9 on the Billboard Hot 100, and was certified triple-platinum by the [[RIAA]].

On August 31, 2015, he was announced as one of the celebrities who will compete on the [[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 21)|21st season]] of ''[[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)|Dancing with the Stars]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OnAirWithRyan/status/638357346738049024|title=On Air/Ryan Seacrest Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=August 31, 2015|date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> He was paired with professional dancer [[Peta Murgatroyd]] until an ankle injury forced her to withdraw.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/article/dancing-stars-season-21-peta-murgatroyd-injured|title=Dancing with the Stars' Peta Murgatroyd Sidelined for Season 21 Due to Injury|last=Gomez|first=Patrick|work=People|access-date=August 31, 2015|date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> His new partner was [[Allison Holker]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abc.go.com/shows/dancing-with-the-stars/cast-announcement|title=Dancing with the Stars Season 21 Cast Announcement|publisher=ABC|date=September 2, 2015|access-date=September 2, 2015}}</ref> Grammer and Holker were eliminated on Week 8 of competition and finished in 7th place.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.buddytv.com/articles/dancing-with-the-stars/dancing-with-the-stars-recap-i-57944.aspx|title='Dancing with the Stars' Recap: Immunity and the Dance-Offs|work=buddytv.com|access-date=November 2, 2015}}</ref>

In addition to his stint on the show, he released a new single "[[Good to Be Alive (Hallelujah)]]" on August 24, which was another gold-certified hit for him, and was included on a re-issued deluxe version of his ''Magazines or Novels'' album.

On October 27, 2015, he performed the National Anthem prior to the start of the opening game of the World Series between the [[Kansas City Royals]] and the [[New York Mets]] at [[Kauffman Stadium]] in Kansas City.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/andy-grammer|title=Andy Grammer|website=Billboard}}</ref> In January 2016, he performed an [[a cappella]] version of "[[The Star-Spangled Banner]]" at the [[Denver Broncos]]–[[New England Patriots]] [[2015–16 NFL playoffs#AFC: Denver Broncos 20, New England Patriots 18|AFC Championship Game]] in Denver.<ref>[http://www.kansascity.com/entertainment/ent-columns-blogs/back-to-rockville/article41656140.html Andy Grammer's national anthem wasn't quite world class] Timothy Finn. [[Kansas City Star]]. October 28, 2015. Retrieved March 9, 2016</ref><ref>[http://www.bostonherald.com/sports/patriots/the_blitz/2016/01/best_worst_from_patriots_loss_to_broncos Best & Worst from Patriots' loss to Broncos] Dan Ventura. Boston Herald. March 9, 2016. Retrieved March 9, 2016</ref>

===2016–present: ''The Good Parts'' and ''Naive''===
On July 29, 2016, Grammer released a new single titled "[[Fresh Eyes]]", which was another platinum hit for him that reach #19 on the Spotify Global Viral 50 Chart.<ref>{{cite web | title=The Spotify & Apple Music tracker | website=Spot On Track | date=2016-08-04 | url=https://www.spotontrack.com/charts/daily/viral/global/2016-08-04 | access-date=2020-06-28}}</ref>

On June 9, 2017, Grammer released his new single featuring [[LunchMoney Lewis]] titled "Give Love". Grammer also wrote and performed the song "A Friend Like You" for the animated film ''[[Captain Underpants: The First Epic Movie]]''.<ref name="BillboardEpicSoundtrack">{{cite news|last1=Tom|first1=Lauren|title=Captain Underpants' Movie Scores Epic Soundtrack With 'Weird Al', Lil Yachty & More|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/movies/7792931/captain-underpants-movie-soundtrack-weird-al-yankovic-lil-yachty|access-date=May 20, 2017|work=Billboard|date=May 12, 2017}}</ref>

The third single from his album ''[[The Good Parts]]'', titled "Smoke Clears", was released on November 3, 2017. The album was released on December 1, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8054935/andy-grammer-new-album-the-good-parts-songs-stories-interview|title=Andy Grammer's New Album 'The Good Parts': Stories Behind Songs|date=December 1, 2017|website=Billboard}}</ref>

On February 21, 2019, Grammer released his new single, "[[Don't Give Up on Me (Andy Grammer song)|Don't Give Up On Me]]", from the soundtrack of the film ''[[Five Feet Apart]]''.<ref name="Soundtrack">{{cite news|title=Five Feet Apart' Movie Soundtrack Andy Grammer - Don't Give Up On Me|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ouEezpuPc3A|access-date=February 21, 2019|via=YouTube|date=February 21, 2019}}</ref> It is also the lead single from his fourth studio album, ''[[Naive (Andy Grammer album)|Naive]]'', which was released on July 26, 2019.
